## Content Refinements: Narrative Alignment

### Tiffany's Talk: Market Critique Integration

**Current issue:** Reads as standard company show; needs stronger analytical positioning  
**Refinement needed:**
- Open with historical context (early internet era, App Store era, personal software as empowerment fantasy)
- Transition: What happened? Why did personal software become a luxury?
- Market analysis: Current state of consumer AI tools (segmented by capability, all underfulfilling agency)
- Problem statement: Why this gap exists (vendor incentives, infrastructure complexity, etc.)
- Solution: Zo as answer to "what would new computer architecture look like?"
- Demo: Personal website as proof—but emphasize *capability*, not *aesthetics*

**Key messaging points:**
- Not "here's a pretty website"; rather "here's persistent, extensible, agent-powered infrastructure"
- Lovable makes UIs; Zo enables autonomy
- The distinction matters; the event is for people who care about autonomy

### Vrijen's Talk: Non-Technical Journey as Validation

**Core narrative:** "I tried everything. Nothing worked. Until Zo."  
**Key pain points to reference:**
1. Zapier fatigue (everyone in audience likely feels this)
2. Integration lock-in (if you've invested in Zapier, switching is costly)
3. AI productivity paradox (AI tools promise efficiency but create overwhelm)
4. The gap between "I can make something" and "I can make something complex"

**Demo strategy:**
- Open with self-intro (non-technical founder; came to Zo with frustration, not expertise)
- Show productivity tracker (proof that non-technical person can build complex automation)
- Reference personal CRM, warm intro workflows, follow-up tracking (other examples of complexity)
- Close: "This is what's possible when you own your infrastructure"

**Tone:** Humble, relatable, aspirational—not "look how smart I am," but "look what's now possible for builders"
