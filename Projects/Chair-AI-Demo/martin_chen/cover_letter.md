## Analysis: Why This Cover Letter Fails

**Generic Opening:**
- "Express my interest" - template language
- "Would be a good fit" - weak, tentative claim
- No hook, no personality

**Boring Middle:**
- Lists responsibilities from resume (no new information)
- "Work closely with teams" - every PM says this
- No specific examples or stories
- No demonstration of insight

**Weak Closing:**
- "Particularly interested because" - surface-level reasoning
- "Following developments in AI" - vague, unspecific
- "Excited to contribute" - everyone says this
- No memorable takeaway

**Missing Elements:**
- No specific insight about Chair AI's product
- No demonstration of understanding user problems
- No story that shows how candidate works
- No personality or distinctive voice
- No reason to remember this candidate

**Comparison to Strong Cover Letter:**
A strong cover letter might include:
- Specific insight: "I noticed your recent update to smart categorization - the way it learns from corrections is elegant"
- Personal connection: "As someone who's spent 200+ hours in customer support queues, I viscerally understand the frustration Chair AI solves"
- Concrete example: "When our engineering team said a feature was 'impossible,' I spent two weeks prototyping alternatives..."

Martin's letter has none of this. It's professional, polite, and completely forgettable.
