## Career Progression

### **Canva (Customer Success Manager, 2019-2021)**
Holden's first job out of Stanford. He picked Canva because he wanted to work at a company that cared about design and user experience, not just raw technical horsepower. Spent 2 years in Sydney managing enterprise customers (education, nonprofits, SMBs).

**What he learned:**
- How designers think and work
- User research fundamentals (conducted sessions with teachers, nonprofits)
- How to translate customer pain into product requirements
- The power of good UX (watched Canva's collaborative editing features go from prototype to 10M+ users)

**Why he left:** Wanted to get more technical again and move back to SF. Loved Canva's design-first culture but craved deeper product ownership.

### **Stripe (Solutions Engineer, 2021-2023)**
Holden joined Stripe as a Solutions Engineer, helping enterprise customers integrate payment APIs. This was his "get technical again" phase. Worked with companies like Lyft, Instacart, DoorDash on payment infrastructure.

**What he learned:**
- How to architect scalable technical solutions
- Developer experience and API design
- How to bridge customer needs and eng constraints
- The importance of documentation and SDK quality

**Why he left:** Loved the technical depth but realized he wanted to own the product strategy, not just implement it for customers. Wanted to build, not consult.

### **Zo (Product Manager, 2023-Present)**
Holden joined Zo as one of the early PMs. Shipped the AI agent scheduling platform (0→1 product), grew MAU from 2K → 12K in 6 months, and established the OKR framework that the product org still uses.

**What he loves about it:**
- Ownership over product direction
- Tight feedback loops with users
- Building AI-native products from scratch
- Working with a small, talented team

## Character Note: Silicon Valley Reference

The name "Holden Hall" is a playful nod to Monica Hall from *Silicon Valley*. In the show, Monica is a stressed-out VC constantly dealing with dysfunction. The character Holden is her **successful younger brother** - the one who stresses her out at Thanksgiving because he's "doing everything right" while she's putting out fires with her portfolio companies.

**Why this works:**
- Monica's maiden name is Hall (before she's revealed to be dating Jian-Yang's landlord, Erlich)
- The dynamic is realistic: overachieving younger sibling who makes family gatherings awkward
- It's a subtle character joke that doesn't overshadow the persona's authenticity
- Holden (first name) is neutral and professional

The reference is a fun Easter egg for fans of the show, but Holden stands on his own as a character: a design-fluent, technical PM who cares about user research and building products people actually want to use.
