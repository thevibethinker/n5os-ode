Hi Chair AI team,

I'm writing to apply for the Product Manager role at Chair AI. I'm currently a PM at Zo Computer, and before that I was at Stripe working on developer tools and payment integrations. I'm drawn to Chair AI because I think event coordination is one of those problems that feels simple but is actually incredibly hard—and AI might finally make it solvable.

**Why I'm interested in Chair AI:**

I've spent the last 4 years building products at the intersection of technical capability and user simplicity. At Zo, I launched an AI agent platform that grew from 0 to 12K+ deployed agents in 90 days. At Stripe, I worked on developer tools and helped reduce API integration time by 35%. The through-line in both roles is taking powerful, complex technology and making it accessible to people who just want to solve their problem.

Chair AI feels like the same challenge. Coordinating events—especially across time zones, preferences, and unclear availability—is a coordination nightmare that most tools don't actually solve. They just digitize the back-and-forth. If Chair AI can use AI to handle the ambiguity and negotiation, that's genuinely valuable.

**What I'd bring to the role:**

1. **User research discipline:** I've conducted 100+ user interviews across Gainsight, Stripe, and Zo. I know how to listen for patterns, not just feature requests, and translate insights into product direction.

2. **Cross-functional collaboration:** At Zo, I work closely with engineers and designers to ship iteratively. I'm comfortable with technical constraints and can speak engineering language, but I also know when to push back and advocate for user needs.

3. **Comfort with ambiguity:** Most of my recent work has been 0→1 product launches where we didn't have a clear template. I'm good at running experiments, validating hypotheses quickly, and adjusting based on what we learn.

4. **Technical credibility:** I can read code, write Python scripts, and understand API design. I spent 1.5 years as a Solutions Engineer at Stripe embedding with developers, which taught me to think in systems and edge cases.

**Where I have gaps:**

I'm not a designer. I've gotten better at design thinking and I collaborate well with design partners, but my instinct is still more "technical product" than "design-first." For a PM/Designer hybrid role, I'd lean heavily on design expertise from teammates while bringing strong user research and systematic product thinking.

**Why now:**

Zo is a great company, but I'm looking for a smaller, earlier-stage team where I can have outsized impact. At 2-5 people, Chair AI is the kind of environment where one PM can meaningfully shape the product. I also want to build something consumer-facing—products that people use in their real lives, not just at work.

I'd love to talk more about how my background in user research, technical products, and iterative shipping could help Chair AI build the future of event coordination. Thanks for considering my application.

Best,
