# V's Hiring Market Crisis Thesis

## Core Claim

The hiring market is entering a **systemic matching crisis** driven by compounding failures in data structures, trust, and technology adoption. This is not cyclical — it's structural.

## The Fundamental Mismatch

**What the system optimizes for:**
- Keywords
- Credentials
- Experience duration
- Hard skills

**What actually predicts job success:**
- Values alignment
- Mindset/adaptability
- Soft skills
- Cultural fit

**Gap = Systemic inefficiency**

## Second-Order Effects (Tradeable Predictions)

### 1. Unemployment Duration Will Increase
- Even in a "recovery," matching takes longer
- More false starts, more churn
- Prediction: Unemployment duration metrics lag recovery

### 2. Layoff-Rehire Cycles Accelerate
- Companies hire wrong → fire → hire again
- Net layoff numbers stay elevated even without macro shock
- Prediction: Tech layoffs persist even if recession avoided

### 3. Productivity Drag
- Mismatched employees = lower output
- Training costs increase (wrong people hired)
- Prediction: GDP growth disappoints despite employment numbers

### 4. Trust Collapse → Gig Economy Growth
- Both sides give up on traditional employment
- Contract/gig work grows as "lower commitment" hedge
- Prediction: W-2 employment share declines

### 5. AI-Washing Amplifies the Problem
- Companies adopt AI hiring tools that optimize broken signals
- Candidates use AI to game those tools
- Arms race with no improvement in match quality
- Prediction: "AI hiring" companies underperform expectations

## Why Markets Might Be Wrong

1. **Markets see layoffs as cyclical** — V sees them as structural
2. **Markets expect AI to improve hiring** — V expects it to worsen matching
3. **Markets look at unemployment rate** — V looks at match quality (hidden)
4. **Markets assume trust is constant** — V sees trust in freefall

## Trading Implications

Look for markets where:
- Consensus assumes "return to normal" after cycle
- Hidden variable is match quality / trust
- Lagging indicators will surprise to the downside
