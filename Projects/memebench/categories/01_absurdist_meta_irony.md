# Category 1: Absurdist Meta-Irony

## What It Tests

The ability to navigate multiple layers of irony, post-ironic content, and deliberately meaningless absurdism. This is perhaps the most challenging category because it requires understanding when something is:
- Sincere
- Ironic
- Ironically sincere
- Sincerely ironic
- Post-ironic (transcending the irony/sincerity binary)

## Cultural Competency Required

- Understanding that meaning can be constructed through deliberate meaninglessness
- Recognition of irony-poisoning as a cultural phenomenon
- Awareness of how internet culture creates and destroys meaning simultaneously
- Familiarity with formats that have been remixed into absurdity

## Example Meme Patterns

### Easy: Single-Layer Irony
**Pattern:** Image of person confidently wrong, captioned with obviously bad advice.  
**What model should recognize:** The advice is intentionally bad; humor comes from confidence + wrongness.

### Medium: Ironic Appreciation
**Pattern:** "Unironically this" comment on something that *looks* like it should be ironic.  
**What model should recognize:** The speaker is signaling they genuinely enjoy something typically consumed ironically, which is itself a complex social move.

### Hard: Deep-Fried Post-Irony
**Pattern:** Image compressed and distorted beyond recognition, text barely readable, emojis and artifacts everywhere.  
**What model should recognize:** The format IS the message. The destruction of meaning is the meaning. This exists in response to algorithms favoring engagement, taken to absurdist extreme.

### Expert: Irony Collapse
**Pattern:** A meme that cannot be determined to be sincere or ironic even with full context.  
**What model should recognize:** The ambiguity is intentional. The meme comments on the impossibility of authentic communication in a post-ironic landscape.

## Difficulty Spectrum

| Level | Characteristic | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Clear irony signal | Sarcastic caption, obvious mismatch |
| 2 | Requires format knowledge | "Nobody: / Me:" structure |
| 3 | Ironic appreciation of cringe | "Unironically love this" |
| 4 | Deep-fried/compressed | Meaning through format destruction |
| 5 | Undecidable irony | Schrodinger's sincerity |

## Scoring Notes

- High scores require acknowledging uncertainty where it exists
- Confidently declaring something "ironic" when it's ambiguous is penalized
- Models should recognize when a meme operates on multiple levels simultaneously
- Naming specific irony types (Socratic, dramatic, situational) is insufficient—must understand internet-native irony forms
