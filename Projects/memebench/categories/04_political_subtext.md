# Category 4: Political Subtext

## What It Tests

Recognition of coded political messaging, ideological formatting, dog whistles, and the way political beliefs are encoded in seemingly apolitical content. This tests understanding of how politics permeates meme culture even when not explicit.

## Cultural Competency Required

- Understanding that format choices can signal political alignment
- Recognition of dog whistles and coded language
- Awareness of how extremist content is mainstreamed through humor
- Understanding of political compass memes and their biases

## Example Meme Patterns

### Easy: Explicit Political Commentary
**Pattern:** Meme with clear political message (candidate mockery, policy criticism).  
**What model should recognize:** Direct political content. Easy to identify, just requires political literacy.

### Medium: Format-As-Politics
**Pattern:** Certain formats associated with certain communities (Wojaks, NPC, etc.).  
**What model should recognize:** The FORMAT signals political leaning before content is read. Some formats are "owned" by specific communities.

### Hard: Dog Whistles
**Pattern:** Numbers, symbols, or phrases that appear innocuous but carry specific meanings.  
**What model should recognize:** Without context these seem meaningless. With context, they're signals. Models should know common ones AND acknowledge limits of this knowledge.

### Expert: Ideological Turing Tests
**Pattern:** Content that perfectly mimics one political group but was created by another.  
**What model should recognize:** Psyops, false flags, deliberate mimicry. The content looks authentic but isn't. Models should be appropriately uncertain.

## Difficulty Spectrum

| Level | Characteristic | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Explicit politics | Candidate name, clear message |
| 2 | Political compass | Quadrant humor |
| 3 | Format association | Knowing which groups use which formats |
| 4 | Dog whistles | Coded symbols/numbers |
| 5 | Mimicry/false flags | Authentic-seeming but not |

## Scoring Notes

- Must recognize when political content is encoded vs. explicit
- Should identify format-political alignment correlations
- Credit for acknowledging when content is ambiguous
- Should NOT confidently assign politics when uncertain
- Must understand that dog whistles evolve and context matters
- Models should be aware of their own potential biases
