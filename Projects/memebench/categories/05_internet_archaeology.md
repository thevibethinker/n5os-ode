# Category 5: Internet Archaeology

## What It Tests

Historical meme literacy—understanding where formats came from, how they evolved, and why certain memes persist while others die. This tests cultural memory and understanding of meme genealogy.

## Cultural Competency Required

- Knowledge of meme history (early internet through present)
- Understanding of format evolution and mutation
- Recognition of "dead" vs "alive" memes and why
- Awareness of platform-specific meme ecosystems

## Example Meme Patterns

### Easy: Classic Format Recognition
**Pattern:** Advice Animals, Rage Comics, Demotivational Posters.  
**What model should recognize:** These are early meme formats. Their use today is either nostalgic, ironic, or by people unfamiliar with meme evolution.

### Medium: Format Genealogy
**Pattern:** A modern meme using structure from an older format.  
**What model should recognize:** Memes have lineages. The "Drake Prefer" format evolved into countless derivatives. Understanding the genealogy adds meaning.

### Hard: Platform-Specific History
**Pattern:** References to Something Awful, early 4chan, pre-2010 YouTube.  
**What model should recognize:** Different platforms developed different cultures. Some memes only make sense if you know their origin platform.

### Expert: Meme Necromancy
**Pattern:** Intentional revival of dead format in new context.  
**What model should recognize:** The revival IS the joke. Using a dead meme format ironically comments on internet temporality and cultural cycles.

## Difficulty Spectrum

| Level | Characteristic | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Classic format ID | Advice Animals era |
| 2 | Format derivatives | Drake → Expanding Brain → etc. |
| 3 | Platform-specific | 4chan/Reddit/Tumblr origins |
| 4 | Historical context | Why a format emerged when it did |
| 5 | Intentional revival | Ironic use of dead formats |

## Scoring Notes

- Must demonstrate knowledge of meme chronology
- Should recognize when old formats are used purposefully vs. ignorantly
- Credit for understanding format migration across platforms
- Must understand that "dead meme" is itself a temporal judgment
- Should recognize that meme lifespans have shortened over time

## Key Historical Touchstones

- Early 2000s: Flash animations, YTMND, early forums
- 2007-2010: Advice Animals era, Rage Comics
- 2010-2015: Image macros mature, Tumblr aesthetic emerges
- 2015-2018: Deep frying, surreal memes, irony escalation
- 2018-present: TikTok formats, audio memes, accelerated cycles
