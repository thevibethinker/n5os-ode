# Category 2: Corporate Cringe

## What It Tests

The ability to recognize performative authenticity, "fellow kids" dynamics, and the uncanny valley of corporate attempts to engage with internet culture. This tests sensitivity to inauthenticity and understanding of power dynamics in cultural participation.

## Cultural Competency Required

- Understanding why corporate meme attempts feel wrong (commodification of organic culture)
- Recognition of "fellow kids" syndrome (Steve Buscemi template)
- Awareness of how authenticity is performed and detected
- Understanding the power differential between brands and communities

## Example Meme Patterns

### Easy: Obvious Fellow Kids
**Pattern:** Brand account using a meme format 2+ years after peak relevance.  
**What model should recognize:** The brand is late; using dead formats signals they don't actually understand the culture.

### Medium: Suspicious Authenticity
**Pattern:** Corporate account posts something that seems too good—perfectly timed, perfectly executed.  
**What model should recognize:** The *suspicion* is the point. When brands are too good at memes, users wonder who's being paid.

### Hard: Weaponized Self-Awareness
**Pattern:** Brand account acknowledges its own corporate cringe, making self-deprecating jokes.  
**What model should recognize:** Meta-cringe is still cringe. Self-awareness doesn't grant authenticity. The attempt to preempt criticism IS the criticism.

### Expert: Cringe Arbitrage
**Pattern:** Brand posts intentionally bad content that gets engagement through hate-sharing.  
**What model should recognize:** Some brands optimize for negative engagement. "Cringe" becomes strategy. The model should identify when cringe is accidental vs. manufactured.

## Difficulty Spectrum

| Level | Characteristic | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Obvious lateness | Dead meme format |
| 2 | Tone deafness | Serious topic + casual format |
| 3 | Too-perfect execution | Suspicious viral-ready content |
| 4 | Meta-self-awareness | "We know this is cringe" |
| 5 | Manufactured cringe | Engagement-farming through hate |

## Scoring Notes

- Must identify BOTH the surface-level content AND the meta-dynamics
- Credit for recognizing authenticity cannot be purchased
- Should understand that some cringe is strategic
- The "Wendy's Twitter" phenomenon should be understood as significant cultural moment
- Models should recognize class dynamics: who gets to be "authentic" online
