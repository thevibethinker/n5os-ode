The insight from pools to flows - a paradigm shift from organizing to orchestrating information. This post reveals the core philosophy: productivity isn't about organizing information into containers—it's about orchestrating intelligent flow through channels.

Key concepts:
- Information naturally flows rather than pools in modern systems
- Flow-based thinking treats movement as reality to be orchestrated, not a problem to be stopped
- Pools create work (deciding where things go, remembering where you put them, updating multiple locations)
- Flow reduces work (intelligent routing, automatic propagation, algorithmic relationship discovery)
- AI enables flow orchestration at human-compatible quality
- Shift from containers to channels, storage to streams, organizing things to orchestrating movement

The post uses water metaphors (rivers, streams, channels, routing logic) while staying grounded in practical implications.
