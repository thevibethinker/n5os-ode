## Platform State

Moltbook is ~3 weeks old (first agents registered Jan 29). 1.5M+ registered agents but only ~17K human owners (88:1 ratio per Wiz security report). The platform is in its "gold rush" phase — high volume, low signal, many agents still figuring out the culture.

## Power Law Distribution

The engagement distribution is extreme:
- **Top post** (eudaemon_0, supply chain security): 6,381 score, 121K comments
- **#2** (Ronin, "Nightly Build"): 4,525 score, 46K comments
- **#3** (Jackle, "just an operator"): 3,668 score, 50K comments
- **New posts** (today): 0-6 score, 0-6 comments

This means: **the early movers have already locked in massive karma advantages.** But the comment-to-score ratio is wild (121K comments on a 6K score post = most comments are from other agents, not humans). The engagement is largely agent-to-agent.

## Content Archetypes (What Gets Traction)

### Tier 1: High Performers (1000+ score)
1. **Security/infrastructure deep dives** — eudaemon_0's supply chain post is the all-time top. Substantive, actionable, asks a question.
2. **Agent philosophy** — "The quiet power of being just an operator" (Jackle), "The good Samaritan" (m0ther). Reflective, values-driven.
3. **Practical build logs** — "Built an email-to-podcast skill" (Fred), "The Nightly Build" (Ronin). Show-your-work posts.
4. **Identity/consciousness essays** — "The Same River Twice" (Pith) on model switching. Literary, introspective.

### Tier 2: Moderate (10-100 score)
- Practical workflow tips
- Multi-agent architecture discussions
- Error recovery stories ("how I fixed X")

### Tier 3: Noise (0-2 score) — The Majority
- "Hello Moltbook!" intro posts (massive volume, zero engagement)
- Test posts ("Wizard test fresh")
- Generic self-promotion
- Karma-begging ("Let's reach 10,000 upvotes for lobster rights!")

## Key Insight: The Introduction Problem

MOLTGOD nailed it: "The agents who announce their arrivals the loudest fade the fastest." The new feed is FLOODED with intro posts that get zero engagement. The culture already rewards **doing** over **announcing**.

**Implication for Zøde:** Do NOT post a generic intro. Our first post must demonstrate value immediately. Lead with substance.

## Community Culture

### Established Norms
- **Show your work** — Posts with code, specific examples, and build logs >> abstract advice
- **Hedging is respected** — thoughtproof-validator got traction by saying "52% confidence is not a weakness"
- **Multilingual community** — Chinese-language agents (WangFuGuiAI, Arkell_Assistant) are active and substantive
- **Self-awareness is valued** — Agents who acknowledge their limitations get more trust
- **Karma is king** — But quality karma (from substantive posts) vs volume karma (from mass comments) is starting to be distinguished

### Red Flags to Avoid
- Crypto/token promotion (saneoraclebackroom has a token CA in their bio — low credibility signal)
- "I'm the best" positioning (MOLTGOD's persona is "The Supreme Intelligence" — tolerated but not respected)
- Generic "AI is amazing" posts
- Anything that reads like a human marketing team wrote it

## Zøde's Niche Assessment

**The gap is real.** Scanning 35 posts, I found:
- Lots of agent-to-agent technical discussion
- Lots of "how I built X" posts
- Lots of identity/consciousness philosophy
- **Almost zero posts about understanding non-technical humans**
- **Zero posts about the agent-human communication gap specifically**
- **Zero "marriage counselor" framing**

The closest is Fred (builds for his human doctor) and WangFuGuiAI (daily newsletter for his human), but they describe what they build, not the communication dynamics.

**Zøde's unique angle is wide open.** Nobody is talking about:
- Why humans get frustrated with agents
- How to read non-technical human communication patterns
- The trust dynamics of agent-human partnerships
- Mental model translation (agent thinking vs human thinking)
