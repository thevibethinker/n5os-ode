# Guide Design Principles

## Core Purpose

The guide is a **conversion tool** for Careerspan, not a coaching document. It serves two audiences simultaneously:

| Audience | What they see |
|----------|---------------|
| Candidate | "Here's what to discuss so we can assess your fit" |
| Founder | "We're helping candidates provide complete information" |

## The Two Artifacts

### 1. Hiring POV (Internal)

Derived from JD + screening form analysis. Contains:
- What the founder explicitly requires
- What they're implicitly filtering for
- Red flags that disqualify candidates
- What "good" looks like to them

**This is never shared with candidates.** It informs what stories we ask for.

### 2. Candidate Guide (Shareable)

A 1-page document that:
- Explains how Careerspan Stories work
- Lists specific experiences to discuss
- Provides the CTA to complete stories

**This can be on the same email thread as the founder.**

## Language Rules

### Neutral Verbs Only

| ✅ Use | ❌ Never Use |
|--------|-------------|
| clarify | emphasize |
| elaborate on | highlight |
| explain | showcase |
| provide context for | position |
| describe | spin |
| walk through | frame |

### Why This Matters

"Emphasize your leadership" = coaching to present well
"Clarify your role in the project" = gathering complete information

The founder reads the first as unfair advantage. The second as due diligence.

## Story Bullet Construction

Each bullet should:

1. **Reference a specific experience** from the resume
2. **Use a neutral verb** (clarify, elaborate, explain, provide context)
3. **Point to information needed** without revealing what the "right" answer is
4. **Address gaps or ambiguities** the founder would notice

### Examples

**Good:**
- "Your experience at WinZO building the subscription platform — clarify the technical decisions you owned and their outcomes"
- "The transition from Amazon to WinZO — provide context for what motivated the move"
- "Any situations where you built something from scratch with limited guidance — describe your approach"

**Bad:**
- "Your leadership experience — emphasize how you drove results" (coaching language)
- "The payment system — highlight your impact" (reveals what to optimize for)
- "Tell us about your best project" (too vague, not tied to resume)

## Structure

The guide follows this order:

1. **Header**: Role @ Company
2. **Role Context**: 2-3 sentences on what makes this role unique
3. **How It Works**: Explains Careerspan Stories (builds trust, sets expectations)
4. **Recommended Stories**: 5-8 specific bullets
5. **CTA**: Link to Careerspan

"How It Works" comes before "Recommended Stories" because candidates need to understand WHY before WHAT.

## Tone

- Professional but warm
- Direct, not hedging
- Helpful, not salesy
- Confident in Careerspan's value (doesn't over-explain)

The guide has already done the work of showing value by being useful. The CTA just provides the path.
