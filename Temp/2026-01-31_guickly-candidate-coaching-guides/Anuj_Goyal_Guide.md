# Careerspan Coaching Guide: Anuj Goyal
**For: Guickly — Founding Engineer Role**

---

## Your Narrative Arc

You're a **systems builder who owns critical infrastructure end-to-end**. At WinZO, you're not maintaining—you're architecting payment systems that handle 400K RPM. You went from Amazon (big company execution) to a high-growth gaming startup where you're building foundational systems from scratch. That trajectory is exactly what Guickly wants.

---

## What to Emphasize with Your Careerspan Coach

### 1. **The Payment Switcher & Fraud System (WinZO)**
This is your flagship story. You built an in-house system integrating 10+ payment gateways. Dig into:
- Why you chose to build in-house vs. buy
- The architectural decisions you made and why
- How you handled edge cases, failures, retries
- The fraud detection system—real-time checks, 70% reduction in fraud

*Why it matters*: Guickly is building infrastructure that "automates the AI workflow." They need someone who's built complex, multi-integration systems from scratch.

### 2. **Asynchronous Systems at Scale**
Your Kafka-based withdrawal system (12% adoption lift) and Redis-based expiry system (32M jobs) show you understand async, distributed work. Explore:
- Why async? What problems did it solve?
- How you handled failures, retries, consistency
- The consistent hashing decision for load balancing

*Why it matters*: ML pipelines are fundamentally async—data curation, training jobs, deployments. Your mental model translates directly.

### 3. **Ambiguity → Shipping**
Talk about times when you had a problem but no spec. The hexagonal architecture redesign—who told you to do that? Did you identify the problem and propose the solution? How did you convince the team?

*Why it matters*: Guickly's JD literally says "There is no Spec Doc. There is a problem and there is you."

### 4. **Your Malware Detection Project (IIT)**
Don't bury this. You built a deep learning model for malware classification with 90% accuracy. This is your only ML experience on paper—bring it forward:
- What was the hardest part? (Likely: data quality, labeling, iteration)
- What did you learn about the ML development lifecycle?

*Why it matters*: Guickly is automating the ML development workflow. Showing you understand the pain points (even from a school project) demonstrates domain awareness.
