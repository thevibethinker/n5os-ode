## What to Emphasize with Your Careerspan Coach

### 1. **The Policy System at VMware (Built from Scratch)**
This is your flagship story. You "designed a rule-evaluation Policy System from scratch to enforce allocation logic." Unpack this:
- What problem were you solving? (Resource allocation conflicts? Manual approvals?)
- Why build from scratch vs. use existing tools?
- How does rule evaluation work? What's the architecture?
- How did you handle edge cases, conflicts, overrides?

*Why it matters*: Guickly needs people who can build foundational systems without a spec. This proves you can.

### 2. **H2O Resource Optimization System (VMware)**
"Built internal H2O systems for optimising resource allocation across VMware teams." This sounds like internal infrastructure:
- What's H2O? (Explain like the interviewer doesn't know)
- What was the optimization problem?
- What was the impact? (Cost savings? Efficiency gains?)

*Why it matters*: Internal tooling that optimizes workflows = Guickly's entire product thesis. Even if it's not ML, the pattern is identical.

### 3. **The Ledger Subsystem (VMware)**
You "managed the Ledger subsystem ensuring consistency of resource requests and states." Ledgers = state management at scale:
- What consistency guarantees did you need?
- How did you handle conflicts, failures, retries?
- What was the hardest bug you fixed?

*Why it matters*: ML training workflows have state (data versions, model versions, training runs). Consistency is hard. You've done hard.

### 4. **Speed of Shipping (FalconX)**
At FalconX, you've shipped multiple features in ~7 months: loan snapshots, interest compounding, RBAC. That's velocity. Explore:
- How do you move fast without breaking things?
- What's your process for scoping, building, shipping?
- Any examples of shipping under pressure?

*Why it matters*: The founder wants "high intensity, high support." Your FalconX pace suggests you can operate in that mode.
