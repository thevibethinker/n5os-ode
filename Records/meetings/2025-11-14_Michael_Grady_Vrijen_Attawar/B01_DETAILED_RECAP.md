## Strategic Context

This meeting served as a soft product advocacy conversation rather than a hard close. Key themes:

**Information Architecture Philosophy:** Vrijen positioned Zo not as "just another app" but as a "third brain" system—a digital mirror for filtering and processing information. Described as fractional distillation: raw data (meetings, reflections) flows through semantic layers until reaching "sacred texts" (worldview, beliefs).

**AI as Exoskeleton, Not Robot:** Critical positioning: AI doesn't break meritocratic structures because quality output depends on quality inputs and prompting. This resonated deeply with Michael's existing thinking about post-processing and AI's role in augmenting (not replacing) human intelligence.

**Data Flow & Pattern Matching:** Michael appreciated the vision of semantic data movement between knowledge buckets combined with AI's pattern-matching capability. Discussions touched on RAG (retrieval-augmented generation), semantic search, and the challenge of verbatim retrieval vs. lossy compression for information retrieval.

**Self-Healing Systems:** Vrijen advocated for AI systems as "both guardrail and weighted jacket"—preventing deviation while enhancing thinking quality, not degrading it.

**Signal Over Noise:** Agreement that most meeting summaries are noise; value is in winnowing to identify gems. This applies to reflections and all captured data.
