# B31_STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

**Perspective:** Speaking as a marketing professional with technical fluency exploring AI tools and knowledge systems

## Key Landscape Insights

1. **Observation:** Technical marketers see RAG/VKG as critical infrastructure, not nice-to-have  
   **Implication:** Knowledge system market differentiating on semantic capabilities, not just storage  
   **Strategic Value:** Position Zo's RAG/semantic layer as core differentiator; technical buyers will evaluate this deeply

2. **Observation:** Experienced professionals frustrated with "uncertainty expression" problem—hunches that don't translate to articulation  
   **Implication:** Second-order problem: not just capturing info, but capturing *early-stage thinking* before it's fully formed  
   **Strategic Value:** Most tools focus on output organization; opportunity in capturing and scaffolding ideation phase

3. **Observation:** Meritocratic thinking pervades high-performer worldview; concerned AI might flatten differentiation  
   **Implication:** Community values specialization and quality inputs as essential (not commoditized)  
   **Strategic Value:** Anti-commoditization messaging will resonate; emphasize "you have to imbue it with your perspective"

4. **Observation:** Post-processing and self-critique frameworks become primary value seeking in 2025  
   **Implication:** Shift from "capture everything" to "surface what matters" as competitive battleground  
   **Strategic Value:** Meeting summaries, reflections, journaling—all need winnowing/filtration layer (B## blocks are example of this)

5. **Observation:** Boston tech community actively networking and exploring at Venture Cafe, CIC spaces—cross-pollination of ideas  
   **Implication:** Community-driven discovery; word-of-mouth and personal demos drive adoption  
   **Strategic Value:** Michael is potential advocate; his feedback on product experience will shape local community perception
