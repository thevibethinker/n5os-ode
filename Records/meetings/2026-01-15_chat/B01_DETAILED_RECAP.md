## Strategic Context

The conversation pivoted away from retention metrics and toward giving Zo a data moat. Everyone agreed that retention won’t come from a cleaner landing page; it comes from exclusive, high-value data streams that users can only get inside Zo. That’s why the focus landed on partners with APIs and subscription models that are itching for consumer distribution (Aviato launching a consumer tier, Nine already pinging Ben, Calendly repositioning toward relationship management, Condo giving Zo rare LinkedIn access). The goal is to package those integrations as pre-built "recipes" (CRM stack, due diligence prep, automated booking, LinkedIn inbox intelligence, trading/news bots) so someone can say, “I just subscribed, and Zo pulls in Aviato, Calendly, and Condo data with one click.”

The team also discussed how these partners unlock fresh revenue streams: the data companies already want new customers and will co-market if Zo demonstrates the user stories (new customers from CRM automation, trading signals, job-seeker pipelines). Tiffany’s list of fintech/credit/banking APIs keeps the compass pointed at measurable cash outcomes, while Vrijen’s YC and New York founder connections aim to turn introductions into generous terms and audience pools. Research-wise, the team will surface human stories from Discord and the Church of Zoe to validate that money-making people already use data inside Zo.

Onboarding surfaced as a second-order lever. Every new user lands on a blank slate, so Tiffany flagged promoting curated prompts, and Vrijen shared a felt idea: treat Zo like a desktop that morphs into a Zone, or at least ship a few familiar folders so users immediately recognize the mental model. Ben is already aware of the problem, but the team agreed the default files experience could do more to establish context.
