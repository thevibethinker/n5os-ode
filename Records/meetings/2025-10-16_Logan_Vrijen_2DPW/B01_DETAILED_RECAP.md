## Key Decisions and Agreements

- **Company Hibernation Timeline**: January 1, 2025 (or "end of year") set as deliberate reset point. No longer building continuously until then; focused shift to skeleton crew operations.
  - Why it matters: Provides a hard stop to exhaustion cycle and forces clarity on what's actually essential vs. nice-to-have.
  - Both parties agree this is necessary and overdue.

- **Skeleton Crew Product Definition**: Committed to building a product that two people (Vrijen & Logan alone, worst case) can maintain and operate.
  - Why it matters: Removes dependency on "Ilsa and the team" continuing; creates sustainable business model; forces ruthless prioritization.
  - This is the new North Star for product decisions through year-end.

- **Payroll Through End of Year**: Team members will be paid through December 31, 2025. After that, the model changes.
  - Why it matters: Removes moral hazard of "let's figure it out later." Employees know their runway; gives clarity for both company and people.

- **Post-January Paths**: Three explicit scenarios identified for after hibernation period:
  1. **Sale Option**: If product reaches saleable state and buyer appears, pursue sale. ("We've had selling interests" - legitimately, from future conversations)
  2. **Profitability Play**: Vrijen funds company continuation; agreement on equity/residuals for team members who stay; focus on smaller, sustainable revenue
  3. **Product Death**: If no traction, company goes to zero; team members eat their losses but leave with equity intact (no dilution punishment)

## Strategic Context

**The Exhaustion Problem**: Both Vrijen and Logan acknowledge they're "deeply tired." The learning curve for Careerspan has been brutal—three years of iterating through product-market fit, pivoting multiple times, dealing with edge cases and ambiguity.

**The Paradox**: Now that they're knowledgeable and the product is clearer, they're too exhausted to execute. This isn't laziness; it's real burnout from learning-by-failing at scale.

**The Reframe**: Getting to January 1 hibernation point creates permission structure for both founders to:
- Stop maintaining everything
- Take a genuine break (6 months implicit in their language)
- Return with fresh energy for whatever comes next (new initiative, sale process, profitability push)

**Product Philosophy Shift**: Rather than "features for everyone," the skeleton crew approach forces definition of the essential product. This paradoxically may be MORE defensible and saleable than the current all-things-to-all-people approach.

**Positioning Insight**: Logan notes they started saying "we're career coaches" and are ending saying it with "full confidence." The product journey validated the core coaching/scaffolding approach—they just did it in the hardest way possible (as a platform, with many edge cases).
