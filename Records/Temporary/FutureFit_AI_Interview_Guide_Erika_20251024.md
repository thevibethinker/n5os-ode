## Interview Structure (45 min)

### 1. Intro & Alignment (5 min)
- Thank candidate, explain role overview
- Set agenda: background dive (15m) → skills/scenarios (20m) → Q&A (5m)

### 2. Background & Motivation (15 min)

**Opening:**
> "Walk me through your career journey from The Data Incubator to Nexford. What drew you to partnerships, and what's prompting you to explore a Growth Manager role now?"

**Follow-ups:**
- What does "Head of Partnerships" mean at Nexford day-to-day?
- How do you split time between strategic partnership development vs. execution/operations?
- What's your ideal next role? What are you optimizing for (scope, comp, learning, industry)?

**Red Flags to Listen For:**
- Looking for director/leadership scope (role is IC)
- Unclear on demand gen vs. partnerships distinction
- Comp expectations above $125k base

---

### 3. Demand Gen & Campaign Execution (20 min)

#### A. Multi-Channel Campaigns (7 min)

**Question 1:**
> "Tell me about a multi-channel campaign you've run to drive qualified pipeline. Walk me through the channels you used, how you tracked performance, and what the outcomes were."

**Looking For:**
- ✅ Owned end-to-end campaign (not just partner-facing)
- ✅ Used multiple channels (email + webinar + paid/organic + content)
- ✅ Tracked MQLs, SQLs, pipeline influenced, conversion rates
- ✅ Iterated based on data (A/B tests, segment optimization)
- ⚠️ Partnership campaigns only (not demand gen)
- ⚠️ Vague on metrics or attribution

**Probing Questions:**
- "What was your MQL → SQL conversion rate, and how did you improve it?"
- "Which channels drove the highest quality pipeline? How did you measure quality?"
- "What budget did you manage, and how did you allocate across channels?"

---

#### B. Event Marketing Execution (7 min)

**Question 2:**
> "Your resume mentions presenting at NAWDP Conference and EdTech Week. Beyond speaking, have you executed event marketing from logistics to lead gen? Walk me through an example."

**Looking For:**
- ✅ Owned logistics: venue, registration, promotion, speaker coord
- ✅ Set attendance targets and tracked actuals
- ✅ Built pre-event nurture sequences and post-event follow-up
- ✅ Measured pipeline/revenue influenced by event
- ⚠️ Only presented/spoke (didn't execute)
- ⚠️ No post-event conversion metrics

**Probing Questions:**
- "How did you drive registrations? What channels worked best?"
- "What was your post-event follow-up process, and what conversion rate did you see?"
- "Have you run webinars at scale (50+ attendees)? What's your playbook?"

---

#### C. Tools & Analytics (6 min)

**Question 3:**
> "You mentioned using HubSpot for segmented email sequences. What other growth/marketing tools have you used, and how did you use data to optimize campaigns?"

**Looking For:**
- ✅ HubSpot proficiency (workflows, attribution, reporting)
- ✅ Used Clay, Customer.io, Marketo, or similar MA platforms
- ✅ Built dashboards, tracked funnel metrics, reported to leadership
- ✅ Ran A/B tests, segmentation experiments
- ⚠️ Basic HubSpot use (just sending emails)
- ⚠️ No MA platform experience beyond HubSpot

**Probing Questions:**
- "What metrics do you track daily/weekly/monthly?"
- "Describe a time you used data to pivot a campaign that wasn't working."
- "How do you think about attribution in a multi-touch environment?"

---

### 4. Collaboration & Execution (Rapid Fire, 5 min)

**Question 4:**
> "This role requires tight collaboration with RevOps, Product, and Sales. Give me an example of a time you partnered with another function to solve a GTM problem."

**Looking For:**
- ✅ Specific cross-functional project with measurable impact
- ✅ Navigated conflicting priorities or constraints
- ✅ Bias for action, autonomy, startup-style scrappiness

---

**Question 5:**
> "Tell me about a time you had to move fast with incomplete information. What was the outcome?"

**Looking For:**
- ✅ Comfort with ambiguity and rapid iteration
- ✅ Experiment mindset (test, learn, scale)
- ⚠️ Needs high structure or complete data before acting
