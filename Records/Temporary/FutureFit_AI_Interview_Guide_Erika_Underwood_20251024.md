## Pre-Interview Prep (5 min)

### Candidate Strengths (Validate & Explore)
- ✅ 9+ years B2B SaaS EdTech partnerships
- ✅ HubSpot CRM proficiency + email campaigns
- ✅ GTM/RevOps collaboration experience
- ✅ Data-driven approach (segmentation, A/B testing)
- ✅ Event execution (webinars, conferences)
- ✅ Strong writing (collateral, campaigns)

### Gaps to Probe
- ⚠️ **Partnerships-heavy vs. pure demand gen** — Does she own pipeline metrics? Or just partner-sourced leads?
- ⚠️ **Seniority/comp expectations** — Head of Partnerships → IC Growth Manager (possible title/comp mismatch)
- ⚠️ **Multi-channel campaigns** — Beyond email and events, what channels has she run? (Paid, organic, content, etc.)
- ⚠️ **Tool stack depth** — HubSpot is confirmed, but what about Customer.io, Clay, Zapier, etc.?

### Red Flags to Watch For
- Vague metrics ("increased engagement" without numbers)
- Overemphasis on partnerships vs. direct demand gen
- Unfamiliarity with startup pace/scrappiness
- Comp expectations >$125k
- Lack of ownership over pipeline conversion metrics

## Interview Structure (45 min)

### 1. Intro & Context (5 min)
**Your goal:** Build rapport, set expectations, give FutureFit AI overview

**Script:**
> "Thanks for joining us, Erika. We're excited to learn more about your background. Before we dive in, let me give you a quick overview of FutureFit AI and this role.
>
> We're an AI-powered workforce development platform helping companies upskill and reskill talent. We're in growth mode and need someone to own demand gen—running multi-channel campaigns, event marketing, and collaborating closely with RevOps to hit pipeline targets.
>
> This is a hands-on IC role where you'll build campaigns from scratch, test and iterate, and move fast. Does that align with what you're looking for?"

**Listen for:** Enthusiasm for IC execution vs. management, comfort with ambiguity, interest in EdTech/AI space

---

### 2. Demand Gen & Pipeline Ownership (15 min)

#### Q1: Walk me through a demand gen campaign you owned end-to-end—from planning to pipeline results.
**What to listen for:**
- Clear ownership (not just "supported" or "collaborated")
- Specific metrics (MQLs, SQLs, pipeline $, conversion rates)
- Multi-channel approach (not just one tactic)
- Experimentation mindset (A/B tests, iteration)
- Cross-functional collaboration (sales, RevOps, product)

**Follow-ups:**
- "What were your target metrics, and did you hit them?"
- "What channels performed best, and how did you optimize underperformers?"
- "How did you measure attribution and impact on closed-won revenue?"

**🚩 Red flag:** Can't cite specific metrics or overly credits "team" without clear personal contribution

**✅ Green flag:** Owns numbers, shows experimentation, ties to revenue

---

#### Q2: Tell me about your experience with event marketing—webinars, conferences, or roadshows. What was your role, and what were the results?
**What to listen for:**
- Event strategy (why this event? target audience?)
- Execution details (logistics, promotion, follow-up)
- Lead quality metrics (not just attendance numbers)
- Post-event nurture (what happened to leads?)

**Follow-ups:**
- "How did you promote the event? What channels drove the most registrations?"
- "What was your follow-up process, and how many leads converted to opportunities?"
- "If you could redo one event, what would you change?"

**🚩 Red flag:** Only talks about logistics, no mention of lead quality or conversion

**✅ Green flag:** Clear ROI focus, closed-loop reporting, strong follow-up process

---

#### Q3: Your background is partnerships-heavy. How do you think about partnerships vs. direct demand gen, and how would you balance them in this role?
**What to listen for:**
- Clear understanding that this role is demand gen first
- Ability to translate partnership skills to direct campaigns
- Not overly reliant on partners for pipeline
- Willingness to learn/shift if needed

**Follow-ups:**
- "Can you give an example of a campaign you ran that wasn't partner-sourced?"
- "How do you think about building audiences from scratch vs. leveraging partners?"

**🚩 Red flag:** Insists partnerships are "demand gen" or can't articulate direct campaign experience

**✅ Green flag:** Acknowledges difference, shows adaptability, has direct campaign examples

---

### 3. Tools & Execution (10 min)

#### Q4: Walk me through your experience with HubSpot—what features have you used, and how do you structure campaigns?
**What to listen for:**
- Workflow automation (email sequences, lead scoring)
- Segmentation and list management
- A/B testing and analytics
- Integration with sales (handoff process)

**Follow-ups:**
- "Have you used Customer.io, Clay, or similar tools? How do they compare to HubSpot?"
- "How do you think about list hygiene and deliverability?"
- "What's one workflow you built that significantly improved conversion?"

**🚩 Red flag:** Surface-level HubSpot knowledge ("just sent emails")

**✅ Green flag:** Deep platform knowledge, workflow automation expertise, integration thinking

---

#### Q5: This role requires strong writing—campaign copy, landing pages, emails. Can you share an example of something you wrote that performed well?
**What to listen for:**
- Specific example (bonus if they can show you)
- Clear metrics (open rate, CTR, conversion)
- Understanding of audience and messaging
- Iteration based on performance

**Follow-ups:**
- "What was your writing process? How did you test messaging?"
- "How do you balance brand voice with conversion optimization?"

**🚩 Red flag:** Vague examples, no performance data

**✅ Green flag:** Shows work, cites metrics, explains iteration

---

### 4. Collaboration & Fit (10 min)

#### Q6: Tell me about a time you collaborated with RevOps or sales to optimize a funnel or improve conversion rates.
**What to listen for:**
- Data-driven approach (funnel analysis, bottleneck identification)
- Cross-functional collaboration (not siloed)
- Impact on business metrics (pipeline, velocity, close rate)

**Follow-ups:**
- "What tools did you use to track and share data?"
- "How did you get buy-in from sales on changes?"

**🚩 Red flag:** No clear examples or adversarial relationship with sales

**✅ Green flag:** Strong partnership mindset, data fluency, results-driven

---

#### Q7: Why are you interested in moving from Head of Partnerships to an IC Growth Manager role? And what are your comp expectations?
**What to listen for:**
- Genuine interest in demand gen (not just "looking for anything")
- Realistic expectations (this is IC, not leadership)
- Comp alignment ($105–$125k range)

**Follow-ups:**
- "What excites you most about this role vs. your current work?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 2–3 years?"

**🚩 Red flag:** Expects senior title or >$125k, views this as a "step back"

**✅ Green flag:** Excited about craft, comfortable with IC scope, comp aligns

---

### 5. Candidate Questions (5 min)
**Your goal:** Assess thoughtfulness, interest level, culture fit

**Strong signals:**
- Asks about growth targets, pipeline goals, success metrics
- Asks about team structure, collaboration with sales/product
- Asks about tool stack, budget, experimentation philosophy
- Asks about company stage, funding, growth trajectory

**Weak signals:**
- Only asks about benefits, vacation, remote work
- No questions prepared
- Questions show lack of research on FutureFit AI

## Decision Criteria

**Advance if:**
- Clear demand gen ownership (not just partnerships)
- Strong tool proficiency (HubSpot + adaptable)
- Metrics-driven with specific examples
- Comp expectations align
- Cultural fit (scrappy, autonomous, startup-minded)

**Pass if:**
- Can't articulate demand gen metrics or ownership
- Partnerships-only experience, no direct campaigns
- Tool knowledge is surface-level
- Comp expectations >$125k or expects senior title
- Red flags on collaboration or execution

## Next Steps After Interview

**If Strong Yes/Yes:**
1. Send follow-up email within 24 hours (thank you + next steps)
2. Schedule final round with [Founder/CEO/VP Sales]
3. Consider take-home assignment: "Design a 90-day demand gen plan for FutureFit AI"
4. Check 2–3 references (focus on demand gen results, collaboration, execution)

1. Send take-home assignment to stress-test skills
2. Request work samples (campaign briefs, emails, landing pages)
3. Schedule follow-up call to clarify gaps

**If No:**
1. Send rejection email within 48 hours
2. Offer feedback if candidate requests it
3. Keep in touch for future partnership-focused roles

## Appendix: Sample Follow-Up Questions

**If she mentions a specific campaign:**
- "What was the budget, and how did you allocate it?"
- "What surprised you about the results?"
- "If you had 2x the budget, what would you do differently?"

**If she mentions a tool/platform:**
- "What's your favorite feature, and why?"
- "What's a limitation you've hit, and how did you work around it?"
- "If you could build one feature into [tool], what would it be?"

**If she mentions metrics:**
- "How did you track that metric? What tool/dashboard?"
- "What was the baseline before you started?"
- "How did leadership react to those results?"
