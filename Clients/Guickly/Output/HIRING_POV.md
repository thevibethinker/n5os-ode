# Hiring POV

## Explicit Requirements

- Founding Engineer / Autonomous AI Engineer
- Technically elite engineering skills
- Ability to automate end-to-end model development workflow (data curation → training → deployment)
- Willingness to work across time zones (SF/NY customers, engineers in Bay Area/Bangalore)
- In-office work (high-intensity, in-office team)
- Comfort with extreme ambiguity (no spec docs)
- Ability to make architectural decisions that survive for the next decade
- Builder mentality (building from scratch, not maintaining)
- Willingness to solve hard problems as part of hiring process (not just interviews)

## Implicit Filters

- Seniority: Not for 'learning'—this is for leading
- Geographic: Must be SF/Bangalore or willing to relocate to those hubs
- Experience level: Has shipped real ML systems, not toy projects
- Domain expertise: Applied ML/MLOps experience at scale (Google Maps-scale is the bar)
- Risk tolerance: Comfortable with startup volatility and multi-million dollar seed runway doesn't mean job security
- Endurance: Can sustain high-intensity work for extended periods
- Cultural fit: 'Good vibes' = someone who brings energy, not drains it
- Equity mindset: Material equity is meaningful to you—you think like an owner, not an employee

## Trait Signals

- High agency: 'There is a problem and there is you'
- Autonomous: Can operate without specs or direction
- Flexible: Willing to sync when work demands it, not clock-driven
- Builder/architect: Wants to design DNA, not inherit tech debt
- Elite/ambitious: Sees themselves as top 1%, wants asymmetric upside
- Merit-driven: Believes in recognition based on contribution, not tenure
- Collaborative yet intense: Wants to build 'in the trenches together' but at high velocity
- Visionary: Buys into 'AI that builds AI' infrastructure narrative
- Founder-mentality: Treats the role as partnership, not employment

## Red Flags

- Needs structure/specs to function
- Works only 9-5 or refuses non-standard hours
- Wants remote-only arrangement (they're explicitly in-office)
- Positioned as 'eager to learn' rather than 'ready to lead'
- Asks about ladder/promotions ('There is no ladder here')
- Cares more about salary than equity
- Can't handle ambiguity or needs clear roles
- Low energy or 'chill' personality clashes with 'high intensity' culture
- Complains about workload or chaos in past roles
- Views this as just 'a job' rather than a 'bet'

## Validation Questions

- Can you operate with zero specification and ship quickly?
- Do you have the technical depth to make architectural decisions with 10-year consequences?
- Are you comfortable working in a high-intensity, in-office environment across time zones?
- Do you haveApplied ML/MLOps experience at meaningful scale?
- Are you motivated by asymmetric upside rather than just salary?
- Can you solve hard engineering problems in real-time during the hiring process?
- Do you bring energy rather than drain it from a room?
- Are you willing to be 'in the trenches' with the team through the grind?
- Do you think like an owner rather than an employee?
- Can you thrive without established processes, documentation, or guardrails?

## Form Traps

- Asking 'What are the hours?' signals you're clock-watching
- Asking about work-life balance upfront signals you can't handle intensity
- Asking 'What's the career path?' signals you don't get the founding engineer paradigm
- Listing too many buzzwords without substance flags you as technical cargo cult
- Asking about team size too early signals you're evaluating company stage more than opportunity
- Asking about documentation/processes signals you need structure over speed
- Not having a clear builder narrative/failed startup signals you're risk-averse
- Discussing past roles as 'learning opportunities' rather than 'building things' signals passivity

## Culture Markers

- Merit-first: Recognition based on contribution, not tenure
- Intensity/urgency: 'Yesterday' customer demand pace
- In-person: Value being 'in the trenches together'
- High agency: Problems exist, you solve them
- Elite/ambitious: 'Special forces', 'top 1%' framing
- Founder-mentality: Everyone is a partner, not employee
- Global-first but India-build: Engineering happens in Bangalore, customers in SF
- Anti-bureaucracy: No ladder, no spec docs, no HR rounds
- Energy-focused: 'Good vibes and lots of energy' is a stated value
- Speed: 'We move fast. No 5-round HR interviews.'

## Story Types Valued

- Architectural ownership: 'I designed and shipped X that's still running'
- Scale impact: 'I built systems processing petabytes/ serving millions'
- Ambiguity navigation: 'We had no spec, so I figured it out'
- Extreme ownership: 'I owned the whole stack from data to deployment'
- Building from zero: 'Started with nothing, shipped in weeks'
- Hard problem solving: 'We had a nightmare edge case and I debugged it'
- Team intensity: 'We crunched for the launch and pulled it off together'
- Founder/leader mindset: 'I wasn't just the engineer, I was the decision-maker'
- Applied AI over theory: 'I didn't just read about it, I shipped it to production'
- Vision alignment: 'I believe in the AI that builds AI mission'
