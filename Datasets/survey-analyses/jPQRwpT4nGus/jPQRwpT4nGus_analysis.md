## Executive Summary

This pre-event survey reveals an **intermediate-level audience stuck in the "competent but not confident" zone**. Key insights:

- **Near-universal AI adoption**: 90% use chatbots, 86% use AI search — this is NOT a beginner crowd
- **The plateau is real**: 41% say they "can't level up from basics"
- **Decision paralysis dominates**: 35% are "overwhelmed by options" (including advanced users)
- **Positive sentiment with a twist**: 65% feel optimistic/excited, yet the most anxious respondent is the most advanced user
- **Clear content needs**: 65% want either "see advanced possibilities" or "build my own system"

**Strategic implication:** Don't teach "what is ChatGPT." Teach decision frameworks, show mastery depth, and give permission to focus.

## Risk Signals

### HIGH RISK: The "Fundamentals" Mismatch

The session is titled "Fundamentals" but:
- 0 respondents rarely use AI
- Only 2 use it "occasionally"
- 3 consider themselves "advanced"
- 35% use agentic AI or coding tools

**If the session covers basic introductions, this crowd will disengage.** "Fundamentals" needs to mean "foundational principles for building with AI" — not "introduction to AI."

### MEDIUM RISK: Audience Diversity

The audience spans early-career to VPs, skeptics to enthusiasts, "give me tactics" to "help me build a system." A single linear presentation will miss most of the room at any given moment.

---

## Recommendations

### For the Session

1. **Lead with "what's possible"** — 35% explicitly want advanced demos
2. **Show ONE workflow deeply** vs. 5 shallow demos. Depth beats breadth.
3. **Offer a decision framework** — Help them decide, not just discover
4. **Address the plateau directly** — "You're not behind—you're in the messy middle"
5. **Start with what NOT to learn** — Constraint is a gift for the overwhelmed

### For Follow-Up

1. Content/research automation workflows are the sweet spot
2. Consider a "systems architecture" track for the 31% wanting long-term approaches
3. Give skeptics ROI stories within first 10 minutes
