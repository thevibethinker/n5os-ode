## Recommendations for Session Content

### Immediate Actions

1. **Address "Overwhelmed by Options"**: Provide a taxonomy/mental model for categorizing AI tools. Help them build a framework rather than add more options.

2. **Assume Everyone Feels "At the Surface"**: Skip the "you're not alone in feeling this way" reassurance. Instead, frame the session as "how to scratch more effectively."

3. **Focus on Systems Thinking**: 66.7% want to "see what's possible at advanced level" or "build their own system." The session should move beyond individual tools to workflow orchestration.

4. **Show Agentic Patterns**: Respondents are already using Agentic AI (Zo.computer, Claude Code, etc.). The audience is ready for multi-agent workflows.

5. **Include Domain Examples**: The excluded researcher's response suggests valuable AI-in-science workflows exist. Include examples from research, design, and marketing to broaden applicability.

### Content Structure Suggestion

```
1. Welcome & Reality Check (5 min)
   - "You're not behind—the surface keeps expanding"
   - Normalizing the exploration phase

2. Building Your Mental Model (20 min)
   - Taxonomy for AI tools (not more tools, but categories)
   - Task-centric vs. product-centric thinking

3. From Individual Tools to Systems (25 min)
   - Orchestrating multi-agent workflows
   - The "advanced level" they want to see

4. Domain Deep-Dives (20 min)
   - Case studies: Research, Design, Marketing
   - Identifying automation opportunities in real work

5. Building Your System (15 min)
   - Personal system design exercise
   - How to iterate vs. overhaul
```
