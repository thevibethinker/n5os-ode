### 1. Branded Career Persona System (Similar to MBTI/Enneagram)

**Description:** Create branded "Careerspan Persona" that users can display on LinkedIn About sections, similar to how people share Myers-Briggs or Enneagram types

**Rationale:** Free self-evaluation tools achieve viral growth through identity signaling. If users start putting "Careerspan Persona: Strategic Builder" on LinkedIn, it becomes growth engine AND signals to employers what to look for.

### 2. Magic Link ATS for Hiring Managers (Lightweight Alternative)

**Description:** Generate magic link that hiring managers distribute via LinkedIn, Indeed, etc. Careerspan automatically processes all applicants and surfaces best candidates. Positions as "ATS for your first hire" - simpler than Workday/Greenhouse, better than Notion page.

**Rationale:** Hiring managers at 1-3 person companies don't need enterprise ATS but waste time screening manually. 00-1000 for automated screening + candidate intelligence is 10x faster than DIY. Can authorize spend without procurement.

**Source:** Vrijen (13:56-14:58), refined in discussion with David
- Quote: "Let me generate a magic link on career Span and just trust that career span will surface me good candidates. I mean, we'll give you all of the candidates if you want as well. Right? But effectively by going to folks at that low of a level, we can essentially charge a hundred bucks to be an ats, but an ATS to folks that are at like my first hire that I need to manage."

**Confidence:** High (this is core product strategy Vrijen is actively pursuing, not speculative)

### 3. Anonymous Job Promotion for VCs (Future Pipeline Building)

**Description:** VCs anonymously promote jobs on Careerspan to build talent pipeline for portfolio companies before they have budget/hiring need. Subscription model: VC pays monthly to keep "future opportunities" visible to relevant candidates.

**Rationale:** VCs invest in Series A companies knowing they'll need to scale team immediately post-funding. Having pre-warmed candidate pipeline ready = faster hiring = better portfolio outcomes. VC can use this as value-add for founders ("I already have talent pipeline ready for you").

**Source:** Vrijen (16:42-17:27)
- Quote: "Eventually I would like for comp to move up to companies that know they have hiring in the future, but they don't have budget for it right now or whatever. They just need to cultivate, but they don't need to act, can anonymously promote jobs. And we're actually thinking VCs would fucking love that. Like you're a VC, you're going to raise a fund, you know you're going to invest in series A founders and you know you're going to want them to scale their team raise right after you put the money in."

**Confidence:** Medium (makes strategic sense, David validated enthusiasm, but not yet built or tested)

### 4. Variable Pricing Based on Candidate Volume Processed

**Description:** Instead of flat subscription, charge per candidate processed (e.g.,  per 100 candidates scanned and ranked). Hiring manager sets budget cap. If job gets 1000 applicants, they pay more; if it gets 10, they pay less.

**Rationale:** Aligns incentives (Careerspan only gets paid for value delivered), reduces buyer friction (no risk of paying for service that doesn't deliver), transparent and scalable.

**Source:** Co-created in discussion, Vrijen (25:45-31:15) with David's pricing advice
- Quote: "We will base it off of the number of people we process for you. Right. If we process a thousand candidates, we'll charge you that. If we process 10 candidates we'll do that. And actually that's kind of fucking perfect because that way the incentives are all aligned"

**Confidence:** High (Vrijen cut meeting short to immediately discuss with Logan - "I'm so fucking excited to discuss that with Logan")
