# Detailed Meeting Recap

## Meeting Overview
V presented Zo Computer to a group (including Logan, Gabby, Beth, and Sheila) as a demonstration of a new approach to knowledge management and AI tooling. The session was positioned as exploring systems thinking approaches to AI in work, rather than just another tool showcase.

## Key Discussion Points

### The Problem Space
V articulated a fundamental challenge with current AI tools: the shift from "capture and store" to "filter and synthesize" paradigms. He noted that with 10x more data and information, users are experiencing 10x more overwhelm. This resonates with founders and VCs who report:
- Things slipping through the cracks constantly
- Uncertainty about daily priorities
- Feeling they should achieve a lot but actually achieve little

### Zo Computer Introduction
V demonstrated Zo as an "AI computer" - essentially a cloud-based computing environment that can be prompted. Key differentiators from tools like ChatGPT:

**File System vs. Projects:** Unlike ChatGPT's "project pit" approach, Zo has a proper file system that allows:
- More reliable reference to structured information
- Information that doesn't grow stale
- Better organization and context management

**Always-On Agent Mode:** Zo operates with continuous access to files and intelligent decision-making about which information to retrieve and process.

**Consumer-First Principles:**
- Uses markdown files (open format, not proprietary)
- Automatic Google Drive sync capability (V has this set up)
- No vendor lock-in - users can leave anytime with their data
- Charges at-cost for AI tokens (same rates as Claude/ChatGPT)

### Live Demonstration
V demonstrated creating a LinkedIn post about metacognition and career growth. The system:
1. Intelligently searched through files for relevant terms
2. Drafted and redrafted the post
3. Applied voice transformation based on V's preferences
4. Automatically activated "writer mode" based on programmed preferences

### Setup and Bootstrapping
When asked about setup complexity, V explained two learning paths:

**Tacit to Explicit Knowledge:** The mental work of articulating what you know
**Technical Documentation:** The actual setup process

V has created a "base version" of Zo with quality-of-life upgrades that can be shared and bootstrapped, including:
- An interpreter that activates with commands (even works in Kandom)
- Scripts and workflows pre-built
- Metaprompting capabilities

He recommended starting with building a "metaprompter" as it forces learning prompting basics while creating a useful tool.

### Gabby's Real-Time Test
Gabby tested Zo during the call with a construction AI research project:
- Had notes in Dutch from a CEO conversation
- Used ChatGPT first - got superficial 3-page report with no URLs
- Switched to Zo with V's "deep research" prompt
- Zo indicated it would produce 40-60 pages and take 45 minutes
- Generated comprehensive, professional-looking report with sources
- Output quality was described as "looks like I paid McKinsey 0,000"

However, there were concerns about source validity (some URLs may not be real).

### Workflow Discussion
The group explored when workflows are worth building. V emphasized that Zo makes workflow creation easier because you describe what you want in natural language rather than visual node-based programming (like N8N or Zapier).

Key example: V built a writing system that:
- Captures his voice effectively
- Transforms thoughts to his voice
- Allows editing before submission
- Creates high-quality samples for future refinement
- "Not cheating" because he did the thinking and does the editing

### Pricing and Access
- Membership tiers: 0, 0, 00, or 00
- All token-based (pay for what you use)
- Charges exact same rate as direct Claude/ChatGPT usage
- V offers a referral code for 50% discount + 5 credits
- He earns compute credits (not cash kickbacks) for referrals

### Other Tools Mentioned
- Cora (email tool) - Logan considering switching from Superhuman
- Howie - mentioned as a tool someone liked
- N8N and Zapier - compared as workflow alternatives

## V's Value Proposition for Zo
Best suited for:
- People who love AI and want to try a different thinking approach
- Those who want to build workflows but found N8N/Zapier not suitable
- Users who want close control and customization
- People doing substantive writing or knowledge work

## Open Questions

- File organization best practices in Zo
- Optimal folder structures
- Whether repetitive tasks justify workflow creation time
