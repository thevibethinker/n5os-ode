# B01 Detailed Recap

The meeting involved a tactical discussion on Careerspan's market positioning, specifically focusing on how to pitch to the New York Founders Club and refining the Emory University proposal.

### Key Discussion Areas:

1.  **Founder Networking & Pitching:**
    *   V shared insights from a strategic narrative design workshop. The core takeaway was designing stories backward from the desired outcome (e.g., DMs or groundswell of interest) and delivering them forward through the emotional experience the audience needs.
    *   The group discussed using "scare tactics" (the high cost and agony of a mis-hire) combined with "competitive advantage" (speed and depth of talent).
    *   Logan suggested a "Make Inbound Great Again" joke for the NY Founders Club audience, framing Careerspan as a better filter than any ATS.
    *   They refined the value proposition: "Referral quality signal at inbound scale." This addresses the two things referrals provide: insight/trust and the feeling of not being "screwed over."

2.  **Competitive Advantage over Referrals:**
    *   V argued that relying solely on referrals taps into only ~10% of the talent pool, putting founders at a disadvantage.
    *   The metaphor of "fighting with one hand tied behind your back" was used to describe ignoring inbound talent.

3.  **Emory University Proposal:**
    *   Logan proposed adding "magic links" as a deal sweetener for Emory. This would involve giving away a set value of direct recruiting (e.g., $1,500–$2,250 worth of roles) to alumni.
    *   This serves as a marketing tool for Emory to show they are helping alums while driving Emory-specific jobs to Careerspan.
    *   The timeline was adjusted: Pilot phase kickoff (Dec 15), Alumni Employer phase (Jan 15).
    *   They discussed "White Glove" onboarding for alumni employer accounts.
    *   A debate began regarding whether charging alumni services is the right move, referencing historical insights on charging alums vs. institutions.
