# B32 – Thought Provoking Ideas

## The "Internet of Careers" / Candidate API
What if Careerspan wasn't a destination, but a ubiquitous layer of intelligence that piped candidates into every recruiter's system? The shift from "Job Board" to "Intelligence API" represents a fundamental change in the unit of value (from a 'post' to an 'analysis').

## Stated Motivation vs. Secret Value
The idea that a product can be sold as a "vanity metric" (looking good to portcos) but retained because of its "functional utility" (actual hiring). How many other B2B tools thrive on this dissonance?

## The Smoothing Function of Aggregators
Alex’s insight that aggregation (VCs) solves the "patchy" demand problem of individual nodes (startups). This principle could apply to other "gig" or "irregular" demand markets.

## Quality as an "Underserved Axis"
The realization that current market players (LinkedIn, Getro) have completely ceded the "quality" axis to "volume." Is the market ready for a tool that says "No" to 95% of candidates automatically?

## Founder vs. Employee Risk Tolerance
The internal tension between a founder’s vision (which ignores short-term unit economics for long-term strategic dominance) and an employee’s focus on immediate viability. How does a founder communicate "strategic runway" effectively?
