# B01 – Detailed Recap

## Overview
Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan) met with Alex Caveny for an advisory session. The conversation centered on Careerspan's shift toward targeting VC platform teams as a primary go-to-market (GTM) strategy, the value of evergreen talent pipelines for VC portfolios, and the challenges of differentiating candidate quality in a market accustomed to "trash heaps" of low-signal applications.

## Key Discussion Points

### VC Platform & Talent Strategy
- **GTM Validation:** Vrijen reported high interest from a VC platform person (Alumni Ventures), noting a much clearer signal of interest than previous GTM attempts.
- **Problem Space:** VCs are increasingly looking to distinguish themselves to their portfolio companies (portcos) by providing talent support.
- **The Competitor:** Getro is identified as the primary player, but critiqued as merely a job board "clusterfuck" that doesn't provide filtering or quality control.
- **Customer Motivation:** VCs want to "look good" to their portcos by showing they are sending candidates, even if they are cynical about the actual hiring outcome.

### Product & Vision
- **Candidate Quality:** Discussion on how quality is currently underserved. People are used to screening themselves; Careerspan's AI aims to reduce this burden by evaluating candidates against specific standards and preferences.
- **The "Internet of Careers":** Vrijen envisions Careerspan as a candidate API. A partnership with Superposition AI (technical recruiters) supports this, where Careerspan acts as the candidate-side engine piping into other systems.
- **Differentiation:** The challenge is overcoming the cynicism of employers who are inundated with "trash" from LinkedIn and traditional job boards.

### Sourcing Mechanics
- **Internal Dynamics:** Vrijen noted pushback from his team regarding sourcing as a primary focus due to unit economics, but is winning them over by demonstrating VC interest in "great eyeballs."
- **The Funnel:** Alumni Ventures gets ~1,000 unique eyeballs/week on their job board. Capturing even 5% (50 candidates/week) creates a high-value evergreen pipeline.

### Target ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
- **Primary Target:** Platform Managers/People at VC firms.
- **Strategic Segmentation:** Alex advised targeting mid-sized funds (Fund 2 or 3) rather than very small funds (which lack support staff) or massive ones (which may be too complex for a first rollout). The "smoothing function" of having hundreds of portcos makes the VC strategy viable.

## Next Steps
- Vrijen to improve his "ground game" and proactively reach out to VCs, specifically targeting platform people.
- Focus on demonstrating the "wow factor" where a VC signs up to save face but stays because the system actually works.
