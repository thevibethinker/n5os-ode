# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

- **The Optics/Utility Paradox:** In B2B sales, the decision-maker (VC Platform) may care more about the *perception* of support (optics) than the *actual* efficacy of the tool (utility), even though the end-user (Founder) needs the utility. How can Careerspan bridge this gap without compromising the integrity of the candidate quality?
- **Candidate Quality as a Forgotten Axis:** Why is the industry so resigned to "trash heaps" of candidates? Is it a technical limitation (screening at scale) or a psychological one (the expectation that hiring is inherently painful)?
- **The Internet of Careers / Candidate API:** What happens when talent becomes a protocol? If Careerspan is an API, it ceases to be a destination and becomes infrastructure. Does this increase or decrease the "stickiness" of the relationship with the talent itself?
- **The Smoothing Function of VCs:** Most startups have extremely volatile hiring needs. VCs, by aggregating hundreds of startups, create a "steady state" of talent demand. This makes VCs a superior infrastructure layer for talent markets compared to individual companies.
- **Founder vs. Team Risk Tolerance:** Vrijen is willing to bet on "sourcing" as the value-add despite unit economics, while his team (specifically the Head of AI) is more cautious. How does a founder navigate this gap when the market signals (customer interest) align with the founder's intuition but conflict with the team's operational caution?
