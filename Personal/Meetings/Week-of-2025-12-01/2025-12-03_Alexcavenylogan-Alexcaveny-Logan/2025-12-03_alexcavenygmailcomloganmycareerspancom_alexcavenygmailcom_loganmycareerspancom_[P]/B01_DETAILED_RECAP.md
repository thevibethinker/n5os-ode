# B01: Detailed Recap

Vrijen (V) and Alex Caveng discussed the evolving go-to-market (GTM) strategy for Careerspan, specifically focusing on the promising feedback from Venture Capital (VC) platform managers.

### Key Discussion Points:
- **VC Platform Signal:** V reported high-intensity interest from VC platform managers (specifically citing a recent "AI builder" event and conversations with Alumni Ventures). This contrasts with previous GTM attempts, showing a clearer indication of product-market fit.
- **Competitor Landscape:** The primary existing solution is Getro, which is viewed as a "clusterfuck" of unfiltered job postings. Careerspan's value proposition is to move beyond the "trash heap" of volume to high-quality, AI-vetted candidates.
- **The "Stealth Value" Strategy:** Alex suggested a sales approach where Careerspan fulfills the customer's immediate desire (looking good to portfolio companies/Portcos) while "secretly" delivering the real value of high-quality candidate matches.
- **Strategic ICP (Ideal Customer Profile):** The conversation explored which VCs to target. Alex advised against very small funds (which lack support staff and "patchy" hiring cycles) and suggested mid-sized funds (Fund II or III) where the "smoothing function" of having 100+ Portcos creates a constant demand for talent.
- **Candidate API Vision:** V discussed a partnership with Superposition AI (technical recruiters) as a way to pipe candidates directly into existing systems, aligning with the long-term vision of Careerspan as a "Candidate API."
- **Internal Team Alignment:** V noted that bringing the Head of AI into demos helped the team internalize the importance of "sourcing" as the primary value driver, despite unit economic concerns.
