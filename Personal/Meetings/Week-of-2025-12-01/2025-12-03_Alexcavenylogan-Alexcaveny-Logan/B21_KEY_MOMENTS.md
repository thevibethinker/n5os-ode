# B21 – Key Moments

## 1. The GTM "Aha" Moment
Vrijen describes a recent meeting with a VC platform person that felt fundamentally different from previous GTM attempts. "I think there was a much clearer indication of interest than I've gotten in... having tried many different go to markets at this point."

## 2. Defining the Competitor (Getro)
The critique of Getro as a "clusterfuck" of 10,000 jobs with no filtering. This establishes the "noise" Careerspan is solving for.

## 3. The "Stated vs. Hidden" Need Advice
Alex advises Vrijen to sell what they *want* (to look good to portcos) but deliver what they *need* (quality candidates). "Tailor your sales pitcher offering towards what they want fulfilled and then secretly get them to the real value."

## 4. The "Smoothing Function" Concept
Alex explains why VCs are better customers than individual startups: individual hiring is "patchy," but a VC portfolio provides a "smoothing function" that makes an evergreen pipeline sustainable.

## 5. The "Internet of Careers" Vision
Vrijen articulates his vision of Careerspan as a "Candidate API," which is validated by the partnership with Superposition AI.
