# B01: Detailed Recap - Hannah (Healthy Gamer GG) & V

## Overview
V and Hannah (Healthy Gamer GG) reconnected after a significant hiatus to discuss potential partnership opportunities, specifically focusing on career coaching research and the evolution of the Careerspan product. The conversation pivoted from internal coaching populations to exploring the impact of Careerspan on university student populations, with a shared goal of establishing a research study.

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Healthy Gamer Research Focus
- Hannah shared that Healthy Gamer is actively looking for external research partnerships for their coaching programs to ensure objectivity (avoiding "internal research on your own stuff").
- They are considering a partnership with the **American Institute of Boys and Men (AIBM)**, as Dr. K is on their advisory board.
- **Demographic:** Primary focus is 18-35, 70/30 male split.
- **Metrics/Surveys:** They have moved from IGD (Internet Gaming Disorder), GAD7, and PHQ9 to **PROMIS** (Purpose, Agency, Anxiety, Depression) and specialty surveys like the **Oldenburg Burnout Inventory**.

### 2. Careerspan Product Evolution
- V demonstrated the evolved Careerspan product, moving beyond storytelling to **candidate-job mapping**.
- Features include a "fit score," skill analysis (resume vs. reflection gaps), and semantic resume generation.
- V shared insights from a recent Princeton/Dartmouth study on "AI slop resumes" causing a 15% increase in miss-hiring, reinforcing the need for Careerspan's qualitative signal.

### 3. Partnership Strategy: The Research Study
- **Initial Idea:** Use Healthy Gamer's existing coaching population for a longitudinal study comparing career outcomes of those using Careerspan vs. a control group.
- **Pivot to Students:** Hannah suggested that for university/non-profit funding, a student population might be more attractive. V highlighted a previous successful pilot with **UMass Amherst** (110 students, 70% response rate, "3-story minimum" engagement).
- **Funding Avenues:** Discussion included corporate sponsors (Indeed, Handshake, Oracle), university career centers, and potential ties to the Massachusetts ecosystem.

### 4. Networking & Outreach
- **Handshake:** V and Hannah discussed reaching out to Handshake (the "College Job Board"). V has a PM contact; Dr. K may have stronger ties.
- **Indeed:** Hannah mentioned Dr. K recently spoke at Indeed, making them a high-value potential partner for business and research.
- **Oracle/Banks:** V mentioned relationships with Oracle and several banks as secondary funding options.
