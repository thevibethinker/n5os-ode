# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

## 1. High-Friction as a Feature
Standard HR philosophy suggests reducing friction in the application process. Careerspan argues that in a "spam-heavy" market, **high-value friction** (40-minute self-reflection) is a superior filter because it forces candidates to prove "thinking capacity" and "motivation" before a human ever looks at a resume.

## 2. The "Stateside Bridge" Requirement
Vrijen posits that for non-technical founders, hiring overseas isn't a cost problem—it's an **architectural problem**. Without a US-culture-aligned "product brain" to draw the line, offshore teams will inherently deliver "bad work" because they lack the "taste and systems thinking" of the founder.

## 3. The Shadow Talent Market
Alan's situation (exclusive contract but needing to hire via back channels) highlights a "shadow market" for talent where the best roles aren't on LinkedIn or public boards, but in gated communities like "Next Play" and "Future of Higher Ed."
