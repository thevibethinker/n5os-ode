# B01 Detailed Recap: Alan (Melly) & Vrijen (Careerspan)

## Overview
Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan) and Alan (Melly) discussed Alan's ongoing struggles with technical hiring for his company, Melly. Alan described a "bad string of hires" and the challenges of managing overseas engineering talent without a strong technical linchpin. Vrijen proposed two solutions: an introduction to a professional placement firm in India (Corridor X) and the implementation of Careerspan's AI-driven recruitment platform to filter for high-signal candidates.

## Key Discussion Points
- **Personal Check-in:** Vrijen shared his recent experience with burnout and his recovery through a wellness resort stay. Alan is currently in LA, while Vrijen is in Brooklyn.
- **Hiring Pain Points at Melly:**
    - Melly has a creative lead, one engineer, and a part-time designer.
    - They are overwhelmed by feature requests but lack the capacity to execute.
    - Recent hires (specifically overseas) have failed during trials or recused themselves before start dates.
    - Alan admits to being "cheap" and struggling with the pay disparities and cultural alignment of overseas talent.
- **The "Technical Linchpin" Theory:** Vrijen explained his model for non-technical founders: having a stateside "product brain" or a trusted technical lead to "draw the lines" for overseas engineers who excel at execution but need clear direction. Alan confirmed his founding engineer (of 2 years) is the "brain," but they need more reliable "soldiers."
- **Financial Levers:** Alan mentioned his top salary limit is $100k, or a combination of cash and 2% equity.
- **Proposed Solutions:**
    - **Corridor X / Shivam:** A placement firm that focuses on cultural alignment and embedding Indian talent with US-based companies.
    - **Careerspan Platform:** Vrijen offered to set up an anonymous job post for Melly. The platform uses conversational AI and structured reflection (in 50+ languages) to force candidates to demonstrate thinking capacity and motivation before Alan even sees a resume.
- **Agreement & Next Steps:** Alan agreed to try Careerspan for his current urgent hire and requested an intro to the dev shop/placement firm.

## Conclusion
The meeting concluded with a commitment to launch a Careerspan pilot for Melly's open role by the following morning, with Vrijen offering the first 100 candidates for free to help Alan find an engineer ASAP.
