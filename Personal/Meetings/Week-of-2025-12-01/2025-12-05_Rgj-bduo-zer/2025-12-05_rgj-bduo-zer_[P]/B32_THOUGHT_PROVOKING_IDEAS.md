# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

## 1. The Resume as a Consensus-Building Tool
V's insight that a resume's primary job is to make the *advocate's* job easier is profound. Instead of seeing the resume as a "test" to pass, it should be viewed as a "sales deck" that an internal champion can use to convince their skeptical peers.

## 2. The Engineering Personality Premium
The discussion about the "1% coder with no eye contact" vs. the "communicative/quirky teammate" suggests a shifting value proposition in tech. As coding becomes more commoditized (via AI), the "Vibe" and "Collaboration" coefficients of an engineer may become the primary hiring criteria.

## 3. The Fatigue Trap (Energy Arbitrage)
The colleague's struggle with the "excuse" of being tired highlights a critical problem in career transitions: Energy Arbitrage. Working a "placeholder" job can provide financial stability but at a high cost to "search energy," potentially extending the transition period significantly.
