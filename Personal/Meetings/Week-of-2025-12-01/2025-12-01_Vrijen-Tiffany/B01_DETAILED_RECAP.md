# B01: Detailed Recap

## Overview
Vrijen and Tiffany met to discuss two primary topics: a planned 10k race in Puerto Rico in February 2026 and a strategic proposal for a "Zo Club" system, a grassroots growth strategy modeled after Multi-Level Marketing (MLM) but focused on community building and "prosumer" empowerment.

## Puerto Rico 10k (February 2026)
- **Concept:** Vrijen is planning to run a 10k race near San Juan (Toa Baja/Savannah Seca) between February 21st and 27th, 2026.
- **Inspiration:** The idea came from a retreat where a participant suggested setting a challenging goal in an exotic location three months out to maintain motivation.
- **Participation:** Tiffany expressed interest in participating or being present. Vrijen offered his place for Tiffany to stay.

## Zo Club Strategy (The "MLM" Idea)
Tiffany pitched a structured plan to drive awareness and user acquisition through "Zo Clubs":
- **Club Structure:** Handpicked passionate users (Vrijen at the top) become "Club Leaders."
- **Branding:** Leaders name their clubs, create custom crests (using Zo symbols like the Pegasus), and receive custom links with discounts/AI credits.
- **Support & Budget:** Zo provides merch, X (Twitter) badges, social shout-outs, and a monthly budget (e.g., $100-$150) for "Zo Giving" (e.g., DoorDash dinners for club work sessions).
- **Communication:** Leaders manage their own channels (WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack, etc.) based on their audience.
- **Mechanics:** Leaders recruit a minimum of 5 people. Check-ins occur on Mondays, with surveys on Fridays to track progress and identify support needs.

## Strategic Discussion & Evolution
- **Incentives:** Vrijen suggested a recruitment bonus (e.g., $10 more per month per new member) to encourage propagation.
- **Metric of Success:** The ultimate goal is for club members to eventually become club leaders themselves.
- **Social Status:** Tiffany emphasized that club leadership should be a "tap on the shoulder" privilege to prevent gaming and maintain social status, similar to crypto communities.
- **Collective Projects:** Vrijen proposed a "software exchange community" or "open-source patchwork" where clubs donate projects to a collective hall of fame.
- **Future Roadmap (V2):** Future iterations could include monetization of prompts/apps, effectively turning clubs into "mini venture studios" once the platform's monetization features are live.
- **The "Zo Advantage":** Unlike YouTube influencers, Zo can offer community, education, and the platform simultaneously, owning the entire value chain.

## Conclusion
Vrijen expressed strong support for the strategy, noting that it validates the "prosumer" desire to build and own their tools rather than just consuming apps.
