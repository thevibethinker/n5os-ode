# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

## The "App Fatigue" Hypothesis
Vrijen posits that society is becoming "averse to buying decisions" because of intense consumerism. If true, the value proposition of a "general-purpose machine" like Zo isn't just power, it's the *relief* of not having to choose, buy, and manage a new SaaS tool for every problem. 

## Clubs as "Mini Venture Studios"
If Zo enables monetization of code/prompts within the platform, these "grassroots clubs" could evolve into decentralized venture studios where members build, test, and monetize products together, sharing the revenue. This transforms a "user club" into an "economic engine."

## "Tap on the Shoulder" Governance
The idea that social status cannot be "bought" or "gamed" but must be "granted" (tapped on the shoulder) is a powerful governance tool. How can this be scaled without creating a bottleneck, and can it be decentralized over time (e.g., current leaders tap new leaders)?

## Passive Contribution (Folding@Home for Zo)
Vrijen asked: "What is the Zo equivalent of a folding folding project?" Is there a way for idle Zo compute or agentic activity to contribute to a collective good (research, decentralized compute, or community knowledge building) that makes the user feel like they are contributing even when not actively "working"?
