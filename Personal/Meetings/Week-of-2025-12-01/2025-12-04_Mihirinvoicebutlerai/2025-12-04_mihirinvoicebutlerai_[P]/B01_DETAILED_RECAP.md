# B01: Detailed Recap

## Overview
Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan) and Mihir (Invoice Butler AI) met to discuss their respective startups, exchange founder experiences, and explore business model advice, specifically regarding invoicing and subscription management for Careerspan's pivot toward an employer-facing model.

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Founder Background & Personal Context
- **Mihir (Invoice Butler AI):** Based in New York (downtown Brooklyn); co-founder is in Boston. Parents are from India (first-gen), born in California. Moved to NY 3 years ago and recently got engaged.
- **Vrijen (Careerspan):** Spent teenage years in Bangladesh, college in US, 20s in Singapore. Background in career coaching.

### 2. Careerspan Evolution & Product Strategy
- **The Pivot:** Shifting from a job-seeker-centric model to an employer-facing matching system. 
- **The Core Tech:** Socratic, reflection-based experience that extracts rich qualitative data on how candidates think and evaluate decisions.
- **The Value Proposition:** Filtering the top 2-5% of candidates based on qualitative thresholds rather than just keywords, potentially making traditional ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) less relevant for high-signal hiring.
- **The "Talent Call" Use Case:** Permanent links for talent funnels to avoid high recruiter fees (e.g., $20k) when a priority hire arises later.

### 3. Business Model & Invoicing Advice (Mihir’s Input)
- **Current State:** Careerspan is starting to collect revenue ($100-$200) manually via Stripe payment links. They have ~5 customers.
- **Automation Threshold:** Mihir advised not to automate invoicing until reaching 25-30 customers.
- **Tooling Strategy:** 
    - Use Stripe for credit card payments but avoid "Stripe Invoicing" specifically, as Stripe takes a higher cut for invoicing features.
    - Switch to QuickBooks Invoicing if a formal invoice system is needed.
- **Contract Strategy:** Vrijen is targeting VCs (specifically platform/talent teams) as a primary ICP. Mihir suggested shifting from monthly to annual contracts to increase revenue quality for future acquisition valuation.

### 4. Personnel & Hiring Discussion
- **Careerspan Team:** Currently on a "skeleton crew" to position for acquisition without further raising.
- **Mihir’s Engineering Challenge:** Mihir discussed a "cracked" (extremely high-performing) engineer who is technically exceptional (top 1% according to his co-founder, a former Google/MongoDB engineer) but has a different social profile (described as "on the autistic side" though not necessarily diagnosed). 
- **Shared Vision:** Both founders noted that high-signal talent often flies under the radar of traditional ATS because they aren't good at self-advocacy.
