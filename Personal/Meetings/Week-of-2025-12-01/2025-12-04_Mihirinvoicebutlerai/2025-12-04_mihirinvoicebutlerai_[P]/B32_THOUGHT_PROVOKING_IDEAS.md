# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

## The "ATS Blind Spot"
The idea that technical brilliance (the "cracked engineer") is often inversely correlated with self-advocacy skills. If an ATS is a proxy for "how well someone can describe themselves," it systematically filters out the most technically gifted individuals who don't prioritize self-marketing. Careerspan's "Socratic" approach might be the only way to solve this "low self-advocacy" signal loss.

## The Cost of Free Space
Mihir’s observation that "free space" (residencies like Grand Central Tech) actually has a hidden cost: the inability to demand operational essentials (sitting next to your team, room availability). "You’re a taker if it’s free." This is a useful analogy for any subsidized service in a startup's lifecycle.

## Data Ownership as the Killer Feature
For VCs, the "talent board" isn't about the board itself; it's about the proprietary database of talent they can leverage across their portfolio. Current tools (Getro) keep that data, creating a wedge between the VC and their own asset.
