# B32 Thought Provoking Ideas

*   **The Cost of "Free"**: Mihir's observation that "you can't demand anything if it's free" regarding office space—and how that applies to business relationships and product expectations.
*   **Qualitative Filtering vs. Keyword Matching**: The idea that 95% of the market is stuck in "keyword hell," and the winner in recruiting tech will be whoever can automate the "Socratic reflection" of a coach.
*   **Founder Goal Alignment**: Mihir's perspective that achieving any set goal (like finding a spouse) is a valid signal of founder-market fit/execution ability.
*   **The "Skeleton Crew" Advantage**: V mentioning the decision not to raise and operating with a skeleton crew to position for acquisition—challenging the "growth at all costs" VC narrative.
