# B01 Detailed Recap

The meeting was an introductory conversation between Vrijen (V) and Mihir (Founder of Invoice Butler). The discussion covered personal backgrounds, the origin stories of their respective companies, business model strategies, and specific advice on invoicing and scaling for early-stage startups.

### Key Discussion Points:

1.  **Personal & Geographic Context**:
    *   Mihir is based in Downtown Brooklyn, while his co-founder is in Boston.
    *   V is based in New York.
    *   Mihir shared his journey from California to NYC (moved to "find a wife" and is now engaged).
    *   V shared his background, having spent time in Bangladesh, the US, and Singapore, where he met his co-founder.

2.  **Careerspan Evolution**:
    *   V explained that Careerspan started as a career coaching product focused on structured self-reflection (Socratic method).
    *   The business has pivoted toward a matching model where employers are charged rather than job seekers.
    *   The value proposition for employers is a "non-lazy" one-click application that provides high-quality qualitative data on candidates, bypassing traditional keyword-heavy ATS filters.

3.  **Invoice Butler & Invoicing Advice**:
    *   Mihir provided tactical advice on early-stage invoicing. He suggested V keep manual processes (Stripe payment links) until reaching 25-30 customers.
    *   Mihir warned about Stripe's cut on invoicing versus credit card payments and suggested QuickBooks for invoicing if needed later.
    *   Discussion on the importance of annual contracts for acquisition valuation.

4.  **Hiring Challenges**:
    *   Mihir shared a dilemma regarding a "cracked" (top 1%) but socially unique engineer.
    *   V related this back to Careerspan's mission: helping high-signal candidates who "fly under the radar" of traditional recruiting systems get noticed.
