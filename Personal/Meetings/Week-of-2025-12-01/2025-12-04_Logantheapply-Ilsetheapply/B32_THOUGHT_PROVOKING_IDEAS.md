# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

- **The "Apocalypse" Value Prop:** The idea that a single investment in Careerspan pays dividends until the "apocalypse" by continuously finding roles without re-applying.
- **Catnip Pricing:** Framing pricing not as a cost, but as a trade-off where the customer gets the specific metric they value most (the "catnip") while the system protects its margins on other variables.
- **SaaS Conundrum:** The realization that software is always "at the mercy of the health of the user's browser," highlighting the boundary between product responsibility and user environment.
- **Human-in-the-Loop Automation:** The counter-intuitive idea that more automation requires *better* (not less) human confirmation at critical deal-breaking junctures.
