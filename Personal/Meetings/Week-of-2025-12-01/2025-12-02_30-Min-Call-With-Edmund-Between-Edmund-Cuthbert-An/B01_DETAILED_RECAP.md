# B01: Detailed Recap

## Overview
Vrijen (V) met with Edmund Cuthbert and Ilsa (Head of AI) to discuss a strategic partnership between Careerspan and Edmund's company (likely Juicebox/Juice Labs, though referred to as "you folks" or "your side"). The focus was on creating a "one-two punch" for technical hiring, specifically for founding and senior engineers in the New York startup ecosystem (Marvin, New York Founders Club).

## Key Discussion Points
- **Partnership Mechanics:** Careerspan will act as the "entry point" to cultivate a talent pool that Edmund's side can tap into. Edmund's side already has the companies/clients wanting this inbound.
- **Integration Flow:**
    - Careerspan uses a Voice Agent to intake role context from hiring managers.
    - This context is transformed into an "LLM-legible job description" string (indented requirements, traits, etc.).
    - This string will be sent to Edmund/Ilsa's platform to automate candidate matching and story-based assessments.
- **Product Demonstration:** Ilsa demonstrated their employer portal, which breaks JDs down into 10-12 responsibilities and maps soft/hard skills with competence levels.
- **Business Model:** V proposed a revenue-share model (e.g., 25% of a 10% salary fee) for successful placements, bypassing immediate pricing/API cost concerns to focus on product validation and design partnership.
- **Candidate Experience:** Edmund's side asks users to "tell stories" rather than just providing resumes, which V found highly aligned with Careerspan's reasoning-based approach over "if-else" logic.

## Strategic Alignment
Both parties emphasized moving away from traditional keyword-based recruitment toward a "dynamical system" that reasons over candidate context and stories. V is interested in how high-taste consumer-facing candidate products can become APIs for company-facing tools.
