# Meeting Summary

Colin Navon reached out through Brady for career help after being unjustly fired. He was terminated after hiring two employees who turned out to be fraudulent actors using stolen identities. Both candidates passed multiple interview rounds, technical assessments, I9 verification, and background checks. The fraud was only discovered 30-45 days into employment when gifts were sent to permanent addresses and the real identity holders reported the theft.

Despite being forthcoming with the investigation, volunteering to build new prevention SOPs, and having no way to detect the sophisticated fraud, Colin was made a scapegoat and fired without severance. He's now job searching with his wife 12 weeks pregnant.

Logan joined the call when V identified relevance to her research on AI-enabled candidate fraud. V and Logan introduced Careerspan's coaching-first approach to solving candidate verification and quality assessment problems. Logan immediately connected Colin with employment attorney Gabby.

The conversation highlighted both the systemic vulnerability of traditional hiring processes to sophisticated identity fraud and the human cost of organizational scapegoating.
