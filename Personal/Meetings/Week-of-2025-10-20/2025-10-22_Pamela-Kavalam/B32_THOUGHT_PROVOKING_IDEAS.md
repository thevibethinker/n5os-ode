# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

## 1. The "Ground Game" over "Big Ticket"
Is there a more sustainable growth model in the AI era by focusing on "free" community-led adoption that builds a massive supply of talent/data, rather than chasing high-CAC enterprise sales?

## 2. VC as an "Efficiency" Product
V's thesis that VCs need custom "deal-flow processors" more than generic CRM tools. The idea that a VC's specific thesis can be encoded as a semantic filter for all inbound meetings and pitch decks is a potent implementation of the "Simple > Easy" principle.

## 3. The End of the Resume?
V's critique of the current ATS landscape: AI is writing resumes, and AI is filtering them, leading to total noise. The "Zero-to-Five" ATS idea suggests that the future of hiring isn't about matching keywords, but about recording a founder's cultural "vibe" and processing all candidates against that one unique signal.
