# B06: Business Context

## Strategic Pivot: Community-Led Growth
Careerspan is moving away from the "big ticket sales" model in favor of a "ground game." This involves embedding the tech into existing communities (like Next Play) where founders already congregate. The goal is to "give away" the tech to candidates to capture the demand side, while monetizing the supply side (hiring managers) who are overwhelmed by inbound volume.

## The "Zo Computer" Factor
V is leveraging his status as a power user/ambassador for Zo Computer to create a secondary revenue stream and a "wedge" into VC relationships. By solving the productivity/deal-flow problem for VCs, he creates a high-trust relationship that can later benefit Careerspan.

## Market Gap: Early-Stage ATS
The conversation identified a specific segment (Founders hiring employees 1-5) that is currently underserved by existing ATS solutions like Greenhouse (too heavy) or Notion (too manual). V's proposed solution uses semantic analysis to solve the "noise" problem in hiring without the overhead of enterprise software.
