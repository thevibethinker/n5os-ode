# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview

**Date:** October 17, 2025  
**Duration:** ~60 minutes  
**Participants:** Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan founder), Zoe team members (product, marketing, engineering)  
**Core Topic:** Go-to-market strategy exploration and potential collaboration paths between Careerspan and Zo

## Key Discussion Threads

### 1. Careerspan's Business Model & Constraints

Vrijen outlined Careerspan's current position:
- Started as B2C career coaching product targeting job seekers, but discovered jobseekers have no budget
- Pivoted to B2B2C through universities, but encountered friction:
  - Universities have minimal budgets despite product benefits
  - Career services offices lack manpower to execute
  - Budget holders (admin) have misaligned incentives with outcomes
- **Strategic insight:** Approaching universities through academic departments (not career offices) yields better traction—they have larger budgets and align with core operations
- **Current strategy:** Building a decentralized talent network by giving Careerspan free to professional communities (Stripe alumni, McKinsey alumni, university networks)
- **Survival model:** Keep Careerspan funded with minimal spend while building network effects; anticipates acquisition as end-goal

### 2. Zo as Enabling Technology for Careerspan's Go-to-Market

Vrijen explored using Zo as a distribution/consulting vehicle:
- **Initial pitch:** Create a "VC Edition" of Zo—a pre-configured base layer that VCs can duplicate and customize themselves
- **Value proposition:** VCs struggle with AI tooling—scattered files, poor prompt engineering, lack of workflow automation. Zo solves this at scale
- **Implementation:** Vrijen would do market research, create templates, offer white-glove implementation for higher-tier customers
- **Revenue model:** One-time setup fee + recurring for customization/consulting
- **Positioning:** Solutions architect / partner / "recipe chef" role within Zo ecosystem

### 3. Zo's Go-to-Market Challenges & Opportunities

Zoe team articulated their current positioning challenge:
- Product is powerful but scope is hard to communicate—it's infinitely customizable
- Need for "tip of the spear" users who create compelling use cases that others can adopt
- Question: Product excellence (Superhuman-like) vs. tastemaker penetration + distribution?
- **Working hypothesis:** Tastemakers + enthusiasts first, then leverage those nodes for word-of-mouth and distribution

### 4. Ecosystem Ideas (App Store / Marketplace)

Multiple concepts emerged:
- **Recipe/automation marketplace:** Compensate builders for recipes ($X per recipe, $Y per install)
- **Site templates:** Pre-built templates for specific use cases
- **Agents:** Deployable AI agents that can run on someone else's Zo instance
- **Gumroad model:** Payment processing layer for Zo creators selling templates, automations, etc.
- **Venture studio angle:** Zo as operating system enabling "Zo companies"—products built on Zo infrastructure with revenue-sharing

### 5. Education & Community Strategy

Potential channels discussed:
- **University hackathons:** Zo-sponsored events to find obsessive builders and surface compelling use cases
- **Student ambassador programs:** Free access + incentive structure to drive campus penetration
- **Influencer partnerships:** Creators building custom "automation ecosystems" (e.g., creator suite for influencers)
- **Professional communities:** Stripe alumni, McKinsey alumni, university networks—use Zo as a hub for these groups
- **ASU Tech Week / edtech conferences:** High-tech-forward environments to demonstrate Zo capabilities
- **Student clubs:** Free licenses + event funding to build adoption

### 6. Persona-Driven Distribution

Vrijen emphasized finding the right end-user personas first, then identifying solutions architects who can build for them:
- Profile: Neurotic obsessives with high productivity needs + desire for customization
- These folks congregate in: hackathons, automation communities (n8n), professional networks, student clubs
- Key insight: Volume (user acquisition) vs. engagement (ongoing value)—VCs measure differently, so strategy depends on funding narrative

### 7. Pricing & Monetization Philosophy

Vrijen's perspective on Zo pricing:
- Subscription model is hard when value varies dramatically per user
- Usage-based pricing makes more sense IF Zo's API costs are transparent
- Concern about ChatGPT lock-in: VCs want privacy-preserving AI; could justify running private models on Zo
- Revenue model for solution architects: Fixed per-recipe fee + ongoing per-install royalty (similar to Zapier/Gumroad model)

### 8. Personal Motivation & Learning Journey

Vrijen's own evolving relationship with Zo:
- Attracted by "a computer you can prompt" positioning
- Transitioning from CEO (high-pressure VC dynamics) to hands-on builder—rewarding shift
- Learning to code through Zo; it's accelerating his tech literacy
- Building on Zo lets him maintain product independence without VC pressure
- Plans to share Zo learnings broadly—already thinking about content strategy with co-founder on AI for career advancement

### 9. Logistics & Mutual Offers

Concrete asks/offers made:
- **Vrijen needs:** Fresh Zo account clones to stand up VC-focused templates quickly; image cloning capability would be helpful
- **Vrijen offers:** Stripe alumni community access for Zo promotion; help getting Zo represented at ASU Tech Week; connection to Major League Hacking (MLH) for hackathon partnership
- **Zoe team wants:** To learn from Vrijen's GTM experiments; potentially collaborative marketing content

## Atmosphere & Tone

- Collaborative, exploratory tone—not a sales call but genuine strategic brainstorm
- Vrijen came with well-formed ideas but open to feedback
- Zoe team energized by his use case and ecosystem thinking
- Shared values: neither wants VC pressure; both value product quality over growth-at-all-costs
- Personal rapport building: background stories (Vrijen from India/Singapore; Zoe team diverse geographies), shared cultural references

## Context Notes

- Meeting took place over lunch—personal, informal feel
- Vrijen has deep career coaching expertise (decade) + 4 years as founder/entrepreneur
- Zoe team includes founders with Stripe/Venmo/Substack pedigree
- Both organizations are in "survival/strategic positioning" mode rather than high-growth mode
- Vrijen is increasingly learning software engineering via Zo—non-technical founder becoming more technical
