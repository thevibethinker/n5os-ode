## Meeting Overview
A collaborative workshop between Careerspan (Vrijen Attawar, Logan Currie) and Zo Computer (Ben Guo) focused on "Fundamentals of AI Productivity." The session explored advanced mental models and practical techniques for using AI systems more effectively, moving beyond basic prompting to context engineering, personal data analysis, and pipeline-level workflows. The meeting combined strategic frameworks with hands-on demonstrations of LinkedIn data analysis and Zo Computer's capabilities.

### Vrijen's Presentation: Mental Models & Context Engineering (05:30-13:31)
Vrijen opened by explaining why he called the session "Fundamentals of AI Productivity" a "head fake"—it wasn't about basic AI fundamentals but about mental models for using AI effectively. He cited survey results showing that while most attendees use ChatGPT and many use Claude code, 80% feel stuck or overwhelmed. His key insight: this isn't a technology or tactics problem, but a mental model problem. People are collecting individual techniques without understanding how they integrate.

Vrijen presented "AI is magic" as collapsing the gap between intent and outcome—comparing it to how ordering food via DoorDash would seem magical to someone from 1,000 years ago, but is now mundane because we have the right mental model. He introduced "context engineering" as the core skill: instead of just prompting, you cultivate context that the AI uses to generate the desired output.

He outlined three levels of context engineering:
- **Conversation level**: Tactical techniques including building context before requesting deliverables, offensive/defensive prompting (expansive vs. critical thinking), and threshold rubrics (asking AI to create and score against evaluation criteria)
- **Environment level**: Persistent context including personalization, memory curation (both explicit and verbatim memory), cognitive guardrails (don't flatter, be critical), and avoiding "boiling the ocean" with too much context
- **Pipeline level**: Connecting external world data into coherent AI systems across environments (Claude code, Zo, ChatGPT, terminals, IDEs)

Vrijen emphasized that mastering all three levels creates an emergent "personal operating system" and urged attendees to give themselves grace for not having this figured out—it's not actually fundamental but quite advanced.

### Logan's Demonstration: LinkedIn Data Analysis (13:31-25:07)
Logan presented a hands-on case study using Claude code to analyze his exported LinkedIn data (48 CSV files spanning 18 years with 5,000+ messages, connections, search history, comments, etc.). He noted a discrepancy between 45,000 imported contacts and only 3,300 connections, revealing networks that don't overlap with his LinkedIn network.

He demonstrated multiple visualizations and analyses:

1. **Network clusters by current employer**: Visualizing his professional bubbles, particularly noting significant presence in AI/ML and big tech. He published this on his personal website as a professional portfolio element.

2. **Lost opportunity analysis**: Processing all 5,000 messages to identify 23 high-value DMs from the last six months that he had accidentally ghosted. The system categorized them by urgency and opportunity type (e.g., podcast invitations with 25,000+ followers, collaboration offers).

3. **Career security correlation**: Demonstrating an 1,800% increase in people adding him as connections correlated with increased posting frequency, supporting his thesis that job security is dead and career security comes from visibility and findability.

4. **Inbound vs outbound connection ratio**: Using this as a proxy for social capital—showing more inbound than outbound requests in the last six months.

5. **Connection history with interaction tracking**: Visualizing who added whom and DM interaction frequency, helping identify leads and deepen specific relationships.

6. **Cross-referencing with Careerspan**: Combining Careerspan's behavioral interview data (capturing skills proven in context, not empty endorsements) with LinkedIn network data to identify career pivot opportunities. For example, while he had a large gap for engineering management with no connections, user research showed strong skill overlap with 12 direct connections in that field.

Logan shared his metacognitive approach: narrative before data, using himself as a guinea pig, not accepting the first answer, asking Claude to "delight me" with insights LinkedIn should be showing but doesn't, and consistently iterating. He referenced the Anthropic concept of "unhobbling the model"—the AI is capable of more than you think if you give it the right context and challenges.

### Ben's Demonstration: Zo Computer (25:07-end)
Ben demoed Zo Computer, a managed cloud computer for AI with a personal environment and built-in integrations. He positioned it as more secure than cloudbot while offering similar capabilities. His key points:

- **Data exploration**: Using GDPR data exports to analyze personal data—demonstrated with Spotify listening data showing a correlation between music volume and coding activity (high during coding, lower post-launch when busy).

- **Model ecosystem**: Zo supports all frontier models and integrates Claude code natively, letting users sign into their Claude Code subscription within the Zo interface.

- **Communication channels**: Built-in text and email integrations enable working with AI via text message or email. Ben demonstrated coding via text while traveling—telling his Claude Code agent to check out a branch and fix issues from the airport line.

- **Parallel productivity**: Using AI during "passive moments" (waiting, transit) to save articles, summarize content, and execute tasks, significantly amplifying what's possible in limited time.

- **Plan-based workflows**: Ben showed a "plan file" from his codebase—a mix of prose and code outlining system architecture in stepwise fashion. He described this as "the future of coding" and noted Zo plans to build first-class UI around long-range AI task planning and execution.

Ben emphasized the importance of intentionality and documentation when working with AI, whether for technical or non-technical tasks.

## Key Takeaways

- **Mental model problem, not tool problem**: Most AI users feel stuck not because they lack tools or knowledge, but because they don't understand how techniques integrate into coherent systems.
- **Context engineering over prompting**: The core skill is cultivating rich context for the AI rather than crafting perfect prompts—across conversation, environment, and pipeline levels.
- **Personal data as AI advantage**: Giving AI access to your data (LinkedIn exports, listening history, etc.) "unhobbles" it dramatically, enabling insights that platforms like LinkedIn themselves don't surface.
- **Productivity through parallel execution**: Using AI in passive moments (travel, waiting) via text/email interfaces creates exponential productivity gains for busy professionals.
- **The future is planning, not coding**: Code will increasingly be generated from plan documents that mix prose with stepwise instructions, with AI handling implementation.
- **Career security through visibility**: Logan's data showed an 1,800% increase in network growth correlated with posting frequency, supporting the thesis that findability and public work create career security in a world where job security is dead.
- **Iteration beyond first answers**: Both Logan and Ben emphasized not accepting initial AI outputs—asking for "delight," challenging findings, and iterating consistently leads to dramatically better insights.
