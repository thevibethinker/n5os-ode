# RECAP: Friends & Next AI Productivity Workshop

**Date:** 2026-01-29  
**Participants:** Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan), Logan Currie (Careerspan), Ben Guo (Zo Computer), Friends & Next Community  
**Type:** External Workshop

## Executive Summary
Vrijen, Logan, and Ben presented an AI productivity workshop titled "Fundamentals of AI Productivity" to the Friends & Next community. The session focused on moving beyond individual AI tactics to building a coherent **personal AI operating system** through **context engineering** at three levels: conversation, environment, and pipeline. The workshop featured live demonstrations of deep LinkedIn data analysis using Claude Code and the unique managed environment of Zo Computer.

## Strategic Framework: Context Engineering
Vrijen introduced a three-level model for context engineering to bridge the gap between intent and outcome:
1. **Conversation Level:** Tactical strategies like delaying output, offensive/defensive prompting, and threshold rubrics.
2. **Environment Level:** Persistent context curation, persona instructions, and cognitive guardrails.
3. **Pipeline Level:** Creating data flows between external sources (CSV, API, docs) and the AI environment.

## Key Demonstrations
- **LinkedIn Intelligence (Logan):** Analyzed 18 years of data to reveal network mismatches (3.3k connections vs 45k contacts), employer clustering, and "lost opportunities" (ghosted DMs from high-value connections).
- **Zo Computer (Ben):** Demonstrated Zo as a managed version of Claude Code, enabling parallel work via SMS/Email and the use of "plan files" as the future of system design.

## The Stance
The session emphasized that AI stuckness is a **mental model problem**, not a tool problem. The goal is "unhobbling" the AI and the user through intentional systems design rather than ad-hoc prompting.
