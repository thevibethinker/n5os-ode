# B21_KEY_MOMENTS

## Key Quotes

**"You don't have a technology problem. You have a mental model problem."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Addressing the survey results showing 80% of respondents feel stuck despite using AI tools. Reframes the core challenge from technical acquisition to cognitive understanding.

**"It's not that you lack the tools or even the knowledge, it's that you're collecting individual techniques without actually understanding how they fit."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Explaining why AI outputs often come off as "bang average"—the missing piece is understanding how techniques integrate into a coherent system, not accumulating disconnected tactics.

**"The idea of context engineering is that you don't prompt the AI per se. You work with the AI to cultivate the context that it then uses to collapse down to the output that you want."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Defining the shift from prompting as command to context as foundation. Establishes the core mental model for effective AI interaction.

**"LinkedIn has everything that you have ever done, but they only show you 10% of it."**  
— Logan Currie  
*Context:* Revealing the hidden value locked in platform data. Highlights the opportunity gap between what companies know about you and what they surface—a fundamental insight driving the personal data analysis work.

**"Job security is dead and you need to be building career security for yourself."**  
— Logan Currie  
*Context:* Stating the philosophical thesis driving the visibility and networking analysis. Shifts focus from employer-dependent security to self-constructed career resilience.

**"You can unhobble your AI a lot when you give it access to your files and data."**  
— Ben Guo  
*Context:* Drawing on Anthropic's "unhobbling" concept to explain why personal data access transforms AI capability. Frames data integration as the key unlock for deeper AI utility.

**"You can unhobble it even more when you give the AI its own home."**  
— Ben Guo  
*Context:* Strategic positioning of Zo Computer as the next evolution—moving from data access to persistent environment. Makes the case for dedicated AI computing environments over ephemeral sessions.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"What should they show us? What could I see with this data?"**  
— Logan Currie (prompting Claude)  
*Significance:* Meta-question used to push beyond initial analysis. Demonstrates the "delight me" pattern for extracting deeper insights from data—asking not just what's there, but what could be revealed.

**"What else can you do?"**  
— Logan Currie (prompting Claude)  
*Significance:* Iterative questioning technique to avoid accepting first answers. Embodies the anti-pattern of stopping at surface-level analysis and instead pushing for unexpected, valuable discoveries.
