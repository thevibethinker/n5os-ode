# B03_DECISIONS

## Decision 1: Primary Monetization Through Organizations
**DECISION:** Focus sales efforts on B2B market—organizations, school districts, and clinics—rather than individual consumers.

**CONTEXT:** Product addresses executive function needs; organizational purchasing enables supporters/teachers/students to use for free via employer-paid access. This creates organizational cohesiveness and consistency in executive function support.

**DECIDED BY:** Chris Hanson

**IMPLICATIONS:** Individual pricing exists but is secondary; primary GTM is organizational sales via quote requests.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Direct B2C sales.

## Decision 2: Current Pricing Model
**DECISION:** Adopt a three-tier pricing structure.

**CONTEXT:** Balance accessibility with revenue; support organizational and individual use cases while maintaining sustainability.

**DECIDED BY:** Chris Hanson

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Individual users: Free trial, then $10/month
- Supporters: Up to 10 profiles for $20/month
- Organizations: Request quote (goes to form, Chris contacts)

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Not explicitly stated.

## Decision 3: No Custom Feature Builds for Organizations
**DECISION:** Decline custom feature builds for organizational clients.

**CONTEXT:** Avoid over-optimizing for one stakeholder, manage bandwidth risk, and prevent scope creep that could alienate other users.

**DECIDED BY:** Chris Hanson

**IMPLICATIONS:** Pilots will have limited feature requests; may forgo large deals requiring heavy custom development.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Accepting large deals with custom feature requirements.

## Decision 4: Pilot Strategy (Aligned Decision)
**DECISION (ALIGNED):** Pursue paid "linchpin" pilot with a design partner who will pay and limit feature requests.

**CONTEXT:** Risk of "pilot purgatory" (endless free pilots); need a cornerstone customer for reliable revenue and social proof.

**DECIDED BY:** Chris Hanson (aligning with Vrijen's recommendation)

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Prioritize partners willing to work with the product as-is
- Pilot must include financial commitment (not free)
- Use pilot as proof point for next growth steps (more sales, expansion, or funding)

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Free pilots with heavy feature requests; immediate fundraising.

## Decision 5: Avoid VC Funding, Hire SDR Instead
**DECISION (TENTATIVE):** Optimize for efficient growth: hire an SDR (Sales Development Representative) for lead generation and outreach; founder handles closing.

**CONTEXT:** Investor scrutiny is challenging for non-technical founders; preference for sustainable, product-driven growth over venture capital.

**DECIDED BY:** Chris Hanson (aligning with Vrijen's recommendation)

**IMPLICATIONS:** Control remains with founder; sales function is split between SDR (prospecting) and founder (closing); customer research and messaging refinement happen organically through closing.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Raising venture capital.

## Decision 6: Create Facebook Group for Users
**DECISION:** Create a branded Facebook group for users—including trial users—for updates and feedback.

**CONTEXT:** Leverage 65k email list; build community; gather continuous feedback; align with authentic "people like you" branding.

**DECIDED BY:** Chris Hanson

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Weekly updates on feature considerations and interviews
- Includes trial users
- Branding: "group for people like you" or similar

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** No community; one-to-many communications only.
