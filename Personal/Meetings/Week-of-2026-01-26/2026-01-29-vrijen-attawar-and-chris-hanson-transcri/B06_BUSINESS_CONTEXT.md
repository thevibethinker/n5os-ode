# B06: Business Context & Implications

## Market Opportunity
The executive function coaching market presents a significant opportunity in the education space. Traditional options for students with executive function needs include therapy, medication, and human coaching—effective but not universally accessible or affordable. Technology can fill gaps by providing "brain crutches" that help people with ADHD operate at more neurotypical levels. The market includes:

- **Organizations**: K-12 school districts, clinics, and supportive organizations willing to pay for tools that help their staff support students
- **Individuals**: Students and supporters seeking accessible executive function support
- **Network advantage**: 65,000 active email subscribers provide a strong foundation for customer acquisition and validation

## Competitive Position
The product differentiates through authentic founder-market fit:
- **Creator credibility**: Built by someone who has ADHD, was a special ed teacher, and continues coaching—creating trust with the audience served
- **Technical execution**: Product appears well-executed despite non-technical founder, with support from a technical generalist (Joe Coleman)
- **Brand alignment**: "Inspired by people like you, created by people like you" messaging resonates with the authentic approach
- **Self-funded positioning**: No external investors allows independence from investor-driven feature creep, though this presents capital constraints

## Strategic Direction
The core strategy is B2B2C: sell to organizations that then provide the tool to end users. This approach:
- **Reduces burden** on teachers and supporters by having employers pay
- **Creates organizational cohesion** when everyone uses the same tool with shared understanding of executive function needs
- **Avoids free pilot traps**: The strategy emphasizes paid pilots with limited feature requests rather than endless free trials that demand custom development
- **Seeks linchpin deals**: Focus on finding cornerstone customers willing to be design partners and pay (even reduced amounts) in exchange for ongoing collaboration

**Avoids venture capital**: Strong preference for customer-funded growth over investor capital, given investor skepticism of non-technical founders and the control trade-offs involved.

## Commercial Model
Current monetization approach:
- **Individual users**: $10/month (launch pricing)
- **Supporters**: $20/month for up to 10 profiles
- **Organizations**: Custom quotes via request form, direct sales conversations

**Key constraints and considerations**:
- Feature creep risk when dealing with large organizations demanding custom features
- Chicken-and-egg problem: large deals ($100k+) dangle but require bandwidth-heavy feature development
- Need to balance boiling the ocean vs. being everything to one stakeholder
- Time efficiency critical with small team (founder + tech generalist + onboarding specialist)

## Implications & Next Steps
**Strategic priorities**:
- Define ideal customer profile (ICP) more narrowly—current thinking is that even when narrowing, it's likely still too broad
- Conduct structured customer interviews to identify which segment will pay for the product as-is vs. demanding features
- Establish clear boundaries on custom development during sales conversations
- Consider building in public to leverage authentic storytelling about executive function challenges

**Channel strategy opportunities**:
- Leverage existing 65,000 email list for customer research and validation
- Explore user community models (Facebook group, private user research group) to engage top users
- Consider joining edtech communities (e.g., Future of Higher Education) for networking and lead generation
- Maintain authenticity in brand communication across channels

**Key risks**:
- Pilot fatigue and feature creep from endless organization-specific requirements
- Over-optimizing for one stakeholder at expense of broader market
- Time allocation efficiency with limited team resources
- Niche definition may require feature prioritization and potential user backlash if features are removed

**Product-market fit signals**:
- Some users are loving existing features and functionality
- Founder has deep domain expertise and authentic connection to the problem
- Early product is functional and well-executed
- Challenge is translating product capability into clear ICP and sustainable monetization
