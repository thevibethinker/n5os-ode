# B03_DECISIONS

## Decision 1: Product Launch Timeline

**DECISION:** Launch new product to market in approximately one month (targeting February 2026)

**CONTEXT:** Max has been building and refining the product over the past year, aligning it with current market conditions where people struggle to make progress in careers and job transitions.

**DECIDED BY:** Maximilian Kwok

**IMPLICATIONS:** Product must be production-ready soon; marketing and go-to-market efforts need to be finalized; messaging is already fine-tuned to address market pain points.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None explicitly stated; timeline appears firm.

## Decision 2: Revenue Model Strategy

**DECISION:** Implement usage-based "energy" monetization model where users pay to deploy/train their AI versions, NOT charging companies to surface candidates in the beginning

**CONTEXT:** Revenue model question explored; Max explicitly ruled out company-paid candidate surfacing as initial revenue stream. Users' portfolio work generates an AI representation of themselves that can be deployed for job search automation.

**DECIDED BY:** Maximilian Kwok

**IMPLICATIONS:** User acquisition and engagement becomes critical; energy consumption/deployment frequency drives revenue; positioning as AI-powered career tool rather than B2B hiring platform initially.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Charging companies to surface high-quality candidates (explicitly rejected for initial phase).

## Decision 3: Product Positioning Priority

**DECISION:** Focus product on portfolio creation first, assessment second

**CONTEXT:** V asked whether the product is for evaluation or portfolio creation. Max clarified the prioritization based on learnings from startup journey: businesses care about value add, not abstract assessments.

**DECIDED BY:** Maximilian Kwok

**IMPLICATIONS:** Feature development prioritized around portfolio/portfolio artifacts; assessment capabilities deferred; focus on helping users create demonstrable work samples.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Assessment-first approach (rejected based on difficulty of convincing both parties of assessment legitimacy).

## Decision 4: Careerspan Strategic Direction

**DECISION:** Aim to sell company mid-2026, but use next few months to attempt revenue generation to improve valuation

**CONTEXT:** Careerspan is vacillating between sale and building revenue. V articulated consensus strategy: give themselves a runway to generate some revenue before pursuing sale, as this would "massively improve valuation."

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar (with team consensus)

**IMPLICATIONS:** Continued resource allocation to revenue-generating activities; sale timeline shifted to mid-2026; balance between acquisition conversations and revenue efforts.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Immediate sale without revenue attempts.

## Decision 5: Careerspan Revenue Pilot

**DECISION:** Pursue B2B partnership with recruiter in India to provide Careerspan tech to recruiting agency

**CONTEXT:** A recruiter friend reached out saying the tech helps their candidates stand out. V is working with them to generate revenue, even if small.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:** Immediate revenue stream validation; potential reference case for B2B sales; leverages Shivam's network in India.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Not explicitly stated.

## Decision 6: Careerspan Team Restructuring

**DECISION:** Team reduced to 3 people, with 2 founders working unpaid

**CONTEXT:** V described current state after capital raise: "shaved down our team to three folks... two out of them are founders not being paid."

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar / Careerspan leadership

**IMPLICATIONS:** Extended runway for extended sell/revenue window; limited execution capacity; founders bearing financial risk.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Not explicitly stated.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar
