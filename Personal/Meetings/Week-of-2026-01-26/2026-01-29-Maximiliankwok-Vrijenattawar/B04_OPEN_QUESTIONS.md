# B04: Open Questions & Unresolved Items

## Question 1: Careerspan Revenue Generation Feasibility

**QUESTION:** Can Careerspan meaningfully generate revenue within the next few months to improve valuation before pursuing an acquisition?

**CONTEXT:** Vrijen mentioned they are "vacillating" between selling the company now vs. aiming to sell "towards the middle of the year" after trying to generate revenue. The team has been shaved down to three people (two unpaid founders) and they're in "a little bit of a tough spot." They have interest from a recruiter in India who wants to use their tech, but the broader revenue question remains unresolved.

**WHO NEEDS TO ANSWER:** Vrijen (CEO/Founder)

**BLOCKING:** Decision timing for whether to pursue acquisition focus vs. revenue generation focus, team runway considerations, investor expectations.

**NEXT STEP:** Vrijen mentioned working with the recruiter in India to "get some revenue off the ground" — this appears to be the near-term experiment to validate revenue potential.

## Question 2: Marketplace Bootstrap Strategy

**QUESTION:** How will Careerspan successfully bootstrap both sides of its marketplace (Indian talent and US founders) to reach critical mass?

**CONTEXT:** Vrijen acknowledged that marketplace bootstrapping is "a bitch to get like both sides bootstrapped." They have advantages (Shivam's network in India, access to talent who've worked at US companies, Careerspan's tech for validation) but the core marketplace chicken-and-egg challenge remains unresolved. The "meta resume" is suspected as the wedge, but execution path unclear.

**WHO NEEDS TO ANSWER:** Vrijen and Shivam (founders)

**BLOCKING:** Ability to consummate matches and generate revenue, business model viability.

**NEXT STEP:** Vrijen mentioned using the "meta resume" as the suspected wedge product offering.

## Question 4: Making Assessments Legitimate

**QUESTION:** Can any assessment tool truly demonstrate legitimacy and value alignment with business outcomes, or is this an inherently flawed approach?

**CONTEXT:** Both founders expressed deep skepticism about existing assessment tools (HackerRank, etc.). Maximilian argued assessments are hard because you must "convince both parties why your assessment is legitimized" and businesses ultimately care about whether someone "can add value to whatever they're making." Vrijen noted the market for "novel hiring signals" is small due to burned trust from past "snake oil salesmen." The philosophical question of whether assessments can ever be properly grounded in business value was raised but not resolved.

**WHO NEEDS TO ANSWER:** Industry-wide open question — no single owner identified.

**BLOCKING:** Adoption of new assessment methodologies, market receptivity to alternative hiring signals.

**NEXT STEP:** Both seem to be positioning away from pure assessment (Maximilian: portfolio-first, assessment-second; Careerspan: validation + insights) rather than trying to solve the legitimacy question directly.
