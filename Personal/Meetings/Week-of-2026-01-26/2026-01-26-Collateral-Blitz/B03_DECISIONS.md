# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Create 45-60 second demo video instead of deck

**DECISION:** Create a short video demo (45-60 seconds) showcasing Careerspan's data capabilities rather than submitting a deck for outreach.

**CONTEXT:** Ray's feedback indicated that the one-pager was too explanatory and ChatGPT-like. The team recognized that showing the product in action is more impactful than lengthy text explanations. They agreed a video approach would avoid the "rope" feel of trying to explain Careerspan through text.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Logan Currie

**IMPLICATIONS:** Outreach emails will include a direct video link demonstrating the scan results and candidate data, allowing recipients to see the value proposition immediately.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Deck, Gamma page, static screenshots, Loom walkthrough - rejected in favor of tight 45-60 second video demo.

---

## Decision 2: Use scan perspective and real anonymized data

**DECISION:** Demo video should show the employer scan perspective with actual anonymized candidate data from the demo account, not mocked-up results.

**CONTEXT:** Starting from scan perspective shows the core value proposition - dropping in job information and seeing matching results. Using real data (Donna/Donatella example showing profile with vs. without stories) demonstrates actual capabilities and data richness.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Logan Currie

**IMPLICATIONS:** Video will show: (1) drop in job info, (2) brief interlude on how Careerspan works (first-party data input), (3) brief mention of bullshit detector/AI detection, (4) reveal scan results with real candidate profiles showing data depth.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Mocking up results, building Gamma page with scan results, showing candidate experience first - all rejected in favor of scan perspective with real data.

---

## Decision 3: Create two video versions targeting different use cases

**DECISION:** Create two video variants: one for sourcing/matching (external hiring) and one for internal mobility.

**CONTEXT:** Sourcing/matching and internal mobility require different hooks. The sourcing version leverages the Eightfold lawsuit and first-party vs. third-party data angle. Internal mobility version uses the "best hire is who you already have" angle and talent development theme.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Logan Currie

**IMPLICATIONS:** Outreach targets will be classified by use case (internal mobility vs. sourcing/matching) and receive the appropriate video variant. Email copy will be tailored accordingly.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Single video for all use cases, three separate videos - rejected in favor of focused two-version approach.

---

## Decision 4: Vrijen will record the video demos

**DECISION:** Vrijen will record the Loom walkthroughs for both video versions, with Zo's assistance in writing a tight script.

**CONTEXT:** Logan noted that from an acquirer perspective, having the CEO record the walkthrough is better aesthetically and carries more weight. Vrijen has the product knowledge to demonstrate effectively.

**DECIDED BY:** Logan Currie and Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:** Vrijen will work with Zo to write a 45-60 second script, record the demo using the scan results, and produce both video versions.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Logan recording, using Arcade-generated content, finding a third party - rejected in favor of CEO-led walkthrough.

---

## Decision 5: Prioritize sourcing/matching video over internal mobility

**DECISION:** Create the sourcing/matching (external hiring) video first, followed by the internal mobility video second.

**CONTEXT:** The sourcing/matching video is more immediately relevant to the broader target list. Internal mobility targets (Reliance, Tata, etc.) are larger organizations that will take longer to get in anyway.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Logan Currie

**IMPLICATIONS:** Vrijen will focus initial effort on the sourcing/matching video to expedite outreach to the majority of targets on the list.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Doing both simultaneously, starting with internal mobility - rejected in favor of prioritization.

---

## Decision 6: Classify targets by use case for outreach routing

**DECISION:** Segment all outreach targets into two categories - "internal mobility" or "sourcing/matching" - to determine which collateral and video they receive.

**CONTEXT:** Different companies will find different value propositions relevant. Rather than company self-classification, the team will determine targeting strategy based on the company's primary focus.

**DECIDED BY:** Logan Currie and Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:** Outreach tracking sheet will include category field to ensure correct video variant and email copy are sent to each target.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** More granular categorization, company self-categorization - rejected in favor of simple two-category segmentation.

---

## Decision 7: Work allocation for outreach preparation

**DECISION:** Logan will work on identifying additional targets and emailing KBJ for warm intros; Vrijen will focus on video production and email body preparation.

**CONTEXT:** Logan needs to follow up with KBJ (who has connections) and hasn't responded in a week - needs to identify who she would know. Vrijen has capacity for video work and email prep.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Logan Currie

**IMPLICATIONS:** Parallel work streams - Logan expands target list via LinkedIn data export and manages intros; Vrijen produces video and prepares outreach email templates.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Both working on video, both working on targets - rejected in favor of parallel specialization.

---

## Decision 8: Leverage Eightfold lawsuit as hook for sourcing video

**DECISION:** The sourcing/matching video and email copy will reference the Eightfold lawsuit and the importance of first-party vs. third-party data in the current regulatory climate.

**CONTEXT:** The Eightfold lawsuit creates a timely moment to emphasize Careerspan's first-party data approach as a strategic advantage and moat in talent acquisition.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Logan Currie

**IMPLICATIONS:** Email narrative will position Careerspan as the solution for companies relying on third-party data that are now facing regulatory scrutiny.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Generic value proposition, focusing on features without context - rejected in favor of timely, relevant hook.
