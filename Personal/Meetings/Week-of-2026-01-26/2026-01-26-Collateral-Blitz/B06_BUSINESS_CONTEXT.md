# B06: Business Context & Implications

## Market Opportunity

The Eightfold lawsuit has created a strategic window for Careerspan to position first-party candidate data as a defensible competitive moat. Companies relying on scraped or publicly harvested talent data face increasing legal and reputational risk, creating demand for opted-in, legally-sourced candidate intelligence. Careerspan has demonstrated market validation with 4,000+ users and 2,000+ recorded candidate stories, proving both adoption and data scale.

## Competitive Position

Careerspan is uniquely positioned as a "human data company" that transforms candidate conversations into structured data through its first-party platform. The core differentiation is explicit, opted-in data collection versus the third-party scraping approaches used by competitors. This becomes strategically valuable as scrutiny around publicly scraped data increases. The platform demonstrates dual value: deep candidate intelligence (richer profiles including stories, assessments, verified information) and practical utility (scanning capabilities against talent pools).

## Strategic Direction

The company is pursuing a targeted outreach strategy to potential acquirers and strategic partners, categorized by primary use case: **sourcing/matching/hiring** (LinkedIn, Workday, Handshake, Dover) versus **internal mobility** (Reliance, Tata). The Eightfold lawsuit timing is being leveraged as an external catalyst—using the "moment in time" to emphasize the defensibility of first-party data approaches. The outreach material is being simplified dramatically: shifting from lengthy explanatory decks to concise 45-60 second demo videos that show rather than tell.

## Commercial Model

The go-to-market approach for this outreach phase prioritizes efficiency and clarity. Rather than sending comprehensive pitch decks explaining what Careerspan does, the strategy is to send a brief email hooking on the first-party data opportunity, paired with a demo video that demonstrates value in under a minute. This reduces the friction of comprehension—the recipient can immediately see the kind of data Careerspan captures and its utility. The commercial thesis is that this data becomes a talent acquisition moat, whether for external hiring or internal mobility use cases.

## Implications & Next Steps

The current outreach effort represents a pivot in communication strategy—prioritizing visceral demonstration over explanatory text. This reflects recognition that the biggest barrier has been helping prospects understand what Careerspan actually does and why it matters. By creating two demo variations (sourcing/matching vs internal mobility), the company can tailor messaging to each prospect's primary use case. The immediate next actions are: (1) Vrijen producing the sourcing/matching demo video first (higher priority) using a tight script, (2) Logan compiling target lists and identifying warm introductions, (3) deploying the outreach to categorized prospects. Success will be measured by engagement and response rates to this simplified, video-first approach.
