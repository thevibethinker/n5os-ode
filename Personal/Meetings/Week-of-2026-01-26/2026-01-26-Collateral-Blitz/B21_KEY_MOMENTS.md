# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"The problem with our existing materials I think is that it takes a fuck ton of word space to explain what it is that we do and why it's relevant to the people."**  
— Logan Currie  
*Context:* Identifying that their current outreach materials are too wordy and struggle to communicate what they do clearly. This explains why they're pivoting to a visual demo approach instead of text-heavy decks.

**"So we go to people where they would benefit from the data and we say, you're working off a third party. Fuck third party work. First party."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Outlining a clear positioning strategy—leveraging the Eightfold lawsuit controversy to position first-party data as the superior alternative to third-party scraping. This becomes their core outreach angle for the sourcing/matching segment.

**"The best hire is who you already have."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Establishing the strategic hook for the internal mobility use case. Simple, memorable positioning that reframes internal talent development as competitive advantage.

**"I don't think we should submit the deck. I think what we should do is just the video of... watching it in action."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Decision to abandon the presentation deck format in favor of a 45-60 second demo video. Recognizes that showing product capability is more compelling than explaining it, especially given their existing materials are already too wordy.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"Why don't we make like a three minute or even a fucking screenshot or something like that where it's like a 30 second look at the information that we capture via careerspan?"**  
— Logan Currie  
*Significance:* This question reframed their entire outreach approach from text-heavy explanations to visual proof points. It led to the decision to create demo videos that show the product in action rather than trying to describe it.

**"What are you going to get out of this?"**  
— [Implicit question from analyzing Ray's email]  
*Significance:* Logan and Vrijen identified that successful outreach should lead with quantifiable value, not just describe the product. This became a template for how they structure their own emails—lead with the "what's in it for you" rather than explaining what the product is.

**"For internal mobility, the Eightfold thing isn't as relevant."**  
— Logan Currie  
*Significance:* This question revealed that their core positioning angle (third-party vs. first-party data controversy) doesn't work for all use cases. It forced them to recognize they need two distinct outreach strategies—one for sourcing/matching (Eightfold angle) and one for internal mobility (traditional "best hire is who you already have" angle).

**"I don't have. What is it that Shivam is seeing?"**  
— Logan Currie  
*Significance:* While seemingly operational, this question about what data to actually show in the demo surfaced an important insight—they need to decide between showing the employer view (what companies can see) versus demonstrating the scanning capability. They ultimately decided to focus on the scan results as the more compelling demo.
