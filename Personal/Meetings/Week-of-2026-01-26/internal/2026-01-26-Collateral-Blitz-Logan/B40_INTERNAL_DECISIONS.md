## Outreach Strategy Overhaul

### D1: Replace text-heavy collateral with short demo video
**Decision:** Stop sending explanatory decks/one-pagers. Instead, lead with a sub-60-second video walkthrough showing Careerspan in action.

**Context:** Ray's feedback on the existing one-pager was that it was "too explaining and very ChatGPT." Logan identified the core problem: *"so much of the uphill battle that we've always faced is showing helping people figure out what the fuck it is that we do."* A video collapses that explanation gap instantly.

**Rationale:**
- Text-based materials require too much word space to explain what Careerspan does
- Known quantities like Shift Up can skip explanation; Careerspan cannot — so the video must do that heavy lifting visually
- > "I honestly think that we can do this, like if we say like in the email, take 45 seconds and watch what our technology can do... a deck is not as impactful as like watching it in action." — Logan

**Owner:** V (records the video), Logan (workshops the script)

**Decision:** The sourcing/hiring outreach email hooks on the Eightfold publicly-scraped-data lawsuit as a timely reason to reach out, then positions Careerspan's first-party, opted-in data as the alternative.

### D4: Video structure — scan-first, not candidate-first
**Decision:** Lead the demo with the employer/scan view, not the candidate experience. Show a JD scan running against the talent pool, then briefly show the candidate input side as supporting proof of first-party collection.

**Rationale:**
- > "What we often default to because we're just so used to it is looking at the candidate experience and everyone's like, fuck, that's not relevant to me." — Logan
- Starting with the scan shows immediate value to an acquirer/buyer
- The candidate side is shown as a brief interlude to reinforce the first-party data point and mention the BS detector (AI-generated input detection)

**Agreed structure:**
1. Open with scan — drop in a JD, show results against the pool
2. Brief interlude: how data gets in (candidate input, first-party, BS detector mention)
3. Return to results — show matched candidates with data depth
4. Close: "This is the future. We're up for sale. Let us know if you want to talk."
