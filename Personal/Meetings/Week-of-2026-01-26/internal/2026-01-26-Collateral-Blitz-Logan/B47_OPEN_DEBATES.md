## Unresolved Tensions & Areas of Disagreement

### 1. Video vs. Deck as Primary Outreach Asset

**Status:** Resolved in favor of video, but execution details unresolved

V initially suggested mocking up a page with scan results via Gamma API — "I could use this and my Gamma API just throw up a really basic... page." Logan pushed back toward a live walkthrough video instead: *"I honestly think that we can do this... a deck is not as impactful as like watching it in action."*

V conceded: *"I don't think we should submit the deck."*

**Still open:** The exact format (Loom walkthrough vs. Arcade recording vs. hybrid) hasn't been locked. Logan suggested Arcade initially, then pivoted to recommending V do a Loom-style CEO walkthrough for credibility — *"from an acquirer perspective, potentially having you do the loom walkthrough would be better... the man of it all."* No final decision on tooling.

### 2. Who Should Present in the Video

**Tension:** Logan advocated for V as the face — *"I think it's a CEO... I'm not trying to get out of doing it, but I'm thinking you have all of this."* V didn't push back but didn't enthusiastically claim ownership either. The implicit tension: Logan has more product demo fluency, but V carries more CEO credibility for an acquisition audience.

**Unresolved:** No explicit agreement on whether this is definitively V's deliverable or could be a collaborative recording.

### 3. How to Handle the "Explanation Problem"

**Core tension:** The team agrees their materials over-explain what Careerspan does — *"the intrinsic problem with even trying to generate it that way or trying to put anything in text is the moment we try to explain, it just has to come off as like a rope on some level."* Ray's feedback confirmed this: too much explaining, too ChatGPT-sounding.

**Unresolved:** They've agreed on a "show don't tell" video approach, but haven't resolved how the **email body itself** avoids the same trap. V mentioned *"getting the email body ready"* but no one addressed how the written copy stays tight when the product still requires context to understand.

### 4. Internal Mobility Hook — Undefined

**Status:** Explicitly deferred but conceptually fuzzy

The sourcing/matching video has a clear hook (Eightfold lawsuit, first-party vs. third-party data moment). The internal mobility version does not. V offered: *"the best hire is who you already have"* and Logan riffed on a *"doomsday around how difficult hiring is"* angle, but neither committed to a specific framing.

Logan acknowledged: *"for internal mobility, the Eightfold thing isn't as relevant"* — meaning the strongest market catalyst doesn't apply to half their target list.

**Open question:** What is the compelling "why now" for internal mobility buyers?

### 5. Shivam's Role in the Collateral

**Tension:** V suggested having Shivam record a 30-second voice message or testimonial to attach — *"ask him to record like a 30 second voice message that we can attach along with his profile or Corridor X."* This was floated but neither confirmed nor rejected. The conversation moved on without resolution.

**Open question:** Does third-party validation from a pipeline partner strengthen or complicate the acquisition pitch?

### 6. Prioritization of Target List Categorization

Logan started live-categorizing companies (internal mobility vs. sourcing/matching) but the exercise was interrupted repeatedly and left incomplete. Several companies were categorized, but:

- **Udemy** — *"I have no idea"* (uncategorized)
- **Dover** — Dismissed as *"cool, right? They don't really..."* but not formally deprioritized
- **Humanly** — Ownership unclear (*"I feel like that was yours. No, that feels like that was mine."*)
- Multiple unnamed companies Logan wants to cross-check via LinkedIn data export — scope undefined

**Unresolved:** No systematic framework for deciding which companies get which version of outreach. Currently ad hoc.

### 7. Two Versions vs. One Core Video

There's an implicit tension between efficiency and specificity. They agreed on two video versions (sourcing/matching + internal mobility) but the sourcing one was prioritized: *"just make that one first and then we can do the internal mobility one second."*

**Risk:** The internal mobility version may never get made if the sourcing version consumes available time before V's travel. V noted: *"I'll have it skipped tomorrow so I'll have more time. I'll just have this ramp call."*
