# B21: Key Moments

## 1. The "Baller Move" Insight (~6:00)
> **Logan**: "It's like kind of a baller move to put the lead list of workforce boards in. That's kind of like just giving value up front."

Springboard's approach analyzed — leads to question of what Careerspan's equivalent "value up front" move could be.

## 2. The Core Problem Articulated (~7:30)
> **Logan**: "The problem with our existing materials is that it takes a fuck ton of word space to explain what it is that we do and why it's relevant."

This frustration became the creative constraint that drove the video solution.

## 3. The "Show Don't Tell" Breakthrough (~8:00)
> **Logan**: "Can we make like a 30 second look at the information that we capture via Careerspan? Because if that's in an email... they don't have to wrap their heads around what it is we're describing. It's just like 'oh shit, I want that information.'"

The strategic pivot from explanation to demonstration.

## 4. First-Party Data Positioning (~8:30)
> **Logan**: "Boom, mic drop for the hiring people. This is opted in candidate data... as things heat up with publicly scraped data, this is a really valuable strategy for you guys."

The Eightfold lawsuit context transformed into a sales hook.

## 5. Video Over Deck (~16:00)
> **Logan**: "I'm thinking a deck is not as impactful as watching it in action."
> **V**: "Yeah, I don't think we should submit the deck."

Decision point that shaped the entire collateral strategy.

## 6. "Human Data Company" Framing (~12:30)
> **V**: "So the demo is like human data. Like it's a human data oriented demo."

Positioning crystallized — Careerspan sells human data, not software.

## 7. The Simplification (~18:00)
> **V**: "Why not just pretend the results in a demonstrator account are the results of a scan?"
> **V**: "Fuck all of that. Let's just do that."

Cutting through complexity to the simplest viable approach.
