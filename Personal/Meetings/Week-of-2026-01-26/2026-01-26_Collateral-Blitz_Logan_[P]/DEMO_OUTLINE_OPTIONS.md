# Demo Video Outline Options (v3)

## Synthesis: What the Demo Must Convey

From your voice reflections + the Logan discussion + Ray's feedback + the portal screenshots:

### Core Positioning
**Careerspan = Human Data Company**

The product is access to first-party candidate data — but data that candidates are *willing* to share because the process is meaningful to them.

### The Data Differentiation
What makes this data special isn't that it exists. It's that:
1. **It's unobtainable elsewhere** — "data that even the candidate struggles to articulate, but can with the right human touch"
2. **We've figured out how to extract it via AI** — the human touch at scale
3. **It's opted-in and first-party** — no legal landmines (Eightfold reference)

### The Network Effect
4,000 candidates aren't just a database. They're **networked through a synthetic talent network** — candidates feel they have a greater chance of being hired because they've shared more data over time. This creates:
- A flywheel (more sharing → better matches → more sharing)
- A moat (competitors can't replicate the depth)

### The Visual Proof
The Donna (90) vs Donnatella (62) comparison is the killer moment. Same person. One submitted resume only. One submitted resume + stories. The gulf in insight quality is visible.

Key elements to show:
- **Dealbreakers surfaced first** ("before you waste a call")
- **Elevator pitch** (AI-generated hiring recommendation)
- **Spider chart** (visual profile of candidate qualities)
- **Stories told count** (evidence of engagement depth)
- **Specific metrics** in the summary (35% conversion lift, $400K deal, etc.)

### Principle: Show, Don't Explain
As you narrate about qualitative data, let the visuals do the work. Encourage viewers to pause and review. Let the data quality speak for itself.

## Three Outline Options

### Option A: "The Twin Reveal" (Curiosity → Surprise → Desire)

**Concept**: Open with a mystery — two candidates who look different, then reveal they're the same person with different data depth.

**Flow**:
1. **Open on candidate list** — Show Donna (90) and Donnatella (62) side by side
2. **Read both summaries** — One sounds like a clear hire, one sounds like a maybe
3. **The reveal** — "Same person. One submitted a resume. One submitted stories."
4. **Dive into Donna's profile** — Dealbreakers, elevator pitch, spider chart, the richness
5. **Pull back** — "This is 4,000 candidates. Our network reaches [X]."
6. **Close** — "The difference isn't the candidate. It's the data."

**Why it works**: The twin reveal creates a memorable "aha" moment. The contrast does the persuading — no argument needed. Viewers remember surprises.

**Risk**: Requires setting up the reveal cleanly. If it's not clear they're the same person, it falls flat.

---

### Option B: "The Data You Can't Get" (Problem → Solution → Proof)

**Concept**: Start with what employers desperately want to know, then show that Careerspan captures exactly that.

**Flow**:
1. **Open with the problem** — "What if you could know how candidates actually think, behave, and work with others — before the interview?"
2. **Show a profile** — Donna's full view with all the qualitative richness
3. **Highlight specific insights** — "This candidate shifted an entire company's data strategy, but you couldn't tell from her resume. Here's how we know that."
4. **The mechanism (brief)** — "Candidates share this because it helps them. We make the process meaningful."
5. **The contrast** — Flash Donnatella's resume-only profile. "This is what you'd get from a job board."
6. **Scale** — "4,000 candidates opted in. Our partner network reaches [X]."
7. **Close** — "Data that even candidates struggle to articulate — extracted through AI."

**Why it works**: Leads with the desire (deep candidate insight), then delivers on it. More explanatory but still visual-forward.

**Risk**: More voiceover-heavy. Could feel like a pitch if not executed well.

---

### Option C: "The Future of Talent Networks" (Vision → Proof → Invitation) ⭐ RECOMMENDED

**Concept**: Position Careerspan as what talent acquisition becomes — a world where candidates want to share, and matching finally works at scale.

**Flow**:
1. **Open with the broken system** — "Hiring is broken because we can't match at scale. Resumes barely told us who someone was before — now they're utterly broken. No amount of scraped data fixes this, especially when companies like Eightfold are being sued to an inch of their life."
2. **The adversarial flip** — "But this whole time, we've been adversarial to candidates. What if they *wanted* to share data with us? What if they regularly logged back in because it actually helped them?"
3. **Introduce Careerspan** — "That's what Careerspan is."
4. **Show the employer view** — "The cognitive puzzle-solving you do when reviewing a resume? Done for you by AI — but with far more context. Information comes pre-digested. You still have access to all the raw data, but now you have framing. Context. Straight from the horse's mouth."
5. **Brief candidate side** — Quick glimpse: "This is what candidates see. They can talk, they can type. It's natural. But it's designed to get to know them on a deep level."
6. **The comparison** — "How much of a difference does this make?" Show Donna vs Donnatella.
7. **Scale proof** — "4,000 users. Over 1,000 hours of stories logged. Active partnerships and LOIs that could scale us to 40,000 within the year. We're embedded in these communities. Ready to activate."
8. **The ask** — "A talented team, just out of runway, looking for a home for a very promising product."

**Why it works**: 
- Leads with a truth everyone feels (hiring is broken)
- Reframes the entire relationship (adversarial → collaborative)
- Shows both sides of the marketplace briefly
- Donna/Donnatella proves the difference
- Ends with clear scale potential and an honest ask

**Risk**: Longer arc, needs tight execution to stay under 60-90 seconds. The "out of runway" honesty is a choice — could be disarming or could raise concerns.
