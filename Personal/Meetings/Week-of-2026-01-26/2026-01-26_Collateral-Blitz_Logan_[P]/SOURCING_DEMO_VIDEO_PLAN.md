# Sourcing/Matching Demo Video Plan

## Purpose
45-60 second demo video showing Careerspan's scan functionality to support email outreach for the sourcing/matching use case.

---

## Strategic Context

### Why Video (Not Deck)
Logan's insight: "A deck is not as impactful as watching it in action."

The problem with existing materials: too much word space explaining what Careerspan does. Video eliminates this — recipients see results and think "oh shit, I want that information."

### Core Positioning
**Careerspan = Human Data Company**

You're not selling software or a platform. You're selling access to opted-in, first-party candidate data that recruiters cannot get elsewhere.

### The Scale Story
The 4,000 candidates visible in the demo are a **microcosm** of Careerspan's true reach.

Through official and unofficial community partnerships, Careerspan is embedded in networks that extend far beyond the demo pool. The total **catchment area** — candidates reachable through existing relationships — is the real asset.

This echoes Ray's "baller move": he led with 60+ workforce board relationships as the asset. Your equivalent: "This is 4,000. Here's the total reach we can activate."

### Traction Proof
- **2,000+ AI conversations** — validates the capture mechanism works; candidates actually engage
- **Strong retention rates** — organic demand and persistent usage, not one-and-done

These metrics answer the skeptic's question: "Is this real or theoretical?" The answer: people use it, they come back, the data grows.

*Note: Traction proof is better suited for email copy than the 45-second video. Video shows the output; email can layer in the credibility signals.*

---

## What Ray's Example Taught You

Ray's successful acquisition email to Springboard (his "baller move"):
- **Led with assets, not explanation** — workforce board approvals, partner relationships, lead list
- **Gave value immediately** — shared a 60+ workforce board lead list upfront
- **Framed around buyer's interests** — "If Springboard may be interested to tap that funding pool..."

Ray's feedback on your previous doc: "reads super obviously ChatGPT-generated" — needs human voice and specificity.

**Apply to demo**: Don't explain what Careerspan is. Show results that make them want what you have.

---

## Demo Structure (45-60 sec)

### Beat 1: The Scan (~10 sec)
Show initiating a scan:
- Drop in a job description
- Click to scan
- *No explanation needed — let the action speak*

### Beat 2: The Data Source (Optional Brief Interlude ~10-15 sec)
Quick glimpse of HOW this first-party data is collected:
- Candidate inputting their information (opted-in, voluntary)
- This is the differentiator — not scraped, not third-party

**Decision**: You and Logan debated whether to include this. The consensus: show it briefly but don't dwell. The results are the star.

### Beat 3: The Results (~25-35 sec)
This is the money shot. Show:
- Matched candidates appearing
- Key data points visible (skills, experience, whatever makes them go "oh shit")
- Quality/depth of information that demonstrates first-party richness

Use real (anonymized) scan results from the demonstrator account.

## Recording Approach

### Tool
Arcade (screen recording with polish)

### Setup
- Use demonstrator account with real anonymized data
- Have a relevant JD ready to drop in
- Results should already be loaded (V mentioned having recent scan results)

### Voiceover
Work with Zo on a tight 45-second script. Keep it:
- Short sentences
- Human voice (not ChatGPT-y)
- Benefit-focused, not feature-focused
- "This is what you get" not "This is what we do"

---

## Messaging Framework for Script

### Opening Hook (2-3 sec)
Something that acknowledges the moment: regulatory pressure on data sourcing, or the pain of finding quality candidates.

### Scan Action (minimal)
"Drop in a JD. Run a scan."

### Data Differentiator (brief)
"Every candidate opted in. First-party data you can't get anywhere else."

### Scale Proof (the kicker)
"This is 4,000. Our partner network reaches [X]."

*Calculate total catchment area from partnership communities before recording.*

### Results (let visuals carry)
"This is what comes back."

### Close/CTA
"Want to see your own results?"

## Proof Points Needed

Each argument needs backing. Here's what to gather:

### Scale & Reach

| Argument | Proof Point Needed | Source |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| "4,000 is a microcosm" | **Total catchment area** — sum of all reachable candidates through partnerships | Internal: enumerate partnerships × estimated reach per partner |
| "Partner network reaches [X]" | **Concrete number** for script | Calculation: workforce board populations, community sizes, partner org membership counts |
| "Embedded in communities" | **List of named partnerships** (official + unofficial) | Internal: partnership inventory |

### Traction & Engagement

| Argument | Proof Point Needed | Source |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| "2,000+ AI conversations" | **Exact conversation count** (current) | Internal: product analytics |
| "Validates capture mechanism" | **Completion rate** — % of conversations that result in usable candidate profiles | Internal: funnel metrics |
| "Strong retention rates" | **Specific retention %** — DAU/MAU, return visit rate, or re-engagement rate | Internal: product analytics |
| "Organic demand" | **Acquisition source breakdown** — % organic vs. paid/referred | Internal: attribution data |
| "Persistent usage" | **Session frequency or depth** — avg sessions/user, avg conversation length | Internal: product analytics |

### Data Quality & Differentiation

| Argument | Proof Point Needed | Source |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| "First-party data you can't get elsewhere" | **Comparison stats** — what Careerspan captures vs. what scraped sources get | Internal: field-by-field comparison |
| "Opted-in, voluntary" | **Consent rate or opt-in %** | Internal: signup/consent funnel |
| "Quality/depth of information" | **Data completeness score** — avg fields filled per profile, or % of profiles with [key field] | Internal: data quality audit |
| "Eightfold lawsuit / regulatory pressure" | **External citations** — lawsuit details, CCPA/GDPR enforcement actions, regulatory trends | External: news articles, legal filings |

### Market Context (for email copy)

| Argument | Proof Point Needed | Source |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| "Regulatory pressure on data sourcing" | **Recent enforcement examples** — fines, lawsuits, policy changes | External: research (news, legal) |
| "Pain of finding quality candidates" | **Industry stats** — time-to-fill, cost-per-hire, quality-of-hire benchmarks | External: SHRM, LinkedIn reports, recruiting industry data |
| "Scraped data risks" | **Eightfold case specifics** + other examples | External: legal research |
