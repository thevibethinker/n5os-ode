# Sourcing Demo Script — DRAFT

**Target length**: 60-70 seconds\
**Structure**: Option C — Future of Talent Networks\
**Voice**: V (founder, direct, no bullshit)

---

## Script

### Beat 1: The Problem (0-12s)

**Visual**: Minimal — text on dark background, or just you speaking to camera

> Hiring is broken. And it's broken because we can't match at scale.
>
> Resumes barely told us who someone was before — now with AI, no amount of scraped data can overcome the signal distortion, especially when companies like Eightfold are being sued to an inch of their life for relying on it.

---

### Beat 2: The Flip (12-24s)

**Visual**: Transition — could be a simple cut or zoom

> But here's the thing — this whole time, we've been adversarial to candidates.
>
> What if they *wanted* to share data with us? What if they regularly logged back in because they knew doing so actually helped them?
>
> That's what Careerspan is. And it works — 20% of our users come back within 30 days. 45% share three or more stories with our AI.

---

### Beat 3: The Product — Employer View (22-40s)

**Visual**: Cut to employer portal — candidate list with scores visible (90, 62, 42, 75)

> Careerspan does the cognitive puzzle-solving you have to do when you're reviewing someone's resume — but with far more context than you have.
>
> Altouhgh the insights come pre-digested, you still have access to all the original data the candidate provided, but now you have framing. Context. Straight from the horse's mouth.

**Visual**: Click into Donna's profile — show dealbreakers, elevator pitch, spider chart

> Dealbreakers surface first — before you waste a call. You get an elevator pitch synthesized from everything they've shared. Strengths, weaknesses, a full picture.

---

### Beat 4: The Candidate Side — Brief (40-48s)

**Visual**: Quick glimpse of candidate interface — talk or type

> This is what they see. They can talk, they can type. It's a natural process. But it's designed to get to know them on a deep level — because that's all you need to match at scale.

---

### Beat 5: The Proof — Donna vs Donnatella (48-58s)

**Visual**: Side by side comparison or toggle between Donna (90) and Donnatella (62)

> How much of a difference does this make?
>
> These are the same person. One submitted a resume. One shared stories.
>
> Look at the gulf. *\[pause — let the visual speak\]*

---

### Beat 6: Scale (58-68s)
**[Visual: Pull back to candidate list — show volume]**

> 4,000 users. Over a thousand hours of stories logged. Active partnerships that could scale us to 40,000 within the year.
>
> D30 retention: 20%. D90: 9%. And we're seeing 1-3% organic return — with almost no lifecycle marketing. People come back because they want to.

---

### Beat 7: Close (68-75s)
**[Visual: Hold on candidate list or fade to Careerspan logo]**

> The next moat in hiring isn't more data. It's better data — data that's only been shared with you. That's Careerspan, and the power of our approach.

---

### Beat 8: The Ask (75-82s)
**[Visual: Contact info or simple end card]**

> Our team is fielding acquisition offers through February 14th. If you want Careerspan in your portfolio, this is your window.

## Recording Notes

1. **Pace**: Conversational, not rushed. Let moments land.
2. **Donna/Donnatella**: The pause after "Look at the gulf" is important — let viewers see it.
3. **The flip**: "What if they *wanted* to share" should have emphasis on "wanted."
4. **Candidate glimpse**: Keep this tight — 8 seconds max. It's supporting, not starring.
5. **Numbers**: If you don't have exact figures, round confidently. "About 4,000" is fine.
