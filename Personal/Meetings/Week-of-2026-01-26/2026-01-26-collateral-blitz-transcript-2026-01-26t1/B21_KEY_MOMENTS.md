**"What is the equivalent of that would be for us?"**  
— Logan Currie  
*Significance:* This question drives the entire pivot strategy. Instead of copying Springboard's approach directly, they needed to find Careerspan's equivalent "value upfront" move. This leads to the 30-second demo idea.

**"Do we just throw in our numerical proof points and hope that just the we're collecting the data, we have this volume like the 4000 user volume at least prove and like 2000 stories or whatever recorded at least proves out that like people will use this at scale?"**

*Significance:* Testing whether quantitative metrics alone are sufficient or if visual demonstration is better. The answer (go with video) shapes the entire outreach approach.

**"Is there a visceral way of communicating that proof point or do we just say, do we just throw in our numerical proof points?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* The key decision point between different communication approaches. Choosing visceral demonstration over metrics is the strategic pivot that defines the new outreach.

**"Why not just pretend like the results in a demonstrator account are the results of a scan?"**

*Significance:* A practical solution that simplifies execution. Instead of building complex demos, they can use existing accounts. This reduces development time while still demonstrating value effectively.
