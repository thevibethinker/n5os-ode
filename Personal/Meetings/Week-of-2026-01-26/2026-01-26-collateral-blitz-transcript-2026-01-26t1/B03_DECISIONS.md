# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Position Careerspan as First-Party Data Solution

**DECISION:** Outreach will position Careerspan as a first-party data solution versus competitors relying on third-party data, using the Eightfold lawsuit as a timely hook.

**CONTEXT:** The Ray email example showed the value of being concise and leveraging context. The Eightfold lawsuit creates urgency around publicly scraped data, providing a timely market opportunity.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen and Logan (joint decision)

**IMPLICATIONS:** All outreach emails will use the "first-party vs third-party" framing. The Eightfold legal context becomes the primary "moment in time" angle for external outreach.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Generic value proposition or detailed product explanations (rejected as too wordy)

## Decision 2: Replace Decks with Short Video Demos

**DECISION:** Abandon deck submissions in favor of short (45-60 second) video walkthroughs that demonstrate Careerspan's technology in action.

**CONTEXT:** Decks require too much explanation to convey what Careerspan does. A 30-45 second visual demo is more impactful and helps prospects immediately understand the value proposition.

**DECIDED BY:** Logan proposed, Vrijen agreed

**IMPLICATIONS:** No more decks for initial outreach. All collateral will include a link to a demo video with clear time expectations ("take 45 seconds").

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Static decks, screenshots, or detailed written descriptions

## Decision 3: Demo Focus: Scan Perspective Over Candidate Experience

**DECISION:** Demo videos will focus on the employer's scan perspective (how they can search and filter a talent pipeline) rather than the candidate input experience.

**CONTEXT:** Employers don't care about the candidate UX; they care about what they get access to. Showing the scan in action demonstrates the product value immediately to acquirers.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen and Logan (joint decision)

**IMPLICATIONS:** Videos will start with "you're building a synthetic talent pipeline that you can scan at any moment" and show scan results. Candidate input shown only briefly to reinforce first-party data nature.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Starting with candidate story submission or full candidate experience walkthrough

## Decision 4: Create Two Video Versions, Prioritize Sourcing/Matching

**DECISION:** Create two versions of the demo video—one for sourcing/matching/hiring, one for internal mobility—prioritizing the sourcing/matching version first.

**CONTEXT:** Different use cases require different hooks. Sourcing/matching can leverage the Eightfold/first-party angle; internal mobility needs a different framing ("best hire is who you already have").

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen and Logan (joint decision)

**IMPLICATIONS:** Vrijen will create sourcing/matching video first using a Loom walkthrough with Zo-generated script. Internal mobility video will be created second.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Single video for all use cases or prioritizing internal mobility first

## Decision 5: Categorize Outreach Targets by Use Case

**DECISION:** Segment target companies into two tracks: "internal mobility" vs "sourcing/matching/hiring" and route appropriate video/copy accordingly.

**CONTEXT:** Different companies align with different value propositions. Ray, Reliance, Tata, and others map to internal mobility; LinkedIn, Workday, Handshake map to sourcing/matching.

**DECIDED BY:** Logan (with Vrijen's agreement)

**IMPLICATIONS:** Outreach spreadsheet will tag each company by use case type to ensure they receive the appropriate collateral version.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** One-size-fits-all approach or more granular segmentation
