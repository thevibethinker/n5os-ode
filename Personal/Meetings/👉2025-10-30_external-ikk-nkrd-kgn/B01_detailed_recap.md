# Meeting Recap: Zo Growth Strategy & November 5th Event Planning

**Date:** October 30, 2025  
**Participants:** Vrijen (Career coach/Zo advocate), Zo team member  
**Duration:** ~37 minutes  
**Purpose:** Strategic discussion on go-to-market approach and event planning

## Executive Summary
This strategic conversation focused on three main areas: (1) competitive positioning of Zo against automation platforms like Zapier and N8N, (2) clarifying the go-to-market strategy between demand-side (founder outreach) and supply-side (recipe incentivization) tactics, and (3) detailed planning for a November 5th founder event in New York to drive user acquisition and validation.

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Product Positioning & Competitive Landscape
**Problem:** Users frequently compare Zo to Zapier and N8N. Both have trade-offs:
- **Zapier:** Feels easy but is limited; constraining linear workflows
- **N8N:** More powerful but requires technical setup and ongoing maintenance
- **Zo's advantage:** More powerful than both but currently less intuitive; supports complex agentic workflows within a unified system

**Key insight from Vrijen:** Zo excels when used with natural language principles rather than hard-coded workflows. Users expect agentic behavior (checking knowledge bases, validating preferences, adapting dynamically) which is Zo's strength vs. rigid automation tools.

**Barrier to adoption:** Even in early-stage Zo, users struggle with:
- Reliability concerns (stochastic technology driving deterministic results)
- File system issues and common operational friction
- Steep learning curve despite being easier than N8N
- Switching costs from existing automation investments

### 2. Go-to-Market Strategy
**Supply-side vs. Demand-side tension:**
- **Demand-side (Vrijen's focus):** Events with founders, direct relationship building, validation exercises
- **Supply-side (Zo team's consideration):** Building recipes at scale that incentivize users to create solutions

**Resolution:** Both needed. Priority is growing upper funnel (new users), not re-engaging existing small user base.

**Key question on recipes:** Generic recipes have limited compatibility with customized Zo environments. Need to resolve whether focus should be on recipe library or pre-configured Zo starter edition.

### 3. Infrastructure & Product Gap
**Hypothesis:** Zo needs a "starter layer" – a pre-built, optimized Zo environment:
- Lower difficulty gradient for new users
- Pre-baked recipes tightly integrated with the system
- Better demo environment showcasing Zo's agentic capabilities
- Similar to Vrijen's N5OS concept (flashable GitHub base layer)

**Why this matters:** The agentic behavior (autonomously checking systems, validating preferences) is the wow factor – not recipes. Pre-configured environment enables better demonstrations.
