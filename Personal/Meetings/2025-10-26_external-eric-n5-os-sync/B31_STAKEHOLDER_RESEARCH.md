### 2. Context switching cost for ADHD users isn't about distraction - it's about cognitive state reconstruction complexity

**Evidence:** "That's such a high cost because I'll be like fuck. Where am I at again now? And I just had a meeting and new ideas were generated from that meeting. But then they're also like inspiration for what I'm building right now."

**Why it matters:** Reframes the ADHD productivity problem from "how do we prevent distraction" to "how do we accelerate context recovery," which suggests completely different product interventions (state snapshots vs. blocking apps).

### 3. Multi-surface information coordination creates compounding context loss beyond simple task switching

**Evidence:** "The challenge that we're thinking about is like... surfaces that you work in and how multiple of those surfaces require both the context and the state and to be merged into those surfaces. For example, if you're on a team and you're working on like your product manager, you're working on a pricing spec doc and that pricing spec doc... once the changes are done there, you also need to update like a JIRA ticket and submit that through to your team and let them... updates and send an email out to your... director or like the sales team."

**Why it matters:** Productivity tools optimize for single-surface efficiency (better Slack, better docs, better task manager) but the real cost is cross-surface context maintenance - user must manually reconstruct and propagate context across disconnected tools.
