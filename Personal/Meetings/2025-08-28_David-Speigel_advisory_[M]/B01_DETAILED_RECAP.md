## Strategic Context

### The Core Problem Space
Careerspan operates in a **three-sided network:**
1. **Communities** (product communities, product managers, designers)
2. **Candidates** (early-to-mid career professionals in product/design)
3. **Employers** (companies hiring for product roles)

Currently, community side shows strong engagement ("competitive consumer numbers on a product that isn't trying to be consumer"). The real leverage is connecting communities → candidates → employers.

### Where They Are
- **Product side:** Solid. B2C product has driven user love; job distribution feature has high open/click-through rates
- **Community side:** Strong - direct access to product communities (ProductTank, etc.); user retention good
- **Employer side:** Early signals but unclear buying motion. Vrijen has had positive reception from both HR departments AND founders ("most of them said yes" when offered shortlists), but ICP still "shaky"

### The Strategic Tension
Two potential paths forward:
1. **Two-sided market play** (sell to job seekers AND recruiters/employers) - David referenced LinkedIn as model
2. **Focused B2B play** (nail employer sourcing through community partnerships)

Founders leaning toward focused play because they "want to be incredibly focused and carry out one hypothesis to a strong conclusion" rather than dilute across segments. David's guidance: pick a few things, do them well, experiment, double-down on what gains traction. Don't try to do everything.

### The Competitive Threat
David raised integration risk: "Are you a feature or a standalone piece?" Greenhouse and other ATS platforms could add similar functionality, making Careerspan redundant if not careful. This argues for focusing on **candidate sourcing** (their moat) rather than competing on information/data richness.
