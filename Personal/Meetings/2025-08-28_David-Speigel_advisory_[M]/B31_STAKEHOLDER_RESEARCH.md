## KEY INSIGHTS

### 1. **VC-Backed Startup Hiring Has a "Stack" - And There's an Entry Point**

**Observation:** "If you're working with well funded... if you're looking at PMs then you're talking about tech and if you're talking about tech and you want early stage, then you're looking at VC funded tech startups... After they get a reasonable size funding in terms of building their team, what infrastructure do they need so they can source candidates and hire good people? ...they're already going to be told like, oh, you need Gusto for payments or you need whatever... Greenhouse for starters."

**Implication:** Careerspan's entry point isn't through best product, it's through being on the vendor recommendation list or partnering with existing vendors. VCs essentially "tell" founders which tools to use. There's a playbook for enterprise software distribution.

**Strategic Value:** High. Suggests Careerspan should be networking with VCs + established vendors, not just trying to sell direct to startups. The distribution channel is already defined.

**Data point:** David mentioned he knows these ecosystem dynamics well ("I was talking to V about the bowling pin strategy").

---

### 2. **Recruiting Is a Solved Problem - With Painful Workarounds**

**Observation:** From hiring managers/recruiters: "I don't want better information. The most necessary thing is like better sources of candidates." Also: "Our blood pressure spikes as we see the pile of the ATS buildup."

**Implication:** The market's pain isn't "candidates aren't good enough" or "we need better vetting." It's "we're drowning in applicants from our job posts, and the ATS is a fire hose." The real problem is candidate *quality* through traditional channels, not candidate information.

**Strategic Value:** Medium-High. Reframes Careerspan's value prop from "better intelligence about candidates" to "better pipeline of candidates." Communities are the edge here - they're pre-vetted supply networks.

**Data point:** Multiple hiring managers gave this feedback independently (weeks of conversations).

---

### 3. **Preferred Vendor Lists + Cross-Portfolio Recommendations Are the GTM Engine for B2B SaaS**

**Observation:** "You get into one firm [VC], then you're all their new port codes going forward... and this VC talks to that VC and then like that's [how it spreads]. ...you need to be either on the list or partnered with the one in your category."

**Implication:** Direct sales to individual startups is inefficient. The leverage is getting onto VC preferred vendor lists (or integrating with existing vendors on those lists). Success with one fund enables network effects across their entire portfolio.

**Strategic Value:** High. Suggests a specific GTM channel that David has seen work repeatedly. This is "Bowling Pin Strategy" - nail series A VC firms → spreads to their portfolio companies.

**Data point:** David's experience across multiple VC-backed startups + as PM at public companies gives him visibility into this pattern.

## Systemic Patterns

### Pattern 1: **Traditional Recruiting Channels Are Functionally Broken for Quality**
Every signal points to same issue: job boards, LinkedIn, applicant tracking systems work for volume, not quality. The answer isn't "better information" or "smarter algorithms" - it's "better supply networks." Communities provide this.

### Pattern 2: **Distribution Beats Product in B2B SaaS**
Direct selling to each startup is inefficient. Success comes from getting on VC vendor lists, doing one deal really well, then riding network effects across portfolio. This applies to Careerspan.

### Pattern 3: **Founder Expertise is Siloed**
Logan's expertise: communities, international hiring, marketing, business development. Vrijen's expertise: career coaching, strategy, McKinsey operations. Gap: corporate hiring, budget cycles, enterprise sales. David's value is bridging these gaps.

---

## Implications for Careerspan Strategy

1. **Prioritize VC relationships over direct sales** - Get on vendor lists; grow via network effects
2. **Double-down on community as defensible moat** - Not information, not algorithm; network effects
3. **Validate supply-side value prop first** - "Better candidates" not "better information"
4. **Understand corporate buying cycles** - Budget timing, procurement, preferred vendor lists
5. **Plan for incumbent competition** - Greenhouse will copy; Careerspan's edge is community + supply
