# Key Messaging & Positioning

## Core Message on Financial Health
**"We are operationally disciplined and fiscally prudent during growth phase."**

### Supporting Points:
- Founders taking reduced salaries to manage cash flow
- Methodical approach to contractor management (clean exits, buybacks)
- Detailed financial tracking and reconciliation
- Proactive preparation for Q4 close

## Positioning on Capital Structure
**"We are strategically managing equity and investor relationships."**

### Supporting Points:
- Cleanly exiting early contributors (Brady, Savvy) with equity buybacks
- 90k recent investment demonstrates investor confidence
- Clear cap table management and documentation process

## Internal Messaging on Operational Status
**"We are a lean, coordinated team managing complex operations efficiently."**

### Supporting Points:
- 4-person core team managing finance, operations, and administration
- Clear delegation and accountability
- Effective communication despite geographic distribution
- Strong appreciation for team coordination

## Financial Story to Investors/Stakeholders
**"We are pre-revenue but operationally efficient with strong unit economics focus."**

### Challenges/Candid Points:
- 1k monthly burn with minimal revenue indicates pre-launch phase
- Strong cash management but runway is limited (~2 months)
- Team is committed (personal salary sacrifices) but needs revenue growth

## Messaging on Documentation & Governance
**"We maintain rigorous financial documentation and audit readiness."**

### Areas of Strength:
- Detailed line-item tracking
- Systematic invoice and payment management
- Proactive prepaid expense categorization
- Organized cap table management

### Areas Needing Improvement:
- Outstanding documentation (investment terms, receipts)
- Formalized salary adjustment policy
- Email-based reminders suggesting process could be automated

## Tone & Style Observations
- Professional but collaborative
- Precise on financial details
- Appreciative of team effort
- Time-constrained but thorough
- Process-oriented (wants formality and emails vs. verbal agreements)

## Key Phrases Used
- "Last quarter of 2025" - emphasizes time sensitivity
- "Soft reminder" - polite but firm on documentation needs
- "Money owed" - clear terminology for salary shortfalls
- "Final payout" + "equity payment in one go" - combined transaction language

## Stakeholder Confidence Indicators
- Steady monthly reviews (recurring meeting)
- Investor participation (presumed from financial scrutiny level)
- Clear governance structure (CEO, COO, CFO roles defined)
- Proactive communication

## Risk Messaging/What NOT to Say Externally
- Avoid emphasizing burn rate without revenue context
- Don't highlight undocumented transactions
- Avoid mentioning salary cuts as crisis measure
- Don't focus on liquidity pressure
