# Key Quotes & Insights

## Core Sales Philosophy

**On why CEO must sell:**
> "The CEO has to be one of the top salespeople in any company... Look at every successful CEO, there are shades of that happening. It's just part of the gig."

**On authentic messaging:**
> "You'll be stating a value proposition... You're going to be saying that stuff a lot to everyone you meet... They have that stuff memorized. Even standing in line at the supermarket, someone's like, hey, what do you do? They know how to tune to me."

**The fundamental sales paradox:**
> "People hate the feeling of being sold to, but they love the feeling of buying and shopping... We have email spam filters because of salespeople. We have caller ID. There are billion-dollar institutions designed and built because we hate salespeople. But people want to buy. They love to shop, they love to buy."

## On Agency & Decision-Making

**Why buyers feel different in shopping vs. sales:**
> "On the selling side it's like 'you're manipulating me, I don't like to be manipulated or fooled.' But on the shopping side it's like 'I have agency. It's my thing. I'm getting something for me.'"

## The "Gangster Move" Strategy

**On value demonstration:**
> "Show up with the valid... I'm putting it at the tip of the spear... You're not answering my emails, you're not answering our ads, you're not doing nothing. But I really want to do business with you. Like this is the one that really gets their attention. I just solved your problem."

**The cybersecurity example (powerful analog):**
> "It was around discovering these issues, cybersecurity threats... We could do 40 hours of work within a few keystrokes... I would package that up into an email and be like, oh yeah, here you go. Here's everything you need to go. Here's a freebie... These are threats you don't even see yet. We see them."

**Real-world traction proof:**
> "I sent it to the one guy, it was the product manager... In two hours, it had been opened over a hundred times by twenty different people in the company... He emails me back after 5:00 and he goes, 'can we talk tomorrow?'... That was it, the sales pitch is over. He was like, okay I get it."

**Why the Gangster Move works:**
> "The fun is the sales pitch is done... It becomes okay we get it. Can you show me real time on the screen, what you're looking at? And then it becomes okay what's payment, what are packages like? The sales pitch is literally two seconds and then it's done."

## On Sales Timing & Confidence

**On recognizing buyer signals:**
> "As soon as you recognize that they're kind of understanding and framing your company and your offering and they're not rejecting it. You can go for the hill right there... You can just say hey I want you to check it out. Even if you've known the person like two minutes."

**Ilya's reflection on his own journey:**
> "I have to consciously do it... I don't walk in there thinking who am I selling something to? I have to consciously do it... Find a way to just make it a habit."

## Marketing Lesson

**On what actually drives clicks/conversion:**
> "We had a marketing campaign for a product many years ago, just saying it's new. Was the most successful campaign. We had all kinds, we were like oh yeah this is deep machine learning and neural networks... It's new with a little star on. There was the one that just got the most clicks."

## Positioning Refinement

**What NOT to say:**
> "If you just want another channel that's going to get you a thousand more resumes for each role, we ain't it... I'll just tell you that right now. We're not that."

**What TO say:**
> "What we are going to do is give you this absolute vision into these candidates that no one else can provide... This level of vision, this level of evaluation."

**On future messaging:**
> "No false starts. Get it right the first time... WYSIWYG—what you see is what you get. With the person that you're trying to hire... And it should flip both ways: what you see is what you get with a company."
