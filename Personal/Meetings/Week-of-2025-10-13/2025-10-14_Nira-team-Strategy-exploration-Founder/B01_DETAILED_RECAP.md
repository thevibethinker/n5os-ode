# B01: Detailed Recap

## Overview
This meeting served as a strategic alignment session between Vrijen (V), Logan, and Fei, the founder of Nira. The conversation marked a significant pivot for Careerspan, moving away from a traditional VC-backed scaling model toward a community-driven, influencer-led approach. Simultaneously, Fei provided an update on Nira's progress, its upcoming seed round, and its vision as an "AI operating system for professional networks."

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Careerspan's Strategic Pivot
- **Shift in Business Model**: V and Logan explained that they are moving Careerspan away from a traditional company structure. They have decided not to charge job seekers and lack interest in selling to employers.
- **Community Focus**: The new strategy focuses on collaborating with communities (e.g., alumni associations like Cornell and Emory) where incentives align for high-touch, low-spam engagement.
- **Brand Building**: V and Logan intend to develop their personal brands and influence around AI and careers to fund the product, keeping it free for job seekers.
- **Synergy with Communities**: V noted that communities want expertise and engagement (workshops/stimulated activity) without having to manage it themselves, which fits Careerspan's new direction.

### 2. Nira's Progress and Vision
- **Current State**: Nira is currently a team of six (mostly engineers) based in San Francisco. They have ~1500 users, primarily through word-of-mouth.
- **Fundraising**: They closed a pre-seed round with South Park Commons, First Round Capital, and Andreessen Horowitz (a16z). They are preparing for a seed raise in January.
- **Product Vision (The "AI Rolodex")**: Fei described Nira as an AI-powered version of a Rolodex. It allows users to query their network (and extended networks) naturally (e.g., "Find me a PM in NY interested in ad tech").
- **Phase 2 (Operating System)**: Post-seed, Nira aims to become a "Narrow Network" operating system where "cards" (profiles) customize themselves based on who is viewing them, providing contextually relevant information.

### 3. Collaboration Framework
- **Mutual Support**: V proposed a "pay it forward" model where Careerspan helps boost Nira's visibility in communities and rooms they access, while Nira provides a platform for high-quality talent discovery.
- **Anti-LinkedIn Positioning**: Both parties expressed a shared critique of LinkedIn as a bloated, 2D behemoth that fails both job seekers and employers. Nira is positioned as the "Narrow Network" alternative.
- **Tastemaker Strategy**: V and Logan want to leverage their "tastemaker" status to selectively recommend tools like Nira that are synergistic with their brand.
