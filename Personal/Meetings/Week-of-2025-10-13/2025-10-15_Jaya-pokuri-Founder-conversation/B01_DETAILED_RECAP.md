# B01: Detailed Recap

## Overview
Vrijen Attawar (V) met with Jaya Pokuri and Dennis (Founding Engineer at Bonfire) to discuss V's transition into AI consulting and explore Bonfire's current challenges in building AI-driven sales intelligence for health tech.

## Key Discussion Points

### V's Transition & AI Tooling
- V shared that he is moving towards making his AI work a formal consulting business.
- V promoted a specific AI tool (referred to as a "computer in the cloud") that he advocates for, highlighting its power for prompting and automation, which he finds more intuitive than Zapier.
- He mentioned using this tool for sales and marketing functionality, emphasizing "vibe coding" and prompting.

### Bonfire's Business Model (Analytics-Driven Sales Intelligence)
- Bonfire builds sales intelligence for health tech companies (e.g., medical devices, digital health).
- Current customers include Growth Therapy, Headway, and Nourish.
- They leverage deep healthcare data (patient panels, procedure volumes, surgical data) to help companies identify and prioritize targets (doctors, hospitals, health systems).

### The New "Downstream" Product Initiative
- Jaya described a new focus: helping reps *during* the sales cycle (prep and collateral generation).
- **Goal:** Arm sales reps with prep materials and AI-generated collateral before/during calls with stakeholders like Chief Medical Officers (CMO) or CFOs.
- **Mechanism:** The system takes user context (goal of the call, stakeholder role) and combines it with:
    1. Proprietary healthcare data (patient/procedure info).
    2. Web-scraped data (PubMed articles, publications).
- **Output:** A compelling narrative outline or sales prep document.

### Technical Approach & Challenges
- Bonfire is exploring a multi-agent architecture:
    - Web search agent (PubMed/web scraping).
    - Data analyst agent (internal proprietary data).
    - Narrative/Structuring agent (consolidating insights into a compelling story).
- Jaya is seeking V's expertise on prompting strategies and agent orchestration to consolidate these disparate data sources effectively.
