# B32 Thought-Provoking Ideas

## The "Dumbest Possible AI" Principle
The idea that technical resilience and thoughtful design come from accommodating the "dumbest possible AI." Using expensive, high-reasoning models (like Sonnet 3.5/4.5) is often a "lazy" substitute for clever orchestration. True engineering prowess is simulating complex behavior with cheap, non-reasoning units.

## Software Economics are Decoupling
Vrijen's observation that "software doesn't scale" in the traditional sense because every LLM output has a tangible COGS. This shifts the competitive landscape from pure feature velocity to "efficiency architecture"—who can deliver the same intelligence for 1/40th of the price?

## Shallow vs. Deep Semantic Extraction
Resumes are "years compressed into 25 lines"—a lossy format. Careerspan's thesis that the *method* of data collection (conversational deep-dives) is more important than the *model* used to process it. Generic output is usually a data depth problem, not a model intelligence problem.
