## Memorable Quotes

### 1. **"You have a great candidate. Like, so what?"** — Vrijen Attawar
- **Context:** Challenging whether simply showing employers a good candidate is sufficient for sales persuasion
- **When:** Early in conversation (~00:37)
- **Why it matters:** Exposes core sales question: What makes employers care about a candidate? Being good isn't enough without context of what problem this solves for them.

### 2. **"That's sales. Right. Here's an example of what we did for this chief of staff. Now we can do this for you if you pay us money."** — Ilse Funkhouser
- **Context:** Translating abstract "proof points" into concrete sales narrative
- **When:** Near end of discussion (~01:40)
- **Why it matters:** Frames proof points as a sales sequence, not a cold fact-dump. Shows clear CTA: example → value → price.

### 3. **"It's still a valuable signal like this is how good of a person we can get you, I guess for this role"** — Vrijen Attawar
- **Context:** Acknowledging value of proof points as sales enablement, even if not sole acquisition lever
- **When:** Mid-discussion (~01:20)
- **Why it matters:** Shows Vrijen recognizing the sales value while remaining skeptical of proof-points-as-primary-motion.
