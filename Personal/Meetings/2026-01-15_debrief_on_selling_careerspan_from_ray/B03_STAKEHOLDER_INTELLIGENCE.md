# B31: Stakeholder Research

**Perspective:** Ray, an operator who walked a symbolic earnout at Shift Up and is now mentoring this sale with fresh runway pressure.

## Insight 1: Treat the acquisition search as a high-volume sales funnel before features or headlines matter

**Evidence:** "It's like a sales process and it basically came like five or six that seem to have some level of real interest. And then of that five or six, it was like two to three that sort of got to the point where they were, like, seriously interested. And then only one of those actually ended up making any sort of move."

**Why it matters:** Simply getting interest from a founder or pilot is not traction—V must stack conversations aggressively and invest in qualification because only a sliver of inbound interest becomes a real offer.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)
- Rating rationale: Ray draws direct parallels between his prior pipeline and this one, giving us a concrete filter for sorting through dozens of early-stage talks.

**Category:** Fundraising & Business Model

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Ray → See B08 Domain Authority
- **Relevant experience:** Founder of Shift Up with franchise over outbound interest, investor conversations, and the full deal funnel.
- **Source type:** PRIMARY
- **Weight justification:** He lived through the thick of a similar outbound runway crunch, so the advice is operational and grounded in bloodied metrics rather than theory.

## Insight 2: Two legit bids are the minimum hedge when the runway is three months and investors need payback

**Evidence:** "If you're backs against the rope and you only have one person that wants to do a real thing, they're not going to pay you anything, basically because you don't have any better options generally, right?"

**Why it matters:** One offer means no leverage and no pricing pressure—getting multiple parties to the finish line at once is the only way to avoid being forced into a distress sale.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)
- Rating rationale: Specific personal experience shows how a single offer forced Ray’s previous deal to deliver pennies, making this warning actionable.

**Category:** Founder Pain Points

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Ray → See B08 Domain Authority
- **Relevant experience:** Noted the painful reality of a symbolic result from Shift Up because there was only one willing buyer.
- **Source type:** PRIMARY
- **Weight justification:** His last exit lacked leverage entirely, so he is unusually sensitive to this scenario and therefore credible.

## Insight 3: Pace negotiations like competing job offers—slow-play some prospects, accelerate others so they align in time

**Evidence:** "You got to be smart with your timing on outreach, such that you can kind of... slow play some conversations and sort of try to speed up other conversations such that they end up getting to roughly the same point in time in any negotiations around the same time... so that you can potentially play them off against each other."

**Why it matters:** Aligning stages across buyers gives V the ability to compare offers in real time instead of letting one laggard anchor the outcome.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)
- Rating rationale: Tactical guidance grounded in negotiation psychology, tied to Ray’s own choreography of his outbound list.

**Category:** Fundraising & Business Model

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Ray → See B08 Domain Authority
- **Relevant experience:** Brokered messy conversations at Shift Up and now exercises the same playbook while prepping this sale.
- **Source type:** PRIMARY
- **Weight justification:** He is steering V through the exact cadence he wishes he’d run last time, so the insight is contemporaneously relevant.

## Insight 4: Earnouts are buyer-side risk mitigation; unless the deal stays in control, most of the upside evaporates

**Evidence:** "If everything went right, I could have been... I walked away in my earnout with, like, basically nothing. So I didn't really make that much... It reduces the risk on their side."

**Why it matters:** V should cap the earnout horizon or favor cleaner asset purchases because the majority of value often lands in contingent payments that buyers can deny by changing course.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)
- Rating rationale: Earnout failure is a common founder trap, and this vivid example (zero payoff despite potential) makes the risk tangible.

**Category:** Fundraising & Business Model

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Ray → See B08 Domain Authority
- **Relevant experience:** Signed a deal where the earnout was supposed to be the upside and then never materialized due to buyer misexecution.
- **Source type:** PRIMARY
- **Weight justification:** He lived the downside of earnout-heavy structures, so prioritizing clean cash or short, measurable earnouts is practical.
