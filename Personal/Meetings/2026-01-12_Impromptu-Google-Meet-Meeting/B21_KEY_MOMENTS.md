# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"Can I suggest a synthesis that I guarantee you will make us a lot of money?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Kicked off the call by framing the collaboration as a unified revenue play and invited David to build a joint solution.

**"We build products for personal productivity that are built atop Zo ... the point of it is in building the productivity tool, you learn how to use Zo."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Linked product development directly to teaching Zo usage, positioning Careerspan’s work as hands-on onboarding for the platform.

**"What else would a larger swath of people want to pay money for than personal productivity to affect their biggest source of income, their job?"**  
— David Speigel  
*Context:* Justified targeting job seekers by tying the product to their primary income stressor and suggesting scale beyond founders.

**"I would prefer to be somebody who builds a product on Zo that automates what I'm teaching people to do, and my students, they pay to sign up for the product that is built on Zo."**  
— David Speigel  
*Context:* Shifted the conversation from course delivery to a Zo-native product that generates recurring revenue and lets Zo get a cut.

**"If we can do that ... that doesn't require accounts ... we can charge like a 10, 20, 30 dollar thing with it."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Pushed for a low-friction MVP that sidesteps auth complexity while still letting them monetize quickly.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"Can you think of a decent MVP that we can build based on that, that doesn't require accounts, that doesn't require auth, that doesn't require us to basically manage user information and state?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* Forced the team to prioritize a session-based product that avoids data liability while proving demand.

**"How do we package the software for purchase? Because either the people download it into their own instance and then run the stuff, but then it's not a subscription, or they have to enter some stuff to us and we run it as a subscription, but then we have access to their stuff."**  
— David Speigel  
*Significance:* Highlighted the trade-off between self-serve downloads and subscription-based managed services, a core monetization decision.

**"It doesn't require any of the state stuff?"**  
— David Speigel  
*Significance:* Checked the feasibility of the simplified MVP before investing in auth or persistent data storage.
