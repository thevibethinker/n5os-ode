## Key Decisions and Agreements

- **Partnership Model Agreed:** Affiliate/distribution partnership where Darius acts as megaphone to his trained community; Careerspan handles matching infrastructure and employer relationship
  - Why it matters: Solves Careerspan's demand generation bottleneck without requiring Darius to operate the matching system
  - Rationale: Darius has engaged workforce-ready community; Careerspan has superior matching tech

- **Revenue Share Mechanism Outlined:** Two-tier structure
  1. **Employer-side revenue (primary):** Commission-based (likely 10% annual salary or similar recruitment fee split)
  2. **Consumer-side revenue (future):** Freemium model ($10-12/month consumer charge) to cover distribution costs; Darius may subsidize for his community
  - Why it matters: Creates recurring revenue stream while keeping barrier low for job seekers
  - Rationale: Darius's affordability concern addressed; Careerspan's unit economics protected

- **Job Distribution Mechanics Clarified:**
  - Darius sends specific roles to his community (e.g., "5 product manager roles")
  - Community members click into Careerspan, receive personalized positioning for that role
  - No direct application friction—roles pre-matched, candidate experience optimized
  - Careerspan handles employer communication; Darius handles community communication
  - Why it matters: Clear division of labor reduces Darius's operational burden while leveraging his distribution muscle
  - Rationale: Darius doesn't want to be a recruiter; Careerspan owns the employer relationship

- **Free vs. Paid Role Distinction:** Roles Darius sources = always free to his community; employer-sourced roles = check full community but only pay Careerspan for placements (no subsidy needed from Careerspan for free roles)
  - Why it matters: Prevents liability concerns (Darius accused of steering people to pay-to-win jobs)
  - Rationale: Protects both parties' brand and addresses Darius's explicit concern about appearing exploitative

## Strategic Context

**Darius's Platform:** Started as payment infrastructure for schools (now commoditized). Evolved to: (1) social media enrollment engine for bootcamps/training programs, (2) accumulating trained job seekers as byproduct of growth. Now building flywheel: fill schools → students complete training → graduates looking for jobs → place graduates. Platform sits at intersection of education + employment.

**Darius's Value Prop:** Has warm, engaged, trained, ready-to-work community at scale. Community built through content marketing (students looking for training) + organic pipeline. **Does not want to operate recruitment**—wants to monetize the talent side without building recruitment infrastructure himself.

**Healthcare Opportunity Surface:** Darius raised hospital systems + allied health as potential vertical (dozens needed vs. one engineer for startups). Careerspan team skeptical about healthcare fit (not qualitative coaching, highly regulated). Darius not pushing; both parties acknowledged but not priorities.

**Positioning Fit:** 
- Careerspan: Marketplace for high-signal communities, but needs supply of candidates + employer relationships
- Darius: Has supply, has community relationships, wants revenue from that supply but not to build matching
- Alignment: Darius provides B2C supply + megaphone; Careerspan provides infrastructure + B2B access
- Darius's framing: "You chase employers, I'll chase community. Both are hard as B2B/D2C hybrid. Let me handle the D2C side so you focus on B2B."

**Why This Matters to Careerspan:**
1. Solves cold-start: Immediate access to trained, pre-vetted talent at scale
2. De-risks supply-side: Darius's community is warm (not cold applications), reducing junk pipeline
3. Founder partnership validation: Another founder validating Careerspan's core thesis (marketplace for high-signal communities)
4. Revenue model diversity: Commission-based from employers (not just B2C)
5. Potential for multiple communities: If Darius works, template is replicable with other community leaders
