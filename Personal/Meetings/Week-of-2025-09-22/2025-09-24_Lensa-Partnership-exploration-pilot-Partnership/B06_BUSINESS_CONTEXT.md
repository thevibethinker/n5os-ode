# B06: Business Context & Implications

## Market Opportunity
Lensa and Careerspan are exploring a partnership centered on high-intent user acquisition and job distribution.
- **Lensa's Scale:** Operates at a massive scale with 25 million jobs, scraping Fortune 1000 companies and aggregating content from agencies and other job boards.
- **Traffic Dynamics:** The job board industry is described as "incestuous," with partners frequently trading traffic via XML feeds and APIs. Lensa is looking for "fresh" job seekers to combat user "bounce" between platforms.
- **Careerspan's Niche:** Targets high-signal professional communities (McKinsey alumni, "Zooglers," Ivy League) to build a rich data set beyond the resume, offering a unique "always job-ready" talent pool.

## Competitive Position
- **Differentiation:** Careerspan differentiates itself through deep qualitative data (career coaching stories) and extremely high engagement rates (75-80% open rates, 15-16% CTR), which contrasts with the volume-heavy, lower-engagement nature of traditional aggregators.
- **Lensa's Utility:** Serves as a primary content source for Careerspan, potentially replacing manual scraping with a clean, structured API/XML feed.
- **Market Timing:** Careerspan is currently in an early expansion phase, planning to onboard 200–400 users per week and scaling from product roles into engineering and growth.

## Strategic Direction
- **Goal:** Establish a "talent network" rather than just a job board. Careerspan offers a free consumer product to embed in communities and capture rich data, which is then used for hyper-accurate matching.
- **Partnership Rationale:** Lensa gains access to a highly engaged, "conscientious" audience that is difficult to reach via standard acquisition channels. Careerspan gains access to a reliable, massive pipeline of jobs, reducing operational overhead (scraping/cleaning JDs).
- **Expansion Roadmap:** Starting with product roles in SF, NY, and Boston, with plans to expand to engineering and growth within three months.

## Commercial Model
- **Monetization Structure:** Lensa will pay Careerspan on a CPC (Cost Per Click) or CPA (Cost Per Action) basis.
- **Initial Test Terms:** 
  - Starting bid: 15 cent CPC.
  - Starting budget: $2,500 per feed.
  - Evaluation Metric: ROI based on how users interact with Lensa job alerts and registration paths.
- **Infrastructure:** Integration will be managed via **Appcast**, with Careerspan acting as a "Master Publisher."

## Implications & Next Steps
- **Immediate Business Impact:** If successful, this provides Careerspan with a consistent revenue stream and a superior job content source.
- **Strategic Decision:** Careerspan may need to "adjust thresholds" (lower matching strictness) if Lensa's incentive for volume outweighs Careerspan's current preference for low-volume, high-accuracy matches.
- **Execution:** Lensa will issue an IO (Insertion Order) and introduce Careerspan to dedicated analysts for direct and lead-gen content to monitor the October test launch.
