# B03: Decisions Made

## Executive Summary

This document captures the explicit decisions, agreements, and action items resulting from the November 14, 2025 partnership discussion between Careerspan and the Emory Alumni Association. All decisions reflect mutual agreement between Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan Founder) and Nicole Holubar (Emory Alumni Association).

---

## Strategic Decisions

### 1. Pilot Launch Structure

**Decision:** Launch a pilot program targeting **500 alumni** as the initial actively-engaged cohort.

**Rationale:** This number balances manageability (within Logan and Vrijen's capacity for 1:1 support) with sufficient data to demonstrate value to Emory leadership.

**Implementation Detail:** Nicole may distribute the opportunity to a larger group, but the primary analysis will focus on the first 500 actively using the platform.

---

### 2. Distribution & Tracking Protocol

**Decision:** All participants must use an **Emory-specific code** when signing up for Careerspan.

**Rationale:** This ensures proper tracking of Emory alumni usage, preventing data loss if individuals sign up independently. Enables accurate measurement of pilot success metrics.

**Implementation Detail:** Careerspan will provide a unique Emory code; Nicole will encourage all target alumni to use this code during signup.

---

### 3. Pilot Timeline

**Decision:** Launch within **3 weeks** (before the holidays) and shift to an **open-ended observation period** rather than a rigid time-limited pilot.

**Rationale:** 
- Pre-holidays timing captures alumni in reflection mode
- Post-holidays aligns with peak job search season ("new year, new me")
- First 6 weeks will serve as the primary observation period for metrics

**Implementation Detail:** Initial marketing push before holidays; success evaluation based on first month of active usage data.

---

### 4. Pricing & Revenue Model

**Decision:** Adopt a **hybrid approach** with two potential pathways:

**Path A (Preferred by Vrijen):** Small subscription fee of **$3,000–$5,000 annually** plus per-license costs of **$2–$4 per user**.

**Path B (Conditional Free Model):** Offer first **X number of licenses free** to drive adoption, with a conditional commitment from Emory to enter a paid contract upon achieving specific benchmarks.

**Rationale:** 
- Path A provides immediate revenue and validates Careerspan's market value to other institutions
- Path B reduces friction for adoption while creating clear conversion triggers
- Both paths align incentives for both parties

**Status:** Pricing structure to be finalized based on internal Emory budget considerations and leadership preferences.

---

### 5. Success Metrics Framework

**Decision:** Evaluate pilot success based on **four key metrics**:

1. **Engagement Rate:** Percentage of alumni who sign up and actively use the platform (target: comparable to Careerspan's 40% story-telling rate)
2. **Retention:** 30-day retention rate (Careerspan baseline: 30%+)
3. **Self-Reported Impact:** Percentage of users reporting improved self-advocacy (UMass baseline: 92%) and discovery of new opportunities (UMass baseline: 80%)
4. **Downstream Engagement:** Increase in alumni engagement with Emory Alumni Association beyond the pilot (e.g., event attendance, donations, platform interactions)

**Rationale:** Quantitative metrics provide objective evidence of value to justify long-term investment and expansion.

---

### 6. Marketing & Messaging Strategy

**Decision:** Leverage **Emory Connects platform** as primary distribution channel, with supplement through official channels where feasible.

**Messaging Themes:**
- "End the year on a high with Emory" (pre-holidays)
- "Start the year on a high note" (post-holidays, job search focus)
- Emphasize that Careerspan is an **alumni-led organization**
- Position as a high-value resource provided by Emory to strengthen alumni connections

**Rationale:** Aligns with seasonal career development patterns and reinforces the Emory-alumni bond.

**Implementation Detail:** Nicole will lead marketing messaging; Vrijen will provide proof points and testimonials.

---

### 7. Product Features for Pilot

**Decision:** Launch with **full Careerspan feature set**, including:

- Career coaching conversations (no changes to user experience)
- Job matching and "Direct Apply" functionality
- Resume optimization and interview preparation tools
- Story-telling and profile-building features

**Additional Considerations:** Explore **white-labeling options** to reinforce Emory branding throughout the platform experience.

**Rationale:** Full feature set maximizes value proposition for alumni and provides comprehensive data on user preferences.

---

### 8. Incentive Alignment for Alumni

**Decision:** Create incentives for alumni to **post jobs through Careerspan** rather than external platforms.

**Rationale:** Establishes a self-reinforcing ecosystem where alumni hiring other alumni increases platform value for all participants.

**Implementation Detail:** Consider low posting fees initially to encourage adoption; frame as "quick check against Emory community" for alumni employers.

---

## Operational Decisions

### 9. Deliverables from Careerspan

**Decision:** Vrijen will compile and deliver a **persuasion package** for Nicole to use with Emory leadership, including:

- **Data summaries** from existing partnerships (Columbia SPS, UMass Eisenberg)
- **Testimonials** from current power users (e.g., Rhett from UMass, Emory alumni if available)
- **Case studies** demonstrating impact
- **Cost-benefit analysis** framing Careerspan as $4–$6/hour for effective career coaching
- **Anchor pricing context** demonstrating value compared to market alternatives

**Timeline:** Deliver by end of the week following the meeting.

---

### 10. Customer Support & Capacity

**Decision:** Careerspan will provide **1:1 support** for the initial 500-alumni cohort.

**Rationale:** Ensures high-quality user experience and mitigates risk of negative pilot outcomes due to technical or onboarding issues.

**Implementation Detail:** Logan and Vrijen will manage support directly; scale support model for larger rollout based on pilot learnings.

---

### 11. Platform Migration Context

**Decision:** Acknowledge Emory's ongoing **platform transition** from Gravity to PeopleGrove.

**Rationale:** Careerspan pilot should not interfere with existing migration efforts; positions Careerspan as a complementary tool that enhances rather than replaces Emory Connects.

**Implementation Detail:** Coordinate with Emory's tech team to ensure seamless integration and avoid conflicts.

---

## Conditional Decisions

### 12. Benchmark-Triggered Conversion

**Decision (Conditional):** If the "first X free" model is adopted, Emory will commit to entering a paid contract upon achieving agreed-upon benchmarks.

**Rationale:** Reduces Emory's risk while creating clear conversion path for Careerspan.

**Status:** Specific benchmarks to be defined; agreement represents an "honor basis" commitment to negotiate in good faith.

---

### 13. Expanded Rollout Trigger

**Decision (Conditional):** If pilot exceeds 500 active users with strong engagement metrics, discussions will immediately shift to full-scale partnership.

**Rationale:** "Viral" success would de-risk expansion and accelerate timeline.

**Implementation Detail:** No hard cap on total users; 500 represents minimum target for pilot evaluation.

---

## Communication & Next Steps

### 14. Internal Stakeholder Buy-in

**Decision:** Nicole will use the persuasion package to secure leadership approval for the pilot and potential long-term partnership.

**Rationale:** Strong internal advocacy is critical for budget approval and long-term commitment.

---

### 15. Post-Pilot Path

**Decision:** Upon successful pilot (based on metrics), transition to **full-fledged partnership** with more robust support and expanded scope.

**Rationale:** Pilot serves as de-risking mechanism for long-term institutional commitment.

---

## Decisions Deferred

### 16. Specific Benchmark Quantification

**Deferred:** Exact numerical thresholds for success metrics (e.g., specific engagement percentage targets) to be defined based on baseline data and Emory leadership input.

**Rationale:** Allows flexibility to align with institutional expectations and realistic performance goals.

---

### 17. Final Pricing Model Selection

**Deferred:** Choice between Path A (paid upfront) and Path B (conditional free) will be determined by Nicole's internal budget discussions and leadership preferences.

**Rationale:** Requires alignment with Emory's fiscal planning and procurement processes.
