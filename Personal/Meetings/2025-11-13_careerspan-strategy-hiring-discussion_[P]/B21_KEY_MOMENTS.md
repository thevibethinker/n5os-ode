# Key Moments: Careerspan Strategy & Hiring Discussion

## Strategic Inflection Points

### B2B Pivot Confirmation
> *"We have built out an employer portal now... companies can hire directly through Careerspan. They get, like, put in a JD and they can essentially do the entire thing in terms of, you know, tracking candidates, going to the next round, etc. etc."* — Logan Currie

**Context:** Careerspan has successfully executed a pivot from pure B2C to a B2B2C model, leveraging existing tech to enable direct employer hiring while maintaining free access for job seekers.

### Monetization Clarity
> *"People are broke and we don't want to charge them. So how do we charge someone else to sort of subsidize that?"* — Vrijen Attawar

**Context:** Clear articulation of the cross-subsidy business model—monetize employers to keep candidate access free, validating the shift away from direct B2C monetization.

## Hard Truths & Market Realities

### The 1% Success Rate Reality
> *"Definition of success for a candidate looking for a job via a talent portal is 1%. So if I like, go online and I find a gig and I'm going to apply for it. 99 out of 100 jobs that I, Alex Garnapudi, or you, Logan, you have an MBA from Harvard. You're gonna get less than 1%. That's fucking insane."* — Alex Garnepudi

**Context:** Brings brutal clarity to the inherent brokenness of the hiring ecosystem—even with elite credentials, success rates are vanishingly low. This reframes the opportunity: improving from 1% to 1.5% is meaningful impact.

### Monetizing Job Seekers Is Fundamentally Hard
> *"Monetizing job seekers is very difficult. Right. Regardless of what part of the value chain they're in."* — Alex Garnepudi

**Context:** Industry validation for Careerspan's strategic pivot away from direct-to-consumer monetization toward employer-side revenue capture.

**Context:** Validates co-founder durability (shared "masochism wavelength" and prior trauma bonding). Survival to three years is a meaningful achievement in a space with high early mortality.

> *"When I think about our numbers, I'm like, man, when we get to like a hundred 50,000 profiles, then I think we'll really feel like we've crossed out of very early adopter and we're in like futurist and into early adopter."* — Alex Garnepudi

**Context:** Quantitative benchmark for market maturity—150k profiles as the threshold crossing from early adopter to broader market acceptance.

### Ambitious Displacement Vision

**Context:** Expansive vision positioning Careerspan not just as a hiring tool but as a replacement for legacy HR infrastructure. The "fourth doggy door" metaphor captures the unique market position between inbound, referral, and recruiter channels.

### Exit Flexibility
> *"Somebody wanted to kind of fold us up into whatever they're doing that. Who knows?"* — Logan Currie

**Context:** Acknowledges strategic flexibility—built cool tech with value across multiple ecosystem players, making acquisition or partnership viable paths alongside independent growth.

**Context:** Probes existential question about ambition scale—lifestyle business (2-5M ARR), high-growth VC path (100M ARR), or strategic exit. Forces explicit conversation about what "success" means today vs. four months ago.
