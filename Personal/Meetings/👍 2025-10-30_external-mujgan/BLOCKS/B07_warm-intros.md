## Introduction Principles Discussed

### Vrijen's Networking Philosophy
"We're working on our careers at the wrong time. The reason people hate networking is because if I had to go to you with my bowl out and be like 'please sir, may I have some more,' I'm gonna hate that. But if I'm going to you saying 'hey, here are the cool things I've been up to because I don't need anything from you, I'm just trying to engage with you,' that would be incredible."

**Implications for Intros:**
- Best introductions happen when neither party is desperate
- Value-exchange mindset > transactional mindset
- Context and timing matter enormously
- Relationship maintenance requires "cutting grass" not just crisis networking

### Context Requirements for Good Intros
Vrijen described ideal AI assistance:
- Infer relationship importance through language/context/explicit instruction
- Proactively surface context before making introduction
- Example: "Faith had a promotion last week, here's what she's working on"
- Don't make caring "totally easy" but organize circumstances for being a good friend
