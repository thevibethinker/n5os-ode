# Meeting Recap: Jake Gates Career Mentoring

**Date:** October 30, 2025  
**Participants:** Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan founder), Jake Gates (RIT student, Marvin contributor)  
**Duration:** ~23 minutes  
**Purpose:** Career mentoring and exploration of Careerspan platform

## Meeting Summary

Vrijen conducted a career mentoring session with Jake Gates, a second-year student at Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT) currently working part-time with Marvin (5 hours/week helping with LinkedIn content, newsletters, and founder showcases). The meeting served dual purposes: providing career guidance and introducing Jake to Careerspan's platform.

## Key Discussion Points

### Background & Context
- Jake is a UK national currently studying in upstate New York
- Attended United World College (UWC) 22-24, which connected him to many high-achieving peers
- Recently joined Marvin through referral from a dean connection
- Planning a semester abroad in Bangkok starting next year (sophomore year)
- Successfully negotiated remote work flexibility with Marvin for Thailand semester

### Careerspan Platform Introduction
- Vrijen shared resources: consulting preparation guides that "went viral" in business schools (general version + McKinsey-specific version)
- Emphasized the McKinsey guide helped him go from zero knowledge to receiving offer in 3.5 months
- Core philosophy: Resume is emergent property of self-understanding, not vice versa
- Careerspan uses conversational coaching (10-15 min conversations) with semantic matching based on 1000+ hours of recorded coaching
- Goal: Build "densest concentration of talent" through semantic matching rather than keyword-based recruitment
- Jake showed preliminary interest in trying the platform

### Career Development Insights Shared
- The real value proposition: Helping candidates understand "who I am, what do I bring to the table"
- Recruiter fees are expensive; Careerspan aims to democratize this process without 0K charges
- Personal branding should be about "playing your own game" not trying to be unique
- Key reframe: Focus on 3-line pillars of personal brand rather than competing on uniqueness
- Example: Vrijen's non-conventional background (sales, tech, coaching) was more compelling than more typical consulting backgrounds

### McKinsey Experience & Pivot to Startup
- Vrijen spent 9 months at McKinsey before leaving to launch Careerspan
- Left March 1st (same day firm cut 10,000-20,000 support staff)
- Context: Poor utilization and "beach time" during economic downturn
- Key learning: McKinsey uses internal accounting where junior consultants start at zero cost; when work is scarce, they become expensive liabilities
- Decision criteria for startups: "Could I live with someone else figuring this out?" - requires psychotic personal commitment
- Does not regret McKinsey but found startup path more compelling

### Personal Connection Points
- Vrijen has soft spot for Rochester-area students; mentioned contacts including Elizabeth Kenyan (PayPal) and Ulrich
- Shared MUN experience in Asia, worked with many UWC alumni during Singapore coaching career
- Common ground around startup mentality and ambition

## Atmosphere & Dynamics
- Warm, mentoring tone with genuine interest in Jake's development
- Vrijen balanced business pitch (Careerspan) with authentic career guidance
- Jake engaged thoughtfully with follow-up questions and reflections
- Meeting concluded with mutual commitment to connect at Marvin dinner in NYC next month
