# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Prototype a Gamma-powered consumer story using Zo as the data curator
**DECISION:** Build a lightweight Gamma integration where Zo streams reference files, runs a Claude code–style synthesis, and renders the output as a demo-ready one-pager for Ben to review.  
**CONTEXT:** The conversation landed on Gamma as the most visual consumer product, and Vrijen volunteered to “fuck with it” and record a demo that proves Zo can feed structured context into the system. Ben regarded it as the clearest path to showing the platform’s App Store capabilities.  
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar (with Ben Guo encouraging the experiment in real time)  
**IMPLICATIONS:** Expect to ship a polishable Gamma demo, iterate off Ben’s feedback, and use it as a reference for other consumer-focused experiments.  
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** The team could have held off until post-Puerto Rico, but Ben was receptive to seeing a live example before the next tour, so a quick prototype won.

## Decision 2: Prioritize small, price-aligned data partners over large incumbent vendors
**DECISION:** Focus on the shortlist that already services AI-first teams (Aviator, Luma, Fillout, Exa, SERP API, Surf) rather than chasing big vendors like X or Apollo.  
**CONTEXT:** Vrijen shared how those partners already offer cheap API plans and demonstrated how Zo could wrap them into the same workspace; Ben agreed that these players are easier to collaborate with, and that Zo can undercut the traditional enrichment stack by co-selling with them.  
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar (with Ben validating cost sensitivity)  
**IMPLICATIONS:** We will build direct connector documentation for those partners, position the integrations as “Zo + [vendor]” combos, and use the results to pitch the App Store narrative at upcoming demos.  
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Returning to the larger partners (e.g., Apollo or X) was rejected because they are less hungry, slower to integrate, and harder to price for the SMB audience we are currently courting.

## Decision 3: Use the upcoming Supra/SF event plus the Fohi hackathon as narrative checkpoints
**DECISION:** Treat the February Zo Valentine’s Day event and the Fohi-influenced hackathon as opportunities to surface the partnership story; Vrijen will keep Ben updated on logistics and Ben will flag introductions (e.g., Ben Erez) so all of them can see the demos live.  
**CONTEXT:** Ben mentioned the Zo party in SF and V celebrated the idea of a Supra/SF meet-up; later, V brought up Fohi’s hackathon request, so both threads got grouped as launchpads for the same story.  
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar (with Ben offering to coordinate once he is back in town)  
**IMPLICATIONS:** We will document the timing, confirm space/per attendee plans, and use the event headlines to get the partnership hypotheses in front of Ben Erez, Supra, and the wider community.  
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Postponing everything until after the PR trip would have left the story untethered; instead, we decided to keep the momentum by running these micro-events while the partnership interest is fresh.
