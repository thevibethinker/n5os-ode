# B31: Stakeholder Research

**Perspective:** Ben is a cofounder with a product-first lens, already guiding the Zo roadmap and looking for high-leverage partner plays that prove the platform can orchestrate more than just chat sessions.

## Insight 1: Partner the smallest, most aligned data vendors before hunting the big names
**Evidence:** Ben warmed up to Vrijen’s list of providers (Aviator, Luma, Fillout, Exa, SERP API, Surf) because they already serve the “newfangled AI” crowd, have manageable pricing, and would happily be part of a Zo-powered bundle.  
**Why it matters:** He believes these vendors are the ones that welcome deep integration (to replace expensive Apollo-style enrichment) and wants Zoe to act as the connective tissue so they can co-sell without losing their brand.  
**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)  
- Rating rationale: Specific names were offered, and Ben qualitatively confirmed the direction, but the integrations have not yet been executed.  
**Category:** Partnerships & GTM  
**Source Credibility:**  
- **Stakeholder:** Ben Guo (Zo cofounder, product lead)  
- **Relevant experience:** Oversees Zo’s App Store-style automation stack and has visibility into partners that already talk to Zo’s customers.  
- **Source type:** PRIMARY  
- **Weight justification:** He is eager to position Zo as the integration hub and chooses which vendor plays get prioritized.

## Insight 2: Zo is an App Store for these partners, not just a chatbot
**Evidence:** “You guys are the App Store, right?” followed by a long riff about the natural progression of building integrations on top of Zo, meaning the platform should trade in packaged experiences (boots, demos, reference files) rather than ephemeral text exchanges.  
**Why it matters:** Packaging the experience this way makes it easier for smaller vendors to say “yes” (they don’t need to rewrite their stack) and easier for Zo to surface differentiated demos that look like proper products.  
**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)  
**Category:** Product Platform & Positioning  
**Source Credibility:**  
- **Stakeholder:** Ben Guo (App Store advocate)  
- **Experience:** He runs the Zo infrastructure and is the gatekeeper of which integrations make it onto the home screen.  
- **Source type:** PRIMARY  
- **Weight justification:** His language shapes the product narrative, so this metaphor should inform commercialization collateral.
