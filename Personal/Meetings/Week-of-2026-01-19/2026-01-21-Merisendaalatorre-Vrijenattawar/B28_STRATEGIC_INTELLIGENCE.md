## AI Product Strategy: From Information Delivery to Adaptive Learning Relationships

**Strategic Shift Required**: The conversation reveals a critical distinction between two AI paradigms in edtech/fitness education:

1. **Traditional Model**: One-directional information delivery where AI simply surfaces content from a repository
2. **Emerging Model**: Dynamic, bi-directional learning relationships where AI builds a compounding learner profile over time

**Key Quote**: *"My belief, my POV is that is where the advantage will come is like a more dynamic interrelationship with the user, not just a we are providing information to you. Because I think when people think start with that mental model, it's very easy to get locked into a like data is only flowing from one direction."*

**Strategic Implication**: Long-term competitive advantage in AI-powered education/fitness training will come from systems that:
- Compound learner knowledge over time
- Integrate external data sources (wearables, genetic data, behavior patterns)
- Provide context-aware, just-in-time guidance
- Enable adaptive role-playing and scenario-based learning

## Technical Talent Strategy: The Hybrid Technical Leader

**Core Hiring Philosophy**: Organizations need at least one in-house technical product hybrid who can bridge engineering and product vision.

**Strategic Requirements**:
- **Recent Individual Contribution History**: Avoid hiring purely managerial candidates—"sometimes the folks that have been in director roles for a long time are actually farthest away from the tech"
- **Ability to Build Directly**: At early stages, "they still need to more importantly be able to just build something, get it out, get it in customers hands"
- **Technical Fluency for Oversight**: Must be "technical enough to know when the developers are bullshitting and can like get realistic, can set and demand realistic things from them"

**Cost Optimization Strategy**: Consider international talent pools to access high-caliber technical leadership at $100-150k rather than $350k+ for US-based talent.

## Build Philosophy: Internal Ownership with Selective Outsourcing

**Strategic Position**: Build core product ownership internally, but consider outsourcing specific engineering tasks.

**Key Quote**: *"I am of the belief that doing it internally is better. Having a internally responsible person, at the very least that is technical, that it's not a problem to have external teams or developer teams or give it to a developer company."*

**Strategic Rationale**:
- External developers can say "we just did what you told us to do" without accountability
- Internal ownership ensures someone is "responsible that on your side that you can catch them by the collar"
- AI engineering can be outsourced, but product direction and technical coordination must remain internal

## Unit Economics: Monitor from Day One

**Critical Strategic Warning**: Many companies fail by deferring unit economics concerns.

**Common Failure Pattern**: *"They will, like, start with an unlimited subscription, then they'll shift to usage, then they'll see their usage tank."*

**Strategic Recommendation**: Even when optimizing for early user experience, "plan for [unit economics]...especially with the scale that y'all operate at, you'll have pretty okay adoption, at least at the outset on some level."

## AI Technical Architecture: Start Simple

**Strategic Reality Check**: Most use cases do not require custom models or fine-tuning.

**Key Quote**: *"You don't need like, yeah, like, you don't need your own model, you don't need fine tuning, you don't need any of that. Like anyone that tells you that, like, they better have a really compelling case."*

**Strategic Implication**: Base LLM capabilities are sufficient for most edtech/fitness AI applications at early and mid-stage scaling.

## Advisory Network: Technical Evaluation Support

**Strategic Asset**: Leverage technical advisors for candidate evaluation, especially when hiring your first technical leader.

**Tactical Approach**: *"Find a reliable in network technical advisor, pay them, have them oversee the interview process for your top candidates or at the very least have one or two calls with your top candidates so that they can provide a. Again a tech[nical assessment]."*

This prevents the trap of hiring credentialed managers who lack recent hands-on technical experience.
