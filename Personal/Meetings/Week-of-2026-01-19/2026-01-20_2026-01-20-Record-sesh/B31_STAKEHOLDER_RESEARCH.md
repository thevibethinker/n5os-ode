# B31: Stakeholder Research

**Perspective:** Speaking as career tech founder (Careerspan) with a decade of career coaching experience and deep understanding of the hiring ecosystem.

## Insight 1: Automated Job Applications Create a Tragedy of the Commons That Dilutes Signal for Everyone

**Evidence:** "The problem is essentially a tragedy of the commons. With regards to a recruiter's time and attention merely. By exponentially increasing the volume of applications for even a subset of the total applicants, you are significantly diluting everyone's signal. So it's not that you doing that doesn't in a vacuum increase the candidates odds. It's that when you do that for everyone, then everyone's odds diminish roughly equally and even the folks that would otherwise stand out in a normal volume level start to get lost in the sauce."

**Why it matters:** The core value proposition of application automation tools is fundamentally eroding their own effectiveness by creating a signal-dilution feedback loop that hurts both qualified candidates and recruiters.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ● ○ (4/5)
- Rating rationale: Specific insight about the systemic dynamics of automated applications, not just an observation about volume; directly relevant to product strategy and positioning in the career tech space.
