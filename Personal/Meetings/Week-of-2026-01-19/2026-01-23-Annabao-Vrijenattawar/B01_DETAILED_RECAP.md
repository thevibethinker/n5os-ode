# B01_DETAILED_RECAP

## Meeting Overview

Vrijen Attawar and Anna Bao met for an initial planning session focused on FOHI's 2026 New York City chapter strategy. The meeting opened with personal context—Vrijen is visiting friends in Raleigh while Anna house-hunts in NYC from Astoria to Greenpoint (renting, not buying). Anna walked Vrijen through recent member survey results (55 responses from 800 total) and outlined a comprehensive event calendar for the year, culminating in Vrijen taking ownership of the February happy hour event.

## Chronological Discussion

### Personal Context and Logistics (0:00-3:00)

The conversation began with Vrijen apologizing for a brief delay due to WiFi setup in his temporary Raleigh accommodation. He explained he'd changed his return flight to avoid the incoming snowstorm, canceling plans to do a workshop with Zoe in New York on Sunday. Anna shared she's staying in NYC through the weekend for house hunting, looking to rent in the corridor from Astoria down to Greenpoint. Vrijen noted the transportation challenges between that area and his downtown Brooklyn location, mentioning his recent participation in a Hearth pod in Williamsburg and the commuting costs involved ($40/night for cabs). This established the geographic constraints that would later inform the neighborhood ambassador strategy.

### FOHI Onboarding and Survey Results Walkthrough (4:00-9:00)

Anna transitioned to reviewing the member survey results, sharing her screen. The demographic breakdown revealed a surprising concentration in edtech and freelancers/consultants, with representation also in administration, faculty, philanthropy, investing, journalism, and policy. HR and L&D representation remains low—a growth opportunity. Regarding event preferences, respondents gravitated toward panels, happy hours, co-working sessions, conferences, and even a running meetup. Anna noted strong interest in AI adoption, evolving skills workshops, education financing, policy/regulations, new higher education models, and fundraising.

A key finding was member communication preferences: while Slack is used, there's significant appetite for email, suggesting a quarterly newsletter could be effective. The survey also surfaced interest in volunteer roles—22 people expressed interest in being neighborhood ambassadors, with good numbers for connectors, greeters, and facilitators. Anna had already begun outreach to Modern Day (a nonprofit) based on survey suggestions for organizational partnerships.

### 2026 Event Calendar and Strategic Initiatives (10:00-16:00)

Anna outlined an ambitious event calendar for 2026. February will feature a happy hour, with March targeted for a hackathon. April discussions are underway with Unmuted—a social club for politically curious people known for "spicier" takes on policy discussions—for a collaborative debate-style event. The most significant initiative is a planned NYC Summit (1-2 days), potentially hosted by Starbridge who offered space. This mirrors a previous Boston summit held a few years ago with Dan Fohi. Anna emphasized this would require months of planning and suggested keeping May and June lighter to accommodate summit preparation, with fallback options like local happy hours or volunteer field trips.

Anna stressed that sponsorship is a major goal for the year and that the workload will be front-loaded over the next few months with extensive outreach and relationship building. She noted the importance of leveraging volunteers to avoid everything falling on the two leads.

### Volunteer Program Design and Andrew Young Benchmarking (16:00-25:00)

Anna presented a three-tiered volunteer structure she designed:

1. **Event Creators**: Similar to Andrew Young's fellowship model, these individuals facilitate networking at specific events. Anna envisions a listserv approach where availability is solicited per event, with volunteers committing to just one event minimum to reduce burden.

2. **Connectors**: Two formats—in-person "social lubricants" at events, and virtual "subject expertise connectors" offering office hours. This would involve experts dedicating one-hour slots where members can sign up for specific connections based on the volunteer's network.

Vrijen contributed that Andrew Young's model does this well with designated fellows whose entire role is conversation facilitation. He offered to speak with Jacob from the Andrew Young program for insider tips, and also mentioned Harvard ilab's office hours system as a successful precedent. Anna noted constraints from Ray and Jake: low-tech solutions are preferred over new platforms. They settled on Google Sheets and calendar links rather than building new technology. Anna also highlighted that Donut (Slack coffee-matching bot) already exists within the community, so the connector role should be differentiated as more specific, expertise-driven networking rather than random pairings.

3. **Neighborhood Ambassadors**: These individuals would facilitate localized co-working and coffee meetups in their own neighborhoods, acknowledging that Manhattan-centric events don't serve everyone geographically.

### Responsibility Assignment and Closing (25:00-26:00)

Anna shifted to assigning immediate ownership responsibilities. She offered to let Vrijen take full charge of the February happy hour as a challenge. Ray was noted as supportive but hands-off for NYC-specific decisions—he will step in for support but defers to Anna and Vrijen for local jurisdiction matters. Anna had to leave at 12:30, so the conversation concluded with Vrijen agreeing to review the shared documentation and provide feedback on the volunteer process design.
