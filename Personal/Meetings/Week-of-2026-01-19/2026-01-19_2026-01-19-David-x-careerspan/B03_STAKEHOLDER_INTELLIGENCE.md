# B31: Stakeholder Research

**Perspective:** Speaking as an experienced Zo power-user translating repeated coaching calls into a scalable, persistent operating model.

## Insight 1: Capture “new units of learning” from every call and map them to slide modules  
**Evidence:** “So my super set deck of slides… if I teach 10 concepts… and a new concept comes up…I say, ‘Zoe should be able to pick up on that in the very first recording that it’s pulled into a new file and say, okay, the slides don’t have this…so therefore it’s a new unit of lesson.’”  
**Why it matters:** David needs a deterministic way to surface novel insights, not just summaries, so he can keep his decks and teachings in sync with the evolving fabric of what he actually says on calls.  
**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)

## Insight 2: Replace Zapier with Zo+Fathom direct webhook to keep transcript processing sustainable  
**Evidence:** “My Zapier trial just ended…what I would like to do based on what you said before is have Zo parse this one file and save them all out into individual files…and then process each unique file independently.”  
**Why it matters:** The desire for a cheaper, more reliable, and fully internal pipeline signals that the stakeholder is already paying for automation and is sensitive to recurring SaaS friction—so the success metric is “fewer moving pieces, direct Zo integrations.”

**Why it matters:** He’s consciously comparing Zo’s persistent state to other AI tooling and positioning our build/orchestrator investments as a differentiator for anyone who cares about long-term memory and cleanliness.
