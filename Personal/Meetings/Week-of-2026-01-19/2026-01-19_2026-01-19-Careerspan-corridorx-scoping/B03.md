# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Proceed with Lovable JD → CareerSpan Integration Workflow

**DECISION:** The team will proceed with a workflow where Lovable-generated job descriptions feed into CareerSpan roles, acting as the connective tissue between CorridorX's business and CareerSpan's platform.

**CONTEXT:** Shivam outlined a value chain where customers land on a Lovable page, fill out a template, and get generated JDs and related outputs. The JD is identified as the most important output that will route to CareerSpan.

**DECIDED BY:** Shivam Desai (CorridorX), with alignment from Vrijen (Careerspan) and Ilse (Careerspan)

**IMPLICATIONS:** This establishes the primary data flow between the two companies. CorridorX will drive traffic to their Lovable page, generate JDs, and then route those to Careerspan.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None explicitly mentioned—this approach was presented and accepted.

---

## Decision 2: Shivam Will Manage Employer-Side Visibility Initially

**DECISION:** Shivam will have visibility into and management access for the employer side of CareerSpan on behalf of his customers, at least in the beginning.

**CONTEXT:** Ilse asked whether Shivam wanted interaction with CareerSpan to be entirely API-based or use the UI. Shivam clarified he wants to see job descriptions and which profiles are relevant to each JD.

**DECIDED BY:** Shivam Desai

**IMPLICATIONS:** This requires setting up an employer account for Shivam with proper visibility. Some workflows will be manual initially (seeing which profiles match JDs).

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Fully API-based integration was mentioned but rejected for the initial phase.

---

## Decision 3: Use CareerSpan's Scan Feature for Candidate Matching

**DECISION:** CorridorX will use CareerSpan's "scan" feature to match JDs against candidate profiles, rather than just sending links for candidates to self-select.

**CONTEXT:** Ilse explained the scan feature: when a role is published, CareerSpan scans through designated talent pool members, filters by fit (e.g., .NET engineers for .NET roles, not Rails engineers), and only exposes roles to clearly qualified candidates.

**DECIDED BY:** Joint decision—Shivam agreed after Ilse presented the option

**IMPLICATIONS:** This reduces manual outreach for CorridorX, saves candidate time by only showing relevant roles, and leverages CareerSpan's filtering capabilities.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Sending links out to talent pool for self-selection—Ilse noted this costs CareerSpan money and is more work for CorridorX.

---

## Decision 4: Careerspan Will Set Up Employer Infrastructure

**DECISION:** Ilse will set up the necessary CareerSpan infrastructure for CorridorX within 48 hours, including an employer account, scanning credits, talent sign-up links, and a button to scan specific users.

**CONTEXT:** Ilse committed to setting up everything Shivam needs, including a button in the employer app to scan specific users rather than everyone on the platform.

**DECIDED BY:** Ilse Funkhouser (Careerspan)

**IMPLICATIONS:** CorridorX will have a functional employer account ready for the pilot, with sufficient scanning capacity and onboarding flow for their talent pool.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None—this was a direct commitment from Ilse.

---

## Decision 5: First Pilot Wave with 15 Engineers

**DECISION:** CorridorX will begin outreach to the first 15 engineers from their talent pool and onboard them to the CareerSpan platform as a pilot wave.

**CONTEXT:** Shivam stated: "today and tomorrow I'm going to actively start messaging first 15 engineers in my pool that are some of the best engineers we have access to and start bringing to the Career Span platform."

**DECIDED BY:** Shivam Desai

## Decision 6: Webhook/Email Integration for Application Notifications

**DECISION:** Careerspan will set up webhook or email notifications for CorridorX when candidates submit applications or when pass/proceed decisions are made.
