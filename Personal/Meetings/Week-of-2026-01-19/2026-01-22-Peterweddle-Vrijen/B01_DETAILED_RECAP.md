# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
A strategic advisory call between Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan founder) and Peter Weddle (TA Tech executive) focused on exploring acquisition opportunities for Careerspan. The conversation was triggered by the Eightfold lawsuit, which Vrijen believes signals a shift toward regulation of scalable talent measurement and creates market opportunity for compliant alternative candidate data collection methods. Vrijen is actively seeking to sell Careerspan rather than raise additional capital, given the product has reached maturity with approximately 4,000 engaged users.

## Chronological Discussion

### Opening and Relationship Building (0:00-1:47)
The meeting began with personal conversation about weather patterns—Peter in Northern Virginia expecting a foot of snow, Vrijen in Raleigh anticipating ice. Peter shared family context: two daughters, a son who flew Apache helicopters in the North Carolina National Guard and now lives nearby. This established a warm tone before transitioning to business.

### Context: Eightfold Lawsuit as Market Signal (1:47-3:36)
Vrijen introduced the Eightfold lawsuit as a catalyst for considering a sale, explaining that he believes it portends regulatory shifts toward large-scale talent measurement. He stated Careerspan's three founders agree they don't want to raise more money—the product is mature and the best path forward is getting it into hands that can provide distribution. Vrijen emphasized the product has approximately 4,000 users with strong engagement and that scraped third-party data collection is coming to a close.

### Product Overview: Careerspan's Value Proposition (3:36-5:23)
Vrijen provided a concise overview: Careerspan uses conversational coaching for guided self-reflection, collecting rich data on how candidates think, behave, perceive others, and perceive themselves. He positioned this as "part of the solution" in a multifactorial approach—candidates sharing about themselves comfortably plus an intelligent matching system. The product can serve either as a talent pool setup tool (leveraging the coaching product people love using and willingly provide data to) or as the matching technology itself.

### Industry Landscape: Consolidation and M&A Trends (5:23-7:04)
Peter noted that M&A activity is ramping up as companies seek more fulsome portfolios to sell to employers. He mentioned the industry is fragmented with many solutions, citing Boom Band (Jeff Taylor's company) as doing something similar but not identical to Careerspan. Peter emphasized that while timing is good given buyer appetite, the challenge is the fragmented landscape.

### TA Tech North America Conference Opportunity (7:04-8:39)
Peter introduced TA Tech North America (May 6-8 in Charleston) as a primary networking opportunity. The conference is small (250-300 attendees) but 100% decision-makers. It features a "Deal Center" where last year's conference generated 663 meetings in just a day and a half. An app releases five days before the conference enabling pre-arrival meeting scheduling.

### Timing Concerns and Sourcing Technology Market (8:39-9:47)
Vrijen expressed concern about the May timing, noting this would be the go-to option if no momentum emerges by March/April. He observed that most recent M&A activity appears concentrated in validation, vetting, and identity verification rather than sourcing technologies. He asked about M&A activity around sourcing, citing Dex and Jack & Jill as UK examples, positioning Careerspan as potentially strategic for companies like Handshake that could deploy the platform across university networks.

### Virtual Deal Center: Immediate Access Path (11:27-14:06)
Peter explained that Vrijen could join the TA Tech network immediately and access the Virtual Deal Center without waiting for May. Membership tiers start at Bronze ($600/year), which includes 50% conference discount and Virtual Deal Center access. There are currently hundreds of companies in the virtual platform. While not everyone responds, Peter described a "halo effect"—even if a primary contact passes, they may refer other interested parties.

### Membership Proration and Platform Mechanics (14:06-15:44)
Vrijen committed to signing up within days and asked about proration for mid-year membership, which Peter confirmed is available. The Virtual Deal Center is a closed garden of 2025 conference attendees. Members can view profiles, company information, and LinkedIn profiles, then initiate outreach through the app.

### Staffing Firms as Strategic Buyers (15:44-17:41)
Vrijen explored whether recruiting companies or staffing firms might be interested in technology that builds talent pipelines without requiring additional recruiter hires. He mentioned True Blue as a potential target he's trying to reach. Peter recommended the American Staffing Association, which represents thousands of firms from mom-and-pop operations to global enterprises—described as "the sherm of staffing companies."

### Reality Check and Product Demonstration (17:41-20:17)
Peter shared his experience having sold multiple businesses, noting the process is tedious—"you're gonna kiss a lot of frogs before you find the prince." He emphasized that employers want more than resume dumps and are seeking human-centric evaluation methods. Vrijen demonstrated the employer portal (with some wifi loading delays), explaining that resumes can't be trusted due to AI fabrication, while 30-50 minutes of candidate reflection can be reconstructed into arguments for and against fit that are highly relevant to specific roles and companies.

### Compliance Angle and Closing Action Items (20:17-22:43)
Peter suggested the Direct Employers Association as another target—this association consists of recruiters responsible for compliance who may be seeking clearly compliant recruiting capabilities. Vrijen expressed he would pitch Eightfold directly if he had a contact. He committed to signing up for TA Tech membership and sending Peter his one-pager for feedback on sales language and positioning. Peter asked Vrijen to spread word about the conference to his network. Vrijen mentioned his HR tech founder group (approximately 30 founders) and offered to make introductions. Peter encouraged them to explore virtual offerings even if they can't attend the in-person conference.

## Key Takeaways
- Careerspan is actively seeking acquisition, motivated by the Eightfold lawsuit as a signal that scraped third-party candidate data faces regulatory headwinds
- The product has approximately 4,000 users with strong engagement and collects candidate data through 30-50 minute conversational coaching sessions rather than passive scraping
- Two primary paths forward: immediate Virtual Deal Center access via TA Tech membership ($600 bronze tier), and in-person networking at TA Tech North America in Charleston (May 6-8)
- Target buyer categories identified: university recruiting platforms (Handshake, College Recruiter), staffing firms (via American Staffing Association), and compliance-focused employers (via Direct Employers Association)
- Peter committed to reviewing Vrijen's one-pager and providing feedback on positioning and sales language
- Vrijen committed to promoting the TA Tech conference to his approximately 30 HR tech founders network
