# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Pursue Sale of Careerspan Rather Than Raising Additional Funding

**DECISION:** Careerspan will not raise more venture funding and will instead pursue a sale/acquisition to get the technology into hands that can provide the distribution it deserves.

**CONTEXT:** The founders (three of them) are aligned that the product is mature with strong engagement (4,000+ users, consistent 3-5% monthly return rate) and the best path forward is to scale via a strategic buyer rather than continued bootstrapping or fundraising.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar (on behalf of the three founders)

**IMPLICATIONS:** Careerspan will actively seek acquisition opportunities, positioning itself as a compliant alternative to data-scraping talent intelligence platforms in light of the Eightfold lawsuit and emerging regulatory concerns around third-party candidate data.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Raising additional venture capital (rejected), continuing as independent bootstrapped business (current path being evaluated).

## Decision 2: Join TA Tech Network and Use Virtual Deal Center for Buyer Outreach

**DECISION:** Vrijen will sign up for TA Tech network membership (starting at bronze level, $600) and use the Virtual Deal Center to initiate conversations with potential acquirers and partners.

**CONTEXT:** Peter recommended this as the most efficient way to have 20-30 meetings with decision-makers over a 1.5-day period. The Virtual Deal Center provides immediate access to hundreds of companies in the TA Tech ecosystem without waiting for the May conference.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:** Careerspan gains access to a curated network of potential buyers/partners in the HR tech space. Membership includes 50% discount on TA Tech North America conference in Charleston (May 6-8) as a fallback option if sufficient momentum hasn't been built by March/April.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Waiting until May for in-person conference only (rejected in favor of immediate Virtual Deal Center access), cold outreach without network leverage.

## Decision 3: Pursue Strategic Partnerships with University Career Platforms and Staffing Firms

**DECISION:** Careerspan will explore strategic partnerships with university career platforms (like Handshake) and staffing firms as potential acquirers, given the product's strong engagement with early-career professionals and ability to build talent pipelines at scale.

**CONTEXT:** Vrijen identified Handshake as an ideal fit—Careerspan could be deployed across Handshake's existing university network to build a vast repository of candidate data. Additionally, staffing firms may be interested in technology that helps build talent pipelines without requiring additional recruiters.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:** Outreach will target two specific segments: (1) university career platforms as distribution partners or acquirers, and (2) staffing firms through the American Staffing Association and direct outreach (e.g., True Blue).

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Focusing only on HR tech buyers (expanded to include staffing and university segments).

## Decision 4: Leverage TA Tech Conference May 6-8 as Fallback Option

**DECISION:** If sufficient acquisition momentum has not been built by March/April 2026, Vrijen will attend the TA Tech North America conference in Charleston (May 6-8) as the go-to option for face-to-face deal meetings.

**CONTEXT:** The conference is an executive-level event (250-300 people, all decision-makers) with a Deal Center that facilitates 600+ meetings. Vrijen committed to booking the ticket as a backup plan.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:** Creates a concrete deadline and fallback mechanism for the sales process. If Virtual Deal Center outreach doesn't yield sufficient interest by Q2 2026, the conference provides a concentrated opportunity for buyer meetings.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Immediate conference attendance (rejected as too far out in timeline given current desire for urgency).

## Decision 5: Seek Feedback on One-Pager from Peter Weddle

**DECISION:** Vrijen will send the Careerspan one-pager to Peter Weddle for feedback on language, positioning, and sales messaging, leveraging Peter's experience selling businesses in the HR tech industry.

**CONTEXT:** Peter has sold multiple businesses and understands the language and positioning that resonates with buyers in this industry. Vrijen wants his input on the one-pager before broader distribution.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:** The one-pager will be refined based on Peter's feedback before being shared with potential buyers and partners identified through TA Tech and other networks.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Distributing one-pager without expert feedback (rejected in favor of leveraging Peter's expertise).

## Decision 6: Promote TA Tech Conference to Founder Network

**DECISION:** Vrijen will promote the TA Tech North America conference to his network of 30+ career tech and HR tech founders, offering to make introductions to Peter Weddle for those interested in attending.

**CONTEXT:** Peter asked Vrijen to spread the word about the conference to other industry folks. Vrijen runs a community of HR tech founders and sees value in connecting them with this networking opportunity.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:** Strengthens relationship with Peter Weddle and TA Tech, potentially creating goodwill and additional networking opportunities. May lead to introductions for Careerspan as well.
