# B06_BUSINESS_CONTEXT

## Careerspan Overview

**Company:** Careerspan
**Founder:** Vrijen Attawar
**Business Model:** Career coaching and signal technology platform
**Team Size:** ~3 people

### Core Value Proposition
Careerspan captures behavioral data (how people think, not just what they've done) to help identify top talent. Unlike traditional profiles, Careerspan's signal technology can tell employers "who to hire, not just whom to reach."

### Target Markets
1. **Universities/Career Centers:** Career coaching infrastructure for students
2. **Recruiting Platforms:** Signal technology layer to enhance talent identification
3. **Employers:** Direct access to behavioral insights on candidates

### Current Partnerships
- Corridor X (recruiters using Careerspan to identify talent)
- Multiple universities (10 pilots, 2 university contracts including Rice, Colombia)
- Various recruiting platforms (partnerships in development)

## Handshake Partnership Opportunity

### Company Profile
**Handshake** is a university recruiting platform that democratizes access - every student can reach every employer through career centers.

**Key Metrics:**
- ~$200M revenue from core recruiting business
- Only 6% growth in last year
- 1,600 universities in network
- Moving away from recruiting line of business
- Still uses recruiting to source PhDs

### Strategic Fit
Handshake has built the "signal distribution layer" (universal access) but lacks the signal technology to identify talent. Careerspan provides the missing layer that makes their product defensible.

**Value to Handshake:**
- Data moat: captures behavioral signal at scale through university partnerships
- Long-term student relationships: reason for students to continue sharing data beyond college
- Elite talent identification: addresses strategic direction
- Low integration overhead: can plug into existing infrastructure

**Value to Careerspan:**
- Massive distribution: immediate access to 1,600 universities
- Validation: partnership with established player in space
- Revenue opportunity: potential revenue share or acquisition
- Data supremacy: access to longitudinal data from millions of students

### Partnership Models Discussed
- Integration layer
- White labeling
- Outright acquisition
