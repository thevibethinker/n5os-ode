# B01: Detailed Recap

**Meeting:** FOHE Local Lead Onboarding  
**Date:** 2026-01-22  
**Participants:** Oleksandra (FOHE Coordinator), Ray Batra (FOHE Leadership), Jviassy (New Local Lead), V (Observer/New Local Lead)

## Session Overview

This was a structured onboarding call for new FOHE (Foundation of Health and Education) local chapter leads. The conversation covered Slack engagement protocols, event management best practices, access permissions, resource management, and organizational policies. The tone was supportive, informative, and well-organized with clear emphasis on lessons learned from previous mistakes.

## Chronological Summary

### Slack Engagement and Attendance Management

**Oleksandra opened** the session discussing Slack engagement as an attendance driver rather than a strict requirement. The approach is framed as encouragement rather than enforcement, with the understanding that sustained collaboration increases event attendance. Tactics include:
- Sending DMs or public channel welcome messages to new members
- Monthly roundups for chapters with high member volume

**Event announcement strategy** was emphasized as critical. Oleksandra highlighted that three @channel messages per event have proven most effective:
1. One @channel a few weeks before the event
2. One @channel a week before the event  
3. Final reminder a day or so before the event

She noted that single @channel messages often go unnoticed.

**Ray Batra provided** nuanced guidance, giving local leads autonomy to calibrate based on event frequency. Minimum of two @channels recommended due to people missing initial announcements. High-frequency event organizers can reduce to two; those with moderate frequency might use three.

### Broader Slack Engagement Strategy

Oleksandra expanded on engagement tactics beyond announcements:
- Sharing local events and opportunities
- Conducting Q&A sessions or polls
- Being reliable and responsive
- Maintaining consistency in event organization
- Acting as the primary point of contact for the chapter

**Time commitment** was confirmed as 30-60 minutes per week on average, though variable.

**Term commitment**: Minimum one year, maximum three years.

### Access Permissions and Boundaries

Oleksandra emphasized critical permission boundaries:
- New leads have @channel permissions for chapter management
- **Strict limitation**: Do not use these permissions for any other purpose
- Do not create new channels or groups
- Written confirmation of understanding required

### Onboarding Actions and Deliverables

**Immediate post-call actions**:
- Meeting recording will be shared
- New leads must submit bio this week for welcome message
- Google form will be shared (demographics, phone number, name)
- Volunteer agreement will be shared
- Written consent needed for @channel/manager status usage

### Data Management and Systems

**Oleksandra demonstrated** the Google Sheets master tracking system containing all local leads and chapters. She emphasized:
- This sheet is critical for newsletter automation
- **Do not make any changes** to this sheet or elsewhere in the system
- Leads have already been added to the system

**Email communication system**: Local group aliases follow format `your-chapter-name@fohe.org`, sending to all attendees in that group. Automatically updates via Google Groups.

**Ray announced** upcoming system improvements:
- Proprietary database launching in 1-2 months
- Will feature local lead dashboard and resources
- Integration with Slack activity data (identifying active vs. inactive users)
- ~80% of Slack users are currently inactive, making email crucial
- Active Slack users serve as proxy for most engaged FOHE members
- Targeting active decision-makers for sponsorship opportunities

### Email Best Practices

Oleksandra emphasized: **Use only BCC** when emailing group lists.

### Event Management Tools and Resources

- **Preferred tool**: Luma (though other tools acceptable)
- **Templates and logos**: Available in FOHE Local Lead Google Drive
- Current assets: mostly logos, some templates from other leads
- Better materials are in development

### Networking and Support

**Local Lead Networking Program**:
- Monthly Donut tool matches local leads for 30-minute conversations
- Purpose: collaboration and tip-sharing
- Voluntary participation (inform Oleksandra to join)

**Support structure**: Primary point person is Oleksandra, with Jake and Ray available as backup.

### Sponsorship and Compensation Philosophy

**Ray addressed KPI questions** by explaining FOHE's volunteer philosophy:
- No formal KPIs for volunteers with limited time
- No direct payment currently
- **In development**: Legal framework for sponsorship revenue sharing
  - First portion: lead's event activities
  - Second portion: stipend for local lead
  - Third portion: FOHE global program and staff support
  - Example: $10,000/year raised

### Expectation Management

**Ray shared hard-learned lessons** about attendance:
- Always expect at least one-third of confirmed attendees to not show
- Sometimes drop-off reaches 50%
- Planning should account for this reality

### Closing and Feedback

V expressed enthusiasm and appreciation for the thoroughness, noting the onboarding materials reflect "every rule is a fuck up" from past mistakes.

Jviassy echoed appreciation for organization and excitement to create events.

Ray confirmed follow-up action items and encouraged ongoing communication for event planning support.

**Final action**: Both new leads confirmed interest in joining the local lead networking program. Oleksandra added them to the channel.

1. **Structured onboarding** with clear boundaries and lessons-learned documentation
2. **Proven engagement protocols** (3 @channel messages) with flexibility for high-frequency organizers
3. **Critical access restrictions** on Slack permissions and data management systems
4. **Upcoming system improvements** including proprietary database and Slack activity integration
5. **Supportive culture** with peer networking, mentorship, and legal compensation model in development
6. **Realistic expectations** around attendance drop-off and volunteer time commitment
