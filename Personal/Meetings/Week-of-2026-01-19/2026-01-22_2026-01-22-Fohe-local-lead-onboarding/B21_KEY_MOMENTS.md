# B21: Key Moments

**Meeting:** FOHE Local Lead Onboarding  
**Date:** 2026-01-22

## High-Leverage Quotes and Turning Points

### "Every Rule is a Fuck Up" - V

**Context:** During the onboarding review, V commented on the thoroughness of the FOHE documentation.

**Quote:** "Every rule is a fuck up, right? So as you were reading that, I was like, wow, this is thorough — because this all happened."

**Why It Matters:**
- Perfect encapsulation of FOHE as a learning organization
- Validates that the comprehensive onboarding materials exist because each rule represents a past failure
- Demonstrates V's appreciation for lessons-learned approaches
- Shows FOHE's transparency about mistakes and commitment to not repeating them

**Ray's Response:** "That's exactly true. That document is a bit scattershot bullet points, but those are all lessons we learned — including always expect at least a third of people to not come to things. Sometimes it's 50%."

**Significance:** This exchange reveals FOHE's culture — systematic documentation of failures to create repeatable success.

### 3 @channel Strategy - Oleksandra

**Context:** Explaining proven event announcement protocols.

**Quote:** "When you create an event, we recommend sending three @channel messages. With the practice before with local leads, it has shown the highest level of success and interest, and people actually following up. When you send just one, it may not come through and people won't see it. So: one @channel a few weeks before, one @channel a week before, and a final reminder a day or so before."

**Why It Matters:**
- Evidence-based protocol, not arbitrary rule
- 3x engagement improvement over single announcements
- Specific timing guidance for maximum effectiveness
- Demonstrates FOHE's data-driven approach to community engagement

**Ray's Nuance:** Added flexibility for high-frequency organizers (minimum 2, adjust based on frequency).

---

### 80% Slack Inactive - Ray Batra

**Context:** Discussing engagement strategies and upcoming database development.

**Quote:** "Roughly 80% of people in Slack are not active — that's why email is important."

**Why It Matters:**
- Counterintuitive insight given Slack's prominence
- Justifies dual-channel strategy (Slack + email)
- Explains investment in Google Groups and email infrastructure
- Foundation for data-driven targeting (focus on active 20%)

**Follow-up Insight:** "If you can see who's active, it gives you a proxy for who's most engaged with FOHE. If you're trying to find a sponsor, figure out who has a decision-maker title at a company headquartered in your city and who's also active — they're likely to get the most value."

**Significance:** Transforms a problem (low engagement) into an opportunity (precise sponsorship targeting).

### "Do Not Mess Up This Sheet" - Oleksandra

**Context:** Demonstrating the Google Sheets master tracking system.

**Quote:** "This sheet is important because when we send newsletters (and we have someone who works on automation), it's very important not to mess up this sheet. You've already been added — region here, Juliet here, you're right here. You should not make any changes here or anywhere else."

**Why It Matters:**
- Emphasizes fragility of current systems
- Single point of failure risk with Sheets
- Justifies strict access boundaries
- Motivates investment in proprietary database (1-2 month timeline)
- Highlights FOHE's growth from ad-hoc to professional systems

**Tone:** Protective, serious contrast with otherwise supportive demeanor.

**Context:** Addressing KPI questions and volunteer sustainability.

**Quote:** "We're not paying you — but we're working with lawyers to figure out a way that if you raise sponsorship money (e.g., $10,000/year), the first portion goes toward your activities as needed, some portion becomes a stipend for you, and some portion goes back to FOHE global to support the program and staff."

**Why It Matters:**
- Acknowledges volunteer sustainability challenge
- Innovative revenue-sharing model in development
- Aligns incentives: more sponsorship = more direct benefit to local lead
- Shows FOHE thinking beyond traditional volunteer exploitation
- Legal framework suggests professional organization maturation

**Significance:** Could transform local lead from unpaid volunteer to compensated community builder with financial upside.

## Themes Across Key Moments

1. **Learning from Failure:** Every rule exists because of past mistakes. Thorough documentation prevents recurrence.

2. **Data-Driven Approach:** 3 @channel strategy (proven), 80% Slack inactive (measured), attendance drop-off (tracked). FOHE doesn't guess.

3. **Transparency:** Hard truths shared openly (attendance drop-off, system limitations, volunteer constraints).

4. **Respect for Volunteers:** No KPIs, compensation model in development, flexible guidance.

5. **System Maturation:** Moving from ad-hoc (Sheets) to professional (proprietary database) with legal frameworks.

6. **Human-Centered Culture:** Supportive tone, humor, peer networking, realistic expectations.
