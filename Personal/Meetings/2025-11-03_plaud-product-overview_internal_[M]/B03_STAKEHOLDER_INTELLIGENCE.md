# B03 – Stakeholder Intelligence

## Plaud / Nextplay contact (Speaker 1)
- **Role & context**:
  - Works at or closely with Plaud / Nextplay ecosystem; in regular contact with high-profile connectors.
  - Recently attended the GoodWork launch party and is embedded in networks of "super-connectors" (Nextplay members, FOHE, etc.).
- **Goals**:
  - Better manage heavy volume of intros, follow-ups, and community interactions.
  - Post more consistently and effectively on LinkedIn and other channels.
  - Understand how to practically use emerging tools (Zo, GoodWork, Evry apps) to augment her workflow.
- **Interests & motivation**:
  - Strong interest in networking platforms that go beyond LinkedIn, including GoodWork.
  - Curious about AI tools but constrained by time and cognitive load.
  - Values tools that respect her voice and relationships rather than spamming her network.
- **Concerns / pain points**:
  - Overwhelmed by the friction of writing, composing emails, and keeping up with follow‑ups.
  - Skeptical of products that promise value but make data ingestion and setup her responsibility.
- **Leverage points**:
  - She is well-positioned to introduce V and Zo to high-value communities (Nextplay, FOHE, friends like Finn).
  - If convinced, she could become a strong advocate for Zo-based workflows within these networks.

## Vrijen (V) – Zo / Careerspan perspective in this meeting
- **Role & context**:
  - Demonstrating Zo-based systems rather than pitching Careerspan directly.
  - Operating as a designer of personal AI systems: meeting ingestion, B‑blocks, content pipelines.
- **Goals in this interaction**:
  - Show a credible, end-to-end workflow that solves real pain (intro overload, content creation).
  - Seed the idea of workshops and events where he can teach these workflows to communities.
  - Understand the participant’s context well enough to tailor a base system that would be immediately useful.
- **Interests & motivation**:
  - Deeply interested in patterns of how people work, take notes, and manage relationships.
  - Wants to turn what he has learned building his own system into a reusable platform for others.
- **Leverage points**:
  - Strong product intuition and hands-on system that already works for him.
  - Credibility as both a career coach and an AI workflows practitioner.

## Relationship dynamics & fit
- **Alignment**:
  - Both care about more humane, less friction-filled ways of working and networking.
  - Both are interested in alternatives to LinkedIn and legacy tools.
- **Potential value exchange**:
  - V provides workflow, system design, and potentially workshops.
  - The contact provides access to high‑quality communities and feedback from heavy network users.
- **Risks / watchpoints**:
  - If the system feels too complex or "technical", the participant may not adopt it despite interest.
  - Need to avoid overwhelming her with tools; focus on solving one or two core pains first (intros + follow‑ups).
