# B21 – Key Moments

1. **Reframing Zo as a personal AI computer.**
   - V explains that Zo is not just another chat interface but a full computer with a file system, agents, and integrations.

2. **Demonstration of persona-based workflows.**
   - V shows how he switches between personas (architect, builder, debugger, teacher) to plan, build, and explain systems.

3. **Walkthrough of meeting-intelligence blocks.**
   - V opens an example B‑block output, showing how a single transcript is turned into a detailed recap, commitments, questions, warm intros, and a follow‑up email.

4. **Introduction of idempotency as a design principle.**
   - V explains why the system must avoid reprocessing the same transcript multiple times and introduces idempotency as a key concept.

5. **Discussion of GoodWork and alternative networking platforms.**
   - The participant shares her experience attending GoodWork’s launch and seeing it dominate her feed.
   - They use GoodWork as a concrete example of both the promise and limitations of new networking tools.

6. **Articulation of the data-ingestion bottleneck.**
   - V argues that many AI products fail because they require users to manually centralize and maintain their data.
   - He proposes a future where users carry a portable, sealed data container that can be selectively connected to services.

7. **Recognition of communities as high-leverage distribution.**
   - They identify Nextplay and FOHE as ideal audiences for learning these workflows and potentially hosting workshops.

8. **Emerging idea of Zo-sponsored or Zo-themed events.**
   - V and the participant discuss virtual and in‑person events where V could teach personal AI workflows, possibly with Zo sponsorship.
