# B06 – Business Context

## Zo / V’s system
- **Product framing**: Zo is a personal AI computer rather than a single chat window.
  - Full file system, scheduled tasks, and the ability to plug in external services.
  - V has layered an N5-style system on top for meeting intelligence, CRM, and content.
- **Differentiation**:
  - Designed to be usable by non‑engineers while giving power users full programmability.
  - Supports persistent, composable workflows (e.g., ingest meetings, generate intelligence blocks, draft follow‑up emails).

## Companion tools discussed
- **Evry suite**:
  - *Monologue* – desktop dictation app (V was an early power user).
  - *Spiral* – AI writing partner for marketing and LinkedIn content.
  - *Quora* – email app positioned as a Superhuman alternative.
- **GoodWork**:
  - New networking platform marketed as an alternative to LinkedIn, popular among "super-connectors" in the participant’s network.
  - Value proposition is still somewhat opaque from the outside.
- **Dictation and transcription (Plaud, SuperWhisper, etc.)**:
  - Used to capture raw voice and convert it to text, which then feeds into Zo’s pipelines.

## Business problems in focus
- **Intro and relationship overload**:
  - Highly connected people are overwhelmed by:
    - A constant stream of warm intros.
    - Expectations to follow up thoughtfully.
    - The cognitive load of tracking who needs what.
- **Content and personal brand**:
  - Both participants see the need to publish more consistently (e.g., on LinkedIn) but are constrained by time and energy.
- **Data fragmentation across tools**:
  - Calendars, email, meeting notes, and transcripts live in different systems.
  - Most AI tools expect users to manually assemble and maintain a clean dataset, which is rarely realistic.

## Strategic opportunity
- **For V / Zo**:
  - Turn Zo into a reference implementation of a personal AI system for high‑leverage connectors and operators.
  - Use workshops and starter systems to make this reproducible for others.
- **For communities (Nextplay, FOHE, etc.)**:
  - Offer members concrete, high‑value playbooks for managing intros, follow‑ups, and content.
  - Potentially co‑host events sponsored by Zo or aligned partners.
- **For companions like GoodWork**:
  - If they can ingest structured intelligence from a system like Zo, they could become more than just another network feed.

## Risks & constraints
- Non‑technical users may be intimidated by "system‑building" unless the starter kit is extremely opinionated and easy to adopt.
- Time and attention of high‑value connectors are scarce; any workflow must produce visible relief quickly (e.g., better follow‑up, fewer dropped intros).
