## Strategic Context

**Market Positioning Insight**: The recruiting market sits on a spectrum from pure job boards ($500/listing, high volume, low quality candidates) to recruiters ($20-25% of salary placement fee, high touch). Careerspan is uniquely positioned to fill the middle: provide quality candidate screening through community vetting + async candidate development (coaching/profile building) at premium pricing. Charles sees this as filling a genuine market gap that emerged as ATSs degraded and AI resumes became commoditized.

**Charles's Framework**: Using the "plumbing analogy" - when introducing a new service, focus on the basic unit of work first (carry buckets of water) to validate that people actually want the product, before optimizing the delivery system. In Careerspan's case: prove placements work with communities first, measure success by number of candidates hired, not secondary metrics like engagement or profile completeness.

**Momentum Signals**: Charles emphasized that founders are "more open now than ever" to new hiring approaches. Legacy methods (pure networking/Silicon Valley insider channel, LinkedIn job posts) are showing signs of staleness. The market is receptive to innovation in this space.

## Strategic Shifts Highlighted

1. **From output metrics to outcome metrics**: Don't lead with engagement numbers (D30/D60 retention). Lead with placements. That's what employers care about.

2. **From feature parity to market positioning**: Async video interview screening isn't a competitor—it's a complementary workflow. Careerspan's coaching/profile building is the *pre-screening* that makes Charles's interview process more effective.

3. **From consumer play to B2B partnership model**: The communities strategy is the key differentiator. Don't try to compete on open job board volume; compete on *signal quality* and trust relationships with specific talent pools.
