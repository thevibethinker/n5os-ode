# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

## 1. The "Superhuman" Vibe Coder
Vrijen suggested that AI raises everyone's level, but specifically makes skilled users "superhuman" in productivity.
- **Provocation:** How can Careerspan measure this "superhuman" delta in a way that traditional coding assessments fail to capture?

## 2. Bridging the Technical/Non-Technical Gap
Vibe Coding acts as a bridge. Vrijen noted that founders often have "the wool put over their eyes" by agencies.
- **Provocation:** Can Vibe Coding become the mandatory "literacy" for all future non-technical founders to ensure accountability in the development process?

## 3. Reverse Hiring as the New Standard
Vrijen proposed an event where founders pitch to pre-selected candidates.
- **Provocation:** In a world where AI makes the *creation* of software cheap, is the most valuable asset the *signal* of who built it and their vision? How does the "hiring event" evolve when the "coding test" is solved by a LLM in seconds?

## 4. Enterprise-Level Vibe Coding
Discussion around deploying AI at scales of "tens of millions of dollars" in transactions.
- **Provocation:** At what point does Vibe Coding stop being "prototyping" and start being "architectural risk"? Where is the line between "vibe" and "engineering" at scale?
