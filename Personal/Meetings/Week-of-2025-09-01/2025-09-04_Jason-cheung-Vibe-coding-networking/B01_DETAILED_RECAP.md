# B01: Detailed Recap - Vrijen Attawar & Jason Cheung

## Executive Summary
Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan) and Jason Cheung (Newsletter Curator/Event Organizer) connected to discuss the burgeoning "Vibe Coding" scene in NYC, potential event collaborations, and the intersection of AI-powered prototyping with the hiring market. The conversation centered on a specific upcoming Vibe Coding face-off event sponsored by Windsurf and the potential for Careerspan to facilitate "reverse hiring" and matching at such events.

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Introduction and Context
- **Vrijen (Careerspan):** Detailed his background (Career Coach, McKinsey MBA) and the evolution of Careerspan from a coaching product to a matching platform. The current focus is providing employers with a "match snapshot" of candidates from high-signal communities.
- **Jason Cheung:** Runs a newsletter for 3,300+ tech professionals (OpenAI, Apple, Meta, etc.). He focuses on placing these experts as speakers and judges at events without payment involved.

### 2. The "Vibe Coding" Trend
- Jason highlighted a major shift in NYC towards "Vibe Coding"—where non-technical or semi-technical people build functional products using AI (e.g., Windsurf, Cursor) faster than traditional dev cycles.
- **Event Mention:** A "Vibe Coding vs. Engineering" face-off is scheduled for **September 23rd, 6:00 PM** in Brooklyn, sponsored by Windsurf. The top prize is one year of Windsurf Pro and 3,000 credits.
- Vrijen expressed high interest in participating/competing in this event, potentially alongside his co-founder (Ginger).

### 3. Philosophical Stance on Vibe Coding
- Both agreed that Vibe Coding excels at rapid prototyping and testing user experience, reducing the reliance on expensive dev agencies for early-stage startups.
- Vrijen noted it "bridges the gap" between technical and non-technical domains without necessarily replacing deep engineering expertise in niche fields.

### 4. Event Collaboration and Infrastructure
- **Civic Hall:** Vrijen mentioned a connection to Kamina (event organizer) who has access to Civic Hall. While the space is great, the AV suite costs approximately $5,000–$6,000 per day.
- **Reverse Hiring Concept:** Vrijen proposed a "matchmaking" or "reverse hiring" event powered by Careerspan, where pre-selected candidates meet founders.
- **Proposed Panel:** Jason suggested a panel featuring founders who have built companies using Vibe Coding to discuss enterprise-level application.

### 5. Talent and Network
- Jason mentioned a specific individual who has won 200+ hackathons using Vibe Coding without knowing how to "code" traditionally.
- Vrijen offered to pull in high-level operators and founders from his network who are deploying AI at scale (series A/B).
