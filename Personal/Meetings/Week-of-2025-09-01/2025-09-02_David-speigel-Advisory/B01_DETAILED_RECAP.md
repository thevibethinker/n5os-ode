# B01: Detailed Recap

The meeting focused on formalizing the advisory relationship with David Speigel and defining the strategic priorities for Careerspan's community acquisition and candidate supply-side growth.

### Advisory Engagement Structure
The team discussed and agreed upon a monthly retainer model for David's advisory services, specifically targeting approximately two hours of work per week. Logan Currie proposed an "upside" incentive structure, which includes commissions for successful introductions and additional commissions for communities that sign on. This aligns David's incentives with Careerspan's immediate need for high-quality community acquisition.

### Community Acquisition & Value Proposition
Logan emphasized that the current priority is "discovery-type" calls to understand pain points across different types of communities. The core value proposition involves providing professional-grade job tools and a unique job distribution mechanism that acts like a custom, exclusive job board for community members. This allows communities to offer exclusive opportunities to their members without the overhead of managing a traditional job board.

### Candidate Supply & Employer Signaling
Vrijen Attawar outlined the strategy for the candidate side, framing it as a "career agent model." The goal is to ingest high-quality candidates (initially 20-30 to start) and use them to signal value to employers. By aggregating 200-300 new product-focused candidates per month through community partnerships, Careerspan can offer employers curated shortlists and warm introductions. Vrijen also mentioned a "gap filling" mechanism designed to help candidates level up their profiles and stay engaged even if they are passive job seekers.

### Strategic Target: Specialized Communities
David Speigel identified specialized professional communities, such as Product Tank (specifically the Chicago chapter), as prime targets. These communities house both active and passive job seekers who are committed to career growth, providing the "best candidates" that employers are looking for beyond just those who are currently unemployed.
