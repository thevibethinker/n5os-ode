# B01 - DETAILED_RECAP

### KEY DECISIONS & AGREEMENTS

**1. Pivot to Recruiter-as-a-Service Model**

**WHAT:** Shift from platform-based pricing (\\$200-250 for database access) to recruiter service pricing (\\$10K+ per successful placement) with guarantee structure (first-year replacement or refund if candidate quits).

**WHY IT MATTERS:** This represents a fundamental business model transformation with 40-50x revenue per customer improvement. Alex identified the pattern of founders underpricing platforms when they could charge service-level rates during early stage. With 1500 profiles and 700 engaged users already in the system, Careerspan can deliver recruiter outcomes today rather than waiting to build more platform features. This decision resolves the unit economics problem where acquiring 50 platform customers equals one recruiter placement.

**2. Community Distribution Strategy Needs Reevaluation**

**WHAT:** Current approach of tying Careerspan to specific communities through exclusivity deals creates distribution dependency and complicates the value proposition into a "four-sided marketplace."

**WHY IT MATTERS:** Alex warned that investors view multi-sided marketplaces as red flags. The recruiter service model allows direct candidate relationships, reducing leverage that community owners have over Careerspan\\'s business. This shifts power dynamics from "we need communities to access candidates" to "we have candidates that employers pay premium prices for." The simpler two-sided model (candidates → employers) is more fundable and scalable.

**3. Supply Side is Validated, Monetization Was the Problem**

**WHAT:** Alex confirmed that 50% conversion to 20-25 minute AI coaching sessions is "pretty strong" signal that product-market fit exists on candidate side.

**WHY IT MATTERS:** This reframes the problem from "we need more users" to "we\\'re not capturing value from existing users." The 700+ engaged candidates represent sufficient supply to begin recruiter service operations immediately. This decision validates that further product development on candidate experience is lower priority than executing on monetization strategy.
