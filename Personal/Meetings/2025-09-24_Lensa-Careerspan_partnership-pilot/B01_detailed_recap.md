# B01: Detailed Recap

## Key Decisions and Agreements

- **Pilot Partnership Approved**: Careerspan to distribute Lensa job listings to its user community via email with AppCast feeds; Lensa to compensate on 15¢ CPC basis with ,500/month starting budget
- **Job Categories Focus**: Initial launch targets product management roles in Boston, SF, and NYC; expansion to engineering and growth roles planned within 3 months
- **Distribution Volume**: Starting at 70-80 jobs/week to 700-1,000 highly-engaged Careerspan users; scaling to 200-300 jobs distributed 2x/week
- **Technical Integration**: Careerspan to set up AppCast master publisher account and pull Lensa jobs via XML feeds or API (weekly batch processing initially)
- **AppCast Content Mix**: Lensa to provide filtered job categories (organic jobs, direct agency, ATS content) matching product roles; expand dynamically as Careerspan grows
- **Application Path**: Jobs distributed via email will route directly to Lensa for signup/registration (maintaining frictionless user flow)
- **Success Tracking**: Lensa assigns 2 dedicated analysts (one for direct content, one for lead gen content) to monitor conversion, user engagement, and ROI on budgets
- **Measurement & Optimization**: Test performance across all content types; scale successful channels, shut down underperformers; budget adjustments based on ROI
- **Timeline**: October 2025 launch confirmed; IO and AppCast setup info to follow via email

## Strategic Context

This is fundamentally a **supply-demand arbitrage partnership** with strong strategic alignment:

**Careerspan's Value Proposition**: Highly engaged talent network with deep candidate data (75-80% email open rates, 15-16% CTR) sourced from elite communities (McKinsey alumni, Google employees, Ivy League grads). Unlike traditional job boards, Careerspan attracts users motivated by career development and self-reflection, not just active job-searching.

**Lensa's Value Proposition**: Scale distribution partner with access to 25M jobs and established ATS/agency relationships. Lensa monetizes user acquisition; Careerspan monetizes through future employer posting fees.

**Why This Works**:
- Careerspan's boutique quality (low volume, high engagement) is asset, not liability—better conversion rates justify lower CPC than mass-market channels
- Lensa gets access to "always job-ready" passive candidates (2-3% monthly reactivation rate suggests dormant but ready-to-engage users)
- Careerspan validates its matching system with real-world partner data while generating marginal revenue
- AppCast technical integration is clean handoff with minimal engineering lift

**Key Insight**: Vrijen positioned Careerspan as a **quality over quantity** channel—explicitly addressing potential CPC concerns by emphasizing engagement rates and candidate quality. Mai's willingness to test and assign dedicated analysts signals genuine interest despite Careerspan's smaller scale.
