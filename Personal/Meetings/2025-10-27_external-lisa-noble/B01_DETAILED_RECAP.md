## Strategic Context

### The Problem: Hiring Dysfunction in Job Market

Vrijen framed two forces creating market froth:

1. **Tech CEOs using AI/layoffs as cover** for performance management; AI itself isn't transformational enough (Vrijen: "It's not that good in anyone's hands")
2. **Hiring paradigm collapse**: ATS-based resume screening is dead (anyone can fabricate perfect resumes); founders now terrified of "fake candidates," trust broken for remote hiring

Lisa corroborated: Top students (Columbia MBAs, Bain interns) not getting offers—first time ever. This signals systemic hiring breakdown, not just market weakness.

### The Opportunity: Signal Through Narrative

Vrijen's thesis: Existing hiring methods can't differentiate because keyword-based systems are gamed. **Solution: Ask candidates to represent themselves through story + AI-enabled matching** (not resume parsing).

Careerspan's model: Students do 40-50 min of self-reflection (stories); AI evaluates against employer's "ideal coworker" voice memo + rubric. Result: 70-80% of UMass pilot cohort doing 3+ stories—meaningful engagement even with GPT boost.

Lisa saw deeper value: **Not ATS replacement, but career advisor amplification**. Her intake currently cold starts ("email + resume"). With Careerspan pre-work, advisors get warm context (values, energy drains, substantiated experience) → better adjacency conversations.

### Stakeholder Alignment: Colby as Beachhead

Lisa is uniquely positioned: Entrepreneurship Center director + career advisor + skeptical of traditional career center leaders ("petty tyrants of small kingdoms"). She's already operating outside old constraints:
- Won't use Handshake (calls it "worst," ripe to displace)
- Manually vetting employers, having "proper conversations"
- Sees non-target schools as underserved by traditional recruiting

**Critical insight**: Lisa's constraint isn't disagreeing with Careerspan value—it's institutional complexity. She's been "told she's no longer allowed to play in job space," responsibility shifted to other offices. **Opportunity: approach through Davis Connects, entrepreneurship center, alumni engagement.**

### Why This Meeting Mattered

This wasn't a transactional sales call. This was **founder validation + strategic seeding**. Lisa:
- Affirmed core problem (hiring broken, students suffering)
- Endorsed product philosophy (narrative > keywords)
- Committed to strategic intros that unlock institutional entry points
- Offered to co-evangelize to peer network

For Vrijen: Colby = case study for liberal arts colleges + proof point that educators are desperate for better tools.
