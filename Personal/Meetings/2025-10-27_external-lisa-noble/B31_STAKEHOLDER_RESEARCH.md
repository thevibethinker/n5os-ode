## INDUSTRY & MARKET LANDSCAPE INSIGHTS

### 1. **MBA Market Collapse Post-COVID**
- **Observation**: "Right now I have three former Colby students of mine who wrapped up at Columbia Business School this May, and they are still looking for work. That is the first time I've ever had that happen... top students who spent their junior summer at Bain did not get return offers for the first time."
- **Implication**: Top-tier MBA programs + prestigious internships are no longer guarantees of employment. The "MBA + consulting internship = automatic offer" pipeline is broken.
- **Strategic Value**: Signals that traditional credential-based hiring is collapsing across industries, not just tech. This is a systemic market shift, not cyclical downturn.
- **Action**: Careerspan's strength is narrative + matching, not resume keywords. This is exactly what employers need when they can't trust credentials anymore.

### 2. **Institutional Hiring Paralysis Among Founders**
- **Observation**: "I'm also speaking to founders whose hiring timelines are getting longer now. That makes no goddamn sense."
- **Why**: "They're scared about fake candidates. They are not willing to hire or trust remotely, especially for engineers, especially with the level of cheating."
- **Implication**: Founder psychology has shifted from "move fast" to "paralyzed by trust." Resume cheating (via AI) has broken the evaluation system so completely that founders would rather delay hiring than risk bad hires.
- **Strategic Value**: This creates urgency around new hiring methods. Founders aren't looking for "better ATS"—they're looking for "how do I assess people I don't know?" Careerspan's voice + story model directly answers this.
- **Action**: Pitch Careerspan to founder networks (not just universities) as "builder-to-builder evaluation."

### 3. **ATS System Collapse**
- **Observation**: "The central thing that we tell everyone was the ATS will sort and rank you in somewhat of a meaningful way is completely gone... the gates are open, the Visigoths are at the gates and no one's doing anything."
- **Implication**: The infrastructure that managed hiring for 20 years is now useless. Employers, educators, and job seekers are all adrift without a shared ranking mechanism.
- **Strategic Value**: This is a market **reset moment**. New standards will be set by whoever solves this first. Careerspan is positioned to own "narrative-first evaluation."
- **Action**: Position Careerspan as the "post-ATS hiring standard," not an "ATS replacement."

### 4. **Referral Hiring as Default Strategy**
- **Observation**: "From founders that they don't even look at inbound. They don't even look. They post for legal reasons. They don't... [interview only internal referrals]"
- **Why Founders Do This**: Can't trust inbound, so referrals become the only evaluation channel. "The bar there has also been raised, right."
- **Implication**: Hiring has regressed to pure network effects. Non-connected candidates (women, minorities, rural students) are systematically excluded. This creates a **fairness crisis** in tech hiring.
- **Strategic Value**: Careerspan can solve for **"How do we expand referral networks without exploding referral corruption?"** by making evaluation more rigorous (narrative + rubric).
- **Action**: Pitch Careerspan to diversity/equity teams as "expanding referral networks responsibly."

### 5. **Liberal Arts Advantage in Uncertain Market**
- **Observation**: "I'm wondering if your best students are probably humanities and social sciences majors... they never thought that there was a clear road anyway, so they're willing to put in the work."
- **Why**: Business/engineering students expect certain trajectories (MBA → consulting → private equity); when that fails, they fracture. Humanities students expect ambiguity and adapt.
- **Implication**: In a broken hiring market, adaptability > pedigree. Liberal arts schools' "weakness" (no clear path) is now a **strength**.
- **Strategic Value**: Colby/similar institutions can own "we develop adaptive problem-solvers" positioning. Careerspan enables this narrative.
- **Action**: Develop case studies showing "how liberal arts grads thrive when hiring gets weird."

### 6. **Handshake as Incumbent Lock-In**
- **Observation**: "Most colleges and universities are not prepared to give up Handshake because... it delivers an all-you-can-eat buffet. None of it very good but there are a lot of options... creates a sense that there's ample stuff."
- **Why Colleges Use It**: Handshake is easy (set it + forget it); it creates perceived value for employers (20k job listings looks impressive); it reduces career advisors' workload.
- **Implication**: Colleges are knowingly sacrificing student outcomes for institutional convenience. "Better" tools face switching costs because they require active management.
- **Strategic Value**: Approach isn't to sell "better than Handshake" (obvious but doesn't overcome lock-in). Approach is to sell "how Careerspan enables YOUR advisors to be strategic connectors instead of gatekeepers."
- **Action**: Position Careerspan as "career advisor tool," not "job board." This avoids direct Handshake comparison.

### 7. **Career Services Dysfunction as Institutional Culture Problem**
- **Observation**: "Heads of career centers and advisors... they're not innovators. They're not... easily threatened. They're kind of moderately functioning introverts who think they're awesome and don't know much about the working world... they don't talk to employers as much as they need to."
- **Why This Matters**: Career center leaders are typically risk-averse, isolated, disconnected from employer reality. They're **not the customer**; they're the **blocker**.
- **Implication**: Top-down institutional sales to career center leaders will fail. **Need to bypass them** (work through entrepreneurship centers, alumni offices, deans).
- **Strategic Value**: Institutional strategy for Careerspan at liberal arts colleges: find Lisa-equivalents (boundary spanners who talk to employers, willing to innovate). Work through them, not against traditional career centers.
- **Action**: Sales outreach to entrepreneurship centers, alumni offices, and employer relations teams—not career center directors.
