## SECTION 2: SALIENT QUESTIONS

### 1. "What is the destination?" (51:17)
**Full question**: "What is the destination on New Year's Eve? What is the destination January 31st? What does that look like?"

**Why it matters**: This is the foundational question for the entire engagement. Ilya is pushing for concrete success criteria so he can reverse-engineer the execution plan. Without clear endpoints, they'll just execute tactics without strategy.

**Who asked**: Ilya (to Logan/Ilse)  
**Action hint**: This became the baseline goal-setting exercise. Ilse responded with "10 businesses using product for one hire by Dec 31, pipeline of loose commitments, one paying customer by Jan 31." These numbers are now the North Star metrics for the engagement.

### 2. "What is the third rail?" (1:03:47)
**Full question**: "Is there a particular corner of the HR marketplace that is a no-no? Is there something you've realized, this is definitely we don't want to talk to these people?"

**Why it matters**: Before running marketing campaigns, Ilya needs to know what NOT to do. This prevents wasted effort on dead-end segments. It's a sign of his experience - rookies ask "who should we target?" Experts also ask "who should we avoid?"

**Who asked**: Ilya  
**Action hint**: Flag for first discovery calls. Team needs to document: (1) segments we've tried that failed, (2) product limitations that exclude certain customers, (3) messaging that tested poorly.

### 3. "What is truly the message around [the product]?" (1:04:01-1:04:02)
**Full question**: "There's what the product does and then there's the message around it. And those two are slightly different sometimes."

**Why it matters**: This is THE strategic marketing question. Ilya is highlighting the gap between technical functionality and market positioning. Careerspan might think their value prop is "AI-powered hiring tool" when the winning message could be "make you the hero of your hiring process" or "never lose great candidates to broken ATS again."

**Who asked**: Ilya  
**Action hint**: During product walkthrough, Ilya will probe: What does the product technically do? What transformation does it create for the user? What pain does it remove? The answers will shape A/B testing of different messaging frames.

### 4. "Did you want me to have a Careerspan email?" (1:05:54)
**Full question**: Context - Ilya asking about operational integration (email, Slack access)

**Why it matters**: Subtle but important - Ilya is thinking about how to position himself when reaching out to prospects. Careerspan email = he represents the company. His LLC email = he's external consultant. This choice affects credibility/authority in outreach.

**Who asked**: Ilya  
**Action hint**: Logan said yes to Careerspan email. This means Ilya will be doing direct prospect outreach, not just advising from sidelines. Increases his ownership/accountability for outcomes.

### 5. "What differentiates you guys from everything else?" (1:05:39)
**Full question**: "What exactly happens when a customer logs into this product... What differentiates you guys from everything else that's out there?"

**Why it matters**: Ilya needs to internalize the product's unique value to craft authentic messaging. If he can't explain why Careerspan is different (and better) in his own words, prospects won't be convinced either. This deep product understanding will inform which features to emphasize in marketing vs. which are table stakes.

**Who asked**: Ilya  
**Action hint**: Ilse committed to demo account + employer portal walkthrough before first standup. This hands-on experience will let Ilya experience the "aha moments" that users have, which become the emotional hooks in messaging.
