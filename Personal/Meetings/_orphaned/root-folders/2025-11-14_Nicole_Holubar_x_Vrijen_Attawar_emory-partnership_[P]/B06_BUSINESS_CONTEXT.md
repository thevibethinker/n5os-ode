# Business Context: Emory University Partnership

## Company Profile: Careerspan

### Overview
**Careerspan** is an AI-powered career coaching and hiring platform founded by Vrijen Attawar. The company provides personalized career guidance through interactive storytelling conversations, enabling users to advocate for themselves and discover career opportunities they might not have otherwise considered.

### Founder & Leadership
- **Founder**: Vrijen Attawar
  - 10+ years experience as a career coach
  - 4+ years as an entrepreneur in tech
  - Alumni-led organization founder (Emory connection)

### Product Capabilities
- **AI Career Coaching**: Interactive storytelling sessions (typically ~1 hour of content)
- **Resume Generation**: Automated resume creation from user stories
- **Interview Prep**: Practice sessions and guidance
- **Job Matching**: Candidate-job alignment for hiring partners
- **Direct Apply**: One-click application system for posted roles
- **Screening Tool**: Quick candidate assessment against job requirements

### User Engagement Metrics
- **Total Users**: 3,000+ signups
- **Engagement**: 40% of new signups create stories; 2-4% monthly re-engagement for active users
- **Usage Patterns**: Users engage over multiple months, with some returning after job placement to update stories
- **Scale Capacity**: Can handle 1,000+ concurrent users without performance issues

## Funding & Financial Status

### Current Stage
Careerspan is currently in growth phase with the following financial characteristics:

**Revenue Streams (Pilot/Pricing Structure):**
- **Hiring Partnerships**: Primary monetization model
  - Employers pay to post jobs and access candidate pools
  - Pricing flexibility: $50 to check against 100 alumni candidates
  - scalable to larger partner volumes

- **Institutional Licensing** (Alumni Association Model):
  - Proposed pricing: $3,000–$5,000 annually for large-scale alumni access
  - Per-license pricing: $2–$5 per alumni member
  - Includes unlimited access to career coaching features

- **Direct User Pricing** (Reference Point):
  - Approximately $4–$6 per hour of coaching
  - Positions Careerspan as highly cost-effective compared to traditional coaching

**Cost Structure:**
- **Technology Costs**: ~$1+ per 20-minute coaching conversation
- **Scalability**: Marginal costs decrease with user volume
- **No stated external funding rounds** mentioned in conversation

## Business Model

### Primary Model: Hiring-Based Monetization

Careerspan has fully transitioned to a hiring-partner-focused business model:

**Value Proposition for Hiring Partners:**
- Quick, efficient candidate screening against alumni pools
- Direct access to qualified candidates from partner institutions
- Reduced time-to-hire through one-click apply functionality
- Ability to "quick check" alumni networks for role fit

**Value Proposition for Institutions:**
- Enhanced alumni engagement and value delivery
- Alternative communication channel with fewer procedural barriers
- Potential for increased donations through demonstrated value
- Modern, tech-forward alumni services offering

**Value Proposition for Job Seekers:**
- Free access to AI career coaching (when sponsored by institutions)
- One-time profile investment enables ongoing job matching
- Personalized advocacy and story development
- Direct access to alumni-posted opportunities

### Secondary Model: Institutional Partnerships

**Pricing Structure for Partnerships:**
- **Pilot Phase**: Often structured as free-to-start with performance-based expansion
- **Full Partnership**: Annual licensing fees (e.g., $3K–$5K) for alumni-wide access
- **Performance Triggers**: Benchmarks for auto-committing to expanded contracts
  - User engagement metrics
  - Alumni association engagement increases
  - Successful placement outcomes

**White-Label Capabilities:**
- Platform can be branded with institution identity
- Email communications can originate from institution
- Custom landing pages and onboarding flows

## Market Context

### Institutional Challenges Addressed
- **Job Search Tool Gaps**: Many institutions lack modern hiring/matching platforms
- **Red Tape in Alumni Communication**: Traditional channels have procedural barriers
- **Demonstrating Value**: Need to prove worth to secure future donations and engagement
- **Scale Limitations**: Traditional coaching cannot meet demand (Emory: multiple daily requests)

### Timing Alignment
- **Holiday Season**: Ideal for year-end reflection and career planning
- **New Year Momentum**: "New Year, New Me" psychology increases engagement
- **Economic Conditions**: Alumni may need more support in job market

## Emory-Specific Context

### Emory's Current State
- **Platform**: Transitioning Emory Connects from Gravity to PeopleGrove (early 2026)
- **Gaps**: Hiring/job matching component has not been prioritized recently
- **Demand**: High demand for career coaching services
- **Channels**: Emory Connects, Salesforce Marketing Cloud emails

### Proposed Pilot Structure
- **Cohort Size**: 500 alumni (initial focus)
- **Marketing Channel**: Emory Connects platform
- **Timeline**: Launch before holidays, 6-week observation period
- **Offering**: Free access for pilot, performance-based expansion discussion

### Emory's Strategic Goals
- Provide valuable resources to alumni total
- Strengthen alumni connection to Emory
- Demonstrate value to support future fundraising
- Elevate hiring/career services as a priority area

## Key Considerations for Partnership

### Incentive Alignment
- **For Emory**: Demonstrate value to alumni, increase engagement, potentially drive donations
- **For Careerspan**: Secure reference customer, defensible pricing for future partnerships, user volume for data and improvement
- **For Alumni**: Free high-value career tool, easier access to alumni-hired opportunities

### Risk Mitigation
- **Pilot Structure**: Low-risk entry with clear performance metrics
- **No Commitment Pressure**: Expansion decisions based on observed outcomes
- **Flexible Pricing**: Careerspan willing to adjust based on institutional constraints

### Success Metrics
- User adoption rate (target: strong initial signup from 500-offered cohort)
- Engagement depth (stories created, coaching sessions used)
- Retention (30-day and ongoing engagement)
- Alumni association interaction (increased touchpoints, feedback)
- Hiring partner interest (alumni posting jobs, direct apply usage)

## Conclusion

Careerspan presents a compelling partnership opportunity for Emory, offering a modern, data-driven approach to alumni career services that addresses current gaps in Emory's offering. The company's proven engagement metrics, scalable technology, and flexible partnership structure provide a low-risk, high-potential avenue for Emory to demonstrate value to its alumni while supporting Careerspan's growth and market positioning.

The proposed 500-alumni pilot before the holidays provides an ideal testing ground to validate the partnership before committing to broader institutional adoption.
