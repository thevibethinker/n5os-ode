# B03: Stakeholder Intelligence

## Meeting Overview

**Date:** November 14, 2025  
**Participants:** Nicole Holubar (Emory Alumni Association), Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan Founder)  
**Context:** Partnership discussion to pilot Careerspan with Emory alumni

---

## Stakeholder Analysis

### Nicole Holubar

**Role:** Emory Alumni Association - Programs & Engagement Staff  

**Interests:**

- **Primary Interest:** Providing valuable, impactful resources to alumni that strengthen their connection to Emory
- **Secondary Interests:**
  - Testing innovative approaches to alumni engagement, particularly in the hiring/job matching space
  - Gathering data and metrics to demonstrate value to leadership
  - Transitioning away from existing tools (gravity → PeopleGrove platform migration)
  - Elevating hiring/job placement as a strategic focus area for the alumni association
  - Increasing alumni engagement that can translate to future donor relationships

**Skepticism & Concerns:**

- **Monetization Model:** Uncertainty about the pricing structure and whether a hiring-based model aligns with Emory's goals
- **Pilot Duration:** Questioned whether a time-limited pilot (originally discussed as ~1 month) is the right approach versus an open-ended rollout
- **Implementation Logistics:** Practical concerns about marketing channels, distribution mechanisms, and internal process constraints (e.g., Salesforce Marketing Cloud approvals, official email channels)
- **Resource Allocation:** Whether the team has capacity to manage the pilot alongside existing coaching program demand

**Leverage:**

- **Access & Distribution:** Direct access to Emory alumni database and ability to approve distribution to specific segments
- **Marketing Channels:** Control over multiple communication touchpoints (Emory Connects platform, official email lists)
- **Leadership Gateway:** Acts as the primary internal advocate and gatekeeper for leadership buy-in
- **Social Proof Authority:** Can validate and legitimize Careerspan to Emory alumni through official endorsement
- **Budget Authority:** Has discretion over small-scale pilot budgets ($3-5K range mentioned as viable)

---

### Vrijen Attawar

**Role:** Careerspan Founder - Alumni-led career development platform  

**Interests:**

- **Primary Interest:** Generating revenue to sustain and grow Careerspan; establishing paid partnerships with universities
- **Secondary Interests:**
  - Building momentum and credibility with alumni associations to leverage with other schools (anchor pricing strategy)
  - Creating a self-reinforcing ecosystem where alumni use Careerspan to hire other alumni
  - Demonstrating proven efficacy through real-world data (92% self-advocacy improvement, 80% discovery of new opportunities)
  - Accumulating user engagement data to prove value and retention (30%+ 30-day retention)
  - Establishing Emory as a flagship partnership case study
  - Centralizing alumni job distribution through Careerspan platform

**Skepticism & Concerns:**

- **Payment Risk:** Concern that Emory may delay or avoid payment despite using the product ("I would love to be paid this time around")
- **Pilot Drag:** Risk that a free or heavily discounted pilot could become indefinitely extended without converting to paid
- **Data Loss:** Alumni signing up independently (without Emory code) could prevent tracking and limit ability to demonstrate value to leadership
- **Leadership Buy-in:** Uncertainty about how Emory leadership will respond to pricing proposals and whether benchmarks will be honored
- **Perception Management:** Need to position Careerspan as cost-effective on a "per-hour coaching" basis ($4-6/hour) versus per-license

**Leverage:**

- **Unique Value Proposition:** Alumni-led organization with authentic understanding of alumni needs
- **Proven Results:** Strong engagement metrics from existing partnerships (Columbia SPS, UMass Eisenberg) with testimonials
- **Flexibility:** Willing to structure deals creatively (low upfront cost, performance-based triggers, revenue sharing)
- **User Experience:** High product engagement with demonstrated retention and reactivation patterns
- **Social Proof Network:** Access to testimonials from current users (e.g., Rhett at UMass, Hugo Award-winning writer, former Netflix chief of staff)
- **Timing Alignment:** Perfect seasonal timing (pre-holidays = reflection period, post-holidays = active job search season)

---

## Power Dynamics

### Balanced Partnership

Both stakeholders bring meaningful leverage to this negotiation:

- **Nicole** controls the distribution channel and internal credibility at Emory
- **Vrijen** controls the product, data, and external credibility with other institutions

The conversation reflected mutual respect and aligned interests in creating a successful pilot that can scale to a long-term partnership.

### Key Decision Points

1. **Pricing Structure:** Vrijen proposed $3-5K annual fee with per-license costs of $2-4; Nicole needs to justify this internally
2. **Pilot Scope:** Agreed to ~500 alumni as initial cohort; Nicole to determine distribution strategy
3. **Success Metrics:** Will track engagement, retention, and downstream alumni association interactions
4. **Conversion Path:** Discussion of "first X free" model with conditional commitment to paid contract upon hitting benchmarks

### Risk Allocation

- **Vrijen assumes risk:** Product performance, customer support, upfront development work
- **Nicole assumes risk:** Political capital with leadership, budget allocation, internal process navigation

---

## Alignment Assessment

### High Alignment Areas

- Both want to serve alumni and strengthen Emory connections
- Both see value in a pilot approach to de-risk the partnership
- Both recognize the importance of data and metrics for internal justification
- Both want to move quickly (pre-holidays timeline)

### Potential Friction Points

- Revenue model: Vrijen wants paid partnership; Nicole may need to justify costs or prefer free/low-cost models
- Timeline pressure: Vrijen wants to launch within 3 weeks; Nicole has internal process constraints
- Long-term commitment: Vrijen wants lock-in; Nicole may want flexibility based on pilot results

### Mitigation Strategies

- **Performance-based commitments:** Conditional paid contract triggered by hitting specific engagement benchmarks
- **Flexible pricing:** Low upfront cost with upside for Careerspan based on usage/success
- **Shared storytelling:** Co-marketing opportunity for both organizations to demonstrate innovation
- **Data-driven decision making:** Clear metrics and reporting to reduce subjectivity in success evaluation

---

## Next Steps for Leverage Optimization

### For Nicole

- Leverage pilot success to secure larger budget for full rollout
- Use engagement data to demonstrate impact to leadership
- Position pilot as innovative/differentiating for Emory among peer institutions
- Build case study for alumni association annual report/donor communications

### For Vrijen

- Deliver exceptional support to ensure high engagement and positive sentiment
- Package testimonials and data for Nicole's internal presentations
- Use Emory partnership to attract other alumni associations (anchor pricing)
- Develop white-label branding options to reinforce Emory connection
