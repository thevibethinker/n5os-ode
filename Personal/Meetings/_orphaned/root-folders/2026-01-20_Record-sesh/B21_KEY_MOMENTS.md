# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"The problem is essentially a tragedy of the commons. With regards to a recruiter's time and attention merely. By exponentially increasing the volume of applications for even a subset of the total applicants, you are significantly diluting everyone's signal."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Analyzing the systemic flaw in AI-assisted job application tools. Identifies that while individually beneficial to candidates, mass automation creates a collective harm where everyone's signal is diluted and genuinely qualified candidates get "lost in the sauce." Frames the issue as an economic externality problem rather than a technical one.

**"It's not that you doing that doesn't in a vacuum increase the candidates odds. It's that when you do that for everyone, then everyone's odds diminish roughly equally and even the folks that would otherwise stand out in a normal volume level start to get lost in the sauce."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Elaborating on the tragedy of the commons mechanism. Clarifies that the problem isn't that individual AI applications don't work—they do—but that their universal adoption negates the advantage and creates a worse system for all. Distinguishes between individual benefit and collective harm.
