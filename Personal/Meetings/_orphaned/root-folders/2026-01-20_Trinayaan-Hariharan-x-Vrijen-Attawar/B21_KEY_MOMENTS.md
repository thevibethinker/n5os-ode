# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"I think the big fucking problem right now is that everyone is accepting the current paradigm, which is resume based and is essentially adversarial between the candidate and the employer."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Framing the core systemic issue in hiring. The resume-based approach creates opposition between candidates and employers, making both parties worse off rather than creating value.

**"What's actually happening is that our ability to assess signal at scale has always been dogshit and is now completely obsolete."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Explaining why the hiring market appears broken. It's not that jobs have become impossibly hard to get—our assessment mechanisms are fundamentally broken in an AI-augmented world.

**"I know multiple very successful founders, big companies don't look at inbound anymore. You know, if they look at inbound, they'll look at the first hundred people."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Revealing a hidden reality that candidates don't understand. High-quality founders have abandoned inbound applications entirely due to signal noise, relying on heuristics and shortcuts instead.

**"So like one of my general rules is like if everyone's like thinking at one level, I go one level deeper... And one level below that is how do I know someone can think?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Strategic framework for differentiation. While everyone races to assess coding skills, the real opportunity lies in assessing how candidates think—especially how they leverage AI to solve problems.

**"There is no candidate likes video interviews. And it's like why would you make a test where, where a non zero portion of your testing pool becomes worse because of the nature of the test. That is the fucking stupidest thing I've ever heard, right?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* First-principles critique of video interviews. A valid assessment tool shouldn't disadvantage a subset of candidates due to the testing format itself.

**"My belief is that a big unused mechanism is the dialectical discursive... Like if the AI could do the interview of helping figure out how I think and really honestly assess that, that is more meaningful than directly assessing capability."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Proposed alternative to direct assessment. Using AI as a conversational partner to understand how candidates think, rather than testing their ability to solve isolated problems.

**"46% of hires fail by the 18 month mark. 90% of the reason they fail is a values mindset or soft skills mismatch."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Mind-blowing statistic revealing what actually matters in hiring. We fixate on technical assessment (which we're good at) while ignoring culture fit (where we fail catastrophically).

**"I think successful new school product will shift the paradigm from looking at a lot of crap candidates for one gem to looking at 10 gems straight."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Vision for the future of hiring technology. The inevitable swing back from volume-based funnels to precision-based candidate evaluation.

**"The ironically the like midwits of the tech world always go towards a hot sexy thing and will inevitably be beaten by the actually competitive people, right? So almost always going for the hot sexy thing is a bad idea."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Strategic advice on market positioning. Competitive advantages often exist in unsexy, overlooked areas where "smart" people aren't flocking.

**"Because AI is consolidating power amongst fewer. There are going to be more overpowered 10x employees of the future. It's not going to be 10x engineers, it's going to be more 10x employees. And like a 10 person billion dollar company is like how it's going to go."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Vision for AI's impact on organizational structure. AI compresses optimal team size, creating superpowered individuals and radically smaller companies.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"How do we assess someone's ability to prompt and how do we assess their ability to think in systems terms?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar (self-posed)  
*Significance:* Reframes the assessment problem from testing coding skills to testing AI-augmented thinking. This is where the real competitive advantage lies in an AI-first world.

**"Is there a better assessment that we can give out to candidates? You can actually see signal of how they can better use who's a good user of AI, who's a better architect of directing the AI towards like making better products."**  
— Trinayaan Hariharan  
*Significance:* Identifies a critical gap in current assessment methodology. Take-home projects don't distinguish between AI-assisted work and genuine understanding—the review process is the weak point.

**"What's actually happening is that our ability to assess signal at scale has always been dogshit and is now completely obsolete."**  
— Vrijen Attawar (framed as question by implication)  
*Significance:* This raises the strategic question: if we can't assess technical capability at scale anymore, what can we assess? The answer shifts focus to thinking ability, communication, and culture fit.

**"There has to be like just logically there is no fucking way we can keep going the current direction, right? There has to be a flip back."**  
— Vrijen Attawar (rhetorical)  
*Significance:* Questions the sustainability of current hiring practices. The market pivot from volume-based to precision-based hiring will happen—but when, and what will trigger it?

**"What if we reimagined a world where instead of recruiters making money in the middle, we got the most talented people to work in a system that provides collective ownership of the profits of the system?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* Radical rethinking of talent market economics. Suggests a cooperative model where top talent owns the recruiting infrastructure, creating aligned incentives and sustainable advantage.

**"How do I get someone to know? How can I know if someone knows how to use AI to code?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar (two-level deep thinking)  
*Significance:* This recursive questioning demonstrates the strategic depth required to solve AI assessment problems. The answer lies one more level down: assessing thinking ability directly.

**"Could you get the top, you know, prospect from your school that was about to go to Jane street, right, to say, I'm committing it. I'm actually going to take a cut from my Jane street bonus and put it into this because I know that this will grow and pay me back?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar (speculative)  
*Significance:* Questions whether talent networks can self-fund through collective ownership. Touches on creating sustainable talent pools without transactional recruiters in the middle.

**"How do you make... bigger scope projects using AI? Is there a better assessment that we can give out to candidates?"**  
— Trinayaan Hariharan  
*Significance:* Raises the challenge of scaling assessment beyond simple coding problems. As AI commoditizes basic implementation, assessment must evaluate architectural thinking and product sense at scale.
