## Product Strategy

**Core Strategic Shift**: [B40.D2] Transition from standalone career search platform to **embedded widgets + personal brand leverage model**

**Key Insight**: "We almost don't leverage [our personalities] as much as we could... we do more than basically anyone can, but we don't do a maximum extent. That's also because we're busy trying to do something we're not good at, which is running a startup company. If we instead focused on selling ourselves..."

**Positioning Evolution**:
- FROM: Full-stack career platform competing on features
- TO: Talent agency with proprietary tech (embedded widgets distributed via personal brand)

**Strategic Rationale**:
- 2-person team cannot maintain complex platform
- Better distribution via communities (universities, alumni groups)
- Aligns with V+L's actual strength (coaching/consulting, not platform ops)
- Returns to "career coaches" identity with tech leverage

## Passive Career Agent Model

**Product Vision**: [B40.D3] Career agent that runs passively on behalf of candidates, surfacing opportunities to hiring managers

**Logan Quote**: "You don't want an agent, you want [one] running on your behalf... you are an agent, your top talent... you've got this working on your behalf, surfacing opportunities."

**Differentiation**:
- NOT active job search (user-initiated)
- Passive background service monitoring opportunities
- Direct-to-hiring-manager matching (bypass HR/ATS)
- Candidate gets paid via signing bonus from saved recruiter fees

**Revenue Model**: [B40.T8]
- Talent agency commission split
- 50%+ of typical recruiter fee ($20k placement → ~$10k to employer savings, $7k to candidate, $3k to Careerspan)
- Scales without platform overhead
- Direct value exchange vs. subscription complexity

## Product Scope for Skeleton Crew

**Core Constraint**: [B40.T5] Product must be maintainable and distributable by 2 people (V + L)

**Requirements**:
1. **Low maintenance overhead**: Self-sustaining features, minimal manual intervention
2. **Low inference costs**: AI efficiency critical for unit economics
3. **Community distribution**: Can be "handed out" to universities/alumni groups
4. **Simple onboarding**: Minimal sales touch required

**What This Means**:
- Focus on core matching engine + career agent
- Eliminate complex platform features requiring ongoing support
- Automated candidate intake and matching
- Partnership-based distribution (not direct sales)

**V Quote**: "We need something, but the good thing is with this approach, it can be long-term. If we largely set this up, we pay us a hard time to maintain it. You and I can actually be liberated to then just build our personal brands in a way that's aligned with the product."

## Distribution Strategy

**V Quote**: "We have something that we can just hand out to universities, hand out to alumni groups... just say, we are a talent agency. We have proprietary tech, and this is what we do."

- Self-service candidate intake

- Candidates acquired via community distribution
