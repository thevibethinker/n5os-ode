# STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

**Perspective:** Speaking as sales & marketing leader (15+ years experience, 11 products to market)

## Market & Industry Insights

1. **Constant marketing necessity in noisy markets**
   - Observation: "Even Apple spends 3-4 billion dollars a year on marketing. Everyone knows the iPhone, but they still have to market it because the marketplace is just noisy."
   - Implication: Marketing must be "always on" - not because consumers are ignorant, but because information environment is saturated
   - Strategic Value: Positions Careerspan marketing not as temporary awareness phase, but as sustained effort; justifies ongoing spend and presence

2. **Pain point universality in hiring**
   - Observation: "HR managers are crying...there's a lot of pain...on both sides of the table for folks, employers who are hiring and people who are just looking to put their best foot forward and be seen for who they really are"
   - Implication: Friction point affects entire ecosystem (supply and demand); not isolated to one side
   - Strategic Value: Validates that solution addresses systemic problem, not niche issue; early-adopter customers likely motivated by acute pain

3. **Emotional energy as market signal**
   - Observation: "You guys cut through all of it...There's a little bit of that magic I'm hoping to bring here. That emotion, that pent up emotion, the frustration that is there on both sides of the table - that's going to work for us...that's a form of energy that just kind of pent up."
   - Implication: Market dissatisfaction creates momentum for new solutions when positioned correctly; messaging should tap into frustration
   - Strategic Value: Suggests messaging should emphasize pain relief and validation of frustration, not just feature benefits

4. **Competitive dynamics in mature product categories**
   - Observation: "There's this idea that some customers are using your number one competitor, but they're tired of them and they really want to give you guys a look"
   - Implication: Market switching possible when satisfaction threshold drops; customers actively seeking alternatives
   - Strategic Value: Positioning should emphasize what incumbents are missing; identify switcher profile as high-value early adopters

5. **Relevance over volume as marketing differentiator**
   - Observation: "It's not about being louder. You just have to be more relevant and more in touch and more in tune."
   - Implication: In noisy markets, precision targeting and message-market fit more valuable than broad reach
   - Strategic Value: May favor targeted, high-relevance campaigns over broad-based awareness plays; quality of audience over quantity
