# B44: GTM & Sales Intelligence

## GTM Strategy

**Partnership Channel - Superposition AI**:
- Partner model: Flat rate per magic link released
- Value prop for partner: Save time on candidate review while maintaining quality
- Partner continues posting where they post, uses magic links instead of regular ATS
- Careerspan handles candidate cultivation, partner doesn't spend effort
- Revenue sharing on placements through mutual back-scratching with founder network (via Marvin connection)

**Target Distribution**:
- Founders that Careerspan has hard time selling to directly
- Superposition handles those relationships, uses magic links
- Careerspan gets cut of revenue

## Messaging & Positioning

**What Works with HR**:
- "Seeing candidates at their best" [B40.D3]
- Cultivating relationships vs. factory farming
- Addressing latent guilt about current hiring process
- Providing hedge for evaluation

**What Doesn't Work**:
- Proprietary methodology claims [B40.D1]
- Psychometrics/behavioral science heavy positioning
- Invites skepticism and methodology validation requests
- HR professionals "regularly inundated" with such claims

**Successful Framing**:
- Talent combinatorics (skill + attitude + capability combination) [B40.D2]
- Reject stack ranking paradigm
- Focus on complementary strengths vs. hierarchical comparison

## Sales Process

**Video Marketing Development**:
- Two 60-second video scripts for employer audience [B40.T2]
- Script A: Technology introduction (what it is, how it works, problems solved)
- Script B: Buyer-centric (HR professional as hero, tool makes them better)
- Process: Script → Audio generation → Visual mockups → Team review

**Buzzword Strategy**:
- Acceptable: "Empirical data" (sounds robust) [B40.T1]
- Acceptable: "AI analysis" for tech early adopters
- Avoid: Specific methodology claims (psychometrics, competency modeling, etc.)
- Note: Mental model HR has = tests (MBTI, Enneagram)
- Challenge: HR struggles to map qualitative → insights as reliable process
