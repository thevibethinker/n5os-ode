# B43: Product Intelligence

## Product Strategy

**Core Positioning Shift**: Move from behavioral science/psychometrics framing to talent combinatorics and "seeing candidates at their best" [B40.D1, D2, D3]

**Key Product Insights**:
- Soft skills quantification is "black art" that didn't exist before AI
- System does NOT do hard skills assessments - captures self-reported hard skills usage
- Analysis engine works across multiple personas: early adopters (AI-focused), science-oriented professionals, HR artistry practitioners

## Technical Approach

**Data Framework**:
- Resume + 10 stories = "empirical data" [B40.T1]
- Systematic observation approach to knowledge extraction
- Quantification of qualitative information

**Differentiation**:
- Rejects stack ranking/ranked lists [B40.D2]
- Focuses on skill combinatorics: "What combination of skills, attitudes, and capabilities make someone qualified?"
- Framework: Compare what different candidates bring (Ilsa vs. Ilya vs. Rochel) rather than hierarchical superiority

## User Experience

**Target Personas**:
1. Early adopters: "AI analysis" is sufficient signal
2. Science-oriented: Want hard science backing into profession (but NOT proprietary methodology claims)
3. HR artistry: Value matchmaking intuition, want tools that enhance rather than replace

**Key Value Propositions**:
- Cultivate relationships vs. processing people
- Judge candidates by their best vs. bad interview days
- Hedge for evaluation: comprehensive view before interview
