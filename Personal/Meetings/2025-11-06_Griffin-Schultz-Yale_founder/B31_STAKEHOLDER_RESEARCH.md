## Insight 2: Current Resume-Based ATS Approach Is Becoming Adversarial, Not Selective

**Title:** The Paradox of AI-Enabled Resume Optimization—It Eliminates Signal, Not Noise

**Evidence:**
> "When the marginal cost of fabrication is zero and I can just say, give me something that will cross the filter and you get it, then you have completely eviscerated the like, incentive paradigm right now. All of a sudden, every sketchy actor out there as a candidate, which of which there are, is now perversely incentivized to say whatever."

**Why it matters:** This signals a **market inflection point**. Traditional resume-based hiring is breaking down not because the tool is bad, but because the incentive structure has inverted. When candidates can cheaply generate perfect resumes via AI, the signal-to-noise ratio collapses. This makes differentiation strategies that rely on deeper candidate data (Careerspan's thesis) suddenly more valuable.

**Signal strength:** ● ● ● ● ○
- Not yet obvious to mainstream; Griffin called this insight "outstanding"
- Aligns with emerging hiring manager pain point (hiring teams reporting increased resume fraud/fabrication)
- Not yet systematic validation from recruiters, but logically sound

**Category:** Hiring Manager Pain Points / Market Dynamics / Product Strategy

**Domain credibility (Griffin):** ● ● ○ ○ ○
- **Source type:** SECONDARY (Griffin is observing this from MBA perspective + conversations, not operating as hiring manager)
- **Firsthand?** Partly—he's job seeking and seeing this dynamic firsthand; not from employer side
- **Weight justification:** Smart synthesis by Vrijen; Griffin validated the logic but isn't a hiring decision-maker. Needs corroboration from actual recruiters/hiring teams.

**Strategic implication:** This validates Careerspan's core thesis—candidates need a better way to represent themselves, and employers need a better way to evaluate. The window is NOW because the old system is collapsing.
