# Key Moments, Quotes & Insights

## Strategic Insights

### Philosophy on Shipping vs. Perfection
**Quote**: "We're already going to be so many leagues better than what resume analysis does that we don't need to be perfect at this stage."
- **Context**: Discussion about accepting internal inconsistencies in candidate profiles when users edit deal breakers
- **Significance**: This encapsulates the team's pragmatic approach to MVP development
- **Implication**: Company is confident in competitive advantage; focused on getting to market and iterating based on real customer feedback

### User Experience Philosophy: Progress Over Lightness
**Quote**: "Keep it feeling light and simple and like the user is making progress as they move along the application."
- **Speaker**: Rochel (on design decision to use modal vs. full page)
- **Significance**: Reveals thoughtful UX philosophy - modal keeps cognitive load low while maintaining sense of accomplishment
- **Related Decision**: This philosophy drove the entire Direct Apply flow design

### Trust-Building Through Transparency
**Quote**: "In the early days to establish trust. That will be important."
- **Speaker**: V (on maintaining access to original resumes alongside Careerspan analysis)
- **Significance**: Shows understanding that employers need both the innovative analysis AND the familiar format while they build trust
- **Tactical**: Demonstrates nuanced go-to-market thinking

### Validation Strategy for Filtering
**Quote**: "As long as we can reverse prove that our system was good at filtering, we should be solid."
- **Speaker**: V
- **Significance**: Team has a clear validation strategy for demonstrating product quality to prospects
- **Implies**: Success criteria is about demonstrating filtering capability through data
