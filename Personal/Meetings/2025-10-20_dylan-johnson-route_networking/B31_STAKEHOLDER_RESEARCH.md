# STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

**Perspective:** Speaking as relationship manager at fintech bank serving founders

## Key Insights

1. **Fintech Banking is Commodity Space**
   - Observation: Dylan mentioned saturated market; Route competes on service/rates vs. Mercury, Brex
   - Implication: Switching costs are real but surmountable if value is clear
   - Strategic Value: Careerspan should benchmark switching timeline against urgency of margin improvement

2. **Founder Relationships > Product Features**
   - Observation: Route emphasizes concierge service, dedicated relationship manager, human support over tech
   - Implication: For founder companies, relationship with banker matters as much as product
   - Strategic Value: Route's go-to-market is community/event-based, not product-focused; suggests partnership potential

3. **Go-to-Market through Events/Community**
   - Observation: Route hosts 5-10 events/week across NY/SF/Boston; prioritizes client participation
   - Implication: Event sponsorship requires being a Route client first (creates flywheel)
   - Strategic Value: Co-hosting Careerspan's "blind date for hiring" event could be pre-negotiated condition for banking switch

4. **Institutional Backing Matters to Founders**
   - Observation: Dylan led with Webster Bank partnership (100-year-old, 0B AUM); distinguished from regional banks
   - Implication: Safety/regulatory risk is top-of-mind for founders; fintech needs institutional comfort
   - Strategic Value: Careerspan should evaluate stability factor when comparing banks

5. **Treasury Rates Are Margin Play**
   - Observation: Dylan asked about treasury usage; Careerspan currently not using it
   - Implication: As revenue grows, 0.5-1% improvement on treasury rates becomes material
   - Strategic Value: December decision likely hinges on cash flow projections and runway runway extension needs
