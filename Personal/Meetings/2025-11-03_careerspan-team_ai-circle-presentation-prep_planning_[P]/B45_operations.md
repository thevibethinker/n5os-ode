## Process Gaps Identified

### Decision Documentation
**Current:** Verbal agreements in meetings, no tracking
**Impact:** Risk of misalignment, forgotten commitments
**Solution:** 
- Meeting notes with explicit decisions
- Owner and timeline for each decision
- Shared doc visible to all team members

### Feedback Cycles
**Current:** Wait until run-through for comprehensive feedback
**Impact:** Late-stage changes, wasted effort on wrong direction
**Solution:**
- Earlier draft reviews (async if possible)
- Specific feedback requests vs. general review
- Clear "ready for review" vs. "work in progress" states

### Asset Management
**Current:** No clear system for storing/sharing work products
**Impact:** Difficulty finding materials, version confusion, lost work
**Solution:**
- Designated storage location (cloud folder, drive, etc.)
- Naming conventions for files
- Version control practice
- Archive strategy for completed events

### Follow-Up Execution
**Current:** General agreement to follow up, no specific process
**Impact:** Risk of delayed or missed follow-ups, inconsistent quality
**Solution:**
- Contact capture template (name, context, interests, promises)
- Follow-up email templates
- Assignment of follow-up ownership
- Timeline and tracking mechanism

## Operational Excellence Opportunities

### Event Preparation Template
**Create Standard Process:**
1. Content development phase (2 weeks before)
   - Draft slides and demos
   - Async review cycles
   - Iteration based on feedback

2. Integration phase (1 week before)
   - Run-through with all elements
   - Technical testing
   - Q&A preparation
   - Final adjustments

3. Logistics phase (3 days before)
   - Equipment confirmation
   - Venue coordination
   - Role assignments
   - Backup plans

4. Event day (day of)
   - Arrival and setup checklist
   - Technical verification
   - Run-of-show document
   - Debrief scheduling

**Benefits:**
- Repeatable process reduces stress
- Earlier problem identification
- Better outcomes through systematic approach
- Learning compounds across events

### Demo Library System
**Build Reusable Asset Repository:**
- Catalog of recorded demos by use case
- Metadata: audience type, length, key messages
- Quality ratings and update status
- Easy search and retrieval

**Benefits:**
- Don't recreate demos for each event
- Mix and match for different contexts
- Consistent quality and messaging
- Easier onboarding for new team members

### User Feedback Pipeline
**Formalize Vrijen's User Research:**
- Standard questions for user conversations
- Note-taking template with key fields
- Regular synthesis and sharing sessions
- Product/marketing implication tracking

**Benefits:**
- Systematic validation of assumptions
- Easier to identify patterns
- Better handoff to product/engineering
- Competitive intelligence gathered consistently

### Content Repurposing Framework
**Maximize ROI on Content Creation:**
- Event presentation → launch video
- Demos → website/marketing assets
- User conversations → case studies
- Q&A → FAQ and documentation

**Process:**
- During creation, tag with potential uses
- After event, schedule repurposing sprint
- Assign ownership for each derivative
- Track usage and effectiveness

## Risk Management

### Identified Risks
1. **Technical Failure at Event** - Demo doesn't play, equipment issues
2. **Message Misalignment** - Ben and Vrijen telling different stories
3. **Overwhelming Audience** - Too complex, people tune out
4. **Lost Follow-Ups** - Interested people fall through cracks
5. **Unsustainable Pace** - Team burning out on event prep

### Mitigation Strategies
1. **Technical:** Pre-recorded demos, equipment testing, backup plans
2. **Alignment:** Run-through with feedback, explicit messaging review
3. **Complexity:** User-test content, simplify where possible, acknowledge strangeness
4. **Follow-Up:** Contact capture system, immediate post-event process
5. **Sustainability:** Realistic scoping, delegation, process improvement for next time
