## STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

**Perspective:** Speaking as career-tech founders navigating transition from growth-mode to survival-focused business model

### Key Insights from Career Tech Market

1. **Market validates the problem, not the solution delivery model**
   - Observation: "We have two extremely well funded competitors who have shittier tech"
   - Implication: Market demand is real (competitors are well-funded); tech quality is table stakes but not differentiator
   - Strategic value: Focus on go-to-market efficiency over product perfection; markets reward market access over features

2. **Enterprise/Secondary-screen use case has structural limitations**
   - Observation: "Issues we've been encountering in using it as a secondary screen"
   - Implication: Enterprise buyers have different priorities than individual users; integration overhead is high
   - Strategic value: Pivot toward B2C or lightweight B2B (direct candidate relationships) may be more viable than complex enterprise sales

3. **Acquisition market in career tech is active and healthy**
   - Observation: Multiple investors saying "work towards an acquisition"
   - Implication: Exits are happening; market consolidation is real
   - Strategic value: Positioning as acquisition target (not independent unicorn) is legitimate and potentially more achievable path

4. **Retention metrics matter more than growth rate to investors in uncertain times**
   - Observation: "All of our retention numbers are extremely strong... reactivation numbers are through the roof"
   - Implication: Engaged user base is rare; signals long-term value even at smaller scale
   - Strategic value: Investors value quality of engagement over quantity of growth; this changes capital requirements

5. **Decentralized/Community-Driven Models Are Gaining Credibility**
   - Observation: "Decentralized talent network line has been landing. Everyone I've spoken to about it has been extremely eager"
   - Implication: Market is shifting away from centralized platform control; open, community-driven approaches resonate
   - Strategic value: Could position Careerspan as "open network player" vs. proprietary competitor; differentiates against well-funded rivals

### Broader Industry Signals

- **Founder burnout/pragmatism wave:** Multiple founders shifting from "build the perfect product" to "achieve sustainable exit"
- **VC market cooling:** Investors actively steering companies toward acquisition paths, not unicorn chasing
- **Talent supply constraint:** Talent quality remains bottleneck even with abundant capital

### Implication for Careerspan Strategy

Current pivot (low-power mode + acquisition focus) aligns with market reality, not just internal need. This is a feature, not a bug.
