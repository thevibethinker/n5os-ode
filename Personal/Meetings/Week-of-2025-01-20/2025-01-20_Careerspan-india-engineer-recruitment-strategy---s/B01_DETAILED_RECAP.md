# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
**Date:** 2025-01-20  
**Topic:** Careerspan India Engineer Recruitment Strategy  
**Participants:** V (Vrijen), Shivam (recruitment partner), Logan (Lovable dev team)  
**Duration:** ~45 minutes  

## Chronological Summary

### Opening & Context
- V and Shivam catch up after a few months
- Shivam shares recent feedback from an Indian engineer about mismatch between US company expectations and accessing talent abroad
- Logan joins the call

### Value Proposition Discussion
Shivam explains the current recruitment model gap:
- Today: Just sending resumes, doesn't paint complete picture
- Proposed: Careerspan profiles provide elevator pitch + resume + GitHub + deeper dive

Shivam envisions sending employers 3 candidates with full Careerspan profiles instead of just resumes. Goal: start with 10 candidates, scale to 20, 25, 50.

### Addressing Candidate Concerns
V asks if they can get 30-50% of engineers to create a Careerspan story. Shivam shares an engineer's concern about US startups nickel-and-diming with low wages. Shivam counters by explaining his deals are at $100-150k/year, which is very competitive in India and shows solid funded startup capital behind hires.

### Strategy: Supply Side
- Shivam has a list of 65-80 high-quality engineers to start
- Will start with this initial pool
- If traction shows, will amplify via Instagram and LinkedIn (Indians very active on both)
- Shivam to spin up a custom landing page specific to this value prop: "Do this Careerspan thing and I'll be able to get you a job faster with better representation of your strengths"

### Strategy: Demand Side
- Lovable has built an intake form for customers (founders/companies)
- Form captures: roles, tech stacks, website, success metrics
- Form will create JD/hiring role in Careerspan
- Lovable team says implementation is easy
- Technical flow: Framer form → embedded intake → Lovable → sends payload to Careerspan

### V's Enhancement Ideas
V suggests adding voice message capability to the intake form:
- Observes founders respond well to "just send me a voice message"
- AI can transcribe voice and convert to structured rubric
- Enhances job descriptions with founder's actual intent
- Addresses pain point: founders/compressed time generate JDs that don't reflect true needs
- Shivam loves the idea, will explore via Zapier/Lovable

### Proof Points & Testimonials
V suggests getting testimonials from 1-2 eager engineers:
- Video testimonials talking about how Careerspan helped them feel more prepped
- Would build hype/credibility
- Logan agrees, wants to do user research calls
- Even mentions "scumbagging it" with an internal dev quick testimonial
- They agree to use existing Careerspan testimonials on landing page for now, pursue Indian engineer testimonials as phase 2

### Job Showcase Idea
V suggests taking Shivam's "sickest job" (most attractive role) and creating:
- A page with the JD + Careerspan link + explainer
- Could be on Shivam's landing page or Careerspan Notion board
- Shivam agrees to put "types of jobs Shivam is sourcing" on landing page

### Technical Implementation
Shivam's engineering team reviewed the Lovable integration:
- Framer site → embedded intake form → Lovable → Careerspan payload
- Team says it's "pretty doable and easy"
- Lovable team confirmed it's "super easy"
- V confident it's feasible

### Closing & Next Steps
- Shivam will get landing page up first
- Then review collateral together
- V will check for existing group chat, set up if needed
- All parties aligned and excited to execute
- Logan will connect with the 3 interested engineers first, send them process flow doc for V's review, then send to engineers

## Key Themes
1. **Two-sided marketplace building:** Simultaneously recruiting engineers (supply) and founders (demand)
2. **Quality over quantity:** Focus on high-quality Indian engineers with competitive salary offers ($100-150k)
3. **Social proof as accelerator:** Testimonials critical for adoption, especially from target demographic
4. **Friction reduction:** Voice messages to enhance JD quality, reduce founder burden
5. **Sequential rollout:** Landing page → initial pilot → testimonials → broader social promotion
