# B21: Key Moments

## High-Leverage Quotes and Turning Points

### KM1: Supply-Demand Gap Identification
**Quote:** *"There's a mismatch between what US Companies think they need to hire and how they access some of good talent abroad. And then between the candidate side, there's just some gaps that we think we can fill."*  
**Speaker:** Shivam  
**Context:** Opening the meeting with market insight after call with Indian engineer  
**Significance:** Defines the core opportunity - Careerspan can bridge the gap in international hiring by providing better candidate evaluation

### KM2: Value Proposition - Beyond Resumes
**Quote:** *"Whenever I get a requirement from a US Company, I go to my vendors or I source myself. We're just sending resumes. That's not a good evaluation or it doesn't paint a good picture or a complete picture of who the person is. I think Careerspan would be that tool that gives you a much better picture of who you're actually considering."*  
**Speaker:** Shivam  
**Context:** Explaining current recruitment model limitation  
**Significance:** Clear articulation of problem and solution - resumes are inadequate, Careerspan provides richer candidate profiles

### KM3: Salary Validation & Competitive Advantage
**Quote:** *"The deals that I'm working on, right, These are at 100k a year, 120, 150k a year, which not all of them are going to be like that. Not saying all of my deals are like that, but that is very competitive in India."*  
**Speaker:** Shivam  
**Context:** Addressing engineer concern about low compensation  
**Significance:** Validates market positioning - high-quality deals at competitive salaries differentiate from nickel-and-diming startups

### KM4: Voice Message Feature Proposal
**Quote:** *"One of the things that I observed in my inter ring founders was they really responded to this idea of us being able to tell them, hey, just send me a voice message... Turn it into a rubric that you can assess against and add that to the job description in a structured way."*  
**Speaker:** V  
**Context:** Proposing UX enhancement for founder intake  
**Significance:** Strategic product insight - addresses founder pain point (compressed time, poor JDs) with simple solution, potential differentiator

### KM5: Speed & Efficiency Vision
**Quote:** *"Yeah, it's really. It's really just a function of how quickly they fill up, because at that point then it's just, what, like a couple hour lag between getting a JD and scanning against it."*  
**Speaker:** V  
**Context:** Discussing speed of candidate matching once platform is live  
**Significance:** Highlights operational efficiency - once supply and demand are in platform, matching is rapid (hours, not days)

### KM6: Social Proof as Accelerator
**Quote:** *"If we see the traction, I can then pump this out on my Instagram, my LinkedIn, and I think it could get fraction... Because they're super active on both those platforms, Indians in general."*  
**Speaker:** Shivam  
**Context:** Explaining growth strategy if initial pilot succeeds  
**Significance:** Clear growth lever - social media amplification on platforms where target demographic is highly active
