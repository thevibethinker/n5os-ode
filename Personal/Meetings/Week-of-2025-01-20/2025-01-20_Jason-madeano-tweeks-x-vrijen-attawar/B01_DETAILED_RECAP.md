# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview

**Date:** January 20, 2025
**Participants:** Vrijen Attawar (V), Jason (Co-founder, Tweeks)
**Duration:** ~45 minutes
**Type:** Product discovery / exploratory call

## Chronological Summary

### Introduction (0-5 minutes)
- V apologized for being late, explained he was showing his co-founder Logan (a budding TikTok influencer with 13k+ subscribers) how to use Tweeks for creating UGC content around making LinkedIn more professional
- V expressed enthusiasm about Tweeks as a "clever cool product" that manipulates UI by interpreting source code and re-rendering based on instructions
- Jason confirmed the technical approach: Tweeks extracts page content, passes it to AI (Claude, etc.), makes surgical modifications to specific elements while preserving the rest of the webpage

### Company Background (5-12 minutes)
- Jason and his co-founder met at MIT 8-9 years ago (undergrad + masters together), worked in industry, then went through Y Combinator about a year ago
- Original company: AI technical interviews (similar to HackerRank, Coderpad) - HR tech/technical assessment space
- They recognized the limitations of traditional technical interviews (regurgitating algorithms under time pressure vs. actual skill assessment)
- They built an IDE plugin that watched how candidates used real development tools, not test environments
- They were "too early" - when pitching in Feb 2024, investors didn't understand "vibe coding"

### Product Evolution (12-18 minutes)
- Pivoted away from HR tech to explore different ideas
- Jason built the earliest Tweeks prototype in a weekend as a personal project
- Shared with co-founder and friends via internal YC message board - positive reception
- Realized this was what they should build naturally
- Tweeks is a Generative UI engine that can work as a browser extension or integrate directly into websites, spinning up PRs or custom builds for individual users

### Philosophy Discussion (18-28 minutes)
- V aligned with Jason's critique of traditional hiring (keyword-based systems, proxies vs. signal)
- V described his own product Careerspan: a conversational tool to extract authentic self-reflection from candidates, creating a new data layer for hiring
- Both agreed that AI makes keyword-based resume screening obsolete (too many turds to sift through)
- Discussion of "liquid UI" / semi-malleable apps - future where users can customize their UI experience (e.g., "I never want to see these fields again")
- Jason confirmed Tweeks has B2B potential for direct integration without extension

### V's Context & Motivations (28-35 minutes)
- V is a brand evangelist for Zo (Zo Computer), actively looking for interesting tools with programmatic APIs to introduce to founders
- V's thesis: future of productivity involves collapsing surface area to essential, programmable products
- V mentioned exploring integrations with companies like Aviato, Nine, and Plaid
- V expressed interest in being a power user and providing feedback
- V is actively trying to become more technical - sees Tweeks as a half-step toward "inspect element"

### Product Feedback & Usage (35-42 minutes)
- V identified "endless possibility problem" in onboarding - too many choices, no clear magic moment
- V suggested onboarding approach: ask favorite website + what they hate about the UI, then solve it
- V noted loading time issue - users might switch tabs before Tweek completes (ADHD attention challenge)
- V suggested gamified feedback loop: let users tweak the onboarding page live for free credits
- V requested API integration - wants a central command interface (Zo) for managing website preferences

### Live Demo & UI Discussion (42-45 minutes)
- V attempted to demo Tweeks on Twitter: replace hearts with peach emojis and "zo" with "bro" - glitched
- Jason explained the selector tool for identifying specific elements to modify
- V discovered "not interested" button was too subtle - UI blindness issue
- V suggested first-time modal to highlight where modifications occurred
- V emphasized importance of multiple touchpoints in first 2-3 days for product retention

### Closing Actions (45-50 minutes)
- V promised to send Jason LinkedIn Tweeks catalog for his co-founder Logan to create UGC content
- Jason confirmed they have a LinkedIn catalog (not extensive but growing)
- Jason invited V to Discord and expressed interest in chatting with Logan about UGC growth strategies
- Both expressed excitement about continued collaboration
- V offered to make intros and continue providing feedback

## Key Themes

1. **Philosophical alignment** on signal vs. proxy in hiring/assessment
2. **Liquid UI future** - customizable, semi-malleable applications
3. **Privacy & trust** - winners will be products that personalize while respecting data boundaries
4. **User experience challenges** - onboarding, time-to-value, attention management
5. **Growth & UGC** - interest in influencer partnerships and community-driven content creation
