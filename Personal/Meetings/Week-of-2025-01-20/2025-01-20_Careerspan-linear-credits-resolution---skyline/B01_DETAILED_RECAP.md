# Detailed Recap: Careerspan-Linear Credits Resolution

## Meeting Overview
A call between V (Careerspan founder) and Skyline (Linear support) to resolve a request for refund of unused Linear credits as Careerspan faces closure.

## Chronological Summary

### Opening and Context Setting
- Skyline confirms audio quality, checks in on V
- V acknowledges busy day, mentions "closing up Careerspan"
- V explains the situation: unexpected expenses and legal issues forced them to sell the company, but more immediately they need to "put it on ice" while selling
- V admits the refund request was a "massively dick move" but wanted to own it
- V states the urgency: needs to make payroll at the end of the week and a refund would help

### Decision and Discussion
- Skyline mentions the request was discussed by multiple people at Linear
- The COO's decision was "against your favor" (no refund)
- V accepts this decision: "That's fair. I can accept that. And I won't make a big fuss"
- V expresses appreciation for the discussion and asks if there's any way to reconsider, acknowledging not wanting to "mess with you guys"

### Exploring Alternatives
- V asks: "Can I get credit? Like, is there any way I can make something of the situation?"

- V reveals current usage: "Barely" - mainly a record of past work, would love to export for posterity
- V's concern: ensuring data doesn't get deleted on the free plan

### Creative Resolution Options
- Skyline presents an alternative: V can dispute the charge with Stripe
- V asks: "Will that ruin your day? Too much?"
- Skyline reassures: Linear is a large company and can handle disputes
- Skyline offers to send an email confirming the credit amount to support the dispute
- V appreciates this "gracious" solution

### Personal Connection and Empathy

### Meeting Conclusion

- Skyline promises to send email shortly with credit amount
- V confirms: "Thank you so much, Skyline. Have a wonderful day"
