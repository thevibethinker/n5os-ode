# B07 - Tone and Context

## Overall Vibe
**Energy Level:** High efficiency, casual rapport
**Tenor:** Informal standup - demo/handoff format
**Subtext:** "Here's what I built, go use it, I need to go"

## Emotional Dynamics

### Ilse's Energy
- **Confident but rushed** - "I have to go, probably not back today"
- **Slightly defensive** about unfinished work - "I know you want more bullshit in there, I haven't gotten to it"
- **Proud of safeguards** - explains failure prevention with clear satisfaction
- **Comfortable being direct** - uses casual profanity naturally

### V's Energy
- **Receptive and appreciative** - minimal interruption, quick affirmations
- **Genuinely excited** - "Hell yeah. Hell yeah. This is rock and roll."
- **Aligned on goals** - "that is exactly what we want"

## Unspoken Dynamics

### Team Cadence
- This feels like a recurring check-in pattern
- Ilse builds → demos to V → goes heads down again
- V tests and provides feedback → cycle continues

### Workload Distribution
- Ilse carrying technical implementation load
- V focused on customer/business side
- Logan's role unclear (mentioned but absent)

### Trust Level
- High - Ilse ships features and expects V to figure out usage
- Minimal handholding - "play around with it"
- V trusts Ilse's judgment on limits and safeguards

## Notable Phrases
- "I know you want more bullshit in there" - acknowledges V's appetite for features while setting boundaries
- "I was going to say you don't need to bother me" - almost said the quiet part out loud, shows desire for autonomy
- "This is rock and roll" - V's genuine appreciation, not performative

## Red Flags / Concerns
- **Logan's absence** - "I don't know where Logan is" suggests minor coordination gap
- **Minimal documentation** - feature handoff via live demo rather than written specs
- **Single point of failure** - Ilse clearly owns all technical implementation
