# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

> **CRITICAL NOTE:** This block captures non-actionable provocations. This meeting was primarily tactical debrief, limited conceptual content.

## Idea 1: The Anti-Interview Thesis

**The Provocation:**  
Video interviews are a losing bet because people fundamentally don't like them. The future isn't better interviews - it's replacing interviews with "nice questions and gentle coaching."

**Why It's Interesting:**  
This reframes the HR tech market. Instead of optimizing the interview experience, eliminate it. The job isn't "make interviews better" - it's "make evaluation delightful."

**Strategic Weirdness:**  
If Calendly builds video interviews, they're fighting for commodity market share. If they build Careerspan, they're creating a category.

## Idea 2: Contrarian Positioning as Trust Signal

**The Provocation:**  
Telling a potential acquirer "don't do what you're planning" is a power move that builds credibility.

**Why It's Interesting:**  
Most pitch conversations involve agreement and validation. Disagreement, when well-reasoned, signals you're thinking independently - exactly what an acquirer wants in talent.

**Strategic Weirdness:**  
The path to acquisition might be through constructive disagreement, not alignment.
