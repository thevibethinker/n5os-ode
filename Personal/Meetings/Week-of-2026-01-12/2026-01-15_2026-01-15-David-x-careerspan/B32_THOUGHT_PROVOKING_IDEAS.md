## 1. Data Companies as Distribution Channel

**Source Quote (V):**
> "These data companies that are basically eating each other alive and need new audiences for their data will be amenable to connecting with Zo and selling their data, having a new revenue stream."

**Provocation:**
In commoditized data markets, the scarce resource isn't data — it's access to users who will *use* data. Zo becomes valuable not as a data consumer, but as a data *distributor*. The platform that aggregates attention can extract rent from data providers fighting for shelf space.

**Strategic Weirdness:** What if Zo's moat isn't AI capability but *demand aggregation* — the ability to bring users to data, not data to users?

## 2. The "Counterintuitive Product" Protective Moat

**Source Quote (V):**
> "I think they are like a counterintuitive enough product that folks will not try to replicate them anytime soon until they see a lot more proof points."

**Provocation:**
Being *hard to understand* is a competitive advantage. If competitors can't quickly grok what you're building, they won't copy it — even if it's valuable. Counterintuitive products buy time for execution because copycats need conviction before committing resources.

**Implication:** There may be strategic value in *not* making Zo instantly comprehensible to the market.
