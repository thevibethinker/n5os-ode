### 2. Ray's Acquisition Framework

#### Leverage Through Competition
- **Ideal scenario**: Have 2-3 active acquisition conversations simultaneously
- Ray was "hopeful" for 2-3 but only one came through - reduced leverage
- Treat it like negotiating job offers - multiple options boost your position

#### Timing Management
- **Critical insight**: Manage conversation pacing strategically
- "Slow play" some conversations while accelerating others
- Goal: Get multiple parties to roughly the same negotiation point simultaneously
- Enables playing offers against each other

#### Transaction Structure Realities

**Upfront vs. Earnout**:
- If acquirer perceives risk, they'll push toward earnout-heavy structures
- "We're not going to pay anything upfront if it seems risky"
- Common: "X amount of value comes from earnout"

**Earnout Warning** (Ray's direct experience):
- Ray's company: Potential earnout was over $1M if everything went right
- Reality: "The company fucked it up" - earnout didn't materialize
- Walked away with "basically nothing" from earnout portion
- *Key insight*: "Earnout doesn't come out the way you hope for and dream for"

#### Personal Considerations
- Consider: "Do you want to get tied to this company for X years?"
- Ray limited his commitment to 1 year max
- Balance potential upside vs. personal freedom
- Model out scenarios: conservative, base case, optimistic
