# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

> **Filter:** High threshold. Only ideas that represent strategic novelty or challenge existing mental models.

---

## 1. The Economic Incentive Gateway

**Idea:** Technical products fail not because they're too hard, but because they lack immediate economic motivation. The learning curve is acceptable when the payoff is clear and personal.

**Source quote:** "You need an economic incentive. What's better economic incentive than... this is going to help you make money?"

**Provocation:** Most developer tools obsess over DX (developer experience) and onboarding. But what if the real unlock is **economic narrative** - making the "why learn this" answer about money rather than capability?

**Implication for Careerspan:** Interview prep tools might sell better if framed as "this gets you the $50K salary bump" rather than "this helps you prepare better."

---

## 2. The Advisory-Execution Gap as Market Position

**Idea:** The consulting market has a structural gap: advisors who diagnose but don't implement, and consultants who implement but lack strategic insight. The hybrid (advise + partner with execution firm) may be an underexploited positioning.

**Source:** David's articulation of the Wes Bush problem ("they say, oh you're missing this piece... Goodbye").

**Provocation:** Is Careerspan in the advisory gap? V diagnoses career problems and provides tools, but does the user have the execution capability to use them? Might Careerspan need an "execution partner" layer?

---

## 3. Learning as Byproduct, Not Goal

**Idea:** People don't want to learn tools; they want outcomes. The best education happens when learning is a side effect of building something useful.

**Source quote:** "In building the productivity tool, you learn how to use Zo."

**Provocation:** This inverts traditional education design. Instead of "learn X so you can do Y," it's "do Y and X becomes obvious." Could Careerspan's products be designed so that users become career strategists *by accident* while pursuing a specific job?
