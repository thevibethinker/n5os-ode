# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
**Date:** 2026-01-15  
**Participants:** Vrijen (V), Heather (Coach)  
**Type:** Coaching Session + Research/Discovery  
**Purpose:** Understand Acuity Scheduler usage patterns; prepare for Calendly conversation

## Context
V has an upcoming meeting with someone at Calendly and wanted to understand how a working coach actually uses scheduling software (Acuity) in practice.

## Chronological Summary

### Acuity Walkthrough (Opening)
Heather demonstrates her Acuity workflow:

**Primary Use Cases:**
1. Rescheduling appointments
2. Looking up client session history (in-session)
3. Quick reference for "when's our next meeting?"

**Key Feature:** Client-Centric View
- Heather can see all of V's scheduled sessions in one place
- Can see all past appointments
- This is different from Google Calendar which shows her schedule but not filtered by client

**Insight from V:**
> "Google is your meetings from your point of view. But sometimes you want your meetings filtered for a specific person."

### Session Counting & Accountability
Heather explains a practical use case:
- When clients approach final sessions, she reminds them
- Some clients dispute: "I don't think I've had 12 sessions"
- Acuity lets her count and verify
- Can track no-shows with notes

### Categorization Discussion
V asks about themes/cohorts:
- Does Heather categorize clients?
- Implied: Scheduling tools could support client segmentation

### Conversion Rate Analytics (Key Section)
Heather reveals her organization's current focus:

**Sales Funnel:**
1. Prospect signs up for intro call
2. Meets with Amy (initial screener)
3. Some drop off
4. Meets with coach
5. Some drop off
6. Signs up for coaching

**Current Initiative:**
- Tracking conversion rates by coach
- Coaches who don't convert get training
- Some coaches resist: "sales feels icky"
- Heather has sales training background, comfortable with it

**Insight:**
Conversion data helps identify coaching skill gaps (not just sales skills, but perhaps clarity, rapport, etc.)

### CRM vs. Calendar Discussion
- Conversion tracking could live in CRM
- But for coaches without robust CRM, scheduling tool could serve this function
- This is relevant positioning for Calendly

### Zapier & Tech Complexity
Heather shares frustrations:
- Zapier is harder than it seems
- Tech products often seem easier than they are
- Developers underestimate non-technical user struggles

**Example:** Domain authentication for email
- Hours spent debugging
- Turned out to be Google's problem
- But assumed it was her mistake

**Heather's Request:**
> "Simplicity while also having the functionality that people need. That's tricky."

### AI Integration Thesis
V articulates emerging thinking:
> "The next phase will involve bringing people into the AI Revolution, but meeting them where they are right now."

Heather translates:
> "How can they integrate AI into their platform in a way that someone like me would use it?"

V adds:
- Psychological safety matters
- Empathy for user pain points
- "Care for the user"

### Close & Next Steps
- Heather shares a document (bullet points for prep)
- V to focus on "big hit" items for Calendly conversation
- Heather asks to be updated on how it goes
