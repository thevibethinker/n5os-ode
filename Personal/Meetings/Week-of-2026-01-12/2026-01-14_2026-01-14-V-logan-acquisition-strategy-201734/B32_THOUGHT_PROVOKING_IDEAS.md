# B32 THOUGHT PROVOKING IDEAS

*High-threshold filter applied: Only genuinely novel or strategically significant provocations included.*

## 1. Exit Type as Portfolio, Not Binary
**The Idea:** V and Logan aren't pursuing "an exit" — they're managing a portfolio of exit types with different risk/reward profiles. Cash acquisition, aqui-hire, and PR exit are all acceptable outcomes with different utility functions.

**Why Provocative:** Most founders treat exits as binary (good/bad) or singular (find the best one). The portfolio approach acknowledges that different outcomes serve different needs — money, reputation, team welfare — and allows simultaneous optimization.

**Potential Application:** This framework could inform how Careerspan advises clients on career decisions. Not "find the best job" but "manage a portfolio of job outcomes."

---

## 2. The "Be Loud" Strategy as Legitimate Signal
**The Idea:** Deliberate attention-seeking ("being loud") isn't just marketing — it's a credibility signal. In acquisition contexts, visibility itself communicates desirability and BATNA (best alternative to negotiated agreement).

**Why Provocative:** Professional norms often dismiss self-promotion as crass. This reframes it as strategic signaling with game-theoretic foundations. The person who's visible has options; the person seeking quietly seems desperate.

**Potential Application:** Career coaching could explicitly teach "strategic visibility" as a negotiation tactic, not just a personal branding exercise.

---

## 3. Temperature vs. Distance
**The Idea:** Relationship value has two distinct dimensions: proximity (how many degrees removed) and temperature (how recently/intensely engaged). These correlate but aren't the same — a first-degree connection can be cold, a third-degree can be hot via recent introduction.

**Why Provocative:** Most networking advice conflates these. The explicit separation enables more precise targeting and prevents the common mistake of treating all first-degree connections as warm.

**Potential Application:** CRM systems (including Careerspan's) could benefit from dual-axis relationship scoring.
