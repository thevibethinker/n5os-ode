## 1. The "Better at Job = Worse at Interviewing" Paradox

**Source Quote (V):**
> "The better you are at a job, the worse you are at interviewing because you've been fucking employed, because people have not wanted to let you go. They wanted you to be on their team."

**Provocation:**
Interview skill and job skill are inversely correlated at the high end. The candidates with the most tenure, deepest expertise, and proven track records are systematically disadvantaged in traditional hiring. We've built a system that selects for people who are good at *leaving* jobs, not *doing* them.

**Implication:** Any hiring system that relies on interview performance is inadvertently filtering *against* retention signals.

## 2. Reallocating Cognitive Assets

**Source Quote (V):**
> "You're essentially reallocating a lot of low grade assets that are filtering and vetting your candidates to maybe a smaller number of higher grade employees that are more so engaging..."

**Mental Model:**
Think of organizational attention as a portfolio of assets. Most companies allocate their lowest-value cognitive resources (junior recruiters, screening tools) to their highest-stakes decisions (who joins the team). This is the equivalent of having interns manage your investment portfolio while partners handle filing.

**Provocation:** What if hiring should be *more* expensive in human capital (senior involvement) but *less* expensive in process (fewer rounds, faster decisions)?

## 3. The "Story Premium" — Information Asymmetry Through Narrative

**Concept (Implicit in V's demo):**
Candidates who can articulate their experience as coherent stories get dramatically better scores than those with equivalent experience but no narrative skills. The Careerspan system showed a "massive jump in score" when stories were added.

**Provocation:** 
We're not actually measuring competence — we're measuring narrative fluency. The story *is* the product. A candidate with 6 rich stories beats a candidate with 20 years of undifferentiated experience.

**Strategic Weirdness:** This suggests coaching candidates on story development might have 10x the ROI of skill development.
