# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
**Date:** 2026-01-16  
**Participants:** Tope Awotona (Founder/CEO, Calendly), Vrijen Attawar (Founder, Careerspan)  
**Context:** High-level strategic conversation between two founders - exploratory discussion about potential collaboration, AI productivity trends, and Careerspan's future.

## Chronological Summary

### Opening & Context Setting
- Meeting opened with technical setup adjustments (Tope typically uses Zoom, Google Meet unfamiliar)
- V discovered Calendly's AI note-taker feature in the meeting - was previously unaware it existed
- V mentioned probing Calendly's API the night before, attempting to access note-taker functionality
- Tope confirmed note-taker is in limited rollout, not fully accessible via API yet

### AI & Productivity Discussion
- Tope expressed unusual humility about productivity - for the first time in a long time, doesn't feel like he's in the top percentile of productivity sophistication
- Acknowledged the rapidly changing AI landscape has disrupted his typical confidence in this area
- V asked about which aspects of AI-driven productivity are interesting to Tope

### Careerspan Pitch & Exit Discussion
- V disclosed Careerspan's situation: **looking to wind down the company responsibly**
- Key metrics shared:
  - Average user engagement: **45+ minutes** of self-reflection with AI
  - User base: **4,000 users** with **zero marketing spend**
  - Core insight: Built strong product but failed at distribution
- Acknowledged difficult macro conditions contributed to struggles
- Team composition: V + brilliant Head of AI + fantastic co-founder
- Exit preference: Not raising more money, want responsible end

### Acquisition Structure Discussion
- Tope asked about preferred exit structure:
  1. Acqui-hire (team joins acquiring company)
  2. Product sale + team joins
  3. IP monetization, team moves on separately
- V indicated most likely: acquirer wants one of two founders, others free to pursue next thing
- Appeared to be soft-pitching Calendly as potential acquirer

### Careerspan's Core Value Proposition
- **Central thesis:** Job market heading toward "apocalyptic" state due to AI
- Problem: Resumes can be entirely AI-fabricated, aligned to ATS systems
- Consequence: Society losing ability to evaluate talent at scale, will revert to proxies (Harvard grads, hiring people who "look like us")
- Careerspan solution: Interview-agnostic, company-agnostic platform that helps candidates authentically articulate who they are and what they do
- Unique data asset: Independently-sourced candidate data that can cross-check against interview performance

### Calendly's Strategic Concerns
- Tope shared existential concern: **Agents replacing interviews = massive meeting volume loss**
- Estimated 15-20% of Calendly meetings are interviews (employment + research)
- Calendly expanding into relationship management platform, but scheduling still drives business
- Working on agentic scheduling (like Howie) to capture non-link-based scheduling market

### Howie Product Feedback Exchange
- Tope explicitly requested V's feedback on Howie
- V offered to send a dossier with thoughts - is a power user of Howie
- Discussion of Howie's business model: $29/mo tier vs $299/mo premium with human review layer
- V's insight: Howie likely uses human-in-the-loop for preference reconciliation at premium tier

### Scheduling Agent Design Challenge
- Key UX problem surfaced: How to instruct scheduling agent privately without exposing preferences to meeting counterparty
- Tope's current solution: Separate 40 instructions to agent (acknowledged limitations, race conditions)
- V suggested this is solvable UX problem

### In-Person Meeting Scheduled
- **Action:** Tope will be in NYC on January 30th
- V confirmed availability, offered to bring laptop for "jam session"
- Both expressed enthusiasm for follow-up

## Key Themes
1. **AI disruption of hiring** - Careerspan's thesis on the collapse of resume-based evaluation
2. **Agent-based scheduling** - Calendly's strategic response to interview automation
3. **Responsible exit** - V's transparent approach to winding down Careerspan
4. **Productivity tool evolution** - Both founders grappling with rapidly changing landscape
