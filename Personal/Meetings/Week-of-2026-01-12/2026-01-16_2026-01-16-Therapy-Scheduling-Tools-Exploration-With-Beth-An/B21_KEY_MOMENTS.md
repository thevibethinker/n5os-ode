# B21: Key Moments

## High-Leverage Quotes

### KM1: The Personal Touch Premium
> "In the suburbs, people will pay more money to see me because they can call."
— Beth

**Why It Matters:** Encapsulates why therapists resist scheduling automation. The personal touch isn't just a preference—it's a differentiator they charge for.

### KM2: Assembly Line Fear
> "It doesn't feel like an assembly line. Like people just, okay, you go in. So there's that sense of extra care."
— Beth

**Why It Matters:** Key objection to scheduling software. Automation may inadvertently signal commoditization of care.

### KM3: The Dual Calendar Problem
> "Calendar, Outlook calendar doesn't sync with my Apple Calendar, so I have to manually input each client and kind of create new settings on my phone, but everything has initials, which sometimes gets a little bit confusing."
— Jen

**Why It Matters:** Concrete pain point with workflow friction. Privacy requirements create real operational burden.

### KM4: AI in Therapy Hesitation
> "It makes me a little nervous because some of the people that I work with..."
— Beth (on AI for crisis de-escalation)

**Why It Matters:** Reveals legitimate concern about AI with vulnerable populations. This isn't tech resistance—it's professional ethics.
