# B03: Stakeholder Intelligence

## Beth (Mom)
**Relationship:** Family (mother)  
**Profession:** Therapist  
**Location:** Suburbs (likely NJ based on context)

### Profile
- Self-described "dinosaur" - highly analog in workflow
- Physical calendar book for all scheduling
- No digital calendar on phone
- Runs her own practice (sounds like solo practitioner)

### Current Pain Points
- Can't access schedule when traveling
- Difficulty changing appointments on the go
- Missing the convenience of digital access

### Resistance Factors
- Habit: "If it ain't broke, don't fix it"
- Tech hesitation
- Values the personal touch that manual processes provide
- Worried automation feels like "assembly line"

### What Would Help
- Simple digital setup that preserves personal touch
- Help getting started (V offered to visit and set up)
- Gradual transition, not complete overhaul

## Market Intelligence: Therapist Persona

### Key Constraints
1. **HIPAA Compliance** - Non-negotiable for scheduling and sessions
2. **Confidentiality** - Can't have full client names visible
3. **Personal Touch** - Clients pay premium for direct access
4. **Solo Practice Reality** - Many therapists self-schedule

### Resistance to Automation
- Therapy relationship is built on human connection
- Assembly-line feeling could erode trust
- Patients value ability to call/text directly
- Automated booking may feel impersonal

### Existing Solutions Space
- Practice management platforms (notes + insurance + scheduling)
- HIPAA-compliant video platforms (Teams, Zoom for Healthcare)
- Generic scheduling tools (Calendly, Acuity) - but privacy concerns
