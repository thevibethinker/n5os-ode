**Participants:** Vrijen (V), Beth (Mom - therapist), Jen (Sister - therapist)  
**Type:** Internal/Family - Research/Discovery  
**Purpose:** Explore therapist scheduling pain points and tool preferences

## Context
V has an upcoming conversation with someone at Calendly and wanted to understand therapist scheduling needs firsthand from family members who practice therapy.

## Chronological Summary

### Opening & Context (0-2 min)
- V introduces the topic: exploring what scheduling looks like for therapists
- Mentions an upcoming conversation about Calendly, where therapists are a key audience
- Frame: genuine research + helping family members with their tech setup

### Beth's Current Situation (2-8 min)
**Profile:** "Dinosaur age" - old school approach
- Uses a physical calendar book - everything written down
- Nothing on phone
- Acknowledges this isn't ideal: can't access schedule when traveling, difficult to change appointments
- Interested in exploring digital options

### Jen's Current Situation (8-15 min)
**Profile:** Digital-first, but with friction
- Uses Outlook Calendar (connected to email, which is helpful)
- Handles all her own scheduling
- Typical flow: Client emails availability → Jen confirms → Creates calendar entry
- Uses Teams for HIPAA-encrypted video sessions
- **Key Pain Point:** Has two calendars that don't sync
  - Outlook Calendar (work - contains confidential info)
  - Apple Calendar (personal phone)
  - Must manually input appointments in both
  - Uses initials only for confidentiality (gets confusing)

### HIPAA & Privacy Constraints (throughout)
- All client information is confidential
- Sessions must be HIPAA-encrypted (Teams)
- Cannot have full client names in synced/shared calendars
- This creates friction with standard scheduling tools

### Discussion: Why Haven't They Adopted Tools? (15-20 min)
- Beth: Simply hasn't explored - "if it ain't broke" mentality combined with tech hesitation
- Jen: Has looked but nothing fits perfectly for therapy practice needs
- Both acknowledge appetite for improvement

### Personalized Touch vs. Automation Discussion
Beth raises important insight about therapy practices:
- "It doesn't feel like an assembly line"
- Patients value being able to call directly
- In suburbs, people pay more for that personal touch
- Automation might erode perceived care quality

### AI in Therapy Discussion
V asks about AI for stopgap care / crisis de-escalation:
- Beth: "Loaded question" - nervous about AI for her population (trauma survivors)
- AI doesn't give single solutions - gives summaries
- Concern about vulnerable populations interacting with AI

### Practice Management Systems Mentioned
- Jen mentions therapist-specific platforms that handle:
  - Notes organization
  - Insurance information
  - Scheduling (as part of suite)
- These exist but Beth hasn't adopted them
