# B03 – Stakeholder Intelligence

## Nafisa Poonawala
- **Role / Context**: Friend, thought partner, and early adopter of V’s Zo workflows. Currently in a liminal career/life phase, experimenting with location and work structure while navigating a new health diagnosis.
- **Health & Capacity Signals**:
  - Recent diabetic‑range labs at age 32; emotionally significant shock despite strong historical self‑care.
  - High sensitivity to stress; cortisol explicitly flagged as a driver of blood sugar spikes.
  - Travel‑heavy lifestyle and variable diet reduce her sense of control; she’s looking for sustainable routines, not aggressive hacks.
- **Psychological Profile**:
  - High self‑expectations and strong identity around “doing things right.” Diagnosis triggered identity dissonance ("if I do all this and it still happens, what’s the point?").
  - Reflective, willing to examine her own patterns, and comfortable naming emotional states directly.
  - Values autonomy with family: prefers time‑boxed proximity to avoid emotional overload, which shapes where she chooses to live.
- **Motivations & Interests**:
  - Curious about using Zo/N5OS to amplify her own work but does not want to become a full‑time system maintainer.
  - Interested in better onboarding and documentation for non‑technical users—implicitly acts as a UX proxy for the kind of customer V eventually wants to serve.
- **Leverage Points**:
  - Can provide candid, high‑bandwidth feedback on whether N5OS feels usable to a smart, non‑technical power user.
  - Serves as a real‑world pilot for onboarding flows, persona configurations, and system ergonomics.
  - Her health and capacity constraints force designs that are low‑friction and emotionally considerate, which is a useful design constraint.

## Vrijen Attawar (V)
- **Role / Context**: Founder of Careerspan and designer of N5OS on Zo. Running very lean on time, energy, and financial runway; effectively “all‑in” on current systems work.
- **Operating Mode**:
  - Extremely high build throughput (hundreds of prompts & scripts) and willingness to run late‑night debugging sessions.
  - Deeply invested in architectural principles (personas, state management, build orchestrators, safety rules) and wants to export those principles—not just point solutions.
- **Motivations**:
  - Short term: get N5OS to a sharable, semi‑productized state that he can demo at South Park Commons and to potential customers.
  - Medium term: turn Zo + N5OS into a credible path to >$2M outcome, effectively justifying years of founder sacrifice.
  - Long term: reduce cognitive load for non‑technical users who want “AI computers” but don’t want to be low‑level operators.
- **Constraints & Risks**:
  - Financial runway is tight; most savings already consumed.
  - Tends to violate his own principles under pressure (e.g., building without a complete plan, skipping some validation), which shows up as bugs in exports.
- **Leverage Points**:
  - Very high tolerance for iteration; will brute‑force through multiple export/install cycles.
  - Has strong narrative and teaching instincts; capable of turning this work into documentation, talks, and live demos that drive adoption.

## Relationship Dynamics
- **Trust Level**: High. Conversation alternates easily between vulnerable health updates, meta reflections, and deep system architecture talk.
- **Feedback Style**:
  - Nafisa offers grounded, user‑centric critique ("this will overwhelm people" / "onboarding must be conversational").
  - V takes criticism as design input, not a threat, and is comfortable exposing half‑baked systems mid‑build.
- **Working Pattern**:
  - Sessions function as both emotional processing and co‑design reviews.
  - Nafisa is not a “customer” in the strict sense but a critical early pattern‑recognizer for how non‑technical but thoughtful users will experience N5OS.

## Strategic Implications
- Nafisa is a **design proxy**: if the system works smoothly for her, it’s more likely to work for the wider audience V imagines.
- V should treat her pain points (health, energy, cognitive load) as **hard constraints** when deciding how heavy onboarding, documentation, and exports can be.
- Future follow‑ups with Nafisa should aim to:
  - Harvest specific usability pain points from her N5OS light installation.
  - Validate whether the persona system and meeting pipeline feel intuitive enough without V in the loop.
  - Check in on how health constraints are interacting with her capacity to experiment—this will mirror real users’ bandwidth.
