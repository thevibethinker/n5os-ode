### Go-to-Market Strategy: Dual-Track Approach

#### Track 1: Careerspan Platform (B2C Growth Play)
**Target Audience**: Passive job seekers, career planners, proactive professionals

**Positioning Statement** (Newly Defined):
"Careerspan is for people who care...people who give a shit about being better...the planners"

**Anti-Positioning** (What We're NOT):
- Not for desperate job seekers "slaving over LinkedIn"
- Not a job board
- Not selling to employers

**Value Props** (User-Facing):
1. **Job Delivery**: "Get 10 jobs you're actually a good fit for delivered to you" (vs. endless scrolling)
2. **Passive Pipeline**: "Hiring managers are building passive pipelines - be in them"
3. **Network Ticker**: Visual proof of activity ("X jobs submitted in last hour")
4. **Quality Over Quantity**: Selective, curated opportunities (not spray-and-pray)

**Distribution Channels**:
- **Logan's Content**: Career insights from real placements → drive organic traffic
- **Word of Mouth**: Happy users share with friends
- **Viral Loops**: Job sharing, recruiter donations (if built)
- **Community Partnerships**: Alumni groups, professional networks

**Pricing**: FREE (platform grows as acquisition asset)

**First Sales Target**: Danny & Rockwell (paid placements that double as team transition)

**Call to Action**: "Send us a voice memo describing your ideal hire. We'll send you matches weekly."

3. **Demo**: Show own Zo setup, explain replicability
4. **Case Study Offer**: "$1k for first 3 clients, full documentation, then $5k standard"
5. **Deliverable**: Custom Zo workspace + training + documentation
6. **Upsell**: "Want me to train your team?" or "Want ongoing optimization?"

#### Logan's Careerspan Select Playbook
1. **Identify**: Ilsa's candidate scores + Logan's gut check
2. **Outreach**: "Hey [Name], I'm Logan, co-founder of Careerspan. You're one of our best."
3. **Discovery**: "What's your dream role? What's blocking you from pursuing it?"
4. **Pitch**: "Let me hustle for you. I'll coach you, intro you, advocate for you."

6. **Deliver**: Weekly check-ins, direct hiring manager intros, interview prep

1. **Target**: People who've complained about recruiters in past conversations
2. **Outreach**: "Hey [Name], remember when you said [recruiter pain point]? I'm building something for that."
3. **Pitch**: "Voice memo your next hire → I send you matches weekly → anonymous until you're interested"
4. **Objection**: "It's free? What's the catch?" → "No catch. We're building a network. Your participation helps us grow."
5. **Onboarding**: "Send me a 2-minute voice memo right now describing your ideal hire"
6. **Delivery**: Weekly email with 3-5 anonymous candidate summaries
7. **Follow-up**: "See anyone interesting? Let me know and I'll make the intro."

### Key GTM Insights from Meeting

#### Insight 1: Services Bootstrap Platform Growth
- **Traditional Model**: Build platform → get users → monetize
- **Careerspan Model**: Provide services (Select + V&L) → case studies attract users → platform grows
- **Key Quote**: "Having placements drives everything else...we can flog the shit out of those case studies"

#### Insight 2: Personal Brands Feed Careerspan
- **V's Brand**: Elite clients become hiring managers on Careerspan
- **Logan's Brand**: Content educates/attracts candidates to Careerspan
- **Separation Strategy**: Own email lists separately (V&L LLC) so they survive post-exit

#### Insight 3: B2C Messaging Unlocks Growth
- **Problem**: "I could not bring myself to talk about the benefit for employers"
- **Solution**: "Website and all messaging is B2C focused...makes everything fucking simpler"
- **Result**: Logan can finally create authentic content aligned with brand

#### Insight 4: "Negative Pressure" Growth Model
- **Concept**: Give away value for free → create vacuum that sucks users in
- **Tactics**: Best career advice, case studies, placement stories - all public
- **Key Quote**: "We can do this ostensibly out of the goodness of our heart, but in a way that it's like negative pressure. It will suck people into the system."

#### Insight 5: Exit Is the Product Strategy
- **Not Building Forever**: 6-12 month timeline to sale
- **Feature Minimalism**: "Put Careerspan on ice" - grow users, not features
- **Clean Asset**: No employees, no debt, clear cap table = attractive acquisition target
