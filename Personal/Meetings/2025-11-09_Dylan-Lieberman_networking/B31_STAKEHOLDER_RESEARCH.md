# B31 - STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

**Perspective:** Speaking as early-stage founder in hiring/recruitment space + college student observing early-career hiring pain points

### Insight 1: Business Students Lack Assessment Venues for Skill Demonstration

**Title:** Business students have no hackathon/challenge equivalent for their domain (finance, consulting, marketing); this creates evaluation gap

**Evidence:** 
> "There's no financial modeling challenge open to students like every couple weeks, like there is for hackathons, as far as I know... I think that we could be an assessment for them... there's not some like, hackathon space."

**Why it matters:** The competitive advantage of challenge-based hiring is highly domain-specific; CS students have Kaggle/MLH, but business students are underserved—this is the real wedge opportunity for Amphoria, not broad competition with Kaggle

### Insight 2: Hiring Assessment Tools Have High Burden of Proof; Credibility Engineering is the Hack

**Title:** Assessment products (matching/evaluation tools) face "snake oil" skepticism because so many vendors claim great matching; credibility moat comes from academic/expert backing, not algorithm

**Evidence:**
> "The burden of proof is very high... if you were able to find some hotshot Cornell professor whose methodology underpinned your... it's like X credible expert whose methodology, pedagogy, whatever is backing up my stuff. If you don't have that, cutting through the noise is very hard."

**Why it matters:** This is a business/go-to-market insight, not a product insight. If Amphoria positions as assessment tool, they need either (a) famous methodology backing, (b) unconventional design (like Pymetrics' early gamification), or (c) famous sponsor. Without one, very hard to break through B2B sales skepticism.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ● ○ (highly specific, based on Vrijen's consulting/hiring advisory experience, somewhat abstract/generalizable but actionable)

**Category:** GTM Strategy / Market Positioning

**Domain Credibility:** ● ● ● ● ○ (Vrijen has decade of hiring expertise; this is his core domain, but he's speaking from advisory/observation perspective, not direct assessment vendor experience)

**Source Credibility:**
- Stakeholder: Vrijen Attawar → Careerspan founder (recruiting/hiring platform)
- Relevant experience: Decade as career coach; current founder in hiring tech; has advised other recruitment platforms
- Source type: SECONDARY (informed perspective from market observation, not firsthand assessment vendor experience)

### Insight 3: Distribution Beats Product in Early-Stage Marketplaces; Cluly Model (Become Marketing Agency First) Works

**Title:** In marketplace business, distribution moat matters more than product lock-in early on; examples like Cluly became known for marketing, not for product, and that visibility enabled product

**Evidence:**
> "You can kind of be a marketing agency before you're a product, and it will work well... I don't know how much help the app would be, but I know for a fact everybody knows about it [Cluly]... very widely known what they're doing and people didn't even know the product"

**Why it matters:** Signals that Amphoria should focus heavily on brand/distribution channels (micro-influencers, viral challenges, PR) before optimizing the product. Suggests that being "cool" matters more than being "good" at early stage.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (specific example, but somewhat anecdotal; Cluly may be outlier; not universally true for all marketplaces)

**Category:** GTM Strategy / Distribution

**Source Credibility:**

- Relevant experience: Founded Careerspan (marketplace dynamics); witnessed Cluly's SF rise firsthand
- Source type: SECONDARY (observed, not direct competitor experience)
- Firsthand? No - Vrijen didn't build Cluly, but was in SF market during their rise

### Insight 4: Creative/Marketing Challenges Have Viral Potential + Corporate Sponsorship Potential

**Title:** Adding ongoing value to companies (not just assessment) changes unit economics; marketing challenges can generate ongoing value for company + viral potential for platform

**Evidence:**
> "If you did hackathons, but for like marketing or for like social media plans or something where the product itself can then generate value, ongoing value for the company, that could be a really cool idea... find the next great art director or find the next great micro filmmaker... seed your competition by finding micro influencers... get huge visibility boosts"

**Why it matters:** Suggests Amphoria could reposition from "assessment tool" to "creative community + ongoing value generator." Changes from B2B assessment sale to B2B + B2C engagement play. Opens sponsorship opportunities (Coca Cola doing creative challenges).

### Insight 5: Macro Hiring Market Headwinds Create Opportunity Window for New Entrants

**Title:** Tech layoffs and reduced internship availability create urgency for new evaluation systems; students desperate to differentiate, companies cautious about hiring = opportunity for alternative credentialing

**Evidence:**
> "Every major tech company laying off like 10,000 people, it looks like it's going to be a bad summer for internships or jobs generally... as terrible as it is, that should be an opportunity for us to at least attract attention as people begin to shift and focus a lot on the job market"

**Why it matters:** Dylan sees macro headwinds as a tailwind for his business (counter-intuitive). This suggests Amphoria should position around "alternative path to hiring" during uncertain times, not wait for market to stabilize.
