# B42: Business Strategy & Market Positioning

## Acquisition Strategy (Primary Focus)

### Current State
- **Active since:** ~2.5 weeks ago (team decision to pursue acquisition)
- **Target pace:** 5-10 potential acquirers per week
- **Goal:** 15-20 conversations in pipeline
- **Current progress:** 1+ promising lead (Darwin Box)

### Process
1. Identify potential acquirers
2. Cross-reference connections to senior decision-makers
3. Initial conversations with founders/executives
4. Look for warm introductions from founder/exit networks
5. Leverage personal relationships and "dark arts"

### Strategic Framing

**Darwin Box Example:**
- Billion-dollar company, headquartered Singapore
- Primary markets: India and Philippines
- Careerspan is "ostensibly very out of their wheelhouse"
- **This is good:** Their wheelhouse is "boring and traditional and stuff they've already mastered"
- Positioning: Careerspan as a "moonshot" for them
- Giving them time/space before demo (early next week) to "not seem desperate"

### Positioning Angles Emerging

1. **Internal Mobility Tool**
   - Retention and talent development priority for enterprises
   - Manager view of employees using employer portal
   - "Diamond in the rough" identification within teams
   - Performance review + career progression guidance
   - "Could you feasibly move to this other role/sector/department?"
   - Tracking promotion readiness

2. **Acquisition Value Framing**
   - Battle-tested through multiple pivots
   - "You're paying for the mistakes we already made"
   - "Skinned our knees so you don't have to"
   - Product is hardened through real market testing

3. **Market Position**
   - "Boring is beauty" - large, stable acquirers preferred
   - Simplicity and viability over innovation
   - Scale difference creates opportunity (not deterrent)

## Negotiation Intelligence (from Ilya)

### Key Lessons Shared

**Tactic: Lowballing via "Inefficiency"**
- Potential acquirer criticizes past missteps/pivots
- Frames inefficiency as reason for lower valuation
- **Counter:** "You're paying for not making those mistakes"
- "Battle-hardened product" has premium value

**Tactic: Subtle Negotiation**
- "You never know when someone is subtly trying to work you"
- Negotiations happening before you realize you're in one
- Need defensive negotiation expertise

**Timing & Valuation**
- Business valuation has no comps (unlike houses/cars)
- "Rolling hills of high and low" - catch the spike
- Groupon example: Turned down $6B from Google, later sold for fraction
- "Laugh after money's in your bank account"
