# Decisions Made

## Strategic Decisions

### 1. Pivot Pricing Model from Subscription to Success Fee
**Decision:** Shift Magic Link pricing from $200-300/month subscription to ~$50/month base + $5,000 success fee per hire

**Rationale:** 
- Current subscription pricing ($200-300/month) undervalues the actual business impact
- Success fees are industry standard for recruiting (typically 15-25% of salary)
- At $5K per hire, only need 5 placements/month to sustain operations (~$25K/month)
- Better alignment with customer value perception - founders gladly pay for results
- Lower upfront commitment ($50 vs $200-300) reduces barrier to entry while maintaining quality signal

**Impact:**
- **Revenue model:** More scalable and aligned with actual value delivery
- **Sales messaging:** Can lead with confidence ("Pay us pennies upfront, give us our money when we close")
- **Unit economics:** Each successful hire worth 50-100x monthly subscription
- **Acquisition value:** Success rate becomes the key metric for M&A instead of subscriber count

**Next Steps:**
- Test new pricing with upcoming sales conversations (head of sales starting soon)
- Implement clawback provision (refund if hire doesn't last 3 months)
- Alex recommends keeping small base fee to filter unserious leads

### 2. Reduce Burn to Extend Runway (Post-money Acquisition Strategy)
**Decision:** Scale team down to core trio (Vrijen, Logan, part-time head of AI Ilsa) to extend runway while building acquisition traction

**Rationale:**

- Three companies already expressed acquisition interest

**Impact:**

- **Strategic optionality:** Can pursue both organic growth and M&A simultaneously

**Key Insight from Alex:** 
"If you just stopped and went M&A route right now, you'd probably not be satisfied with where it ends up. But with at least like three months of runway, whether you go M&A route or just grow and get to next round, you're going to be basically doing the same thing: needing those people in and generating revenue."

### 3. Acquisition Strategy: Tech/Data Over Community
**Decision:** Position for acquisition based on unique technology and data assets rather than brand/community at this stage

**Rationale (Alex's analysis):**
- At current stage, acquirers primarily want technology and dataset
- "Main thing I would be looking for is to like gut your technology, kill all of you and then just integrate it in my platform"
- Need statistically significant success rate to prove tech works (can't do with 10 users)
- Uniqueness of voice-based qualitative evaluation dataset is key differentiator
- Data on how people describe solving "product org problems" (human interaction) is analogous to world model concept

**Target Acquisition Profile:**
- Larger HR tech / hiring platforms (Indeed, LinkedIn, etc.)
- Companies with reach but lacking success rate optimization
- Value prop: "We have reach, they have success, we bring them together"
- Likely outcome: Aqua-hire team, put on garden leave, integrate tech into platform

**Minimum Exit Target:**
- Break-even: $1.6M (total capital in)
- Investor returns: $2M+ needed for meaningful gains
- Any company with reasonable cash reserves could afford at this level
- "Pretty much anyone could do it. If it's only 2 million... they could convince their investors to put up acquisition money as a special round"

### 4. Voice Feedback Loop as Core Product Feature
**Decision:** Implement voice-based feedback mechanism where founders record reactions to candidates for continuous optimization

**Mechanism:**
- Founders record stream-of-consciousness feedback on candidates received
- "Just leave the recorder on and just speak into it" - WhatsApp voice messages
- AI team processes overnight, refines rubric, can rerun entire evaluation "on the house"
- Builds individual "personas" of what each hiring manager values

**Strategic Value:**
- Creates proprietary dataset of hiring manager preferences and decision criteria
- Enables committee intelligence ("Alex isn't going to like this person")
- Captures qualitative signals that traditional ATS systems miss
- Differentiates from competitors who rely solely on structured filters

**Alex's insight:** "I think you could have like... as you have bigger organizations that have multiple hiring managers and HR people, you can sort of get their own little personas put together of like, oh, John likes this... Like I'm a little bit of an MBA hater... So like Alex isn't going to like this person and that's fine."

---

### 5. Focus Metric for Acquisition: Success Rate Over Volume
**Decision:** Prioritize proving superior match success rate with statistically significant cohort over maximizing user/revenue growth

**Target Metrics (Alex's guidance):**
- Get to ~100 companies/month as minimum viable cohort
- Prove hiring time is half of traditional methods OR
- Prove success rate significantly beats larger platforms OR  
- Prove happiness/satisfaction metrics are superior

**Rationale:**
"The main thing they're going to be buying at this moment is technology. So if you have... let's say you get to like a hundred companies a month and their hiring time is half of what traditional gets them or their success rate... the math they're going to do is, okay, we have reach, they have success, we're going to bring them together."

**Implication:**
- Need baseline of users to make statistics meaningful ("can't do that with 10 users")  
- But emphasis should be on tracking and proving match quality
- Success rate becomes the killer metric for valuation
- Revenue is secondary to proving the tech actually works as claimed
