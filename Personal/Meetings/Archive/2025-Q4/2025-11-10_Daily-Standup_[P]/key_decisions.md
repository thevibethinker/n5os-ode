# Key Decisions

## Project Management Approach
**Decision:** Continue with informal communication methods (Slack, Google Docs, email) for GTM launch
**Rationale:**
- Small team size (5-6 active members) supports ad-hoc coordination
- High time overlap between Ilya and Logan prevents blocking issues
- Current "warm, comforting blanket" of mixed communication working effectively
- Linear already paid for but formalization deemed premature
**Review Trigger:** Retrospective after first launch to assess if process improvements needed

## Launch Timeline
**Decision:** Target approximately 1 week out (not current week) for launch readiness
**Rationale:**
- Ads still need review and professional beautification
- Campaign placement decisions not yet finalized
- Customer journey completeness (click → confirmation) needs validation
- Mock email templates for trial inquiries still in development
**Dependencies:** Ad finalization, campaign strategy, email template approval

## Trial Framework Approach
**Decision:** Define trial offering using two-column framework (internal logistics vs. customer-facing language)
**Rationale:**
- Clarity needed on minimum viable commitments (e.g., 4-5 days from job description to invitation)
- Customer messaging must align with realistic operational capabilities
- Mock email template will serve as forcing function for clarity
**Output:** GTM1 chart review to lock in "headline numbers" for each operational dimension

## Customer Experience Philosophy
**Decision:** Prioritize perception of process completion over backend implementation
**Principle:** "If a customer clicks something, they should feel something has occurred"
**Examples:**
- Demo booking → confirmation email sent immediately
- Trial signup → trial start date confirmation sent
- Video viewing → clear next steps communicated
**Note:** Doesn't require full backend functionality on day one, but must deliver psychological closure

## Creative Asset Quality Bar
**Decision:** Move from mockups to "beautified" versions before launch
**Rationale:**
- Current mockups serve internal validation purposes
- External-facing materials need professional polish
- Emotional storytelling angle ("tell your story") requires visual sophistication
**Assets in Scope:** Video voiceovers, Instagram campaigns, LinkedIn campaigns, landing pages

## Communication Norms
**Decision:** Maintain high-frequency informal syncs rather than formalize blocking/dependencies
**Supporting Structures:**
- Daily standups continue
- GTM1 doc serves as shared reference
- Marketing channel in Slack for asset sharing
- Direct messages for urgent coordination
**Escalation Path:** Ilse available to implement Linear if coordination gaps emerge

## Scope for First Launch
**Decision:** "At least enough done" standard vs. "everything done"
**Definition:** Minimum viable launch = customer can engage, receive confirmation, feel process worked
**Trade-off:** Accept some backend gaps if customer-facing experience feels complete
**Review:** Will assess quality bar in retrospective

## Absence Protocol
**Decision:** Continue standups even when key members (e.g., V) are absent
**Rationale:** Maintains team rhythm and prevents dependency cascades
**Information Flow:** Absentees catch up via Slack/docs rather than rescheduling sync meetings
