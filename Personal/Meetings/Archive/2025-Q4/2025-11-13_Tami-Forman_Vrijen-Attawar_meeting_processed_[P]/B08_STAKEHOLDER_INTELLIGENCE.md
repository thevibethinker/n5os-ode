# Stakeholder Intelligence: Tami Forman

## Foundational Profile

**Name:** Tami Forman

**Current Role:** Founder/Leader, Women Back to Work (staffing agency model)

**Previous Role:** Founder, Path Forward (nonprofit workforce re-entry organization)

**Organization:** Women Back to Work - staffing and recruiting firm focused on placing workforce returners (primarily women re-entering after caregiving breaks) into tech roles

**What They Do:** Operate a recruiting infrastructure (100+ recruiters, majority engineers-themselves based in India) that:
1. Screens candidates who've been out of the workforce
2. Provides technical vetting (HackerRank, cogility assessments)
3. Provides AI screening and human recruiter review
4. Places candidates into roles at companies like Google, Intuit, Amazon, Adobe
5. Manages contingent workforce and consulting engagements

**Motivation:** Deep passion for creating pathways for talented people re-entering the workforce after caregiving breaks (primarily women). Tami is not motivated by maximizing placement volume but by solving a systemic problem: companies throw away qualified talent due to hiring biases and process dysfunction.

**Funding Status:** Women Back to Work is part of a larger staffing agency (CRIA), so presumably venture-backed or internally funded by the parent company. Less capital-constrained than early-stage startup.

**Key Challenge (Stated):** The hardest part is not identifying candidates who can do the job. The hardest part is getting employers and managers to take a risk on non-traditional candidates. Even with top-tier vetting, placement friction remains high because hiring managers perceive empty seats as safer than hiring someone with a resume gap.

**Standout Quote:** "The long term trend is in our favor... but the question is how you get individual managers to change their behavior."

## What Resonated

**Resonance 1: ATS Dysfunction as Root Cause**
- Tami said: "In a world of AI, it's like spy versus spy... the whole thing is going to fall apart"
- Why it resonated: This directly validates Careerspan's foundational thesis—that keyword-based ATS systems are fundamentally broken
- Signal: She's skeptical of tactical solutions and sees the systemic problem clearly; this is someone who thinks deeply about problems

**Resonance 2: Manager Psychology, Not Candidate Quality**
- Tami said: "From the manager's perspective, an empty seat is less of a liability than a seat that has someone in it who can't do the job"
- Why it resonated: This reframes the problem away from "candidate supply" toward "employer risk tolerance." Vrijen seemed to lean into this insight hard
- Signal: Tami is flagging a psychological/structural blocker that many solutions miss

**Resonance 3: Demographic Inevitability**
- Tami said: "The workforce is shrinking... we're not doing enough immigration... generations are shrinking"
- Why it resonated: This creates urgency—companies *will* have to get better at finding talent in non-traditional places
- Signal: Long-term market tailwinds, not short-term demand generation problem

**Resonance 4: Internal Mobility as Underexplored Opportunity**
- Tami identified internal mobility (helping large enterprises match current employees to new roles) as a potential use case
- Vrijen's response: Asked clarifying questions, seemed genuinely interested
- Signal: This reframing from "external hiring" to "internal efficiency" might unlock a new TAM for Careerspan

**Slight Hesitation/Reality Check:**
- Tami emphasized repeatedly that employer adoption is hard, and even with top-tier recruiting support, placement friction remains
- This wasn't negativity but professional skepticism grounded in experience
- Signal: Vrijen should not expect Careerspan to "solve" the employer problem; it enables but doesn't eliminate friction
