# Business Context

Careerspan is repositioning itself from a candidate‑paid coaching product to an employer‑paid hiring tool. The company has meaningful engagement from a few thousand job seekers, but the core business thesis is now that employers will pay for **better signal** rather than more volume. Instead of acting as an ATS or job board, Careerspan provides structured, story‑driven analysis of candidates that helps hiring managers see who could succeed and what support they would need.

This shift is unfolding in a market where ATS platforms are overloaded and underperforming. HR teams are inundated with applications and rely heavily on referrals, yet still struggle to distinguish quality; hiring managers lose more from a bad hire than they gain from a good one, so they become risk‑averse and slow. Careerspan’s three‑conversation minimum and narrative analysis aim to fix this by turning candidate stories into a rich, comparable data layer for employers.

On the demand side, Aspire and similar organizations sit between job seekers and employers, trying to deliver real placement outcomes. They need tools that both level up alumni (better narratives, clarity on gaps) and give employers confidence. A structured, six‑month program with Careerspan as the core narrative engine could become a differentiated offering for Aspire while giving Careerspan a credible employer‑facing impact story.

The constraint is economic: large‑scale usage has real token and operational costs. Any serious pilot must be designed so partners like Aspire cover those costs, and in exchange they get measurable impact, richer data on their alumni, and a stronger story to tell employers and funders.
