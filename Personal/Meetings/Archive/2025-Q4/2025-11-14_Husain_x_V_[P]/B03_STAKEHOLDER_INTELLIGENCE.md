# Stakeholder Intelligence

## 1. Husain Rasheed (Aspire / External Partner)

- **Role & Context**: Represents an alumni‑serving organization (Aspire) that previously distributed ~500 Careerspan licenses; exploring a deeper, programmatic partnership.
- **Motivations**:
  - Wants better outcomes for alumni (actual hires, stronger narratives), not just free tools.
  - Seeks partners who can provide both capability (careers product) and credible impact evidence.
  - Interested in structured programming (Discord community, challenges, AMAs) that keeps alumni engaged over months.
- **Concerns**:
  - Chicken‑and‑egg risk: employers typically demand scale/volume; how will Careerspan persuade them with a comparatively small but engaged user base?
  - Lack of employer‑side case studies showing concrete ROI (time‑to‑hire, quality, cost per hire).
  - Whether previous collaboration gave Careerspan enough value; he explicitly notes that Aspire "wasn’t able to drive" the outcomes Careerspan deserved.
- **Leverage & Influence**:
  - Can design and staff a multi‑month alumni program (community manager, programming calendar).
  - Can broker introductions to employers and other ecosystem partners if convinced by impact and economics.
  - Acts as an internal champion who understands the product and can translate it to Aspire leadership.

## 2. Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan)

- **Role & Context**: Founder responsible for product strategy, ethics, and business model decisions.
- **Motivations**:
  - Preserve alignment with job seekers while building a sustainable, bootstrapped business.
  - Prove that narrative‑driven, coaching‑based hiring can outperform volume‑driven ATS workflows.
  - Use partnerships like Aspire as both impact channels and evidence generators for employers.
- **Concerns**:
  - Token and infrastructure costs of large‑scale usage; needs partners to cover real operational expenses for big pilots.
  - Avoiding misaligned VC incentives that could push the product toward extractive behavior.
  - Ensuring any collaboration creates value for Careerspan (employer credibility, case studies), not just free usage.

## 3. Implied Stakeholders

- **Aspire Alumni**: Benefit from better narratives, clearer next steps, and employer‑relevant data, but will only stay engaged if programming is compelling and tied to real opportunities.
- **Employers / Hiring Managers**: Overloaded by broken ATS pipelines; their buy‑in depends on seeing clearer signal and reduced screening effort, not just "another platform."
- **Ripen & Similar Partners**: Potential collaborators in a multi‑party ecosystem (projects + coaching + hiring) where Careerspan provides the narrative and signal layer.
