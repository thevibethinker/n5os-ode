# Decisions

## Confirmed Decisions

1. **Business model and funding path**  
   Careerspan will continue on a **bootstrapped, B2B hiring‑tool** path rather than returning to a job‑seeker subscription model or raising VC in the near term.

2. **Employer‑centric monetization**  
   Revenue will come from employers paying per role (roughly $200–300 for a 6‑week campaign with 100 free candidates) rather than charging candidates.

3. **Signal model for candidates**  
   Careerspan will enforce a **three‑conversation minimum** for candidates to be treated as serious applicants, using those stories plus resumes for semantic assessment and employer‑facing analysis.

## Working Agreements / Directional Calls

1. **Next collaboration should be programmatic, not one‑off access**  
   Both parties agree that repeating the previous "here are licenses" model is insufficient; any new collaboration should include ongoing engagement programming, measurement, and a final impact report.

2. **Aspire‑led engagement program concept is worth exploring**  
   There is alignment in principle on Husain’s idea of a six‑month alumni engagement pilot with a dedicated community manager and Discord‑based programming; details are deferred to a follow‑up proposal.

3. **Cost‑sharing principle**  
   For large pilots (e.g., ~1,000 alumni), Aspire would need to cover OpenAI/token and operational costs (possibly with a small admin margin for Careerspan) rather than expecting unlimited free usage.

## Open Decisions (To Be Resolved Later)

- Final design of the six‑month program (cohort size, cadence, specific activities, success metrics).  
- Exact commercial terms (who pays what, revenue‑share structures, employer pricing for Aspire‑sourced roles).  
- Which employers and ecosystem partners (e.g., Ripen) to formally bring into the pilot and in what sequence.
