## Tactical Communication Frameworks

### The Three-Line Pitch Structure
For any recommender/promoter to use in any context:

**Template:**
1. "Rochel specializes in [unique capability relevant to context]"
2. "She [specific achievement that proves capability]"
3. "You should talk to her about [mutual interest/opportunity]"

**Example for Ryan Vanderbilt:**
1. "Rochel specializes in designing vulnerable interactions - making self-reflection actually appealing"
2. "She brought coherence to Career Span's platform and solved some genuinely hard HCI problems"

## Key Message Don'ts

**Avoid Generic Claims:**
- ❌ "I'm passionate about user experience"
- ✅ "I design for contexts where emotional safety determines product success"

**Avoid Credential Listing:**
- ❌ "Experienced product designer with skills in Figma, user research..."
- ✅ "I solve the problem of making vulnerable interactions feel accessible"

**Avoid Vague Strengths:**
- ❌ "Strong communicator and team player"
- ✅ "I extract principles from intuition, making my design decisions teachable"

**Avoid Underselling:**
- ❌ "I think I might be okay at simplifying complex things"
- ✅ "I make complicated experiences feel simple - it's my core superpower"

## Message Testing & Iteration

### Validation Questions
1. Is this message specific enough that someone could act on it?
2. Does this differentiate me from other strong product designers?
3. Can I back this up with concrete examples?
4. Does this feel authentic when I say it out loud?
5. Would someone remember this 3 days later?
