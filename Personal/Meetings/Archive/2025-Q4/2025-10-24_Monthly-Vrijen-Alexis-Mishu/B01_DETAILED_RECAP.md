# B01_DETAILED_RECAP

## Meeting Overview
Monthly coaching call between Vrijen Attawar (career coach) and Mishal Sayed (co-founder) discussing strategic hiring challenges and team composition for their startup. This was a reset conversation following a critical realization about inefficient development practices.

## Context & Background
The co-founders (Mishal and Alexis) recently completed a 2-month exploration phase where they attempted to build their product entirely with AI assistance. They discovered they were "coding in circles"—working in silos on interconnected components without clear technical decisions or architectural alignment. AI tools provided inconsistent answers and shifted strategies whenever challenged.

**Key realization:** Two months of development exposed serious coordination gaps but also crystallized exactly what technical expertise they needed to hire for.

## Major Topics & Flow

### 1. The AI Phase & Pivot Decision
- Initially tried AI-first development approach
- Found that AI provided different answers for similar questions and completely changed direction when disagreed with
- Vrijen validated this wasn't unique—every bootstrap founder considers AI tooling as primary development force
- Decided NOT to blame themselves; important to recognize the "coding in circles" pattern early (could have been 12 months wasted)
- First year for most founders is "always a wash" with mistakes being the norm

### 2. The Hiring Strategy & Requirements
- Realized they needed dedicated engineering talent rather than continuing to stretch their own capabilities
- Identified specific gap: need someone with deep backend/systems engineering expertise who can present options with pros/cons analysis
- Mishal's role: evaluate those options against 6-month vs. 5-year strategic needs
- Job posting approach: WealthFound (avoided Upwork after finding many unserious contractors treating it as side projects)

### 3. The Hired Candidate
- **Role:** Senior Systems Design Engineer
- **Geography:** Based in Malawi
- **Terms:** 1-year contractor, $3,000/month
- **Background:** Brilliant but non-traditional education path; recognized within Malawi for AI company work; got connected to Salesforce engineer; has worked on various US startup contracts at San Francisco-level compensation levels
- **Motivation:** Still a pay bump from current earnings; attracted to long-term vision with company equity and stability vs. limited contracts

### 4. Advisor/Mentor Acquisition
- Tapped an Uber friend (senior engineer) who worked on logistics backend infrastructure and has data engineering/forecasting expertise
- Participating on weekly basis; giving advice and technical depth reviews
- **No equity given**—operates as side project because he's comfortable in his current role but bored
- First startup exposure for this person; controlled way to explore

### 5. Vrijen's Direct Coaching on Hiring Patterns

**Equity/incentive alignment concern:** Flagged that "a hundred percent of nothing is nothing"—partial equity deals often misalign incentives. Folks who know their worth only commit what they think they're worth. Illustrated with recruiter pattern: first-time founders avoid recruiters (cost resistance), second-time founders always use them.

**Big tech founder bias:** Warned about hiring people from large tech companies without startup experience. They often bring flawed strategic priorities (focusing on customer support when early stage needs product-market fit). They're trained to "fit within the schema" rather than achieve breakthrough outcomes. Mishal countered that Uber leans startup-forward internally, so infrastructure more resilient than typical big tech.

**Candidate evaluation:** Expressed concern about the Uber friend taking advisory role without equity ("same pattern as the Canada guy")—but acknowledged Mishal's clarification that Canada candidate showed early disinterest and was never paid.

## Implicit Decisions Made
- Committed to hired contractor starting immediately
- Weekly involvement from Uber mentor will continue
- Six-month review checkpoint built into contractor agreement
- Shifted from DIY AI development to hiring professional engineering talent

## Tone & Dynamic
Vrijen was notably "more forward than normally used to being" with his critique, explicitly stating he wanted to be upfront given the context of their reset. Mishal receptive to feedback, clarifying misunderstandings. Underlying respect and trust evident despite direct coaching feedback.
