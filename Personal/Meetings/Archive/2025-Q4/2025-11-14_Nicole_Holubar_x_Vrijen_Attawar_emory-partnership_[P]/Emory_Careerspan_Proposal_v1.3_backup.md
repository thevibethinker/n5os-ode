## The Opportunity

Emory alumni deserve more than generic career resources. They deserve a tool that helps them articulate what makes them distinctive—and connects them to opportunities within their own community.

**Careerspan** is an AI-powered career coaching platform that helps professionals tell their story, tailor their materials, and find roles that fit. We're proposing a **500-alumni pilot** to launch in January 2026, giving Emory's Wise Hearts a meaningful advantage as they enter the new year's hiring season.

---

## What Careerspan Does (and Who Uses It)

Careerspan guides users through structured conversations that surface their most compelling experiences and translate them into:

- **Career stories** ready for interviews and networking
- **Tailored resume bullets** matched to specific roles
- **Fit assessments** showing how their background aligns with target positions

Crucially, Careerspan is not just for students or early-career talent. Today it is used by:

- Former chiefs of staff and senior operators at major tech and media companies
- Staff and senior engineers navigating high-stakes transitions
- Product leaders and managers at growth-stage startups
- Career changers re-framing non-linear paths into clear, compelling narratives

Across these groups, the through-line is the same: people need help turning messy, real-world experience into **concise stories and artifacts** they can actually use. Whether someone is a first-generation alum, a senior engineer, or a mid-career parent re-entering the workforce, the underlying need is universal.

The result: alumni who can confidently articulate their value—and measurable engagement the alumni office can report to university leadership.

## Proof Points: Results From Two Recent Partnerships

### Aspire Institute (Organization Alumni Population)

Careerspan partnered with Aspire Institute to serve its 627 alumni—most of whom are overseas job seekers.

With just **one live demo and self-serve access**:

| Metric | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Alumni invited | 627 |
| Fully activated users | 155 (24%) |
| Stories created | 87 |
| Resumes generated | 103 |
| Fit checks completed | 84 |
| **Coaching-equivalent value delivered** | **$12,300** (123 hours at $100/hr) |
| **AI Cost** | **&lt;$500** |
| **ROI** | **27×** |

No workshops. No ongoing coaching staff. Just structured AI conversations that produced real career artifacts.

#### What This Means in Terms of ROI

At Aspire, every **$1** spent on Careerspan content effectively produced about **$27** of coaching-equivalent value within the first 3 months:

- \~$453 in AI content cost → \~123 hours of 1:1-equivalent coaching → \~$12,300 in value.
- Roughly, **one activated alum attained the equivalent of several hours of coaching** for well under the price of a single traditional session.

### UMass Isenberg School of Management (Student Population)

In October 2025, 110 transfer students at UMass Amherst's Isenberg School completed three brief Careerspan conversations as part of ISEN 291T. The results:

| Metric | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Students who said Careerspan strengthened their narratives | **92%** |
| Students who discovered new experiences to highlight | **80%** |
| Confidence increase in interview/networking settings | **+12.3%** |
| Story completion rate (low abandonment) | **88%** |
| Post-survey completion | **68%** |

**What students said:**

> *"I have more experience than I thought I do. You don't have to have every single qualification to apply for a job."*  \
> — McKinley Chabot, Finance & Accounting

> *"Even everyday jobs can show valuable qualities like leadership, teamwork, and communication if you describe them the right way."*  \
> — Zachary Rodrigues

These results are not specific to "international" or "transfer" populations—they point to a **general pattern**: when people are guided to talk about their experiences in concrete, story-driven ways, confidence and clarity increase, regardless of starting point.

## Proposed Pilot: Emory Alumni

### Structure

| Element | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Cohort size** | 500 alumni (Wise Hearts) |
| **Launch** | January 2026 |
| **Duration** | 3 months |
| **Pricing to Emory (pilot)** | **$3 per license** (total: **$1,500** for 500 alumni) |
| **Support model** | Self-serve with light-touch onboarding (live demo + recording) |

This anchors the pilot at the heart of what we discussed on the call: **a low per-license cost**, with Emory making a modest upfront investment so Careerspan can prove out its model.

### Compensation & Longer-Term Model

On the call, we talked about a structure with **two layers of value and compensation**:

1. **Pilot phase (now):**

   - Emory pays a small, fixed per-license fee
   - Careerspan delivers the full experience, support, and reporting
   - Together we generate a concrete case study Emory can share internally and we can share (with permission) as a leading example nationally.

2. **Flywheel phase (later):**

   - As alumni profiles deepen and more Emory alumni employers post roles, Careerspan becomes a **hiring channel** for the Emory network.
   - Alumni employers who want access to this talent pool (or premium features) **pay Careerspan**, not Emory.
   - Emory continues to benefit through stronger alumni outcomes, stories, and philanthropic upside—without needing to fund everything directly.

In other words: **Emory makes a small bet now so we can co-design a model where ongoing costs are increasingly covered by the employers who benefit from hiring Wise Hearts.**
