# B25: Collaboration Opportunities & Partnership Dynamics

## Primary Partnership: Emory Alumni Association + Careerspan

### Partnership Scope: **Hiring Platform Pilot Rollout**
- **Scope**: 500 Emory alumni participating in career/hiring journey through Careerspan
- **Duration**: Pilot phase (duration not specified, but implies 6-12 months)
- **Geography**: Emory alumni base (national/international)
- **Primary Use Case**: Job discovery, application, coaching support during job search

### The Mutual Benefit Structure

#### What Careerspan Brings
1. **Platform & Product**
   - Job posting interface for alumni employers
   - Profile-matching algorithm
   - Career coaching integration
   - One-click application interface
   - Sustained engagement mechanism (not one-time transactional)

2. **Proof Points**
   - UMass Eisenberg study (92% advocacy impact, 80% new opportunity discovery)
   - Columbia SPS embedded usage data
   - Rhett's testimonial (Rice power user example)
   - 3,000+ total signups across diverse sectors

3. **Operations**
   - Vrijen + Logan commit to managing customer service for 500-alumni pilot
   - Flexible pricing structure to align incentives
   - Technical setup and integration support

#### What Emory Brings
1. **Access**
   - Direct access to 500+ qualified alumni
   - Ability to expand to full alumni base if pilot succeeds
   - Alumni network's trust and credibility

2. **Strategic Positioning**
   - Elevates hiring/job matching (area of strategic need)
   - Demonstrates value to alumni association members
   - Positions Emory as forward-thinking in career support

3. **Validation**
   - Pilot results become social proof for other universities
   - Alumni testimonials and success stories
   - Data about engagement and outcomes

### Deal Structure Options

#### Option A: Subscription Model (Preferred by Vrijen)
- **From Emory**: $3-5K/year subscription
- **Careerspan**: Provides platform access to designated alumni cohort
- **Rationale**: Creates defensible precedent for other alumni associations
- **Leverage**: Allows Vrijen to tell Columbia, Rice, other schools "Emory is paying for this"
- **Advantage**: Provides capital runway signal to investors/stakeholders

#### Option B: Material Support + Distribution
- **From Emory**: In-kind or financial support (amount TBD)
- **Careerspan**: Centralizes job distribution through platform
- **Mechanism**: Alumni employers post roles through Careerspan
- **Positioning**: "Official job board for Emory alumni hiring"
- **Advantage**: Creates viral loop (employers hire Emory → alums engage)

### Pricing Structure
- **Per-License Cost**: $2-4 per alumni (paid by Emory)
- **Rationale**: Low enough to feel symbolic and maintain incentive alignment
- **Flexibility**: Can adjust as platform grows (mentioned $50 for access to 100-person pool example)
- **Volume**: 500 alumni × $2.50 = $1,250/year (low but establishes relationship)

### Implementation Timeline
- **People Grove Migration**: Emory transitioning from Gravity → People Grove
- **Parallel Initiative**: Careerspan doesn't need to wait for full People Grove launch
- **Pilot**: Could begin immediately if leadership approves

## The "Self-Fulfilling System" Mechanics

### For Alumni Job Seekers
1. Create Careerspan profile with career story
2. See jobs posted specifically for Emory alumni
3. One-click application (automatic processing)
4. Get coaching support if needed
5. Once hired, increase profile visibility for future roles

**Incentive Loop**: "If I fill out my profile, I'll get assessed for Emory jobs immediately"

### For Alumni Employers
1. Want to hire from their alma mater
2. Post role through Careerspan
3. Get pre-screened Emory alumni matching role
4. Don't have to wait around or sift through applications
5. Future talent pool: same platform

**Incentive Loop**: "Easiest way to source qualified Emory talent is through Careerspan"

### For Alumni Association (Emory)
1. Invest in low-cost pilot ($3-5K)
2. See alumni engagement metrics rise
3. Demonstrate value to leadership
4. Attract more alumni employers
5. Create revenue stream AND engagement platform
6. Becomes competitive advantage vs. other associations

**Incentive Loop**: "Members are using this, employers are posting, roles are being filled—this is valuable"

## Success Metrics (Implied)

By end of pilot, measure:
- Alumni profile completion rate
- Alumni profile updates/engagement over time
- Job applications through platform
- Alumni employer adoption rate (roles posted)
- Placement rate (hires made)
- Alumni satisfaction/NPS
- Cost per placement for Emory

## Post-Pilot Path to Scale

If pilot succeeds:
1. **Expand to full Emory alumni base** (currently 3,000+)
2. **Replicate with other alumni associations** (Columbia, Rice, UMass, etc.)
3. **Standardized partnership model** for higher-ed customers
4. **Potential exclusivity discussions** (does Careerspan become "official Emory hiring platform"?)

## Collaboration ROI for Careerspan
- **Short-term**: Revenue ($3-5K/year), proof of concept
- **Medium-term**: Case study, social proof, template for scaling
- **Long-term**: Category leadership in higher-ed alumni hiring

## Collaboration ROI for Emory
- **Short-term**: Test hiring platform, engage alumni cohort
- **Medium-term**: Demonstrate value to leadership, attract employers
- **Long-term**: Sustained engagement platform, revenue stream, competitive advantage

## Strategic Importance
This partnership represents **Careerspan's evolution from coaching-only to full career platform**, with **institutional partnerships** as primary distribution channel. Emory is ideal proving ground: existing relationship, clear pain point, quantifiable outcomes, replicable model.
