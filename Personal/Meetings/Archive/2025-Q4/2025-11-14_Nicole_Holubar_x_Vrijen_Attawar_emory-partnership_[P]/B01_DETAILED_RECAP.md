## Strategic Shift: Careerspan's New Model
Careerspan has completely shifted to a **hiring-based monetization strategy** rather than traditional coaching subscription. Key partnership deals are locked down with groups, and the results are compelling:

### Validation Data
- **UMass Eisenberg Study**: 100 students completed a full hour of career coaching
  - 92% said Careerspan positively helped them advocate for themselves
  - 80% said it helped them find new experiences they previously wouldn't have considered
  - Demonstrates both advocacy impact AND career discovery value

### Diverse User Base Validation
Vrijen emphasized the breadth of users:
- Hugo award-winning science fiction writer
- Netflix ex-COO/former chief of staff
- HVAC technician
- Salesperson
- Public school teacher
- Over 3,000 folks signed up in total

This broad adoption validates that Careerspan is "very broadly usable" and "in really good shape right now."

## The Product: Job Hunting + Integrated Coaching
The platform remains fundamentally a job hunting/career development tool with integrated coaching:
- User experience remains unchanged for most users
- Jobs posted through Careerspan have a "Direct Apply" badge indicating the poster
- One-time account setup, then automatic processing for future job applications
- Users get tested against every job matching their profile

## Partnership Model Discussion

### Problem Nicole Identified
- High demand for coaching services (multiple requests/day)
- Need to elevate the **hiring space** specifically
- People Grove transition: moving from Gravity to People Grove platform for their higher-ed tools
- Job search/matching has not been a focus area in recent years

### The Dilemma: Cost vs. Retention Value
Vrijen articulated key insight about Careerspan vs. traditional tools:
- **Resume generation tool**: one-time use per job (then becomes "a dud")
- **Interview prep**: limited usefulness
- **Careerspan**: Every dollar invested is dollar invested in **engagement retention with Emory**
  - Students must invest upfront time/energy
  - This locks them into sharing with the Emory-associated system
  - Creates ongoing relationship, not transactional

### Unit Economics
- Direct coaching call cost: ~$1-$1.20 per 20-minute call = ~$5-6/hour just for human attention
- Effective Careerspan coaching value: ~$4/hour
- Creates compelling value proposition vs. one-off tools

## Proposed Pilot: Emory Deal Structure

### Initial Approach (Option 1): Small but Meaningful Subscription
- Lock in modest revenue to establish defensible precedent for other alumni associations
- Then leverage to Columbia SPS and UMass Eisenberg success stories
- Has strong evidence of usage and value

### Initial Approach (Option 2): Material Support + Distribution
- Emory contributes material support
- Careerspan centralizes distribution (prevents distribution chaos)
- Alumni jobs distributed through Careerspan
- Employers can "quick check" against Emory community for open roles

### Proposed Deal: 500 Alumni Pilot
- **Scale**: 500 Emory alumni (manageable for Vrijen + Logan for 1:1 customer service)
- **Pricing**: $2-4 per license from Emory's side (keeps it tiny, maintains incentive alignment)
- **Timeline**: Get it deployed to alumni
- **Incentive Structure**: Low price to Emory + encouragement for Emory alumni to post roles through platform

### Why This Works (The Self-Fulfilling System)
- Alumni invest time because they expect roles to come in from alumni network
- Employers (alumni) incentivized to post roles because they know they can "quick check" against alumni
- Creates virtuous cycle where everyone is invested
- Alumni with filled-out profiles get immediate assessment for matching roles
