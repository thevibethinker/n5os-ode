# Meeting Detailed Recap

**Meeting:** Zoe Weber (Gorgeous Ventures) - Networking & Partnership Discussion
**Date:** October 21, 2025
**Duration:** ~17 minutes
**Context:** Alumni networking call between Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan founder) and Zoe Weber (MBA student at Johnson, Gorgeous Ventures fellow)

## Key Narrative Arc

This conversation centered on building partnership potential between Careerspan and distribution channels within MBA alumni networks, specifically through Gorgeous Ventures and Cornell's career ecosystem. The discussion progressed from personal rapport-building to a strategic product demo, culminating in concrete collaboration opportunities.

## Core Discussion Points

### Part 1: Rapport & Background (00:00-02:30)
- Zoe is a second-year MBA student at Cornell Johnson, former management consulting associate at Carney (PE/M&A focus)
- Currently a Park fellow building in Elab: a sample box marketplace for period products
- Vrijen shared his international student background and entry into career coaching
- Both are MBA alumni from Johnson—immediate shared context

### Part 2: Product Introduction & Philosophy (02:30-06:43)
- **Careerspan core philosophy:** Career development requires understanding personal narrative and life story
- **Product mechanism:** Guided self-reflection through AI conversation to log experiences and extract insights
- **Key differentiation:** 10-20 minute invested conversations (not swipe-based), building narrative depth
- **User engagement metrics:**
  - 3,000 signups
  - 1,000+ hours of coaching recorded
  - ~33% of users have multiple conversations with AI
  - D30 retention: 20% (halves through month 6, stabilizes at 5-6%)
  - Competitive benchmark: retention rates best-in-class for consumer products

### Part 3: Business Model & Strategic Pivot (06:43-08:43)
- **Previous approach:** Attempted to compete in HR recruiting space
- **Current strategy:** Shift away from traditional fundraising/recruitment focus to acquisition-focused growth
- **Monetization thesis:** Keep user base growing; position as acquisition target for well-funded UK competitors (Dex, Jack & Jill—Series A from Andreessen Horowitz)
- **Competitive positioning:** Careerspan has superior technology but lacks recruiting credibility that UK founders bring
- **New hypothesis:** Drive organic user growth through community partnerships rather than traditional sales

### Part 4: Alumni Network Strategy (08:43-09:13)
- Cornell alumni association will distribute Careerspan to entire alumni base (tens of thousands)
- Emory alumni association partnership already arranged
- Strategy: Free access to highest-quality product in category to build network effects and user scale

### Part 5: CMC (Career Management Center) Integration & Product Demo (09:13-16:57)
- Zoe raised CMC opportunity: students mandated to use VMock resume tool (which Vrijen assessed as inferior)
- **Gap identified:** CMC overloaded, unable to provide deep career coaching to students
- **Proposal:** Integrate Careerspan with CMC so students can pre-record experiences; CMC coaches receive richer context before meeting
- **Product capabilities demonstrated:**
  - Extracts structured skills from unstructured conversation
  - Maps competency gaps against job requirements
  - Generates adaptive narrative positioning
  - Incrementally updates as users add more stories
  - Comparative analysis: how applicant ranks against typical candidate pool

### Part 6: Concrete Next Steps (10:55-14:30)
- **Survey distribution:** Zoe to share CMC feedback survey in first/second year class WhatsApp group
- **Vrijen's value-add:**
  - Offering to give back to Johnson community (potential speaker or mentorship)
  - Positioning Careerspan as resource for international students facing H1B/visa anxiety
  - Offering recruiting partnership: has hired international Johnson students in the past
  - Sharing consulting resource document previously created for Johnson community
- **Target audience:** International MBA students specifically (less stable recruiting environment, visa concerns)

## Key Themes

1. **Network effect strategy:** Growth through distribution channels (alumni associations, business schools) rather than enterprise sales
2. **Narrative-driven product:** Core value is turning lived experience into marketable story
3. **International student segment:** Explicit focus on MBA students managing visa/recruiting uncertainty
4. **Community reinvestment:** Vrijen positioning himself as returning value to Johnson ecosystem
5. **Strategic partnerships over direct sales:** CMC integration as model for B2B2C distribution
