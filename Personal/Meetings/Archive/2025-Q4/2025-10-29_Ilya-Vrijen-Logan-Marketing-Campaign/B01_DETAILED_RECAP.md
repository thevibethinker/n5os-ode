# B01_DETAILED_RECAP

## Key Decisions and Agreements

- **Marketing campaign strategy**: Moving forward with digital ads (LinkedIn, Instagram) as the primary channel for reaching hiring managers. Cost-efficient, repeatable, and can operate on "low boil" for brand awareness at minimal cost.
- **Trial format**: Employer trial experience is undefined ("blank canvas"). Team will decide whether trials are time-based, candidate-count-based, or another metric. Ilya advocates for multiple trial paths (basic, medium, high) with gamification to upsell users from basic to more robust experience.
- **Value proposition for employer trial**: Minimum viable value: better filtering than existing ATS/internal systems. Bonus/differentiation: access to Careerspan talent pool, speed, trust (no "fakery"), and data quality. Psychological motivators: FOMO (access to hidden candidates), speed, and trust.
- **Job description to first value demonstration**: Timeline target is 24-48 hours, potentially 3 days, from job description submission to showing measurable value across filtering, speed, trust, and candidate data quality.
- **McKinsey portfolio opportunity**: Vrijen secured unofficial sanction (pending formal signature) from McKinsey partner to post ~400 jobs across ~250 portfolio companies. Provides ethical pathway to run aggressive "gangster marketing" pilot without permission issues—company identifies 10 additional portfolio-like datasets for consent-based campaigns.
- **Message discipline**: Ilya hasn't reviewed all documentation yet but will consolidate messaging and positioning. Team agrees the marketing narrative must convert: story that must move employer toward trial and eventual customer status.

## Strategic Context

This meeting represents a pivotal GTM moment. The team is transitioning from product-readiness conversations to aggressive customer acquisition via digital marketing. Ilya brings 21 years of commercial revenue experience and deep AI consulting background, creating a skilled marketer/strategist role. The conversation balances aggressive sales tactics (pre-filling job descriptions, proactive candidate seeding) with ethical constraints, identifying McKinsey as the de-risking mechanism.

Key tensions surfaced:
- **Time vs. Effort optimization**: Vrijen distinguishes between founders who optimize for time-spent-hiring vs. effort-expended, suggesting messaging should target the right persona (effort-optimizers are more willing to pay).
- **"Better" as insufficient**: Logan captured hard-won insight that "better filtering" alone doesn't motivate adoption. Psychological triggers (FOMO, speed, trust) and fear of competitor adoption are stronger drivers.
- **Automation vs. high-touch**: Team is exploring low-friction (voice message drops, automated email intake) vs. high-touch (personalized outreach) trial activation. The tension is sustainability—can't scale "high RPM, in-your-face" activation to 100 customers today.

Underlying belief: Careerspan has solved the core product problem (hiring manager discovery, candidate matching, job board aggregation). The bottleneck is now customer acquisition and trial-to-payment conversion.
