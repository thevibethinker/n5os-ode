# B27_KEY_MESSAGING

## Executive Summary (30 Seconds)

Careerspan co-founders held strategic session on November 3, 2025, acknowledging founder burnout and mission misalignment. After honest assessment, they committed to pursuing $2-3M acquisition within 6 months rather than continued growth grind. Logan leading target evaluation using three-dimension framework (deep pockets + urgency, relationship advantages, strategic fit). Vrijen sacrificing personal retirement funds to maintain runway through Q1. Team focus shifting to building self-service buyer loops for 5-10 priority ICPs to maximize acquisition attractiveness.

**Key Takeaway**: Strategic pivot from growth to acquisition, driven by founder wellbeing and investor pressure, with clear target and timeline.

## Message Variants by Audience

### For Team (Ilya, Ilsa)

**Tone**: Honest, reassuring, inclusive

**Key Points**:
1. **We're pursuing acquisition**: After reflection, we've decided acquisition is the right path for the company and for us as founders. This isn't failure—it's strategic.

2. **Your roles are critical**: Ilya, your work on self-service buyer loops is now core to acquisition strategy. Ilsa, we're protecting your position even as we tighten belts personally.

3. **6-month timeline**: We're targeting $2-3M acquisition by spring 2026. This gives us clear focus and urgency.

4. **What changes**: We're focusing on building systems that demonstrate acquisition value (user growth, self-service sales capability) rather than pursuing every growth opportunity.

5. **What doesn't change**: Quality of our work, commitment to each other, belief that the product has real value.

**Anticipated Questions**:
- **Q**: Does this mean layoffs?  
  **A**: We're protecting team positions. Vrijen is going salary-less; we're not cutting team.

- **Q**: What happens to our equity?  
  **A**: Acquisition would provide liquidity event, though terms depend on buyer. We'll keep you informed as we progress.

- **Q**: Should we be looking for other jobs?  
  **A**: We want you here through this. If 6-month timeline doesn't materialize, we'll give you advance notice.

## Objection Handling

### "Your engagement numbers don't justify $2-3M"
**Response**: Our value isn't just current users—it's proven engagement model, technical infrastructure, career coaching methodology, and experienced team. You're acquiring the formula for career engagement, not just current metrics.

### "Why should we buy vs. build?"
**Response**: We've spent years perfecting conversation-based career coaching. We understand the nuances, have working infrastructure, and proven user love. Building would take you 2+ years and cost more than acquisition. Plus, you'd be guessing at what works—we know.

### "You're too early / too small"
**Response**: Exactly—we're acquisition-priced, not IPO-priced. You get proven technology, engaged users, strong team, at a fraction of what it would cost to build or what we'd cost in 2 years. This is strategic timing.

### "How do we know founders will stay post-acquisition?"
**Honest Response for Acquirers Asking Directly**: We're open about founder involvement post-acquisition being negotiable. What we're selling is the product, user base, methodology, and team (Ilya's technical strength). If you need founder involvement for transition, we can structure that. If you prefer acquiring the assets and integrating your way, we're open to that too.

**Diplomatic Response If Not Asked Directly**: We're focused on finding the right strategic home for Careerspan where it can reach its full potential. Transition terms are part of deal structuring we're happy to discuss with serious buyers.
