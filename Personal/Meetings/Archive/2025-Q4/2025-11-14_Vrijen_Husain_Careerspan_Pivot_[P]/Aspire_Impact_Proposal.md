# Aspire x Careerspan

## Alumni Impact Snapshot & 1,000-Learner Proposal

### 1. Executive Summary

- Aspire partnered with Careerspan to give alumni an AI-powered career coach focused on **self-advocacy**, not just document generation.
- With one live demo, a recording, and fully self-serve usage, Aspire alumni still generated meaningful engagement: dozens of stories, resumes, and "vibe checks" (role-fit checks), with minimal staff time.
- Based on historical usage and locked-in unit costs, Aspire has already realized **five-figure coaching-equivalent value** for **hundreds of dollars** in AI cost.
- We propose a **1,000-learner, 6-month program** with two pricing structures:
  - A **usage-linked price** based on observed activity with a modest margin to keep the program sustainable, and
  - An optional **fixed price** that caps Aspire's downside risk.

---

### 2. What We Did With Aspire Previously

**Rollout model**

- One **live demo session** to a large group of alumni.
- Recording re-shared; alumni used the platform **self-serve**.
- Occasional light troubleshooting; no ongoing workshop or coaching series.

**Audience characteristics**

- Primarily **international** alumni and others **less familiar with U.S. job-search conventions**.
- Many face cultural and structural barriers in telling their story and navigating U.S. hiring norms.

Despite this low-touch model and this audience profile, Aspire alumni engaged meaningfully with Careerspan.

```text
Cost_hist =
```

That implies:

- Total minutes ≈ **7,380**

- At $100/hour (a conservative rate for 1:1 career coaching), that's ≈ **$12,300** of coaching-equivalent value.

> **In other words:** Aspire effectively delivered **five-figure coaching value** to alumni for an AI content cost of **\~$453**, using a low-touch, self-serve model—that's a 27x ROI

### 4. Proposed 1,000-Learner, 6-Month Program

We propose scaling to **1,000 alumni invited** over a 6-month period, with two conservative assumptions:

1. **Higher uptake:** improve from \~10% to **\~16%** active usage (≈ **160 fully activated users**).
2. **More depth per user:** assume **2× the total usage** relative to the prior cohort.

These assumptions are grounded in results from the **UMass Isenberg x Careerspan pilot**, where the updated Careerspan experience produced roughly **2× the story and resume output per engaged learner** compared with the earlier Aspire run. In that Fall 2025 pilot with 110 transfer students in ISEN 291T, students completed three brief, structured AI-guided conversations and reported:

- **92%** said Careerspan helped **strengthen their experience narratives**.
- **80%** discovered **additional experiences or angles** they hadn't emphasized before.
- A **12.3% increase** in confidence **discussing experiences in interview-style settings**.
- **Low conversation abandonment** – stories that were started tended to be finished.
- A **68% post-survey completion rate**, indicating strong follow-through after the required assignment.[^1]

Together, these results provide concrete evidence that brief, structured use of Careerspan meaningfully strengthens students' ability to talk about their experiences – exactly the outcome Aspire is trying to drive for its alumni.

Projected content usage for Aspire under these assumptions:

- Stories: **174**
- Resumes: **206**
- Vibe checks: **168**

#### 4.1. Projected AI content cost (usage-linked option)

```text
Raw content cost =
  1.50 * 174 stories
+ 1.50 * 206 resumes
+ 2.00 * 168 vibe checks

= 1.50 * 380 + 2 * 168
= 570 + 336
= $906 (approx.)
```

To keep the program sustainable, we apply a **5% service margin**:

```text
Billed program cost (usage-linked) =
  906 * 1.05
≈ $951  → round to **$950**
```

So under the usage-linked structure, the 6-month, 1,000-learner program is **\~$950 all-in**, based on the conservative usage assumptions above.

#### 4.2. Coaching-equivalent value at scale

Doubling the usage roughly doubles the coaching-equivalent time:

- Coaching hours ≈ **246 hours**
- Value at $100/hour ≈ **$24,600**.

So even under the usage-linked structure at **\~$950**, Aspire would be delivering learning and support comparable to **hundreds of hours of 1:1 coaching**, concentrated into a single 6-month window with measurable artifacts (stories, resumes, interview answers).

#### 4.3. Fixed-price option

As an alternative to the usage-linked model, Aspire can elect a **fixed-price structure**:

- **Fixed program price:** **$1,250** for the same 1,000-learner, 6-month scope.
- This option effectively **de-risks cost-related downside**  for Aspire: if engagement and impact exceed the conservative assumptions above, Aspire's spend remains capped at $1,250.
- For Careerspan, this provides the headroom needed to support and learn from higher-than-expected engagement without revisiting commercial terms mid-pilot.

Both structures are grounded in the same underlying unit economics; the fixed-price option simply trades a slightly higher ceiling for **predictability and upside protection** on Aspire's side.

### 5. Why Careerspan Instead of Generic AI Tools

**What generic tools do**

- Generate a **single resume or cover letter** tuned to a specific posting.
- Optimize for **keywords and formatting**, not for the learner's own understanding.
- Produce outputs that **expire with the job application**.

From an ROI perspective, this is **transactional spend**: each dollar works once, for one application.

**What Careerspan does differently**

Careerspan is designed as **an underlying coach**, not a template generator:

- Guides alumni to **surface real experiences and achievements**, then turns those into:
  - Reusable career stories,
  - Tailored resumes,
  - Concrete interview answers and examples.
- Each "story", resume, and vibe check is both:
  - A near-term asset (for the next application or interview), and
  - A **building block of a durable narrative** they can re-use across roles, industries, and time.

For Aspire's alumni—often international and less familiar with U.S. hiring norms—the binding constraint is **self-advocacy**, not access to yet another document generator. Careerspan helps them **practice that skill repeatedly**, generating artifacts they carry forward:

- Stories that appear in LinkedIn profiles, networking conversations, and future interviews.
- Resumes they can adapt quickly to new roles.
- Clearer language for explaining their value to employers.

From a leadership perspective, this makes Careerspan **compounding spend** rather than transactional spend. Every **$5–6 per fully activated learner** buys:

- A set of stories,
- Better resumes,
- Clearer interview answers,
- And a **repeatable mental model for advocating for themselves** in the U.S. market.

---

### 6. Why This Price Point Needs to Hold

The **usage-linked price (\~$950)** and **fixed-price option ($1,250)** are grounded in:

- **Actual historical Aspire usage**, scaled conservatively and informed by outcomes from the UMass Isenberg pilot.[^1]
- Transparent per-action pricing (stories, resumes, vibe checks).
- A modest **5% margin** on the usage-linked model to ensure we can support Aspire responsibly over the full 6 months, and a fixed-price alternative that leaves room for higher engagement.

Dropping materially below these levels would force trade-offs in:

- Reliability of service,
- Ability to support troubleshooting and improvements during the pilot,
- Capacity to incorporate Aspire-specific and Isenberg-style insights into future iterations.

At the proposed levels, the program remains:

- **Sustainable for Careerspan**, and
- **Highly leveraged for Aspire**, with coaching-equivalent value that far exceeds the spend under either pricing structure.

### Conclusion

For under **$1 per invited alumnus**, Careerspan users get:

- Persistent stories,
- Better role selection,
- Reusable interview answers,
- Greater confidence in the hiring process.

Given Aspire's mission and alumni profile, the Careerspan program offers a way to deliver **structured, repeatable, skill-building support at scale**, at a price point that remains small in absolute terms while generating outsized, compounding value for alumni.
