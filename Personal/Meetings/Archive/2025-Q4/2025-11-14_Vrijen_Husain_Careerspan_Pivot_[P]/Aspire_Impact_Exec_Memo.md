# Aspire x Careerspan – Executive Memo

**Subject:** Proposal for 1,000-Learner Careerspan Program (6 Months)

### 1. In One Page

- Aspire piloted Careerspan as an AI-powered career coach for alumni.
- With a single demo + recording and a self-serve rollout, alumni still produced:
  - 87 career stories
  - 103 resumes
  - 84 "vibe checks" (role-fit checks)
- Internal AI content cost for that usage was ≈ **$453**.
- The equivalent work via 1:1 coaching is ≈ **123 hours**, or **$12,300** at $100/hour—a **~27x ROI** on the AI spend.
- We propose a 6-month program for **1,000 alumni** with **two pricing options**:
  - A **usage-linked price** of roughly **$950** (based on conservative projections with a 5% margin), or
  - A **fixed program price of $1,250** that caps Aspire’s spend even if engagement significantly exceeds projections.

### 2. What We Are Proposing

- **Scope:** 1,000 alumni invited over 6 months.
- **Engagement assumptions (conservative):**
  - ~16% fully activated users (~160 alumni with meaningful usage).
  - ~2× the total content usage vs. the prior Aspire cohort.
  - This 2× expectation is informed by the **UMass Isenberg x Careerspan pilot**, where the updated product and tighter instructional framing led to substantially higher story and resume output per engaged learner than in the original Aspire run.
- In the Fall 2025 Isenberg pilot (110 transfer students in ISEN 291T), after completing three brief, structured AI-guided conversations, students reported:[^1]
  - **92%** said Careerspan helped **strengthen their experience narratives**.
  - **80%** discovered **additional experiences or angles** they had not emphasized before.
  - A **12.3% increase** in confidence **discussing experiences in interview-style settings**.
  - **Low conversation abandonment** (started stories were typically finished).
  - A **68% post-survey completion rate**, indicating strong follow-through on the required assignment.

**Projected activity for Aspire (for pricing):**

- 174 stories
- 206 resumes
- 168 vibe checks

**Pricing mechanics (usage-linked option):**

- Internal prices: $1.50/story, $1.50/resume, $2.00/vibe check.
- Raw content cost ≈ **$906**.
- 5% margin for sustainable service ≈ **$951**, rounded to **$950** billed.

**Program economics under usage-linked pricing:**

- Cost per invited alumnus ≈ **$0.95**.
- Cost per fully activated alumnus ≈ **$5.95**.
- Coaching-equivalent value at this scale ≈ **246 hours**, or **$24,600** at $100/hour.

**Fixed-price alternative:**

- Flat **$1,250** for the same 1,000-learner, 6-month scope.
- This de-risks cost-related downside for Aspire: if engagement and impact exceed conservative projections, Aspire’s spend is capped while Careerspan retains the headroom to support higher usage.

### 3. Why Careerspan vs. “Just Another AI Tool”

Most AI tools:

- Generate one-off resumes or cover letters optimized for keywords.
- Provide value that effectively ends when a specific job application is submitted.

Careerspan instead functions as an **underlying coach**:

- Helps alumni surface real experiences and achievements.
- Turns those into reusable **stories, resumes, and interview answers**.
- Builds **self-advocacy skills** that persist across roles, industries, and time.

For Aspire’s largely international alumni, who often face cultural and structural barriers in U.S. hiring, the bottleneck is not access to another template generator—it is the ability to **tell their story clearly and confidently**. Careerspan is built specifically to close that gap.

### 4. Why This Price Structure Needs to Hold

- The **usage-linked price (~$950)** is grounded in Aspire’s own historical usage, scaled conservatively and cross-checked against results from the UMass Isenberg pilot, with a small **5% margin** for sustainable service.[^1]
- The **fixed-price option ($1,250)** offers Aspire predictability and upside protection if engagement materially exceeds expectations.
- Pushing either option significantly lower would:
  - Undercut service reliability,
  - Limit support during the pilot,
  - Reduce our capacity to incorporate Aspire-specific and Isenberg-style learnings.

At these levels, Aspire buys **hundreds of hours of coaching-equivalent value** for comfortably under two thousand dollars, in a way that:

- Scales to 1,000 alumni,
- Requires minimal staff involvement,
- And builds durable skills rather than one-off documents.

**Recommendation:** Approve the 6-month, 1,000-learner Careerspan program under either the **usage-linked (~$950)** or **fixed ($1,250)** structure, with the above assumptions and metrics as the basis for evaluating impact.

[^1]: UMass Isenberg x Careerspan Pilot Report, ISEN 291T (Transfer Student Career Preparation), October 2025, internal report shared via Google Docs.
