# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
**Participants:** Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan), Logan Currie (Careerspan), Alex Garnepudi (Ola Registry)
**Duration:** ~17 minutes
**Date:** November 13, 2025
**Type:** External strategic partnership discussion

## Key Discussion Points

### Ola Registry/Hello Platform Status
Alex presented the business model and market position:
- Ola Registry: Domain registry for .CV (career/CV) domains
- Hello: Registrar and profile builder interface on top of Ola
- Took over registry operations from Cape Verde in June 2024
- Officially launched Hello and Ola consumer sites in October/November 2024
- Currently entering first renewal cycle phase

### Premium Domain Strategy
- Originally restricted premium domain list to top 20,000 globally (down from traditional 100,000-150,000)
- Rationale: Latency concerns when querying registrars with large premium lists (>100,000 domains causes bandwidth/speed issues)
- Early pricing: Premium domains like "Logan" were priced at $5,000
- Current approach: Selective premium positioning while maintaining fast API response times
- Birthday gift: Alex offered Vrijen a free year of "Vrijen.CA" domain (six-letter name, premium tier)

### .CV Platform Metrics
- 25,000 profiles built to date
- 700,000 views on .CV profiles in past 30 days
- Product Hunt ranking: #1 product of the day on Hello
- Early adopter base without clear segmentation (portfolio careers vs. corporate 9-5)
- Normal distribution of profile usage (no skewed tails)

### Platform Architecture & Go-to-Market
- Ola serves 99.99% of people who don't know these platforms exist
- Hello targets profile builders seeking their name.CV domain
- Hello is technically a registrar on the Ola registry (business model within business model)
- Infrastructure investment focus: latency optimization, billing alignment, integration support
- Renewal infrastructure must be reliable for registrar partnerships

### Careerspan Status & Evolution
Logan reported significant pivot and progress:
- **Nearly 3-year milestone:** Company founded approximately November 28, 2022
- **Employer portal launch:** Built B2B portal allowing companies to:
  - Post job descriptions directly
  - Track candidates through hiring workflow
  - Use "deeper analysis" for candidate evaluation
- **Market shift:** Observed growing frustration with traditional hiring ecosystem; companies now willing to try alternatives
- **UMass Amherst pilot:** Recently completed collaboration with Eisenberg (higher ed partner)
- **Go-to-market:** Beginning paid marketing campaigns (Logan's background in marketing)
- **Investor conversations:** Marvin Ventures involved; Adam introducing potential Flybridge partnership

### Business Model Discussion
Central theme: Monetization strategy divergence

**Challenge identified:**
- Job seekers are price-sensitive ("people are broke")
- Charging job seekers directly creates friction
- Need to subsidize job seeker experience

**Alex's strategic insights:**
- Monetizing job seekers is "very difficult" regardless of value chain position
- Successful model: Charge hiring firms (recruiters, companies)
- Existing precedent: 10-20% headhunting fees
- Case study: Solo Latin American engineering talent recruiter makes $500K/year matching 20 people to Series A/B companies
- Market opportunity is concentrated in firms willing to pay for efficient hiring

### Strategic Context & Ambition
Alex presented framework for startup scaling:
- **Validated market idea:** Necessary to reach ~100-150K profiles (beyond early adopter phase into early mainstream)
- **Execution to $100M ARR:** Requires "mad people" talent and meticulous execution
- **Beyond $100M ARR:** Mostly luck/market timing ("swan events")
- Examples: Solo founder in Mexico running niche job board, thriving business model

### Relationship & Shared Context
- Alex and Opa have known each other through school, built products together
- Vrijen and Logan built together before, have "shared masochism wavelength" and trauma-bonded teams
- Survivorship: Most companies fail by 18-month mark; most co-founder pairs conflict by that point

## Emergent Insights

### Structural Opportunity
Careerspan's positioning in hiring ecosystem monetization mirrors validated patterns: charge firms, not job seekers. Alex validated this as the primary lever.

### Platform Maturity Signals
- Ola's technical infrastructure challenges (latency, billing alignment) are normal for platform at scale-up phase
- Hello's consumer traction (700K views, 25K profiles, #1 ProductHunt) suggests product-market validation in early adopter segment

### Market Readiness
Both Ola (in CV/career domain space) and Careerspan (in hiring) are positioned in spaces where frustration with status quo is driving adoption. Market appears more receptive than 2022-2023 baseline.
