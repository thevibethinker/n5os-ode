# B28: Strategic Intelligence

## Business Context

### Careerspan Strategic Moment

Vrijen is in critical business development phase:
- Successfully proved hiring-based monetization works (partnerships already signed)
- Has validation data (UMass study, diverse user base, power users)
- Needs institutional customers to prove repeatability of model
- $3-5K deals would be early proof points for larger institutional sales (Rice, others)

**Why Emory matters for Careerspan:**
- Second-tier university alumni network (well-connected, professionally successful)
- Lack of job placement focus creates clear need state
- Nicole appears to have real authority to move; not just exploratory
- Could become reference customer + case study for other schools
- Model tested at smaller schools (Eisenberg at UMass) but not major research university yet

**Careerspan's leverage:**
- They are not desperate (have pilots elsewhere); this is "let's add this to growth pipeline"
- Pricing is flexible (can subsidize early partners for strategic value)
- Product is real (not vaporware); has working customers

### Emory's Strategic Position

Emory is in post-PMF consolidation:
- Recognizes hiring/jobs gap in current offerings
- Platform transition underway (Gravity → People Grove)
- Alumni engagement appetite exists (multiple daily coaching requests)
- Budget constrained but not zero

**Why Careerspan at Emory now:**
- Timing coincides with new platform launch (People Grove)
- Could be packaged as "new alumni benefit" at transition
- Relatively low cost ($2-4K) makes it low-risk pilot
- Hiring focus aligns with alumni life stage (career establishment)

**Emory's risk:**
- Another tool/platform in ecosystem
- Potential integration complexity with People Grove
- Depends on marketing/awareness to get alumni adoption
- Depends on employer interest (Careerspan can't force employers to use it)

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct Competitors for Alumni Job Placement:**
- LinkedIn (dominant but generic; no alma mater advantage)
- Campus-specific boards (often poorly maintained, low volume)
- Resume generators/interview prep (transactional, not lasting)
- People Grove (their upcoming platform - could compete or integrate)

**Careerspan's Differentiation:**
- Includes coaching (not just job board)
- Employer can screen against verified alumni
- Lasting engagement model (alumni stay engaged for jobs over time)
- Better ROI story (ongoing relationship vs. one-off transaction)

**Why Emory would choose Careerspan over People Grove's native job tools:**
- Careerspan brings recruiting demand (employers as customers)
- People Grove will have generic job tools; Careerspan brings specialization
- Different business models: People Grove transaction-heavy, Careerspan engagement-heavy

## Revenue Model Implications

### For Careerspan

**Direct Revenue from Emory pilot:**
- $2K-4K annually for 500 alumni licenses
- Not significant in absolute terms but proves "institutions will pay" model

**Indirect Revenue from Emory pilot:**
- Employer recruiting (Careerspan charges employers for access to vetted Emory alumni)
- Depends on: (1) alumni adoption, (2) employer awareness, (3) job volume
- Could be $5-15K+ annually if employers actively recruit

**Strategic Value:**
- Reference for Rice, other universities
- Data to show institutional customers VCs/investors
- Proof of "multi-sided platform" (alumni + employers) working

### For Emory

**Direct Spending:**
- $2-4K for 500 alumni licenses annually

**Potential ROI:**
- Reduced burden on coaching program (Job matching is self-service)
- Enhanced alumni satisfaction (more options, more engagement)
- Potential recruiting relationship revenue (if employers want to pay for access)
- Brand enhancement (alumni see Emory investing in their career success)

**Hidden Costs:**
- Marketing/awareness effort to drive adoption
- Customer service for alumni issues
- Potential technical integration with People Grove

## Scaling Scenarios

### Conservative: Pilot Fails (30% probability)

**Triggers:**
- Alumni adoption < 5% of 500 pilots
- No employer interest in recruiting
- Emory leadership doesn't see ROI

**Outcome:**
- Careerspan: Writes off Emory; moves on to Rice/other prospects
- Emory: Tries different approach; potential distrust of Careerspan

### Base Case: Pilot Succeeds Modestly (50% probability)

**Triggers:**
- Alumni adoption 20-30%
- 5-10 employer job postings
- Positive leadership feedback on "engagement metric"

**Outcome:**
- Careerspan: Scales to full Emory alumni (10K+) at higher price ($5-8/seat)
- Emory: Becomes reference customer; adds to alumni benefits suite
- Employer recruiting becomes revenue stream ($1-2 per recruiter position)

### Optimistic: Pilot Becomes Flagship (20% probability)

**Triggers:**
- Alumni adoption > 50%
- 20+ employer postings monthly
- Leadership wants to promote it widely (PR, fundraising story)

**Outcome:**
- Careerspan: Full alumni rollout + employer recruiting becomes significant revenue
- Emory: Becomes case study; Vrijen presents at alumni conference
- Rice/others fast-follow; Careerspan establishes institutional sales playbook

## Critical Success Factors

### For Careerspan

1. **Customer Service Delivery**: Must deliver 1:1 support for 500 alumni during pilot (reputation maker/breaker)
2. **Employer Recruiting Activation**: Cannot rely solely on organic employer interest; must actively recruit employers
3. **Coordination with Logan**: Partnership operations must execute flawlessly
4. **Data Collection**: Must gather metrics Emory leadership needs (adoption, engagement, outcomes)

### For Emory

1. **Leadership Communication**: Nicole must frame as "alumni retention infrastructure" not "tool purchase"
2. **Alumni Messaging**: Must explain value proposition (coaching + job board, not just board)
3. **Employer Outreach**: Needs Careerspan help recruiting Emory alumni employers (not passive)
4. **Timeline Coordination**: Pilot must fit within People Grove transition without being shadowed

## Open Strategic Questions

1. **Product Roadmap**: Is Careerspan building employer recruiting products (like resume screening)? Or just a job board?
2. **Institutional Pricing**: Is $3-5K the template for all universities, or does it scale (% of alumni base)?
3. **Employer Economics**: How much can Careerspan charge employers? Competitive with LinkedIn/ZipRecruiter?
4. **Lifetime Value**: What's the expected alumni lifetime value if they stick with Careerspan for 5+ years?
5. **People Grove Integration**: Could Careerspan integrate into People Grove natively? Or stay separate platform?

## Recommendations for Vrijen

1. **Over-deliver on customer service**: Make 500-alumni pilot feel white-glove; builds expansion case
2. **Activate employers proactively**: Don't wait for organic recruiter demand; reach out to known Emory alumni employers
3. **Measure everything**: Track adoption, engagement, employer job postings, outcomes; give Nicole weekly dashboard
4. **Build case study in parallel**: Start drafting Emory case study now (metrics, quotes, process) for Rice pitch
5. **Maintain relationship diversity**: Keep building other university pilots; don't make Emory bet the company

## Recommendations for Nicole

1. **Lead with ROI narrative**: Frame to leadership as "alumni engagement infrastructure" not "job board tool"
2. **Get success metrics agreed early**: Define KPIs with leadership before launch (adoption rate, engagement, costs per hire)
3. **Pilot with engaged cohort first**: Select 500 alumni who are most likely to use (recent grads, career-changers, active in alumni events)
4. **Require Careerspan employer outreach**: Don't accept vague "employers will come"; require them to actively recruit Emory employers
5. **Plan for integration**: Start conversations with People Grove team about potential integration points (data, identity, UX)

## Longer-term Strategic Implications

If this partnership succeeds, it could signal:

- **For higher education**: Alumni engagement models shifting from transactional (events, magazines) to ongoing economic value (job matching, career support)
- **For Careerspan**: Institutional revenue stream becoming viable (not just B2C or employer recruiting)
- **For the market**: Opportunity for specialized alumni career platforms (vs. generic LinkedIn)
