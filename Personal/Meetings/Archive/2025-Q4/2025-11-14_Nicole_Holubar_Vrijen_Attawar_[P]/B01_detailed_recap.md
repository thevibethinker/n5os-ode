# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Context

Nicole Holubar (Alumni engagement/partnerships role at Emory University) and Vrijen Attawar (Founder, Careerspan) met to discuss potential partnership bringing Careerspan hiring-based monetization model to Emory alumni community.

Nicole recovering from illness but prioritized reconnecting. Vrijen has successfully transitioned Careerspan from coaching-only to hiring-based revenue model and is actively seeking institutional partnerships.

## Key Discussion Areas

### Careerspan Business Update

**Product Metrics & Validation:**
- Study with 100 UMass Eisenberg students: 92% reported Careerspan positively helped them advocate for themselves, 80% found new career experiences they wouldn't have considered
- 3,000+ users signed up across diverse demographics (Hugo award-winning science fiction writer, former Netflix chief of staff, HVAC technician, salesperson, public school teacher)
- Average student engagement: Full 1-hour coaching session shows strong adoption
- Underlying engagement metrics strong beyond institutional boosting

**Revenue Model Shift:**
- Moved from coaching-only to hiring-based monetization
- Established partnerships with multiple institutional groups
- Careerspan now acts as job matching/career coaching platform with employer recruiting as revenue driver

### Emory Partnership Opportunity

**Nicole's Position:**
- Willing and excited to try different monetization approaches
- Alumni coaching program already receiving multiple daily requests
- Recognizes hiring/job search as critical gap in Emory's current offerings
- Transitioning Emory Connects platform from Gravity to People Grove (similar higher ed tool, launching later 2026)
- Hiring/job matching has not been focus in recent years but is needed now

**Vrijen's Value Proposition:**
- Framed Careerspan not as one-time tool (like resume generator, interview prep) but as ongoing investment in alumni engagement
- Economics: Coaching costs ~$4/hour for institution vs. $5-6/hour for 1-on-1 calls; alumni get persistent value even after initial conversation
- Pricing paradigm: Focus on "lasting use" and alumni lock-in through relationship, not one-off transactions

**Market Evidence:**
- Aspire Institute (nonprofit) exploring partnership but concerned about cost
- Rice University in active conversations
- Testimonial from power user (Rhett) demonstrates qualitative impact: specific, genuine benefits beyond AI generation

### Proposed Partnership Structure

**Option A: Small-Scale Subscription + Wide Distribution**
- Emory pays low subscription fee for Careerspan licenses
- Careerspan distributes widely to alumni
- Allows both parties to establish pricing defensibility for other partnerships

**Option B: Material Support + Employer Recruiting**
- Emory provides support beyond direct licensing fee
- Careerspan centralizes job distribution through platform
- Alumni encouraged to post roles; Emory alumni can quick-check against talent pool
- Creates self-reinforcing incentive: employers recruit because talent pool is there, alumni engage because jobs exist

**Negotiated Outcome:**
- Pilot with 500 Emory alumni (~middle ground for manual customer service)
- Careerspan pricing: 2-3 or 3-4 dollars per license per year (low enough to maintain goodwill narrative)
- Emory's investment: $1,000-2,000 annually for pilot
- Both parties can use pilot data for external credibility ("This is working" proof)

### Product Integration & Mechanics

**User Experience Continuity:**
- Nothing about core job-hunting process changes for end users
- Coaching still integrated into platform (no removal of this feature)
- New element: employers posting jobs indicate "direct apply from [employer name]"
- Alumni with Careerspan account: one-click "I want to apply" → automatic processing against all jobs
- **One-time investment for alumni**: Set up profile once, get assessed for all future job postings automatically

**Pricing Flexibility for Alumni:**
- Could offer tiered pricing early: $50 to check against 100 alumni during growth phase
- Can adjust pricing as pool grows and value increases
- Customizable to align with leadership priorities (PR outcome, revenue, engagement)

### Strategic Alignment Discussion

**From Emory (Nicole's perspective):**
- Need to show good data from pilot to leadership before long-term commitment
- Pilot success could lead to broader engagement opportunities
- Hiring/job placement directly addresses alumni needs
- Metrics for success: alumni adoption, job posting from employers, ongoing engagement beyond initial use

**From Careerspan (Vrijen's perspective):**
- $3-5K annual commitment gives proof point for other institutions
- Access to larger alumni pool incentivizes investment in employer recruiting
- Self-selecting group that invests time in profiles = higher quality outcomes = better employer experience
- Creates flywheel: employers see results → post more → alumni see opportunities → engage more

### Key Insights

**Why Careerspan Matters:**
- Alumni gain affordable ongoing career support, not just job board access
- Employers get vetted, motivated talent pool aligned with alma mater values
- Emory alumni association positions itself as active career enabler, strengthening institutional brand
- Measurable engagement metric for alumni relations department

**Negotiation Framework:**
- Vrijen explicitly acknowledged flexibility on structure ("super flexible to create a system")
- Both parties want data/metrics before committing long-term
- Neither party has hard position; collaboration around incentive alignment is possible
- Leadership buy-in at Emory contingent on pilot results, not pricing alone
