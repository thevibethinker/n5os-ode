# Business Context

Aviato operates as a broad, API‑first people and company data provider with impressive reach (1.1B people, 72M companies). Its core proposition is to make rich, continuously refreshed profiles and signals (vesting, movement, open‑to‑network flags, social profiles, etc.) available to builders who want to power investment, sales, and recruiting workflows.

For Careerspan and Zo, this meeting comes at a moment when API access and workflow tooling have become much more accessible. Zo gives non‑technical founders a way to wire together APIs (via n8n and similar tools) into durable systems, and Careerspan already has a unique qualitative dataset (long‑form candidate stories, coaching transcripts). The missing piece is a high‑quality quantitative enrichment layer that can answer "who" and "when" questions at scale.

Aviato’s recruitment intelligence—especially vesting and movement signals, plus hidden open‑to‑work flags—directly addresses that gap. If paired intelligently with Careerspan’s qualitative depth, it could enable a differentiated "AI talent agency" model that identifies high‑leverage candidates at the right time and helps them tell their stories, instead of simply scraping LinkedIn and spamming generic outreach.

From Aviato’s perspective, Zo and Careerspan represent an on‑ramp into a growing ecosystem of AI agents and power users: solo founders and small teams who are building opinionated workflows on top of API data. A successful integration here would not only bring incremental revenue but also serve as a case study for how Aviato’s data can combine with conversational AI to create more human‑centered systems.
