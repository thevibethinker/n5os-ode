# Key Moments & Notable Quotes

## Strategic Pivots & Insights

### Moment 1: Product Philosophy Articulation (Early Call)
**Vrijen on Careerspan's Core Thesis:**

### Moment 2: The Vibe Check & Information Asymmetry Problem
**Vrijen on why candidates are undervalued:**
> "What we found was all you really need to do is tell them, why do I need to pay attention? If they trust that the source itself is filled with legitimate people, they're generally much more willing to do that."

**Supporting Quote:**
> "The best candidates are passive. They're not updating their LinkedIn, they're not working on their stuff. Their shit is out of date. So actually, the candidates that you see out there, the visibly good ones are what we're all fighting over. And there's the invisible amazing ones that just need like, 'oh, there is this opportunity. Let me actually put in the effort to update my stuff.'"

**Significance**: Identifies the core market failure Careerspan solves—passive candidate engagement and information updating problem. Not about filtering bad candidates; about awakening great passive ones.

**Strategic Implication**: For partnership with Sam, this reframes value prop: Careerspan provides "re-activated good candidates," not "pre-screened candidates."

### Moment 5: Reactivation Loop Discovery
**Vrijen on organic reengagement:**
> "Careerspan is so aggressively pro candidate we have folks...we see a average of 2 to 3% natural reengagement any given month, regardless of how long ago they last logged on. So even around, like, 10, 12 months, around that mark. We're seeing the 2 to 3% folks just coming back organically."

**Vibe Check Adoption:**
> "I think the last just this past month where finally, I think we cracked the UX for vibe checks. So now we're seeing about 30% of our users that complete sign up do a vibe check and 50% of them do two or more. So we're starting to see, that is like a whole loop for folks where they like getting that feedback and then they incorporating it."

**Significance**: Not a marketing claim; actual product engagement metric. 30% adoption of new feature (vibe checks) among new users is strong. 2-3% passive reactivation monthly is exceptional for a talent pool. Sam explicitly noted this is what they need for "candidates who haven't quite gone through placements."

**Strategic Implication**: This addresses Sam's explicit need state: "How often do you reengage candidates who don't get placed?" Careerspan has operationalized this.

**The Symbiotic Insight:**

**Strategic Implication**: This is the partnership's core value creation thesis—neither party would be as valuable alone.
