# Decisions: Careerspan <> Sam Partnership Call

---

## Primary Decisions Made

### **Decision 1: Proceed with Partnership Exploration** ✅ APPROVED
**Status:** DECIDED & COMMITTED  
**Authority:** Sam founding team vote (2 of 3 thumbs up: Saul + Ash)  
**Terms:**
- Partnership model: Careerspan on sourcing, Sam on placement/employer side
- Economics: 5% kickback when candidates placed (up to 12 months)
- Next phase: Formal partnership agreement development

**Rationale from Sam team:**
- Strong product-market fit alignment between Careerspan's candidate data depth and Sam's employer network
- Non-competitive positioning: Careerspan stays sourcing-focused, Sam stays dual-sided marketplace
- Complementary advantages: Each side has asymmetric strength the other needs

**Careerspan's commitment:**
- Ready to move forward with partnership structure discussions
- Team will prepare partnership agreement and integration mechanics

---

### **Decision 2: Acquisition is Viable Exit Path** ✅ DECIDED (Conditional)
**Status:** DECIDED & CONDITIONAL  
**Timeline:** 3-6 month minimum before serious acquisition discussions  
**Key Condition:** Team acquisition (including ILSE, AI reliability engineer) is non-negotiable

**Sam's Acquisition Interest:**
- Willing to pursue acquisition structured like Neighborly precedent (IP + assets + equity stake)
- Acquisition would narrow Careerspan focus to startup/entry-level talent (from current broad market)
- Would maintain Careerspan platform as low-cost user asset (retain user love, organic engagement)

**Careerspan's Acquisition Conditions:**
- Must include founding team (Vrijen + Logan minimum, ILSE preferred/critical)
- Prefers team acquisition over maximum valuation optimization
- "Rather make less money and work with you guys 100 years" = prioritizes cultural fit + Sam team
- Willing to be transparent about acquisition priorities (stable exit > maximum payout)
- Acceptable to take equity + employment packages instead of all cash

**Mutually Agreed:**
- Acquisition not default outcome; partnership is primary exploration
- Acquisition only materializes if: (a) Sam's funding allows, (b) strategic alignment remains, (c) team terms negotiable

---

### **Decision 3: Careerspan Will NOT Pursue Broad Marketplace Expansion** ✅ DECIDED
**Status:** STRATEGIC PIVOT CONFIRMED  
**What Changed:** Careerspan explicitly ruling out competing as dual-sided marketplace  
**Current Model:** Staying focused on sourcing/candidate-side value (not adding employer-side product)

**Rationale:**
- Recognizes employer-side already well-served by platforms like Sam
- Candidate-side is actual competitive advantage (passive candidate reactivation, organic engagement)
- "Best candidates are passive" = focus on candidate depth better investment than employer breadth
- Avoids muddy market competition with partners

**Implication for Partnership:**
- Positions Careerspan as pure sourcing supplier to Sam (vs. competitor)
- Enables cleaner P&L for Sam (sourcing cost line-item) vs. full platform competition

---

### **Decision 4: Market Focus Will Remain Broad (Near-Term), With Optionality for Narrowing** ✅ DECIDED
**Status:** FLEXIBLE COMMITMENT  
**Current State:** Careerspan sourcing from broad talent pool (all experience levels, all sectors)

**Conditions for Narrowing:**
- If acquisition proceeds: Sam prefers startup/entry-level focus + eliminates super-senior/executive category
- Careerspan acceptable to this narrowing (if acquisition terms otherwise favorable)
- If partnership remains standalone: Careerspan retains flexibility to pursue other employer channels (Marvin Ventures, other job boards)

**Rationale:**
- Current broad model = optimal for network building phase
- Narrowing only justified if financial/strategic terms warrant (i.e., acquisition economics)
- Preserves optionality during 3-6 month "low power mode" phase

---

### **Decision 5: Team Approach to Acquisition Will Prioritize Stability Over Valuation** ✅ DECIDED
**Status:** PHILOSOPHICAL COMMITMENT  
**What Agreed:** Founding team (Vrijen + Logan) willing to accept lower acquisition valuation if conditions met:
1. Team stays intact (ILSE included)
2. Stable employment terms post-acquisition
3. Dignified exit (everyone leaves with respect/career momentum)
4. Working with cultural-aligned partner (Sam vs. alternatives like ETS)

**Investor Management:**
- Vrijen has good personal relationships with current investors
- Can navigate investor disappointment if acquisition doesn't maximize return
- Will explain team chose partnership over max payout

**Implication:**
- Removes valuation as blocking issue for acquisition
- Focuses negotiation on: team structure, employment terms, role clarity post-acquisition
- Signals flexibility to Sam on financial structure (cash vs. equity vs. mixed)

## Assumption Validation Needed

### **Critical Assumptions to Validate Before Next Call**

1. **Careerspan's 5% economics work for Sam**
   - Has Sam modeled placement volume × 5% margin?
   - Is this competitive vs. other talent sourcing costs?

2. **Candidate data integration is seamless**
   - Can Careerspan export candidate profiles to Sam's platform?
   - What data governance rules apply?

3. **Acquisition timeline is realistic**
   - Will Sam's funding close before 3-6 month Careerspan window?
   - If earlier close, will Careerspan feel pressured to accelerate?

4. **Team acquisition is truly non-negotiable for Sam**
   - Does Sam want ILSE specifically or just "Careerspan team"?
   - What's Sam's capacity to absorb 3 new full-time team members?

5. **Market narrowing (startup/entry-level) doesn't harm Careerspan network effects**
   - How much current revenue/engagement is from super-senior segment?
   - Is narrowing strategically acceptable or just financially driven?
