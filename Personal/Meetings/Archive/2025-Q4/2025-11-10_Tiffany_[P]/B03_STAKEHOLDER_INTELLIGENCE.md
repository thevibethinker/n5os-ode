**Role / Positioning**
- Senior leader at Zo focused on storytelling, launch strategy, and community‑driven growth.
- Acts as a bridge between product vision (Robin/Ben) and real‑world user narratives.

- Showcase concrete, credible user stories that prove Zo’s value beyond generic “AI assistant” framing.
- Build co‑marketing muscles with ambassadors, early adopters, and partner communities (n8n, hacker houses, higher‑ed, AI collectives).

**Worldview / Filters**
- Thinks in terms of threads, clips, and hooks: content must be natively social and thumb‑stopping.
- Pragmatic about scope: asks are framed as “doable lifts” given V’s energy and time constraints.
- Sensitive to format/channel fit (e.g., interview good for Logan’s TikTok, but single‑speaker better for Zo’s official feed).

**Skepticisms / Risks**
- Concerned about complexity or over‑produced formats that might underperform on social.
- Wants to avoid over‑burdening V; explicitly says she doesn’t want this to consume too much of his energy.

**Leverage Points / How to Work With Her**
- Bring clear, short, hook‑driven video concepts that map 1:1 to Zo’s value propositions.
- Provide organized lists of communities, people, and angles V can unlock so she can fold them into the broader launch system.
- Offer fast, usable assets (clips, blurbs, intros) that slot into her pre‑planned launch calendar.
