# B01: Detailed Recap

**Meeting:** Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan), Logan Currie (Careerspan), Alex Garnepudi (Ola/Hello.cv)  
**Duration:** ~28 minutes  
**Key Topics:** Domain registry operations, job market dynamics, B2B2C business model pivots

## Context
Birthday celebration meeting between co-founders of competing HR tech companies. This is a reconnection after 5 months (last spoke in June). Vrijen/Logan are approaching 3-year anniversary of Careerspan.

## Main Discussion Flow

### 1. Ola Registry Operations & Premium Domain Strategy
Alex explains the mechanical decisions behind Ola's domain premium pricing model:
- Limited top 20,000 globally as "premium" instead of traditional short-domain-only approach
- Rationale: Bandwidth/latency constraints with registries (100k+ domain premium list creates latency issues)
- Customer integration issues drive speed requirements (integrations fail if lookup slow)
- Logan purchased "logan.cv" after it dropped from $5,000 price point (was previously premium)
- Alex provides birthday gift: free 1-year registration for "vrijen.ca"

### 2. Platform Architecture & Positioning
- **Ola** = Registry (domain purchasing)
- **Hello.cv** = Registrar + consumer profile builder (99.999% of users know one or the other, not both)
- ~25,000 profiles built on Hello.cv platform
- 700,000 views in past 30 days on .cv profiles
- #1 Product of the Day on Product Hunt for Hello platform

### 3. Customer Cohort & Adoption Analysis
- Early adopter phase: 25k → 150k profiles = transition from "Innovators" to "Early Adopters" (Alex's adoption theory)
- First customer cohorts are "useless" for insights (Pete Fader reference on customer lifetime value)
- Use case unknown; distribution normal (no extreme tail behavior)
- Market segment: Mix of consulting/advisory AND corporate 9-to-5

### 4. Registry Renewal Challenges
Alex and Logan were discussing technical infrastructure:
- Recently took over Cape Verde registry (June 2024)
- Officially launched Hello.cv and Ola (October/November 2024)
- Now handling first renewal cycles
- Backend stability critical: registrars also renewing on behalf of customers
- Latency + billing alignment required

### 5. Careerspan Pivot & Growth
Logan: Employer portal launched (B2B2C model)
- Use existing B2C tech stack for direct employer hiring
- Companies input job descriptions, track candidates, manage rounds
- Still early; only small/futurist companies adopting
- Recent UMass Eisenberg pilot wrapped
- Marketing campaigns launching (Logan's background in marketing)
- Approaching B2C free model; monetizing B2B hiring side
- VC conversations: Marvin Ventures engaged; Flybridge intro pending

### 6. Monetization Strategy Discussion
Core insight: Job seeker monetization is fundamentally difficult.
- Alex references solo job board operator (Latin America tech focus): $500k/year via headhunting fee model (matches ~20 people)
- Careerspan pivoting toward employer-side monetization (vs. candidate monetization)
- Jobs aggregation partnership potential: Careerspan could pipe direct job applicants to Ola
- Current scraping source: Fantastic (Africa's largest job site, through co-founder Ope's network)

### 7. Job Market Realities & Strategic Positioning
- Success rate for job applicants: <1% (even with Ivy League credentials)
- Alex/Logan/Vrijen represent: Harvard MBA, Cornell MBA, Wharton MBA credentials
- All operate in <1% success rate space
- Careerspan positioning: "Fourth door" to hiring (beyond inbound/referral/recruiter)
- Value prop: Bypass ATS, reduce recruitment fees, replace outdated HRIS systems
- Target: Replace "ossified" HR systems

### 8. Three-Year Anniversary Milestone
- Careerspan founded November 20, 2022 (7 days before this call, 3-year mark)
- Founder bonding: Shared trauma/masochism from previous working relationship
- Most startups fail at 18-month mark; survival to 3 years significant
- Original ideation via WhatsApp message

### 9. Success Definition Evolution
Logan on current success metrics:
- B2B hiring side: Time-to-hire, pipeline quality (unchanged from industry standard)
- B2C candidate side: Easier process + higher placement success rates
- NOT focusing on placement/recruitment fees (different model than gringo-style job boards)
- Open to partnerships/acquisition scenarios ("who knows" language about bigger companies)
- 2026 outlook: Exploring partnership conversations that could reshape business

### 10. Closing Strategic Questions
Alex poses framework for Careerspan's ambitions:
- $2M ARR? $5M ARR? $100M ARR?
- What are the KPIs needed to reach target?
- How has definition of success shifted from June conversation?
