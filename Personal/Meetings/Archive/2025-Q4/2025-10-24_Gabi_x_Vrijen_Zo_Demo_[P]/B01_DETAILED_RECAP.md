   Gabi joins the call a bit distracted, dealing with insurance and other admin tasks. V acknowledges the annoyance of vendors calling at inconvenient times and they settle that they have time until around noon.

   Gabi expresses strong curiosity about what V has been "cooking up." V frames the session as a show-and-tell of his recent AI work and how it might help with her workflows and productivity.

   V explains he has been heavily experimenting with tools like ChatGPT and Claude, building relatively advanced custom functionality. He believes his advantage is asking unusual, second-order questions that unlock capabilities most people do not realize are possible.

   V introduces Zo Computer as a "computer in the cloud you control by prompting"—like ChatGPT but with a persistent file structure, access to multiple models, and an always-on "agent mode" that can keep working between interactions. Gabi confirms she has not gone deep on workflow automation yet because she has not been sure where to start or what would meaningfully help.

   They align that most real work is not linear "if this, then that" flows. Gabi notes her work often branches and recombines in complex ways and largely lives in her head. V reframes the core problem for founders and VCs as managing **context and state**: how much context they can hold and the evolving state of many parallel relationships, tasks, and ideas.

   V explains that instead of stitching together many isolated workflows, he is designing a comprehensive, well-bounded system on Zo that can handle entire chains like processing meetings, generating summaries, intros, and follow-ups as one coherent environment.
