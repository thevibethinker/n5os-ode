# Key Moments

1. **Gabi’s explicit curiosity about V’s work**  
   Gabi opens by saying she is very curious about what V has built and eager to see it. This sets a high-curiosity tone and establishes the meeting as a serious exploration, not a casual chat.

2. **V’s articulation of Zo’s value proposition**  
   V frames Zo as a "computer in the cloud you control by prompting"—a persistent, multi-model, agentic environment rather than a single chatbot. This is a crisp, memorable description that can be reused in future conversations.

3. **Pricing and subsidized compute discussion**  
   When Gabi asks about pricing, V explains that Zo passes through OpenAI API costs without markup and adds a discount, resulting in subsidized compute. This directly addresses a common objection about AI tool costs for heavy users.

4. **Agreement on the failure of linear workflows**  
   They converge on the view that real work is not a simple linear "this, then that" chain. Gabi describes her own workflows as branching and looping back, and V crystallizes the problem as one of context and state management. This alignment is a critical conceptual moment.

5. **Commitment to continue via Zoom despite tech issues**  
   Rather than abandoning the demo when screen sharing fails, they choose to switch to Zoom and proceed. This shows Gabi’s sustained interest and V’s determination to deliver a high-quality demo experience.
