## B03_INSIGHTS

### Workflow Design Paradox
Traditional workflow tools fail for knowledge workers because they assume sequential, discrete processes. Founder/VC work is inherently graph-structured: one meeting triggers multiple non-sequential follow-ups (summaries, intros, analysis). The breakdown comes from trying to decompose interconnected cognitive work into independent automations.

### Context-as-Bottleneck Mental Model
Vrijen identifies the true constraint for founders/VCs: "context and state." Not time, not task volume—but the cognitive overhead of maintaining situational awareness across dozens of parallel threads. This reframes productivity from "doing more tasks" to "reducing context-switching tax."

### Differentiation Through Question Quality
Vrijen's competitive advantage: "asking questions nobody else thinks of asking." This reveals a meta-skill: the ability to find novel framings unlocks capabilities others miss even with identical tools. Intelligence is less about answers, more about question formulation.

### Tool Selection Signal
Vrijen's strong endorsement of Zo ("I believe they're going to be huge") coupled with intent to "support them as best as I can" signals both conviction and potential strategic partnership thinking. His willingness to pay top-tier pricing validates product-market fit.

### Unspoken Implication: Zapier Criticism
By stating Zapier "is actually not as good as a lot of people think," Vrijen challenges industry consensus. This suggests either (a) most users don't stress-test Zapier's limitations, or (b) Zapier's marketing/reputation outpaces its utility for advanced use cases.
