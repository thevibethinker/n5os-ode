# Key Moments & Quotes

- Bram highlights Ilsa’s line: **"Communication can often be cheaper than just adding another knowledge base, and a deprecated knowledge base becomes a cost center."**  
  → Reframes the cost of unmanaged tools and archives.

- V articulates a key aspiration: a system where **transcripts are decomposed into basic work blocks**, and different downstream outputs (follow‑ups, research summaries, etc.) are assembled from those blocks depending on context.

- Bram warns about recursive degradation: adding AI‑generated judgments to the knowledge base can create a **"garbage in, worse garbage out"** cycle as models repeatedly reference their own outputs.

- Bram’s "master ledger" metaphor lands strongly: a **highly selective, dignified table of entries** (like a club with a strict door policy) that anchors organizational knowledge.

- V acknowledges that he has been **implicitly delegating judgment** to AI (e.g., "name all the decisions") and sees how this can erode sovereignty over choices.

- Bram proposes the pattern of shortening meetings and using reclaimed time for **participants to review and highlight the transcript**, emphasizing that different highlights are valuable signals rather than noise.

- V has a meta‑insight: solving his **personal information discipline** and the company’s **organizational knowledge discipline** are two sides of the same problem; progress on one can accelerate the other.
