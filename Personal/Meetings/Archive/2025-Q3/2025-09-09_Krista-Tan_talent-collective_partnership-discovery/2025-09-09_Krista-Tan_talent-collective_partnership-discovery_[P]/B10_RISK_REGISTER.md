# Risk Register

## Identified Risks & Mitigation Strategies

### Risk 1: Market Saturation in Recruiting/Talent Tools
**Severity**: Medium  
**Probability**: High  
**Impact**: Difficulty differentiating Careerspan from incumbents; community partners may question unique value proposition

**Context from Conversation**: Krista stated: "I've seen so many demos of recruiting tools that I'm like, what else? Like what else you got? You know, like, I just don't see a future for you, honestly." This reflects vendor fatigue in the recruiting tools space. The market is flooded with ATS systems, job boards, candidate sourcing platforms, and resume builders. Most fail to deliver differentiated value.

**Why This Matters**: If Careerspan is perceived as "another recruiting tool," community partners may deprioritize integration and members may deprioritize adoption. This is especially true if the free-to-use model doesn't translate to strong adoption metrics within Talent Collective.

**Mitigation**:
- Lead with **positioning clarity**: Emphasize that Careerspan is not a recruiting tool but a "career development platform that happens to generate better candidate profiles for employers."
- Focus messaging on the **3D resume concept**: Differentiate from traditional resume builders by highlighting the mental model generation and values/achievement mapping.
- Lean on **Krista's validation**: If Talent Collective formally endorses Careerspan, that community seal of approval becomes the differentiator—it's not "another tool," it's "the tool Talent Collective uses."
- Track competitive landscape: Monitor recruiting tool announcements to anticipate feature parity erosion.

### Risk 2: Integration Friction & Support Burden
**Severity**: High  
**Probability**: Medium  
**Impact**: Failed pilot; stalled integration; damaged relationship if members have poor experience

**Context from Conversation**: The conversation did not dive deep into technical integration requirements. Unclear points:
- How does Careerspan integrate with Talent Collective's platform architecture?
- What's the member onboarding friction? (Does someone have to sign up separately? Can they SSO through Talent Collective?)
- Who supports members if they run into issues with Careerspan?

**Why This Matters**: If the integration is clunky, members won't use it, regardless of product quality. Community-based adoption is more friction-sensitive than B2B enterprise adoption because members are volunteers, not employees obligated to use a tool.

**Mitigation**:
- **Make the pilot phase explicit**: Before full rollout, commit to a small cohort (20-50 pilot members) with direct support from Careerspan team.
- **Design frictionless onboarding**: Work with Krista to embed Careerspan sign-up in Talent Collective's member onboarding flow, or enable SSO.
- **Establish support SLA**: Define who owns support tickets (Krista? Careerspan? Shared?), response time, and escalation path.
- **Conduct technical design review**: Schedule the second call with emphasis on architecture. Build integration roadmap with clear milestones and dependencies.
- **Create member feedback loop**: Use pilot to gather UX/integration feedback before scaling.

### Risk 3: Community Skepticism & Trust Erosion
**Severity**: Medium  
**Probability**: Medium  
**Impact**: Members view Talent Collective as "selling out" to vendors; Krista loses credibility with members; partnership backfires

**Context from Conversation**: Krista explicitly stated that Talent Collective is "values-aligned" with long-term relationship building and distrusts vendors chasing "instant ROI." Members have chosen Talent Collective because they trust that the community prioritizes member interests over vendor relationships. If Careerspan is perceived as extractive or pushy, it undermines that trust.

**Why This Matters**: Community platforms are trust-based systems. Recommendation credibility is fragile. If Krista recommends a tool that doesn't deliver value or that aggressively monetizes members, it erodes her credibility and potentially damages Talent Collective's positioning as a member-first organization.

**Mitigation**:
- **Prove values alignment first**: Demonstrate that Careerspan is genuinely interested in member success, not just member acquisition. This means:
  - Prioritize member feedback over vanity metrics
  - Be transparent about monetization plans (freemium model)
  - Don't push for aggressive community promotion until pilot validates genuine member value
- **Co-brand carefully**: Position the partnership as "Talent Collective + Careerspan" rather than "Careerspan has access to Talent Collective." This maintains Krista's agency and positioning.
- **Establish community norms**: Define what "value" looks like for members (e.g., "better career storytelling," not "better resume formatting"). Measure against these norms.
- **Give Krista veto power**: Maintain the explicit understanding that Krista can recommend the partnership terminate if she detects member dissatisfaction.

### Risk 4: Integration Demands from Hiring Companies
**Severity**: Medium  
**Probability**: Medium  
**Impact**: Scope creep on Careerspan's roadmap; distraction from core product; strained partnership if demands can't be met

**Context from Conversation**: Not explicitly discussed, but implied: Talent Collective's hiring company partners will eventually want integration with Careerspan (e.g., "Can we see Talent Collective members' profiles in our ATS?", "Can we submit job orders directly through Talent Collective?"). These integrations sound simple but often create complex dependencies.

**Why This Matters**: If hiring companies start making integration demands, Careerspan's technical roadmap becomes driven by B2B customer needs rather than community member needs. This is the classic "tail wagging the dog" problem in community-driven models.

**Mitigation**:
- **Define integration scope upfront**: On the second call, establish clear boundaries on what integrations Careerspan will and won't build (e.g., ATS integrations vs. job order integrations).
- **Hiring company channel**: Create a separate GTM path for hiring companies. Don't route hiring company requests through Krista/Talent Collective. Instead, position Careerspan as the primary vendor relationship.
- **Prioritize community member features**: Use the community roadmap as the north star. Hiring company integrations are "nice to have," not "must have."
- **Transparent trade-offs**: If hiring company demands conflict with member feature development, make that explicit. Let Krista weigh in on priorities.

### Risk 5: Freemium Model Monetization Uncertainty
**Severity**: Medium  
**Probability**: Medium  
**Impact**: Careerspan's unit economics don't work; partnership doesn't drive sufficient B2B revenue; pressure to monetize members aggressively

**Context from Conversation**: Vrijen stated: "all of the coaching conversations are free and always will be" + "free platform forever" + freemium model with cap on features. The B2U (business-to-university) track is listed but not deeply explored. The B2B hiring company track is hinted at but not quantified.

**Why This Matters**: If Careerspan can't achieve sustainable unit economics on the B2B side, pressure will build to monetize the B2C/community side more aggressively. This could manifest as upselling to members, aggressive in-app prompts, or feature gatekeeping that degrades the user experience and trust.

**Mitigation**:
- **Model hiring company economics**: Before scaling the partnership, validate that hiring companies will pay enough to justify the cost of serving community members for free. Get early data from pilot.
- **B2U diversification**: Accelerate the university partnership track (Columbia, UMass pilot programs). If 20-30% of revenue comes from B2U and 70% from B2B hiring companies, the unit economics are more stable.
- **Transparent monetization strategy**: On the second call, share Careerspan's monetization roadmap with Krista. This builds trust and gives her early warning if monetization might pressure members.
- **Quarterly business review**: Once partnership is live, establish a quarterly review to discuss unit economics and sustainability. This provides early warning if the model is breaking down.

### Risk 6: Limited Scope of Initial Partnership Validation
**Severity**: Low-Medium  
**Probability**: High  
**Impact**: Partnership stalls because scope is too vague; commitments aren't clear; second call uncovers misalignment

**Context from Conversation**: The call was productive but deliberately open-ended. Specific commitments:
- ✅ Krista will test the product with Talent Collective members
- ✅ Careerspan will provide content/code
- ? Unclear on timelines, success metrics, integration architecture, data ownership, support model

**Why This Matters**: Vague commitments often fail because both parties have different mental models of success. Six weeks after this call, Careerspan might be waiting for Krista to evangelize to the community while Krista is waiting for Careerspan to have a better mobile experience.

**Mitigation**:
- **Schedule second call immediately**: Don't let momentum die. Lock in date/time for 30-45 days out.
- **Pre-call prep**: Careerspan team documents what it needs from the partnership (pilot cohort size, timeline, feedback criteria). Krista documents what Talent Collective needs (integration architecture, support SLA, content formats).
- **Structured agenda**: Second call should have a written agenda with specific outcomes (e.g., "agree on integration roadmap," "define success metrics," "commit to pilot timeline").
- **Shared documentation**: After second call, produce a one-pager on partnership commitments, timelines, and success metrics. Both parties sign off.

### Risk 7: Dependency on Krista's Advocacy
**Severity**: Medium  
**Probability**: Low-Medium  
**Impact**: If Krista leaves Talent Collective or becomes less engaged, partnership loses its champion and stalls

**Context from Conversation**: Krista is the primary point of contact and the key decision-maker. She controls member communication, community events, and partnership credibility. If she leaves (e.g., takes another job, shifts focus), the partnership lacks institutional anchor.

**Why This Matters**: Community partnerships are relationship-dependent. If the relationship is built on one person, it's fragile.

**Mitigation**:
- **Cultivate co-founder relationship**: Logan and Vrijen should also build relationships with Krista's co-founder. Ensure at least two people from Talent Collective are engaged with the partnership.
- **Formalize the partnership**: Even early on, document commitments and decision rights. Don't rely on Krista's informal approval.
- **Community-level adoption**: Push for community-level adoption (member testimonials, case studies, integrated member journey) so the partnership becomes embedded in Talent Collective's identity, not just Krista's personal initiative.
- **Regular sync cadence**: Monthly check-ins (vs. ad-hoc) keep the partnership on track and visible to other stakeholders.
