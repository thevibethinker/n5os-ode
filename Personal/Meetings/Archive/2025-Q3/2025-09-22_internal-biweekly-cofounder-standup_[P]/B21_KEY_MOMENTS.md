# Key Moments

1. **Reframing the interview as a flow, not a script**  
   Logan and Vrijen agree that they are "good off the cuff" and should treat the question list as a set of anchors. This unlocks a more natural conversation and reduces anxiety about hitting every prepared question.

2. **Clarifying handoff mechanics**  
   They design an implicit handoff pattern: the person leading a segment watches the clock, responds briefly to Kathy’s answer, and then smoothly bridges to the next segment, instead of saying something jarring like "Now it’s over to Vrijen."

3. **Prioritizing high‑value topics**  
   They consciously cut lower‑yield questions and focus the interview on three core areas: Kathy’s decision‑making "algorithm," AI integration in her work, and what job seekers should know about the future of HR tech and hiring.

4. **Recognizing the magic‑link failure point**  
   Logan surfaces a subtle but important flaw: candidates must explicitly click "Yes, I want to apply" inside the vibe check, otherwise they never appear as applicants—even if they interacted with the link. This becomes a clear target for product and GTM fixes.

5. **Realizing how strong the product experience has become**  
   While watching a demo, Vrijen remarks that the product "looks really good" and is something they should be "yelling about from the rooftops," highlighting the gap between product quality and current awareness.

6. **Committing to community‑first distribution**  
   They articulate a concrete plan to use communities like Superposition and Rise as anchor partners, using non‑binding LOIs with explicit rates as a way to build early consensus and proof points.

7. **Deciding to test a commission‑based SDR**  
   The decision to pilot an India‑based SDR on a commission model crystallizes a strategic preference for reversible experiments over immediate fixed‑cost hires.

8. **Rescheduling internal rituals to protect deep work**  
   They choose to move the bi‑weekly company standup and potentially analysis weekly to protect focus for Kathy prep and recording, signaling that this interview and GTM design work are genuinely high priority.
