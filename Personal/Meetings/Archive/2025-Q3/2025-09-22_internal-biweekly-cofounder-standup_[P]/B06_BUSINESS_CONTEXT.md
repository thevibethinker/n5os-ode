# Business Context

Careerspan is in an inflection phase where the core candidate product experience is strong but distribution and instrumentation are still catching up. The co‑founders see a gap between how good the product feels in demos ("we should be yelling about this from the rooftops") and the current volume and clarity of signups, especially through magic‑link flows and community posts.

At the same time, there is a strategic opportunity to differentiate by being deeply embedded in communities rather than behaving like a generic job board. Communities like Superposition and Rise offer concentrated pools of high‑intent job seekers, but they require thoughtful rate structures and messaging that respects community norms. Non‑binding LOIs are being used as a tool to lock in early commitments and pricing while preserving flexibility.

Operationally, the team is still stabilizing key infrastructure: Braze + Sendgrid loops for outbound campaigns and candidate journeys are in progress, with Brady handling backend complexity. These systems need to be reliable before any large‑scale GTM push, and current measurement gaps (for example, not clearly distinguishing magic‑link candidates from existing database records) make it hard to attribute outcomes.

On the hiring side, Careerspan is deliberately avoiding premature fixed‑cost expansion. Instead, they are experimenting with a commission‑based SDR based in India who can execute targeted LinkedIn outreach. This experiment is designed to test whether outsourced, incentive‑aligned outreach can deliver high‑quality meetings at a sustainable cost, and how that model might interface with community‑driven GTM.

The Kathy interview sits at the intersection of these dynamics. It is both a content asset and a credibility play: showcasing a Workday AI leader’s thinking on hiring tech, AI integration, and career decisions strengthens Careerspan’s positioning when talking to communities, partners, and candidates. Done well, it can provide reusable proof points and insights that feed directly into the emerging GTM narrative.
