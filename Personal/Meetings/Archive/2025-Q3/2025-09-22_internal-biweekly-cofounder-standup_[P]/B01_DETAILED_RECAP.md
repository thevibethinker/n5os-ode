# Detailed Recap (Bi-Weekly Co‑Founder Standup)

This internal standup is a working session between Vrijen and Logan to tighten the upcoming podcast interview with Kathy, a senior Workday AI leader, and to align on near‑term GTM and hiring experiments.

They begin by reviewing the draft question set and quickly agree that the goal is not to march through every question, but to design a conversational flow. Logan proposes a structure where he leads the origin‑story segment, Vrijen takes the "algorithm reveal" (how Kathy makes big career and leadership decisions), and they jointly explore AI integration and the future of hiring. Rather than rigid scripting, they decide the interviewer whose section it is will watch the clock, respond briefly to Kathy’s answer, and then hand off implicitly into the next topic, avoiding jarring "now over to you" transitions.

A major part of the conversation is about timing and facilitation craft. Logan pushes for a looser, flow‑based style where they trust their ability to ask good follow‑ups, and use the questions only as anchors. Vrijen voices concern about knowing when to move on, especially with small 2–5 minute windows per segment, and they resolve this by defining rough time budgets (for example, ~8 minutes on origin story before shifting to algorithm reveal). They explicitly deprioritize generic "ethics" lip‑service unless it is clearly Kathy’s focus, preferring concrete questions about AI integration and what candidates should know about HR tech and the hiring market.

From there, they zoom out into GTM and distribution. Logan describes the complexity of spinning up Braze + Sendgrid "loops" for outbound campaigns and candidate flows—work that Brady has been handling on the backend. They talk about the gap between strong product experience (the candidate stories product now looks "really good") and the reality that signups have softened, in part because current magic‑link flows are brittle and it’s hard to track which candidates come from which communities. This leads to a shared conviction that they need clearer landing pages and job‑board‑style surfaces for specific roles rather than long, dense messages in communities.

They then discuss a community‑first GTM motion. Superposition (Tim) is already positive on sending jobs, and they are talking to Rise, a ~4,000‑member tech‑facing community with high Slack engagement. The plan is to send non‑binding letters of intent (LOIs) to Superposition, Tim, and 1–2 other partners that lock in pricing and signal that Careerspan is a "trusted recruiter" working with communities instead of competing with them. These LOIs would become proof points when selling into other communities.

On the hiring side, Vrijen outlines an experiment with a commission‑based SDR contractor in India, someone who has previously worked with US startups and can independently prospect on LinkedIn for relevant candidates (for example, people posting about product roles in target cities). They align that this is a reversible test: compensate per meeting or placement, evaluate performance after a short pilot, and only then decide whether to scale or move toward a more traditional inside‑sales model.

The meeting closes with schedule coordination and capacity management. They decide to skip or move the bi‑weekly company standup to make room for Kathy prep and intro/outro recording, and note overlap with other recurring sessions like analysis weekly. Logan describes the state of Braze/Sendgrid integration and the challenges in measuring magic‑link candidate flows. They also sketch next‑step experiments: potential letters of intent to communities like Superposition, sending a demo video to specific partners, and possibly spinning up a more product‑specific candidate demo to showcase the improved experience. Overall, the standup solidifies how the Kathy interview, community partnerships, and SDR experiment fit together into a coherent near‑term GTM and hiring strategy.
