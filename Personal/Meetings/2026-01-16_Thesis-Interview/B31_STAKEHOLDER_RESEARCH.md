# B31: Stakeholder Research

**Perspective:** Speaking as neurodivergent founder (ADHD, generalized anxiety, bipolar) of HR tech startup

## Insight 1: The "Differentiated Reality" Problem in Neurodivergent Teams

**Evidence:** "One problem I've noticed in themes with neurodivergent people and like just in human beings in general is that we could all be on the same call, we could all have the same conversation and then we could all walk away with different views of reality and what was agreed on"

**Why it matters:** Misaligned mental models after meetings cause downstream execution gaps—plans diverge because there's no shared "source of truth" for what was actually decided.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ● ○ (4/5)
- Rating rationale: This is a profound observation about the failure mode of verbal communication that isn't captured/validated. Not immediately obvious but highly actionable if addressed through documentation systems.

## Insight 2: Cognitive Load Exceeds System Value for Self-Managed Tools

**Evidence:** "Any system that is like, reliant on managing itself to stay organized is going to add cognitive burden that eventually exceeds value of maintaining system"

**Why it matters:** Explains the adoption cliff of productivity tools—why people start strong with Notion/Linear then abandon them. The maintenance cost exceeds the organizational benefit for neurodivergent users.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)

## Insight 3: AI Acceptance Barrier Requires "Authenticity Theater"

**Evidence:** "People don't like receiving AI generated stuff, right? And so I've had to adapt like the context in which I share AI generated stuff versus the context in which I have... So using AI, I have to put in a curse, at least a cursory effort to like show that I wrote it"

**Why it matters:** AI productivity tools face a hidden social friction—recipients perceive AI-generated communication as inauthentic. This forces users to add manual overhead, negating AI's speed advantage.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ● ○ (4/5)
- Rating rationale: Non-obvious insight about the social dynamics of AI adoption. Most discussion focuses on capability, not perception/authenticity trade-offs.

## Insight 4: Communication as "Active Process" with Branching Work

**Evidence:** "Communication for me isn't like, I told someone and now it's done. Communication is, I told someone I need something done, and then now it's all of the work associated with getting it done... there's a lot of work that branches off from the original task"

**Why it matters:** Reveals why neurodivergent people get "bogged down"—they perceive the full tree of downstream work from a single ask, not just the ask itself. This creates overwhelm before starting.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)
- Rating rationale: Good mental model of task overwhelm, but not uniquely neurodivergent—many experience branching work as daunting. Still useful for understanding why simple tasks feel heavy.

## Insight 5: Productivity Must Be "Fitted to Your Things"

**Evidence:** "My belief is that productivity is personal. Productivity is something that, like, it has to, like, be fitted to your things... For neurodivergent people, a lot of prototypical planning practices don't, they're just, like, very optimistic from my perspective. Like, I don't know how else to explain it that, like, they are, they assume a 'version of me' that is, like, not real"

**Why it matters:** Most productivity tools assume a "standard user" with standard cognitive capacities. Neurodivergent users don't fit that mold—tools overestimate their ability to maintain discipline, follow through, and stay consistent.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ● ● (5/5)
- Rating rationale: Fundamental insight about why productivity tools fail neurodivergent users. The "version of me that is not real" framing is powerful—tools assume capabilities users don't actually have.
