## PRODUCT_IDEA_EXTRACTION

### Product Idea 1: Solutions Architect Marketplace / Recipe Bounty Program

**Description:** Create economic model where enthusiasts ("solutions architects") build verticalized Zo setups (recipes/templates) and get compensated via bounties per recipe + revenue share per user install. Job board or application system where Zo posts vertical needs (VCs, students, creators, HR) and builders apply to create templates.

**Rationale:** Solves go-to-market challenge of making Zo accessible to non-technical users without constraining flexibility. Creates two-sided marketplace: end users get instant value, builders monetize their expertise. Scales distribution through community rather than internal team building every vertical.

**Source:** V suggested Gumroad-style model; Ray immediately latched onto "$100 per recipe + $1 per install" economic structure (Quote: "We should like put on a job board and like a pinned tweet and just say, we're looking for like, you know, so architects will pay you a hundred dollars for every recipe or thing that you build and then we'll give you a dollar for every like user install")

**Confidence:** High - team actively excited about this model, fits their ecosystem vision, V is proof-of-concept user willing to build first recipe

---

### Product Idea 2: Deployable Agents (Combo of Prompts + Config + Triggers)

**Description:** Enable users to install pre-configured "agents" on their Zo instances that run automatically - not just static templates but living workflows with triggers, prompts, API configs bundled together. Goes beyond files/scripts to actual running automation.

**Rationale:** More powerful than recipe templates - user gets working system, not just starting point. Could enable sophisticated use cases (deal flow processor, research synthesizer, content scheduler) as one-click installs.

**Source:** Ben mentioned "another form factor that doesn't exist yet but it's kind of like agents that you can kind of like deploy onto somebody else's Zo and they like live. Some combo of like prompts and configuration and like triggers."

**Confidence:** Medium - team called it "fussy" and "doesn't exist yet," suggesting significant engineering lift. But aligns with ecosystem vision.

---

### Product Idea 3: Website/Service Templates (Like create_website but for specific use cases)

**Description:** Similar to Zo's current create_website command, but verticalized - pre-built service templates for common needs (CRM, deal flow tracker, content calendar, job board, etc.) that deploy as hosted services on user's Zo.

**Rationale:** Extends Zo's "host your own services" capability with turnkey solutions. Users get functional web apps without building from scratch.

**Source:** Ben mentioned "kind of like sight templates" as one variant of the app store idea

**Confidence:** Low-Medium - mentioned briefly, less developed than agent/recipe ideas. Overlap with existing create_website command suggests incremental vs. novel.

---

### Product Idea 4: University/School-Specific Zo Editions

**Description:** Pre-configured Zo instances customized for specific schools - includes class APIs, campus service integrations, school calendar, relevant automations for that student body. "University of Pennsylvania Zo edition" with Penn-specific setup out of box.

**Rationale:** Reduces friction for student adoption - they get value immediately without setup. Creates viral potential within single campus (network effects). Differentiates from generic productivity tools.

**Source:** V suggested "if someone if there was like a University of Pennsylvania, like Zoe edition, would that make you, it easier to distribute in the schools if it started up with a like package that was for that school you know had its classes had its stuff things like that."

**Confidence:** Medium - team engaged with idea, said "interesting" and discussed how to tag recipes to see what's coming from different schools. Would require school-specific customization work but potentially strong distribution payoff.

---

### Product Idea 5: Hackathon/Builder Competition Model for Recipe Generation

**Description:** Host hackathons (virtual or campus-based) where participants compete to build best Zo recipes for specific verticals. Winners get bounties, best recipes added to marketplace. Could partner with Major League Hacking (V has CEO contact).

**Rationale:** Generates pipeline of recipes while building community. Creates urgency/time-bound event that drives signups. Generates content/case studies for marketing.

**Source:** V suggested "if you guys want to do, like, a Zo-based hackathon, we could maybe do that. That could be potentially very cool. I'll get you in touch with them [Major League Hacking]."

**Confidence:** Medium-High - V has direct connection to enable it, team discussed mechanics (bounties, preventing exploitation), hackathons are proven model for developer tools. Ray expressed concern about "engineers just wanting rewards" but V pushed back that quality gates and incentives can be designed properly.

---

### Product Idea 6: Campus Ambassador / Student Solutions Architect Program

**Description:** Identify top Zo users at each university, pay them to: (1) build school-specific recipes, (2) host campus events (pizza + Zo build sessions), (3) provide ongoing support to student users. Student becomes "Zo expert" for their campus.

**Rationale:** Creates localized evangelism and support network. Students know their campus's needs better than Zo team does. Ambassador gets income, Zo gets organic growth + user research + customized recipes per school.

**Source:** V described proven campus penetration playbook: "do some sort of event, get it into a bunch of hands, identify who the top users are and then incentivize them to further proselytize on your campus or be like student ambassadors"

**Confidence:** High - V has executed this model before, team aligned, Ray mentioned "I sent out emails yesterday" showing active university outreach. Lower-risk than broad hackathon approach.
