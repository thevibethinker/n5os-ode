# B32 Thought Provoking Ideas: Jatin Sandilya x Vrijen Attawar

## Strategic "Weirdness" & Mental Models

### 1. The "Invisible 70%" Hypothesis
The standard recruitment market operates on a massive inefficiency: everyone competes for the 30% of talent that is "visible" through standard signals (titles, prestige companies, resumes), leading to 60% inflated market rates. The real alpha lies in building a "proxy for agency" to unlock the other 70% of invisible, high-potential talent.

### 2. The "Recruiter Commission" Democratization
Instead of building tools *for* recruiters, Careerspan's pivot suggests that the real opportunity is *making everyone else a recruiter* by providing them with the data superiority required to claim a commission. This shifts the platform from a "SaaS tool" to a "Financial Instrument/Brokerage."

### 3. The "Internet of Careers" as a Socialist Data Pot
A mental model where individual professional reflections (qualitative data) are contributed to a collective "pot." The exchange is "socialist" in the sense that the benefits of high-fidelity data exchange are collectively felt by both candidates and employers, fixing the "conflict of interest" inherent in owning both sides of the hiring equation.
