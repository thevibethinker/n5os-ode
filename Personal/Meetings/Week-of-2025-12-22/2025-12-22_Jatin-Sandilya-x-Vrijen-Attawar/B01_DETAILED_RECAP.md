# B01 Detailed Recap: Jatin Sandilya x Vrijen Attawar

**Date:** 2025-12-22
**Participants:** Vrijen Attawar (V), Jatin Sandilya

## Executive Summary
V and Jatin discuss the strategic evolution of Careerspan from a direct recruitment tool to a "talent sourcing API" and "data provider." They explore potential partnerships with other platforms (like Alex's company and Ribbon), the value of qualitative candidate data, and the vision of an "Internet of Careers."

## Chronological Breakdown

### 1. Initial Catch-up and Context (00:00 - 02:00)
- V and Jatin exchange greetings, noting the slow "holiday season" energy.
- V mentions flitting between company work and side projects to maintain energy.
- Jatin asks about V's recent chat with Alex.

### 2. Careerspan's Strategic Pivot: The Sourcing API (02:00 - 08:30)
- V reports a successful chat with Alex and has set him up with an employer account.
- V clarifies that Careerspan isn't competing directly but specializing in **sourcing**, which is the "most painful part" for most platforms.
- V describes a vision where Careerspan acts as a "demand network," cross-leveraging partnerships to guarantee candidate inflow for others.
- **The "Recruiter Commission" Model:** V suggests helping non-recruiters take recruiter commissions by providing them with superior candidate data.
- V mentions considering giving space on the cap table to companies that source through Careerspan as a quid pro quo.

### 3. Networking and Introductions (08:30 - 11:30)
- Jatin suggests V connect with **Pete** (a Cornellian recruiter in California, described as "most connected" and looking like a member of the Red Hot Chili Peppers).
- V is familiar with Pete's profile and agrees to an introduction. Jatin asks for a "small blurb" to facilitate the intro.
- V reiterates his goal: "Building the Internet of Careers" and avoiding the burden of dealing with employers directly if others can do it.

### 4. Product Philosophy: Data Superiority (11:30 - 18:00)
- V shares his screen to show the employer platform, highlighting the "stories" candidates tell which provide a high-fidelity summary of strengths and weaknesses.
- **Matching System:** V demonstrates how the system matches candidates to roles (e.g., a PM candidate being a good fit for an AI native role but a bad fit for Fintech).
- V mentions receiving acquisition offers and why they are no longer actively seeking investment (team is slim, tech is ready).
- **The "ZoomInfo for Talent" Vision:** Jatin likens Careerspan to "ZoomInfo for people data," which V confirms as "ZoomInfo for talent."

### 5. Future Outlook and The "Talent War" (18:00 - 28:00)
- Discussion on the "weirdest time in history" where there is high capital but low visibility into talent.
- V argues the talent exists but is "invisible" (the 70% hidden slice).
- V defends the value of **qualitative data** (reasoning patterns, communication styles) as a proxy for AI capability and agency.
- V describes a "socialist view of data" where collective contributions to a data pot benefit everyone.

### 6. Conclusion and Next Steps (28:00 - End)
- Jatin and V agree to stay in touch and grab coffee in the New Year.
- V decides to circle back for the Pete introduction at the start of next year for better timing.
