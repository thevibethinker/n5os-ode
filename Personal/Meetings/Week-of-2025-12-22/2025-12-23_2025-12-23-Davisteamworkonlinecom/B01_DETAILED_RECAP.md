# B01: Detailed Recap — Teamwork Online X Careerspan Partnership

**Meeting Date:** 2025-12-23
**Participants:** Vrijen Attawar (V), Michelle Price (Business Ops, Teamwork Online), Davis Filippell (President, Teamwork Online)

## 1. Sports & Background Rapport (V & Michelle)
The meeting began with V and Michelle building rapport over sports. Michelle shared her background working for the New York Mets, Boston Celtics, and Washington Nationals. V mentioned his interest in Arsenal (Premier League) and their mutual connections to Emory and Cornell. Michelle explained her role as the "glue" overseeing business operations at Teamwork Online, ensuring tech implementation and customer messaging run smoothly.

## 2. Teamwork Online Product Context
Michelle provided a history of Teamwork Online, noting it has existed for ~25 years, with the digital ATS/sourcing component being around for ~15 years. They focus exclusively on sports and entertainment, a highly competitive field where candidate volume far exceeds job availability. They offer "MVP Access" (a premium tier similar to LinkedIn Premium) which has existed for over 10 years to help candidates stand out via advice, data insights, and dynamic profiles.

## 3. Careerspan Evolution & Pitch
V shared Careerspan's journey from a career coaching product to a hiring/matching modality. He highlighted the "Structured Self-Reflection" (Stories) feature, which uses AI to push candidates to answer deep questions, helping them "translate" their skills (especially for athletes, vets, and teachers) into a white-collar context. V emphasized that the signal of a candidate being willing to complete these stories is itself a powerful data point for employers.

## 4. Technical Demonstration
V demonstrated the Careerspan matching engine, which breaks roles and candidates down into component skills/responsibilities. He showed how the system assesses "Criticality" and "Level of Expertise" rather than just binary keyword matching. He noted the architecture is built on resilient, cost-effective models (GPT-4 mini) to ensure stability and low processing costs (~$0.20 - $0.30 per story at scale).

## 5. Partnership & Integration Exploration
The group discussed three primary integration points:
- **Profile Completion:** Using Careerspan's conversation-based resume generation to help Teamwork Online users build high-quality profiles from the start.
- **Assessment Layer:** Positioning "Stories" as a post-application assessment. Davis expressed interest in the richness of the data but raised questions about the cost structure and who pays (employer vs. candidate).
- **Data Monetization:** V proposed a model where employers pay for the data acquisition (assessment), while Careerspan/Teamwork Online retains the candidate signal for long-term pipeline cultivation.

## 6. Closing & Next Steps
Davis expressed that the tech is "slick" and he is impressed by the build. He requested screenshots of the employer view. The group agreed to let the ideas settle over the holidays, with V scheduled to follow up in the first two weeks of January 2026.
