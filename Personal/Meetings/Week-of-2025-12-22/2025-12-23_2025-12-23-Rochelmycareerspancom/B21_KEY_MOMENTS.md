# B21 Key Moments: Vrijen Attawar & Rochel Polter

## Turning Points & Conceptual Shifts

*   **The "Forest Gump" of the Tech Stack:** Vrijen describes Zo as the ubiquitous, background presence in his life that facilitates everything from biometric tracking to habit formation, marking a shift from manual apps to an integrated "computer in the cloud" workflow.
*   **Biometric Machine vs. Human Motivation:** A pivotal realization that for some, motivation comes from treating oneself as a "biometric machine," but the barrier has always been the "patience to do it." Zo removes this barrier by consolidating data automatically.
*   **Data-Driven Patience:** Vrijen shares a "crazy thing" he learned from scanning his Fitbit data: pushing too hard too fast (his usual habit) prevents the heart from conditioning properly, leading to plateaus. This insight shifted his approach to a more disciplined, slower "ramp up."
*   **The Spiritual Victory of Product:** Vrijen reflects on Career Span not just as a business, but as a "spiritual victory," noting that they built something people truly cared about and that changed how users see themselves.

## High-Leverage Quotes

*   **On Zo:** "Behind every behind is the Forest Gump of my tech stack... it's Zo."
*   **On Habit Formation:** "The hypothesis was: what if the thing that was missing is that it texts me and it is anthropomorphized... and it feels human that I feel compelled to respond."
*   **On Technical Growth:** "Just even the idea of, 'hey, set up a database' forces you to engage with the concept of a database... it certainly made me more technical."
*   **On the Recruiting Industry:** "Some days I honest to God I feel like Cassandra, where I’m like, nobody’s listening to me. If you would all just shut up and listen to me, this would all be so much easier... You don’t need video interviews. You don’t need more resumes scanned faster."
*   **On Excellence:** "I believe the target of anything in life should be to do it so well that it becomes an art." (Referencing Arsène Wenger)
*   **On Entrepreneurial Naivety:** "I was so naive to think that I could do any of this on any reasonable time scale... but if you only criticize the act and you don't study the origin... you miss the study of the backdrop."
*   **On Future Strategy:** "The more degrees of freedom you move, it’s like, laterally, the less far out you go... do I want to do another thing that will be a way higher likelihood of a winner, or do I follow the heart and do something crazy again?"
