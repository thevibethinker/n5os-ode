# B01: Detailed Recap - Christine Song (Ribbon) Partnership Sync

## Meeting Overview
Vrijen Attawar (V) met with Christine Song to discuss a potential partnership between Careerspan and Ribbon. Christine is based in Toronto and has an extensive background in recruitment and human resources. The conversation focused on the limitations of current ATS systems, the value of CareerSpan's "storytelling" approach to candidate validation, and potential synergy with Ribbon's video-centric platform.

## Chronological Summary

### 1. Introduction and Rapport Building (0:00 - 3:53)
*   **The Weather:** V and Christine discussed the cold weather in Canada and New York. V shared his experience moving from India to the US (college at 17), then Singapore (5 years), and eventually settling in New York.
*   **New York Life:** V confirmed he is settled in New York (married to a lifelong New Yorker). Christine expressed her preference for the East Coast (New York/Toronto) over the West Coast due to the distinct "work pace" and personality of the people.
*   **Background:** Christine has lived in Canada her whole life but frequently visits the US for tech work.

### 2. Careerspan Vision and Positioning (3:53 - 11:50)
*   **The Problem:** V outlined the "death of the ATS" and the insufficiency of the resume as a data structure (no authentication, low signal).
*   **The Solution:** Careerspan uses structured self-reflection sessions and AI-driven coaching to build a "mental model" of who a candidate is, rather than just indexing skills.
*   **Product Demo:** V walked Christine through the user portal (telling stories via voice/text) and the employer portal (Vibe Checks, skill-level mapping, and "Why Yes/Why No" insights).
*   **Key Distinction:** Careerspan scores candidates higher if they have "story evidence" to back up their resume claims.
*   **Business Model:** V described the transition from a coaching tool to an ATS alternative/sourcing engine. Partnerships already exist with groups like Superposition.ai and Marvin (McKinsey Alumni Fund).

### 3. Partnership Opportunities with Ribbon (11:50 - 16:02)
*   **The "Iron Triangle":** V proposed an integration where Careerspan's story data, Ribbon's video data, and interview data create an "iron triangle of integrity" for candidate validation.
*   **Ribbon Context:** Christine mentioned Ribbon is early-stage, New York-backed, and currently focused on video-based snapshots of candidates. She noted that people often don't have time to watch full videos, which is where Careerspan's text-based insights could add value.
*   **Introductions:** Christine offered to introduce V to the Ribbon team. V promised to send a demo account and a "blurb" for the intro.

### 4. 529 Society and Networking (16:02 - 27:00)
*   **529 Society:** Christine described her organization, 529 Society, as a confidential peer network for founders, executives, and senior operators (VP level+) to combat the "isolation" of leadership. They host curated private dinners (12-15 people).
*   **New York Expansion:** Christine is looking to expand 529 Society in New York.
*   **Intros from V:** V offered to introduce Christine to the founders of NYC Founders Club (Adam Alpert, Mike, Akash) and potentially Andrew Young (the "networking dude").

### 5. Conclusion and Next Steps (27:00 - 27:00)
*   **Action:** V will send the intro blurb and demo info via WhatsApp.
*   **Meeting Close:** The call ended with mutual appreciation and plans to stay connected.
