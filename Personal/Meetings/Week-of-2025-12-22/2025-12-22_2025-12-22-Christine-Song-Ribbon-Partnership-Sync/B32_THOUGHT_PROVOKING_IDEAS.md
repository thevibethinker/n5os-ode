# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

1.  **The Death of Inbound Signal:** As AI makes it trivial to generate "perfect" resumes and cover letters, the "inbound" signal for hiring collapses to zero. This necessitates moving the evaluation threshold to *asynchronous unscripted dialogue* (Careerspan's model) as the only remaining source of truth.
2.  **The "Iron Triangle" of Integrity:** The concept that integrity isn't checked by a single deep dive, but by **triangulating three distinct modalities** (Voice/Story, Video, and Real-time Interview). If a candidate can't maintain a consistent narrative across all three, the "mask" slips. This creates a deterministic proof of character.
3.  **The Mid-Layer as the New ATS:** The realization that the "ATS" shouldn't be a destination (a database of names) but a **persistent mid-layer** that lives between the candidate's career and the firm's needs. A system that stays "on" for five years until the signal matches the archetype is a fundamental shift from the "apply-now-or-never" transactional hiring model.
