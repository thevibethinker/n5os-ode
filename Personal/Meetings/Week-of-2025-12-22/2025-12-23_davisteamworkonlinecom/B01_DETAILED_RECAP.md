The meeting began with Vrijen (V) and Michelle Price chatting while waiting for Davis Filippell. Davis Filippell joined the call. V demonstrated the Careerspan candidate experience, focusing on:
*   **Structured Self-Reflection ("Stories"):** AI-driven questioning to help candidates articulate their experience, particularly useful for athletes, teachers, and veterans transitioning to white-collar roles.
*   **Matching Engine (Fit Score):** A granular skills-based assessment that breaks down roles and individuals into specific responsibilities and expertise levels.
*   **Model Architecture:** V mentioned they use "dumb" (cheap/efficient) models like GPT-4o-mini for most operations, with Claude used for resume parsing.

### 11:00 - 15:00 | Careerspan Demo (Employer Side) and Partnership Potential
V showed the employer view, highlighting the "Why you should say yes/no" insights generated from candidate stories. They discussed potential integration points:
*   Slotting Careerspan tech into the beginning of the Teamwork application flow.

Davis expressed interest in the "stories" feature but raised questions about the cost structure and who would pay (employer vs. candidate).

### 15:00 - 20:00 | Business Model and Next Steps
The conversation shifted to business models. V explained they are looking for partnerships rather than immediate fundraising. They discussed the cost of generating "stories" (currently ~$1.00, potentially dropping to $0.20-$0.30). Davis suggested syncing up again in the first or second week of the new year to explore overlap and potential collaboration. The meeting ended on a positive note with mutual holiday wishes.
