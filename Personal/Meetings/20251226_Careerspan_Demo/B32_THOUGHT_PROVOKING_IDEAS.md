# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

## 1. The "Problem Stack" Abstraction
V's thesis is that AI should not just automate existing tasks (like resume checking), but should move human experts "higher in the problem stack." This suggests a mental model where the value of a career coach is not in the "nuts and bolts" of bullet point editing, but in the higher-level strategy, goals, and objectives of the candidate. This raises the question: what happens to the "entry-level" expertise if the foundational tasks are entirely handled by AI?

## 2. Semantic vs. Syntactic Identity
Michael Maher's concern about "different resumes for the same job" highlights a tension between *syntactic identity* (the exact words on a page) and *semantic identity* (the underlying experience). V's model argues that a person is a collection of "meaningful stories" that can be truthfully re-rendered into different forms without losing internal consistency. This challenges the traditional notion of a "canonical resume" as a static document.

## 3. Latency in Trust Networks
V noted the "fall in latency" when an alum reaches out to a school and has to wait days or weeks for candidate names. By using AI to maintain a constant "readiness state" for every student (via the 2-story minimum), the "trust network" (Alums → School → Students) becomes real-time. This transforms the school's role from a *manual broker* of talent to a *platform orchestrator* of talent.
