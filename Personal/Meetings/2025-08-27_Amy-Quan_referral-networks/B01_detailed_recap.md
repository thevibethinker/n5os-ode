# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
13-minute video call between Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan) and Amy Quan to finalize logistics for a co-hosted founder hiring event planned for September 30. The conversation focused on venue options, budget planning, commitment timelines, and the division of responsibilities between partners.

## Key Discussion Points

### Opening Context
Amy has significant upcoming activity including a two-day go-to-market event, breakfast meetings, and a dinner with Rank. She's running tactical gathering sessions. Vrijen is actively pushing into talent and founder communities and has secured potential JP Morgan sponsorship/venue support.

### JP Morgan Sponsorship Opportunity
Vrijen reported that JP Morgan is willing to either:
- Co-host the event
- Sponsor it (e.g., cover bar tab for first $X hundred)
- Provide office space as venue

**Key constraint:** JP Morgan requires some founders they'd like to bring into their business to attend, which Vrijen framed as an advantage since it guarantees "relatively high quality founders" from the sponsor side. They can organize 30-40 person events "quite easily" while larger events require more lead time.

### Event Format Discussion
Vrijen proposed moving away from the matchmaking format (being tested with Shivam separately) toward a "thought leadership" or "helping founders hire better" oriented event. He suggested leveraging expert speakers on hiring and is willing to pull from his network or source New York area experts.

Vrijen also introduced the concept of a "knowledge exchange session" - doing legwork upfront to identify topics founders want to discuss or give advice on, creating "small intimate atmosphere."

### Amy's Requirements for Commitment
Amy needs confirmation on:
1. **Date locked:** September 30
2. **Budget commitment:** What Vrijen/Careerspan can allocate
3. **Format finalized:** What was discussed

**Amy's constraint:** She's planning entire September/October and needs confirmed details to begin founder curation. Currently everything feels "in the air" and she can't effectively recruit high-quality founders without commitment.

### Budget Discussion
Vrijen asked for budget recommendations and main expense areas.

Amy referenced a previous 100-person panel event with Morrison Foerster that cost ~$8K but included AirPods giveaways. She emphasized that if JP Morgan covers venue and potentially food/beverage, costs drop significantly. Main remaining expenses would be:
- Operations and event coordination
- Promotion/marketing
- Founder curation and outreach

Amy is also partnering with wellness-focused beverage brands (tested at yesterday's breakfast) to provide healthy options, showing "personal touch" and care for attendee health. This provides backup beverage coverage if needed.

### Venue Alternatives
Amy offered **General Assembly** as backup venue option if JP Morgan falls through:
- Two space types available: Classroom-style rooms (used for workshops) and larger event spaces (for networking/rotating format)
- Established partnership with General Assembly making it "fairly easy to get"
- Amy has used them for multiple events

### Decision Timeline
Vrijen committed to:
1. Discussing with Logan (co-founder) immediately after the call
2. Confirming the date same day
3. Finalizing budget estimate ($1.5-2K range mentioned)
4. Determining optimal timing relative to major company initiatives

He acknowledged the need to time the event with a major push since they're investing budget and using a key partnership card (JP Morgan).

### Amy's Value Proposition
Amy emphasized her ability to curate quality founders over simply filling seats. She noted the importance of knowing "what kind of founders are coming" rather than just hitting headcount targets. This curation capability and her access to founder networks is her primary contribution to the partnership.

## Outcome
Vrijen committed to providing Amy with answers by end of day, prioritizing at minimum the date confirmation so she can begin founder outreach. Amy expressed excitement about moving forward pending these confirmations.

## Meeting Tone
Collaborative and professional with clear mutual respect. Some minor connection issues early in call. Both parties demonstrated understanding of each other's constraints and contributions. The negotiation was constructive with both sides trying to reduce friction and find workable solutions.
