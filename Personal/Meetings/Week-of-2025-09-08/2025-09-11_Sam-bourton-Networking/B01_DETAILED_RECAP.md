# B01 Detailed Recap

Vrijen Attawar (V) met with Sam Bourton (Sam), a former McKinsey/QuantumBlack (QB) leader with eight years of experience at the firm, to discuss Careerspan and potential networking/synergies. The conversation was initiated following a recommendation from Ulrich, whom both parties respect.

### McKinsey & QB Context
Both V and Sam shared their experiences at McKinsey. V described his tenure (starting in 2022) as a "lull" period where he was frequently unstaffed, which partially motivated his transition into startups. Sam reflected on his eight-year journey, noting how the firm "took off the brakes" post-COVID, leading to undisciplined growth and a subsequent drop in morale when there wasn't enough work to support the massive hiring in digital/QB. They bonded over the concept of "flow" and the necessity of having just enough challenge to foster growth.

### Careerspan Thesis
V detailed the evolution of Careerspan:
- **Pedigree:** Originally boutique career coaching/admissions in Singapore.
- **Pain Point:** The "informational problem" in hiring. Candidates struggle to articulate their value (the "translation gap" from brain to resume).
- **Solution:** Moving beyond superficial resume/interview tools to a qualitative, 3D repository of a candidate's achievements.
- **Differentiator:** Unlike competitors (e.g., Jack and Jill, Dex), Careerspan provides an intrinsic, long-term reason for candidates to engage (strategic career construction) rather than a transactional "get a job and leave" model.

### AI in Hiring
Sam provided insights into the London startup scene (mentioning "Jack and Jill") and the high user acceptance of AI agents for career conversations, especially among Gen Z. He suggested that Careerspan could act as a vital tool for navigating job displacement by leveraging qualitative data and expert market knowledge to guide upskilling.

### Shared Interests
The meeting included a lighthearted exchange regarding their rival football loyalties (V is an Arsenal fan; Sam is a Spurs fan). Sam joked about the "thankless and unrewarding" experience of supporting Spurs.
