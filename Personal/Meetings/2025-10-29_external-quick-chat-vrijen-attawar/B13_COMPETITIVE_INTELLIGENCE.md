## Positioning Gaps & White Space

### Dylan's Identified White Space:
**"Hackathons for business/creative students"**
- CS students have hackathons every weekend (MLH, local university events, etc.)
- Marketing students have...case competitions? Agency projects? But no structured challenge infrastructure
- Financial modeling students have...nothing at scale?
- Strategy/operations students have...nothing at scale?

**Why this might be a real gap:**
1. Business schools are huge (outnumber CS programs at most universities)
2. Portfolio/project work matters just as much for business students as CS students
3. Existing solutions (case competitions) may be too formal, too slow, or too limited in scope

**Why this might NOT be a real gap:**
1. Maybe business students don't actually care about portfolio projects (need validation)
2. Maybe companies don't value challenge submissions from business students (they want degrees/internships)
3. Maybe case competitions already fill this need adequately

## Competitive Advantages (Dylan's View)

**Flexibility on prize range (sh-0K):**
- Allows bootstrapped startups to participate without financial barrier
- Creates network effects: more companies = more challenges = more students

**Work-product focus:**
- Companies get actual deliverables (marketing campaigns, financial models, strategy decks)
- Students get portfolio pieces
- Differentiated from resume-screening platforms (Handshake, LinkedIn)

**Creative/marketing angle:**
- Work is inherently shareable (vs code which is abstract)
- Viral potential through student social media
- Micro-influencer seeding strategy possible

**Timing (market conditions):**
- Tech layoffs making job market more competitive
- AI resume spam making traditional hiring worse
- Students need differentiation more than ever

## Competitive Vulnerabilities (Vrijen's Implied Concerns)

**Two-sided marketplace cold-start:**
- Need both companies and students; chicken-and-egg problem
- Established players (MLH, Handshake) already have liquidity on both sides

**Evaluation burden for companies:**
- With 100 submissions, reviewing is time-consuming
- Companies might not have bandwidth to evaluate creative work at scale
- Could lead to companies abandoning platform if too much friction

**Quality control:**
- How do you prevent low-quality submissions from flooding platform?
- How do you ensure companies get value?

**Student motivation:**
- Will students actually invest time if they don't win?
- Prize money vs portfolio value vs job opportunity - which matters most?
