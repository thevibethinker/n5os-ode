## DC Event Debrief
Caleb shared results from his recent DC pitch event, describing it as their most successful to date with unprecedented capital in the room—4 billionaires attending, including Paul Allen (founder of Ancestry.com, sold for $4.2B). The event attracted the highest net worth audience they've hosted, with 100 attendees selected from a 380-person waitlist. Caleb emphasized his selective curation process, deliberately excluding people who wouldn't "move the needle" to protect founders' time.

Two companies are expected to secure immediate funding based on their performance:

**Move AI** - Most popular pitch. Positions as moving service but has much broader application. Users scan their house with photos, system inventories everything including weight/dimensions, arranges packing and shipping. But the real value: sends homeowners reports on energy-saving opportunities (e.g., replacing refrigerator could save $42/month), identifies maintenance issues and watermarks, can automatically schedule repair services. System accounts for clutter based on years lived in residence (32 years = high clutter baseline vs 5 years). Also sends rental companies proactive repair notifications before tenant move-out to secure deposit returns. Booz Allen's military investment arm immediately expressed interest in securing contract the next day—said it's a "no brainer" for tracking military moves and inventory.

**Hill Genius** - Legal tech AI for law firms. Tracks rapidly changing laws and regulations, helps firms stay ahead of legal evolution. Functions like "a typewriter for a judge that anticipates the future"—documents current proceedings while predicting likely legal direction over next 6 months. Can scan firm's entire document corpus, identify specific practice area (e.g., life science medical device), then connect to similar portfolios in lawmaking system to auto-educate itself on relevant precedents and terminology. Helps firms write better documentation and better protect clients under current administration.

Caleb noted these two succeeded because they have **obvious, immediate value propositions**—save time, save money, reduce risk. Contrasted with a postpartum depression AI app that was "compelling" but struggled with the fundamental question of adoption rates. Caleb shared his view that not everyone will seek help through an app when struggling, using suicide awareness as example—he personally wouldn't use such services, and founders can't assume their own behavior generalizes. Those companies need to prove the concept through actual customer traction.

Event also produced high-quality photography and videography that Caleb wanted to share with Vrijen.
