# B01 Detailed Recap

The meeting focused on internal variable pricing dynamics for Careerspan's products, specifically addressing the "Pricing Triangle" constraint: Unlimited Job Descriptions, Unlimited Seats, and Annual Duration. The speaker (Ilya) emphasized that at least one of these three variables must be capped to prevent operational nightmares where high-volume HR teams could drive variable costs beyond sustainable levels.

Key discussion points included:
- **The Pricing Triangle**: A conceptual framework where one cannot offer unlimited job descriptions, unlimited seats, and a full-year term simultaneously without risking runaway internal costs.
- **Risk Mitigation**: The need to put "caps" on usage to avoid scenarios where a 20-person HR team generates 10,000 job descriptions, causing internal variable costs to spike.
- **Sales Strategy**: Utilizing the triangle as a mental model during sales discussions to identify what clients prioritize (e.g., growth in volume vs. seat count) and "boxing" them into annual agreements or shorter contracts to manage exposure.
- **Package Weaving**: Playing with numbers to create packages that navigate potential overages by adjusting the three main levers.
