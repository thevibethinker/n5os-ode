# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

## The "Pricing Triangle" as a Sales Weapon
The idea that a salesperson can let a customer "walk themselves" into a constraint is a powerful framing. It shifts the dynamic from the company imposing limits to the company helping the customer choose the most efficient trade-off for their specific growth stage.

## Internal Variable Cost Visibility
The "nightmare scenario" of 10,000 job descriptions highlights a critical need for real-time cost monitoring. If the business is "not as expensive" as thought, does this imply a high margin that can withstand some volume, or just a lack of previous cost-attribution?

## Boxing in Time
Ilya mentions that "ultimately time is boxed into the year." In SaaS, time is the ultimate buffer. Even with "unlimited" volume, an annual limit forces a renegotiation point, effectively acting as a macro-cap on risk.
