# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

### The "Single Candidate" Ideal
Is it possible to build a system so predictive that "applying" becomes a formality for the one person already identified as the hire? V's scenario suggests that in a perfect world, the ratio of applicants to hires is 1:1, and any deviation from that is "waste."

### "Dense" vs "Cheap" Analysis
How do we balance the "dense analysis" (30-point alignment) with user experience? If we gate the dense analysis behind a Vibe Check, are we missing high-potential candidates who don't "vibe" but have the "dense" skills?

### AI Cost as a Friction Agent
Usually, we want to remove friction from applications. Here, the internal cost of processing applications creates a business incentive to *add* intentional friction (the Vibe Check) to ensure only high-signal candidates proceed. This flips the traditional "growth at all costs" applicant volume model on its head.
