# B01: Detailed Recap

The meeting focused on the internal costing structure of the Careerspan application process and the strategic value of the "Vibe Check" in optimizing those costs. 

### Internal Costing Mechanics
Logan explained the "dense analysis" costs that occur when a candidate applies to a specific job. 
- **Initial Costs:** Analyzing a resume and stories (initial tell-all) are baseline costs incurred when the candidate joins the platform.
- **Application-Specific Costs:** When a candidate applies to a specific job, an additional "finite analysis" ($0.30 to $0.40 baseline) is triggered. This cost is driven by the system aligning the candidate's resume and all told stories against approximately 30 different specific items/requirements of the job description to identify specific "gaps."

### Strategic Optimization (The "Greedy" Scenario)
V introduced a thought experiment regarding the "most shrewdly capitalistic" scenario for the business:
- **Ideal State:** One high-quality candidate applies to one job and gets hired, avoiding the cost of processing 99 other unsuitable candidates.
- **The Role of the Vibe Check:** The "Vibe Check" serves as a low-cost filtering mechanism. By requiring candidates to pass a certain threshold on the vibe check before triggering the expensive "dense analysis," Careerspan can significantly reduce wasted internal spend on misaligned applicants.
