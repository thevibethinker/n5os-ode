# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

### 1. The Resume as a Failed Data Stream
Vrijen posits that resumes are a fundamentally "crap-heavy" data stream (99% noise). Most AI attempts to fix hiring fail because they try to "extract gold" from this low-quality source. The provocative shift is that you cannot fix hiring through better extraction; you must fix it through **high-friction data collection**. Friction is not a bug; it is the only way to generate a high-signal data stream.

### 2. "Badges of Legitimacy" vs. Utility
The Investor admits that tools like Getro may have de minimis utility but serve as "badges of legitimacy" for LPs and founders. This highlights a "signaling tax" in VC—where funds pay for tools not for their function, but to look like a "real" fund. The strategic opportunity is identifying where these signals have become so standardized that they actually signal mediocrity rather than excellence.

### 3. Market Darwinism (Recessions as Forest Fires)
The idea that economic downturns are biological necessities ("Forest fires, plagues") to clear the "deck of all of the crappy players." This suggests that the current "vicious" market isn't just an obstacle but a cleansing mechanism that restores the "feedback loop" that was broken in 2021.
