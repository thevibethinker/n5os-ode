# Key Moments & Quotes

1. **Operator → fund manager narrative**  
   - Jonathan’s story of spinning out Zoombini, doing successful SPVs, and then parlaying that into a ~$20M fund is a clean, compelling arc: *"Ended up having some wins… enough of a story to go back to the same people and say, instead of 30–50k a deal, let’s just do a fund."*

2. **Reality check on emerging manager life**  
   - He articulates the core anxiety of his role: even without disasters, if portfolio companies don’t quickly show clear breakout trajectories, it is hard to prove repeatability to himself, his team, and LPs.

3. **Fund math and founder outcomes**  
   - The QED example crystallizes how large funds think: *"We don’t do a deal unless we think our return on the deal can be 500 million or more."*  
   - This leads directly into the cautionary point that raising large, high‑priced rounds can leave founders working for years under a preference stack that still yields **zero** in many exit scenarios.

4. **The ATS / résumé critique**  
   - Your metaphor of a mineral extraction process where **99% of the ore is worthless** but still has to be processed lands clearly: there is no world where AI on top of résumés alone can be both accurate and economically rational.  
   - This frames Careerspan not as "AI ATS" but as a **different data‑collection layer**.

5. **Jonathan’s inbound experience from LinkedIn**  
   - He notes that both of his current team members came from LinkedIn inbound, but only after **~1,500 applications per role**, underscoring how brutally noisy the current funnel is.

6. **Careerspan’s honest strategic positioning**  
   - You openly acknowledge that monetizing job‑seekers is painful and that a highly probable outcome is being acquired by a larger player that needs your tech.  
   - This honesty about exit paths reinforces trust; you are not overselling a billion‑dollar inevitability.

7. **Intro and connector offers**  
   - Jonathan’s offers – to look at your deck when you’re raising and to introduce you to Faison – are the concrete bridge from philosophical alignment to future opportunities.

8. **Closing on mutual goodwill**  
   - The call ends with both of you affirming the relationship and leaving the door open: you promise to send materials at the right time; he reiterates his willingness to help with intros and says he’s happy for you and what you’re building.
