# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

## 1. LinkedIn as a "Blown Product Lead" Case Study
Vrijen posits that LinkedIn will eventually become the canonical example of "blowing a product lead," comparing it potentially to the decline of Skype. The core idea is that in its aggressive pursuit of becoming a "social media network," LinkedIn has actively degraded its primary utility as a professional inbox and utility tool. This suggests a strategic "weirdness" where a platform's growth as a social network is inversely proportional to its survival as a functional professional infrastructure.

## 2. The "Narrative Design" Pivot
Careerspan’s shift from 1:1 career coaching to an AI-driven matching layer is framed not just as a business pivot, but as the scaling of "narrative design." This mental model treats professional history not as a static record (the Resume Industrial Complex), but as a fluid data stream that can be structured agnostically to bypass traditional hiring biases. The "provocation" here is that high-fidelity candidate data is best collected through "the pill of self-reflection wrapped around the dessert of coaching," suggesting that the best hiring data is a byproduct of candidate growth, not candidate evaluation.

## 3. Humans as "Bundles of Trade-offs," Not Ranks
The explicit rejection of "stack ranking" in favor of viewing candidates as "bundles of skills and trade-offs" challenges the foundational logic of most ATS platforms. Vrijen argues that people do not exist in ranks, but as sets of trade-offs. This mental model shifts the recruiter's job from "finding the best person" to "understanding which specific trade-offs the organization is willing to make," moving hiring from a search for optimization to a search for compatibility.
