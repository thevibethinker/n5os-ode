# Detailed Recap

1. **Introductions and context**  
   - You meet **Ethan**, who works with **Oscar** at Marvin Ventures while finishing school in Utah.  
   - Ethan explains that he found Oscar through an alumni connection, has been interning with Marvin, and now helps manage their founder resources.

2. **Marvin Ventures resources / Startup Stack overview**  
   - Ethan walks you through Marvin’s **"Startup Stack"** resources: a curated set of tools and perks ("offers") that portfolio founders and community members can redeem.  
   - The stack is hosted on Marvin’s site and drives *low‑volume but high‑intent* traffic; in the last month there were roughly ten redemptions across offers.  
   - Each offer consists of a company card (logo + short description), a brief offer summary, and a more detailed landing page description that users see after entering their email.

3. **Positioning Careerspan within the stack**  
   - You explain that **Careerspan** started as a coaching product but is now focused on **employer‑side screening and matching**: helping founders cut down noisy inbound and see who is genuinely worth talking to.  
   - Ethan confirms that this fits well into Marvin’s **hiring / HR** and **coaching / mentorship** categories and tags Careerspan accordingly (HR support, recruiting, staffing services, coaching, learning / continuing education, sales hiring, etc.).

4. **Defining the concrete offer**  
   - Together you define the core perk as: **unlimited candidate filtering and matching for one role for three months**, roughly a $900 value.  
   - The perk is free for Marvin founders ("freemium" incentive type), with **broad eligibility** – any startup founder or team using Marvin’s resources can apply.  
   - Lifecycle targeting is set to companies that are at least at **product stage and beyond** (post‑idea).

5. **Copy and messaging work**  
   - Ethan shows you how the resource interface separates:
     - **Company description** (what Careerspan is).  
     - **Short offer description** (what the perk is).  
     - **Landing‑page description** (longer explanation sent after email capture).  
   - You draft and refine copy for each slot, with the short offer framed as turning **inbound from a firehose into a filter** and the long description emphasizing that Careerspan only surfaces the 2–5% of candidates a founder should spend time on.

6. **Redemption flow via Fillout form**  
   - You spin up a quick **Fillout** form titled something like "Marvin Perk Redemption" to capture: founder name, email, company, number of roles, and consent to be contacted by Careerspan.  
   - Ethan configures the offer so that the "Get Offer" button sends users to this form URL; you plan to wire a webhook from Fillout into your systems separately.

7. **Field mapping sanity‑check**  
   - To avoid confusion about which description appears where, you and Ethan cross‑reference another offer (Atio) live on the site.  
   - You confirm that:
     - The **company description** is what shows on the main card.  
     - The **offer description** appears when founders click into an offer.  
     - The **landing description** is what gets emailed / shown post‑email capture.  
   - You adjust Careerspan’s copy accordingly so that each layer reads cleanly in context.

8. **Review and next steps**  
   - Ethan submits the Careerspan offer for Startup Stack review; timing is variable (from ~30 minutes to a couple of days).  
   - He commits to keep you updated when the offer is approved and live.  
   - You thank him for the partnership and highlight how this gives Marvin founders a low‑friction way to trial Careerspan for one role.

9. **Career coaching advice for Ethan**  
   - Before closing, you switch hats briefly into coach mode and encourage Ethan to **actively record his own wins and stories**, not just those of founders he supports.  
   - You explain that building a catalog of concrete stories (like this project) will materially help his future career narrative—whether or not he uses Careerspan to do it.
