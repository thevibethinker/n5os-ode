# B01 Detailed Recap: Careerspan x Marvin Partnership

### 1. Introduction & Background
- **Vrijen (V)** and **Ethan** connect via video call. Ethan is based in Utah and works for Marvin (a VC firm), having connected with Oscar (McKenzie alum) through school networking.
- Ethan describes Marvin's "Startup Stack"—a resource guide and website section where they promote deals, perks, and incentives from partner companies to their network of founders.

### 2. Careerspan Product Overview
- **V** explains Careerspan's evolution from a coaching product (helping job seekers with structured self-reflection) to an employer-facing hiring platform.
- **The core value proposition:** Candidates who "tell stories" through the platform stand out significantly more than traditional resumes. It allows employers to filter for the top 2-5% of talent based on deeper insights.

### 3. The Offer Definition
- Vrijen proposes a perk for Marvin's portfolio companies: **one role "on the house" for three months**, valued at approximately $900 ($300/month).
- This includes access to the Careerspan ATS/screening agent for that specific role.

### 4. Direct Implementation on Marvin's Platform
- Ethan shares his screen while filling out the offer form on Marvin’s backend.
- They categorize Careerspan under **HR, Hiring, Recruiting, Staffing, Sales Hiring, Coaching & Mentorship, and Learning & Continuing Education**.
- Ethan runs descriptions through his own AI to generate options, while Vrijen provides a punchy "short description": *"Turn inbound from a fire hose into a filter."*

### 5. Technical Workflow for Redemption
- Vrijen builds a **Fillout.com** form in real-time during the call to capture lead data from Marvin founders.
- Ethan plans to use this link as the redirect URL for the perk redemption, ensuring a clean handoff between systems.
- The call concludes with V and Ethan syncing on where the company description vs. offer description appears on the Marvin site by cross-referencing existing partner pages (like Atio).
