# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

## 1. The "Effort Signal" as the New Filtering Standard
Vrijen mentions that AI has destroyed the traditional "effort signal" (e.g., a well-crafted cover letter). Careerspan reintroduces this by tracking "Stories"—how much effort is a candidate willing to put into self-reflection?
- *Provocation:* In an era of mass AI-generated applications, is "effort-based proof" more valuable than "skill-based proof"?

## 2. Professional "Data-Poorness" vs. Athletic "Data-Richness"
The central thesis: Professional quality is hidden because it relies on weak self-articulation.
- *Provocation:* If we solve the articulation gap, does the concept of a "resume" become obsolete entirely, replaced by a "living achievement ledger"?

## 3. The Talent War of Qualification
Vrijen suggests that as job retention drops, fewer people will be "qualified" for high-level roles because they lack tenure/depth.
- *Provocation:* Will "qualification" become a scarcer commodity than "talent"? How does a system like Careerspan manufacture "qualification" through better achievement mapping?

## 4. Pedagogy over Forms
The critique of competitors Dex and Jack & Jill is that they are "forms" not "coaches."
- *Provocation:* Does the *method* of data collection (coaching/pedagogy) materially change the *quality* of the data collected, or is the AI just better at cleaning up bad inputs?
