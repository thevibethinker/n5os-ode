# B06 – Business Context

## Company snapshot: Careerspan (as of this meeting)

- **Stage:** Early-stage startup; product live with real employers and candidates.
- **Model / mechanism:**
  - Candidates record **structured stories** about their experiences instead of (or in addition to) traditional resumes.
  - Employers receive a much smaller, highly curated pool of candidates whose stories and traits align with what they *actually* need.
- **Market:**
  - Broadly in the recruiting / hiring space, which is crowded with AI résumé-screeners and “ATS optimizers.”
  - Careerspan is differentiated by **depth of signal** (narrative, agency, qualities) rather than volume of applicants.

## Current traction indicators

- **Jobs live:**
  - V reports **almost a dozen roles** posted on Careerspan, a mix of paid and free postings.
  - Another employer has just agreed to post a job, indicating growing willingness to pay.

- **Candidate behavior:**
  - Early funnel data from PostHog shows candidates are **starting and completing stories** immediately upon landing:
    - Example: candidate “Kevin” comes in via the job application flow and records multiple stories right away.
  - V estimates that **~15–20%** of candidates opt into story creation—high given how passive and low-effort most online job applications have become.

## Customer pain / problem framing

- **For founders / hiring managers:**
  - Traditional process involves:
    - Writing a JD.
    - Posting across channels.
    - Sifting through hundreds of low-signal resumes.
    - Many hours of screening calls before reaching a few serious contenders.
  - Alex’s anecdote: hiring designers consumed ~100 hours of his time, much of it wasted on clearly unqualified candidates.
  - Core pain: **screening is the main time and energy sink**, not final interviews.

- **For candidates:**
  - Existing systems reduce them to **keywords and bullet points**, feeding opaque, Palantir-like data systems.
  - There is no good way to show nuance: agency, curiosity, resilience, personality, or nontraditional experience.

## Careerspan’s intended position

- **Candidates:**
  - “Show what you’ve really got” / “show your whole self.”
  - Use stories to demonstrate how they think, act, and solve problems—especially attractive for candidates whose traditional credentials understate their capabilities.

- **Employers:**
  - “Ask for what you really want.”
  - Capture **qualities, not just keywords** (energy, agency, communication style, comfort with ambiguity, etc.).
  - Shorten the hiring cycle by spending time only on a high-signal shortlist.

## Strategic implications from this meeting

- Early behavioral data suggests the **story-based application thesis is valid** enough to double down on.
- To stand out in a saturated space, Careerspan must foreground:
  - Its **philosophy** (agency, authenticity, fairness).
  - Its **mechanism** (stories as rich signal).
  - Its **business value** (massive reduction in wasted screening time).
- Upcoming work on the website, visuals (e.g., Sankey diagram), and one-pagers should be framed around these realities.
