# B03 – Stakeholder Intelligence

## Participant: Vrijen Attawar

- **Role / Position**
  - Founder of **Careerspan**, early-stage company reinventing hiring around narrative “stories” rather than only resumes.
  - Primary owner of product vision, go-to-market, and founder-brand.

- **Context in this meeting**
  - Bringing early quantitative signal that candidates *are* recording stories to apply for jobs.
  - Actively working through founder anxieties: “what if no one ever tells stories?” and how to turn early data into conviction.
  - Seeking guidance on how to translate his strengths and worldview into a scalable sales and marketing motion.

- **Motivations and goals**
  - Prove that story-based applications are behaviorally viable at scale.
  - Reduce founder time wasted on low-signal candidate screening and make hiring more humane and precise.
  - Clarify Careerspan’s core narrative so it cuts through “yet another AI recruiter” noise.
  - Grow as a founder who can sell confidently **without** losing authenticity or empathy.

- **Strengths observed**
  - Deep, wide-ranging curiosity; can find something interesting in almost any problem domain.
  - High social range and comfort with diverse people (from bouncers to CEOs), which lowers friction in new conversations.
  - Strong analytical and philosophical framing (agency, excellence, fairness in labor markets).
  - Willingness to be vulnerable about fears, mistakes, and insecurities; takes feedback seriously.

- **Risks / friction points**
  - Can get stuck in his own head in sales conversations, over-optimizing for politeness and not asking directly for next steps.
  - Tends to over-index on empathy and courtesy, sometimes at the expense of clear asks and strong positioning.
  - Overwhelmed backlog of follow-ups and ideas; risk of dilution if not prioritized.

- **Leverage points**
  - Lean heavily on his authenticity and curiosity as *differentiators* versus generic HR-tech founders.
  - Turn his nuanced thinking (agency, “show what you’ve really got,” “ask for what you really want”) into crisp, repeatable messaging.
  - Use data (PostHog events, conversion rates) as emotional proof points for himself and a compelling story for buyers.

## Participant: Alex Caveny

- **Role / Position**
  - Founder coach and advisor (Wisdom Partners context), former **CPO** and product leader with direct startup experience and an exit.

- **Context in this meeting**
  - Serving as executive coach / thought partner focused on go-to-market, sales craft, and personal positioning.
  - Recently co-hosted a sales workshop and is bringing fresh, structured sales frameworks into the conversation.

- **Motivations and goals**
  - Help V turn intrinsic strengths into concrete GTM, sales, and brand moves.
  - Keep the coaching relationship grounded in real progress, not abstract pep talks.
  - Work with founders he finds intellectually engaging and values-aligned; not interested in forcing fit.

- **Strengths observed**
  - Calm, low-ego presence that makes it easy for V to be candid.
  - Sharp pattern recognition across sales and founder behavior (e.g., “sell the hole, not the shovel,” lock in meetings live, authenticity as brand asset).
  - Can translate dense sales wisdom into practical, founder-specific tactics.
  - Strong instinct for messaging and positioning, especially for two-sided products.

- **Risks / constraints**
  - Limited time bandwidth as a coach; relies on founders to implement between sessions.
  - Success depends on V converting insights into concrete experiments (website changes, outbound patterns, event strategy).

- **Leverage points**
  - Provide V with reusable artifacts (sales deck, one-pager patterns) that V and the team can adapt.
  - Continue reflecting back V’s strengths so he *sees* and operationalizes them.
  - Pressure-test V’s copy, website, and outbound scripts to ensure they match the authenticity that comes through in live conversation.
