# B06 – Business Context

## Macrolandscape

- **Labor market:** 2024–2025 is described as the worst period for layoffs since 2008, and possibly worse in raw numbers. Many qualified tech workers are competing for a limited number of roles, especially in product, devrel, and community.
- **Hiring stack:** Widely used screening tools and auto‑apply behaviors create a flood of low‑signal applications. Differentiated effort (e.g., carefully crafted resumes, thoughtful stories) is rarely recognized by automated filters, making the process feel nihilistic for candidates and noisy for employers.
- **Candidate behavior:** Jobseekers invest in coaches, resume services, and generic AI tools, but still end up sending large volumes of applications with low response rates. Emotional exhaustion and a sense of futility are common themes.

## Erica / Community Context

- **Profile:** Senior devrel / program manager with deep community‑building experience (11+ years in devrel, 17 years at Google, plus startup experience). Currently in New York and embedded in local tech networks.
- **Recent history:** Personally experienced two layoffs (Google and a startup), which increased her empathy for jobseekers and sharpened her interest in building supportive infrastructure.
- **Community initiative:** Founded "Coffee & Career Transitions NYC" as a recurring, in‑person gathering for laid‑off and transitioning professionals. Emphasis is on emotional support, sharing job‑search tips, and normalizing the experience of layoffs (many attendees feel shame or reluctance to post about layoffs publicly).
- **Audience:** Primarily laid‑off tech workers and career‑changers, with some hiring managers and people interested in networking. Attendance has grown meaningfully (e.g., mid‑week 9am events with ~37 attendees and waitlists of 90+ for earlier sessions).
- **Near‑term trajectory:** Plans to continue events roughly monthly, with a likely pause around February for maternity leave and resumption afterwards.

## Careerspan Business Context

- **Company stage:** Early‑stage startup with ~3–4k users acquired over roughly a year via word of mouth and a few podcast pilots; no paid marketing to date.
- **Core product:** Structured self‑reflection system that:
  - Prompts candidates to record detailed stories about their experiences.
  - Decomposes job descriptions into responsibilities and skills.
  - Scores candidates on direct and transferable evidence for each dimension.
  - Generates high‑quality resume bullets, narrative positioning statements, and strengths/concerns analysis.
  - Can rewrite resumes to semantically align with specific roles.
- **Initial thesis:** Started as a career‑coaching and resume‑generation tool focused on helping people articulate their stories, not just format documents.
- **Current strategy:**
  - Keep the candidate experience free; "jobseekers are terrible customers" and charging them feels misaligned with the mission.
  - Charge employers for access to a smaller pool of well‑understood, story‑rich candidates and higher‑signal matching.
  - Position Careerspan as a place where employers can see applicants as three‑dimensional people, not just keywords.
- **Early ecosystem moves:**
  - Piloting with communities (e.g., FOHI, Marvin Ventures’ portfolio) by hosting their jobs on Careerspan and matching against the existing candidate pipeline.
  - Building community‑centric workflows so organizers like Erica can: host jobs on Careerspan, distribute a single application link, and give members reusable stories instead of repetitive form‑filling.

## Strategic Fit of This Meeting

- **Why Erica matters to Careerspan:** She combines three valuable roles—senior candidate, devrel domain expert, and community operator—with lived experience of layoffs. Her feedback can shape the product for similar profiles, and her community could become a high‑quality cluster of users and employer matches.
- **Why Careerspan matters to Erica/community:**
  - Offers a more humane, narrative‑driven alternative to the current resume + ATS grind.
  - Gives her members tools for deep self‑reflection, better storytelling, and more targeted applications.
  - Provides protective utilities like the Bullshit Detector to help candidates avoid exploitative or misaligned roles.

The business opportunity sits at the intersection of **community‑led support for jobseekers** and **higher‑signal, story‑rich matching for employers**, with Erica’s group as a potential flagship community example if the product proves compelling for her personally.
