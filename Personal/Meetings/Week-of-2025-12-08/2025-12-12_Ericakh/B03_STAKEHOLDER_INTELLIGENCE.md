# B03 – Stakeholder Intelligence

## 1. Erica (ericakhgmailcom)

**Role & background**
- Program manager / developer relations professional with ~11 years in devrel and 17 years at Google prior to layoffs.
- Recently laid off twice (Google, then a startup) and now job‑seeking while pregnant (due in February).
- Organizer of "Coffee & Career Transitions NYC" and prior co‑founder of a women‑in‑tech community in Singapore; central node in a network of developers and devrel practitioners.

**Current situation**
- In active search for senior PM/devrel roles; recently completed a fifth‑round interview at Amazon for a community‑adjacent role.
- Has invested in coaching (Kadima Careers) and interview prep; feels prepared and realistic but uncertain about outcome, especially given pregnancy disclosure dynamics.
- Balancing career decisions with impending maternity leave; open to taking a year off if the right role does not land.

**Motivations & goals**
- Primary: stable, meaningful work in developer‑facing roles where she can continue building and supporting technical communities.
- Strong mission orientation toward helping laid‑off and transitioning professionals feel supported, de‑stigmatized, and better equipped.
- Interested in tools that genuinely reduce wasted effort in job search (vs yet another resume builder) and that respect the emotional load of layoffs.
- Curious about ways to sustainably support and possibly monetize community work without compromising trust.

**Constraints & sensitivities**
- Time and energy are constrained by late‑stage pregnancy and recurring community events.
- Cautious about how new employers will react to her maternity timeline; prefers not to lead with it but also not to be penalized for it.
- Skeptical of superficial or hype‑driven tools; prefers products that show depth, clarity and real candidate advantage.
- Deeply protective of her community; will only recommend tools that feel genuinely additive and non‑exploitative.

**Signals of interest in Careerspan**
- Strong resonance with "structured self‑reflection" as the core problem and with the idea of AI assembling bullets from deep stories.
- Positive reaction to narrative positioning, strengths/weaknesses analysis and the notion of using Careerspan to test her own identity across roles.
- Express willingness to try the product herself, share feedback, and consider sharing it with her community or co‑hosting events.

**Leverage points / approach**
- Anchor around her community’s needs: support after layoffs, better storytelling, reduced wasted effort, and protection from exploitative roles.
- Emphasize that the candidate side is free and that the business model charges employers, not job seekers.
- Offer low‑lift collaboration formats (e.g., co‑branded workshops, integrated tools like the Crystal Ball and Bullshit Detector) that do not burden her with extra logistics.
- Provide concrete examples of how Careerspan has helped similar non‑technical or career‑switching candidates reposition themselves.

## 2. V / Careerspan

**Role & background**
- Co‑founder of Careerspan; decade‑plus experience as a career coach and narrative‑focused advisor.
- Product built from first‑hand work in college admissions and career coaching, with emphasis on helping people articulate stories rather than just format resumes.

**Motivations & goals**
- Strategic: Grow Careerspan’s footprint among high‑quality communities of jobseekers and operators (e.g., FOHI, Marvin Ventures, Coffee & Career Transitions NYC).
- Product: Validate and refine the structured self‑reflection + matching engine across diverse roles (PM, devrel, community, etc.).
- Ethical: Keep the candidate experience free while charging employers for higher‑signal, story‑rich applicants.

**View of this relationship**
- Sees Erica as both an ideal power‑user (senior candidate with complex narrative) and a high‑leverage community partner (access to devrel and laid‑off tech talent).
- Values her domain expertise in devrel and community operations as input into how Careerspan should serve those personas.

**Leverage points & risks**
- Leverage: Provide tangible value to Erica personally (clarity on narrative, better targeting, reduced search fatigue) as the foundation for any broader collaboration.
- Leverage: Offer programming and tools that make her events more impactful without adding significant ops burden.
- Risk: If the product feels generic, shallow, or like "just another AI resume tool," trust and enthusiasm will evaporate quickly.
- Risk: Over‑emphasizing monetization or employer‑side benefits too early could clash with her current focus on community care and support.

**Recommended posture**
- Lead with mission and genuine candidate impact; let monetization discussions arise organically once she has seen value.
- Stay explicit about boundaries: Careerspan should feel like a partner that amplifies her community’s goals, not a vendor trying to capture her list.
- Schedule a focused follow‑up once she has had hands‑on time; prepare questions that invite candid critique and co‑design rather than a sales pitch.
