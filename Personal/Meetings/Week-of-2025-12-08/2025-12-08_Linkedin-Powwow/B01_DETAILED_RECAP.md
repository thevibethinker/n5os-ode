# B01: Detailed Recap - LinkedIn Powwow

## Overview
The meeting was a strategic sync between Vrijen (V) and a colleague to refine Careerspan's LinkedIn posting strategy. The discussion focused on shifting from a single, broad announcement about the new job board functionality to a "multi-day case study" approach designed to resonate specifically with founders while also providing value to candidates.

## Key Discussion Points
- **Post Fragmentation Strategy**: Instead of one large post, the team decided to break down the messaging into daily, themed chunks. This addresses the short attention span of LinkedIn users and allows for deeper dives into specific value propositions like "no ghost jobs" and "founder case studies."
- **Founder-Centric Messaging**: The primary objective identified was reaching founders. The strategy involves using "David" (a specific user/founder) as a case study to highlight pain points (like recruitment friction) and show how Careerspan solves them.
- **Candidate Value Prop (The "No Ghost Jobs" Angle)**: To appeal to candidates, the team highlighted Careerspan's vetting process, which guarantees all roles on the platform are live. This "anti-ghost job" stance was identified as "easy pickings" for engagement.
- **Outcome Definition**: V initially thought about a broad company page post, but the colleague pushed for a more targeted outcome: getting founders to see themselves in the case studies and candidates to trust the job board as a "black box-free" experience.
- **Deadline and Review**: A draft of the LinkedIn company page post is due by 1:00 PM for review by the colleague.

## Evolution of Strategy
The conversation started with V feeling "off" and seeking a reset, but quickly pivoted into a productive brainstorming session that transformed a generic announcement into a structured content marketing campaign. The "Plot Twist: We're now a job board" angle was retained for the company page, while personal posts will handle the nuanced case studies.
