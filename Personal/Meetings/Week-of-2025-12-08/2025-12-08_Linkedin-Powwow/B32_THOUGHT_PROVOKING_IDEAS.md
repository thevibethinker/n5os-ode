# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas - LinkedIn Powwow

## 1. The "Hope and Cognition" Taxonomy
The idea that professional tasks can be categorized by the amount of "hope" (emotional energy) and "cognition" (mental energy) they require. 
- *Provocation*: Could we map a user's productivity based on these two axes? What does "low hope, high cognition" work look like?

## 2. Narrative-Led SaaS Marketing
The advisor’s point that "founders don't want to be talked at; they want to see themselves in a case study" suggests that for early-stage B2B, narrative resonance is more valuable than feature announcements.
- *Provocation*: Is the "Case Study" the only effective form of marketing for non-technical founders selling to other founders?

## 3. The "Anti-ATS" Manifesto
The "black box" of the ATS is a universal candidate grievance. Careerspan’s positioning isn’t just about *finding* jobs, but about *humanizing* the measurement of a candidate's fit.
- *Provocation*: If Careerspan is the "Anti-ATS," what are the core "laws" of its platform that traditional systems violate?

## 4. Fragmentation as a Solution to Attention
The decision to break one big announcement into 5 small "bang for your buck" posts.
- *Provocation*: In an age of algorithmic volatility, is the "One Big Launch" dead? Is "Micro-Launching" over several days the only way to ensure reach?
