# B32 - Thought Provoking Ideas

## 1. The Monetization Wall for Job Seekers
- Vrijen's observation that job seekers are "terrible to monetize" is a fundamental insight into why many career tech companies fail. It forces a move toward high-signal B2B services.

## 2. Friction as a High-Signal Filter
- In a world of "easy apply" and mass-generated AI resumes, the most valuable thing a platform can offer is *friction* that filters for intent. Does the effort required for a Careerspan reflection act as a proxy for candidate quality?

## 3. The Death of the "Polish" Interview
- Bridget's point about distinguishing between "talking about the job" and "doing the job" suggests that as AI helps candidates fake polish, companies must shift to capturing raw, unpolished "operator data."

## 4. Community-Led Growth in the Bay Area
- The Coffee Space founder's reliance on Livia for "tips from people doing growth" highlights that even in a digital-first matching market, local community and high-trust human nodes (like Livia) remain the primary engine for early-stage startup traction.
