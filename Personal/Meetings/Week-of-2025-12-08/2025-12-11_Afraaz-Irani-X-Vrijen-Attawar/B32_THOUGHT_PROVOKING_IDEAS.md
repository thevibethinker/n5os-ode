# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

- **The "Sponsorship" Myth:** Most founders avoid international students because they fear complex sponsorship. Vrijen posits that for OPT (the first year), the administrative load is negligible (30 mins), creating a massive arbitrage opportunity for early-stage startups to get elite talent.
- **Resume as "Zero Signal":** Vrijen’s core thesis is that resumes are now entirely detached from performance. The move toward "Stories" and voice-input reflection as a vetting gate suggests a future where *articulation* and *reflection* are the primary filters, not credentials.
- **"Unsexy" Scaling:** The idea that a $1M revenue stream in a tech startup is currently bottlenecked by a human making phone calls—and that the solution isn't "better code" but "better low-cost humans"—is a grounded reminder of early-stage operational reality.
