# B01 Detailed Recap

Vrijen (V) met with Celia Davis, founder of Connexions, to discuss their respective startups in the career and social networking space. Celia, a recent Brandeis graduate, is building a mobile-first social network for young professionals focused on "relationship cultivation" rather than just matching. The platform is currently six months old and preparing for a beta launch and a Q1 pre-seed raise.

V shared the evolution of Careerspan, highlighting its transition from a B2C conversational career coach to a B2B2C hiring platform where reflective AI conversations replace traditional job applications. He emphasized the "data advantage" gained through structured self-reflection, which provides deeper insights than resumes.

Key discussion points included:
- **Connexions POV:** Solving professional isolation for remote/freelance young professionals through reciprocity-based networking and in-person events.
- **Careerspan POV:** Resumes are "poor transmitters" of quality; deep data via AI coaching is the resilient advantage.
- **Collaboration:** V invited Celia to his Slack group of ~30 career-tech founders and suggested a potential intro to Jerry from Latte Connect.
- **Academic Links:** Both shared backgrounds in test prep/tutoring. V mentioned his co-founder's recent contact with Brandeis Career Services, where Celia has strong ties.
