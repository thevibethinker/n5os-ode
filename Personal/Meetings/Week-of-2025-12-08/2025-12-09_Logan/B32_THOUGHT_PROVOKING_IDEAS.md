# B32 Thought Provoking Ideas

- **The "Director Bias" in Hiring:** Startups often hire for the stage they *want* to be at (VP of Sales) rather than the stage they *are* at (Scrappy Salesperson). How can founders identify when a recommendation (like Ilsa's) is based on a "prestige" model vs. an "utility" model?
- **Pricing vs. Acquisition:** At what point does detailed marginal cost analysis become a distraction from the core problem of user activation? Ilya's "overkill" pricing model suggests a mismatch between administrative rigor and market product-market fit.
- **Selling "The Look" vs. "The Business":** V's assertion that "we're not trying to make this a viable business, we're trying to get it sold" shifts the entire rubric of value. If the product isn't the value, but the *potential* or *image* is, how does that change daily task prioritization?
- **The "Man Cave" Logic in Engineering:** Logan's observation about engineers hoarding "wires and components" across countries as a parallel to "squirreling" into deep projects. Is there a fundamental conflict between the "builder" mindset (hoarding/depth) and the "operator" mindset (lean/sales)?
