# B32 Thought Provoking Ideas

## The "Director" Trap in Early Stage
How often do startups fail because they hire "VP-level" leaders who have forgotten how to be "operators"? V’s critique of Ilya suggests that in a four-person team, there is zero room for a director who doesn't do "nuts and bolts" work. Is the "rigor" Ilya provides actually a form of procrastination against the "scary" work of cold calling and lead generation?

## HR Tech's "Vibe" Economy
V’s assertion that HR sales are fundamentally different from standard SaaS is profound. If the trade is "people," then relationship and "vibe" are the primary currencies, not "pamphlets" and "process." Does this mean that standard B2B sales playbooks are actively harmful in this niche?

## The Psychology of Acquisition Readiness
If the goal is to sell, is the priority to show a "viable business model" (Ilya's approach) or "momentum and user love" (V's approach)? At what point does financial rigor stop being an asset and start being a distraction from the "guerrilla" growth needed to look attractive to a buyer?

## Onboarding Friction vs. Technical Glitch
The 10% activation rate (5/50 folks) is catastrophic. Is it more likely a "glitch" (a comfortable excuse) or a fundamental "confusion" (a harder truth)? The fact that people sign up but don't start stories suggests the "value proposition" isn't landing the moment they enter the app.
