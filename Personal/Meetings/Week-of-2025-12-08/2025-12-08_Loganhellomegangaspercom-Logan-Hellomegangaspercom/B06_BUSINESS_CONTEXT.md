# Business context

## Company snapshot
- **Company:** Careerspan
- **Stage:** Post‑seed/early stage, ~3 years into the journey.
- **Team:** Transitioning from a larger team to a **lean founders‑only core** (Vrijen, Ilsa, Logan) starting in the new year.
- **Product:** A career and hiring platform whose technology is used by organizations to **post jobs and run career‑related workflows**. Users genuinely like the product and experience.

## Traction and product value
- V reports **strong qualitative traction**: people love using the product and there is growing excitement from organizations leveraging Careerspan’s tech to publish jobs.
- Recent **sales interest and offers** from external parties have reinforced that the product and technology are perceived as valuable assets.

## Financial and fundraising posture
- The team has been through multiple fundraising cycles over three years and is now **exhausted by the fundraising process**.
- There is **limited appetite for another major raise**; the emotional and time cost is judged too high relative to current energy levels.
- The near‑term goal is to **shrink burn** by cutting down to a founders‑only team while still maintaining their salaries at a sustainable level during the exit process.

## Strategic options on the table
- **Acqui‑hire:** Potential buyers who may want to bring in the team (or key members) along with the technology.
- **Tech or product acquisition:** Parties interested in absorbing or repurposing the Careerspan technology stack.
- **Spin‑out / “something else”:** Possibility of the founders or acquirers using the tech in a new configuration or adjacent problem space.

The strategic emphasis is now on **making these conversations concrete** rather than indefinitely continuing as an independent, fundraising‑driven startup.

## Exit goals and constraints
- **Primary goals:**
  - Achieve a **stable landing** for the founders and remaining team members.
  - Ideally secure a **PR‑worthy exit** that reflects positively on Careerspan’s journey.
  - At minimum, **break even financially** on the effort and capital invested.
- **Constraints:**
  - Founder energy and emotional bandwidth are **binding constraints**; there is not enough capacity for another intensive fundraising cycle.
  - Cash and headcount must be managed tightly, hence the decision to reduce to three core team members and wind down external spend.

## Forward‑looking posture
- V expects to **build another startup within roughly a year**, drawing on lessons learned from Careerspan.
- In the interim, the focus is on:
  - **Executing a thoughtful wind‑down/exit**,
  - **Taking care of teammates**, and
  - Allowing founders to **rest and reset** before the next ambitious build.
