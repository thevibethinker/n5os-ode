# B21 – Key Moments

1. **Careerspan’s strategic pivot articulated clearly.**
   - V explains that while Careerspan has a working product and growing usage, the team does not want to:
     - Charge job seekers directly, or
     - Spend years selling into employers.
   - Instead, they will lean into building a brand and distribution engine around "AI x careers" and keep Careerspan free, using other revenue streams to fund it.

2. **Mutual recognition that LinkedIn’s incentives are misaligned with better hiring.**
   - Fei and V dissect how LinkedIn’s revenue model depends on long, noisy hiring cycles and upselling recruiters.
   - They agree that truly improving matching (fewer, better matches) would actually reduce LinkedIn’s revenue—and that this creates space for new systems.

3. **Shared vision of deeper data and agent‑to‑agent interactions.**
   - They imagine a world where a candidate’s and employer’s agents talk over rich, structured data instead of résumés and job descriptions.
   - Nira provides the network-level routing; Careerspan provides deep, qualitative signal about people.

4. **Fei frames Nira’s near‑ and long‑term product vision.**
   - Near term: "AI Rolodex" for individuals and small groups, focused on narrow, high-trust networks.
   - Long term: dynamic, personalized profiles where the same contact appears differently depending on who is viewing them and what they care about.

5. **Emergence of the "anti‑LinkedIn playbook" concept.**
   - V talks about building an anti‑LinkedIn playbook and explicitly wants to feature Nira as a core tool.
   - This becomes a shorthand for a broader movement: AI‑native, humane, high-signal approaches to networking and hiring.

6. **Nira ambassador program as a concrete next step.**
   - Fei proposes an ambassador model where select users upload their networks, invite contacts, and share in subscription revenue.
   - V responds that this is an "easy win" and is open to participating.

7. **Agreement to experiment with joint workflows.**
   - They discuss flows where:
     - Founders voice-record what they need in a hire.
     - Careerspan builds a candidate pipeline and develops candidates toward that bar.
     - Nira helps find, route to, and communicate with those candidates and allies in the network.

8. **Affirmation of mutual excitement and long-term collaboration.**
   - The call closes with both sides explicitly naming their excitement and intention to keep each other in the loop, suggesting a high-likelihood of future joint work.
