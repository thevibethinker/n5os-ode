# B03 – Stakeholder Intelligence

## Fei (Nira co‑founder)
- **Role & context**: Founder of Nira, leading a small (≈5–6 person) engineering-heavy team; recently closed a pre‑seed with South Park Commons, FRC partners, and a16z; preparing for a seed raise.
- **Goals**:
  - Make Nira the trusted operating system for professional networks.
  - Grow adoption via word of mouth and carefully selected ambassadors before a broader public launch.
  - Find high-signal partners (founders, community builders, influencers) who can both use and credibly advocate for Nira.
- **Interests & motivation**:
  - Deeply believes current networking/hiring infrastructure (especially LinkedIn) is misaligned and ripe for reinvention.
  - Values high-agency, thoughtful people who care about quality signal over volume.
  - Keenly interested in Careerspan’s ability to generate rich, qualitative data about candidates and professionals.
- **Concerns / skepticism**:
  - Sensitive to partners whose incentives may not be fully aligned (e.g., communities obsessed with revenue splits rather than user value).
  - Wants to avoid bloated teams and unfocused GTM motions; prefers lean, high-leverage experiments.
- **Leverage points**:
  - Offering early, privileged access (credits, ambassador status) to Nira.
  - Open to deep product integrations where Careerspan’s data can become a moat for both sides.
  - Motivated by narratives about dismantling legacy systems ("anti‑LinkedIn") in favor of more humane, efficient matching.

## Vrijen (Careerspan founder)
- **Role & context**: Founder of Careerspan; experienced career coach; currently pivoting away from a traditional venture-scale SaaS path toward a model driven by thought leadership, brand, and a free product for job seekers.
- **Goals**:
  - Preserve and grow Careerspan as a high-quality, free (or very accessible) product for job seekers and forward-thinking hiring managers.
  - Build a strong personal and company brand around "AI for careers" and humane hiring.
  - Align with a small set of products (like Nira) that embody the same philosophy and can form a new ecosystem.
- **Interests & motivation**:
  - Strong strategic and ethical stance against exploitative, noisy hiring funnels and shallow data (résumés, ATS keyword games).
  - Prefers to invest energy into systems that actually improve candidates’ and employers’ experiences.
  - Excited by the idea of being an opinionated tastemaker (e.g., the "anti‑LinkedIn playbook").
- **Concerns / skepticism**:
  - Skeptical of spending years selling into corporate HR for marginal impact.
  - Wary of misaligned incentives where communities or platforms prioritize their own monetization over user benefit.
- **Leverage points**:
  - Strong narrative and communication skills; can credibly evangelize Nira to founders and high-agency professionals.
  - Deep practitioner credibility on both sides of the hiring table (candidate + employer).
  - Willingness to plug Nira into public content, talks, and workshops as a flagship example.

## Logan (Careerspan co‑founder / partner)
- **Role & context**: Co‑builder with V at Careerspan, with background leading projects in workforce and AI (including Harvard context mentioned on the call).
- **Goals**:
  - Similar to V: build a durable, respected presence at the intersection of AI and work.
  - Use Careerspan + partner tools (like Nira) to demonstrate what good, modern hiring and networking can look like.
- **Interests & motivation**:
  - Interested in building distribution through relationships, content, and communities, not just ad spend or cold B2B sales.
  - Cares about high-caliber communities (alumni networks, founder circles) as a natural audience.
- **Concerns / skepticism**:
  - Shares V’s concerns about the misaligned incentives in employer sales and traditional hiring tooling.
- **Leverage points**:
  - Academic and institutional credibility (Harvard, workforce projects) that can be used to open doors with communities and early adopter orgs.
  - Operationally minded; likely to help design pilots and experiments that produce strong case studies for Nira + Careerspan.

## Relationship dynamics & fit
- **Alignment**:
  - All parties are sharply critical of LinkedIn and legacy hiring funnels.
  - They share a desire to build AI‑native, human‑centric systems that use richer data to connect people.
  - There is clear mutual respect and intellectual chemistry; conversation is fast, collaborative, and exploratory rather than transactional.
- **Potential friction points**:
  - Careerspan is deliberately stepping off the classic venture track, while Nira is leaning into it; over time, this could create tension around pace, expectations, or focus.
  - Ambiguity about concrete commercial structures (revenue splits, integration ownership) could cause misalignment if not clarified as experiments mature.
- **Net assessment**:
  - High-potential strategic relationship between a network OS (Nira) and a deep candidate-signal engine (Careerspan).
  - Early-stage trust is strong; both sides are candid about their evolving strategies and motivations.
