# B31: Stakeholder Research & Landscape Insights

## Market Landscape: Talent Sourcing

### Incumbent Solutions (High Cost/Friction)
- **Recruiters**: High cost, slow, unreliable quality
- **Resume Screening**: Passive, doesn't capture soft skills or full candidate dimensions
- **Job Boards**: Passive supply, low signal-to-noise

### Careerspan Opportunity
- **Gap Identified**: Mid-signal alternative between "free job boards" and "expensive recruiters"
- **Competitive Moat**: First to comprehensively map product/community landscape as talent source
- **Key Advantage**: Employers can source from specific communities they trust (reduces hiring risk)

## Community Landscape Intelligence

**Key Communities Mentioned**:
- Rise (targeted for partnership expansion this week)
- Product communities (general category - multiple communities aggregated)
- McKinsey alumni groups (mentioned as relevant sourcing pools)

**Sourcing Strategy**:
- "Compose ideal candidate by looking at different compositions of communities"
- Approach: Different communities = different types of talent (like fishing different parts for different fish species)
- Validation: Employers recognize community names + respond positively to brand association

## Employer Behavior Insights

**What Drives Decisions**:
- Simplicity: "Here's a good candidate,  to review"
- Demonstrable pool quality: Community associations matter more than algorithmic claims
- Low friction: 5 minutes setup, 10 minutes/week to screen candidates

**What Doesn't Drive Decisions**:
- Sophisticated claims about "behavioral dimensions of candidates"
- Complex methodology/matching algorithms
- "Better than resumes" aspirational positioning

## Strategic Positioning: Market Timing

**Hypothesis**: Resume system is "crumbling"—subjective decision-making will eventually dominate. However, can't claim superiority yet. Instead, focus on being reliable/consistent alternative while building network effects around community dominance.
