### GTM Strategy

**Strategic Shift: Community Partnership → Direct Hiring Manager Sales** [B40.D2] [B40.D5]

**From:** Community-mediated B2B2C model
- Community leaders distribute Careerspan to members
- Revenue share with community partners (e.g., Foe partnership)
- Indirect relationship with hiring managers

**To:** Direct hiring manager B2B sales
- Target hiring managers at Series A companies directly
- "We know a fast fuckload of hiring managers at this point" - leverage existing network
- Remove community middleman overhead

**Rationale:**
- Hiring managers feel acute pain (bad hires, time waste)
- Direct relationship = faster feedback loop and iteration
- Community partnerships add complexity without proportional value
- "People don't like new things in general" - need pain-driven adoption not partnership-driven

### Target Market Segmentation

**Primary ICP: Series A Hiring Managers** [B40.D5]
- **Profile:** Companies with dedicated/semi-dedicated HR, experiencing growth pains
- **Size:** Post-product-market-fit, pre-scale (typically 20-100 employees)
- **Pain Point:** Acute verification anxiety, resourced enough to pay
- **Decision Maker:** Hiring manager (feels consequences) with HR support (budget authority)
- **Competitive Advantage:** Less founder ego blocking than early stage
- **Access:** Existing network connections via V + Logan relationships

**Secondary ICP: Small Companies (<Series A)**
- **Pain Point:** High-profile fraud cases (Soham Parikh) create anxiety
- **Budget:** Limited but anxious enough to try new solutions
- **Sales Approach:** Case study-driven - "This is how Careerspan would have stopped Soham Parikh at every step" [B40.T8]
- **Market Size:** Broader market, lower ACV but potentially higher volume

**Excluded: Pre-Series A Founders**
- **Blocker:** "Founder ego - 'I trust myself to sniff stuff out'"
- **Strategic Response:** Skip this segment entirely, focus where pain + buying power intersect

**Alternative ICP: Top Candidates (B2C Model)** [B40.D4]
- **Profile:** Elite candidates (90+ Careerspan score), passive job seekers
- **Offer:** White glove service + signing bonus (redistributed recruiter fees)
- **Access:** Careerspan Plus members (~15 identified), unofficial elite community sourcing
- **Value Prop:** Effort-free job matching with financial upside
- **Positioning:** "Creator program" for talent - get paid for being excellent

### Sales Process

**Field Test Phase (Week 1-2)** [B40.T11]
- **Goal:** Validate verification pain point severity and adoption willingness
- **Approach:** Leverage existing hiring manager relationships
- **Questions to Ask:**
  - "Are you worried about fake/AI-assisted candidates?"
  - "Have you experienced the 'gaslighting moment' of questioning candidate authenticity?"
  - "If we had a solution, would you use it?"
  - "What would make it worth the integration effort?"
- **Target:** 5-10 hiring managers at Series A companies
- **Timeline:** Immediate (this week)

**Proof-of-Concept Sales (Month 1)**
- **Offer:** Manual verification service (Ilse's process) at low/no cost
- **Deliverable:** 8-10 case studies demonstrating verification value [B40.T2]
- **Customer Development:** Learn integration preferences, pricing sensitivity, feature priorities
- **Success Metric:** Companies willing to pay for scaled version

**Acquisition Pitch Phase (Month 2-3)**
- **Targets:** 
  - Category A: Verification companies (Checkr, Tofu, First Advantage)
  - Category B: ATS systems (warm intros via Kathy Vam/Workday, 42 Geek/Greenhouse)
  - Category C: Workforce-adjacent companies
- **Pitch:** Case studies + proof points + existing customer traction
- **Ask:** Acquisition at target valuation (TBD based on traction) [B40.T2]

### Messaging & Positioning

**"Filter Plus" Positioning** [B40.T5]
- **Core Message:** We integrate with your existing ATS, we don't replace it
- **Value Prop:** "We will be selective. We won't give you so many people that it will ever be a problem."
- **Differentiation:** High-effort barrier (stories) vs. algorithmic claims
- **Objection Handling:** Integration addressed upfront, not as afterthought

**"Selecting for High-Effort Individuals" Frame**
- **Insight:** Verification isn't just about fake candidates, it's about quality filtering
- **Message:** "Bullshitters, fake candidates, whatever - you're selecting for high effort individuals"
- **Positioning:** Time-waster elimination, not just fraud prevention
- **Market Resonance:** Broader pain point than just fake candidates

**Ethical Verification Frame**
- **Message:** "We're not trying to catch people out, but...that's a red flag"
- **Positioning:** Candidate-friendly verification, not adversarial screening
- **Differentiation:** Story-based vs. invasive background checks
- **Candidate Adoption:** Reduces resistance, positions as quality signal for genuine candidates

**Populist Brand Angle** [B40.D4]
- **Message:** "It's bullshit that people don't get their own signing bonus"
- **Positioning:** Anti-recruiter, pro-candidate, value redistribution
- **Market Timing:** "Next 2 years recruiting agencies will make fucking bank" - disruption opportunity
- **Brand Differentiation:** Missionary not mercenary, aligns with "outstanding and authentic" brand

**Soham Parikh Case Study** [B40.T8]
- **Hook:** High-profile fake candidate case (North Korean hacker)
- **Message:** "Careerspan would have stopped [him] at every step of the way"
- **Target:** Small companies with acute fraud anxiety
- **Deliverable:** Detailed deconstruction showing multiple catch points
- **Credibility:** Specific, technical, demonstrates depth of verification approach

### Pricing & Packaging

**Proof-of-Concept Pricing**
- **Strategy:** Low/no cost manual verification to build case studies
- **Goal:** Demonstrate value, learn pricing sensitivity
- **Timeline:** Month 1

**Future Pricing Models (Post-Acquisition or Raise)**

**Model A: Per-Verification SaaS**
- **Unit Economics:** Price per candidate verified
- **Target:** Small companies, usage-based pricing
- **Advantage:** Low friction adoption, scales with usage

**Model B: ATS Integration License**
- **Unit Economics:** Annual license fee for integration + data access
- **Target:** ATS companies acquiring technology
- **Advantage:** Recurring revenue, enterprise pricing

**Model C: Recruiter Fee Replacement** [B40.D4]
- **Unit Economics:** 5-10% placement fee (vs. 15-30% recruiter standard)
- **Target:** Companies hiring top candidates through Careerspan agent model
- **Advantage:** Clear ROI vs. recruiter fees, candidate upside (signing bonus)
- **Revenue Share:** Split savings with candidate as signing bonus

### Competitive Differentiation

**vs. Traditional Background Verification (Checkr, etc.)**
- **Their Strength:** Criminal/employment history verification
- **Our Strength:** Story-based consistency, AI fraud detection, role-appropriate language validation
- **Positioning:** Complementary not competitive - different verification types

**vs. ATS Incumbents**
- **Their Weakness:** Filtering broken by spam applications, no verification capability
- **Our Strength:** High-effort barrier + story-based verification
- **Positioning:** "Filter Plus" integration, not replacement

**vs. Funded Competitors (Jack, Jill)**
- **Their Advantage:** A16Z backing, marketing budget
- **Our Advantage:** Story-based verification is unique, "messaging not yet mature"
- **Positioning:** Use their raises as market validation in acquisition pitch

**vs. Recruiter Model**
- **Their Weakness:** High fees (15-30%), adversarial candidate experience
- **Our Strength:** Signing bonus redistribution, candidate-aligned incentives
- **Positioning:** Populist, missionary, "outstanding and authentic" brand
