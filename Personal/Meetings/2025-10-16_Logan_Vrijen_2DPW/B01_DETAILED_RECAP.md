# B01: DETAILED_RECAP

## Key Decisions and Agreements

**1. Skeleton Crew Product Strategy (Core Pivot)**
- **Outcome:** Committed to building Careerspan as a self-sustaining product that can run with just Logan and Vrijen as minimal viable team through end of year
- **Why it matters:** Defines survival economics and sets realistic scope for product roadmap; eliminates scope creep that has historically exhausted the team
- **Rationale:** Recognizes that maintaining "perfect" features was unachievable; skeleton crew approach creates self-imposed constraint that forces MVP discipline

**2. Personal Brand Alignment Strategy (Founder Positioning)**
- **Outcome:** Shifted mental model from "Careerspan as product company" to "Logan and Vrijen as career coaches with Careerspan as scalable enabler"
- **Why it matters:** Resolves identity tension that's been draining; founders return to core expertise (coaching) rather than fighting tech/product battles
- **Rationale:** Ironic return to original positioning (we started as coaches, ending as coaches) unlocks psychological liberation and aligns with both founders' consultant instincts

**3. End-of-Year Decision Gates (Organizational Clarity)**
- **Outcome:** Three explicit outcomes by Jan 1, 2026: (a) Product dies/no value, (b) Sell (if interest exists), or (c) Founders fund + retain equity for team
- **Why it matters:** Creates clear evaluation framework instead of ongoing ambiguity; removes decision paralysis
- **Rationale:** Three distinct paths prevent "zombie company" state and clarify what "success" means for each scenario

**4. Alternative Opportunity Recognition: McKinsey-Style AI Consulting**
- **Outcome:** Acknowledged 2-month spin-up time to launch McKinsey-style AI project-based consulting for companies to assess employee AI learnability
- **Why it matters:** Represents viable pivot if Careerspan product path fails; plays to founders' consultant strengths
- **Rationale:** Andrew overheard mention; validates market demand; represents path to quick revenue vs. long product road

## Strategic Context

**Energy & Burnout Reality**

Both founders expressed exhaustion from learning expensive lessons over ~3 years:
- Logan: "I'm just fucking tired dude. Like, I would really like, like a million dollars."
- Vrijen: Resonated on fatigue; noted that learning + implementing lessons was draining but now they're "knowledgeable... and tired"
- Critical insight: 6 months of stress relief could restore motivation for "doing something else in this space"

**Historical Pattern Recognition**

Founders acknowledged repeating pattern: "At every stage, we were like, no, that's cool, let's do that" → scope creep → exhaustion

This time: Skeleton crew constraint forces ruthless prioritization and prevents "cool but unnecessary" features

**AI as Intellectual Leverage (Vrijen's Framework)**

Vrijen articulated that AI is an "intellectual horsepower multiplier" but requires:
- Design pattern discipline (which he's maintained via "very disciplined implementation")
- ~120 IQ threshold to wield effectively (top 5% of humans)
- Most people won't make the effort ("I don't want to vibe code Uber every time I want a car")

**Implication:** Creates unfair advantage IF they stay disciplined

**Founder Temperament Alignment**

Both identified as consultants at heart: "We want the novelty of somebody else's problems that we can tell them how to solve"

This self-awareness unlocks permission structure: consulting (in various forms) is the mode they excel at, not pure product/engineering
