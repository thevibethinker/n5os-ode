# Business Context

- **Matchbox pivot:** Holly is doubling down on the recruiting marketplace after several product pivots and a co-founder breakup. The focus is on building a *visibility / funnel* dashboard for early-stage startups, layering structured candidate intelligence (100+ startups feeding requests via a 600-company list, 713 records in the database) on top of a lightweight ATS that could be re-activated or sold self-serve for ~$59/month once there is traction. The recruiting team is still operating with a lean burn (~20–25K USD/month) and is deliberately rebuilding traction before deciding whether to raise again.

- **Customer dynamics:** Early-stage startups hate switching heavy ATSs (Greenhouse/Ashby) because migration is painful, CRM and resume data is trapped, and they default to recruiters or manual screeners. Founders are drowning in inbound noise, but they are also price sensitive—many first-timers keep their hiring curated to a handful of hires, while more funded teams go straight to agencies. In Australia, employers asked for resume browsing/downloading, not just AI signal, and they still lean on CVs despite automated summaries.

- **Careerspan position:** Career coaches (V and the team) have stopped chasing VC rounds and are instead proving that their narrative-rich candidate scoring + ATS-friendly portal can make inbound work again. The value props are:
  - *Signal*: Story-based assessments expose what resumes miss (motivations, story thresholds, AI-generated gaps) and surface the 2‑5% of high-attention candidates.
  - *Distribution*: A high-engagement community (70‑80% open, ~15% CTR) and a job board ready to email roles such as DeepTune/Yarn (NYC back-end/front-end searches with $175‑300K). The platform can spin up employer accounts, anonymize posts ("Matchbox" branding), and deliver links via email.
  - *Monetization*: Instead of commission-heavy placements, Careerspan is pitching a link-rental model—matchers pay a flat monthly compute/posting fee (roughly $200‑500) and Careerspan drives filtered inbound into the employer’s ATS.

- **Mutual opportunity:** Combine Matchbox’s employer relationships + channel trust with Careerspan’s candidate signal and automation. Specific levers discussed:
  1. Career Span filters the inbound, boils it down to the ~2‑5% of high-quality candidates, and feeds them into Matchbox/holly’s ATS or feeds them straight into the startup’s system.
  2. Matchbox owns employer relationships, legalese, and brand; Careerspan provides the link technology, analytics, and candidate screening. Together they can convert roles that are hard to fill (DeepTune, Yarn, Agave) without asking employers to migrate off Ashby/Greenhouse.
  3. Zo’s platform can act as an on-ramp before the prototype is fully engineered—human-enabled service proof points could become cash-flowed stories for future raises.

- **Signals to track:**
  - Delivery rockets such as DeepTune and Yarn (NY roles launching now) and Marianne Ray / Ginkgo Bioworks profiles that can prove out the offering.
  - Employer willingness to pay a monthly "link" fee, not a hit-or-miss placement percentage.
  - Career Span’s ability to keep compute costs (database scans, embeddings, AI feedback) under control as the candidate network scales.

- **Risks & guardrails:**
  - Execution bandwidth is tight—Holly is essentially solo rebuilding the business, while V is stretched across product, coaching, and GTM. Overcommitment on the Matchbox front risks leaving the recruiting marketplace half-finished.
  - Market noise from AI hiring startups makes clarity essential; any joint offer must translate to *meaningful speed/quality* improvements, not another data dump.
  - Dependency on a handful of proof-point roles (DeepTune, Yarn, Marianne/health-tech) means the narrative could stall if those searches slow over the holidays. Need to diversify quickly with other high-value openings.
