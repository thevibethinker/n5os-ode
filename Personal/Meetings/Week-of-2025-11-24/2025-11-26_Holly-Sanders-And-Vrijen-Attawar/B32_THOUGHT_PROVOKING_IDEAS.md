# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas — 2025-11-26_Holly_Sanders_and_Vrijen_Attawar_[M]

Identify 1-3 highly provocative themes, strategic "weirdness," or original mental models surfaced during the meeting. (Strictly non-actionable).

### 1. The "Reverse Flywheel" of Alpha Products
Holly describes a phenomenon where a startup cannot move forward because users won't use a broken product, and the engineering team won't fix the product until people are using it and giving feedback. This creates a "head against the wall" dynamic where the traditional lean startup loop (build-measure-learn) fails because the initial friction of a non-functional product prevents any "measurement" or "learning" from happening in the first place.

### 2. The "Bloodbeat" of Resume Signal Decay
Vrijen posits that the ATS (Applicant Tracking System) space is headed for a "bloodbath" because resumes have lost their signal due to the ease of AI generation. The strategic shift being explored is moving away from subjective evaluation (which AI has commoditized and polluted) toward objective verification (ID, credibility) and high-friction "reflection sessions" as the only remaining ways to identify high-quality candidates.

### 3. The "Cloaked Company" Recruitment Model
The idea of a recruiter acting as a "white-label" front-end for another recruitment platform (Matchbox using Careerspan tech while anonymizing/cloaking the end client) suggests a strategic pivot where brand equity is shared or outsourced to bypass the "who are you?" friction of new market entry. It treats "representation" as a separate, tradable layer from "sourcing" and "vetting."
