### Idea 1: Embed Career Span in EAW's student-to-job matching workflow

**Description:** Use Career Span as the matching engine when EAW assigns students to specific roles/companies (their second hiring process, not initial intake). Students complete Career Span profile during onboarding, then get matched to best-fit roles based on their stories and competencies.

**Rationale:** EAW already does two hiring processes: (1) hiring students into EAW program, (2) matching those students to specific company roles. Career Span could own step 2, providing better fit quality and giving students immediate job placement value from using the tool.

**Source:** Vrijen suggested (~32:47): "If you use...instead of using us as the intake, if you bring us in an onboarding and use us during the matching process of like, connecting people to job. Connecting people, Collecting your students to jobs based on what they've shared on career span, I think that might be the other way"

**Confidence:** Medium - Stephanie showed interest but flagged she's already committed to Paradox for intake. Needs more scoping to understand their internal matching process and whether Career Span adds value beyond Paradox's capabilities.

### Idea 2: Supervisor-integrated career development dashboard

**Description:** Make Career Span the system of record that EAW supervisors reference during career development check-ins with students. Supervisors can see: skills students have developed, learning gaps, career goals, and progress over time. Creates structured conversation framework for growth discussions.

**Rationale:** Transforms Career Span from isolated student tool to embedded organizational workflow. Supervisors gain visibility into student development; students get accountability and guidance. Aligns with Career Span's "cultivation model" vs one-time transactional use.

**Source:** Stephanie proposed (~30:41): "I would want this sort of integration of career span to be earlier on like let it be something that the supervisors can refer to for that career development and like having check ins on. All right, here's what you're learning, here's the skills you've developed. How does that compare with where you want to be cool? What do you want to work on that sort of thing."

**Confidence:** High - Stephanie explicitly articulated this vision; suggests she's thought about this use case before. Would require building supervisor dashboard/permissions model that doesn't exist in Career Span today.

### Idea 3: Job distribution service for community partners

**Description:** Career Span scrapes/aggregates relevant job postings and distributes them to partner organizations' members with smart matching. Partner (like EAW) gets turnkey job board for their population without building/maintaining one themselves.

**Rationale:** Reduces partner's operational burden (they don't need to find/curate jobs); gives Career Span data on what roles convert to applications; creates value even before Career Span has direct employer relationships.

**Source:** Vrijen suggested (~32:47): "We can find jobs that are scraped on the Internet and just distribute it to your students. Like, that's a service you could pay us for and we would just distribute it to the most fitted student."

**Confidence:** Low - Stephanie pushed back hard on this. Her concern: generic scraping won't help students "stand out" without relationship context ("are our students really standing out?"). She needs employer recognition of EAW brand, not commodity access to open roles. This idea doesn't solve her core problem.

### Idea 4: "Talent pool visibility" as primary value prop for student activation

**Description:** Lead with "by completing your Career Span profile, you join a curated talent pool that employers actively scout from" rather than "get insights about your career fit." Make job placement promise the headline, development insights the beneficial side effect.

**Rationale:** Stephanie identified this as "the missing piece" for student engagement. Students won't engage for abstract development value; they will engage for concrete employment access. Reframes Career Span from introspection tool to job opportunity access platform.

**Source:** Stephanie recognized (~26:42): "I think that might be the missing piece for us...that last piece of it of you'd be in a talent pool. It's an opportunity to truly stand out against the resumes, like, get that actual leg up."

**Confidence:** High - This is validated pattern from both student-side (Stephanie's experience) and employer-side (Career Span's sales learnings). Should become primary messaging for all early-career/student-facing communications. Requires Career Span to actually deliver on promise (curated employer scouting relationships).

### Idea 5: "Vetted pipeline" certification/badging for community alumni

**Description:** Career Span provides certification or verification that partner organization (like EAW) has vouched for this candidate's quality. Employers see "EAW-certified" or similar trust signal when reviewing candidate, not just generic applicant.

**Rationale:** Solves Stephanie's "holy shit, that's a great pipeline" recognition problem. EAW students have higher baseline (work experience, proven professionalism) but no way to signal that to employers. Partnership badge/certification makes invisible quality visible.

**Source:** Implied by Stephanie's repeated emphasis (~31:43): "These are quality candidates...they show up to work, they've increased their professionalism...we can vouch for that. So I feel like there's something there if you can use that piece to sell."

**Confidence:** Medium-High - Clear pain point, but implementation unclear. Would require building trust/verification layer into Career Span. Could be as simple as "[EAW Alumni]" tag on profile or as complex as multi-tier certification system. Needs design thinking to avoid feeling gimmicky.
