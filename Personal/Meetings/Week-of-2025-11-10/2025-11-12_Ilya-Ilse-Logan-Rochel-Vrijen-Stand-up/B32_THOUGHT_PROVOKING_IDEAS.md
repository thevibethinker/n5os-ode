# B32 Thought-Provoking Ideas

## The "Black Art" of Soft Skills
Ilse's observation that quantifying soft skills was historically impossible (relying on self-reporting) until AI. This positions Careerspan not just as an "improvement" but as an "enabler" of a previously non-existent capability.

## Talent Combinatorics vs. Stack Ranking
The idea that "ranking" is an "improper frame" for talent evaluation. By framing hiring as a search for a specific "mix of facets" (combinatorics), Careerspan challenges the fundamental architecture of most Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).

## The Latent Guilt of HR
Vrijen's insight into the "latent guilt" HR professionals feel regarding the "factory farming" approach to hiring. The product is framed as a "moral hedge" or a "restoration of humanity" to their profession.

## Transparency in AI-Collaboration
Ilya's decision to be prideful about the "heart" of human-written content while being transparent about AI assistance. This reflects a broader societal tension in the "post-AI" creative landscape: how to value the human signal while leveraging the machine's speed.
