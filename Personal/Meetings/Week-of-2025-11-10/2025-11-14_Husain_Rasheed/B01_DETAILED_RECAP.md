## Strategic Context

Aspire is under pressure to justify partner investments to their CEO. The Director of Programs uses a "free mentorship from Harvard professors" benchmark to push back on tool costs, missing the distinction between volunteer contribution and sustainable partner economics. Husain recognizes this gap and is building the case for paid partnerships grounded in quantifiable ROI.

Careerspan's key positioning advantage: **reusable learning infrastructure** (scaffolded reflection, career narratives) versus **disposable outputs** (one-off resume reviews or job applications). The token cost is essentially transparent usage-based pricing for AI-powered coaching—a fundamentally different model than job boards.

The partnership hinges on:
1. Getting CEO to allocate $1-2k for 6 months (modest ask if framed as coaching equivalent)
2. Demonstrating measurable impact for Aspire to pitch back to their stakeholders
3. Building sustainable mentor ecosystem (Careerspan as coaching platform + external mentors)
