# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
A strategic partnership discussion between Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan) and Edmund Cuthbert (Superposition) focused on collaborative hiring experiments and advice on Careerspan's path forward. The conversation covered Careerspan's current product capabilities, proposed joint experiments with PM/designer roles, three strategic monetization options, and a significant partnership opportunity through Marvin Ventures (McKinsey alumni fund).

## Chronological Discussion

### Product Update & Strategic Context (01:22-05:30)
Vrijen provided context on Careerspan's current state: approaching 1500 hours of coaching tracked with accelerating user engagement. The product now includes a hiring portal that functions as a zero-touch ATS—companies generate "Magic Links" from job descriptions, and only the 2-5% of candidates who meet standards are surfaced. However, Vrijen acknowledged that contorting the company for VC appeal had led to runway depletion, shifting strategy to minimal burn while building toward becoming an acquisition target. The tech is production-ready, with stealth job posting options and candidate cultivation tracking over time.

### Sunset Connection & Candidate Pipeline Discussion (05:30-14:52)
Edmund recommended Sunset, a company that specializes in startup pivots and helping find soft landings for employees being let go. He suggested this as both a strategic advisory resource and potential partnership opportunity for Careerspan to provide candidate pipelines. Vrijen demonstrated the employer portal interface during this segment. The conversation deepened on candidate representation—both agreed that Careerspan's approach of presenting candidates as bundles rather than stacked rankings was philosophically superior. Edmund's founder had noted: "your product's not a thermometer, it's a traffic light."

### Superposition Partnership Proposal (14:52-21:28)
The conversation turned to concrete collaboration. Edmund proposed testing Careerspan on PM and designer roles (currently Superposition only handles engineers), with revenue sharing to give Careerspan "skin in the game." They identified two particularly interesting role categories: Forward Deployed Engineers and Go-to-Market Engineers—both cases where title-based filtering is ineffective (FDEs largely work at Palantir; GTMEs mostly run agencies and use the title for marketing). Edmund emphasized these roles have "latent signals" that Careerspan's coaching interactions could reveal—signals no one else has access to. Edmund agreed to experiment: "I'll be up for experiment with that."

### Three Strategic Pathways (21:28-25:12)
Edmund offered three strategic options for Careerspan's monetization path:
1. **API-first play**: Become an API delivering candidates into recruiting platforms (design partners needed, including Superposition)
2. **Candidate-side play**: AI talent agents for candidates—like "free agency but fully AI enabled," leading to sports-athlete style compensation structures for top talent
3. **Niche focus**: Find one niche where coaching engagement generates clear hiring alpha, such as customer support (where verbal conversation ability directly correlates with job performance)

Vrijen noted his dream for over a year has been the API play, and suggested Superposition could help capture the emerging FDE/GTME market.

### Marvin Ventures Partnership Opportunity (25:25-29:11)
Vrijen revealed Careerspan is close to signing an LOI with Marvin Ventures (McKinsey's unofficial alumni fund), which has ~200 founders and 400 active roles on their job board. He proposed a combined approach: Careerspan provides Magic Links for candidate filtering, Superposition provides voice-agent-powered JD extraction and credibility as the interface. Candidates would be presented as "Superposition powered by Careerspan." Edmund agreed enthusiastically, emphasizing that onboarding Marvin Ventures founders into Superposition's product would produce richer JDs through voice-based context extraction. The partnership would use Superposition's brand as the company interface with Careerspan as the talent source, with revenue sharing for candidates sourced directly through Careerspan. Both committed to moving forward immediately.

## Key Takeaways
- Partnership confirmed: Superposition will experiment with Careerspan for PM and designer roles using revenue-sharing model
- Three strategic options for Careerspan defined: API-first, AI talent agents (candidate-side), or niche play
- Marvin Ventures partnership approved: joint approach to pitch ~200 McKinsey alumni founders using Superposition's voice agent + Careerspan's candidate filtering
- Forward Deployed Engineers and Go-to-Market Engineers identified as high-value test cases where Careerspan's "latent signals" from coaching provide unique value
- Sunset connection offered as both advisory resource for Careerspan's strategic pivot and potential partnership for candidate sourcing
- Clear technical integration path: Superposition's voice agent extracts hiring context → generates JD → Careerspan Magic Link filters candidates → surfaced through Superposition interface
