# B28_STRATEGIC_INTELLIGENCE

## Strategic Context

Careerspan is at a critical inflection point: strong product traction (~1,500 coaching hours) but constrained runway after pursuing VC funding. The company has built functional product capabilities (zero-touch ATS for hiring, semantic evaluation, high-explainability recommendations) and is now pivoting to a monetization-first strategy with acquisition as a potential exit path.

## Three Strategic Paths

Edmund outlined three potential strategic directions for Careerspan:

### Path 1: Company-Side API Play (Preferred)
"Become an API to deliver candidates into other recruiting things... keep the whole candidate side and it's just an API into. So you become the picks and shovels for people building, sourcing things."
- **Positioning:** Infrastructure layer for recruiting platforms
- **Customers:** Superposition, Juicebox, and similar sourcing/ATS companies
- **Value prop:** Intelligence layer with explainability, trade-off analysis (not stacked ranking)
- **Requirements:** Multiple design partners for validation and iteration

### Path 2: Candidate-Side AI Talent Agents
"Maximally candidate side and you do actually charge on the candidate side. And it's like AI talent agents... free agency but like fully AI enabled."
- **Trend:** Moving toward sports athlete-style compensation for top-tier AI-enabled talent
- **Opportunity:** Structured deals for one-person armies who are AI-augmented
- **Challenge:** More competitive, requires candidate-side monetization shift

### Path 3: Niche-First Alpha Generation
"Find one niche where engagement with the careers coach thing generates alpha for that type of job. You know, something where you can't filter on paper... there's a big set of possible people. It's very hard to figure out who's a good fit on paper. But there is alpha on their suitability for the job."
- **Target roles:** Go-to-market engineers, forward deployed engineers, customer support
- **Value driver:** Latent signals from coaching conversations that predict job performance
- **Key insight:** Roles where verbal conversation ability directly correlates with job success

## Strategic Partnerships

### Superposition Partnership (Immediate Action)
**Model:** Revenue share on success fees, Superposition as customer-facing interface, Careerspan as intelligence layer
- **Superposition role:** Voice agent onboarding, client communication, interview scheduling, Slack updates
- **Careerspan role:** Magic Links for candidate filtering, semantic evaluation, bundle-based candidate presentation
- **Experiments:** Test with roles requiring latent signals (FDE, GTM engineers), A/B test against existing sources
- **Strategic value:** Validation without building customer base, credibility through Superposition brand

### Marvin Ventures Partnership (Strategic Access)
- **Assets:** ~200 founders, 400 active roles, official sanction to approach
- **Combo pitch:** "Superposition powered by Careerspan" with joint go-to-market
- **Strategic value:** Market credibility, relationship capital, path to Series A+ customers

## Differentiation Strategy

**Core Competitive Moat:** High-explainability recommendations grounded in reasoning traces
- "What is the intel I need to engage with this talent and what are the trade-offs?"
- Bundle-based candidate presentation ("traffic light, not thermometer")
- Trade-off space mental model vs. stacked ranking idiot approach
- Every recommendation can be justified to employers

## Exit Strategy
- "Create, cut staff to bare minimum, keep the product running and essentially start to slowly focus on monetization with an eye towards continuing to build up scale so that we could maybe even be an acquisition target"
- Focus on creating "strong marketable relationships" that can be leveraged for next funding round or acquisition
- Build momentum until becoming "juicy target" for acquirers (mentioned Jack and Jill as example)

## Immediate Strategic Priorities
1. **Execute Superposition partnership:** Send LOI/email, select test roles, launch experiment
2. **Lock down Marvin Ventures:** Secure official sanction to pitch 200 founders jointly
3. **Choose strategic path:** Evaluate API play vs. niche-first, likely pursue hybrid (API with niche validation)
4. **Minimize burn:** Cut staff to bare minimum while maintaining product functionality

## Key Strategic Risks
- VC funding exhaustion requiring rapid monetization
- Competition in recruiting AI space
- Need to prove alpha signals are valuable vs. traditional filtering
- Acquisition target strategy depends on building scale and market position quickly
