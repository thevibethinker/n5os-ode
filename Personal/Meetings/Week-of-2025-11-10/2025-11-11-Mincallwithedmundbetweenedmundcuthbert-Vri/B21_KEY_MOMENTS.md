# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"Your product's not a thermometer, it's a traffic light."**  
— Founder at Paidaworks (relayed by Edmund Cuthbert)  
*Context:* Powerful reframing of Careerspan's value proposition. Instead of ranking candidates numerically (thermometer), the system signals qualified vs. not qualified (traffic light). This insight crystallizes why traditional stack-ranking approaches are flawed and why Careerspan's trade-off-based mental model is superior.

**"Every fucking engineer goes down this rabbit hole of like, oh, but is this person a 78 out of 100 and the best? Like it's so seductive and wrong."**  
— Edmund Cuthbert  
*Context:* Critiquing the obsession with numerical precision in talent evaluation. Captures the systemic problem Careerspan is solving and validates why their approach is necessary. Shows Edmund's alignment with the product philosophy.

**"It's absolutely where the market's going. It's going to be sports athlete style compensation or at least like structured deals for the very top talents that are like basically one man armies or one person armies because they are AI enabled."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Discussing the future of talent representation. Signals strategic foresight about how AI will empower individual talent and create new models for compensation and agency. Shows Vrijen's vision for where the market is heading.

**"You cannot distill someone to just keywords. Those are where we shine because that's the kind of data that we collect that no one else does."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Differentiating Careerspan from keyword-based matching systems. Captures the core technical advantage and why the coaching conversation data provides unique insights. Articulates the competitive moat.

**"When I talk to you, I actually believe that there are people that can, like, change this crap system for the better. So I would love to stay in the fight, you know?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Closing statement showing emotional investment in the mission and belief that meaningful change to recruiting is possible. Reveals Vrijen's passion and resilience despite current challenges.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"What would you do if you were us? Like, if you had sort of attack, if you had tech in a product, but we're struggling to sort of get the ignition on monetization, like, where would you go from here to make sure that we still exit this company with like a good outcome?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar (to Edmund)  
*Significance:* This is the core strategic question that drove the entire conversation. Vrijen is seeking advice on monetization strategy and exit path. Edmund's response (API play, AI talent agents, or niche focus) provided three distinct strategic directions that shaped the rest of the discussion and partnership opportunities.

**"What about forward deployed engineers? Could we, would there be signaling your product of people who are engineers but like more customer facing or want to be?"**  
— Edmund Cuthbert  
*Significance:* Edmund identifies a specific niche where Careerspan's approach could shine—roles where the talent has latent signals not visible in traditional channels. This question led to deeper exploration of GTM Engineers, Customer Support, and other roles where conversational alpha provides unique advantage. Shows Edmund's strategic thinking about finding product-market fit.
