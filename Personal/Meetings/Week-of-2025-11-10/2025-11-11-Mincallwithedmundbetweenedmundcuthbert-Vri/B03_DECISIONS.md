# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Partnership with Superposition

**DECISION:** Superposition will partner with Careerspan and use Careerspan Magic Links for candidate filtering and surfacing to employers.

**CONTEXT:** Vrijen presented the Magic Links technology which allows companies to generate links from job descriptions and have Careerspan act as a zero-touch ATS that only surfaces the top 2-5% of candidates that meet standards. Edmund agreed to the partnership approach.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen (Careerspan) and Edmund (Superposition) - mutual agreement.

**IMPLICATIONS:** This creates a pathway for both companies to access Marvin Ventures' 200 founders together. Superposition will use Careerspan's tech to filter candidates, and they'll present to customers as "Superposition powered by Careerspan."

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None explicitly discussed - this was the proposed direction that both agreed to.

## Decision 2: Technical Integration Flow

**DECISION:** Superposition's voice agent will extract maximal context from clients during onboarding, output JDs in the correct format that feeds into Careerspan to generate Magic Links.

**CONTEXT:** Edmund explained that Superposition's product is voice-based for onboarding and that clients don't actually look at JDs inside their product. This integration ensures the correct context flows through the system.

**DECIDED BY:** Edmund specified this technical requirement; Vrijen agreed.

**IMPLICATIONS:** This ensures the correct context flows through the system. Careerspan will get richer JDs than random ChatGPT output, which should maximize the efficacy of the filtering.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None mentioned.

## Decision 3: Attribution Flexibility

**DECISION:** Superposition is not precious about candidate attribution to the customer; they can present to customers that all candidates came from Careerspan if desired.

**CONTEXT:** Edmund emphasized that neither Superposition nor their customers care about precise attribution of where candidates came from. What matters is getting good candidates.

**DECIDED BY:** Edmund.

**IMPLICATIONS:** This provides flexibility in how the partnership is marketed. Careerspan can be positioned as the source of talent to customers, while Superposition will still track and do revenue sharing for candidates that actually came from Careerspan.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None mentioned.

## Decision 4: Experiment with Engineering Candidates

**DECISION:** Superposition will test Careerspan with 1-2 engineering roles as an experiment.

**CONTEXT:** Previously, Edmund had indicated Superposition didn't want to use Careerspan for engineers. However, after Vrijen presented the tech and explained how Careerspan excels at finding candidates who can't be reduced to keywords, Edmund agreed to experiment.

**DECIDED BY:** Edmund, after Vrijen's persuasive presentation.

**IMPLICATIONS:** This will test whether Careerspan can provide value for engineering candidates, which Edmund initially had doubts about. They'll select specific roles and A/B test with Careerspan links.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Waiting until Careerspan had PM/Designer roles (which Vrijen noted they actually have all types now).

## Decision 5: Experiment Selection Focus

**DECISION:** Focus experiments on roles where candidate suitability has "latent signals" that Careerspan's coaching conversations would reveal but that can't be filtered on paper.

**CONTEXT:** Edmund suggested forward-deployed engineers and go-to-market engineers as examples of roles where the right candidates don't necessarily have the title on paper but would engage with a career coaching product.

**DECIDED BY:** Edmund proposed this; Vrijen agreed enthusiastically.

**IMPLICATIONS:** This focuses the partnership on Careerspan's strengths - identifying candidates through qualitative data and conversation insights that keyword-based screening can't capture.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Testing generic engineering roles that could be filtered by keywords.

## Decision 6: Email Follow-up and LOI

**DECISION:** Vrijen will prepare an email summarizing the discussion and sketch out a lightweight letter of intent (LOI).

**CONTEXT:** Edmund requested an email summarizing the discussion. Vrijen suggested including some form of lightweight LOI to formalize the intent to work together.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen, at Edmund's request.

**IMPLICATIONS:** This will formalize the partnership intent and provide a document to share with teams and potential investors. It will include details about how Superposition will use Careerspan Magic Links.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None mentioned - this was the natural next step to document the agreement.

## Decision 7: Joint Marvin Ventures Pursuit

**DECISION:** Careerspan and Superposition will jointly pursue the Marvin Ventures partnership together.

**CONTEXT:** Vrijen is close to signing an LOI with Marvin Ventures (McKinsey alumni fund with 200 founders, 400 active roles). He proposed bringing Superposition in together to approach these founders.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen proposed; Edmund agreed.

**IMPLICATIONS:** This provides a credible entry point to Marvin Ventures' 200 founders, combining Vrijen's relationships with Superposition's credibility. Vrijen will lock down the Marvin Ventures deal.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Pursuing Marvin Ventures separately.

## Decision 8: Revenue Sharing Model

**DECISION:** Superposition will do a "decent revenue share" for candidates that actually came from Careerspan.

**CONTEXT:** Edmund outlined that Superposition charges a success fee and would share revenue with Careerspan for candidates sourced through their platform.

**DECIDED BY:** Edmund.

**IMPLICATIONS:** This aligns incentives and gives Careerspan "skin in the game." Edmund noted this structure might change once revenue is flowing.

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None mentioned.

## Strategic Direction Note (Not a Formal Decision)

**STRATEGIC DIRECTION (MENTIONED):** Edmund outlined three potential strategic paths for Careerspan: 1) Maximal company-side API play (become the picks and shovels for recruiting tools), 2) Maximal candidate-side AI talent agents (sports athlete style compensation), or 3) Find one niche where career coaching generates alpha for that job type. These were presented as options to consider, not decisions made in this meeting.
