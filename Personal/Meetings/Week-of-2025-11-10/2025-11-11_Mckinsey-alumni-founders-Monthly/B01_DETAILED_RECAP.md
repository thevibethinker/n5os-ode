# B01: Detailed Recap

## Overview
The "McKinsey Alumni Founders Monthly" meeting served as a roundtable for various startup founders to share updates, pitch their current ventures, and request specific help from the community. The discussion touched on data services, AI-driven productivity tools, conversational AI for sports/gaming, pediatric clinic automation, and agricultural technology.

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Data Services and AI Model Enhancement (Ahmed Rashad)
- Ahmed Rashad discussed his company's growth in the niche data labeling and enrichment space.
- He emphasized that they are not just a vendor but a partner in making AI models better by staffing experts from Mila, Stanford, and Berkeley.
- He noted that they often bid against themselves due to the high quality and specialization of their work.

### 2. Careerspan and AI Productivity (Vrijen Attawar)
- Vrijen introduced Careerspan, a coaching product for candidates and a matching tool for employers that bypasses traditional ATS resume reading.
- He highlighted his role as a brand evangelist for "Zoe," focusing on AI productivity and effective workflows for small teams (1-5 people).

### 3. Conversational AI for Fan Engagement (Abby - Season)
- Abby, CEO of Season, shared that they are building conversational AI for sports, gaming, and media to convert fans into long-term relationships.
- They are currently in a $1.5M pre-seed fundraise (half committed) and recently completed their YC application.
- She positioned the product as a non-e-commerce alternative to traditional CDPs like Braze or Klaviyo.

### 4. Pediatric Clinic Automation (Alex Chumbley)
- Alex is building AI agents to automate insurance operations and back-office tasks for pediatric clinics.
- He announced that they are under LOI with their first target clinic and are fundraising to close the deal within 3-4 weeks.

### 5. AI in Consulting and Voice Insights (Deeya Dsouza)
- Deeya is building tools to automate the role of an "FDE" (Full-Time Equivalent) in consulting.
- Her product uses voice AI to gather insights from frontline employees to calculate the ROI of automation.
- Her primary ICP includes boutique consulting firms and transformation leads in retail/consumer sectors.

### 6. Finance and Procurement Agents (Nikita Komarov - Dobzi)
- Nikita introduced Dobzi, which uses agents to eliminate leakages in finance and procurement (e.g., identifying overpayments and duplicate invoices).
- They have signed Uber and HelloFresh as clients and are exploring mid-sized business use cases for document-heavy processes.

### 7. Agricultural Technology (Olivier Ceberio - Sensing)
- Olivier discussed "Sensing," a handheld scanner for farmers to measure plant health and nutrients.
- They launched in 2025 and have already enabled 500,000 scans across 200 devices.
- Ulrik Soderstrom mentioned a potential strategic connection via a Chipotle fund investment that works with thousands of farms.
