# B01: Detailed Recap

The meeting focused on refining the marketing and messaging strategy for Careerspan, specifically addressing how to describe the technical and scientific aspects of the platform to different employer personas.

### Key Discussion Points:
- **Terminology & Buzzwords**: Ilya Kucherenko sought advice on appropriate "buzzwords" to describe the analysis engine. He explored terms like "psychometrics" and "empirical data."
- **Persona Alignment**: The team discussed tailoring the message for three distinct personas:
  1. **Early Adopters**: Interested primarily in the "AI" and "Machine Learning" aspects.
  2. **Scientific/Hard Science Skeptics**: Need to know the tool is backed by reliable methodology.
  3. **HR Professionals (Artisans)**: Value the human element and matchmaking artistry.
- **Rejecting Stack Ranking**: Vrijen emphasized a core Careerspan value: rejecting ranked lists in favor of "talent combinatorics" or "skill combinatorics," which focuses on the unique mix of skills and attitudes a candidate brings.
- **Seeing People at Their Best**: Vrijen highlighted that Careerspan allows HR professionals to see candidates at their best, moving away from a "factory farming" approach to hiring.
- **Soft Skills Quantification**: Ilse noted that Careerspan's ability to quantify soft skills is a relatively new phenomenon enabled by AI, as traditional peer-reviewed evidence for candidate analysis varies.
- **Video Content Progress**: Ilya is finalizing two storyboarded videos for employers:
  - **Version A**: Focuses on the technology and problem-solving.
  - **Version B**: Puts the buyer (HR professional) at the center of the story.
- **AI Tooling Transparency**: Ilya shared that he used ChatGPT to help refine a difficult paragraph in his scripts. Vrijen appreciated the transparency and noted it as a valid use case for overcoming creative blocks.
