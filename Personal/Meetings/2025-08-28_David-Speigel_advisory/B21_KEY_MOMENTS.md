## MEMORABLE QUOTES

### 1. "Everybody find your Lewis"
**Speaker:** David Speigel  
**Context:** Opening story about friendship group that takes annual trips. One group member (Lewis) is wealthy, doesn't need sleep, organizes everything. David saying you need someone like that or become that person yourself.  
**Why it mattered:** Sets tone of the meeting - practical wisdom about what actually works. Frames relationship-building as requiring coordination + commitment + often a "Lewis" to drive it.  
**What it reveals:** David values pragmatism over idealism. Also reveals he's self-aware about his own role-playing (trying to "become the Lewis in some other aspect").

### 2. "I try to explain to people that project management is hard and planning things into the future is hard, and it's easier to look back."
**Speaker:** David Speigel  
**Context:** Explaining why he can list 20 trips but it took 20 years.  
**Why it mattered:** Foundational insight about startup strategy. Easy to list accomplishments; hard to plan forward. Directly relevant to Careerspan founders who are in the thick of forward-planning.  
**What it reveals:** David thinks in time horizons; doesn't expect founders to have crystal clarity. Sets realistic expectations for strategic planning.

### 3. "I don't want better information. The most necessary thing is like better sources of candidates."
**Speaker:** Hiring manager feedback (cited by Logan)  
**Context:** After weeks of conversations with hiring managers/recruiters, this was the key insight Logan extracted.  
**Why it mattered:** Invalidates Careerspan's assumption that information richness is the value prop. Reframes the problem: supply-side scarcity, not data scarcity.  
**What it reveals:** Logan and team have been listening carefully to customer signals. This quote shows they're willing to challenge their own product roadmap.

### 4. "My wife puts it this way: David, all those accomplishments you had today sound great, but like, how many checks are you getting?"
**Speaker:** David Speigel (quoting his wife)  
**Context:** In response to Vrijen's comment about the startup journey being "how close can two people be to success without actually being successful?"  
**Why it mattered:** Grounds the conversation in business fundamentals. Startup accomplishments ≠ revenue. Wife is the reality check.  
**What it reveals:** David has supportive partner who keeps him honest. Also suggests he's worn this tension before (high-risk companies).

### 5. "Consult to learn, productize to earn."
**Speaker:** David Speigel  
**Context:** When founders described manual-first approach with employers before building features.  
**Why it mattered:** Crystallizes GTM philosophy in one phrase. Logan immediately echoed it.

### 6. "We want to be incredibly focused and carry out one hypothesis to a strong conclusion. I think our strength is that we sort of are very fluid. I think it's also a weakness in that we probably want to let things bake a little bit before hard course correcting."

## SALIENT QUESTIONS

### 1. **"Are you clear on your main ICPs and then do you have a channel to them and do you have feedback from them that they're pulling from you versus you selling and pushing to them?"**
**Asked by:** David Speigel  
**Strategic importance:** CRITICAL. This is the framework David uses to assess startups. Three tests: ICP clarity, channel access, pull vs. push dynamics.  
**Action hint:** Founders need to validate all three with evidence. Currently ICP is "shaky" on employer side; channel access unclear on hiring manager side.

### 2. **"Are you a feature or are you a standalone bolted on piece of the process? ...Are you a feature or a standalone bolted on piece of the process? Because you don't want like Greenhouse or those other companies like adding enough features of what you do..."**
**Asked by:** David Speigel  
**Strategic importance:** HIGH. Competitive positioning question. Careerspan could be absorbed by incumbent ATS if not careful.  
**Action hint:** Must position as supply-side player, not information player. Otherwise Greenhouse can copy in 6 months.

### 3. **"Which community? On the community side. Like what. What types of communities?"**
**Asked by:** David Speigel (Clarifying)  
**Strategic importance:** MEDIUM. David was checking if founders had thought through community segmentation.  
**Action hint:** Good news - they have. Product managers, product designers specifically. Bad news - not yet validated at scale.

### 4. **"But then within there you're also like, I use this example a lot, especially with startups is, you know, the founder of TurboTax, the person who invented TurboTax said, Other software is not my biggest competitor, the pencil is."**
**Asked by:** David Speigel  
**Strategic importance:** HIGH. Forces founders to think about true competition (not just ATS platforms, but status quo: manual recruiting, LinkedIn, etc).  
**Action hint:** Must understand what employers currently do without Careerspan. What's the "pencil"?

### 5. **"If you're working with well funded... If you're looking at PMs then you're talking about tech and if you're talking about tech and you want early stage, then you're looking at VC funded tech startups. Like that's what it is. ...what are they told to do?"**
**Asked by:** David Speigel  
**Strategic importance:** HIGH. Bowling pin strategy - what do VCs tell founders to do? Creates natural channel for Careerspan.  
**Action hint:** Get on VC recommended vendor lists. Partner with placement firms that already have VC credibility.

### 6. **"Can I pause you for a second? ...I want to back up before we dive in that deep [into product features]."**
**Asked by:** David Speigel  
**Strategic importance:** MEDIUM (meta-question about conversation flow). But signals David's prioritization: validation before building.  
**Action hint:** Product roadmap discussions come AFTER product-market fit is validated.

## Discussion Pattern

David asked clarifying questions throughout to:
1. **Challenge assumptions** ("Are you clear on your main ICPs?")
2. **Expand perspective** (TurboTax "pencil" analogy; bowling pin strategy)
3. **Force strategic prioritization** (which segment first?)
4. **Check competitive positioning** (feature vs. standalone?)

Founders were receptive - they paused product talk, answered questions, and incorporated feedback immediately. No defensiveness observed.
