# Stakeholder Research

**Perspective:** Speaking as community founder + job market observer during 2023-2025 recession

---

## INSIGHT 1: Job Market Collapse Has Inverted Candidate Pool Composition & Urgency

**Title:** VP-level, over-qualified professionals now dominate job search - not junior roles or "underqualified" candidates. These experienced professionals are desperate for support and willing to invest time in genuine career work.

**Evidence:** "The vast majority are people that are between 35 and 55, vice president level, multiple master's degrees. They don't need resume help. They don't need someone to tell them, like, they worked at high companies. They almost all manage teams...so it's this very actively engaged group of people that are continuing to be actively engaged in the lowest time of their lives."

**Why it matters:** Job market disruption isn't affecting junior roles most—it's hollowing out mid/senior career stability. These professionals are high-signal candidates for employers BUT most are invisible in traditional ATS systems. This inverts the usual recruiting problem (too many juniors, not enough seniors).

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ● ● (Game-changing insight; very specific data from 200+ community surveys; confirmed by Vrijen's observation that "9 out of 10 founders don't look at inbound anymore")

**Category:** Job Seeker Pain Points | Market Dynamics

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Giovanna Ventola (ref: B08)
- **Relevant experience:** 1.5 years running Rise community; surveys every member entering; three personal layoffs across recession period; networks directly with 200+ VP-level professionals
- **Source type:** PRIMARY (direct operational observation)
- **Firsthand?** Yes - she's measuring this directly in her community
- **Weight justification:** VERY HIGH - this is primary market data from an active community, not anecdotal. Contradicts common "entry-level job market is hardest" assumption.

## INSIGHT 2: ATS Systems + Auto-Apply Functionality Have Destroyed All Signal from Resumes

**Title:** The confluence of ATS keyword-matching + one-click auto-apply tools has made resumes useless as hiring signals. Quality candidates are buried in floods of 20,000+ applications per role. No correlation anymore between resume quality and candidate quality.

**Evidence:** "21,000 applications for a generalist role...because the ATS systems there, people now want to beat the ATS so they fire off their whatever easy apply, easy. [And] ChatGPT ruining the recruiting side of things." Also: "People would...put in white text on the sides they'd put their keywords...you mean to tell me someone's gonna get through because they hid words on a page?...And then they'd be people that are unqualified for that actual role."

**Why it matters:** The entire traditional hiring funnel (resume screening) is broken. Employers get overwhelmed with noise. Candidates who game the system actually harm both sides by creating unqualified candidates in interview rounds. BOTH sides know this but the system persists because there's no alternative infrastructure.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ● ● (Validated by multiple sources; contradicts "better resume" narrative; appears industry-wide, not just Giovanna's view)

**Category:** Hiring Manager Pain Points | Market Dynamics

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Giovanna Ventola (ref: B08)
- **Relevant experience:** Direct observation from Rise community members who are applying; conversations with recruiters; personal experience across three job searches post-2020
- **Source type:** PRIMARY (direct observation of both job seeker AND recruiter side through her network)
- **Firsthand?** Yes - her community members report on their applications; she's interviewed recruiters/hiring managers
- **Weight justification:** VERY HIGH - she's observed this from both sides of the market. Frames the core opportunity for Careerspan (if resumes are useless, what replaces them?).

## INSIGHT 3: Professional Communities Solve Isolation Problem Better Than Traditional Platforms, But Lack Monetization Infrastructure

**Title:** When isolated job-seekers find peer community (Rise via TikTok, then private community), engagement and resilience dramatically increase. Community members are "actively engaged in the lowest time of their lives" and willing to invest in learning/growth. BUT monetizing community without destroying trust requires separating the community platform (free, peer-support) from the matching/monetization layer (infrastructure provider like Careerspan).

**Evidence:** "TikTok gave me that real human connection with multiple people across the country...it's this very actively engaged group of people...they want to use the time to figure out the next best move forward for them...I think those people are the biggest asset for companies because if you can continue to show up every day, be resilient, you have no one to answer to but yourself, then you're going to be the next best employee."

**Why it matters:** Communities outperform platforms at generating authentic engagement and trust. But community founders hit a wall: monetizing kills trust if not structured carefully. Careerspan positioned as "infrastructure" (not competitor to community, but service layer) unblocks this.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ● ○ (Very specific to Rise model; actionable; requires validation on scalability)

**Category:** Community Owner Pain Points | Product Strategy

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Giovanna Ventola (ref: B08)
- **Relevant experience:** Built Rise from zero to 200+ engaged members; personally experienced the tension of wanting to monetize without selling out
- **Source type:** PRIMARY (direct operational experience building exactly this dynamic)
- **Firsthand?** Yes - she's built the community and is navigating monetization decisions right now
- **Weight justification:** VERY HIGH - this is foundational insight for community-based talent networks. Directly applicable to Careerspan's GTM strategy.

## INSIGHT 4: Credibility In Career Advice Requires Lived Experience of Struggle; Generic/Theoretical Advice Is Rejected Regardless of Source

**Title:** Career advice from people who haven't personally experienced job search crisis, layoffs, or career transition is categorically rejected by this professional population. Real human connection + shared vulnerability creates trust; credentials/titles create skepticism.

**Evidence:** "I will not take advice from somebody who hasn't gone through it. You cannot give me career advice if you've never been laid off, if you've never struggled, if you've never been at rock bottom...the recruiters [on TikTok] were like, 'here's how you get a job in this market'—you've been employed for 20 years by the same person. You can't give me advice."

**Why it matters:** Positions this as a market where authenticity/vulnerability are competitive advantages. Generic career coaches lose. People who share real, often messy stories win. This explains Rise's success (Giovanna shares her struggles) and why it's hard to sell traditional career coaching to this population.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ● ○ (Very specific observation; strong conviction from Giovanna; aligns with Rise's differentiation)

**Category:** Job Seeker Pain Points | GTM Strategy

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Giovanna Ventola (ref: B08)
- **Relevant experience:** Has built a following on TikTok by sharing authentically; has engaged 200+ professionals who consciously reject traditional advice sources
- **Source type:** PRIMARY (direct observation of which advice sources her community trusts/rejects)
- **Firsthand?** Yes - she's testing this in real-time in her community
- **Weight justification:** VERY HIGH - actionable insight for Careerspan positioning. Suggests Vrijen's authenticity (sharing his own MBA journey) is more valuable than typical founder narrative.

## INSIGHT 5: Information Asymmetry About Certification ROI Is Destroying Trust In Upskilling

**Title:** Job seekers in transition are desperate to invest their time in high-ROI learning (new certifications, skills, degrees) but NO ONE can tell them what actually generates job search returns. Without transparent data on wages/hiring lift from different credentials, professionals waste time/money on low-signal certifications.

**Evidence:** "Nobody knows what is worth spending time or money on...I wasn't going to pay to go get a degree in something that I wasn't sure if it would get me a job...people that have paid for different coaches, resume writers, all this stuff and they get nothing."

**Why it matters:** Creates opportunity for tools that surface outcome transparency (Logan's "transparent certification checker" showing wage gains from different credentials). Also validates Careerspan's approach: giving candidates real outcome feedback (e.g., "this person who got hired had X certification") is genuinely valuable.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (Valid pain point; specific to upskilling gap; but hasn't been validated as market-size problem)

**Category:** Job Seeker Pain Points | Product Strategy

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Giovanna Ventola (ref: B08)
- **Relevant experience:** Observes Rise community members struggling with this decision; has personally struggled with it across three transitions
- **Source type:** PRIMARY (direct observation)
- **Firsthand?** Yes - she's living this and her community reports it constantly
- **Weight justification:** HIGH - real pain point, but needs validation on whether there's sufficient market demand for this to be a product lever.
