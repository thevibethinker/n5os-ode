## Strategic Context

Both Careerspan and Rise are solving the same fundamental problem: how to surface authentic career narrative (not ATS-optimized keywords) to make hiring/job-seeking more efficient. The job market crisis mentioned by Logan (last 6 months) has shifted Careerspan from university partnerships to employer direct outreach—exactly where Rise's community becomes valuable. Giovanna has built a trust-based community of 35-55 year old professionals (VP-level, multiple master's degrees) who are actively engaged during transition—high signal for employers. The tension both identified: traditional LinkedIn/resume approach fails both sides. Careerspan + Rise could solve this if the integration feels natural to Rise's mission (support community, not monetize it).

Giovanna's positioning as a non-recruiter community founder is strategically important—she's solved the "how do we actually connect with real people" problem via TikTok and community, but she lacks recruiting/matching infrastructure. Careerspan has exactly that. The monetization opportunity for Giovanna (revenue share on placements) only makes sense if Careerspan handles the matching work.
