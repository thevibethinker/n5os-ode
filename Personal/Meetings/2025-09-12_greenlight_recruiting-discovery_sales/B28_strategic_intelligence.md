## Strategic Insights

### What This Meeting Reveals
1. **Market validation:** The "looks good on paper, fails technical screen" problem is widespread and acute enough that a head of eng at Autodesk with 8+ years coasted and bombed their interview. This is a significant market pain point.

2. **Positioning opportunity:** Careerspan's semantic matching directly addresses Greenlight's stated pain, but the demo didn't include a technical depth validation component. Opportunity to emphasize how the system pre-filters for technical substance, not just keyword matching.

3. **Competitive landscape:** Lenny's List quality degradation as scale increases is a known phenomenon. Careerspan's curation model could be selling point, but needs proof it won't suffer same fate.

4. **GTM gap:** Careerspan starting with product/eng only, but Greenlight hiring profile is multidisciplinary (mechanical engineers, sales, ops). Either expand talent pool scope or accept narrow wedge entry and prove value before expanding.

### What Matters Most to Greenlight
- **Quality bar over volume:** Explicit preference for higher filter threshold upfront
- **Technical validation at scale:** Can't ask candidates to do tech screens without talking to a human first, but can't waste eng time on weak candidates
- **Referral-quality signals:** The gold standard is someone you know vouching for someone. How do you replicate that systematically?
- **Bandwidth efficiency:** Allie has 22 roles and is learning. Ed is screening all tech. They need systems that reduce their cognitive load, not add to it.
