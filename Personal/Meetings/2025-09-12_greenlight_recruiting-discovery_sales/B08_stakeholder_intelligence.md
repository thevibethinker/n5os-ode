# B08: Stakeholder Intelligence

## Foundational Profile - Allie Cialeo

**Name:** Allie Cialeo  
**Title:** Head of Talent  
**Organization:** Greenlight Simulation  
**Tenure:** Joined mid-July 2025 (~2 months at time of meeting)  

**Background Context:**
- Previous role involved using Lenny's List in agency capacity for recruiting
- Experienced with evaluating recruiting solutions and vendor relationships
- Currently managing 22 open roles across multidisciplinary company
- Building talent systems from relatively green field (post-fundraise expansion)

## What Resonated

### 1. Network Quality Degradation at Scale
**Quote:** "At my last role, we use them [Lenny's List] in more of like an agency capacity... I think it just got almost too big. I'm curious, like, how you navigate like one of these, like a space that truly is like really great, exceptional talent in one place, and then also how you prevent it from being super saturated."

**Why it matters:** This reveals Allie's mental model - she's seen "exceptional" talent networks decline as they scale. She's not naive about brand names or community credentials. She wants proof that any solution (including Careerspan) has sustainable quality mechanisms.

**Signal/Implication:** Allie will be skeptical of "we partner with top communities" pitches without understanding the curation layer. She's probably seen vendors over-promise on quality and under-deliver. Careerspan needs to specifically address how semantic matching prevents the quality dilution she's experienced.

### 2. Upfront Filtering Preference
**Quote:** "I think the upfront screening is a big part of it. I want to... the recruiters at the top of the funnel are super helpful in a volume perspective and just being able to see like a diverse set of candidates... and then figuring out a little deeper in the funnel, like after Ed's call, like where we need them to hit more deeply."

**Why it matters:** Allie values high-quality pre-filtering but hasn't fully defined her criteria yet. She's in learning mode, building mental models of what "good" looks like for Greenlight's specific needs.

**Signal/Implication:** Early stage opportunity to shape her evaluation framework. If Careerspan can demonstrate value on one high-priority role and help Allie articulate what quality looks like for Greenlight, they become trusted advisor rather than just vendor. This is classic "land and expand" setup.

### 3. Self-Awareness About Process Immaturity
**Quote:** "I think it's kind of early in the funnel to see to know what we are today. But I think the upfront screening is a big part of it... he'll send feedback on why he likes them and like I'm kind of mental mapping remembering all of it."

**Why it matters:** Allie explicitly states she's still learning Ed's preferences and building her own recruiting playbook. This isn't a buyer with rigid requirements - this is someone in formation mode.

**Signal/Implication:** Patience and education will win here more than aggressive sales. Allie needs time to evaluate her current approach (agencies), learn what works, and then identify gaps. Careerspan should position as long-term partner who helps her build systems, not just a transactional vendor. Check in 30-60 days.

### 4. Bandwidth Constraints
**Quote:** "My first focus from James and Ben has been on more so sales and our operations team, like, the client services side of things, just because those were the most important, the most urgent. I just started digging into tech about a week ago."

**Why it matters:** Allie is underwater. 22 roles, 2 months in, prioritizing sales/ops over tech. Tech hiring only got attention "about a week ago." This explains why she's leaning on agencies - she literally doesn't have bandwidth to do anything else.

**Signal/Implication:** Any solution Careerspan offers needs to reduce Allie's cognitive load, not add to it. The "candidates spend a month filling out profile, you scan in a second" pitch directly addresses this. But Allie won't evaluate pilots that require heavy lift from her. Offer white-glove service for pilot, prove ROI on time savings, then expand.

### 5. Future Internal Hire Consideration
**Quote:** "My goal is to bring on a tech recruiter or a recruiter who maybe leans more technical could also help with my side of the business because I think it's kind of interesting that we also have like mechanical engineers and things like that on our team."

**Why it matters:** Allie is thinking about hiring an internal tech recruiter. This could be competitive to Careerspan (internal recruiter might prefer traditional sourcing tools) or complementary (internal recruiter uses Careerspan as platform).

**Signal/Implication:** Careerspan should position as recruiter-enablement platform, not recruiter-replacement. When Allie hires that tech recruiter, they should be excited to use Careerspan because it makes their job easier (better candidate quality, less time screening). Frame this proactively.

- Allie is in information-gathering mode, not buying mode. She just started agency partnerships and needs to evaluate those first
- Real budget exists (post-raise), real pain exists (22 roles, quality issues), real decision-maker engaged (Allie owns talent decisions)
- But timing is 30-60+ days out minimum. She needs to see agency performance, potentially hire internal recruiter, then reassess
- A-2 reflects "qualified but not active deal" status - keep warm, check in monthly, offer pilot when timing improves

## Relationship Strategy

**Short-term (0-30 days):**
- Send thoughtful follow-up addressing quality/scale question specifically
- Share case study or data on how semantic matching maintains quality as network grows
- Light touch - don't push for meetings, just stay valuable

**Medium-term (30-60 days):**
- Check in on agency performance
- Offer targeted pilot on one specific role (SRE or senior ML engineer) with white-glove service
- Request James facilitate intro to Ed for technical validation conversation

**Long-term (60-90+ days):**
- Position as partner for when Allie hires internal tech recruiter
- Expand conversation beyond product/eng to address Greenlight's multidisciplinary needs
- Explore strategic partnership if pilot proves value

**Key Success Factor:** Allie values thoughtfulness, data, and low cognitive burden. She's allergic to overselling and vendor noise. Earn trust through insight and patience, not aggressive follow-up.

**Name:** Paul Lee  
**Title:** [Not stated, but involved in early eng hiring before Head of Engineering joined]

**Background Context:**

- Now mostly out of hiring loop, but has strong opinions from direct experience
- Technical credibility - conducted or reviewed technical screens firsthand

## What Resonated

### 1. Referral Networks as Gold Standard
**Quote:** "For me, I'm always, like, always referral as king. But just like, yeah, it's definitely been hard. Just, like, meet somebody completely random who's, like, amazing, and it doesn't come through some network or at least has some level of backlink from somebody."

**Why it matters:** Paul has seen the delta between referred candidates and "random" candidates. He trusts networks and backlinking. This is the fundamental challenge Careerspan claims to solve.

**Signal/Implication:** Paul is the technical validator type. If Careerspan can demonstrate that their semantic matching + community curation replicates referral-quality signals at scale, Paul becomes internal champion. He understands the problem deeply because he's lived it.

### 2. Technical Screening Pain
**Quote:** "Our engineers really was, like, the worst technical screen they've ever done in their life. This person's probably just been coasting there."

**Why it matters:** Paul isn't exaggerating - he's describing real, acute pain. Staff engineer at Autodesk, 8+ years, completely bombed technical screen. This isn't edge case, it's pattern.

**Signal/Implication:** Paul will be receptive to any solution that pre-filters for technical depth, not just credentials. But he'll be skeptical until proven. Careerspan should offer to show how their system would have flagged this candidate vs. surface-level resume screen.

### 3. Market Dynamics Insight
**Quote:** "I think there's a lot of just, like, cutting of unnecessary bloat... those people are kind of starting to bubble up more in interviews and like, kind of be exposed."

**Why it matters:** Paul has a sophisticated read on the post-RIF market. He understands that impressive tenures at brand-name companies no longer signal quality because many of those people were coasting.

**Signal/Implication:** Paul thinks like a product person - he sees market dynamics and adjusts mental models accordingly. He'll respect vendors who demonstrate similar market understanding and offer solutions calibrated to current reality, not pre-2023 hiring environment.

## CRM Tags

**LD-INFLUENCE:** Technical influencer, no longer primary hiring decision-maker but has credibility  
**GPT-AWARE:** Aware of problem and solution space, not actively evaluating but supportive  
**A-1:** Passive - engaged in conversation but not driving decision

**Rationale:**  
Paul is valuable as technical validator and potential internal champion, but he's no longer in the decision-making path for hiring (Ed owns that now). He's worth maintaining relationship with because:
1. He has direct experience with the pain Careerspan solves
2. He has technical credibility that Allie respects
3. He could be advocate if Careerspan proves value on a pilot

But he's not the person to sell to. Focus on Allie (decision-maker) and eventually Ed (technical gatekeeper). Paul is supporter, not buyer.
