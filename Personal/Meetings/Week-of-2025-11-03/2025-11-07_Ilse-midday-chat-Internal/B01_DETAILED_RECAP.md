# B01: Detailed Recap

## Overview
V and Ilse discussed the evolution of Careerspan's value proposition, moving from a complex behavioral matching narrative to a concrete, low-friction "we give you a list of high-quality candidates from specific communities" approach. The conversation addressed Ilse's concerns about the gap between resume-based data and qualitative reality, and the strategic decision to prioritize market entry over perfecting a matching algorithm.

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Framing Candidate Value
- **Story Score Impact**: Discussed showing how telling stories raises a candidate's score without making it look like the score is "irrationally" boosted.
- **Resume vs. Reality**: V hypothesized that candidates with soft skills and "dimensions" not captured by resumes are where Careerspan adds the most value.
- **Employer Framing**: Options discussed: framing candidate boosts as "correcting for resume BS" or "shining through despite the resume."

### 2. The Shift to "Mid-Signal" Alternative
- **Market Positioning**: Careerspan is positioning itself as a "mid-signal" alternative between low-signal referrals/job boards and high-cost recruiters.
- **Simplicity in Sales**: V noted that the "tangible and concrete" offer of a candidate list is getting more traction than the "behaviorally based matching" pitch.
- **Employer Commitment**: Aiming for 5 minutes of setup and 10 minutes a week for hiring managers to review 5 candidates.

### 3. Supply and Pool Management
- **Community Sourcing**: Leveraging the "community landscape" (e.g., product communities) as a primary source of quality candidates.
- **Talent Pool Depth**: Ilse expressed concern about the need for an "incumbent talent pool." V countered that they can "swarm these communities" and mentioned an upcoming call with the founder of Rise to solidify a community partnership.
- **Pacing Recommendations**: Strategy to space out candidate submissions to prevent "crowding each other out" and maintain long-term engagement (2+ weeks).

### 4. Strategic Alignment
- **Pragmatism vs. Idealism**: Both acknowledged a shared dislike for sales but agreed on its necessity to build a viable business.
- **Confidence Building**: V emphasized his increasing command of the sales narrative compared to previous weeks, aiming to reassure Ilse of the project's direction.
