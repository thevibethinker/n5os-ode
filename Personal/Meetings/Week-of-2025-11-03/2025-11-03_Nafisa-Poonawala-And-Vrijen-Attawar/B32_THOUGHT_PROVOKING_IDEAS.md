# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

## The "Show of Faith" Distribution Model
V's idea to give away his entire architectural setup (Personas/Rules) instead of a traditional "installation file." 
- **Core Idea:** In an agentic world, "installation" isn't deterministic; it's about "teaching the AI" to achieve an effect.
- **Provocation:** Does the value of an AI system lie in its specific code/prompts, or in the "ingestion function" that allows it to evolve within a user's unique environment?

## The "Jared Dunn" Standard
Referencing a beloved, hyper-reliable, yet non-threatening Chief of Staff character.
- **Core Idea:** AI personas shouldn't just be "useful tools"; they should embody archetypes that users already understand and trust.
- **Provocation:** Can "likability" and "character" in an AI system be a primary driver of technical adoption?

## Stress as a Bio-Signal (Health & Tech Parallel)
The discussion on cortisol and "deranged" reports despite external discipline.
- **Core Idea:** High performance (in people or systems) often masks underlying inflammation/stress that isn't visible via simple activity metrics (like exercise or "grinding").
- **Provocation:** How can we build "health checks" for AI systems that measure the "cortisol" (technical debt/complexity) before the system becomes "deranged"?
