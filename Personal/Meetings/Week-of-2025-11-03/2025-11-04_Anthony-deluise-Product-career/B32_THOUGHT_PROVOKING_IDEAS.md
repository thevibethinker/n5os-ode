# B32 Thought Provoking Ideas

- **Job Hunting as a Perishable Skill**: The idea that job seeking is a set of pedagogical skills that expire every few years, making traditional static advice ineffective.
- **The "Application Meat Grinder"**: The systemic failure of using automated filters to select humans, and the resulting necessity of "high agency" to bypass it.
- **UX Gap in Institutional Sales**: The specific tension in EdTech where the user (student) has Silicon Valley expectations but the buyer (university admin) has antiquated standards.
- **Inventory vs. Resume**: Shifting the focus from a broad "what can I do" to a specific "stock take of capabilities" to identify the most achievable next steps.
- **AI Automation Bias**: The risk of AI developers solving "fake problems" (like auto-applying) because they don't understand that experienced professionals value networking and specific research over volume.
