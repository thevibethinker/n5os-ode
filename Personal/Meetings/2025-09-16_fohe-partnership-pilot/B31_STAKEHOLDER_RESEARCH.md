## Perspective

Speaking as: **Community platform leader** (FOHE community strategy) and **Job market participant** (understanding hiring ecosystem from both job seeker and employer perspectives)

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Employer Hiring Systems Are Broken (Hiring Landscape)

**Observation:**
Employers are no longer looking at inbound applications. Major companies (Logan cited: Zapier head of recruiting) are using internal tools only, or posting jobs purely for legal compliance with zero intent to actually review applications.

**Why It Matters:**
This signals a fundamental market shift. Resume-based filtering is obsolete. Employers are desperate for alternative sourcing because their own systems can't surface good candidates. The ATS is broken.

**Strategic Value:**
- Opportunity for alternative sourcing models (like Careerspan)
- Community platforms (like FOHE) become distribution channels because employers want direct access to curated talent
- Job aggregators become increasingly obsolete; employers want quality over volume
- Career platforms need to solve employer pain, not just job seeker pain

**Action for FOHE:**
Position pilot as "curated opportunities with employer introductions" not "job listings." This aligns with employer need (quality sourcing) and job seeker need (better matches).

---

### 2. Resume-Matching AI Commoditizes Traditional Hiring (Market Dynamics)

**Observation:**
AI can now generate perfect resume matches automatically. When all you're doing is keyword matching, the entire traditional ATS filtering system becomes worthless. This commoditization is accelerating.

**Why It Matters:**
Resume as a differentiator is dying. This creates opportunity for platforms that go beyond resume (like Careerspan's story-based approach). But it also means hiring will fragment away from centralized job boards.

**Strategic Value:**
- Career platforms must evolve beyond resume hosting
- Soft skills and context become MORE valuable (hard to automate)
- Non-technical/career transitioners are MOST hurt by resume-only systems → They're the people who benefit most from story-based matching
- Hiring is fragmenting: employers using internal tools, referrals, and niche sourcing

**Action for FOHE:**
This is exactly why FOHE members (likely non-technical or career transitioners) benefit from story-based matching. FOHE can position as solving a real market problem: "Resumes don't work anymore; we're offering something better."

---

### 3. FOHE's Role Evolution: From Network to Distribution (Community Strategy)

**Observation:**
FOHE is being positioned not just as a member benefit (job discovery) but as a distribution channel for curated opportunities. FOHE becomes infrastructure for employers to reach members. Revenue sharing model proposed (if aggregation paywall activated).

**Why It Matters:**
This is a strategic opportunity for FOHE to monetize community trust. But it also creates risk: if FOHE becomes just a job aggregator, community trust diminishes. Quality must remain the differentiator.

**Strategic Value:**
- Community platforms can be valuable distribution channels if positioned as curators, not aggregators
- Trust is the asset: maintain quality standards or lose community credibility
- Revenue opportunity: FOHE could become a meaningful revenue source for Careerspan; Careerspan could become a meaningful benefit for FOHE
- Partnership model (not one-off integration): Ongoing collaboration on supply, messaging, community education

**Action for FOHE:**
Define FOHE's role clearly in pilot: Are we a distribution channel (passive)? Or a curator (active selection of roles/members)? Curator role is higher value but requires more work.

---

### 4. Job Seekers Want Outcomes, Not Access (Job Seeker Behavior)

**Observation:**
Careerspan has ~50% day-30 retention for users receiving job recommendations, with 80-90% open rates and 12-15% click-through rates. These are strong numbers. It indicates job seekers want actual opportunities, not just job listings.

**Why It Matters:**
This suggests the matching is working. Job seekers feel like recommendations are relevant. This is different from typical job board churn (where people apply randomly to 100 jobs and hear nothing).

**Strategic Value:**
- Quality matching creates retention: People stay engaged if recommendations feel genuine
- Low-volume, high-relevance is better than high-volume, low-relevance
- Community-based distribution amplifies this: FOHE members get recommendations, they're more likely to be high-signal, retention is even better
- This is a data advantage: FOHE can provide richer member profiles → Careerspan can make better matches → Better outcomes

**Action for FOHE:**
Frame pilot around outcomes: "We're testing if curated opportunities + story-based matching leads to better outcomes for members." This positions as outcome-focused, not just feature-focused.

---

### 5. Employer Relationship Economics Are Fragmenting (Sourcing Market)

**Observation:**
Careerspan is pricing at $300-500 per intro (current), targeting 10% of annual salary (future standard). This is between inbound (free but low quality) and full recruiters (15-30% of salary). Comparable companies (Jack and Jill) using same 10% model. Multiple pricing models coexisting.

**Why It Matters:**
The sourcing market is stratifying. There's no longer one model (recruiters). Instead, multiple models (referrals, internal tools, niche platforms, recruiters) are coexisting. Employers are buying multiple sourcing channels simultaneously.

**Strategic Value:**
- Specialization wins: Careerspan's focus on story-based matching + community distribution is a niche that recruiters aren't filling
- Pricing power comes from outcomes: If Careerspan can show better hire quality, $500/intro becomes cheap relative to recruiter cost + risk
- Distribution partnerships (like FOHE) become high-value: Pre-filtered, curated candidates reduce employer risk and cost
- Market is growing: As resume-based systems fail, employers will increase spend on alternative sourcing

**Action for FOHE:**
Position partnership as "premium sourcing channel" not "job board." Employers will pay for quality + curation because that's what works now.

---

### 6. Soft Skills Are the New Competitive Advantage (Market Positioning)

**Observation:**
Career transitioners and non-technical roles (common in FOHE, likely) have qualitative strengths that resumes don't capture. Story-based platforms make this visible. Employers increasingly realize they need soft skills + technical skills.

**Why It Matters:**
Traditional hiring filters out people with strong soft skills but thin resume. As technical skills commoditize (AI can do more), soft skills become MORE valuable. This creates an opportunity for platforms that surface them.

**Strategic Value:**
- FOHE members (likely diverse backgrounds, strong soft skills) are undervalued by resume-based systems
- Story-based matching becomes a major advantage: FOHE members get discovered by employers looking for soft skills + execution ability
- This is especially valuable for career transitioners: Can show why their previous experience is relevant despite title change
- Employer benefit: Get access to undervalued talent pool (less competition, potentially higher quality)

**Action for FOHE:**
Position pilot as "Help members tell the story that resumes can't capture." This is about empowerment, not just job matching.

---

## Synthesis: The Hiring Market is Fragmenting

**Core Insight:** The days of centralized job boards are ending. Hiring is fragmenting into:
1. **Internal tools** (employers prefer this)
2. **Referrals** (highest quality, but limited supply)
3. **Niche platforms** (specialized sourcing: Careerspan, etc.)
4. **Recruiters** (expensive, declining market share)

FOHE + Careerspan can win in this fragmented market by being a niche platform that combines:
- **Curator advantage** (FOHE trust + curation)
- **Matching advantage** (story-based, not resume-based)
- **Outcome focus** (measured by placements, not clicks)
- **Community advantage** (pre-filtered, motivated candidates)

This is NOT about becoming a job aggregator. It's about becoming a specialized sourcing channel that employers prefer because it works.
