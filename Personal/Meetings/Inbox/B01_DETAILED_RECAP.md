# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
An advisory call between Careerspan (Vrijen, Logan) and Merisenda from ACE Fitness, transitioning from personal reconnection to strategic AI consultation. The discussion focused on ACE's upcoming AI initiatives and Careerspan sharing hard-earned lessons from building an AI product, including hiring strategies, technical decisions, and cost management.

## Chronological Discussion

### Opening and Personal Catch-up (0:00-4:20)
The meeting began with Vrijen and Merisenda reconnecting. Merisenda shared that she had moved from Las Vegas to California and was thriving at ACE Fitness, appreciating her team and work-life balance. She mentioned she had been experimenting with AI tools including Copilot, ChatGPT, and AI features from vendors, and had begun leading pilot programs to help shape ACE's AI strategy.

### ACE Fitness AI Strategy (4:20-13:14)
Vrijen asked about ACE's AI roadmap. Merisenda explained their vision of using AI as a "study coach or mentor" for fitness professionals at various career stages. The strategy involves creating a content repository for just-in-time learning segments—such as explaining planes of movement with video demonstrations—and progressing to interactive role-playing scenarios where learners practice client interactions.

Vrijen then shared a live demonstration of Zo, describing it as a "computer in the cloud that you control by prompting." He showed how Zo integrates data from 23andMe and Fitbit to provide personalized fitness coaching based on his genetic profile and daily vitals. He emphasized that the wow moment for him was having an AI system that already possessed his learner profile and could compound knowledge over time, rather than just providing one-off information.

Vrijen also showcased Zo's "personas" feature, specifically the "vibe teacher," which tracks learning progress and extends understanding with follow-up ideas. He argued that the competitive advantage in AI will come from dynamic, two-way relationships with users—where the system truly understands their needs—rather than one-directional information delivery.

### Careerspan's AI Build Experience (13:14-26:05)
Merisenda asked for advice on building AI products, specifically around internal vs. external development. Vrijen strongly advocated for having at least one strong technical/product leader in-house, even if all engineering is outsourced. He explained that this person needs enough technical expertise to hold developers accountable and enough product intuition to guide the build.

Vrijen shared that Careerspan had hired Ilse as both CTO and Head of AI, which proved invaluable. He offered a key hiring insight: avoid "director-level" candidates who may have lost touch with actual coding. Instead, look for builders with recent individual contribution experience. He also noted that companies don't need their own models or fine-tuning—existing large language models are sufficient for most use cases.

Logan joined the call around 16:19, briefly catching up before reinforcing Vrijen's points. He agreed that you don't need a team of PhD-level AI experts, and noted that both he and Vrijen had become comfortable with AI tools despite starting non-technical.

On costs, Vrijen revealed that approximately 80% of Careerspan's development spend went to salaries. He advised watching unit economics from the outset, warning against starting with unlimited pricing and then shifting to usage-based models once costs become apparent—a common failure mode.

Merisenda mentioned that ACE doesn't currently have a CTO. Vrijen recommended that even when hiring a technical lead, they should secure a technical advisor to oversee the interview process to ensure the candidate actually has the skills they claim.

### Offers to Help and Next Steps (26:05-end)
Vrijen offered to scan Careerspan's candidate pool if ACE provided a job description. Logan suggested connecting Merisenda with Shivam, a partner who sources high-caliber development talent from India at approximately $100-150k rather than the $300k+ price tag for equivalent US-based talent.

The call wrapped up with both parties offering ongoing assistance and exchanging goodbyes.

## Key Takeaways
- ACE Fitness is developing AI capabilities as a study coach/mentor for fitness professionals, with plans for content repositories and interactive scenarios
- Careerspan strongly advises having in-house technical/product leadership, even if engineering is outsourced
- Existing LLMs are sufficient—custom models and fine-tuning are rarely necessary for early-stage products
- When hiring technical talent, prioritize recent builders over senior managers who may have lost coding skills
- Monitor unit economics from the start; avoid pricing models that don't account for AI usage costs
- Development costs are dominated by salaries (~80%), though infrastructure can be kept low initially
- Careerspan offered to help ACE with hiring via their candidate pool and introduced a partner for cost-effective talent sourcing
