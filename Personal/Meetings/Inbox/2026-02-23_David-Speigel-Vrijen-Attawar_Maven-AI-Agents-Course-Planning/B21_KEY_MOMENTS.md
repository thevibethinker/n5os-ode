# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"If somebody wants to accelerate the shit out of that, that's where I would come in."**  
— David Speigel  
*Context:* Discussing his value proposition after spending six months learning Maven's platform. Reveals his positioning as a course launch accelerator rather than just an instructor, turning his learning curve into a service offering.

**"We create a little like, like ZO based mafia almost, right?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Proposing a collaborative business model around ZO affiliation. Signals a shift from individual course creators to a coordinated group leveraging shared infrastructure, distribution, and branding for mutual benefit.

**"Product managers, they're using AI to make slides, they're using AI to summarize their meetings, but they're not using AI agents to do the job of a product manager."**  
— David Speigel (citing Jacob Bank, CEO of Relay)  
*Context:* Identifying a market gap where product managers lag behind other functions in agentic AI adoption. This observation became the foundation for course positioning targeting corporate PMs rather than founders.

**"What I mean by that is you can spin up agents, I.E. Individual office workers, same level of. With the same structure as the office."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Explaining the fractal architecture concept where the same seven-capability structure applies from individual agents to entire teams. This became the technical framework for the proposed course.

**"When it comes to Maven, I really do think that there's an aspect of a takeaway that you don't just learn some knowledge."**  
— David Speigel  
*Context:* Articulating Maven's value proposition as outcome-focused rather than information-focused. This distinction drove the conversation toward tangible deliverables and measurable results for course participants.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"Are you saying that will sell?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* Repeated twice during the conversation when David presented technical concepts. Reveals a crucial tension between technical sophistication and market viability—pushing for validation of business potential before building.

**"Why not just build your own agentic product studio?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* Challenged David's framing of targeting big company PMs by suggesting a broader, founder-focused positioning. Led to clarification that the B2B corporate market was intentional, not just one option among many.

**"How do you standardize them for your group?"**  
— David Speigel  
*Significance:* Framing the core problem his course would solve. Moved from "teach people about AI agents" to "solve the standardization problem for teams"—a more concrete, actionable outcome that companies would pay for.
