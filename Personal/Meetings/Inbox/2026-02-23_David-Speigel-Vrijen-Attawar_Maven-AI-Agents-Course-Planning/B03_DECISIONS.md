# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Form ZO-Based Collaborative Mafia

**DECISION:** Create a collaborative group of four committed individuals (Vrijen, David, and two others) to launch businesses and courses affiliated with ZO, leveraging ZO's reputation, distribution, and potential subsidies.

**CONTEXT:** Multiple people have expressed interest in course creation and business development using ZO's platform. By forming a coordinated group, they can leverage ZO's brand, access free credits/subsidies, and create cross-promotional opportunities. This addresses the challenge of individual course creators struggling to gain traction.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen proposed; David agreed to participate

**IMPLICATIONS:** 
- Group will pursue ZO affiliation and subsidies from the platform
- Members can offer "ZO-affiliated courses" as a differentiator
- Access to ZO's distribution channels as they grow
- Potential for revenue sharing and collaboration across courses

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Individual course launches without coordination

## Decision 2: Target B2B Market (Companies) Rather Than Individual Founders

**DECISION:** Focus course offerings on employees at large companies rather than exclusively targeting founders or individual consumers. Specifically aim at product managers and corporate teams who need AI agent workflows.

**CONTEXT:** David noted that many functions (engineering, user research) are using AI agents extensively, but product managers lag behind. Companies represent a more sustainable revenue model than individual course-takers. The ML engineer mentioned Reddit as a potential corporate client. David's insight that "companies will pay" aligned with observations from the ML engineer.

**DECIDED BY:** David and Vrijen converged on this during discussion

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Course positioning shifts from "build your startup" to "build your agentic product studio within a company"
- Maven could enable a B2B play targeting corporate training budgets
- Broader addressable market beyond founders
- Need to make content appeal to both founders AND corporate employees

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Targeting only founders/entrepreneurs (rejected as too narrow)

## Decision 3: Use Fractal "Office" Framework as Course Foundation

**DECISION:** Build the course around Vrijen's fractal office infrastructure concept with seven core capabilities: memory, data ingestion (email/voice/text), outbound communication, and orchestration layer. This structure scales from individual agents to full teams.

**CONTEXT:** Vrijen has already developed this framework in his ZO setup. The fractal nature allows it to apply at multiple scales—individual AI workers, teams/offices, and multiple zones. This aligns with Jacob Bank's (Relay CEO) org chart model of AI agents, where each agent has 2-4 specific functions working as a coordinated team.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen proposed; David endorsed as "worth a course" and suitable for the outcome-focused Maven model

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Course will teach standardization of AI agents for teams/companies
- Framework provides repeatable structure students can implement
- Can demonstrate with concrete agent examples instantiating all seven principles
- Provides technical depth while remaining accessible to product managers

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Generic "AI agents for product managers" approach (incorporated but not sufficient alone)

## Decision 5: Prioritize Outcome-Focused Course Design Over Pure Knowledge Transfer

**DECISION:** Courses must deliver concrete, actionable takeaways that students can apply in their own environments and produce measurable results, not just teach knowledge.

**CONTEXT:** David emphasized that successful Maven courses demonstrate: (1) the approach has produced specific results somewhere name-brand, and (2) students can apply similar results in their own work. This distinguishes Maven from passive learning platforms. Examples include James Gray's Notion/Maven integration where AI agents maintain and update course materials, and Jacob Bank's Relay company operating with 10 people + 40 AI agent "employees."

**DECIDED BY:** David stated this as course design principle; Vrijen agreed

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Course must include demonstrable results/case studies
- Framework must be immediately applicable to students' work contexts
- Need to show concrete agent implementations, not just theory
- Higher bar for course quality but better student outcomes and marketing

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** General educational approach (rejected as insufficient for Maven's model)
