# B06: Business Context & Implications

## Market Opportunity
The team is shifting focus from a high-touch B2B sales model to a high-volume B2C candidate acquisition play.
- **Data Play:** Positioned as a "play to hoover up individual data profiles" to create a proprietary sourcing pipeline.
- **Growth Goal:** Aiming to double or triple the user base (targeting 10,000 users) in the next two months to validate the product case for acquisition.
- **Candidate Pain Point:** Identified a market gap in providing comparative feedback to candidates (showing how they stack up against others), which is being leveraged as a "Robinhood era" movement to drive viral usage.

## Competitive Position
Careerspan is differentiating through its ability to provide high-signal candidate data and a unique "vibe check" analysis that manual systems or generic easy-apply tools cannot match.
- **Moat:** Distribution Moat through embedded community partnerships and a synthetic candidate pool that is arguably better than generic job boards.
- **Advantage:** Low-touch point, automated pitching to CEOs/Founders ("Career from Careerspan") that bypasses traditional gatekeepers.
- **Leverage:** Leveraging localized "clout" in cities like New York, Boston, and SF to increase the appeal to enterprise acquirers.

## Strategic Direction
The primary strategic goal is a successful exit within the next 3 months, with a target valuation of $3M - $5M.
- **Pivot:** Explicit shift from B2B sales (deemed "irrelevant" for current acquisition goals) to B2C usage and "clout."
- **Exit Strategy:** Targeting larger "enormous" enterprise players (e.g., Korn Ferry, Darwin Box) rather than startups, as startups lack the liquidity for the desired exit price.
- **Cost Management:** Aggressive reduction of overhead (canceling subscriptions, letting Ilya go) to preserve runway for critical technical assets (Ilsa).

## Commercial Model
The business is currently operating in a "last-ditch" survival mode with a ~$15k bank balance and a significant runway shortfall.
- **Revenue Approach:** Exploring a secondary headhunting fee/commission model from Marvin founders or recruiters, though the primary focus remains the exit rather than current revenue.
- **Pricing:** Considering a consumer paywall ($20 range) or charging candidates for detailed "pitch" messages, but these are secondary to the acquisition narrative.
- **ROI Thinking:** The "gamble" is keeping technical lead Ilsa paid to maintain the tech asset and enable the "pool scanning" capability, which is viewed as the primary value driver for buyers.

## Implications & Next Steps
- **Strategic Decision:** Transitioning from "direct apply" promises to a "recruiter-facilitated" or "scanning" model requires careful legal and marketing alignment.
- **Key Takeaway:** The "tech plus Ilsa" bundle is the core asset; everything else (B2B dashboard, sales pipeline) is being deprioritized to fuel the B2C usage surge.
- **Business Impact:** High risk of "whiplash" for the team; requires delicate framing to maintain internal morale while cutting costs.
