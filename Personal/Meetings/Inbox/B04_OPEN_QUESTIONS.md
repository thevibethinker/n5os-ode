# B04: Open Questions & Unresolved Items

## Question 1: External Data Integration Strategy for Learner Profiles

**QUESTION:** How can ACE integrate external data sources (e.g., genetic testing from 23andMe, fitness tracking from Fitbit, other health/wellness apps) into its learner experience to create more personalized coaching?

**CONTEXT:** Vrijen demonstrated Zo's capability to pull in external data (Fitbit vitals, 23andMe genetic markers) to provide contextualized, personalized coaching. Merisenda acknowledged ACE hasn't yet considered integrating external data sources and noted the team lacks internal expertise in this area. This represents a potential competitive advantage but is currently undefined.

**WHO NEEDS TO ANSWER:** ACE product team + technical leadership (once hired)

**BLOCKING:** Conceptualization of the AI study coach's learner profile feature; integration requirements for MVP

**NEXT STEP:** Investigate what external data sources would be most valuable for ACE's learners and explore technical approaches for secure data integration

## Question 2: Optimal Team Structure for AI Product Development

**QUESTION:** What is the right mix of in-house vs. external technical talent for ACE's AI initiative, and what specific role profiles should they target?

**CONTEXT:** Discussion centered on whether to hire a CTO/Head of AI internally vs. outsourcing. Vrijen emphasized having at least one strong technical product leader in-house to manage external vendors, while Logan suggested a hybrid model with US-based leadership + India-based developers to control costs (100-150k vs. 300k+ for US talent). ACE currently has no CTO or technical lead.

**WHO NEEDS TO ANSWER:** ACE leadership + potential technical advisor

**BLOCKING:** Hiring decisions; ability to execute AI strategy

**NEXT STEP:** Determine budget and then engage a technical advisor to help interview candidates and assess the optimal team structure

## Question 3: Unit Economics & AI Usage Cost Management

**QUESTION:** How can ACE proactively manage AI usage costs and unit economics from the outset, given the potential for runaway expenses?

**CONTEXT:** Vrijen warned about common failure modes where companies start with unlimited AI subscriptions, then shift to usage-based pricing and see adoption tank. He emphasized planning for unit economics early, even while optimizing for experience in initial stages. With ACE's scale, they expect meaningful adoption and therefore meaningful costs.

**WHO NEEDS TO ANSWER:** Product team + technical lead

**BLOCKING:** AI implementation decisions; pricing strategy; vendor selection

**NEXT STEP:** Define usage monitoring and cost control mechanisms before building; research what usage patterns to expect for similar coaching products

## Question 4: MVP Scope for AI Study Coach

**QUESTION:** What is the precise MVP scope and feature set for the AI study coach, and what constitutes the minimal viable product that will drive user retention and engagement?

**CONTEXT:** Mentions included "just-in-time" content delivery (e.g., planes of movement videos), role-playing/case scenarios for client interactions, and a learner profile that compounds knowledge. However, no clear definition of MVP boundaries or prioritization. Vrijen emphasized finding the "nugget of functionality" that creates retention as the first priority.

**WHO NEEDS TO ANSWER:** ACE product team + potential product lead

**BLOCKING:** Development roadmap; budget allocation; timeline

**NEXT STEP:** Define core hypothesis, success metrics, and minimal feature set before beginning development
