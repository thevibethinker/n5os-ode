# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
A co-founder standup focused on strategic partnership opportunities, specifically exploring collaboration with the Next Play community (700-member professional network) to drive Careerspan adoption and job board visibility. The conversation examined multiple event/workshop formats and go-to-market approaches.

## Key Themes

### 1. Next Play Community Partnership
- **What is Next Play?** A selective professional community (~700 members) for late-stage professionals exploring their "next place" in their career
- **Value prop**: Peer learning from experienced professionals, networking, job marketplace
- **Opportunity**: Ben Lang (Next Play leader) expressed openness to a digital workshop format with maximum flexibility on content concept

### 2. Partnership Structure Options
Three main approaches were discussed:

**Option A: Careerspan + Zo Co-Hosted Webinar**
- General workshop on career storytelling, strategic moves, and AI for career advancement
- Separate track option: AI-powered career strategist tool (exclusive to Next Play members)
- Risks: Adds product complexity, complicates messaging

**Option B: Zo-Led Event with Careerspan Sponsorship**
- Zo hosts event (site building, portfolio careers, or career-related content)
- Careerspan acts as primary sponsor (no payment required from organizers)
- Careerspan announces job board availability to attendees
- Clean separation of themes; leverages event momentum for job board visibility

**Option C: City-Based Digital Events**
- Geographically-targeted workshops (e.g., NYC companies hiring)
- Allows location-specific value prop (local job availability)
- Scalable model for city-by-city expansion

### 3. Job Board Go-to-Market Strategy
Central insight: **The real win is getting Ben to post his jobs (~20-30/month) through Careerspan**

Key tactics discussed:
- **Early access model**: Next Play members get 3-7 day head start before public posting
- **Curator jobs approach**: 5-6 founder partners agree to post jobs through Careerspan
- **Pre-screening value add**: Careerspan pre-processes top candidates for hiring founders
- **Talent pipeline framing**: Position as exclusive pipelines (e.g., "Engineering at Zo," "Data at Bonfire")

### 4. Economic Positioning
- Problem: More users without employer revenue = net negative for Careerspan
- Solution: Focus on demonstrating immediate value to employers (pre-screened candidates)
- Strategic lever: Getting 5-6 founder partners to use platform creates proof points and incentivizes Ben's distribution

### 5. Demand Generation Mechanics
Two approaches considered:
1. **Explicit job drops**: Announce specific roles at event with early access
2. **Company credibility**: Feature founder testimonials/logos ("I trust Careerspan") with generic job count (6 roles minimum)

Emerging concept: **"Job drops"** (similar to Supreme product drops)—limited-time exclusive job postings at events to create urgency and scarcity.

## Conversation Arc
1. **Setup**: Acquisition process coordination + Next Play exploration
2. **Exploration**: Understanding Next Play community and Ben's flexibility
3. **Brainstorm**: Multiple event/product formats
4. **Critique**: Identifying cost of user acquisition without employer revenue
5. **Pivot**: Simplified approach focusing on sponsorship + job distribution
6. **Refinement**: Location-based strategy, early access mechanics, pre-screening value add
7. **Framework**: Talent pipelines as the mental model for user positioning

## Unresolved Questions
- How many jobs needed initially for credible launch? (discussed: 5-6 minimum, possibly just 6)
- Email distribution system status (whether currently live for targeted outreach)
- Specific timeline for event execution (December 1st referenced for initial job drop)
- Whether exclusivity framing (jobs only for Next Play) creates legal/platform issues
