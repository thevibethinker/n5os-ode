# B06: Business Context & Implications

## Market Opportunity
- **LinkedIn Alternative:** Opportunity to capture spend from recruiters currently paying $300-$400/month for LinkedIn Recruiter, which V notes has "dubious value" due to scale issues.
- **Low-Friction Entry:** A "$100 punt" model for recruiters to surface candidates on Careerspan for roles they are struggling to close.
- **Flywheel Effect:** Increasing the number of high-quality roles is seen as the primary lever to "supercharge" job seeker attention and user acquisition.

## Competitive Position
- **Differentiation:** Positioning Careerspan as a "passive scouting" engine where users are "always being scanned" and "always being scouted," contrasting with traditional active job boards.
- **Recruiter Integration:** Leveraging external recruiters (e.g., "Jeff’s smiling mug") as verified partners to build platform trust and increase job volume without Careerspan owning the entire incumbent talent pool.

## Strategic Direction
- **Recruiter Decentralization:** Moving away from V being the sole source of jobs; accepting that Careerspan users may get hired through other recruiters on the platform.
- **Infrastructure over Acquisition:** Debate over whether to scale job volume (cost-effective) or candidate quality (outcome-effective). Current lean is toward gathering "stellar candidates" to ensure recruiters get ROI, rather than risking money on "shitty users."
- **Active User Filtering:** Proposed strategy to limit scanning to "most active folks" (e.g., active in the last two months) to manage compute costs while maintaining signal quality.

## Commercial Model
- **Revenue Approach:** Potential to charge for job postings and scanning access. Initial testing of a one-off payment model ($100) before moving to recurring fees.
- **Cost Structure (LLM Scaling):** Scanning costs are roughly $400 for 60 jobs against 300 people. Costs are a function of the number of "stories" (data points) a user has told and "backbees" (technical processing units). 
- **Operational Leverage:** Future average costs per user are expected to decrease as job/salary preferences are collected upfront, reducing the "check" surface area.

## Implications & Next Steps
- **Financial Risk:** Scaling could lead to a $10k-$15k OpenAI bill. V is willing to "stick that on a credit card" if usage warrants it, provided there is a 24-hour window to lock down systems if costs spike.
- **UI Pivot:** Changing user consent language from "Send me jobs" to "Consent to being contacted by recruiters" to align with the headhunting/scouting value proposition.
- **Recruiter Outreach:** V to contact "Lodes" (Logan) to generate a list of recruiters for a pilot of the paid job-posting/scanning service.
