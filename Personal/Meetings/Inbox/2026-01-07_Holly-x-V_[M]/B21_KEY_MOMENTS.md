# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"I don't know if I could be the next Mark Zuckerberg leading, you know, a 6,000 person organization... So happy to hold my hand up and pass it over."**  
— Holly Sanders  
*Context:* Discussing long-term goals and scaling. Holly identifies her passion for the 0-to-1 and 1-to-10 stages rather than massive corporate leadership, signaling a preference for agility over institutional scale.

**"The underlying point there being that visibility has gotten trust in the system has gotten so low and that we have basically just gone back to like the most fundamentally believable signals."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Analyzing the current state of hiring where employers are over-indexing on "safe" credentials (X-Stanford, X-Stripe) because they lack deeper visibility into candidate quality.

**"I have long suspected that the only reason Juice Box is that popular is because LinkedIn's UX is a war crime."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Critiquing current recruitment tools. This highlights the opportunity for any product that provides a superior user experience and better data accessibility than the industry standard.

**"They're not even the recruiters... it's candidates who are being exposed and, like, basically taking the piss out of... because they're looking for a job."**  
— Holly Sanders  
*Context:* Expressing frustration with "AI slop" and mass-apply tools that charge desperate candidates for services that actually damage their hireability.

**"Unfortunately, the going rate for integrity is pretty low in the market."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Reflecting on the "vicious circle" of recruitment technology where companies profit from worsening the signal-to-noise ratio in the job market.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"If you're not going to look directly at solving a particular thing, what are things you can do adjacent that strengthen you up in the long run for what you're working through towards?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* Reframes the problem of "lulls" or lack of direction from a search for a specific product to a meta-level focus on network and expertise building.

**"Is it better to show them the list and say you'll get what you get based on candidates that respond or is it better to wait and engage with the candidates [at the risk of delay]?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* Probes the tension between "immediacy of the scan" and "guaranteed response," a critical product design choice for recruiter-facing talent networks.

**"Do I just spend a month or two and placing your candidates, get some money in and then just go absolutely ham on product? But the thought of, like slowing myself down that much is obviously always a bit daunting."**  
— Holly Sanders  
*Significance:* Surfaces the classic founder dilemma of survival vs. speed. It reveals Holly's immediate pressure regarding runway and the strategic tradeoff of manual work to fund technical development.
