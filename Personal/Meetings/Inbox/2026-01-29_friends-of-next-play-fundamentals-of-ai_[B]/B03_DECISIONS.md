# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Share LinkedIn Data Analysis Guide with Attendees

**DECISION:** Logan will publish and distribute the complete instruction set for the LinkedIn data export and visualization methodology to all attendees.

**CONTEXT:** The presentation demonstrated a comprehensive workflow using Claude Code to analyze 48 CSV files of LinkedIn data (18 years of history, 5,000+ messages, connection data). Given the positive response and the accessibility of this as an entry point for Claude Code beginners, the guide will be made available for replication.

**DECIDED BY:** Logan Currie (presenter)

**IMPLICATIONS:** Attendees will have a tangible, step-by-step resource to export their own LinkedIn data and perform similar network analysis, lost opportunity identification, and skill gap assessments. This serves as both an educational tool and a practical productivity asset.

---

## Decision 2: Publish Metacognitive Patterns Analysis

**DECISION:** Logan will publish the analysis of conversation patterns and metacognitive strategies used during the LinkedIn data exploration sessions.

**CONTEXT:** As part of demonstrating how to work effectively with AI systems, Logan had Claude analyze their own conversation history to extract patterns like "narrative before data," "use yourself as a guinea pig," and iterative challenge of findings. This meta-analysis was deemed valuable enough to share externally.

**DECIDED BY:** Logan Currie (presenter)

**IMPLICATIONS:** Creates a reusable resource on effective prompting and AI collaboration strategies, extending the educational value of the session beyond the specific LinkedIn use case.

---

## Decision 3: Meeting Format and Sequence

**DECISION (IMPLIED):** The presentation will follow a structured sequence: V opens with mental model/framework content → Logan demonstrates practical data analysis with Claude Code → Ben demos Zo Computer capabilities.

**CONTEXT:** This sequencing was established to build conceptual understanding before showing tactical implementations, creating a logical flow from "why" (mental models) to "how" (tool demonstrations).

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar, Logan Currie, Ben Guo (organizers/presenters)

**IMPLICATIONS:** Sets a precedent for future "Fundamentals of AI Productivity" sessions to combine conceptual frameworks with live demonstrations from multiple tools/perspectives.

---

**Note:** This was primarily a demonstration and educational webinar format. No binding organizational decisions, resource allocations, or policy changes were made during this session.
