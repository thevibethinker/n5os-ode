# B03_DECISIONS

## Decision 1: Prioritize an Accelerated Career Span Exit Process

**DECISION:** Vrijen and Logan will shift from opportunistic acquisition conversations to a more aggressive outbound push to pursue a Career Span sale in the near term.
**CONTEXT:** Career Span is being shut down, cash is constrained, and the current best acquisition option has been unreliable. A faster, more concentrated process was framed as the right move given timing and market conditions.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar
**IMPLICATIONS:** The team will actively market the company for acquisition rather than waiting for existing interest to develop. This also sets up the exit as a reputational asset for future fundraising and career positioning.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Continuing with lighter, opportunistic outreach and waiting on the current best option.

## Decision 2: Use the Exit Primarily as a Reputation and Signaling Asset

**DECISION:** The Career Span outcome will be treated not just as an operational wind-down, but as a strategic credibility event to support future fundraising, narrative building, and professional positioning.
**CONTEXT:** Vrijen explicitly framed the most valuable gains available at this stage as reputation and future fundability rather than maximizing business continuation.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar
**IMPLICATIONS:** Messaging around the sale will emphasize what Career Span achieved and what it signals about Vrijen and Logan as builders. This decision shapes both PR strategy and how the exit story will be told.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Treating the shutdown as a quiet operational closure without investing in narrative leverage.

## Decision 3: Build Vrijen’s Near-Term Narrative Around Zo Before Launching the Next Startup

**DECISION:** Vrijen will go all in with Zo for a defined period to generate measurable outcomes and strengthen his credibility before making a larger push around his next startup concept.
**CONTEXT:** He believes the main gap in his current narrative is large, externally legible numbers. Zo’s funding, brand cachet, and distribution opportunities make it a better platform for producing those outcomes first.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar
**IMPLICATIONS:** Rather than immediately jumping into a new venture, Vrijen will first use his Zo role to build evidence, visibility, and a stronger story for future investors and founders.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Going directly into the next startup immediately after the Career Span exit.

## Decision 4: Focus PR Efforts on Owned and Shared Channels Rather Than Traditional Media

**DECISION:** The near-term communications strategy should prioritize owned and shared channels—such as Vrijen’s website, LinkedIn, and direct network amplification—over relying on “true PR” or broad media coverage.
**CONTEXT:** Megan assessed that traditional media traction would likely be difficult given the timing, uncertainty, and likely niche nature of the exit story. Owned channels offer more control and are more realistic for building a personal founder brand.
**DECIDED BY:** Megan Gasper, with agreement from Vrijen Attawar
**IMPLICATIONS:** Time and money should go first into strengthening Vrijen’s personal platforms and narrative infrastructure. PR support, if any, should complement that rather than substitute for it.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Trying to drive the strategy primarily through conventional media coverage or a broader PR campaign.

## Decision 5: Sequence Communications in Two Waves

**DECISION:** The communications effort will likely happen in two stages: first, capitalize on the Career Span exit while the news is fresh; later, make a stronger personal-brand push once Vrijen has more outcomes to show from Zo or is closer to launching the next startup.
**CONTEXT:** Vrijen proposed, and Megan reinforced, that the exit can create a moment of attention, but the larger personal narrative will be more compelling after additional proof points exist.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Megan Gasper
**IMPLICATIONS:** Short-term storytelling will focus on the exit event itself, while broader personal positioning is deferred until there is more traction and evidence. This avoids pushing the individual story before it is fully substantiated.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Doing a full personal PR push immediately, before new outcomes exist.

## Decision 6: Invest Personal Resources in Growing Vrijen’s Channels Before Paying for Broader Personal PR

**DECISION:** For Vrijen’s individual brand, the better immediate use of time and money is to grow his owned and shared channels rather than spend heavily on PR support right now.
**CONTEXT:** Megan argued that if the goal is personal founder branding, people need strong places to land and learn about Vrijen directly. Vrijen agreed that waiting until he has more to show would make later PR spend more effective.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar, informed by Megan Gasper’s recommendation
**IMPLICATIONS:** Personal brand infrastructure becomes the priority, and any larger PR spend is deferred until the narrative is stronger and more evidence-backed.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Hiring immediate PR support for Vrijen’s personal story despite limited current proof points.
