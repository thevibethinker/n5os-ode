## Strategic Questions Raised

**"What would happen to Career Span just in terms of like the overall kind of like narrative?"**  
— Megan Gasper  
*Significance:* This question immediately moved the discussion beyond transaction mechanics into story design. It surfaced that the exit was not just an operational event, but a reputational moment that needed intentional framing.

**"Do you have like a full concept or a general like where are you at with that in terms of like what you would be asking angels for?"**  
— Megan Gasper  
*Significance:* This was a key pressure-test on readiness. It exposed the gap between Vrijen’s strong directional thesis and the still-developing shape of the next venture, which matters for both investor messaging and PR timing.

**"Where do I come in in terms of like PR?"**  
— Megan Gasper  
*Significance:* This question forced clarity on the actual job to be done. It separated vague hopes for “PR” from specific possible roles: supporting the Careerspan exit story now versus amplifying a more mature founder narrative later.

**"What do you think is a realistic timeline? For sale?"**  
— Megan Gasper  
*Significance:* This grounded the conversation in execution reality. Since PR windows depend on timing, this question mattered because it tested whether there was a real near-term event to organize around or just a hypothetical one.

**"How you can tell that story, whether you get true media interest or whether it's just truly about like owning the story and leveraging it in whatever it is that you do next."**  
— Megan Gasper  
*Significance:* This reframed the entire communications strategy. Rather than treating media pickup as the objective, Megan positioned narrative ownership itself as the asset, which better fits Vrijen’s stage, goals, and timeline.
