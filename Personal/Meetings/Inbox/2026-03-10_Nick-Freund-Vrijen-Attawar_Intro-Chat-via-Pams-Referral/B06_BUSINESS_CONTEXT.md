- Massive gap between AI tool availability and actual adoption/competence among non-technical founders and CEOs
- Current AI training approaches (masterclasses, workshops) fail to translate into concrete, independent capability — the "scaffolding problem"
- ChatGPT/Claude desktop products "flatten understanding" for non-technical users, creating a ceiling on what they can accomplish
- The durable opportunity is in **systems thinking education** — teaching people to think in pipelines, not prompts

## Competitive Position

**Zo Computer vs. Alternatives**
- V positions Zo against Claude Desktop, ChatGPT, and OpenClaw (likely referring to open-source desktop agents)
- **Key differentiator articulated:** Zo is a full containerized Unix machine in the cloud with a prompt-driven frontend — not just an API wrapper returning raw text
- This architecture enables reliable integrations without MCP quality/reliability variance
- **vs. ChatGPT memory:** Zo offers curated, structured data sovereignty vs. ChatGPT's unmanaged memory accumulation
- **vs. Delphi:** Zo offers sovereign, model-agnostic data ownership vs. platform lock-in
- **vs. Second Brain tools (Notion, Roam Research):** V argues the "second brain" paradigm fails because maintenance effort exceeds value — Zo automates the maintenance layer ("third brain")

**V's Personal Positioning**
