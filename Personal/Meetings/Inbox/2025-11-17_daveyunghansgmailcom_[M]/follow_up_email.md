David,

I really enjoyed our conversation yesterday. A few things stood out to me:

**On your product philosophy**: Your commitment to manual data input (not automated resume parsing) is smart. Yes, it creates friction, but it preserves data integrity and gives you control over the UX—something automation-first approaches sacrifice. I appreciate you taking seriously the feedback that people are often bad at articulating their own value; that's exactly the gap Careerspan addresses too, and it's worth solving holistically.

**On model strategy**: The conversation about moving from Sonnet 4.5-only to a mixed approach (Sonnet + Haiku) felt important. The 40-50% cost reduction we've achieved at Careerspan through task decomposition could be meaningful for you as you scale. Your engineering team's audit on which tasks can use cheaper models without quality loss is worth the investment—especially before you go harder on revenue growth.

**On university relationships**: Your instinct to *not* charge universities (only employers) is the right one. I've learned that the hard way. Universities perceive any new hiring tech as an existential threat to their career services staff, and that creates friction that no product excellence can overcome. Your positioning as "less of a threat" is genuinely strategic.
