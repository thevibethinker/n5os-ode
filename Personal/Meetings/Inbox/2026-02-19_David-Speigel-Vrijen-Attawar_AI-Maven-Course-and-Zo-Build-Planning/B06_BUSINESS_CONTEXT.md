## Market Opportunity

**Founders fear being left behind on AI.** The signal V cites: funded, competent founders — including the Calendly founder — openly admitting they no longer feel in touch with AI-driven productivity. The tech landscape moves faster than even well-resourced teams can track (model versioning confusion, new paradigms like Open Claw emerging and disappearing).

- **Target segment:** Funded founders already executing well, but losing ground on AI fluency and productivity tooling.
- **Core need:** Bite-sized, tactical AI education that simultaneously delivers real productivity gains — not theory, not product-market-fit promises.
- **Adjacent need (David's angle):** Founders and professionals who want "agentic networking" — AI-assisted relationship management, outreach cadence, and messaging — but lack the engineering capacity to build it.
- **Market dynamic:** The pace of AI evolution (Claude versioning, new tools weekly) creates an information asymmetry that founders will pay to close.

## Competitive Position

**V's thesis on differentiation:**
- Very few founders combine deep AI skill, available bandwidth, and motivation to teach. Most AI-skilled founders channel everything into their own company.
- V claims to occupy a small group: founders who are (a) genuinely advanced on AI, (b) not exclusively building a company with that skill, and (c) motivated to monetize the teaching/community angle.
- Tiago Forte's "Second Brain" empire is the aspirational comp — V wants to own "agentic productivity" the way Forte owned personal knowledge management, and evolve the brand toward "cognitive amplification."
- David's differentiator: deep career/networking knowledge (the "Spiegel knowledge") that can be packaged as a queryable system via Zo-to-Zo protocol.

**Competitors/adjacents mentioned:**
- IDO (building something similar to CRM/networking tool)
- Logan (building related functionality)
- General X/Twitter chatter about AI-assisted CRM/networking — V and David want to get ahead of this wave.
- Dan Hockenmeyer and Ben Thompson (Stratechery) — cited as thought leaders who codify software industry forces. V/David want to do the same for AI + productivity/networking.
- Maven (course platform) — potential distribution channel for structured course content.

**Key strategic insight from David:** Vibe coding accelerates creation but doesn't solve maintenance, customer understanding, or ongoing support. Software companies that don't use AI to cycle at the new speed will be replaced. V and David can position at this intersection — helping founders use AI to keep pace.
