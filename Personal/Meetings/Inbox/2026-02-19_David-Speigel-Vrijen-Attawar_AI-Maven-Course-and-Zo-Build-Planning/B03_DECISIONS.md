## Decision 1: Start with Ambient CRM as First Co-Build

**DECISION:** V and David will co-build a basic ambient CRM as their first joint project — a tool that tracks contacts via email and calendar, creates profiles for each person encountered.
**CONTEXT:** David wanted agentic networking functionality (who to contact, how often, messaging). V proposed splitting the difference and starting with a minimal viable slice: ambient contact tracking. David had been debating between Copilot and Zo for building; this gives them a concrete starting point.
**DECIDED BY:** V proposed, David agreed ("Okay, let's just start with that.")
**IMPLICATIONS:** Scopes down the sprawling agentic networking vision to one buildable unit. Sets the stage for iterative expansion. Platform-agnostic — V noted "let's leave Zo aside for a second" and consider ChatGPT or Zo or wherever.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Full agentic networking tool (messaging cadence, follow-up generation, outreach recommendations) — explicitly deferred as too broad for first pass.

## Decision 2: Use Zo-to-Zo Protocol for David's Knowledge Sharing System

**DECISION:** Build David's coaching knowledge distribution system using V's Zo-to-Zo protocol architecture — David's Zo holds his deconstructed knowledge database; clients query it through a controlled protocol rather than getting raw data access.
**CONTEXT:** David wants clients to access "the Spiegel knowledge" (his networking parameters, frameworks, calibrations) without requiring heavy engineering or exposing all his data. V realized the infrastructure he built last week for the CRM handles this exact pattern.
**DECIDED BY:** V proposed the architecture, David agreed ("Okay, let's just start with that.")
**IMPLICATIONS:** David should set up a Zo Pewter (secondary Zo) as his commercial-facing instance, keeping personal Zo separate. Clients need only know how to handshake David's Zo and request information. Tools can be layered incrementally. Each client can run whatever infrastructure they want on their side.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Custom-built platform, standalone course content — rejected in favor of the Zo-to-Zo approach because infrastructure already exists.

## Decision 3: Collaborative Build Sessions at $25/3 Hours to Start

**DECISION:** V and David will do structured collaborative building sessions. V's Zo architects the solutions; David controls the process via temporary access to V's Zo. Pricing starts at $25 for the first three hours.
**CONTEXT:** David wants to learn by building, not just consuming. V wants to be fair on pricing given early stage. The workflow: David states what he wants → V instructs his Zo → Zo sends instructions to David's environment → David validates → loop.
**DECIDED BY:** V proposed, David agreed ("Yes.")
**IMPLICATIONS:** Establishes a working relationship and comp model. First round treated as a design cycle — no pressure to monetize. Compensation structure (ongoing fair comp for tech access) to be figured out after initial sessions.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** David building independently with Copilot — deferred in favor of collaborative approach V advocated for.

## Decision 4: Treat First Round as Design Cycle, Not Monetization

**DECISION:** The first collaborative building round will focus on design and learning, not revenue. Monetization comes after proving the model.
**CONTEXT:** V explicitly said: "We can treat the first one as just like a design cycle. We don't need to really worry about monetizing it." Want to validate that people respond to the format before optimizing economics.
**DECIDED BY:** V proposed, David did not object.
**IMPLICATIONS:** Removes pressure to ship a polished product immediately. Allows experimentation with the Zo-to-Zo workflow. Sets expectations that comp discussions come later.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None explicitly discussed.

## Decision 5: V's Brand Direction — "Agentic Productivity" → "Cognitive Amplification"

**DECISION:** V will brand his founder-focused offering around "agentic productivity," with the long-term brand evolution toward "cognitive amplification."
**CONTEXT:** V's pitch: "I want to give everyone 30 more IQ points." Productivity is the entry point because it's visceral and universal. The deeper thesis is that building AI-augmented productivity develops genuine AI fluency that founders can then apply to their businesses. Referenced the Calendly founder feeling out of touch with productivity as a market signal.
**DECIDED BY:** V declared, David acknowledged and engaged ("Cognitive amplification, yeah.")
**IMPLICATIONS:** Shapes messaging, website copy, target audience selection. Differentiates from generic "AI courses" by anchoring in tangible productivity outcomes.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** "Agentic networking" (David's angle) — positioned as complementary, not competing. Both create cross-halo effect.

## Decision 6: Referral Compensation Structure for David

**DECISION (TENTATIVE):** David will receive a one-off commission for each person he brings into V's founder program, plus an ongoing smaller amount for as long as they remain active.
**CONTEXT:** V recognizes that linchpin members (people David can bring via his network — "the Benarezes of the world") are worth more than the first few hundred-dollar subscriptions. Getting credible founders in the room early sets an experiential floor.
**DECIDED BY:** V proposed the structure. David did not explicitly confirm terms — details to be finalized.
**IMPLICATIONS:** Aligns David's incentives with long-term member retention, not just acquisition. Specific amounts not yet set.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** None explicitly discussed — V presented this as his proposed thank-you structure.
