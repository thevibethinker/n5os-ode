## Strategic Pivot Opportunity: Alumni Office Channel

**Core Strategic Insight**: Alumni offices represent an untapped distribution channel that solves Careerspan's customer acquisition problem. They are:
- Technologically archaic and data-starved
- Financially constrained but "love free"
- Desperate for career spike data to align donation campaigns
- Hard to penetrate but defensible once established

**The Trade**: Give alumni offices free product/access to career data; gain privileged access to their alumni pools for employer matching.

> "If I went to alumni offices and said, I'll give you a free fucking product or basically free... I'll give you a data stream that you never had... insight into their career spikes... And I'll give you the halo effect of placing them in great jobs."

## Business Model Evolution: Talent Agency Model

**Proposed structure**:
1. Create function-specific pools (e.g., "Emory Engineering Pool")
2. Candidates opt into pods relevant to their function
3. Employers pay for curated access ($500/candidate floated)
4. University gets value; Careerspan monetizes employer side

> "We essentially do a talent agency model where we tell folks like, hey, this is the engineer pool... opening up the engineering pool first click on this link to apply."

**Monetization flexibility**: Could shift from placement fees (contingent) to per-candidate access fees (more predictable revenue).
