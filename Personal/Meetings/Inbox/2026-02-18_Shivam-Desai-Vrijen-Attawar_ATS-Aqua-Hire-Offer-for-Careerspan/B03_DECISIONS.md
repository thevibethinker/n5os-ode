## Decision 2: Prioritize Alumni Office Strategy for Careerspan

**DECISION:** Vrijen will pursue alumni offices at elite universities (starting with Emory, UCLA, Duke, Cornell, Michigan) as the primary distribution channel for Careerspan
**CONTEXT:** Alumni offices are hard to penetrate but desperately need career spike data and ongoing alumni contact info for donation alignment. They "love free." Careerspan can provide data streams they've never had while building exclusive candidate pools.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen (with Shivam's implicit agreement through conversation flow)
**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Careerspan positioned as free/low-cost data tool for alumni offices
- Creates "Careerspan Select" pools by university (e.g., "Emory Engineers")
- Enables premium candidate sourcing for employers by university/function
- Long-term play requiring multi-month commitment
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Career Services (equally hard to penetrate, less immediate need for the data Careerspan provides)

## Decision 5: Talent Agency Model for Candidate Pools

**DECISION:** Structure candidate sourcing as a "talent agency model" with function-specific pods (e.g., "Emory Engineers") rather than a single undifferentiated pool
**CONTEXT:** Allows segmenting candidates by function and university without heavy engineering investment. Employers get curated access to specific talent pools.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen (proposed and adopted)
**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Intake forms can be dynamically generated based on employer preferences
- Creates premium positioning for "select" university pools
- Enables function-by-function rollout (starting with engineering)
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Single unified candidate pool (rejected as less differentiated)
