# B03_DECISIONS

## Decision 1: Prioritize Personal Utility Before Broad Monetization

**DECISION:** David will prioritize building Alfred to be highly useful for himself before scaling/monetizing it for wider users.  
**CONTEXT:** Managing multiple assistants already introduced operational overhead; both agreed that founder leverage is higher when the system works deeply for the builder first.  
**DECIDED BY:** David Szabo-Stuban (endorsed by Vrijen Attawar).  
**IMPLICATIONS:** Product direction remains user-zero-first; commercialization is delayed until core personal value is proven.  
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Immediate scaling via automated VM deployment for external users.

## Decision 2: Continue Collaboration and Resource Exchange

**DECISION:** They agreed to continue collaborating and share implementation resources (docs, setup guidance, contacts).  
**CONTEXT:** Strong overlap in vision (personal AI, semantic memory, knowledge-graph-driven assistance) created a clear basis for ongoing exchange.  
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and David Szabo-Stuban.  
**IMPLICATIONS:** Follow-up communication channel and material-sharing workflow will support future joint exploration.  
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Keeping the conversation one-off without follow-up.

## Decision 3: Defer Platform Migration Until Evaluation

**DECISION (TENTATIVE):** David will evaluate Zo/Zoe for potentially migrating Alfred, but no immediate migration commitment was made.  
**CONTEXT:** He expressed interest in reducing management overhead and exploring Zo capabilities, while still validating what matters in his current system.  
**DECIDED BY:** David Szabo-Stuban.  
**IMPLICATIONS:** Near-term effort goes to review/testing; migration remains conditional on fit and operational benefit.  
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Immediate full migration now; no migration exploration at all.
