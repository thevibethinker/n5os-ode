# B01: Detailed Recap

## Overview
Vrijen Attawar (V) and David Speigel (David) met to discuss personal motivation, the current state of Career Span, and a strategy for selling the company. The conversation touched on personal challenges from the previous year, the shift in the hiring market from matching to sourcing, and potential pricing models for Career Span's candidate database.

## 00:00 - 05:28: Personal Motivation and Year-Start Reflection
- David shares his transparency about working roughly 10 hours a week last year and feeling unmotivated.
- He discusses his parenting journey (one kid in college, one senior in high school, and a 4-year-old) and how taking a break felt necessary after years of "the grind."
- Vrijen and David discuss intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation, with David leaning toward extrinsic for professional work.

## 05:28 - 09:00: Career Span Update and Sale Strategy
- Vrijen provides a "number one" update: Career Span is looking to sell within the next two months.
- They have enough runway for about two months.
- Vrijen notes a shift in the market: people are jaded about "AI matching" and are now more interested in **verifying, vetting, and sourcing**.
- Career Span's value prop is shifting toward its unique pool of high-quality users ("best of the best").

## 09:00 - 16:30: Sourcing and The "Emperor Has No Clothes" Narrative
- David shares an anecdote of a client (ex-Meta) who was "perfect fit" but ignored by ATS until he reached out directly to the hiring manager.
- Vrijen discusses the "Emperor has no clothes" reality of current hiring tech (matching doesn't work effectively).
- Proposal for a "synthetic data pool" or vetting community where candidates are scanned automatically.
- Concept of the "Daily Scan": Vrijen proposes a "Noon Scan" where employers scan the database for candidates, creating a sense of urgency for candidates to keep their profiles (two/three stories) updated.

## 16:30 - 21:00: Revenue Models and Market Positioning
- Discussion of pricing: Free scans with a high placement fee vs. paid scans with lower or no placement fees.
- David compares the model to LinkedIn Sales Navigator vs. job postings.
- Vrijen likes the "densest concentration of talent" branding (invoking Sam Altman's phrasing).
- Goal is to position Career Span as the "21st-century job board" where the work happens in the background.

## 21:00 - 24:39: Potential Buyers and Next Steps
- Vrijen targets an acquisition price in the $1M - $2M range.
- Potential buyers identified: Large staffing agencies, recruiting firms, or AI interview companies like **Ellie AI** (video interview co-pilot).
- They discuss the possibility of an acqui-hire for the team (Logan, Ilsa, and Vrijen).
- Vrijen concludes the "Part 1" segment to perform a quick task/check.
