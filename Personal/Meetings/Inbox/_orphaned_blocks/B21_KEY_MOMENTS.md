# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"My belief, my POV is that is where the advantage will come is like a more dynamic interview inter relationship with the user, not just a we are providing information to you. Because I think when people think start with that mental model, it's very easy to get locked into a like data is only flowing from one direction."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Explaining how AI coaching tools like Zoe work. The key insight is that sustainable advantage comes from bidirectional, dynamic learning relationships with users, not just unidirectional information delivery. This reframes the opportunity from content delivery to personalized, adaptive engagement.

**"I firmly believe that you need at least one really strong technical leader and product mind on your side who can, who's technical enough to guide, who's technical enough to know when the developers are bullshitting and can like get realistic, can set and demand realistic things from them."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Core advice on how to build AI products successfully without being technical. Emphasizes that you need in-house technical leadership to manage external developers effectively, not just to build the product but to hold them accountable and set realistic expectations.

**"We made a big mistake with our first co founder... What we found was that this person had been so senior at a company and this is something that as a non technical person I did not know was that sometimes the folks that have been in director roles for a long time are actually farthest away from the tech."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Revealing a painful hiring lesson that saved them from repeating the mistake. The failure mode is seeking someone with strong managerial credentials (director, VP titles) who may have moved away from hands-on building. At the start, you need someone who can actually build and ship, not just manage.

**"Start with an unlimited subscription, then they'll shift to usage, then they'll see their usage tank. Right. So like, why is that? Because they go in saying, oh, we'll make the unit economics work later."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Warning about the dangerous failure mode of ignoring unit economics early. Describes a pattern where companies optimize for user experience first, get hit by AI costs, then introduce pricing changes that tank adoption. Key advice: plan for unit economics from the outset even if optimizing for experience initially.

**"Zoe's fucking awesome. It's like the actual best tool for any non technical person I've ever encountered. And Zoe is basically like a computer in the cloud that you control by prompting. So it's not just a conversation like chat gbt, it's an actual computer. So when I tell it to do something it doesn't just send it to a large language model and return a answer like an auto generated text. It will actually then proceed to take steps to operate that online computer to take out the actions that you want."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Demonstrating Zoe to Merisenda as an example of what's possible with AI that goes beyond chat. The enthusiasm underscores a key insight: the future is AI agents that can take actions, not just generate text.

**"I know it sometimes seems, like, cheesy when people say, like, oh, stuff happens, or, like, you end up where you're supposed to be, but, like, I actually, like, feel like that, like, oh, I went through everything I did. Like, then I wouldn't be where I'm at now."**

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"How do we leverage our sort of at least as it relates to fitness, our models and approaches to like behavior coaching or fitness models and take elements of that and combine it with AI?"**  
— Merisenda Alatorre (internal question discussed)  
*Significance:* This frames Ace Fitness's core strategic challenge—how to augment their human expertise and established coaching methodologies with AI at scale. The question reveals they're thinking about hybrid human-AI approaches rather than pure replacement.

**"The question isn't whether we can build this ourselves — it's whether we should, given opportunity cost."**  
— Vrijen Attawar (paraphrased framework)  
*Significance:* A fundamental decision framework for Merisenda's team as they consider building AI capabilities. Shifts the conversation from technical feasibility (can we?) to strategic prioritization (should we?), introducing opportunity cost as the key consideration.

**"How can we take elements of that so that at any time anyone going through our content can kind of have that, you know, learning assistant learning tool wherever they go?"**  
— Merisenda Alatorre  
*Significance:* The core product vision—democratizing access to human coaching expertise through AI. The question reveals the ambition to scale their coaching methodology to be available anytime, anywhere, not limited by human bandwidth.
