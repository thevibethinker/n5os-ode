# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

*High-threshold filter applied: Only genuinely provocative, non-obvious, strategically "weird" ideas.*

## 1. The Legitimacy Arbitrage
> **Core insight:** In talent marketplaces, having the *name* of a prestigious firm on your website may be worth more than their actual business contribution.

Superposition wouldn't bring users. They might not even bring significant revenue. But their logo creates a trust cascade. This is pure signaling economics—the brand IS the product.

**Provocative question:** What other "empty calories" legitimacy plays exist? Could Careerspan collect prestigious recruiter logos without meaningful partnerships?

---

## 2. The 500-User Moat
> **Counterintuitive reframe:** Small user bases can be positioning strengths, not weaknesses.

V's counter to "you only have 500 users" was essentially: "Yes, but they're the *right* 500." This inverts the network effects playbook. Instead of "we're valuable because we're big," it's "we're valuable because we're exclusive."

**Provocative question:** What if Careerspan *capped* user signups to maintain exclusivity positioning? The Soho House model for talent platforms.

---

## 3. The Cadaver Supply Chain Problem
> **From tangent, but applicable:** Logan's biotech friend disrupted her industry by going directly to morgues instead of through middlemen.

"My middleman is charging me too much money" → go direct to source.

**Provocative question:** Who is Careerspan's "middleman" that could be bypassed? Alumni associations themselves? Recruiters? LinkedIn?
