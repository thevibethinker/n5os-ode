# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"Welcome to Careerspan, you're already hired."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Describing the intended feeling for candidates entering the new Careerspan ecosystem. It reframes the platform from a job board to a privileged talent community where presence alone signals high quality.

**"The real proof point is not B2B. It is a person saying, yes, Careerspan is the reason I got cut through."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Emphasizing that candidate success stories are the ultimate validation of the platform's value, shifting focus from business features to individual outcomes.

**"Careerspan: the highest density of high agency talent on the Internet."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Proposing a core brand positioning statement. It targets the specific desire of founders for "high agency" individuals and sets a clear benchmark for the talent pool.

**"I'm all about elitist messaging. So like I'm fine with that. It's more that like, I think the open question is like, what are we optimizing for in the quickest way for the best outcome?"**  
— Logan Currie  
*Context:* Responding to Vrijen's suggestion of "semi-elitist messaging." Logan accepts the brand direction but steers the conversation back to tactical optimization and immediate results.

**"What we know to be true is... that sourcing is possible and we can lead with that."**  
— Logan Currie  
*Context:* Grounding the conversation in current capabilities amidst a discussion of many hypothetical future pivots. This statement identified sourcing as the proven "lead" for the business.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"Why would someone get it through a community [if the product is widely available]?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Questioning the current open-access model. This drove the controversial suggestion to close open signups and move to community-only access to create artificial scarcity and partner incentives.

**"What are we optimizing for in the quickest way for the best outcome?"**  
— Logan Currie  
*Context:* Challenging the "sexiest" long-term vision vs. the immediate need for revenue and proof points. It forced a discussion on whether the current strategy appeals more to startups than to the high-value enterprise clients they ultimately need.

**"Who would benefit from a company that has solved sourcing plus matching?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Analyzing the HR tech landscape (sourcing, verifying, matching, vetting) to identify where Careerspan's unique edge lies and who the most likely acquirers or high-value partners would be.
