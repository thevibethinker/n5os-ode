# B03_DECISIONS

## Decision 1: Keep Careerspan Alive Regardless of Acquisition Outcome

**DECISION:** Careerspan will continue pursuing acquisition, but if a deal does not happen, Vrijen will keep the company alive personally rather than let it die.
**CONTEXT:** The acquisition path has become less certain, with ETS quieter and Logan still shepherding the process. Vrijen stated he has made peace with the financial downside and views preserving Careerspan’s mission as more important than avoiding the cost.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar
**IMPLICATIONS:** Careerspan remains active under either outcome; parallel promotion to other potential acquirers will continue, and personal funding becomes the fallback plan if no buyer emerges.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Letting the company wind down if acquisition fails; relying solely on the current ETS path.

## Decision 2: Prioritize Large-Scale University Growth Work in Current Role

**DECISION:** Vrijen will align with the organization’s current priority of driving large-scale university growth, while reserving smaller-scale experimental work for evenings and weekends.
**CONTEXT:** Vrijen described disappointment that the mandate had shifted away from smaller-scale initiatives he had been doing, but acknowledged that the organization’s main objective is achieving very large signup volume by year-end.
**DECIDED BY:** Organizational leadership set the priority; Vrijen accepted and aligned to it.
**IMPLICATIONS:** Vrijen’s day-to-day effort will focus on scale-oriented growth work, while side initiatives relevant to Zo and related experiments will happen outside core working hours.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Continuing to emphasize smaller-scale projects within the main role; trying to expand the mandate immediately beyond university-scale growth.

## Decision 3: Frame the Joint Offer Around a Customizable Zo-Based Productivity System

**DECISION:** The core product/course direction will be a customizable personal productivity system built on Zo, rather than a narrower networking-only offering.
**CONTEXT:** During discussion, both aligned that a broader “customize your personal productivity suite” concept is more scalable and commercially compelling than staying confined to a networking niche. They agreed the value is providing a base package plus teaching users how to tailor it.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and David Speigel
**IMPLICATIONS:** The offering will center on giving users a Zo-based foundation, then teaching customization rather than delivering only one fixed workflow. This also differentiates the course from standard Claude-based AI instruction.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Staying in a networking-specific niche; teaching generic AI productivity without Zo as the base system.

## Decision 4: Use a Lightning Lesson as the Initial Go-to-Market Test

**DECISION:** They will start with a basic Lightning Lesson rather than committing immediately to a full course build.
**CONTEXT:** After exploring a larger Maven course idea, the conversation converged on using a lightweight Lightning Lesson as the fastest way to test demand, validate messaging, and reduce upfront commitment.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and David Speigel
**IMPLICATIONS:** The immediate next deliverable is a concise Lightning Lesson promise, not a full curriculum. This lowers setup time and creates a rapid market signal before deeper investment.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Building the full course first; waiting for a more complete product package before going public.

## Decision 5: Immediate Workflow for Launching the Lightning Lesson

**DECISION:** Vrijen will propose two or three candidate Zo-stack functionalities, David will review them the next morning to choose the teaching angle, Vrijen will package them into an installable repo, and then they will move toward launching the Lightning Lesson.
**CONTEXT:** They clarified that the lesson setup itself is minimal and only requires a strong title and three learning points. This led them to agree on a division of labor that supports a rapid launch.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and David Speigel
**IMPLICATIONS:** Work is now sequenced: functionality selection, positioning, packaging, then publishing. David will handle Maven-side setup; Vrijen will handle the underlying productization/export work.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Building slides first; delaying until the full system is polished; launching without packaging reusable installable components.

## Decision 6: Use Lightning Lessons as the Distribution Mechanism to Prove Value to Zo

**DECISION:** The near-term distribution strategy is to use free Lightning Lessons to drive Zo signups and demonstrate viability to Zo.
**CONTEXT:** Vrijen stated that Zo currently cares most about signups, and both agreed that a Maven course alone would not create the same direct signup effect as a free Lightning Lesson paired with a discount/incentive.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and David Speigel
**IMPLICATIONS:** Messaging and lesson design will optimize for signup conversion rather than only course revenue. Success will be measured partly by whether the lesson can attract roughly 100–200 attendees and validate Lightning Lessons as a scalable acquisition channel.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Relying primarily on paid Maven courses for distribution; positioning the effort as education-first rather than signup-first.
