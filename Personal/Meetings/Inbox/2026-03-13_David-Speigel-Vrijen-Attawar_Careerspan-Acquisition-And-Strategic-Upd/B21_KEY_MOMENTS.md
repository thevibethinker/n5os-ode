## Strategic Questions Raised

**"Does Zo get into security? Like, are they messaging on that?"**  
— David Speigel  
*Significance:* This question opened up one of the deepest strategic tensions in the conversation: whether Zo can credibly differentiate on privacy and IP protection. It mattered because it could shape both product positioning and course messaging, but it also exposed a gap between perceived and actual security guarantees.

**"Am I able to, does it have to run locally on my own machine for it not to do that?"**  
— David Speigel  
*Significance:* This pushed the discussion from branding into infrastructure reality. It forced a distinction between trust, architecture, and true data control—surfacing that some user concerns cannot be solved by positioning alone.

**"What do you need from me to move you forward on getting a Maven course that is Zo-enabled?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* This was a transition question from ideation to collaboration design. It signaled readiness to support execution and invited David to define the bottleneck, which helped clarify that the need was not just content but personal AI adoption and product framing.

**"What does everybody need? Do they need a three? Do they need a five? And then how do we make it theirs?"**  
— David Speigel  
*Significance:* This was arguably the most important product-design question in the meeting. It reframed the challenge from showcasing the full sophistication of Vrijen’s system to finding the minimum viable usefulness for a broader audience and then enabling customization.

**"What we can do is equip them not just with the tools, but with the understanding to collectively bootstrap their learnings."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* This was less a question than a strategic proposition, but it functioned as one for the business model: should the offer be software, training, community, or all three? It pointed toward a model where adoption compounds through shared learning rather than isolated use.

**"If we can get 100 to 200 folks on the lightning lesson... will prove out to Zo that this is a viable mechanism for them distributing the product."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* This introduced the core validation question behind the weekend push: can Lightning Lessons become a scalable top-of-funnel for Zo signups? It tied educational content directly to measurable distribution value, making the experiment legible to internal stakeholders.
