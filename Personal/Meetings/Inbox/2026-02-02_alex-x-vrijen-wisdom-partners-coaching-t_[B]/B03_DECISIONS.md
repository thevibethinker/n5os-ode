# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Continue Operating Through March/April Before Final Decision on Exit vs. Growth

**DECISION:** Continue running Careerspan through March/April to generate revenue and traction before deciding whether to pursue acquisition or continued growth.

**CONTEXT:** The company needs concrete signals to inform next steps. Current position is too early for favorable acquisition terms, but there's potential for the business to become a viable revenue generator. The timeframe allows for validation of the recruiter partnership model.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:** 
- Resources remain committed for 1-2 more months
- Focus shifts to revenue generation as demonstration of value
- Final decision point established for acquisition vs. continued operation
- Demonstrated revenue would improve saleability regardless of ultimate direction

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Pursuing immediate exit (rejected due to unfavorable expected terms), shutting down immediately (not seriously considered given depth of investment)

## Decision 2: Revenue Target as Litmus Test ($10K/month covering Ilsa's salary)

**DECISION:** Use ability to generate $10,000 in monthly revenue (sufficient to cover Ilsa's salary) by end of February as the key indicator for whether to continue pursuing the business or pivot to acquisition.

**CONTEXT:** Need an objective measure to cut through ambiguity about whether there's genuine market traction or just reluctance to quit. Revenue serves as concrete validation of product-market fit.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Clear success/failure metric established
- Revenue generation becomes primary focus for February
- If target is missed, acquisition becomes the preferred path
- Achieving target would indicate sufficient traction to justify continued operation

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Purely qualitative assessment of traction (rejected for being too subjective)

## Decision 3: 50/50 Commission Split Partnership with Shivam

**DECISION:** Split recruiting commissions 50/50 with Shivam on placements made through the Careerspan-powered pipeline.

**CONTEXT:** Shivam brings established recruiting business with candidate relationships and employer access. Vrijen provides matching technology and narrative generation. Revenue share aligns incentives without requiring upfront investment from either party.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Shivam (mutual agreement)

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- $3,000-$5,000 per placement to Careerspan (based on typical $7K-$10K commission)
- Need ~3 placements per month to hit revenue target
- Shivam becomes de facto distribution partner
- Model could be replicated with additional recruiters if proven successful

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** SaaS/subscription model (still considered ideal long-term but longer path to revenue), direct hiring without recruiter intermediary

## Decision 4: Prioritize Candidate Acquisition Over Job Acquisition

**DECISION:** Focus on rapidly building the candidate pipeline (getting candidates to complete two stories on Careerspan) rather than prioritizing job acquisition.

**CONTEXT:** The matching technology requires candidate data to function. Once candidates have two stories, they can be repeatedly matched against any incoming job description. Candidate-side growth is more scalable through automated systems.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar (with input from Shivam's stated bottleneck)

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Automation focus on candidate outreach and onboarding
- Build system to convert resume submissions into completed Careerspan profiles
- Shivam pushes analyzed candidates back through the system once they reach the two-story threshold
- Creates asset that compounds value as candidate pool grows

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Prioritizing job acquisition (rejected because jobs without matched candidates don't generate revenue; candidates can be stored and matched repeatedly)

## Decision 5: Recapitalization Option if Partnership Model Succeeds

**DECISION:** If the Shivam partnership demonstrates viable recurring revenue, explore recapitalizing Careerspan with Shivam as a co-founder/partner, potentially buying out Logan.

**CONTEXT:** Investors have indicated flexibility. A clean recapitalization could reset the cap table with Shivam and Vrijen as operators. This transforms the business from a founder-led startup to a cash-focused recruiting technology company.

**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar (conceptual agreement with investors, contingent on proof of revenue)

**IMPLICATIONS:**
- Potential path for Logan to exit with payout rather than continued involvement
- Business model shifts to cash generation rather than venture-scale growth
- Shivam transitions from contractor/partner to equity holder
- Preserves core technology while changing go-to-market approach

**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Continued three-founder structure with Logan, full acquisition by third party, shutting down without recapitalization
