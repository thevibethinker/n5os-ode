## Strategic Questions Raised

**"Do you think you're still interested in again, you start getting positive traction for the business? Are you still interested in this business or do you think you're kind of looking at doing something else?"**  
— Alex Caveny  
*Significance:* Direct inquiry that forced clarity on Vrijen's commitment level. This question bifurcated the conversation into "sell path" vs. "grow path" — two mutually exclusive strategic directions.

**"Do you have like a sort of a tactical execution plan for, let's just say the next two months of like the things you want to try just sort of like throw stuff at the wall and see what sticks type of breadth strategy?"**  
— Alex Caveny  
*Significance:* Pressed Vrijen to move from abstract planning to concrete weekly actions. Exposed that much of the "plan" was still in formation.

**"Which side do you think you could supercharge best? Do you think you could bring in a lot of interesting candidates, need to get filtered or a lot of jobs people can apply to?"**  
— Alex Caveny  
*Significance:* Classic marketplace chicken-and-egg question. Forced prioritization of supply (candidates) vs. demand (jobs) — a critical strategic decision for two-sided platforms.

**"What's the main barrier to Super Shivam right now?"**  
— Alex Caveny  
*Significance:* Focused inquiry on the bottleneck in Vrijen's current growth strategy. Revealed that the constraint wasn't technology or matching capability, but candidate volume with sufficient profile depth.

**"What's his core slowdown? Let's just say there's going to be more Shivams, but for now you're trying to create Super Shivam so that he has a lot of value for hirers and states, so that he gets more jobs so that you get more cut. What's the main barrier?"**  
— Alex Caveny  
*Significance:* Forced articulation of the value chain and where friction exists. Led to the insight that the real problem was helping candidates get past the resume barrier — the fundamental value proposition of Careerspan.
