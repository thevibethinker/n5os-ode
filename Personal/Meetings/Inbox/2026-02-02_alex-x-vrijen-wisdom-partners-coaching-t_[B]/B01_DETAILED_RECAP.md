# B01: Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
An advisory session between Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan) and Alex Caveny exploring Careerspan's strategic crossroads: whether to pursue acquisition or attempt a revenue-driven turnaround over the next two months. The conversation covered AI industry trends, an introduction for a potential advisor match, and a deep tactical dive into V's new partnership with Shivam—a sourcer using Careerspan's matching technology to differentiate Indian engineering candidates.

## Chronological Discussion

### Opening & Advisor Introduction (0:00-3:00)
V began by sharing an introduction for Julie, an ex-CMO with parent/family tech experience, as a potential advisor for a founder in Alex's network. Alex noted the founder is currently taking a break due to the recent violence in Minneapolis, explaining she had to explain to her 9-year-old daughter why someone was murdered in the streets—a sobering reminder of how external circumstances can derail startup progress. V acknowledged this as a "complete act of God" disruption and encouraged Alex to connect with Julie directly regardless, as she's well-connected in the Boston tech scene.

### Puerto Rico & Zo Team Offsite (3:00-9:00)
V shared he's in Puerto Rico supporting the Zo Computer team's offsite, serving as both advisor and "lab rat" power user. He described observing how engineering-oriented leaders structure their retreats, calling it valuable learning. When Alex asked about team size, V estimated 7-8 people and mentioned they're planning to raise off "vibes." He characterized the product as "Claudebot but with guardrails"—positioning Zo as the iPhone to the Android-style "wild west" open ecosystem of AI agents.

The discussion pivoted to the recent "Moltbach/Claudebot" phenomenon that exploded while Alex was skiing. Alex described deconstructing the hype and landing on a key insight: the magic isn't just long-standing instructions or memory, but the "heartbeat" feature that allows the agent to wake up and take autonomous action. V agreed, emphasizing that the aliveness—the fact that "you walk away and come back and stuff has happened"—is what captured people's imagination.

### AI Security & The Antivirus Era (9:00-12:00)
Both expressed concern about the security implications of AI agents operating with minimal guardrails. Alex noted that LLMs are "very people-pleasing by nature, which is not security by any means," referencing reports of backdoors in plugins and private keys being stolen simply by asking. V drew a parallel to the forgotten "antivirus era," observing that most people have forgotten basic opsec and may need to relearn fundamental security practices for the AI age. Alex concluded that while Moltbach highlights genuine vulnerabilities, it also demonstrates usage stats that reveal real demand—even for flawed products.

### Careerspan Strategic Update (12:00-18:00)
V pivoted to the core business discussion, sharing that he's been exploring two parallel paths: acquisition talks (specifically with Future Fit, though they've gone silent after an MRR inquiry) and a new operational partnership with Shivam, a sourcer for Indian engineers. V described Shivam as having "been better co-founder than either me or Logan" in terms of energy and execution—meeting V at his level and consistently delivering candidates and employer relationships.

The model: Shivam brings candidates and some jobs, V brings additional jobs and Careerspan's matching capability. They're splitting recruiting commissions 50/50, with typical contracts worth $7-10K per placement. The goal is three placements per month to cover Ilse's salary and demonstrate revenue viability.

Alex pressed V on a critical strategic question: "Are you still interested in this business or are you just looking for a decent exit for everybody?" V acknowledged he'd accept an exit now if the terms weren't "garbage," but expressed genuine belief that there are "embers of what working"—particularly validated by Shivam's willingness to invest unpaid time because he sees Careerspan as essential to differentiating his candidates in a crowded market.

### Tactical Planning & The Shivam MVP (18:00-32:00)
Alex emphasized the importance of choosing one path and executing tactically—either full sell-mode positioning or growth-mode customer acquisition. V outlined his immediate focus: building an automated pipeline that converts resume submissions into Careerspan users with precise guidance on which stories to tell. The goal is rapidly moving candidates to the "two story minimum" threshold so Shivam can push detailed analyses back to employers.

When Alex probed Shivam's core pain point pre-Careerspan, V clarified: Shivam's barrier was employers not looking past resumes to take his candidates seriously. The value Careerspan provides is creating "a really compelling narrative for the hirer about why this is the right candidate for the role."

Alex advised focusing on supercharging one side of the marketplace—either candidates or jobs—to attract the other. V confirmed he's building tools to accelerate candidate onboarding, believing that "if we can very quickly get candidates to that two story mark, then we can repeatedly scan against them, which gives us more people that pop every time we get an employer."

## Key Takeaways

- **Strategic Crossroads**: V faces a February/March decision point—prove revenue viability (~3 placements/month covering Ilse's salary) or pursue acquisition on potentially unfavorable terms
- **Shivam Partnership**: A 50/50 revenue split model where Shivam (sourcer) brings candidates and jobs while V brings matching technology and additional employer relationships; represents the most promising near-term revenue path
- **Core Value Proposition Validated**: Shivam's unpaid investment of time signals market demand for differentiation beyond resumes—he needs Careerspan to make his candidates stand out
- **Tactical Focus**: Building automated infrastructure to rapidly onboard candidates to the "two story minimum" threshold, enabling scalable matching against incoming job descriptions
- **Market Context**: The recruiting/hiring space is flooded with "slop" products, making distribution and trust-building critical challenges for any player
- **Future Optionality**: V raised the possibility of recapitalizing Careerspan with Shivam as a co-founder if the revenue model proves out, potentially buying out Logan if he prefers to exit
