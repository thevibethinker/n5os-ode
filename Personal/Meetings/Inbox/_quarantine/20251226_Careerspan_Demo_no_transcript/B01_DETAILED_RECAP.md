# B01: Detailed Recap - Careerspan Demo & CMC Strategy

## Overview
Vrijen Attawar (V) presented a demo of Careerspan to Michael Maher and the CMC (Career Management Center) team at Johnson. The meeting focused on how Careerspan's AI-driven platform can automate the "nuts and bolts" of career coaching—specifically resume tailoring and interview prep—to allow CMC counselors to operate at a higher level of strategic abstraction.

## Discussion Timeline

### 1. Introduction & Context
- **V's Background:** Johnson '22 alum, former consultant, background in college admissions and career coaching (Singapore).
- **CMC Context:** Michael Maher introduced the team (Amanda, Victoria, Chase) and explained they are currently assessing their "toolbox" of resources, starting with resume tools.
- **Problem Statement:** Traditional resume tailoring is laborious for both students and counselors. CMC has limited time/availability to help every student flesh out their deep work stories.

### 2. The Careerspan Demo (User Experience)
- **Persona:** Demoed using a simulated account for "Donna" (Product Manager).
- **Core User Motions:**
    - **Job Analysis:** Students drop in a JD and receive an alignment analysis.
    - **Storytelling (The "Yap" Interface):** Students engage in conversational "yapping" with the AI coach (via microphone or text) to flesh out work stories.
- **AI Intelligence:** The system is adaptive and "exists on a meaning level," not just keyword matching. It draws out specific attributes (e.g., diligence, proactiveness) and creates STAR-formatted interview answers and resume bullets.
- **Explainability:** Unlike other tools (VMock mentioned), Careerspan explains *why* a specific bullet or change is recommended for a particular job.

### 3. The Counselor View (CMC Interface)
- **Granular Insights:** Counselors can see a student's activity, the stories they've told, tailored resumes generated, and a graph of their readiness.
- **Gap Analysis:** Visually illustrates gaps (e.g., "this skill is on your resume but you don't have a story for it").
- **Narrative Positioning:** The AI generates a "workable angle" or positioning for the candidate based on all their stories.

### 4. Strategic Pivot & Hiring
- **Shift to Hiring:** V mentioned a reorientation of the product toward hiring/matching.
- **Value for CMC:** Alums can share "magic links" for jobs; if a student has told 2+ stories on Careerspan, they can be instantly matched and presented to employers via a portfolio portal.
- **Efficiency:** Reduces latency between an alum reaching out and receiving qualified candidate names.

### 5. Deep Dive: Resume Tailoring
- **Consistency vs. Customization:** Michael questioned if resumes differ significantly between roles (e.g., PM vs. Consultant).
- **Semantics over Keywords:** V emphasized that Careerspan rephrases experiences based on the *attributes* the company cares about, while maintaining internal consistency to prevent "hallucinations" or conflicting versions of the same person.

### 6. Business Model & Partnership
- **Monetization:** Primary revenue comes from employers.
- **CMC Pricing:** V expressed flexibility, suggesting a model based on student usage + fixed percentage, aiming to be more cost-effective than incumbents like VMock.
- **Evidence:** Mentioned positive results/stats from UMass Amherst undergrad business students and ongoing co-design with Emory's Alumni Association.
