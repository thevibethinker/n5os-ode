# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

- **Mineral Extraction Analogy:** If 99% of a data stream (resumes) is "crap," the cost of finding the "gold" (1%) must be extremely high. AI can't change the quality of the raw material; it can only help sort it. Therefore, the win is changing the extraction method (input) rather than the sorting algorithm.
- **The Acceleration Trap:** Acceleration is priced in. If you grow too fast on paper, firms pay a premium that forces you into a "stupid price" valuation. This can lead to a "liquidation preference" trap where founders get nothing in a mid-tier exit.
- **Recessions as Forest Fires:** Recessions serve a vital function in "clearing the deck" of crappy ideas and players, allowing higher-quality signals to emerge.
- **Coaching as a Data Stream:** Coaching is often diluted into "advice," but its true power lies in helping people build a "personal narrative" which generates high-signal data points (200+) that resumes can't capture.
