# B01: Detailed Recap - Jonathan Beda x Vrijen Attawar

## Executive Summary
This meeting was a catch-up between Vrijen Attawar and Jonathan Beda, former colleagues/acquaintances from Emory. Jonathan shared his journey from operator to VC, having raised a 0M fund. Vrijen pitched Careerspan, focusing on its recent pivot toward supporting employers and VCs with high-signal candidate matching that reduces screening friction. They discussed the brutal funding market, the flaws of existing ATS systems like Getro, and potential collaboration opportunities between Careerspan and Jonathan's portfolio.

## Key Discussion Points

### Jonathan Beda's Career Trajectory
- **Zumba & Zoombini:** Jonathan worked at Zumba and spun out a company called Zoombini in early childhood education.
- **Fundraising:** Transitioned to doing SPVs opportunistically, which led to raising a 0M fund.
- **Investment Strategy:** Focuses on post-product market fit companies (typically M–M ARR) with a "finance angle." They often co-invest alongside larger funds to mitigate risk while providing meaningful customer connections.
- **Market Sentiment:** Jonathan described the current funding market as "vicious," noting that even winners struggle to raise subsequent rounds compared to the "paper markups" of 2021.

### Careerspan Pivot & Value Proposition
- **The Problem:** Job seekers are difficult to monetize, and traditional ATS systems (resumes) are low-signal, leading to wasted founder time on shitty/fake candidates.
- **The Solution:** Careerspan uses an AI-driven coaching/reflection loop to gather ~200 discrete data points per candidate, delivering a "Why say Yes/No" analysis.
- **Traction:** Signed a deal with Marvin Ventures (McKinsey alumni fund). Emory is also looking to provide it to alumni.
- **VC Use Case:** Enabling "smart referrals" within a VC's network and making inbound recruiting viable by filtering for sincerity and depth through the reflection loop.

### Industry Insights
- **ATS Criticism:** Vrijen argued that resumes are technologically impossible to fix with AI alone without asking for more data from candidates.
- **Getro:** Jonathan uses Getro but views it as "shallow marketing" or a "badge of legitimacy" rather than a sourcing tool. Vrijen suggested pattern disruption is better than following the Getro standard.
- **Founder Mistakes:** Jonathan noted that founders often misunderstand how round size implies valuation and ownership, sometimes sitting under heavy "prep stacks" that leave them with nothing upon acquisition.

### Relationship & Networking
- **Emory Connections:** Discussed several Emory alumni including Greg (Beacons), Dan Idell (dbt), and Faison Body (Halo Cars).
- **Future Collaboration:** Jonathan offered to connect Vrijen with VCs when he is ready to fundraise and requested his materials. Vrijen offered to help Jonathan's portfolio companies with talent pipelines.
