# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

- **The "Thirsty Snake" Principle:** Sometimes obstacles (or competitors) aren't malicious; they just have a basic need (thirst) that can be solved with a simple gesture, shifting the relationship from conflict to cooperation.
- **Sociability of Tools:** The preference for Google Docs over Notion because it "looks like a piece of paper" and feels like a "collab" highlights that user experience in productivity tools is often about emotional familiarity and technical "lightness" rather than pure feature depth.
- **The "Hallmark Holiday Movie" Acquisition:** Viewing acquisition suitors through the lens of romantic tropes (the billionaire next door, the mission-aligned partner) provides a useful semantic framework for prioritizing leads based on the "story" of the potential exit.
