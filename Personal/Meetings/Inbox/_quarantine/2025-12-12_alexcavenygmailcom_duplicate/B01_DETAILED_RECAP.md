# B01: Detailed Recap - Alex Caveny x Vrijen Attawar (Wisdom Partners Coaching)

## Executive Summary
This coaching session between Alex Caveny (Wisdom Partners) and Vrijen Attawar focused on Careerspan's go-to-market strategy, brand identity, and the psychology of sales. Vrijen reported positive early signals from users telling "stories" to apply for jobs, validating a core product assumption. Alex provided strategic advice on leaning into V's authentic personality as a brand differentiator, "selling the hole, not the shovel," and locking in follow-up meetings immediately during interactions.

## Detailed Narrative

### 1. Product Validation & Early Traction
Vrijen shared that Careerspan now has almost a dozen jobs listed. A critical milestone was reached: users are actually completing the "stories" required for applications. Vrijen noted that 15-20% of applicants are going "above and beyond" by filling out stories, which Alex interpreted as a very positive signal in a market dominated by blind applications. They discussed how job-seeking behavior reflects "intrinsic agency," with the jaded "blind appliers" on one side and those seeking a better process on the other.

### 2. Personal Branding & "Secret Sauce"
Alex prompted Vrijen to identify his "secret sauce" in sales and networking. Vrijen identified his genuine curiosity about almost any problem and his comfort level with diverse types of people (from bouncers to CEOs). Alex advised that at this early stage, Vrijen's personality *is* the brand identity. He encouraged Vrijen to lean into his grounded, empathetic, and unassuming nature to cut through the noise of "stodgy HR platforms" and "aggressive tech bros."

### 3. Sales Strategy: "Sell the Hole, Not the Shovel"
Alex shared insights from a recent Wisdom Partners sales workshop. A key takeaway was the principle of "selling the hole, not the shovel"—focusing on the customer's specific hiring pain points before presenting the Careerspan solution. They discussed how founders often feel "ashamed" both to ask for what they really want in a candidate and to appear as a "slave driver." The proposed framing for Careerspan:
- **Employer Side:** "Ask for what you really want."
- **Candidate Side:** "Show what you've really got."

### 4. Conversion & Agency
Alex emphasized the importance of maintaining agency during sales interactions. He advised against the "pleasant conversation followed by a LinkedIn message" pattern, which often leads to stagnation. Instead, he recommended "locking in" the next meeting while face-to-face or on the call, leveraging the social pressure of immediate commitment.

### 5. Website & Marketing Refinement
The duo brainstormed ways to make the Careerspan website reflect a two-sided marketplace more effectively. Alex noted that the current "above the fold" content feels like a generic recruiting platform. They discussed visualizing the "time distribution" of hiring to show where the waste occurs (screening) and how Careerspan recaptures those "100 man-hours."

## Strategic Takeaways
- **Authenticity as Competitive Advantage:** Vrijen's natural empathy and "build in public" approach are key differentiators against incumbents.
- **Problem-First Selling:** Transition from explaining the product to identifying the "hiring woe" first.
- **Immediate Commitment:** Shift to a more assertive approach in scheduling follow-up calls to prevent lead decay.
- **Visualizing Value:** Develop a "one-pager" or visual (e.g., Sankey diagram) comparing the traditional hiring workflow vs. Careerspan.
