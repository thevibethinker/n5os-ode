# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"The way you job seek is a reflection of, to a limited extent, the level of intrinsic agency you have as a human being."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Discussing why 15-20% of users filling out "stories" is a high-signal indicator of the type of candidates Careerspan attracts.

**"You have to sell the hole, not the shovel."**  
— Alex Caveny  
*Context:* Sharing a core sales principle from a recent workshop; advises V to focus on the customer's specific hiring pain points before presenting the product.

**"While you're talking to them, you have agency over the next steps in the process. If you let them leave and just try to shoot them a message, you have no more agency. You have no leverage."**  
— Alex Caveny  
*Context:* Explaining why scheduling the next meeting *during* the current interaction is a critical sales tactic.

**"I'm going to shoot both of you. So if you want to live, you must compromise now."**  
— Alex Caveny  
*Context:* Describing his former role as CPO (Product) acting as the bridge/mediator between a CEO who wants the impossible and a CTO who says everything is impossible.

**"I think I could like get better at like hey, before you go into an interaction, just like take a deep breath and just talk to this person and like see what naturally sort of emerges."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Identifying how to leverage his "secret sauce" (genuine interest) in sales conversations by reducing performance anxiety.

**"What do you think is your secret sauce when talking to people?"**  
— Alex Caveny  
*Significance:* Forced V to articulate his unique brand advantages, leading to the insight that "authenticity" is a major differentiator in the SF tech scene.

**"Is this person crazy or not crazy enough?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* Discussing the "dark triad" of founder skills; explores the fine line between the "dark arts" of salesmanship and the necessity of high-energy persistence.

**"What would you do with like that those hundred hours back?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar (following Alex's prompt)  
*Significance:* A powerful framing for future marketing materials; shifts the value proposition from "recruiting tool" to "reclaiming life/time."
