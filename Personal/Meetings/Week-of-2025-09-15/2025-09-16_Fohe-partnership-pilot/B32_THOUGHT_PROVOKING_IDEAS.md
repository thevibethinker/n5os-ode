# B32 Thought Provoking Ideas: Fohe Partnership Pilot

## 1. The Resume as a Structural Bias
Vrijen’s point that resumes are "inarguably" biased against qualitative strengths suggests that the current hiring infrastructure isn't just inefficient—it's exclusionary for specific personality types and skill sets (non-data/non-engineering).

## 2. "Common App" for Careers
If Careerspan can truly achieve the "Common App" level of ubiquity and trust, it shifts the power dynamic from the employer (who currently forces the applicant into their specific silo) back to the applicant (who brings their verified "Story" to the table).

## 3. The "ATS Lottery" vs. The "Second Look"
Logan’s framing of "queue-skipping" as the "X-factor" acknowledges a grim reality: for many high-quality candidates, the goal isn't to be "the best," it's simply to be "seen." This suggests that "Attention" is the scarcest resource in the hiring market.

## 4. Supply-Side Job Ethics
Jake's concern about blasting 5,000 people when supply is limited highlights an ethical tension in career platforms: is it better to give 5,000 people "hope" or to give 50 people "results"? This pilot must solve for this "disappointment management."
