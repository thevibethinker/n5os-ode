# B01: Detailed Recap

## Overview
The meeting focused on defining proof points for Careerspan's value proposition, specifically how to demonstrate the "Careerspan lift" to employers and communities. The team debated the effectiveness of showcasing "archetype" candidates versus actual placement success stories. There was a strong consensus that "butts in seats" (actual hires) are the primary success metric needed to prove the model.

## Key Discussion Points
- **Showcasing Non-Traditional Candidates:** Logan proposed finding examples of "badass" users who wouldn't be considered based on resumes but are high-value. Vrijen countered that while valuable for candidate persuasion, employers might find this signal weak without an existing relationship or actual placement.
- **Success Categories:** Logan defined three categories of success for tracking:
    1. **Pre-screening:** Moving from the resume pile into the process.
    2. **Active Process:** Passing pre-screening and interviewing.
    3. **Offer/Hiring:** Getting the job (the ultimate goal, though currently longer-term).
- **Careerspan Select (Internal Shorthand):** The team discussed "Careerspan Select," an internal name for a specific inflow of candidates (e.g., friends of friends) captured via a Typeform. This is used to prove the motion of pushing people into the Careerspan system.
- **Community Engagement:** Vrijen suggested sponsoring memberships for engineering, data, and design communities (like Lenny's Newsletter/Community) for the team to "dogfood" the product and learn how to sell to those groups.
- **Product Adjustments:** Ilse planned to update "vibe checks" to specifically ask about relocation openness and track deal-breakers more effectively in a spreadsheet.

## Strategic Shift
The team decided to deprioritize the "archetype" project (manually finding and telling stories of candidates) in favor of focusing on actual sales and placements. The "lift" of Careerspan is best demonstrated through successful hires rather than theoretical potential.
