# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

## The "Select" Friction Ratio
Logan noted that the "Careerspan Select" form takes only 3 minutes, but it requires users to then create a full Careerspan account to be considered. This "automated push" is a clever way to convert casual referrals into platform users. *Question: How can we measure the drop-off between the form completion and the account creation to optimize this conversion?*

## Dogfooding as Sales Training
Vrijen's idea to sponsor community memberships isn't just for networking; it's to "sell to that group or build for that group." *Insight: Product and sales empathy can be engineered by immersing non-technical team members in the digital "watering holes" of their target users.*

## Time to Hire as the Ultimate Success Metric
Logan mentioned tracking the time from pre-screening to offer. *Opportunity: If Careerspan can prove a significantly shorter time-to-hire than traditional recruiters, that becomes a more powerful B2B lever than candidate quality alone.*

## The "Relocation" Gap
The realization that "vibe checks" were missing people open to relocating suggests a potential systemic bias in early-stage automated matching. *Action: Auditing previous "vibe check" failures to see if high-quality candidates were filtered out solely on location could provide a quick win for the candidate pool.*
