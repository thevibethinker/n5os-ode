# B05: Open Questions

## Critical Business Questions

### 1. How Should Careerspan Position vs. Large ATS Players?
**Context:** Ilya raised the strategic pattern observed across industries where B2B SaaS companies split into two camps: "full volume/everything to you" (integration-free monoliths) vs. "boutique/quality/specialized" (integration-heavy, focused on signal vs. noise). Greenhouse appears to be in the former camp but struggling with AI economics.

**Specific Question:** Which positioning should Careerspan optimize for? Are we the "boutique" player that integrates and focuses on quality, or are we the specialized play that can be the front door to broader ATS ecosystems?

**Owner for Resolution:** Likely V and Ilya (strategic positioning)

---

### 2. What Is Greenhouse's Actual AI Capability?
**Context:** V challenged Logan to dig deeper during the demo on actual technical capability rather than sales narratives.

**Specific Questions:**

- Can they demonstrably show filtering candidates beyond keyword matching?

### 3. Are Token Economics Making Resume AI Unviable at Scale?
**Context:** V hypothesized that large ATS players (like Greenhouse) recognize that analyzing high-volume, low-quality resume data using token-based LLMs is economically unviable.

**Specific Questions:**
- At what volume does this economics flip?
- Is this a permanent constraint or might LLM costs drop enough to make it viable?
- Should Careerspan be planning around this constraint as a permanent feature of the market?

**Owner for Resolution:** Ilya (technical/market analysis) or V (strategic)
