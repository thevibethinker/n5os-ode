## Key Decisions and Agreements

**1. Focus on concrete proof points over abstract claims**
- **Decision:** Lead with specific examples of successful candidate placements that demonstrate value rather than theoretical positioning.
- **Why it matters:** Employers need concrete evidence that Careerspan's matching identifies exceptional candidates—this is more compelling than generic value prop messaging.
- **Rationale:** Logan and team emphasized real examples create credibility. Logan: "You have a great candidate. Like, so what?" — meaning employers care about *results*, not promises.

**2. Tier proof points by use case**
- **Decision:** Structure examples by role type (e.g., Chief of Staff placements where candidate wouldn't have been considered based on resume alone).
- **Why it matters:** Employers evaluate candidates differently by role; showing Careerspan's match quality across diverse roles demonstrates matching sophistication.
- **Rationale:** Ilse's framing: "That's sales. Right. Here's an example of what we did for this chief of staff role. Now we can do this for you if you pay us money." This approach converts proof → CTA.

**3. Prioritize employer relationships before scaling proof points**
- **Decision:** Build deeper relationships with employers first; proof points are valuable once relationships exist and trust is established.
- **Why it matters:** Employers won't seek out proof points cold; relationship foundation is prerequisite for proof point persuasion.
- **Rationale:** Vrijen noted that unless employers are *already engaged*, examples alone won't drive acquisition. The proof points become persuasive once we have employer ear.

## Strategic Context

The team is debating the core sales motion for employer acquisition. Two viewpoints emerged:

**Logan's perspective:** Focus on proof (showing we find exceptional candidates) as the primary persuasion lever for employers.

**Vrijen's perspective:** Proof points are valuable for demonstrating matching capability to top talent *and* for employer persuasion once relationship exists, but employer acquisition likely requires prior relationship cultivation.

**Consensus:** Proof points matter, but they're most effective as *sales enablement* within an existing conversation, not as cold acquisition drivers. The sequence is: build relationship → share proof → convert to paid.

**Implicit tension:** How do we *get* the initial employer conversation if we're not leading with proof? (Unresolved)
