**Perspective:** Careerspan internal team reflecting on employer acquisition strategy and market positioning

## Key Landscape Insights

### 1. **Employer Skepticism Toward Generic Value Props**
- **Observation:** Employers won't be persuaded by abstract claims of candidate quality; they need specific proof
- **Implication:** Career platforms must lead with evidence, not promises. This increases burden of proof for early-stage platforms
- **Strategic Value:** Suggests Careerspan's differentiator (we can show *how* we find great candidates) is more valuable than generic "better matching" messaging

### 2. **Proof Points as Sales Sequence, Not Standalone Tactic**
- **Observation:** Proof points work best within a relationship context (Ilse's sales framing: show example → explain value → ask for business)
- **Implication:** Cold outreach with proof points likely converts poorly; warm intros likely work better
- **Strategic Value:** Suggests Careerspan may need relationship/referral-based GTM more than cold acquisition at this stage

### 3. **Candidate Quality as Credibility Signal for Role Types**
- **Observation:** Employers evaluate candidates differently by role (Chief of Staff success stories different from Engineer success stories)
- **Implication:** Generic case studies won't work; proof points need to be role-specific
- **Strategic Value:** Opportunity to build role-specific playbooks that compound over time (Chief of Staff playbook → Chief of Staff proof points → Chief of Staff employer playbook)

### 4. **Resume Limitations Create Selection Bias**
- **Observation:** Strongest evidence of Careerspan value is candidates who wouldn't pass resume screening but succeed in role
- **Implication:** This is a powerful signal but requires post-hire validation (outcomes data)
- **Strategic Value:** Early focus should be on measuring placement success + building outcome evidence before broad marketing
