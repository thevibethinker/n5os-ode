# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

## Idea 1: The "Third Dimension" of Candidate Data
Vrijen’s concept of capturing the **"edges and nodes"** between career data points (rather than just the points themselves) suggests that hiring is currently a 2D exercise (resumes) trying to solve a 3D problem (human fit). By using AI to extract the context of skill deployment, Careerspan creates a "third dimension" of unfakable verified expertise.

## Idea 2: Longitudinal Identity Verification as a Moat
The idea that a multi-year, storytelling-based relationship with a candidate is the best form of **ID verification** provides a non-technical solution to a deeply technical problem (AI-generated deepfakes in hiring). If a candidate has a "verified career history" built over time on an independent platform, they become more valuable than a candidate with a one-off "verified" LinkedIn profile.

## Idea 3: Digitization vs. Innovation in Recruit-Tech
Nic’s observation that most "innovation" in recruiting has just been **digitization** (moving paper to PDF) highlights why the current AI wave is so disruptive. For the first time, the tech isn't just "storing" the data; it's "interpreting" the data, which shifts the recruiter's role from a "filter" to a "facilitator" of human connection.
