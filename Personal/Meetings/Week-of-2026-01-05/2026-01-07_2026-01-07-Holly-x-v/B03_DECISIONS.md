## Decision 2: Geographic Expansion and Distribution Strategy (Holly/Matchbox)

**DECISION:** Holly decided to focus her product distribution efforts on building a candidate-facing job board and talent network to aggregate data, specifically targeting the US market.

**IMPLICATIONS:** Matchbox will prioritize features like the AI interview/transcript tool to build a pre-vetted candidate pool of 10,000+ people, which can then be monetized via recruiter access fees or recruitment rates.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Selling the tech immediately to a competitor (this was considered but she is currently leaning toward buying the competitor's tech instead to reshape/sell).

## Decision 3: Strategic Partnership Calibration (V & Holly)

**DECISION:** V and Holly agreed to test their respective talent pools against each other's open roles (Agave and Deep Tune) to calibrate their matching technology.
**CONTEXT:** Both founders have talent databases but need to prove the efficacy of their matching/scanning technology to potential buyers or clients.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Holly Sanders
**IMPLICATIONS:** V will scan Holly's open roles against the Careerspan database; any successful placements will be handled via a "split share" agreement.

## Decision 5: Visibility-First Value Proposition

**DECISION:** Both founders aligned on "Visibility" (providing deeper insight into candidates than anyone else via transcripts/data) as the core competitive advantage of their technology over legacy resume-based systems.
**CONTEXT:** Traditional signals (Stanford, Stripe, YC) have become too expensive; the market needs "hidden gems" identified through richer behavioral and trajectory data.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Holly Sanders (Consensus)
**IMPLICATIONS:** Both will continue to prioritize candidate-focused UX that encourages deep data investment (transcripts/stories) over standard "AI slop" or video interviews.
