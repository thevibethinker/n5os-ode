# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

## 1. Engagement as the True Signal
The team identified that recruiting agencies are increasingly drowning in sourcing tools but starving for **engagement**. The idea that a platform's value is derived not from the *breadth* of its database but from the *open rate* of its communications suggests a shift from a "Search Engine" model to an "Attention Engine" model.

## 2. Rebranding via "Community Sourcing"
The concept of rebranding not by changing names, but by "making a big fuss" about sourcing from exclusive communities. This uses exclusivity as a proxy for signal, effectively creating a "Velvet Rope" effect for recruiters looking for high-caliber talent.

## 3. The "Creepiness" Threshold in AI Personalization
A strategic realization that hyper-personalization has a diminishing return when it crosses the "uncanny valley" of privacy. Maintaining a "vibe" that respects the user while providing value to the recruiter is a delicate balance that could be a significant competitive moat if managed correctly.
