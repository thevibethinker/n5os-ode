# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"You need to accept that someone who's using Careerspan because of you might be getting a job because of another recruiter... That's the floor that the people's job."**  
— Them  
*Context:* Discussing the need for V to relinquish "incumbent power" and control over the candidate pool to allow the platform to scale as a true marketplace.

**"Hey, you've got a job. You're not able to close it. Fuck it. Throw $100 punt at seeing if Careerspan can pull up a candidate for you."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Framing the low-friction entry point for recruiters to test Careerspan's value proposition against traditional, expensive tools like LinkedIn Recruiter.

**"If you just call it what it is: We have a thousand shitty users, we are just costing money because no one is going to get good results."**  
— Them  
*Context:* A blunt assessment of the risk of scaling job volume without maintaining a high-quality, "stellar" candidate base, leading to wasted compute costs and poor outcomes.

**"The idea of getting into users' heads: You're always being scanned. You're always being scouted."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Defining the psychological shift for job seekers on the platform—moving from active searching to a state of continuous, passive discovery by recruiters.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"Where do we expect the most scale most rapidly?"**  
— Them  
*Significance:* This question forced a discussion on the technical and financial trade-offs between job volume and applicant volume, specifically regarding OpenAI API costs.

**"Does increasing one and reducing the other... scale in the same way?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* A probing question regarding the internal "math" of the scanning engine, surfacing how the platform's cost structure changes based on the ratio of jobs to candidates.

**"Is this something you'd be willing to fucking pay us for right here, right now?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* A foundational "sales-first" question aimed at validating the immediate market demand from recruiters before building further infrastructure.
