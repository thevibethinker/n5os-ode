### Careerspan Evolution (~5:00-15:00) — Strategic Pivot Discussion
Ivor prompts V about "CareersBank" (Careerspan) and its evolution since their May dinner at the Chinese restaurant.

**Key pivot explanation:**
- Original hypothesis VALIDATED: conversational modality is the correct way to capture human data
- Strategic gap identified: going B2C to job seekers under duress was problematic
  - Crowded marketplace with "dog shit" auto-apply tools
  - Hard to differentiate; loyalty but no revenue
- Pivot to HIRING side: realized they've built a high-signal community
  - ~3-4K users, 1/3+ with multiple stories
  - Deep data unlike any other platform
  - All from curated communities (alumni associations) with zero marketing

**Current positioning:**
- Exploring making database scannable (alternative to Sales Navigator)
- Present state: send JD via email → Careerspan runs against database → returns matches
- Future state: integration/plugin model

### Product Demo (~15:00-30:00) — Live Walkthrough
V shares screen to show employer-side interface:
- JD semantic breakdown (why role needed, success criteria, skills map)
- Skills as "co-occurring clusters" 
- Candidate matching with threshold control (e.g., 70%+ only see job)
- Candidate view: strengths/weaknesses relative to specific job
- Quality of insight: "101 on who you're getting" from actual reflections

**V's pitch framework:**
- "Subtracting cognitive effort" from resume review
- Everything framed as "why say yes / why say no"
- High-signal, low-volume approach

### Market Analysis & Critique (~20:00-25:00) — Ivor's Feedback
Ivor challenges: 4,000 people isn't huge.

V's counterarguments:
1. Average candidate value is higher (no shotgun applicants)
2. Behavioral selection: people who do the work of telling stories are inherently higher quality
3. Companies will never invest in true AI for resume scanning—99% crap for 1% signal
4. Even that 1% could be totally fake now with AI

Ivor reframes: the real problem is how companies ATTRACT top talent (the 1-20% who have options).

V agrees and articulates his "black pill" thesis: ATS reality limits true insight into candidates before talking to them. Video interviews just get flattened into summaries anyway.

### Strategic Vision (~25:00-35:00) — Long-term Play Discussion
Ivor's "off the cuff" strategic advice:
- **Capture every college graduate** from today going back 18-24 months
- Position as career coach/journal/notebook from first job to retirement
- 6-month value vs. 6-year value proposition
- Users become "fans and advocates"
- Requires YEARS to build but enormously valuable

V confirms this was exactly their original vision ("career companion angle"). Explains why they moved away:
- Also tried selling to universities when Trump admin was pressuring higher eds
- Budgets tightened; "one damn thing after another"

V shares his macro thesis using football (soccer) academy analogy:
- Academies lock down regional talent pipelines
- Future of work: shrinking worker pool → consolidation of power
- "99 people with no options and one person with 100 options"
