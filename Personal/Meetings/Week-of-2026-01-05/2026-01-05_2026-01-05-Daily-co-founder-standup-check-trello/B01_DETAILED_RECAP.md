# B01: Detailed Recap - Co-Founder Standup (2026-01-05)

## Executive Summary
This meeting served as the first post-holiday strategic realignment for the Careerspan co-founding team (Vrijen Attawar, Logan Currie, and Ilse Funkhouser). The primary objective was to address a critical financial shortfall of $25k-$30k due to unforeseen Indian tax complications and to pivot the company’s strategy from B2B sales toward an aggressive B2C "usage upswell" to maximize acquisition value. The team made the difficult decision to terminate Ilya’s contract to preserve runway and discussed a plan to support Rahul in his job search while potentially keeping him on for limited hours.

## Detailed Narrative

### 1. Strategic Pivot & Positioning
The founders reached a consensus to shift focus from long-cycle B2B sales to high-volume B2C usage. Vrijen argued that demonstrated engagement and a "synthetic candidate pool" would be more attractive to major acquisition targets (like Darwin Box or Korn Ferry) than flimsy partnerships.
- **The "Robinhood" Era:** Logan proposed leaning into a movement-based narrative, offering candidates transparent feedback and comparative data on how they stack up against other applicants—a feature currently missing in the market.
- **Candidate Feedback System:** The team discussed automating or semi-automating feedback via SendGrid. This includes notifying candidates of their "competitiveness" and encouraging them to tell more "stories" to improve their standing.
- **The "Pitching" Model:** Vrijen proposed a high-touch automated pitch system where qualified candidates are directly introduced to CEOs/hiring managers via email, bypassing traditional ATS friction.

### 2. Financial & Personnel Realignment
The company is facing a tighter runway than previously calculated ($15k currently in the bank, with a $25k-$30k shortfall).
- **Termination of Ilya:** To reduce burn, the team agreed to trigger the five-day notice period in Ilya’s contract. The justification will be framed as a strategic necessity due to the funding shortfall rather than performance-based.
- **Rahul’s Transition:** Ilse expressed concern for Rahul (who is also job hunting). The team plans to offer him a "career coach blitz" and potentially keep him on for a few hours a week to maintain morale and assist Ilse with the B2C transition.
- **Burn Reduction:** Vrijen has already begun cutting secondary costs (e.g., canceling Claude subscription) and plans a "slice in half" day to audit all remaining expenses.

### 3. Acquisition Strategy (The "War Room")
The team is focusing on a 3-month window for an exit.
- **Target Profiles:** Identifying buyers with $3M-$5M "to throw at a problem," specifically legacy enterprises or enormous players looking for a modern tech/distribution edge.
- **Fishing Expedition:** Logan and Vrijen will "blitz" their senior network using a position of strength, framing the outreach as broadening the scope of an existing acquisition conversation.
- **Data as a Moat:** Vrijen will consult with contacts at Aviato and Nyne to validate the value of Careerspan’s data stream as a primary selling point.

### 4. Technical Roadmap & Product Bodges
Ilse outlined the technical constraints and possibilities for the new "scanning" focus.
- **Pool Scanning:** Ilse will build/refine a system to scan the existing user base (specifically active users from the last 30-90 days) against new job descriptions.
- **Marvin Job Integration:** The team plans to ingest ~200 roles from "Marvin" (and potentially Getro) to provide immediate volume for the scanning engine.
- **Classification vs. Search:** Discussed the cost-efficiency of pre-classifying users into "Product," "Design," etc., to reduce the overhead of cross-referencing every user against every job.

## Key Decisions
- **DP1:** Shift primary KPI from B2B revenue to B2C "stories told" and active user count.
- **DP2:** Terminate Ilya’s contract effective immediately to preserve remaining runway.
- **DP3:** Pivot to an "Acquisition First" communication style with external leads.
- **DP4:** Implement a 30-day "Active User" definition (requires 1 story told per month to remain in the scanning pool).
