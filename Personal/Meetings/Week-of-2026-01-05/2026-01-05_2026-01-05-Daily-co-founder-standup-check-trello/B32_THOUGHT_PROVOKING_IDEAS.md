# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

## Idea 1: The "Russian Doll" Acquisition Theory
Logan’s framing of the company’s value as a "Russian Doll" (Tech at the core, B2C usage as the next layer, B2B success stories as the outer layer) provides a framework for deciding what to "cut" when runway is short. If the outer layer (B2B sales) is too expensive, you focus on the inner layers that still hold value for an enterprise acquirer.

## Idea 2: Prediction Markets for Hiring
Ilse’s suggestion of a "Recruiter Jim Cramer" and prediction markets for candidate hiring status ("Will John Smith be hired by Jan 31?") highlights an untapped layer of **market efficiency** in recruiting. By turning "reputation" into a tradable asset (recruiter clout), the market could theoretically filter for the most effective signal-gatherers.

## Idea 3: The "Active User" as a 30-Day Window
The decision to define active users as those who have "told a story in the last 30 days" creates a **dynamic currency** for the platform. Candidates aren't just "in the database"; they must "pay" with fresh data/engagement to remain liquid in the scanning pool.
