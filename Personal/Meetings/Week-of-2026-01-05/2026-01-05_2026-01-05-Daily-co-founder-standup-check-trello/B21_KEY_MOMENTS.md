# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"I think right now what we have going for us overall is that we have the tech plus Ilsa... That means we technically should do everything we can to de-risk that a little bit."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Highlighting that the lead engineer (Ilsa) is a core asset for any acquisition, making her retention and cooperation the top strategic priority for the coming months.

**"The good news is that we are genuinely really confident that we can sell this company and you will be getting a payout... The bad news is that we do not have as much runway as we thought."**  
— Logan Currie  
*Context:* Framing the "good news/bad news" delivery for a difficult conversation with Ilsa regarding budget cuts and the release of other staff members.

**"Our ability or inability to monetize has no correlation with a larger company's ability to do something with us."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* Explaining the rationale behind focusing on candidate pool volume rather than immediate revenue, arguing that a large player (like Darwin Box) values the distribution moat more than the current business model.

— Ilse Funkhouser  
*Context:* Identifying a significant pivot in the proposed strategy (moving toward manual recruitment brokering) and flagging the risk of changing the value proposition so close to a potential exit.

**"I'll lie until this comes... If someone tells me to look the other way because we are going to abuse that system and lie and it's not actually direct apply, I'm not gonna cry about that."**  
— Ilse Funkhouser  
*Context:* Expressing a pragmatic, "last-ditch effort" willingness to compromise on product transparency if it means hitting the metrics necessary to get the company sold and protect her "house."

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"What is it actually going to get us in terms of talent? ... The likelihood that we'll actually have any meaningful talent?"**  
— Ilse Funkhouser  
*Significance:* Challenging the "volume over specificity" strategy by questioning if a broad pool of 10,000 users actually provides the "signal" that acquirers need.

**"Why isn't there a market on betting on if specific people will get hired or not by a specific time frame?"**  
— Ilse Funkhouser  
*Significance:* A provocative question that reframed the recruitment problem as an information/incentive issue, leading to a discussion on how to use "skin in the game" mechanics to improve candidate signal.

**"How do we prove to them that this amount of effort is worthwhile? ... What is everybody crying out for is I don't know how I stack up against other people."**  
— Logan Currie  
*Significance:* Probing the core B2C value proposition, identifying that comparative feedback is the unique hook that can drive viral usage where jobs alone cannot.

**"Is it physically possible to do that subset scan to both identify a subset of people that are probably going to be good regardless of deal breaker?"**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Significance:* Driving toward technical clarity on whether the current AI matching system can be used cost-effectively to demonstrate "pipeline capability" without needing a perfect marketplace match.
