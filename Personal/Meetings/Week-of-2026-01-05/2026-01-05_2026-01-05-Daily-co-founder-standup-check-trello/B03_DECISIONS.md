# B03: Decisions Made

## Decision 1: Immediate Termination of Ilya's Sales Contract

**DECISION:** Ilya will be let go with five days' notice and one pay period's worth of severance (approximately $15,000).
**CONTEXT:** Careerspan is facing a $25,000–$30,000 cash shortfall due to unforeseen Indian tax issues. The team needs to preserve runway to keep Ilsa paid through April.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar and Logan Currie.
**IMPLICATIONS:** The B2B sales push planned for January is effectively cancelled; the company will pivot focus entirely toward B2C usage and "movement" building.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Negotiating with Ilya to pay him after an acquisition (discarded as too risky/unlikely to be accepted).

## Decision 2: Strategic Pivot to B2C Usage and "Movement" Branding

**DECISION:** Pivot the primary business objective from B2B sales to maximizing B2C candidate data and usage volume.
**CONTEXT:** To attract a $3M+ acquirer (like Darwin Box), the team believes they need to prove a scalable distribution moat and a high-quality candidate pipeline rather than early B2B revenue.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar, Logan Currie, and Ilse Funkhouser (aligned during the call).
**IMPLICATIONS:** The landing page and app copy must be updated to soften "Direct Apply" language; marketing will lean into a "Robinhood era" movement branding.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Continuing the slow-burn Maven/Content/TikTok strategy (discarded as too "slow-burn" for current runway).

## Decision 3: Implementation of Daily Talent Pool Scanning

**DECISION:** Ilse will implement a manual/daily system to scan the entire active user base (users active within 30–90 days) against new job roles.
**CONTEXT:** This provides the "magic sauce" for both candidates (automated matching) and potential acquirers (demonstrable sourcing capability).
**DECIDED BY:** Ilse Funkhouser and Vrijen Attawar.
**IMPLICATIONS:** Candidates will be notified via email of matches or rejections with comparative feedback; monthly story updates will be required to maintain "active" status.
**ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED:** Automated real-time scanning (discarded due to high cost and technical complexity).

## Decision 4: Automated Candidate Pitching to CEOs

**DECISION:** Implement a low-touch system where candidates passing a high qualification threshold trigger a webhook to send a personalized pitch email to the hiring CEO, CC’ing the candidate.
**CONTEXT:** Inspired by "the board" experience, this increases candidate gratification and demonstrates high-signal sourcing to employers.
**DECIDED BY:** Vrijen Attawar.
**IMPLICATIONS:** Requires Ilse to hook up webhooks to an email system (e.g., SendGrid) and automate the "pitch" copy.

## Decision 5: Support for Rahul’s Career Transition

**DECISION:** Careerspan will keep Rahul on for a few hours a week to assist with transition while he searches for a new role.
**CONTEXT:** Ilsa expressed high concern for Rahul's well-being; keeping her involved prevents team morale collapse and provides "buddy" support for Ilsa.
**DECIDED BY:** Logan Currie and Vrijen Attawar.
**IMPLICATIONS:** Small additional cost but serves as a "win-win-win" for team stability and social capital.

## Decision 6: Targeting Enterprise/Legacy Acquirers

**DECISION:** Focus acquisition outreach on larger, "enormous" companies (e.g., Darwin Box, Korn Ferry) rather than startups.
**CONTEXT:** Startups are unlikely to have the liquidity for a $3M+ cash exit; enterprise players need the tech/distribution combination to enter the AI-career market.
**DECIDED BY:** Logan Currie and Vrijen Attawar.
**IMPLICATIONS:** The "War Room" outreach list will be prioritized for senior contacts at these larger organizations.
