# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

## Idea 1: Agents as a "Team Sport"
Ilya’s belief that individuals will eventually manage teams of ~100 agents (10 teams of 10) reframes the "AI replacement" narrative. Instead of one human being replaced by one AI, the human becomes a manager of a massive, distributed agentic workforce. The challenge shifts from "doing the work" to "managing the efficiency of the agent-to-agent handoffs."

## Idea 2: The "Setup Chapter" of AI Adoption
Logan’s insight that AI adoption is stalling due to a lack of **metacognition** (employees not knowing how to break down their own routine tasks) suggests that the next phase of tech consulting won't be about "selling software," but about "teaching metacognition."

## Idea 3: The "Long Race to the Middle"
Ilya’s advice about the "long race to the middle" (entrepreneurs burning years on a company that always seems 6 months from the promised land) is a sobering strategic framework for exit timing. It suggests that "time burned" is often the most expensive and unrecoverable capital in a startup.
