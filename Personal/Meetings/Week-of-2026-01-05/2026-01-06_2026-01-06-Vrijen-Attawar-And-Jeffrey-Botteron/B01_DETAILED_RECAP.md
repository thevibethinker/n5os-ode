# B01: Detailed Recap - Vrijen Attawar and Jeffrey Botteron

## Introduction and Personal Rapport
The meeting began with technical troubleshooting regarding power cords and loud office environments. Vrijen mentioned his "furry coworkers" (cats Gary and Avocado), and Jeffrey shared that he currently has no pets, though his son previously had hamsters. Jeffrey explained he was on a different floor of his building due to loud events on his usual floor.

## Tech Buffalo Overview
Jeffrey Botteron introduced Tech Buffalo's mission: developing Buffalo's talent profile to ensure local enterprise companies expand there and startups have the density of talent needed to grow. He noted they are at a decision point between building or buying a product to "professionalize" their offerings and manage community engagement more effectively.

## Careerspan Value Proposition
Vrijen discussed the inherent failure of Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to scalably sort talent, arguing that resumes are inadequate data points, especially as AI makes the cost of "bullshit" (perfectly tailored resumes) zero. He explained Careerspan's Socratic dialogue approach, which helps candidates exposit their experiences into actionable data labels.

## Pre-Career Talent and Semantic Analysis
Jeffrey expressed particular interest in the "pre-career" market (Grade 11 through Doctoral candidates). Vrijen demonstrated the "Donna/Donatella" profile (a Silicon Valley reference) to show how the platform helps candidates who aren't naturally good self-advocates. He showcased "vibe checks" and semantic analysis against job descriptions (JDs), which identify gaps in stories rather than just binary keyword matching.

## Ecosystem and Community Engagement
They discussed the "employer portal" and the "longitudinal view" of candidates. Vrijen emphasized that Careerspan encourages 3-5% monthly re-engagement, allowing Tech Buffalo to track talent over a decade. This helps avoid "brain drain" by showing seasoned talent local opportunities before they are recruited to cities like NYC or Chicago.

## Business Model and Next Steps
Jeffrey clarified that Tech Buffalo is foundation-funded and not driven by profit, focusing instead on community value. Vrijen offered to provide the community tool for free while charging employers for "magic links" (distribution), or a low per-person annual license (-) if that fits better. Jeffrey requested community-specific paraphernalia and pilot examples to review with his team.
