2. **Alignment on "Always Job-Ready" User Segment**: Vrijen's explanation of Careerspan's retention model (users return 3-5% within 6-12 months, 2-3% monthly base reactivation) triggered positive response. Mai explicitly valued this: "nice to tap into just fresh job seekers."

4. **Analyst Pairing Strategy**: Mai's offer to assign 2 dedicated analysts (direct content + lead gen content) for performance monitoring was met with enthusiasm from Vrijen: "Sounds good. This is really exciting." This signals Mai's willingness to invest human capital (not just budget).
   - *Signals*: Seriousness about the partnership; signal that Lensa sees potential; resourcefulness in managing at scale

5. **Geographic Specificity Appeal**: When Vrijen specified "Boston, SF, NY product roles," Mai immediately offered to filter feeds by job category/title. This responsiveness (rather than forcing Careerspan into mass-market fit) was received as collaborative.
   - *Signals*: Both parties seeking customization over cookie-cutter integration; Mai's agility and desire to make this work
