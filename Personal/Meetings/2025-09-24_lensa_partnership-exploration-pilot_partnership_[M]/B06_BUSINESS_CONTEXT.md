# Business Context

## Careerspan
- **Model:** Consumer‑first career coaching and reflection product embedded in selective, high‑signal communities (e.g., McKinsey alumni, ex‑Googlers, Ivy alumni associations).
- **Data advantage:** Users invest 20+ minutes into structured storytelling, yielding rich qualitative data that goes far beyond resumes. This powers tight job‑to‑candidate matching.
- **Engagement:** Email/job recommendation flows see **75–80% open rates** and **~15–16% click‑through**, indicating strong trust and relevance.
- **Current constraint:** V1 matching pipeline is cost‑inefficient and designed for **tens of jobs/week**, not millions. The system is being improved, but current reality requires careful scoping of input jobs.

## Lensa
- **Model:** Large‑scale job board aggregator focused on the US market.
  - Monetizes by distributing jobs for paying clients (job boards, agencies, ATS partners, Fortune 1000 employers).
  - Operates a US‑focused marketplace with development in Budapest.
- **Inventory:** ~24–25M jobs from a mix of organic scrapes, direct agency content, ATS feeds, and syndicated job boards.
- **Growth lever:** User acquisition—bringing in new and returning job seekers to register for job alerts and interact with job content.

## Strategic Fit
- Lensa brings **scale of jobs and sophisticated distribution infrastructure** (XML feeds, APIs, co‑registration, analytics).
- Careerspan brings **depth and quality of candidate engagement** from curated communities and a differentiated product.
- The partnership is essentially an experiment in **quality‑weighted performance marketing**:
  - Lensa tests whether a smaller but highly engaged, high‑intent audience can outperform typical traffic sources on ROI.
  - Careerspan tests whether a large, clean job source can power its matching engine without expensive scraping.

## Key Risks / Considerations
- **Metric misalignment:** If evaluation is overly focused on raw clicks or registrations, Careerspan may look underwhelming despite high downstream quality.
- **Operational complexity:** Appcast onboarding and feed configuration add integration overhead for a small team.
- **Scaling path:** If the test works, both sides must plan for capacity expansion—more jobs, more users, and potential broadening into new roles (engineering, growth, etc.).

Overall, this is a classic early‑stage distribution and data partnership: Lensa de‑risks by starting with a small, clearly bounded test; Careerspan gets leverage and validation from partnering with a scaled player in the job board ecosystem.
