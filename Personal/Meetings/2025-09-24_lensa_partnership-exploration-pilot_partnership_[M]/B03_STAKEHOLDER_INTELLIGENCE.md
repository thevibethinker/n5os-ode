# Stakeholder Intelligence

## Careerspan
- **Primary contact:** Vrijen Attawar
- **Role:** Founder of Careerspan; owns product vision and early‑stage partnerships.
- **Stated goals:**
  - Build a high‑signal talent network by embedding a free coaching product in selective professional communities.
  - Leverage deep, story‑level user data to drive superior job matching and high engagement.
  - Access a large, clean job feed so Careerspan can reduce scraping/clean‑up work and focus on matching.
- **Key interests & motivations:**
  - Quality over volume: prefers sending fewer, highly relevant candidates rather than maximizing clicks.
  - Preserving Careerspan’s differentiated matching experience and strong engagement metrics.
  - Finding partners (like Lensa) whose incentives can align with Careerspan’s precision‑focused approach.
- **Concerns / constraints:**
  - Current V1 matching pipeline is cost‑inefficient; cannot process millions of jobs at once.
  - On CPC‑only evaluation, Careerspan’s lower volume may look weak unless quality‑based metrics are included.
- **Leverage points:**
  - Very high open (~75–80%) and click‑through (~15–16%) rates on recommendations.
  - Access to curated, high‑intent candidates from elite communities.
  - Flexibility to adjust thresholds to increase volume modestly if economics require it.

## Lensa
- **Primary contact:** Mai Flynn
- **Role:** User acquisition lead; responsible for acquiring job seekers and driving monetizable registrations.
- **Stated goals:**
  - Acquire new and returning job seekers for Lensa’s platform.
  - Monetize traffic via job alerts and ad inventory tied to 24–25M jobs.
  - Test whether Careerspan’s audience can deliver strong ROI (CPC/CPA) and scalable volume.
- **Key interests & motivations:**
  - Access to “fresh” job seekers outside of Lensa’s usual partner network.
  - Keeping user flows low‑friction; wary of added steps that could increase drop‑off.
  - Being able to segment job feeds by category (starting with product) to match Careerspan’s initial focus.
- **Concerns / constraints:**
  - Industry standard is high‑volume traffic from many partners; there is skepticism about small‑volume sources.
  - Need to justify CPC and budget to internal stakeholders based on measurable ROI.
- **Leverage points:**
  - Ability to customize job feeds by category, location, and content type.
  - Internal analytics team (two assigned analysts) who can optimize placements and budgets.

## Relationship Dynamics
- **Mutual fit:**
  - Lensa brings scale (large job inventory, mature distribution tooling).
  - Careerspan brings depth (high‑signal, highly engaged candidates and strong matching).
- **Potential friction:**
  - Metrics alignment: Lensa is accustomed to traffic and registrations; Careerspan optimizes for high‑quality matches.
  - Operational complexity around Appcast setup and managing filtered feeds.
- **Overall read:**
  - Both sides are open and collaborative; Mai is pragmatic and test‑oriented, while Vrijen is candid about constraints but confident in Careerspan’s quality advantage.
