# Key Moments — Ballon d'Or Discussion

## Media Perception Over Actual Performance

*"Up to this point in the video I've been basing the winner of the Ballon d'Or not off my criteria, but the media's. Because if you didn't know already, the Ballon d'Or is entirely decided by international journalists, which in my opinion already a bad idea to get shit like this every year. But it also means that this is an award based entirely on media perception."*

> **Core insight**: The award is fundamentally flawed because it prioritizes media narratives and "savior of football" narratives over actual statistical achievement.

## The Raphinha Robbery

*"He tied Cristiano Ronaldo's Champions League record of 21 goal contributions in a single season and didn't receive a number one vote from any journalist. Did you catch that?"*

> **Statistical anomaly**: 56 goal contributions across all competitions, tying CR7's UCL record, yet zero first-place votes—a clear indicator that numbers alone don't win this award.

## "Savior of Football" Narrative vs. Reality

*"So when Lamine Yamal popped up on the scene and was making defenses look silly, everyone saw him as this savior of football. Especially the media who were getting tired of writing about the newest up and coming Magic's form of the Gagan penis tactic or whatever."*

> **Media fatigue**: Journalists were desperate for a new individual brilliance story after the "boring" post-Messi/Ronaldo era, creating artificial hype around flashy players.

## Dembélé's Underrated Impact

*"If you're an avid PSG fan or hate watcher like myself, you saw Dembélé all over the pitch, and in doing so, he unlocked so much space for his teammates. Not only was he scoring goals like an absolute, but he was also the reason why PSG opened up so much, why they were playing so incredibly fluid."*

> **Invisible work**: Dembélé's off-ball movement and space creation were more impactful than Yamal's flashy highlights—but these aren't "camera-friendly" contributions.

## The Aura and Celebration Bias

*"Do you see Serhu [Girassi] doing those mass celebrations or having those aura edits of him? Exactly."*

> **Personality over performance**: Players with marketable personalities and social media presence get ranked higher despite inferior stats.

## Haaland's Expectation Trap

*"But because expectations are greater for him, his lower figures and missed shots were put under a microscope more than anyone else. And in the end he placed 26."*

> **The curse of excellence**: When you set an impossibly high bar, a "down" season that's still elite gets punished.

## Cole Palmer's Recency Bias

*"This is the biggest case of recency bias right here because Cole Palmer throughout the second half of the season had only three goals and two assists in the Premier League. But because he starred in the Club World Cup and let Chelsea pass PSG in a matter of like 30 minutes, he's ranked eighth."*

> **Moment over season**: A single high-profile performance overrides an entire season of mediocrity. Palmer ranked 18 spots higher than last year—despite having a worse overall season.

## Serie A's Invisibility

*"Scott McTominay was essential to Napoli's Scudetto run and also won Serie A MVP. But because Serie A doesn't get any international attention really, he's placed below the likes of Victor Jokers and Cole Palmer."*

> **League hierarchy bias**: Players in less internationally-visible leagues get systematically undervalued regardless of actual achievement.
