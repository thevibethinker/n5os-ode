# B06 Business Context

## Market Hypothesis: Micro-Communities
The meeting validates a core Careerspan thesis: the "undiscovered niche" of micro-communities. These high-intent, specialized groups (like Social Impact SF) offer a more effective recruitment channel than broad job boards. By embedding a "virtual recruiter" model within these communities, Careerspan provides value to both the community leader (monetization/member value) and the employer (high-signal candidates).

## Careerspan Maturity
* **Employer Side:** ~12 active employers with commensurate roles.
* **Tech Readiness:** Deployment is described as "ready to go" and a matter of "plugging everything in."
* **Strategy:** Shifting from general recruitment toward a structured "case study" model with community founders.

## Relationship Ecosystem
The relationship between Careerspan and Social Impact SF is built on a high-trust referral from Jessica Greenwald (Nice Industries). This emphasizes the importance of "connector nodes" in Careerspan's GTM strategy.
