# B01 Detailed Recap: Vrijen Attawar, David A, & Rochel Polter

## Overview
Vrijen Attawar (V) and product designer Rochel Polter met with David A to gather feedback on his experience using Careerspan to apply for a role at Zoe. David provided critical UX insights regarding the onboarding flow, story generation, and the "vibe check" feature. David also shared details about his technical background in SRE and AI security.

## Chronological Summary

### 1. Introduction and Context (0:00 - 5:00)
- V and David connect; David is trying out a new recording app (Adio) but notes he prefers Fireflies for its reliable Zoe integration.
- V introduces Rochel Polter, Careerspan's product designer.
- V explains Careerspan's shift from a pure coaching product to a sustainable hiring solution.

### 2. User Feedback: The Application Experience (5:00 - 15:00)
- David describes finding the Careerspan link via the Zoe Discord.
- **Initial Impression:** The framing was good, and the extraction from his federal/consulting resume was mostly accurate.
- **Pain Points:**
    - **Parsing Delay:** The application parser spun for over 20 minutes, leading David to assume it was broken.
    - **Vibe Check Confusion:** David found the "vibe check" and "dream company" fields confusing in the context of applying for a specific job (Zoe).
    - **UI State:** The "Apply" button was stuck in an "In Review" status before he had even finished his submission.
- **The "Gaps" Problem:** David didn't initially realize that "telling stories" was the mechanism to "fill the gaps" for the Zoe position. The general "stories" page felt too generic and lacked the Zoe-specific branding needed to compel engagement.

### 3. Proposed UX Improvements (15:00 - 25:00)
- David suggests "drip-feeding" critical story tasks or using badges to indicate how many stories are needed for a specific application.
- Rochel and V discuss the need to fill the "wait time" (while AI generates gaps) with meaningful, goal-oriented actions.
- V notes the ethical importance of keeping the story-telling portion optional for candidates who are already deep in the process.

### 4. Technical Deep Dive: SRE & AI Security (25:00 - 35:00)
- David shares his background in SRE and management consulting for government/federal sectors.
- **Current Project:** Building a general LLM MCP AI firewall to identify prompt injections and agent manipulation.
- V notes that this aligns perfectly with the "ideal candidate" profile he heard from Ben (Zoe) the previous night.

### 5. Closing and Next Steps (35:00 - End)
- David is starting a trial at Zoe tomorrow.
- V offers to facilitate introductions within his network and encourages David to use Careerspan for future applications.
- Discussion of potential LinkedIn integration (pasting JDs for analysis).
- Agreed to connect for drinks in Brooklyn.
