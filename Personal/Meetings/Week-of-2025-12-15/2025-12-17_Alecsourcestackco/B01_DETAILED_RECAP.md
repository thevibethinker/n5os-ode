# B01 Detailed Recap: Vrijen Attawar & Alec Barrett-Wilsdon (SourceTech)

## Overview
Vrijen Attawar (V) met with Alec Barrett-Wilsdon of SourceTech to discuss Careerspan's transition into an AI-driven ATS, a new partnership with Marv Adventures, and the strategic positioning of Careerspan for a potential acquisition. Alec provided market context on the acquisitive nature of the HR tech space and offered potential connections.

## Chronological Summary

### 1. Introduction and Catch-up (0:00 - 2:00)
- V and Alec connect; Alec is in San Francisco and planning a sailing trip to the British Virgin Islands in January.
- V expresses sympathy for the New York weather and mentions becoming "homebound" during winter.

### 2. Careerspan Product Evolution: The AI ATS (2:00 - 4:00)
- V describes the new "hiring side solution" which functions as an AI-driven ATS.
- Instead of forms, candidates engage in self-reflection conversations with an AI. Qualifying for the Careerspan network requires two such conversations.
- V mentions they currently have about a dozen jobs on the platform.

### 3. Marv Adventures Partnership (4:00 - 6:00)
- V reveals a new partnership with Marv Adventures, which has ~50-100 portfolio companies.
- V's goal is to identify these companies and run automated polls of their job boards to stream data directly to Careerspan (Ilsa).

### 4. Acquisition Interest and Strategic Positioning (6:00 - 10:00)
- V shares that Careerspan is receiving more acquisition interest than investment interest (4-5 companies currently in talks).
- Alec notes the space is very acquisitive, mentioning that some of his own customers are being acquired.
- V's strategy: Create "froth" and energy by flooding the job board with roles to incentivize applications, positioning the company for a lean sale (target ~M) in the next 3-4 months.

### 5. Market Context and Potential Acquirers (10:00 - 14:00)
- Alec suggests that "last generation" software tools like resume builders should be interested, as they are threatened by one-shot AI resume generation.
- ATS companies are also seen as primary candidates for acquisition.
- Discussion of Peter Weddle (TA Talent Network); Alec offers to make a warm introduction.

### 6. Data Sourcing and Competitor Insights (14:00 - 18:00)
- Discussion of Getro sunsetting its jobs data products and the potential impact on Marv Adventures.
- Discussion of a past fallout with a CEO who turned a potential data partnership into a competing business.
- V admits the team is "drained" by the brutal nature of the HR tech game, reinforcing the desire to exit.

### 7. Closing and Next Steps (18:00 - End)
- V will provide a list of portfolio companies for Alec to review for a potential data feed/API setup.
- Agreed to meet for lunch in New York around January 12th.
