# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

### 1. The "Interface Inversion" Hypothesis
The emergence of ChatGPT Apps and MCP-based UI components represents a fundamental shift in the SaaS hierarchy. In this model, the proprietary UI—once the primary moat and customer touchpoint—becomes a liability or a "distribution tax." The value shifts entirely to the "Agentic Backend." This meeting highlights a future where successful software is designed primarily to be *invisible*, optimized for ingestion by an agentic host (like ChatGPT or Zo) rather than for human direct manipulation. The "weirdness" here is the intentional de-prioritization of the brand's own visual real estate in favor of "ghost distribution."

### 2. Protocol-First vs. Product-First Engineering
Erica mentions that MCP at its core is "more lacking than one would think," requiring abstraction layers like Alpic (Skybridge). This suggests a "Protocol Gap" in the current AI era: the industry has standardized the *communication* (MCP) but not the *experience* (UI/state management). The provocative theme is that we are currently in the "CGI era" of AI interfaces—where the underlying protocol is raw and unrefined, and the winners will be those who build the "Photoshop" for agentic protocols, rather than just building another agent.

### 3. The "Non-Technical Indie Hacker" Paradox
Vrijen describes himself as a "non-technical indie hacker," a term he acknowledges some might call a "cursed phrase." This meeting captures the exact moment this paradox resolves into a new professional class. By using agentic systems (Zo) to set up webhooks and implement documentation (Alpic/Skybridge) without "coding" in the traditional sense, the definition of "technical" is being decoupled from "syntax proficiency" and moved to "architectural intent." The original mental model here is that "Software Engineering" is becoming a subset of "Systems Orchestration."
