# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

- **"High-Friction as a Feature":** The idea that for high-stakes roles, *adding* friction (via conversational coaching) actually increases candidate quality and signal, whereas traditional recruiting focuses on *removing* friction (easy apply).
- **"The Disgruntled Consultant Persona":** Eric's specific thesis that the best early-stage startup hires are smart consultants who are frustrated by the lack of "building" in their current roles.
- **"Agentic Order vs. Agentic Capability":** Vrijen's observation that Zo's true advantage is the "degree of order" it creates for non-technical users, rather than just raw agentic capability.
- **"The AI Red Flag Gap":** The current belief among experienced professionals that AI is incapable of detecting subtle "red flags" in human behavior/profiles—a gap that could be bridged by semantic analysis or a better UI for "AI-human collaborative evaluation."
