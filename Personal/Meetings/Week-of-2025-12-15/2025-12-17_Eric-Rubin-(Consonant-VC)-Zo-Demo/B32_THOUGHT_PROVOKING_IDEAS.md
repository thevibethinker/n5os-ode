# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas
**Meeting:** 2025-12-17_eric____vrijen__zo_demo

### 1. The "UX Problem as Ontological Blindness" Thesis
V posits that the failure of AI tools (and ATS systems) to "stick" isn't a technical or feature gap, but a fundamental failure to solve the "real UX problem." He suggests that the "order" created by Zo is a distinct category of computing that technical founders often miss because they view it as an engineering problem rather than a systems-thinking or "vibe" problem. The implication is that "magic" in software isn't about code complexity, but about the degree of semantic order it can impose on human chaos without manual labor.

### 2. Networking as an "Unsolved Social Capital Deployment" Problem
The dialogue identifies a systemic inefficiency in social capital: humans are "blind" to 95%+ of their own network at any given moment, defaulting to the last 5 people they interacted with. V frames this as a "retrieval of social capital" problem. If AI can solve the "cognitive labor of retrieval," it doesn't just improve efficiency; it fundamentally changes the nature of social power, allowing individuals to deploy their entire universe of connections as a liquid asset rather than a static, mostly forgotten list.

### 3. The "Manual Sniff Test" vs. Programmatic Evaluation
There is a strategic tension discussed regarding the "human ability to sniff out BS." Eric argues that AI cannot pick up on the "little red flags" that a seasoned founder spots instantly. This suggests a "weirdness" in current AI development: we are building hyper-logical agents that are strategically blind to the "nuance" and "vibes" that actually dictate success in high-stakes human systems (like hiring or investing). The provocative idea here is that the next leap in AI isn't better logic, but better "cynicism"—the ability to model human deceptive signaling.
