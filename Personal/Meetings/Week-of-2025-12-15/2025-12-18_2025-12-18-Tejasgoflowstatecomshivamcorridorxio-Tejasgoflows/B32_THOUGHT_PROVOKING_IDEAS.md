# B32 Thought Provoking Ideas

*   **The Death of the Resume for Builders:** If the Careerspan story-based model succeeds here, does it signal the end of the resume for the "Indie Hacker" economy? Could "stories of teamwork" be the new primary currency of tech hiring?
*   **Conversational Scoping:** Tejas’s idea of an AI that listens to a "lobby" of voices and automatically spins up a "Strategy Room" or "Scope of Work" removes the friction of documentation. How does this change the speed of moving from "Idea" to "Code"?
*   **The "Refugee" Market:** Identifying 17,000 people as "refugees" of a failed community is a powerful framing. What other defunct platforms have orphaned communities waiting for a new home?
*   **Taste as a Moat:** In a world where LLMs generate perfect code, Tejas argues that "taste" and "design" are the only differentiators left. How do we quantify or teach "taste" to AI-augmented builders?
*   **Non-Salesy Ecosystems:** Shivam’s insistence on not being a "salesman" suggests a shift in how service businesses must operate in builder communities. Value must be permissionless and integrated, not pitched.
