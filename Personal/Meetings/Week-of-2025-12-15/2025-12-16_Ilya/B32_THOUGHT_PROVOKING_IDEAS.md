# B32 Thought Provoking Ideas

1. **The "Inventory of Honesty" as a Sales Tool**: Ilya's concept that radical sincerity can "stop traffic" in an industry conditioned to expect sleazy sales tactics. It suggests that transparency isn't just a moral choice, but a high-leverage competitive advantage.
2. **The Fragility of the "Boutique Dominant"**: The idea that owning a niche (like lumberjacks or sports) is a powerful but non-resilient advantage. If a boutique firm doesn't control the candidate *relationship* (sourcing), their "ownership" of the market is merely temporary stewardship of a job board.
3. **100x Visibility vs. 100x Volume**: The strategic shift from trying to provide *more* candidates to providing *more depth* on fewer candidates. This challenges the traditional "top of the funnel" metrics that most HR tech companies optimize for.
