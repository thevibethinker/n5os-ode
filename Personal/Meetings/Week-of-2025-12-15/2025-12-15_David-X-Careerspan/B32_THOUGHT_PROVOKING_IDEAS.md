### 1. The "IKEA Effect" as a Software Deployment Strategy
The conversation introduces the idea of the "IKEA Effect" not as a byproduct of a product, but as a deliberate cultivation strategy for complex AI systems. The provocation is that for an AI system (like Zo) to be truly integrated into a user's life, the user *must* be the one to "plug it together." This challenges the traditional software-as-a-service (SaaS) model of "seamless onboarding" and suggests that high-friction, high-agency setup might actually be the superior path to long-term tool retention and deep personalization.

### 2. Meaningfulness as an Unstructured Data Filter
There is a nuanced distinction made between "meaningful re-connects" and "blips" in networking history. The mental model here is that relationship health cannot be tracked via binary communication logs (did an email happen?), but requires a semantic layer to determine if the "snooze" on a relationship should be reset. This implies a future where CRM intelligence is less about logging activity and more about the qualitative assessment of "relational energy" to prevent mechanical spamming of one's network.

### 3. The "Networker in a Box" as a Distributed System
The idea of "flashing" a networker-in-a-box onto a personal system via GitHub suggests a shift from centralized platforms (like LinkedIn or traditional CRMs) to distributed, locally-owned networking intelligence. It posits that the most effective networking tool isn't a platform you join, but a capability you "flash" onto your own compute, treating professional relationships as a programmatic infrastructure rather than just a contact list.
