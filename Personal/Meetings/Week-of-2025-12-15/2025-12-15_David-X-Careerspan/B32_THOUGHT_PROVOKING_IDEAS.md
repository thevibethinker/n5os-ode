# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

1. **The IKEA Effect as a Product Strategy**: The idea that making software *more* difficult (or requiring more assembly) creates more psychological ownership and stickiness for the user.
2. **Networking as 'House Ordering'**: David’s mental model that networking isn't about the outreach, it's about 'getting your house in order' (profile, list of companies) before you ever touch a message.
3. **Unstructured Data vs. SaaS Rigidity**: The hypothesis that CRMs fail because they are too structured; the 'killer app' for networking is a system that understands the *meaning* of a casual check-in and adjusts schedules accordingly, rather than just clicking 'Snooze'.
