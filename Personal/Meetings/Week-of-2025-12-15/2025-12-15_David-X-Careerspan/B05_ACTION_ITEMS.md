# B05 – Action Items

## Owned by V

1. **Socialize "networker in a box" with the Zo team**
   - Present the concept (David’s networking system encoded on Zo, delivered as a live series) to the internal Zo team.
   - Clarify what baseline components already exist (personas, tagging patterns, scheduled agents, CRM-ish structures) that can be packaged.
   - Capture any constraints or enthusiasm signals that would materially shape the first iteration.

2. **Draft a rough program skeleton for a pilot series**
   - Sketch 2–4 session outlines that show what attendees will install, configure, and practice in each meeting.
   - Identify where David would appear (live teaching, Q&A, case studies) and what outcomes participants should leave with.

3. **Identify candidate partner communities (starting with Next Play)**
   - Confirm with Ben Lang whether Next Play is interested in hosting a pilot.
   - If yes, align on rough timing, format (webinar vs. cohort), and expectations around sponsorships and cross‑promotion.

## Owned by David Spiegel

4. **Externalize his networking frameworks in more detail**
   - Write down the three networking arenas (job-search, internal, personal/keep‑in‑touch) with concrete steps, examples, and failure modes.
   - Highlight which pieces are good candidates for automation vs. which must stay human.

5. **Collect exemplar messages and flows**
   - Gather a small set of real or lightly anonymized messages and follow-up sequences that worked well in each arena.
   - Flag which examples best showcase tone, authenticity, and batching behavior so they can seed Zo personas/templates.

## Shared / Cross-functional

6. **Define success metrics for a pilot**
   - Align on a small set of measurable outcomes (e.g., number of meaningful check‑ins per week, number of revived dormant contacts, number of participants who actually install and keep using the system).
   - Ensure metrics are realistic for an initial cohort and observable from Zo + connected tools.

7. **Risk and constraint mapping**
   - Jointly document major risks: LinkedIn API limitations, user overwhelm, over‑automation of messaging, and vendor dependencies.
   - Use this to decide what must be excluded from v1 (e.g., heavy LinkedIn scraping) to keep the pilot safe and credible.
