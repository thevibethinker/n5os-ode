# B21 – Key Moments

1. **Framing the three arenas of networking**
   - David cleanly decomposes networking into job-search, internal, and personal/keep‑in‑touch arenas.
   - This becomes the conceptual backbone for how any Zo-based system should be organized.

2. **Articulating the human-in-the-loop boundary**
   - David: people keep asking for "Spiegel in a box" so they "don’t have to think about what to write," but he insists they still need to write (or heavily edit) their own messages.
   - V agrees and reframes the goal as surfacing opportunities and context, not auto-sending everything.

3. **Recognizing the limits of existing tools (Dex, Condo)**
   - David describes Dex as "meh" and explains why Condo deliberately avoids being a CRM.
   - This clarifies the gap a Zo-based system could fill: structured relationship tracking plus flexible messaging.

4. **Introducing the IKEA effect as a design principle**
   - V argues that part of Zo’s power is that users assemble and adapt their own systems.
   - They agree that a networking kit should be opinionated but require some assembly, increasing understanding and ownership.

5. **Option maximizers as the likely first audience**
   - David distinguishes between people in acute job-search pain and those quietly preparing for what’s next.
   - Both conclude that the latter—high-intent, "slow burn" professionals—may be the best initial fit for a pilot series.

6. **Commitment to explore a pilot with Zo and possibly Next Play**
   - David explicitly says he wants to build this with V in Zo and asks what pieces V needs from him.
   - V commits to speak with the Zo team and return with a clearer proposal and next steps.
