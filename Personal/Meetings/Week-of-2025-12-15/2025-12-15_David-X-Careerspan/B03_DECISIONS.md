# B03 – Decisions

1. **Anchor the product concept around a Zo-based "networking system", not a black-box SaaS app**
   - **Decision:** Treat the target offering as a *system* (files, agents, workflows on Zo) that encodes David’s methods, rather than a sealed SaaS tool.
   - **Rationale:** Preserves the IKEA effect (people assemble/customize their own system), keeps Zo’s flexibility, and avoids overpromising "push‑button" results.

2. **Keep humans firmly in the loop for messaging**
   - **Decision:** Design the system so automation surfaces *who* to contact, *when*, and *with what context*, while humans author or at least strongly edit final messages.
   - **Rationale:** Protects relationship quality and reflects David’s belief that last‑mile communication requires human judgment and tone.

3. **Start from David’s three networking arenas as the conceptual backbone**
   - **Decision:** Use David’s taxonomy—(1) job-search networking, (2) internal networking, (3) personal/keep‑in‑touch networking—as the organizing frame for any Zo kit or curriculum.
   - **Rationale:** This maps cleanly to real user needs and provides a clear mental model for features, cadences, and templates.

4. **Bias the first iteration toward option maximizers / slow-burn networking**
   - **Decision (soft, but explicit):** Initial experiments are more likely to target "option maximizers" (Next Play‑type members who want to keep their network warm) rather than only acute job seekers.
   - **Rationale:** This audience is well represented in Ben Lang’s community, has motivation but not full-blown crisis pressure, and can tolerate a slower, system-building curriculum.

5. **Frame vendor integrations as optional accelerators, not hard dependencies**
   - **Decision:** Treat tools like Condo, Dex, and LinkedIn data providers as *optional modules* the kit can plug into, not mandatory prerequisites.
   - **Rationale:** Maintains accessibility for users who want to start with just email and Zo; avoids locking the design to any one vendor’s roadmap or pricing.

6. **Use Zo’s existing patterns (tags, scheduled agents, Howie-style markers) as the implementation substrate**
   - **Decision:** Reuse proven patterns—hidden signature tags, scheduled agents that scan and act on those tags, lightweight files/CRMs—rather than inventing entirely new mechanisms.
   - **Rationale:** Reduces implementation risk, leverages V’s current ecosystem, and makes the result easier to support.

7. **Next concrete step: V to socialize the idea with the Zo team**
   - **Decision:** V will take ownership of presenting the concept to the Zo team ("Zokes"), gauging appetite for an official series, and understanding technical baselines.
   - **Rationale:** Alignment with Zo’s roadmap and infrastructure is necessary before committing David’s time to curriculum and content production.

8. **Treat this meeting as exploration; defer pricing/offer specifics**
   - **Decision:** Do *not* lock in detailed pricing or offer mechanics in this call; those will be co‑designed later with input from Zo and, potentially, Next Play.
   - **Rationale:** Too many constraints are still unknown (sponsorships, partner expectations, infra constraints); premature commitments would be fragile.
