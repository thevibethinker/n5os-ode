# B07 – Tone and Context

## Overall vibe

- Warm, collegial, and intellectually curious; this felt like two peers co‑designing a system rather than a sales call.
- Conversation moved fluidly between personal stories (family, faith, geography) and deep operational thinking about networking.

## Emotional undertones

- **Trust and respect:** V treats David as a domain expert whose methods should be honored, not replaced; David reciprocates by taking Zo seriously as a platform.
- **Mild frustration with tools:** Shared annoyance at limitations of current products (Dex, LinkedIn itself) and the friction of doing networking well.
- **Optimism:** Both see a path to something genuinely useful that could help a lot of people if designed carefully.

## Subtext and unspoken dynamics

- V is implicitly auditioning Zo as a worthy home for David’s IP; the call tests whether David sees Zo as capable and trustworthy.
- David is cautious about anything that could oversimplify or commoditize his work, but is clearly excited by the idea of amplifying his impact.
- There is an undercurrent of **"we’re early, but we could build something important here"**, with both parties acknowledging the need to move thoughtfully.

## Risk signals

- Complexity risk: many moving parts (Zo, Condo, Dex, scrapers, scheduled agents, tags) can easily overwhelm a non‑technical user if not packaged well.
- Expectation risk: participants might expect an instant, fully automated solution instead of a guided system they must help assemble.

## Relationship trajectory

- This meeting strengthens the relationship and sets the stage for deeper collaboration.
- David explicitly expresses interest in building the system together and asks V to define concrete next steps after talking to the Zo team.
