# Business Context (B06)

## Johnson CMC situation

- **Mandate:** Reassess the “toolbox” of career resources, starting with **resume tools**.
- **Pain points:**
  - High manual load on staff to edit and tailor resumes for each posting.
  - Students struggle to articulate rich, differentiated stories; many treat the resume as a formatting exercise.
  - Existing tools (e.g., VMock) are increasingly generating **generic, similar bullets** for very different roles.
- **Constraints:**
  - Strongly enforced Johnson resume template and formatting standards.
  - Early and intense recruiting timeline (April diversity recruiting; resumes due August 1; main recruiting in the fall).

## Careerspan’s positioning in this context

- **Core proposition:** Use AI to help students deeply understand and express their experiences, then generate tailored bullets and interview stories for specific jobs.
- **Key workflows:**
  - Students talk/type stories; the AI coach asks adaptive questions and produces STAR‑style narratives and multiple bullet options.
  - Students run “vibe checks” against job descriptions to see alignment and gaps.
  - Coaches get a dashboard that surfaces coverage, strengths, and missing competencies.
- **Philosophy contrast:** Rather than optimizing solely for ATS keywords, Careerspan is optimized for **meaningful human evaluation** (hiring managers, alumni, career coaches) and for helping students advocate for themselves.

## Market & ecosystem signals

- **Comparable institutions:** UMass Eisenberg and Columbia SPS have already deployed or piloted Careerspan; early data from UMass is strong (high perceived value and confidence gains).
- **Competitive trend:** Many legacy tools are bolting AI onto templates, leading to homogenized bullets and shallow “optimization.” Johnson is wary of this and is explicitly looking for deeper functionality.
- **Macro hiring context:**
  - Smaller and mid‑sized employers often do not rely heavily on inbound ATS funnels.
  - Referrals, curated candidate pools, and narrative strength increasingly drive decisions, especially in startups and high‑growth environments.

## Strategic fit questions raised in the meeting

- Where in the **student journey** should Careerspan sit? (Admit, pre‑MBA summer, early recruiting, or only core fall recruiting?)
- How many stories/bullets are “enough” to reach a good baseline before recruiting heats up?
- How can Johnson preserve the benefits of automation while retaining coach discretion and guardrails on quality?

## Summary

This meeting situates Careerspan as a potential **next‑generation resume and reflection layer** for Johnson: a tool that could reduce resume drudgery while enabling deeper engagement with students’ stories. The key open questions are not about theoretical fit, but about execution details (timing, onboarding, data guarantees, and evidence of success at peer schools).
