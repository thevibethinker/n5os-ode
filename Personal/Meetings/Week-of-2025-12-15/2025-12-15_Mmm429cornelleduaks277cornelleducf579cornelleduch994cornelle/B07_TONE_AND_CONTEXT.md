# Tone and Context (B07)

## Tone

- **Curious and constructive:** The Johnson team comes with genuine questions rather than skepticism. The mood is exploratory: “show us how this works; help us understand where it fits.”
- **Candid but collegial:** Michael and colleagues share pain points with existing tools (especially AI‑generated sameness and ATS behavior) without sounding adversarial toward current vendors.
- **Pragmatic:** Repeated emphasis on student activation, template adherence, data privacy, and internal reporting shows they are thinking about practical rollout, not just shiny features.

## Relationship dynamics

- **Alumni trust:** Vrijen’s status as a Johnson alum lowers the barrier to candid discussion. Michael references having “been intrigued” for some time, suggesting prior informal rapport.
- **Distributed ownership:**
  - Michael: strategic direction and overall toolbox.
  - Amanda: student‑facing coaching implications and content quality.
  - Victoria: formatting and operational risk.
  - Chase: provisioning, reminders, and metrics.
- The conversation feels like a **joint design review** more than a vendor pitch.

## Subtext

- **Desire for differentiation:** There is implicit frustration that current tools reduce students to template‑driven, ATS‑chasing profiles. Careerspan’s story‑first approach resonates because it promises distinctive narratives.
- **Caution about student overwhelm:** Multiple comments about hand‑holding and the need for structured guidance show a concern that even good tools can fail if students don’t know how to start.
- **Openness to change, bounded by standards:** The team is clearly open to replacing or augmenting existing tools, but Johnson’s resume template, quality bar, and privacy expectations are non‑negotiable constraints.

## Context in the broader journey

- This meeting represents a **serious but early** step toward institutional adoption at Johnson.
- It may also function as a pattern for how to sell into other MBAs: start with alumni credibility, emphasize story‑first AI, and show both student and coach workflows while addressing privacy and formatting concerns head‑on.
