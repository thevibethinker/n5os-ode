# Key Moments (B21)

1. **Michael frames Careerspan as first in a new evaluation cycle**
   - Johnson is formally reassessing resume tools and chooses to speak with Careerspan first, citing long‑standing interest in AI used “in a new and different way.”

2. **Story‑first demo lands with the team**
   - The walkthrough of Donna’s account—stories, STAR outputs, and resume generation—visibly clicks with the group, especially the idea that reflection is a prerequisite for good resumes and interviews.

3. **Explicit frustration with generic AI bullets**
   - Amanda notes that current solutions (e.g., VMock with AI turned on) often produce **identical bullets** for students with completely different roles and companies.
   - This pain point clearly differentiates Careerspan’s approach in the team’s mind.

4. **Professional summary and ATS debate**
   - The team shares nuanced views: summaries can help if they’re precise, but vague ones can hurt students in ATS‑driven flows.
   - Vrijen counters with the thesis that “the ATS is dead” in many hiring contexts, especially at smaller firms, and that self‑advocacy and referrals are becoming more important.

5. **Pre‑MBA timing opportunity**
   - Discussion around early recruiting (April) and the August 1 resume deadline surfaces a strong use case: giving admitted students a structured way to work on stories and resumes *before* they arrive on campus.
   - Amanda responds positively to using Careerspan as a yield/early engagement tool.

6. **Data and privacy reassurance**
   - Questions about data usage and LLM training lead to a clear articulation of safeguards: “do not train” flags, student account ownership, deletion rights, and limits on what employers can see.
   - This appears to reduce latent risk concerns and keeps the conversation focused on value.

7. **Request for references and outcome data**
   - Michael asks specifically to speak with Columbia and UMass counterparts and to see empirical results.
   - This is the main gating item between “this is interesting” and any concrete pilot or purchase discussion.

These moments collectively shift Johnson from curiosity to **serious consideration**, while making clear that references, outcomes, and a thoughtful onboarding plan are prerequisites for any adoption.
