# B03 – Decisions

## Confirmed Decisions

1. **Use Ben’s upcoming talk as the primary test asset.**
   - The **velocity coding** talk Ben is already giving this Thursday will be used as the reference content for future community collaborations.
   - Success will be judged in part by **sign‑ups and attendee interest**, including response from Next Play members.

2. **Promote the talk directly into Friends of Next Play.**
   - Vrijen will (and in this call, effectively does) **post the Luma/Maven event link into Friends of Next Play**, optionally wrapped in a **Short.io** link for tracking.

3. **Leverage AI Collective as a parallel distribution channel.**
   - Vrijen will send the talk to **Tejas (AI Collective – NJ lead)** and ask him to **post it in AI Collective forums**.
   - AI Collective is treated as a peer community to Next Play for purposes of event distribution.

4. **Sequence collaborations around talks first, course later.**
   - For now, the plan is to **continue running talks**, including with AI Collective, and **defer any course commitment** until Ben has more reps and capacity.

5. **Anchor Q1 in recurring events via a small set of communities.**
   - With just **Next Play** and **AI Collective**, they can plausibly sustain **an event every 3–4 weeks** in Q1.
   - Other communities (South Park Commons, AI Circle, etc.) are considered optional additions rather than primary pillars.

## Tentative / Directional Decisions

1. **Timing for deeper collaboration with Ben Lang.**
   - Direction: **wait until after Thursday’s talk**, then email Ben with results and a concrete proposal for a Next Play event in the **new year**.
   - The group leans toward a **virtual event** as the first concrete offering, though IRL and newsletter options remain open.

2. **Framing networking as a flagship Zo use case.**
   - There is strong mutual agreement that **networking is a natural wedge** for Zo, given its ability to connect email, LinkedIn, and other data sources.
   - Directionally, this will likely become a **core storyline** in how Zo is presented to these communities, even if the exact feature set is not yet fixed.

3. **Using partner tools via revenue‑sharing.**
   - There is interest in positioning tools like **Aviato** as “preferred enrichment providers” embedded into Zo, with **revenue share** based on user spend.
   - No specific partner is chosen yet, but the **model** is implicitly accepted as viable.

## Explicit Non‑Decisions / Deferred Items

1. **Full course with AI Collective.**
   - Tiffany suggests that Ben could eventually run a **course** in partnership with AI Collective.
   - Ben notes he is **not ready** to commit and that designing and running a course would be a heavy lift.
   - Decision: **defer**; treat it as a future possibility contingent on talk performance and Ben’s bandwidth.

2. **Final format mix for Next Play (talk vs IRL vs newsletter).**
   - Options discussed: virtual talk, IRL event in New York, or content for Ben’s **newsletter**.
   - Decision: default to **virtual talk first**, then revisit IRL and newsletter after seeing data; no final multi‑format plan locked in yet.

3. **Exact product spec for the networking tool.**
   - Concept is clear (social graph + cross‑channel activity + cadence‑based follow‑ups), but **no concrete product spec or build commitment** is made in this call.
   - Collaboration with **David Spiegel** and potential partner tools remains at the idea stage.

4. **How heavily to tap each community beyond Q1.**
   - They acknowledge that between **Next Play, AI Collective, South Park Commons, AI Circle**, etc., they have more potential inventory than they will use.
   - No decision is made on **long‑term frequency** or which communities will be prioritized beyond the initial Q1 experiments.
