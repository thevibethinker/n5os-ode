# B06 – Business Context

## Zo’s Strategic Context
- Zo is looking for **high‑signal distribution channels** where a small number of well‑executed events can drive outsized awareness and adoption.
- The near‑term GTM strategy leans heavily on **community collaborations** rather than paid marketing:
  - Co‑hosted talks with credible partners.
  - Embedding Zo into workflows that communities already care about (e.g., networking, hiring, AI practice).
- Networking is framed as a **core wedge use case**:
  - Zo can see activity across channels like email and (potentially) LinkedIn.
  - This makes it uniquely positioned to help users understand **who is in their life** and **how often they are in touch**.

## Next Play
- Described as a **1,000+ member paid community** where every participant pays **around $12/month**.
- Considered **high‑signal**:
  - Members are willing to pay for access.
  - The community runs **relatively few events**, so each one stands out.
- There is a semi‑exclusive **“Friends of Next Play”** area that can amplify specific opportunities (e.g., Ben’s velocity‑coding talk, Zo collaboration).
- Business value for Zo:
  - Early‑adopter audience for Zo as a tool for ambitious, engaged professionals.
  - A setting where a **single well‑received event** can generate both users and social proof.

## Ben Lang & the Velocity‑Coding Talk
- Ben’s **velocity‑coding talk** is a proven asset:
  - It has already been run multiple times (e.g., including an upcoming session with >100 registrations via Luma).
  - Audiences respond well enough that the talk is being “run back” based on demand.
- For Zo, Ben represents:
  - **Credible content** that communities are already excited about.
  - A partner whose success metrics (sign‑ups, engagement) can be aligned with Zo’s distribution goals.
- The plan is to **instrument interest** (especially from Next Play) using **Short.io links** and event analytics, then use those numbers to justify deeper collaboration.

## AI Collective
- AI Collective is described as a **large AI‑focused community**, with an estimated **80–90k people** across the network.
- Tejas, a close friend of Vrijen, leads the **New Jersey chapter**, giving Zo a warm entry point.
- AI Collective is:
  - Another high‑leverage venue for **velocity‑coding talks**.
  - A potential partner for a more substantial **course** in the future, once talks have proven consistently successful.
- Business value:
  - Access to a broad base of AI‑interested practitioners.
  - An opportunity to position Zo as a **default personal AI environment** for builders inside AI communities.

## Sponsors and Cost Structure
- **RO** and **Oracle** are mentioned as eager sponsors.
- Implication: **Monetary costs of events (venue, tooling, etc.) can likely be covered** by sponsors.
- The real constraint is **time and coordination effort**, not budget.

## Networking Tool Opportunity
- Pain point articulated: there is **no great tool for staying on top of networking** across channels.
- Desired capabilities:
  - A **social graph** that knows who you talk to and through which channels.
  - Ability to set and manage **cadences** (e.g., every 3 months for mentors, every 6 months for former managers).
  - Integration with email and LinkedIn, potentially enriched by third‑party data providers.
- Business hypothesis:
  - Zo can fill this gap by leveraging its position as a **central personal server / AI environment**.
  - It can also form **partnerships with enrichment tools** (e.g., Aviato‑like services) where Zo handles the integration and receives a share of revenue.

## Multi‑Community Event Inventory
- Between **Next Play**, **AI Collective**, **South Park Commons**, and **AI Circle**, Zo has access to several communities with overlapping but distinct audiences.
- For Q1, the plan is to:
  - Use Next Play and AI Collective as the **core event pipeline** (roughly one event every 3–4 weeks).
  - Optionally layer in other communities as capacity allows.
- This structure is intended to:
  - Give Zo **repeated exposure** to aligned audiences.
  - Provide enough repetition for Ben to refine his talk and potentially build toward a course.

## Summary
- Zo is positioning itself at the intersection of **AI, networking, and community‑driven growth**.
- This meeting anchors that strategy in two concrete pillars:
  1. **Event collaborations** with Next Play and AI Collective.
  2. A **networking tool concept** that Zo is uniquely positioned to deliver, potentially in partnership with enrichment providers.
- The immediate business objective is to **validate interest and engagement** via talks, then use that as a springboard for deeper integrations and offerings.
