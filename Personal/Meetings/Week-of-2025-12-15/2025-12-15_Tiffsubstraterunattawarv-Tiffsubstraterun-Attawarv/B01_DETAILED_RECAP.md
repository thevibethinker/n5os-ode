# B01 – Detailed Recap

## Overview
Conversation between **Tiffany Huang** and **Vrijen Attawar** focused on event strategy for Zo, specifically leveraging the **Next Play** and **AI Collective** communities, and exploring a broader **networking-tool** concept that Zo is uniquely positioned to power.

## Chronological Recap

1. **Check‑in and personal context**
   - Tiffany joins from Puerto Rico; they briefly discuss quality of life there and a recent power outage.
   - Tiffany mentions being a bit under the weather after Ben made her sick; light teasing (“Good job, Ben”) sets a friendly tone.
   - Vrijen shares that things are going well overall: year‑end is shaped by encouraging conversations about potential **acquisition** and solid **sales** momentum.

2. **Framing the call: two main reasons**
   - Vrijen explains he wanted to talk for two reasons:
     1. Positive momentum with **Ben Lang** and the **Next Play** community.
     2. A broader **networking use case** for Zo that he wants to think through together.

3. **Next Play community opportunity**
   - Next Play is described as a **1,000+ person, paid community** (members pay about **$12/month**), considered **high‑signal** relative to typical communities.
   - They don’t run many events, so any event Zo does there will stand out rather than be lost in noise.
   - Vrijen has already sent Ben **five or six different event options**; Ben replied that they can do **one or multiple**, signaling high flexibility.
   - There is also a “**Friends of Next Play**” space that Ben keeps relatively exclusive, making it a strong channel for experiments.

4. **Virtual vs in‑person formats and sponsors**
   - Tiffany asks whether the events must be virtual or could be **in‑person in New York**.
   - Vrijen clarifies they can do **either**:
     - Ben expressed a **preference for virtual**, which is why that was proposed first.
     - In‑person is still on the table, especially given other communities and local networks.
   - Vrijen mentions a close friend **Tejas**, who just became the **New Jersey head of AI Collective** and is eager to bring Zo into that community.
   - He notes that **RO** and **Oracle** have both shown eagerness to **sponsor**, so **money is not the main constraint**; the real cost is coordination and effort.

5. **Event concepts: velocity coding and networking**
   - One straightforward option is to re‑run the existing **“velocity coding” intro talk**, which has already worked well in past settings.
   - A second idea is a more **networking‑oriented session**:
     - Vrijen argues networking is a **natural wedge use case for Zo** because Zo can blend data across systems (email, LinkedIn, etc.) in a way no conventional networking tool can.
     - He describes pain points around staying on top of relationships and setting **cadence‑based check‑ins** for mentors, former managers, and other contacts.
     - He imagines leveraging tools like **Aviato** or similar enrichment services inside Zo, potentially via **revenue‑sharing partnerships** where Zo helps users embed these tools and receives a cut.

6. **Clarifying the two threads**
   - Tiffany summarizes that there are effectively **two separate (but linkable) ideas**:
     1. An event (or series of events) with **Ben Lang / Next Play**.
     2. A **networking product / workflow** for Zo that could be surfaced via events.
   - She agrees Zo is well positioned to understand “who is in your life” if it’s integrated with email, SMS, and LinkedIn, and sees real potential in the networking tooling direction.
   - She suggests they **don’t have to couple** the two ideas; they can run the event track and the product exploration track in parallel.

7. **Ben Lang talk and instrumentation**
   - Tiffany proposes starting with a **virtual event** for Next Play and potentially using **Ben’s existing talk** (e.g., the “velocity coding” session) as the anchor.
   - Ben is giving that talk again **this Thursday** (via **Luma / Maven**), which provides another data point on performance and audience fit.
   - Tiffany notes there are already **~132+ registrants** for an upcoming session, reinforcing that the content resonates.
   - Vrijen wants to **measure interest** specifically from Next Play:
     - Tiffany shares the event link and references Ben’s recent newsletter.
     - Vrijen posts the link in **Friends of Next Play**, mentioning he can also put it behind a **Short.io** link to track click‑throughs.

8. **AI Collective opportunity**
   - They discuss **AI Collective** as another major lever:
     - It is described as having **80–90k people** across the broader community.
     - Tejas, who runs the New Jersey chapter and is a close friend of Vrijen’s, is already enthusiastic about Zo and impressed by Ben.
   - Plan:
     - Vrijen sends Tejas the talk link and asks him to **post it in AI Collective forums**.
     - Once that’s done, he intends to **set up a 20‑minute intro call** between Tejas and Ben so they can coordinate directly on future collaborations.

9. **Course idea vs talks**
   - Tiffany raises the possibility of Ben doing a more substantial **course** with AI Collective as a distribution partner.
   - She notes that after several repetitions of the talk, Ben will have the reps and content needed to consider a course.
   - Ben (speaking briefly in the transcript) is **not ready to commit** to a full course yet, acknowledging it would be a significant lift.
   - They converge on a **staged approach**: keep running talks (including with AI Collective), then revisit the course idea once there is more data and Ben has more cycles.

10. **Q1 event inventory and year‑end goals**
    - Vrijen sketches a rough **event inventory** for Q1:
      - With just **Next Play** and **AI Collective**, they could comfortably run **an event every 3–4 weeks**.
      - Other communities like **South Park Commons** and **AI Circle** offer additional optional inventory.
    - They align on concrete **year‑end objectives**:
      - **“Lock down” Ben Lang** and the Next Play collaboration (post‑Thursday follow‑up and new‑year event planning).
      - **“Lock down” Tejas and AI Collective** as a partner community for recurring events.

11. **Close and personal logistics**
    - The call ends with friendly small talk:
      - Tiffany thanks Vrijen and reiterates excitement about the scale of AI Collective.
      - They discuss potential overlapping time in **Puerto Rico** around a **5K/10K “Ruta del Encanto”** race and Tiffany’s family plans for Chinese New Year.
      - They close on a warm, appreciative note with mutual thanks and well‑wishes.
