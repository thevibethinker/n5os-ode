# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

1.  **The "Hallmark" Strategy:** The idea of positioning a business acquisition as a "Hallmark movie suitor" dynamic—where the suitor "falls in love" with the company's "Christmas tree farm" (tech/mission)—as a way to humanize and strategize negotiation leverage.
2.  **Sovereign Data as Leverage:** Using the fundamental "brokenness" of a larger competitor's data (e.g., the Clara founder's data) as an immediate, aggressive entry point for a buy/partnership conversation.
3.  **The Browser vs. Desktop File System Divide:** The psychological gap between "Zoomers" who see the web as the OS and "Grandpas" who require a file-based, document-centric mental model to feel in control of their work.
