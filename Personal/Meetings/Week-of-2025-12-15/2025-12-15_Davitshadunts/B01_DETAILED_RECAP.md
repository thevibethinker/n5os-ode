# Detailed Recap (B01)

1. **Opening and personal check‑in**
   - V and Davit open with light rapport: pronunciation of V's name, health updates, and Davit's status as a graduating student dealing with exams and general job‑search stress.
   - V shares that he recently recovered from a short but intense COVID bout and checks that a family situation on Davit's side has resolved; both confirm things are okay.

2. **Context: intro and why they're meeting**
   - V notes that Haik spoke highly of Davit and that Davit has a strong personal site, which motivated this 1:1 call.
   - V frames the conversation as a chance to show how Careerspan can support Davit and to explore concrete opportunities through Careerspan's hiring network and Marvin Ventures.

3. **Walkthrough of Careerspan stories experience**
   - V confirms Davit has already tried the "tell stories to the AI" workflow on Careerspan.
   - Davit reports that the tool helped him structure his experience in a STAR‑like framework and that he found the exercise positive enough that he plans to add more stories.
   - V emphasizes the compounding value of building a large story library: once enough experiences are captured, Davit can effectively "one‑click apply" to multiple roles without redoing work.

4. **Marvin Ventures job board and first role exploration**
   - V reminds Davit that Careerspan is an official talent partner for Marvin Ventures and that the main jobs on the Careerspan job board flow through that partnership.
   - He explains that if Davit flags specific roles he likes, V can reach out directly to founders or hiring contacts and advocate on his behalf using Davit's Careerspan profile and resume.
   - They focus on roles at Deep Tune and Yarn; Davit raises concern that many startups explicitly do not sponsor visas.

5. **Deep Tune vs. Yarn: narrowing to a viable path**
   - V checks visa policies and confirms that Yarn does **not** sponsor visas, but Deep Tune **does**.
   - Together they decide to prioritize Deep Tune as the primary near‑term opportunity.
   - Davit mentions that he was already in contact with Holly, a recruiter working on Deep Tune roles, but that a series of scheduling and time‑zone mishaps have prevented the two from actually speaking.

6. **Plan around Holly and Deep Tune**
   - V explains that he already knows Holly and has been forwarding candidates to her from Careerspan.

7. **Additional tools: Warmer Jobs and leveraging networks**

   - He explains that Davit can upload his LinkedIn connections, get a list of roles where he has first‑degree contacts, and then add V on Warmer Jobs to inherit V's network visibility.
   - V shares a discount code ("careerspan 20") and positions this as an extra channel alongside Careerspan, not a replacement.

8. **True Meter and AI‑native engineer opportunities**
   - V brings up True Meter, where a contact (Ali) is hiring for several AI‑native backend engineer roles.

   - V plans to message Ali and use those analyses to advocate for Davit as one of a small set of recommended candidates.

9. **Candidate profile and constraints**
   - Davit shares his background: five years as a full‑stack software engineer, now doing AI research at Columbia.
   - He describes the gap employers perceive: they want production‑level agentic/GenAI experience, but he was in school during the recent AI boom and thus lacks shipped, production agent projects despite strong conceptual understanding.

10. **Zo Computer and higher‑leverage advocacy**

    - He explains that he is close with the Zo founders and would be comfortable strongly advocating for Davit if his Careerspan match score for the role is high enough (e.g. 80+).
    - The path here is: Davit runs the Zo role through Careerspan, fills gaps with stories, and then V uses that narrative plus his relationship to secure founder attention.

11. **Resume generation and reducing application fatigue**
    - Davit notes he has roughly eight versions of his CV and finds customizing it for every application exhausting; he has already submitted ~250 applications overall.
    - V describes Careerspan's resume generator: once stories and role analyses are in place, the system can generate a semantically tailored resume aligned with each job description, including explanations and suggestions for how to improve bullet points.
    - V encourages Davit to lean on this workflow rather than manually juggling multiple resume variants.

12. **Closing: commitments and mutual expectations**
    - Davit commits to: adding more stories on Careerspan; running the Deep Tune, True Meter, and Zo roles through the platform; sending V his resume; and sharing the Careerspan strategic positioning outputs.

    - They close on a positive, collaborative tone with a shared objective: "let's get you hired," and Davit expresses gratitude and intent to recommend Careerspan to classmates who are also struggling in the current market.
