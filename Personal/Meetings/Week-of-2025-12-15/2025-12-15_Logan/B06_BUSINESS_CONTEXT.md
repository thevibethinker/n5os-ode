# B06 – Business Context

## Careerspan – current positioning

- **Product concept**: Careerspan helps candidates tell richer, story‑driven narratives that “go beyond bullets” on a resume. It uses structured story collection (multiple “stories” per candidate) and tools like the “crystal ball” and “detector” to surface signal for hiring managers.
- **Business model (implied)**:
  - B2B SaaS / services hybrid, working with hiring leaders and founders (e.g., Davide) to structure roles, job descriptions, and candidate pipelines.
  - Also building a **public job board** where companies list roles and candidates apply through Careerspan’s storytelling flow.
- **Core motion**: Partner with high‑leverage operators (e.g., Erica with a large DevRel following) and high‑intent hiring teams (Davide, Davis/TeamWork Online, Darwinbox) who value depth over volume.

## Market conditions

- **Edtech and adjacent markets**
  - Logan notes the broader edtech environment is “brutal”: very little funding and few companies actively hiring.
  - In founder communities (e.g., Transcend), people aren’t sharing many open roles; conversations are more about scarcity than expansion.
- **Developer relations / platform hiring**
  - Erica’s experience illustrates volatility in DevRel: she’s been laid off twice, including as part of big Google cuts.
  - Even strong operators in DevRel are wrestling with whether the market sees them as engineering/product or as marketing—this ambiguity shapes how Careerspan might need to frame value for that segment.

## Current go‑to‑market channels

- **Communities and networks**
  - **Transcend Network**: alumni pool that could be valuable as they move into new roles with hiring authority.
  - **Next Play** and similar communities: effective for distributing job board links and reaching talent.
  - Erica’s communities and LinkedIn audience: concentrated developer and DevRel talent.
- **Job board performance**
  - Early signal: ~80% of candidates who complete one story go on to complete a second, which validates the “two‑story minimum” concept and suggests strong engagement with the narrative format.
  - Posts on Next Play are getting positive engagement (e.g., “rockets” and “hugs” reactions) and driving real traffic.

## Key accounts and prospects

- **Erica (DevRel / community leader)**
  - Represents a distribution‑first partnership opportunity: if she uses and endorses Careerspan with her 26K developer‑centric followers, this could meaningfully expand reach into senior engineering/DevRel talent.

- **Davide**
  - Running multiple roles through Careerspan (client success architect, design/product roles) and wants rigorous A/B testing of JDs.
  - If structured well, his roles could become a case study in how JD framing impacts funnel quality and volume.

- **Davis / TeamWork Online**
  - Viewed as a potential inflection‑point customer whose decision could materially affect runway and morale.
  - Represents a more traditional recruiting/talent‑platform opportunity where Careerspan might plug into an existing ecosystem.

- **Darwinbox**
  - HR‑tech/HRIS‑style platform; potential partner where Careerspan could be layered on as a candidate signal/intelligence layer.

- **Bradford’s K‑12/AR software company**
  - Deprioritized due to lack of budget and limited strategic upside; mainly reinforces the need for strict opportunity‑cost discipline.

## Operational context

- **Internal capacity**
  - Team is small and stretched: Logan is nearing burnout, V is carrying heavy sales and relationship load, and Ilsa is juggling complex operational and personal challenges.
  - This makes **focus** and **leverage per conversation** critical; they cannot pursue every warm lead.

- **Systems in motion**
  - Job board + Notion + magic links architecture is powerful but can become confusing when clients like Davide run ad‑hoc experiments.
  - The team is mid‑stream in tightening this system so they can support A/B testing without sacrificing clarity or analytics integrity.
