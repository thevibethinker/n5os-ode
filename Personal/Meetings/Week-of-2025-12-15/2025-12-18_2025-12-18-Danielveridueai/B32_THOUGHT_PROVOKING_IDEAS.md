# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

## 1. The Zero Marginal Cost of Bullshit
The core insight is that generative AI has destroyed the "Resume as a Signal" era. If an AI can perfectly fabricate a resume at zero cost, the traditional ATS (Applicant Tracking System) becomes a "Noise Multiplier" rather than a "Signal Extractor." This necessitates a shift from *evaluating documents* to *evaluating interactions* (like conversational coaching) which have a higher "cognitive cost" for candidates but yield infinitely higher signal for founders.

## 2. Hiring as a "Moneyball" Exercise in Hidden Markets
The example of the Bozeman, Montana infrastructure engineer highlights a strategic "weirdness": top-tier talent is increasingly geographically dispersed and "underpriced" because they lack the "prestige signals" (Elite Universities, Tier 1 Startup experience) that traditional recruiters look for. A platform that can find "Messi on a budget" through semantic evaluation rather than credential-matching provides a massive competitive advantage for early-stage startups with limited capital.

## 3. The Career Coach as the "Trojan Horse" for Truth
Traditional interviews are a "bad compromise between getting to know someone and evaluating them"—candidates are guarded and performative. By contrast, a *coaching* interaction encourages vulnerability and deep reflection because the candidate is trying to understand their own value proposition. Capturing that coaching data and transforming it into hiring intelligence creates a "Truth Layer" that traditional interview processes cannot access.
