# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

### 1. The Paradox of AI Productivity: "Concentrated Talent Gravity"
The traditional paradox of "competitive hiring in a high-layoff market" is resolved by the theory that AI doesn't just increase productivity; it increases the "performance floor" required for retention. If AI makes a "cracked" engineer 10x more effective, the marginal utility of a mediocre engineer drops to near-zero or negative (due to coordination overhead). This creates a "gravity well" where top-tier talent becomes exponentially more valuable and passive, while the available pool of applicants is systematically composed of those whose productivity was insufficient to justify an AI-augmented seat.

### 2. The Death of the "Documentary Signal" (Resume as Fiction)
When the marginal cost of "bullshit" (AI-fabricated resumes) reaches zero, the resume ceases to be a document of record and becomes a pure artifact of fiction. We are moving from a **Documentary Economy** (where credentials/resumes provide signal) to a **Substantive/Cognitive Economy**. In this new state, "truth" can only be extracted through high-friction, cognitively intense interactions (like Careerspan’s "Stories") that are too expensive for a low-signal actor to fake, effectively re-introducing "costly signaling" into a world of free digital fabrication.

### 3. Hiring as "Moneyball" via Semantic Vibes
The shift from "Skills Assessment" (LeetCode, Whiteboards) to "Semantic Evaluation" (Vibes/Values/Mindset) represents the "Moneyballing" of human capital. Since 89-90% of hires fail due to soft skills mismatch rather than technical inability, the "underpriced asset" is no longer the engineer with the best credentials, but the "cultural outlier" whose semantic profile perfectly matches a specific team's "vibe" but who would be filtered out by traditional, credential-heavy ATS systems.
