# Decisions (B03)

1. **Run a targeted email campaign to career‑services leaders**
   - **Decision:** Vrijen will draft and send an email blast to prior and current university contacts (e.g., Rice, Columbia, UMass, Cornell Johnson) highlighting 2025 outcomes and positioning Careerspan for 2026.
   - **Rationale:** Create urgency and FOMO by framing Careerspan as the tool students already love and peers are adopting, while schools themselves “have nothing.”

2. **Anchor the campaign around concrete proof points and marquee logos**
   - **Decision:** The email must include UMass study results (usage and impact metrics) and name high‑signal schools (Rice, Columbia, UMass, Cornell) directly in the subject line and/or body.
   - **Rationale:** Quantitative evidence plus recognizable brands is necessary to move skeptical or slow career‑services offices.

3. **Use personal, not generic, sending identity**
   - **Decision:** Emails should be sent from a personal sender identity (not a generic contact@) to maximize open and reply rates.
   - **Rationale:** Recipients respond better to a known, human sender; it also matches the advisory, relationship‑driven nature of the product.

4. **Prepare a concise metrics/one‑pager package for partners**
   - **Decision:** Vrijen will compile a tight summary (metrics + logos + narrative) from UMass and other pilots and send it to Kamina.
   - **Rationale:** Kamina will not pitch ASA, Strada, or other national programs until she can “go in solid” with data and a clear story.

5. **Pursue ASA and Strada as priority strategic acquirer/funder targets**
   - **Decision:** ASA and Strada are explicitly prioritized as high‑fit organizations to explore for funding or acquisition.
   - **Rationale:** Both control meaningful capital, care about student outcomes (especially for HBCUs and underserved institutions), and already know Kamina and/or Careerspan.

6. **Conditional: Kamina to activate ASA/Strada intros once materials arrive**
   - **Decision:** After receiving the metrics pack and seeing the email blast, Kamina will reach out to Jane (ASA) and Darren (Strada) to explore partnership/funding/acquisition conversations.
   - **Rationale:** Leverage her credibility and relationships at the moment when proof and momentum are fresh.

7. **Use upcoming events as GTM and BD platforms**
   - **Decision:** Kamina will keep Careerspan in mind for upcoming events (Mount Saint Vincent, BMCC, St. Peter’s) and will invite Vrijen to attend when timing aligns.
   - **Rationale:** These events gather high‑intent students and decision‑makers in one place; they are efficient settings to seed pilots or deepen relationships.

8. **General orientation for exit**
   - **Decision (soft):** Vrijen will continue exploring acquisition/acqui‑hire paths while still pushing forward on school and employer GTM, with the explicit intent of creating a “good exit” for the team and advisors.
   - **Rationale:** A stronger product and customer base improves acquisition terms; exit and GTM are not mutually exclusive in the near term.
