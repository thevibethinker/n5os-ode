### Product Strategy: Direct Apply Focus
**Core Tension:** Ilse advocated strongly for committing fully to direct-apply strategy vs. hedging bets with general job application positioning

**Ilse's Position:**
- Current UX is confusing—users don't understand they're applying to specific companies via direct links
- Messaging needs overhaul: "We're killing the resume. You tell stories for THIS job, not general job hunting"
- Positioning should be: "Prove yourself to Skillcraft" not "Prepare with Careerspan"
- Most users applying to jobs aren't interested in general career guidance; they want direct job applications

**Competing Priority (V's concern):**
- Suggested better data capture and analysis might be more valuable use of remaining dev resources
- Mentioned potential LinkedIn-based signals research from Aviato founder (Conrad) could differentiate product
- Concerned about over-investing in UX when strategic differentiator (better data/insights) might drive acquisition

**Resolution:**
- Team agreed direct-apply UX improvements are highest priority with available resources
- Reduces unnecessary friction without eliminating productive friction (actual conversation/story-telling)
- Enables next phase: ensuring promoted jobs get qualified candidate flow

### Funnel Analysis & Candidate Outreach
- **Data Shared:** Ilse circulated spreadsheet with 20-25+ people who clicked Skillcraft link but didn't apply
- **Distributed Column:** Indicates who got job added to account after clicking direct-apply link (not necessarily viewed it)
- **Low Apply-to-View Ratio:** 30+ people distributed job but only 5 applied
- **Root Cause Hypothesis:** UX friction—users land on homepage instead of application page
- **Solution:** Redirect users directly to application page on click; don't require explicit navigation
- **Follow-up Plan:** Logan to email non-applicants to understand drop-off points (offer both calls and written feedback)

### UX/Product Friction Points Identified

**Issue 1: Homepage Redirect Problem**
- When users click magic link and create account, they land on homepage/welcome page
- Should land directly on application page
- Users don't know to navigate to "Applications" section
- Creates unnecessary friction point

**Issue 2: Messaging Misalignment**
- "Vibe check" language no longer makes sense for direct-apply jobs
- Should reframe as "Pre-flight check" or "Are you ready for this?"
- Messaging should emphasize: "Tell stories once, reuse everywhere" value prop
- Missing context: "This will take time. Do it right, and it transforms your candidacy for THIS role"

**Issue 3: Notion Template / Sync Block**
- V had changed job posting template language but sync blocks weren't updating across all postings
- Fixed by removing JD from sync block; now regenerating per listing
- Cannot discuss score/algorithmic details in template text (compliance/positioning)

### Strategic Positioning Discussion

**V's Key Insight:** Employers want candidates "proving themselves to [Company]" not "preparing with Careerspan"
- Even job board language should emphasize company focus
- Score is only relevant as "Are there red flags?" indicator

- Real-world context: Senior candidates expect non-trivial application processes; lower-level candidates need encouragement
