# B21 – Key Moments

1. **Pushing Fireflies past its design boundaries**
   - Ilse repeatedly frames a dramatic poetry reading as "mission critical" and part of the 2026 vision, while Fireflies insists it is only for meeting tasks.
   - This playful standoff surfaces a serious insight: tools must be positioned and used within their real capabilities, not wishful ones.

2. **Ilse proposes a configurable job-analysis endpoint**
   - Ilse floats an endpoint that would let someone specify how they want a job analyzed, bypassing repeated chat tweaks.
   - V acknowledges the idea but confirms there is no strong user pull yet, leading to a decision to defer this feature in favor of conversation UX.

3. **Reality-check on analytics and infrastructure costs**
   - V asks for a centralized way to see how many people view roles, apply, and complete onboarding.
   - Ilse explains the Firebase-style data model and how rollups across employers could become read-expensive, especially beyond ~1,000 views.
   - The group aligns on using manual spreadsheets plus carefully scoped endpoints instead of fully-automated dashboards.

4. **Crypto regret as a lens on risk**
   - Logan and Ilse share stories about friends who became wealthy through crypto and Ilse’s near-miss with a Bitcoin ATM business.
   - V reflects on “every smart, reasonable person” missing crypto versus “degenerate” risk-takers winning, highlighting how hard it is to calibrate risk.
   - This conversation quietly informs how cautious they choose to be with infra bets versus GTM experiments.

5. **Ilya’s LinkedIn-led outbound plan**
   - Ilya notes he has more recruiter and hiring-manager connections than expected and wants to message them directly to “get off your butt and try Careerspan.”
   - He walks through possible response paths (no reply, curiosity, willingness to pay) and asks for clarity on offers and next steps.
   - This is the pivot from internal plumbing to externally facing revenue motion.

6. **V’s stance on December experiments and pricing**
   - V is open to creative December offers (e.g., first ~100 candidates processed, or small role bundles) as long as they feel premium, not cheap.
   - This sets a clear tone: experiments are encouraged, but brand positioning and perceived value cannot be compromised.

7. **GTM2: committing to LinkedIn groups as a channel**
   - Ilya describes GTM2: ghostwritten posts under V/Logan’s names in curated LinkedIn groups where buyers congregate.
   - The team acknowledges it as a slow-burn strategy but agrees it is strategically important because it targets real decision-makers.

8. **Affirmation of Ilya’s multi-stream progress**
   - Near the close, V explicitly appreciates that Ilya’s various workstreams (sales content, GTM2, outreach) are converging.
   - This reinforces Ilya’s role as GTM owner and signals continued investment in his initiatives.
