# B32: Thought Provoking Ideas

1. **The Rationality Trap in Early Tech:** The discussion of how "every smart, reasonable person missed the crypto thing" because they saw the concrete risks (legal, regulatory), while "every degenerate dumbass" succeeded. It highlights a recurring theme where high intelligence/risk-aversion acts as a barrier to entry for paradigm-shifting, high-volatility technologies.
2. **The Visceral vs. Intellectual Divide in Sci-Fi:** The comparison of *The Good Place* (intellectual/uplifting) vs. *Upload* (visceral/horrific) as a lens for user acceptance of technology. It suggests that the "loss of bodily control" is a hard line for human comfort, even when presented in a comedic or "convenient" digital afterlife context.
3. **Analytics as an Anti-Pattern in NoSQL:** The idea that "nightly computations... is just work for the sake of analytics." It raises the question of whether startups over-pivot to data collection before they have the infrastructure to support it, potentially bankrupting their "read budget" on vanity metrics.
