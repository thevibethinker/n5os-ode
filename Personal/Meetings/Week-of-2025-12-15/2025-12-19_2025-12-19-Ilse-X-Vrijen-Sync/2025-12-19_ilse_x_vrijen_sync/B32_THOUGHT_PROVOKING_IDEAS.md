# B32 Thought Provoking Ideas

- **The Destiny of the "Good Enough"**: Vrijen's observation that the most load-bearing parts of an application are often those that are just "good enough," because the moment they reach that threshold, resources are diverted to more critical failures, locking the system in a permanent state of local optima.
- **"Sexy vs. Cheap" as a Strategic Pivot**: The conscious decision to ignore unit economics (OpenAI costs) in favor of high-friction removal. It suggests a thesis that in the current AI market, user delight and perceived "magic" (sexiness) are more valuable than a sustainable business model during the traction phase.
- **Loading States as Content Fodder**: Turning a technical weakness (10-15 minute processing times) into a data acquisition opportunity by using "story suggestions" to turn idle waiting into active profile building.
