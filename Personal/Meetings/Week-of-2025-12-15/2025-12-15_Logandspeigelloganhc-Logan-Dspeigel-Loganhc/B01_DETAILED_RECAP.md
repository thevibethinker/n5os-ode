# B01 Detailed Recap

- **Technical Setup & Context**: The meeting began with technical troubleshooting regarding screen sharing and the Zo API. V explained the vision for 'Join Decks' (or similar browser-integrated tools) to act as a middle layer between Zo and the browser (e.g., LinkedIn).
- **Use Case: Social Proof & Intro Suggestions**: V shared a specific example involving a LinkedIn post by John (CEO at Madison). He described the mental process of connecting that post to Christian Debrado (founder-led marketing expert) and how Zo could automate these 'suggested share' moments by recognizing relevance between content and CRM contacts.
- **Networking Rules & Persistence**: V discussed his existing 'rules' for follow-ups (e.g., three follow-ups, avoiding 'checking in' fluff, providing value/facts). He proposed 'stapling' these rules to a user's file system via git-ignore or N5 protections to ensure they aren't easily broken by users.
- **Product Strategy: 'The Box' vs. 'The Cookbook'**: A significant portion of the talk focused on the business model. V used a meal-prep analogy: users can buy the 'cookbook' (instructions) or 'the box' (pre-configured building blocks). They discussed a webinar strategy where users are given a pre-configured 'Zo building block' set to get them up and running in 20 minutes.
- **Tone & Personalization**: V demonstrated how Zo drafts messages in his tone. He explained his 'neutral base + stylistic shift' approach to tone transformation, which he finds more effective than direct one-shot prompting.
- **Market Segmentation**: The discussion shifted from 'job seekers' (high pressure) to 'option maximizers' (type A, slow burn, maintenance-focused). V argued that this 'silent group' is more amenable to a consistent, low-pressure networking system (e.g., one reach-out per week).
- **New Year's Resolution Play**: They identified the current time of year (late December) as the perfect window to pitch this 'keep in touch' resolution system.
