# B06 – Business Context

## Business problem refinement

- This meeting sharpens the view that **ongoing relationship maintenance** is a distinct, underserved need.
- Many high-talent professionals are "option maximizers"—they are not in crisis but want to be ready for future opportunities.
- Existing tools rarely help them:
  - Track who matters most and at what cadence.
  - Turn ambient content (posts, articles) into thoughtful touchpoints.
  - Maintain a feel for tone and history across channels.

## Role of Zo, Dex/join decks, and Condo

- **Zo** provides durable state, search, and orchestration (agents, tags, files).
- **join decks** can act as an in-context assistant surface that knows what’s on screen and can route to Zo.
- **Condo** remains the execution layer for email/LinkedIn messages where users actually send communication.
- The combined stack aims to offer a **calm, low-friction networking OS** without forcing users into a new standalone SaaS.

## Revenue and strategic upside

- For Careerspan/Zo:
  - Enhances their story as infrastructure for knowledge workers’ personal operating systems.
  - Creates a natural upsell path into hiring-related products when job-search needs arise.
- For David:
  - Offers a scalable, semi-productized way to monetize his playbooks beyond 1:1 coaching.
  - Strengthens his positioning as a systems thinker and not just a webinar host.

## Constraints and tradeoffs

- **API & scraping constraints:** LinkedIn remains a bottleneck; heavy automation here is risky, so v1 de‑emphasizes it.
- **User cognitive load:** Too many features or knobs will reduce adoption; the kit must feel approachable and explainable.
- **Data privacy:** Any use of email history or contact data must be explicit and consented to, especially for a sophisticated user base.
