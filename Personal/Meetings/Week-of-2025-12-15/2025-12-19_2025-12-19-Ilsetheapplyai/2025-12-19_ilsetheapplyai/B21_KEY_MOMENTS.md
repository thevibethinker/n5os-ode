# B21: Key Moments

## High-Leverage Quotes

> "I think making for a better user experience at this moment in time is going to count for more." — **Vrijen Attawar**
*Context: Authorizing a potential doubling of application processing costs by moving tasks to OpenAI's priority queue to achieve faster performance.*

> "I need, I know that like we're gonna need to fix that, but I don't know how to fix that without subverting the fact that we're trying to collect such high fidelity information. It's just hard." — **Ilse Funkhouser**
*Context: Discussing the fundamental tension between shortening the "story" completion time and maintaining the high-quality data collection standards of the platform.*

> "The actual product manager me says no stats are more important... but I think improving the user experience is going to be more beneficial to us since we're trying to sell, not since we're trying to grow." — **Ilse Funkhouser**
*Context: Making an executive decision to prioritize UX improvements over technical tracking infrastructure (user behavior stats) because the current business goal is sales/conversion rather than viral growth.*

## Turning Points

### 1. The Cost vs. Speed Pivot (07:16)
Ilse proposes a "flashy and fast" move: bypassing DeepSeek or other slower methods and moving application processing to OpenAI's priority queue. Despite the fact that this could double application costs, Vrijen immediately authorizes it, signaling a clear shift in priority toward user experience and speed over margin during this specific sales period.

### 2. T-Shaped Data Collection Strategy (04:37)
The team discusses moving to a "T-shaped" collection model. Instead of an exhaustive sequential drill, the system would gather "shallow" basics first to provide a quick win for the user, then selectively (or retroactively) drill deep into specific areas. This addresses the critical feedback that the current process takes too long.

### 3. Standards vs. Friction Realization (11:37)
A moment of shared clarity where Vrijen notes that they either have to "lower standards for data or increase standards for people." They identify that the current "slow burn" approach of high-fidelity data collection might be ahead of where most users are, leading to the decision to investigate if users are over-answering questions beyond the required minimums.

### 4. Sales-First Engineering Prioritization (12:07)
Ilse chooses to delay fixing "low-hanging fruit" in analytics (tracking user behavior through sign-up) to focus on Rockle/Applicant process updates. This marks a strategic decision to focus on the "sell" (the presentation and experience for high-stakes meetings like Darwinbox) rather than long-term "growth" metrics.
