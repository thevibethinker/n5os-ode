# B32: Thought-Provoking Ideas

### The "Slow Burn" Hypothesis
V posits that the ultimate solution for data collection isn't a better one-time form, but **"Business Journaling."**
- **The Idea**: Collecting metadata incrementally over weeks rather than in a high-friction burst.
- **Provocation**: Is the current "long process" complaint a failure of the UI, or a failure of the timing? If we were a "journaling app," the friction would be negligible. How can a recruitment tool adopt a journaling identity?

### The "Cost for Flash" Trade-off
V's colleague proposed doubling API costs to solve a 15-minute wait time.
- **Provocation**: At what point does "flashiness" (speed/UX) become a higher ROI investment than gross margin? In early-stage startups, UX speed is often a marketing feature, not just a technical spec.

### The "Standard" Dilemma
"You either have to lower our standards for data or increase our standards for people."
- **Provocation**: If the market is flooded with ChatGPT-written resumes, the "standard for data" must actually *increase* to maintain signal. Does this mean the only viable path is to build tech that makes "hard data collection" feel like a game or a low-effort journal entry?
