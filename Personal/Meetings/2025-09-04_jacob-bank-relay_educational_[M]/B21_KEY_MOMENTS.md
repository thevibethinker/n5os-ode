### Salient Questions

**1. How will organizations manage the transition from large, specialized teams to small, generalist teams with AI agents?**
- Why it matters: Execution risk is real; cultural resistance and change management will be major barriers
- Who should explore: Organizations planning to adopt super IC model
- Action hint: Pilot with one high-trust team, document learnings, measure morale/output before scaling

**2. What skills distinguish a "super IC" from someone who just gets laid off?**
- Why it matters: The distinction between "generalist IC with AI leverage" and "generalist IC struggling without specialization" is the entire value chain
- Who should explore: Individual career strategists, L&D teams
- Action hint: Deep domain expertise + taste + creative direction + AI fluency = super IC; Without first three, just unemployed

**3. Which specialized roles will resist disappearance longest, and why?**
- Why it matters: Different industries and functions will face different timelines; some roles have human relationship/trust elements that slow automation
- Who should explore: HR leaders, function heads
- Action hint: Map roles by: (a) how rote the task is, (b) how much human relationship matters

**4. How do organizations shift from "request headcount" to "be creative with AI" as default instinct?**
- Why it matters: Cultural/incentive structure change is prerequisite to technical execution
- Who should explore: Leadership, OKR owners, budget authority
- Action hint: Anchor budget discussions on AI ROI, not headcount multipliers; reward creative AI solutions

**5. Will the "super IC" model work for roles that are inherently relational (sales, customer success, therapy, coaching)?**
- Why it matters: Jacob's framework works for marketing, writing, content; unclear if it generalizes to all knowledge work
- Who should explore: Service-oriented roles, people-facing functions
- Action hint: Test hypothesis with hybrid model (super IC + specialized AI agents) on a pilot customer segment

**6. How does this transformation affect geographic labor arbitrage and offshoring economics?**
- Why it matters: If small teams in developed markets become viable, labor cost advantages of outsourcing compress
- Who should explore: Global organizations, offshore centers
- Action hint: Implies competitive advantage shifts from cost to speed/quality; reshapes geographic strategy
