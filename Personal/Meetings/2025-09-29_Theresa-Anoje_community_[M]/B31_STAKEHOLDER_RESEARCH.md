# STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

**Perspective**: Speaking as community organizer/impact-aligned event curator in SF ecosystem

---

## Landscape Insights

### 1. **Impact/Social Good Event Aggregation is Underserved**
**Observation**: Theresa built Social Impact SF calendar manually (100+ events for nonprofits, political, social good initiatives). Platform fragmentation means good opportunities get buried.

**Implication**: 
- Community members are hungry for curated discovery (not random LinkedIn job posts)
- Event + career discovery are connected workflows (someone at a climate tech event might want climate tech jobs)
- First-mover advantage for "impact + career" aggregation

**Strategic Value**: Validates Careerspan's thesis that communities can become recruitment channels if positioned authentically around values, not job-first.

---

### 2. **Founder Burnout + Over-Commitment is Industry-Wide**
**Observation**: Theresa mentioned pressure to "always be on" + managing 4+ conflicting Tech Week opportunities. This is explicit founder pain.

**Implication**: 
- Founders hosting events/communities are squeezed for time and monetization options
- "Saying no smartly" is a core competency gap
- Monetization models for community organizers are unclear (she asked "how do I monetize?")

**Strategic Value**: Careerspan can position as a no-friction monetization layer for community organizers (vs. building their own recruiting dept or taking equity deals).

---

### 3. **Connectors are Ecosystem Hubs (Jessica Greenwald Model)**
**Observation**: Theresa emphasized Jessica Greenwald as "everywhere"—Seattle-based but heavily connected to SF + NY. High-touch connectors punch above weight class.

**Implication**: 
- Relationship capital matters more than formal position
- Connectors can bridge geographic markets
- Real value = access to pre-filtered networks (Jessica's impact leaders track)

**Strategic Value**: Identify and cultivate connector relationships (Jessica, Major League Hacking founders, etc.) as force multipliers. These folks can unlock 10x communities.

---

### 4. **Mission + Events = Pre-Qualified Recruiting Pool**
**Observation**: Theresa's Social Impact SF event attendees are self-selected for (a) career interest, (b) impact values, (c) geographic proximity (SF). This is a pre-qualified pipeline.

**Implication**: 
- Traditional recruiting misses this: treats "attending climate tech event" as uncorrelated with "hiring intent"
- Community members are MORE likely to engage with jobs if positioned as mission-aligned
- Diversity naturally emerges (impact communities are more diverse than typical tech pipelines)

**Strategic Value**: Reframe recruitment positioning: "We match you with roles aligned to the causes you care about" vs. generic job boards.

---

### 5. **Diversity/Equity Hackathons as Recruitment Vectors**
**Observation**: Theresa planning November diversity/equity hackathon. Hackathons traditionally source talent but rarely plug into hiring.

**Implication**: 
- Hackathon attendees are high-signal (self-motivated, build-oriented, values-aligned if it's equity-focused)
- Post-hackathon recruitment is under-monetized (most hackathons don't have employer partnerships)
- Early-stage talent + experienced mentors in one place = recruiting goldmine

**Strategic Value**: Proactively offer hackathon employer sponsorship model (we'll introduce employers + talent pipeline in exchange for community/hackathon value). Theresa becomes distribution channel; Careerspan becomes access layer.
