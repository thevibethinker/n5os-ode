## Strategic Context

The meeting explored two primary "sell" scenarios:

1. **Verification/Authentication**: Using Careerspan's unique capability to validate candidate narratives. Ilse has already built manual verification prototype checking consistency across resume, conversation, and interview transcripts. The key insight is detecting when candidates are lying or using AI assistance—a growing hiring manager pain point.

2. **ATS Integration**: More complex but potentially bigger, requires API integration into existing HR systems. Generally lower priority than verification due to integration overhead.

Both players recognize a significant market moment: background verification companies (Checkr, Tofu, First Advantage) have just received major funding, validating the space. The concern is differentiation—Careerspan's angle is narrative consistency, not traditional background checks.

Strategic debate centered on:
- **Founder psychology**: Early-stage founders won't adopt because they trust their intuition; Series A founders have HR processes and will be more receptive
- **Integration friction**: Customers won't send all candidates to Careerspan; they'll use it as a secondary check, requiring seamless API integration
- **Case study potential**: If verification works, a few case studies could create higher valuation narrative

Underlying belief: If Careerspan can solve the integration puzzle, it proves they're defensible enough that market will pay attention.
