## Strategic Context

**The Opportunity:**
Dylan and team have identified a gap in hiring: while major platforms (LinkedIn, Indeed) handle volume, and specialized platforms (Kaggle for data science) handle specific niches, there's no standardized competition-based recruitment for business/early-career talent. Amphoria positions itself as "Kaggle for business students + early-career professionals."

**Market Tailwinds:**
Dylan sees both macro headwinds (tech layoffs, reduced internship availability) and micro tailwinds (students desperate to show differentiated talent, companies needing affordable talent assessment). The macro headwinds paradoxically create urgency for his solution.

**Positioning Insight:**
Vrijen helped Dylan clarify a critical strategic choice: Is Amphoria building in the "assessment/matching" space or the "talent sourcing/visibility" space? Dylan initially conflated both; Vrijen's framework helped him see they're distinct problems with different competitive dynamics. Dylan appeared to lean toward creative/marketing assessment space (where there's less hackathon competition) but has flexibility to pursue both if he finds the right angle.

**Product-Market Fit Signals:**
- 100 users (mostly students)
- 5 businesses signed up for January launch
- Viral Instagram moment that helped acquisition
- Team has done accelerator work, showing seriousness
- Dylan has prior startup experience (was at Fordham doing finance startup work)

**Validation Strategy:**
Dylan is focused on pre-launch positioning and acquiring ~6 months of "core user base" before summer (when hiring market becomes more competitive). He's thinking tactically about distribution and finding the right positioning before scaling.
