# STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

**Perspective:** Speaking as an early-stage founder in recruiting/talent tech space (SIEM co-founder)

---

## Key Industry Insights

**1. VC Investors Don't Understand Recruiting Tech**
- *Observation:* Ash described repeated VC feedback: asking if they charge job seekers for upskilling (fundamentally misunderstanding their model), asking "how will you get acquired," not reading decks, asking generic questions about defensibility.
- *Implication:* Recruiting/talent space may be underfunded relative to its actual addressable market. VCs gravitating toward easier-to-understand categories (maybe B2B SaaS, AI infrastructure).
- *Strategic Value:* Opportunity to raise from angels/family offices who "get it" rather than fighting VC conventional wisdom. Ash actively exploring this: "Just get me some fucking angels that want to write a check."

**2. Employer Hiring Process is Broken Across Multiple Dimensions**
- *Observation:* Both Ash and Vrijen expressed frustration: "People are headless chickens in this job market," inbound recruiting channels ignored entirely, job postings sitting for months, hiring managers desperate for qualified candidates.
- *Implication:* Market pain is real and widespread. Not niche problem - standard operating procedure for growing companies.
- *Strategic Value:* Validates both companies' core theses. SIEM's 5-day placement cycle and Careerspan's curated matching directly solve acute pain points.

**3. Hiring Manager Pain: Volume vs. Quality Trade-off**
- *Observation:* Vrijen described: "Hiring managers prefer curated shortlists over long candidate lists. If you're not a good fit, I'm not going to send them to you. I'm not going to give you 50 best options."
- *Implication:* ATS-based systems optimize for quantity (flood with options). Market has swung far from human judgment. Successful recruiting is returning to curation and judgment.
- *Strategic Value:* Differentiator for both companies: they're saying "no" to candidates rather than yes-ing everyone. This is actually a feature, not a limitation.

**4. Startup Talent Has Specific Characteristics Worth Filtering For**
- *Observation:* SIEM explicitly targets candidates with 2+ years startup experience (preferably early-stage). They reason: startups need people with "scrappy mentality."
- *Implication:* Startup hiring is fundamentally different from big company hiring. Skills differ, but more importantly, *mindset* differs. Candidates from stable, large organizations often struggle with startup pace.
- *Strategic Value:* Clear thesis on candidate segmentation. Could be template for other vertical markets (healthcare startups need healthcare experience + startup mentality, etc.).

**5. Revenue-Share Models Are Becoming Competitive Advantage**
- *Observation:* Ash praised Careerspan's 12-month performance-based model ("I actually fucking admire the stones on you guys for doing that"). SIEM itself uses similar model with recruiting partners.
- *Implication:* Traditional recruiting (upfront fees) is losing to outcome-based pricing. Clients want partners that share risk.
- *Strategic Value:* This shift punishes bad actors (people who place poor candidates) and rewards quality. Market is self-selecting for genuine matching algorithms vs. volume spray.

**6. Community-Based Candidate Sourcing is Underutilized**
- *Observation:* Both Vrijen and Ash discussed alumni communities (ex-Googlers, McKinsey alumni, A16Z employees, Postmates alumni, etc.). These communities have natural affinity + startup experience. Yet most recruiting platforms ignore them.
- *Implication:* Information asymmetry. Many communities exist; most recruiting platforms don't know about them or haven't built relationships. Early-mover advantage for platforms that do.
- *Strategic Value:* Vrijen proposed positioning as "source candidates from world's best communities" - actual credibility through relationships vs. marketing speak.
