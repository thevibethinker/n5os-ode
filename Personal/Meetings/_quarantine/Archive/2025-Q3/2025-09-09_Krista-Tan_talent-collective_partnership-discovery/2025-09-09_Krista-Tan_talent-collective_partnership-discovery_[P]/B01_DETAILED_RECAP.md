# Partnership Discovery: Careerspan + Talent Collective

## Meeting Context

**Participants:** Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan founder), Logan (Careerspan), Krista Tan (Talent Collective co-founder)

**Date:** September 9, 2025

**Type:** Strategic partnership exploration and discovery call

## Careerspan Overview

Careerspan is a 2.5-year-old platform focused on helping job seekers with career transition support, positioning, and storytelling. The product evolves around "3D resumes"—deeper professional narratives beyond bullet points.

### Product Architecture
- **Core mechanism:** Audio-driven conversation interface where candidates talk stream-of-consciousness; the platform reflects back learned mental models of professional experiences
- **Current model:** Completely free B2C product with freemium considerations (future $10/month usage tier after hitting usage caps)
- **Coaching conversations:** Free and always will be
- **Vibe checks:** Feature testing candidates against job descriptions for alignment beyond ATS compatibility—measures values and achievement fit
- **Resume generation:** Capped initially (cap being progressively lifted)

### Business Evolution
- Started with B2C focus (first 1.5 years)
- Shifted to B2B/B2U (university partnerships) for revenue: Columbia, UMass, others on multi-year outcomes-driven contracts
- Current trajectory: Free product for candidates + employer connections + community partnerships
- Positioned as "middle signal alternative" between high-signal referrals (scarce) and low-signal inbound recruiting

## Talent Collective Overview

Community-first platform for talent acquisition professionals, particularly women in TA. Structure: 10 chapters (geographic/network-based), organic growth model through word-of-mouth.

### Community Governance
- Co-founder Krista Tan + co-founder (name not disclosed) bootstrapped for 2 years
- Both co-founders recently took full-time employment for stable income (community remains as passion project)
- Member composition: Mix of job seekers and gainfully employed TA professionals
- Focus: Quality over quantity in programming (reduced from 2 workshops/week to curated offerings to avoid decision fatigue)
- Programs: Workshops, fireside chats, pure mastermind groups, mentorship programs

### Community Priorities
- **Not growth-focused at costs:** Natural organic expansion through snowball effect (new chapter requests happening organically)
- **Member engagement & quality:** Programming must be high-value and worth attendee time
- **Relationship-driven partnership philosophy:** Long-term ROI focus vs. immediate gratification metrics; partners must align with community values

## Core Insights from Discussion

### What Moves Job Seekers
- **Primary need:** Warm introductions to hiring contacts (described as "quickest way to get to an interview")
- **Secondary driver:** Confidence building, career clarity, story positioning
- **Behavioral signal:** Membership quality maintained through filtering: only members who contribute to larger good are welcome; transactional "just here for a job" members don't fit

### Employer Market Reality
- **Market noise:** "A million tools" claiming to provide talented pools; companies overwhelmed by inbound vendor pitches
- **Integration fatigue:** Hiring companies constantly thinking about ATS/integration requirements
- **Key vulnerability:** First impression is critical—if a vendor doesn't show traction immediately, they won't get a second chance in 6 months

### Krista's Talent Professional Perspective
- **Market observations:** Candidates are exhausted, depressed, hopeless in current hiring environment
- **AI opportunity:** Tools getting better/faster; companies starting to assess AI-enabled recruiting solutions
- **But:** Still massive noise and difficulty cutting through competing solutions

### Partnership Criteria (From Krista's Lens)
1. Product/service quality must be evident and differentiated
2. Values alignment essential (long-term relationship building vs. transactional ROI demands)
3. Community-first thinking (give-take symbiotic relationships)

## Strategic Positioning Explored

Careerspan pitched a positioning as a "warm introduction alternative" for employer sourcing. The mechanism:
- Tap Talent Collective's network of talented recruiters (both job seekers and employed TA pros)
- Surface candidates specifically from requested communities (geographic/demographic targeting)
- Freemium-friendly: Careerspan provides free product to candidates; employers get access to sourced talent
- Differentiator: "Reliably provenance" talent vs. mass inbound channels

## Key Market Challenges Identified

1. **Market saturation:** Too many competing solutions claiming similar value prop
2. **Integration demands:** Hiring companies expect seamless ATS/platform connections
3. **Proof requirement:** "Proof is in the pudding" approach needed—free trial with guaranteed traction or credibility loss
4. **Role/industry niching critical:** Careerspan currently focused on Product Management and Engineering roles in tech (described as "still broad" by Logan)

## Partnership Positioning

Not framed as a vendor/client transaction, but as a values-aligned partnership where:
- Careerspan gains access to Talent Collective's curated network for employer sourcing
- Talent Collective members get free access to Careerspan's platform for career development
- Both organizations maintain community-first, relationship-driven philosophies

## Monetization Non-Move

Krista explicitly stated Talent Collective chose NOT to pursue placement monetization despite being recruiters (which is unusual). Reasoning:
- Potential conflict of interest (building community vs. profiting off placements)
- Market hiring not strong enough to justify distraction
- Preference to build community first, see what emerges organically
- Open to future models but not currently aligned with community values

## Conversation Tone & Dynamics

- Respectful, exploratory, values-aligned
- Krista positioned as trusted advisor/market validator (not typical vendor interaction)
- Explicit mutual interest in understanding whether partnership makes sense (not a hard pitch)
- Secondary benefit: Potential to leverage Talent Collective's platform for content sharing and community visibility
