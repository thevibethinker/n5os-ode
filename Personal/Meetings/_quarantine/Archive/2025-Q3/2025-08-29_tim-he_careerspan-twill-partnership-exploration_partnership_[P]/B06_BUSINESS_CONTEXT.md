# Business Context: Twill Partnership Implications

## Revenue Model Discussion & Pricing Agreement

### Model Confirmed: Flat Subscription (B2B2C)
**Structure**: Monthly subscription to companies + community participation
- **Pricing Point**: $2,000/month (established during call)
- **Rationale**: Tim advocated for flat subscription over per-name pricing to avoid "doing math on every name"
- **Economic Logic**: MRR predictability > transactional variance; reduces friction on customer side
- **Careerspan Advantage**: Described as "nice clean for us"; enables forecasting vs. patchy recruiting income
- **Value Proposition to Customer**: Search unlimited, contact unlimited; access to Careerspan database + ongoing community expansion

### Go-to-Market Positioning
**Current Challenge Addressed**: Founders/companies still struggling to find talent despite existing recruiting solutions
- **Root Cause**: ATS systems creating friction (not improving accessibility)
- **Careerspan's Angle**: Offer on-demand shortlist service vs. traditional ATS/recruiter model
- **Competitive Advantage**: Fresh candidate pool updated as new communities onboard; higher-quality matches due to behavioral insights

### Community Revenue Split Model
**Issue**: Community incentive structures create margin trade-offs
- **Twill's Experience**: Offering member kickbacks is "taking away from us, which isn't ideal, but it's like good for the members"
- **Trade-off Recognition**: Most platforms don't offer kickbacks; those that do gain loyalty/stickiness advantage
- **Twill's Position**: Worth taking the hit because of member loyalty and reduced churn
- **Implication for Careerspan**: Need to model similar splits while maintaining unit economics; differentiation through community trust vs. platform scale

## Strategic Partnership Positioning

### Why Twill as a Partner Makes Sense
1. **Complementary Platforms**: Careerspan = supply (talent access); Twill = demand (community building + trust)
2. **Shared Philosophy**: Both reject broken status quo; both building toward better recruitment systems
3. **Non-Competitive**: Twill serves as aggregator of communities; Careerspan serves as talent sourcing layer within those communities
4. **Market Timing**: Both optimizing for seed-stage; partnership validates GTM for both (Careerspan seed narrative; Twill's partner ecosystem)

### Twill's Competitive Advantage Tim Highlighted
- Solves referral friction problem ("I want to help you. I don't have any fucking time to get to know you.")
- Member loyalty through authentic incentives (not just tokens)
- Network effects from multiple communities with aligned hiring incentives

### Careerspan's Differentiation That Resonates with Tim
- Data-driven matching vs. gut-feel referrals
- Behavioral insights from actual candidate activities (not just resume parsing)
- Community trust mechanism (candidates opt-in; not scraped)
- Speed (minutes to quality shortlist) vs. ATS lag

## Unit Economics & Scaling Implications

### Subscription Model Economics
- **Revenue Predictability**: $2k MRR per customer = clean forecasting
- **Scalability Challenge**: Need to acquire customers faster than typical recruiting sales cycle
- **Competitive Pressure**: Tim's suggestion "you just need to get the supply side and the demand will come" is partially correct but assumes supply = communities are sticky (they need value too)

### Community Economics (Hidden Complexity)
- **Revenue Split with Communities**: Not yet finalized; needs modeling
- **Risk**: If Careerspan takes too large a cut, communities won't participate; too small a cut breaks unit economics
- **Twill Precedent**: Tim accepts margin hit for loyalty; Careerspan should model similar
- **Scalability**: Community splits must work at 10x scale (hundreds of communities, potentially)

### Customer Acquisition Economics
- **Twill as Bridge**: If Twill recommends to customers, reduces CAC significantly (warm intro vs. cold outreach)
- **Expansion Opportunity**: Twill could become channel partner; revenue split on referrals a possibility
- **Network Effect**: Each new community → more candidates → more attractive to companies → more subscriptions
