# Key Decisions & Strategic Choices

## Decisions Made During Call

### 1. Careerspan Revenue Model: Flat Subscription (CONFIRMED)
**Decision**: Adopt flat subscription pricing over per-transaction/per-name pricing

**Context**: 
- Vrijen posed the specific question: "Do we charge based on the number of names you want us to reveal and put you in touch with or do we just charge like a flat subscription?"

**Tim's Input**: 
- Flat subscription makes more sense because it eliminates customer math and value comparison friction on each transaction
- Per-transaction pricing forces the buyer to rationalize cost/benefit repeatedly

**Vrijen's Alignment**: 
- Agreed this aligns with preference for predictable MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
- Cleaner for unit economics and financial modeling
- Preferred over "patchy" recruiting-based income volatility

**Impact**: 
- Simplifies GTM messaging
- Aligns with SaaS positioning
- Supports seed fundraising narrative (predictable, scalable revenue)
- De-risks customer adoption (no friction per search)

---

### 2. Careerspan Strategic Pivot: Community First, Revenue Second (FORMALIZED)
**Decision**: Officially shift organizational focus to community onboarding as primary growth engine, with revenue generation as parallel (not primary) responsibility

**Components**:
- **Logan's role**: Dedicated exclusively to community onboarding (as of conversation date)
- **Vrijen's role**: Shift to revenue-generating customer partnerships and business development
- **Rationale**: Solved product is ready; bottleneck is supply-side (community growth) and demand-side (customer adoption)

**Strategic Reasoning**: 
Vrijen articulated that matching algorithms won't be defensible—the true moat is in community relationships and scaled partnerships. This decision operationalizes that insight.

**Timeline**: 
- This shift was implemented "as of this week" (week of August 29, 2025)
- Reflects deliberate reallocation of founder time and organizational resources

**Implications**: 
- Organizational structure now mirrors market strategy (community-focused co-founder, business-focused founder)
- Positions for rapid scaling once product-market fit in communities is proven
- Enables differentiated positioning vs. competitors still focused on algorithm superiority

---

### 3. Hiring System Philosophy: Authenticity Over Performance (ARTICULATED)
**Decision**: Commit to authentic pro-candidate approaches rather than performative signaling

**Context**: 
Vrijen made a forward-looking observation about market evolution: Companies will increasingly "fake signal how pro candidate they are because the backlash from candidates is coming."

**Decision Made**: 
- Careerspan and Twill will remain committed to authentic alignment with candidate interests (vs. performative "employer branding")
- Examples of authentic approaches: Twill's consistent member kickbacks, Careerspan's community-split monetization model
- Positions both as market leaders when authenticity becomes premium

**Business Implication**: 
While immediate ROI isn't clear, being early to authentic positioning creates competitive advantage as market backlash mounts and candidates demand proof of pro-candidate commitment.

**Shared Conviction**: 
Both founders agree that the hiring system is "so much more broken than anyone possibly can even realize" and that genuinely solving problems (not performatively addressing them) is the path to durable competitive advantage.

### 5. Careerspan's Market Positioning: Supply-Side Play Over Matching Innovation
**Decision**: De-prioritize algorithm matching as primary differentiator

**Reasoning**: 
- Matching algorithms will soon be table stakes, not defensible
- Real value is in community access + curated supply
- Competitors will quickly replicate matching capabilities
- Defensible moat is relationship/partnership-based

**Customer Impact**: 
- Frames value proposition around "access to communities" not "better AI"
- Targets customers who want recurring talent pipelines vs. one-off placements
- Positions for long-term relationship value vs. transaction value
