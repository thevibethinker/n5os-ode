# Meeting Recap: Careerspan & Twill Partnership Exploration

## Overview
This was an exploratory partnership conversation between Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan founder) and Tim He (Twill co-founder/operator) to explore synergies between their talent-focused platforms. The discussion centered on Careerspan's job search and candidate matching capabilities and how Twill might leverage them as a customer or strategic partner.

## Context & Opening
Tim acknowledged a delay in responding to Vrijen's outreach, citing typical startup operational demands. Vrijen reciprocated by expressing admiration for Tim's strategic approach to recruiting and Twill's positioning, noting how observing successful operators has been educational for his own work.

## Careerspan's Current State & Direction

### Go-to-Market Evolution
Careerspan initially positioned itself around behavioral insights and candidate data, but customer feedback revealed the true unmet need: **community-based talent sourcing and rapid access to candidate pools**. The business model has shifted from a technology-first narrative to a community-first approach.

### Business Model
- **Revenue approach**: Flat subscription model rather than per-name pricing (Tim validated this preference)
- **Target customers**: Companies seeking recurring access to curated talent pools
- **Core value proposition**: Auto-shortlist service—search the database, get pre-qualified candidates within minutes, Careerspan handles introductions
- **Differentiation**: High-engagement candidates from specific communities (Lenny's Product Community, Future of Higher Education alumni, etc.) with 80-90% email open rates and 12-16% click-through rates on job distributions

### Current Metrics
- **Active users**: ~400 at any given time (8-month trajectory)
- **Average engagement cycle**: 3 months per user
- **Repeat usage**: Users who complete one "vibe check" go on to do 3-4+ on average; power users reaching 10-20+
- **Total addressable base**: ~800-900 people with sufficient Careerspan engagement
- **Community progress**: Already embedded in FOHI and alumni associations; working to break into Lenny's Product Community

### Growth Strategy
Over the past 3 weeks, the team focused on product closure (completing promised community features). Near-term priorities:
- Secure exclusive/proprietary jobs for Careerspan
- Augment supply with purchased job listings
- Target onboarding 1-2 new communities per week
- Leverage consumer-facing design for easy deployment (community link sharing)

## Careerspan's Competitive Positioning

Vrijen articulated a nuanced view of the market:
- The ATS isn't actually the problem—matching algorithms won't be a sustainable differentiator anytime soon
- **Real moat**: Building and scaling community relationships and supply partnerships
- **Customer focus**: Recruiters, talent leaders, and hiring teams willing to pay for access to talent pipelines (both organic and supplemented)
- **Strategic shift (as of this week)**: Logan (team member) focusing exclusively on community onboarding; Vrijen shifting to revenue generation and customer partnerships

## Twill Relevance & Potential Integration

Tim explored a hypothetical use case: finding candidates for a company job posting using Careerspan's search (e.g., "founding engineers in SF"), then reaching out with anonymized profiles. Vrijen refined this to clarify:
- Careerspan handles matching, intros, and opt-in management (users control whether they're searchable)
- Flat subscription removes friction from multiple searches/outreach campaigns
- Founders still face acute talent-finding challenges despite abundant recruitment tools

Tim identified two communities loosely connected to him that could be candidates for Careerspan integration, signaling interest in supporting the platform.

## Market Context & Philosophy

Both founders share a critique of the current hiring system's brokenness:
- ATSs have made sourcing harder, not easier
- Referrals work but consume time and energy from potential advocates
- Twill's solution (structured referrals) addresses referral process friction
- Careerspan's solution (community-based sourcing) addresses talent discovery at scale

Vrijen highlighted the emerging risk: companies will increasingly **fake pro-candidate signals** as backlash mounts, making authentic approaches (like Twill's loyalty incentives and Careerspan's community partnerships) increasingly valuable.

## Organizational Structure & Logistics
- **Vrijen**: Based in Brooklyn; responsible for strategy, positioning, and revenue generation
- **Logan**: Based in Boston; leading community onboarding
- **Rest of team**: Mostly distributed across Europe
- **Fundraising timeline**: Targeting seed round in January; actively gathering proof points

## Key Impressions & Tone
Tim expressed genuine enthusiasm about Careerspan's vision, calling it a "pretty sexy company" and stating he's now "personally invested" in its success. The conversation maintained a collaborative tone rather than competitive, with both founders exploring ways to support each other's missions.
