# Follow-up Conversation Strategy: Twill Partnership

## Immediate Follow-up (48-72 Hours)

### Message to Tim
**Tone**: Warm, informal, grateful; acknowledge his busy schedule; offer low-friction next steps

**Suggested Content**:
- Thank you for the time + enthusiasm; genuinely appreciated the strategic thinking
- "We're moving fast on a few fronts and wanted to share what we're working on"
- Send 1-2 sample JDs with matching results as concrete proof-of-concept
- Brief paragraph on how we're thinking about community splits + revenue (show that we've thought through his concern)
- **Specific Ask**: "Would love to get those community intros from you when you have bandwidth. No rush—we're moving into planning mode for the next 6 weeks anyway."

**Channel**: Email (acknowledge his email backlog with a light touch; keep message to 2-3 short paragraphs)

**Timing**: Send within 48 hours while conversation is fresh; gives him time to respond before his next crunch

## Phased Conversation Sequence

### **Phase 1: Product Proof (Week 1-2)**
**Owner**: Vrijen

**Objective**: Demonstrate Careerspan's matching capability with Twill's potential use cases

**Deliverable**:
- Email: Sample JDs + results (2 roles: one tech-heavy like "founding engineer," one ops-focused)
- Include: # of candidates, quality signals, how behavioral data improves matches vs. resume keywords
- CTAs: (1) "Does this match your thinking?" (2) "Know anyone hiring we could test against?"

**Expected Response**: Tim likely to either:
- Confirm fit for Twill customers or
- Ask clarifying questions about implementation/pricing

**Next Step Trigger**: If positive signal, move to Phase 2 (customer pilot proposal)

---

### **Phase 2: Community Introductions (Week 2-3)**
**Owner**: Logan (with Vrijen support)

**Objective**: Gather specific community names from Tim; begin outreach preparation

**Conversation Starter** (from Vrijen to Tim):
- "Logan is leading our community partnerships and would love to chat about those two communities you mentioned"
- "We've been thinking about how to structure community participation (revenue splits, how many jobs/month, etc.) — would be great to have her talk through framework"

**Logan's Prep**:
- Community onboarding package ready (benefits to community, to members, revenue split models)
- 2-3 example communities to reference (show we understand community dynamics)
- Questions: What are the communities? How many members? What are their hiring pain points?

**Expected Outcome**: 
- Get community names + soft intro
- Tentative interest from communities to pilot

**Timeline**: Schedule call with Logan + Tim for Week 2-3

---

### **Phase 3: Customer Pilot Discussion (Week 3-4)**
**Owner**: Vrijen (with Tim's potential facilitation)

**Objective**: Move from partnership interest to concrete customer pilots

**Setup**:
- Send Twill customer pilot proposal (1-pager: pricing, how it works, timeline to first results)
- "Would love your perspective on whether this would work for a customer you know"
- Tim can either: (a) introduce specific customer, (b) give feedback on positioning, (c) both

**Structure for Tim Introduction** (if he offers):
- Tim makes warm intro to customer founder/recruiter
- Vrijen takes first conversation
- Positioning: "Tim recommended we reach out because he thinks this could help with your [specific hiring need]"

**Timeline**: Week 3-4; concurrent with community conversations

---

### **Phase 4: Strategic Alignment Check (Week 4-5)**
**Owner**: Vrijen

**Objective**: Confirm partnership direction; clarify competitive positioning; discuss long-term collaboration

**Topics**:
1. **Competitive Positioning**: How Careerspan + Twill positioning in market (both pro-candidate, but different layers)
2. **Revenue Model Alignment**: Confirm community splits make sense for Twill partnerships
3. **Tim's Public Support**: If interested, discuss content collaboration, testimonial, public positioning
4. **Seed Narrative**: Brief mention that partnerships validate GTM (if appropriate in conversation flow)

**Format**: 30-min call or email exchange (based on Tim's preference/bandwidth)

**Success Metrics**: 
- Cleared positioning concerns
- 1-2 customer pilots in motion OR community intros progressing
- Tim clear on how to support (publicly, via intros, via communities)

## Communication Template & Tone

### Tim's Communication Style to Mirror
- **Strategic but warm**: He uses humor ("I gotta remember to reply in the morning... morning happened..."); appreciate that
- **Direct but collegial**: Asks clarifying questions; not trying to sell; wants to understand value genuinely
- **Busy but interested**: Always responsive when it matters; respect his time
- **Community-minded**: Values relationships over transactional value; emphasize partnership not just business

### Vrijen's Communication Approach
1. **Keep messages short and valuable** (respect his time)
2. **Use specific examples** (not abstract promises; show sample JDs, actual metrics)
3. **Make asks explicit but low-pressure** ("would love your perspective when you have time")
4. **Highlight mutual benefit** ("this helps Careerspan AND gives Twill customers better sourcing")
5. **Thank him for thinking differently** about recruiting (genuinely; he's rare in his perspective)

## Contingency Plans

### If Tim Goes Silent
- **Week 1**: Don't follow up immediately (give him 7-10 days)
- **Week 2**: Send brief, non-pushy follow-up: "Want to make sure the sample JDs landed—no rush, just checking"
- **Week 3+**: Move focus to other partnerships; stay on Tim's radar for future opportunities

### If Tim Says "Not Sure If This Works for Twill"
- **Response**: "Totally understand—no pressure. Would you still be open to connecting Logan with those communities? Or we can stay in touch and explore other ways to collaborate."
- **Pivot**: Preserve relationship for future (Tim's positioning/network is valuable regardless)

### If Communities Show Interest But Tim Doesn't Actively Support
- **Response**: Work with communities directly (they don't need Tim's blessing; he just made intro)
- **Keep Tim Updated**: "Thanks for the intros—really valuable. Community conversations are moving well; wanted to keep you in the loop"
- **Maintain Relationship**: Tim's support is optional; communities are the goal

---

## Key Messages to Reinforce Across All Follow-ups

1. **"We're not trying to replace recruiting"**: We're augmenting it with fresh talent access + community trust
2. **"Community-first model is differentiated"**: Candidates opt-in; behavioral data > resume keywords; faster than ATS
3. **"$2k/month subscription = clean economics for everyone"**: Twill, communities, candidates, companies all aligned
4. **"Tim's perspective matters"**: He's one of the few people actually fixing the broken referral process; his input shapes our direction
5. **"Partnership = mutual credibility"**: For Careerspan seed narrative; for Twill's positioning as pro-candidate platform
