## Key Decision Factors

### Information Governance Principles Agreed Upon
1. **Communication over Knowledge Bases:** "Communication can often be cheaper than adding another knowledge base" - deprecated knowledge bases become cost centers
2. **Master Ledger Concept:** Single source of truth for organizational information with principled governance
3. **Data Quality vs. Quantity:** Focus on what information actually serves decision-making, not data hoarding
4. **Human Dignity:** Information systems should enhance cognition, not create cognitive laziness
5. **Vocabulary Alignment:** Shared terminology across teams reduces friction ("team vocabulary" concept)

### Why Bram Specifically
- Founder-level thinking about system architecture
- Deep knowledge of personal library science
- Experience with YCombinator network (relevant to Zo's market positioning)
- Philosophical alignment on information as enabling human capability vs. data collection for its own sake
- Track record of translating complex concepts into implementable systems

## Implications for Careerspan/Zo Strategy

**Zo Product Angle:** Positioning Zo as "personal AI computer" requires solving information governance at its core. Bram's consultation elevates this from "AI chat tool" to "cognitive operating system."

**Internal Process Change:** Moving from ad-hoc knowledge management (scattered Google Docs, deprecated projects) to principled, governed information architecture.

**Team Alignment:** Ilsa's frustrations with information chaos (searching for "CareerX" returning 15 dead projects) should be addressable through proper governance, not additional tools.
