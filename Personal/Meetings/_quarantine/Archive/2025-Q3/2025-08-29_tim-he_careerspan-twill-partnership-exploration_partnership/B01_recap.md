The conversation centered on Careerspan's evolved go-to-market strategy, business metrics, and potential collaboration models with Twill to solve mutual challenges in the recruiting marketplace space.

## Careerspan Business Update

### GTM Pivot (Critical Strategic Shift)

Vrijen explained that Careerspan has pivoted from its original positioning around "behavioral insights and candidate data" to a more market-responsive positioning around **community-driven talent sourcing**.

**What Changed:** Customer feedback revealed that the primary unmet need isn't data; it's the ability to:
1. Cultivate talent over time (community building)
2. Quickly access talent when needed (on-demand sourcing)

**How They're Solving It:** Embedding in existing communities (Lenny's Product Community, Future of Higher Ed, alumni associations, etc.) and allowing community leaders to:
- Give members a great job search tool (candidate-facing value)
- Surface members to employers (monetization)
- Gain visibility into community talent at scale

### Business Metrics & Engagement

- **User Base:** ~400 active users (average 3-month lifecycle), 800-900 cumulative who've sufficiently engaged to be matchable
- **Engagement Pattern:** Users who do one "vibe check" for a job typically do 3-4 more; power users do 10-20+
- **Email Performance:** Achieved 80-90% open rates and 12-16% click-through rates on job distributions—far above industry norms—because Careerspan carefully matches jobs to candidates
- **Growth Trajectory:** Targeting onboarding 1-2 communities per week

### Product Positioning

Careerspan is positioning itself as an **automated shortlist service**: give them a job description, they scan their database within minutes and return high-quality candidates. No waiting, high signal.

The value prop to community leaders: "You get to give your folks a great career tool AND you get to split monetization when people get placed from your community."

## Revenue Model Discussion

**Question:** Should they charge based on:
- Number of names revealed/introduced, OR
- Flat subscription (search as many times as you want)

**Tim's Input:** "Flat subscription makes sense because then I don't have to do as much math comparing value each time I get a name."

**Vrijen's Preference:** Flat subscription (aligns with their desire for predictable MRR vs. "patchy" hiring-dependent income)

**Why This Matters:** Reliably recurring revenue signals traction and sustainability to investors pre-seed round (targeting January 2025).

## Careerspan's Strategic Thesis

Vrijen articulated a nuanced view of the recruiting market:

**The Problem:** Founders don't look at inbound anymore—only referrals. But referrals are too slow ("drops of water"). Inbound is too noisy ("fire hose"). There's no middle ground that's both fast AND high-signal.

**The Careerspan Thesis:** Position themselves on the "continuum" between inbound and referrals—high-signal sourcing that's faster than traditional referral-based recruiting but more curated than inbound. "Semi-warm" leads that are pre-qualified by community membership.

**Why It Matters for Fundraising:** This isn't just another resume database; it's solving a real founder pain point with a defensible approach (community leverage, data, matching algorithm).

## Twill Context (Tim's Perspective)

Tim leads operations/partnerships at Twill, a recruiting marketplace built on member referrals. Key challenges:

1. **Premium Pricing + Subscription Model:** Twill charges both upfront subscription + placement fees, making company acquisition harder than competitors who only take placement fees
2. **Member Fatigue:** Members see the same roles repeatedly and grow fatigued. Referral pipeline dries up when there isn't variety
3. **Company Acquisition Problem:** Twill's limiting factor is getting companies to buy in, not member supply

**Branding Constraint:** Twill's messaging emphasizes that placements are "member-referred," so they can't publicly attribute candidates to external sourcing without messaging confusion

## Potential Partnership Models Discussed

### Model 1: Careerspan as "Sourcer of Last Resort"

- Twill gets sample JDs from companies
- Careerspan provides candidates when Twill members can't fill the role
- Publicly framed as member referrals (no public attribution to Careerspan)
- Helps Twill fill roles without disrupting member-first narrative

### Model 2: Careerspan-Powered Org Structure

- Create a private Careerspan org within Careerspan platform
- Only Twill-invited members can access/contribute
- Twill distributes jobs via Careerspan's matching algorithm
- Can set signal threshold (e.g., 70-80+) for job matching
- Candidates apply through Careerspan's link (keeps relationship manageable)

### Model 3: Tim as High-Velocity Member

- Tim becomes a "member" on Careerspan ($2,000/month subscription)
- Gets unlimited monthly scans of Careerspan's database
- Acts like an internal sourcer for Twill, but powered by Careerspan's matching
- Anonymized profiles, but Careerspan controls whether candidates engage
- Gives Careerspan "proof point" of successful matching for messaging/sales

### Model 4: Co-Hosted Events

- Partner to host "match fests" bringing founders + candidate community members together
- Use Careerspan's system to pre-match founders with candidates
- Helps both companies solve the "company acquisition" problem

## Pricing Discussion

Vrijen proposed **$2,000/month** for ongoing Careerspan sourcing access to Twill.

**Tim's Response:** "Let me write that down. I'll pass it along to our recruiter" + comparison to internal sourcer costs

**Why the 3-Month Limit:** Careerspan's matching algorithm is "compute expensive" and they're still optimizing unit economics.
