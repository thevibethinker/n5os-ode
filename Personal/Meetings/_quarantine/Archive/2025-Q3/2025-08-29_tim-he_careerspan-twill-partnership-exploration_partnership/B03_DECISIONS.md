# Decisions & Strategic Agreements

## Decision 1: Subscription-Based Revenue Model (Careerspan)

**Decision**: Adopt flat subscription pricing model rather than per-name pricing  
**Made By**: Vrijen (Careerspan) with Tim's input  
**Context**: Discussion of how to charge companies for candidate access  

### Rationale
- **Tim's perspective** (decisive): "I think flat subscription makes sense because then I don't have to do as much math of like comparing value each time I get a name."
- **Vrijen's preference alignment**: "Yeah, no, that makes sense. That's what I've been sort of biased towards as well."
- **Business benefit for Careerspan**: "Nice clean for us. I think just being able to say that we have MRR instead of like patchy ass...recruiting income is ideal."

### Implications
- Simpler customer value calculation
- More predictable revenue (MRR-focused)
- Removes friction from customer decision-making on each candidate reveal
- Encourages higher usage (unlimited searches/contacts within subscription)

**Status**: Adopted as pricing strategy  
**Confidence Level**: High (explicit mutual agreement)

## Decision 3: Community Kickback Alignment (Partnership Philosophy)

**Decision**: Careerspan will offer community revenue-sharing on placements made through community members  
**Made By**: Vrijen (Careerspan)  
**Context**: Discussion of how to compensate community leaders/operators  

### Rationale from Vrijen
- "We want to split the take with communities as well, so it's all upside."
- Aligns with Twill's successful community member incentive model
- Builds loyalty and community engagement
- Addresses community leader concerns about commercializing their networks

### Tim's Perspective (Validation)
- "That's been a big incentive for us. Like giving the members a kickback."
- Acknowledged trade-off: "It's taking away from us, which isn't ideal, but it's like good for the members."
- Confirmed effectiveness: "I've only heard your members sort of say super positive things."

### Strategic Implication
- Differentiates Careerspan as "pro-candidate" and "pro-community"
- Acknowledges that community leaders create value and deserve share
- Contrasts with competitor approaches that don't share economics

**Status**: Committed philosophy; specific terms TBD per community  
**Confidence Level**: High (explicit commitment from Vrijen)  
**Consideration**: Margin implications; sustainable at scale only if conversion rates remain strong

## Decision 6: Product Positioning - Community-First Over Technology-First

**Decision**: Pivot from technology-first positioning (behavioral insights, data) to community-first positioning (network effects, curated access)  
**Made By**: Careerspan (Vrijen explicitly stated reasoning)  
**Context**: Discussion of market differentiation and moat building  

### Rationale
- **Market reality**: Matching systems commoditizing quickly; not a sustainable differentiator
- **Strategic pivot**: "aggressively focus on grabbing communities and partner with or supply to or have customers as people that want to be able to like get those vast swaths of talent in"
- **Philosophy**: Building moat through exclusive community relationships, not technology superiority

### Implications
- De-emphasizes technical competitive advantage
- Focuses on community relationships and speed
- Aligns with marketplace dynamics (supply-side liquidity matters more than algorithm)
- Reduces need for large R&D investment

**Status**: Active strategy as of meeting  
**Confidence Level**: High (explicit and deliberate)

## Decision 7: Distribution Model - Easy, Consumer-First Onboarding

**Decision**: Maintain consumer-facing product design for easy community integration (link-sharing, code-based signup)  
**Made By**: Careerspan (product/go-to-market decision)  
**Context**: Discussion of community onboarding pace and scalability  

### Mechanism
- "Share this specific link with your community"
- "If you're an existing member, add this code and boom, you're part of that community"
- Gets members "all of the perks that we would be giving to folks"

### Implications
- Reduces sales/implementation friction
- Enables rapid community onboarding (1-2 per week goal)
- Aligns product design with go-to-market strategy
- Creates network effects (easier to join → more members → more valuable → more join)

**Status**: Actively implemented  
**Confidence Level**: High (confirmed as current practice)
