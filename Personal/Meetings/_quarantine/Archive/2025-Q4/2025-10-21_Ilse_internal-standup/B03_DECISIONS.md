# Decisions: Strategic Pivot to Acquisition-Focused Mode

## Core Strategic Decision: Shift to Acquisition Path

**Decision:** Careerspan will pivot from a traditional VC-backed growth model to an acquisition-focused strategy with significantly reduced burn rate.

- **Rationale:** After extensive conversations with VCs and investors, the clear recommendation is to "stay alive, go into low power mode." The company has strong product-market fit signals (excellent retention and reactivation metrics) and doesn't need to build a massive independent business to create value.
- **Timeline:** Target acquisition within 12 months, with team coverage through the process.

## Financial Model Decision: Runway Extension Through Reduced Spend

**Decision:** Restructure financial commitments to extend runway to approximately 6 months for founders/core team, with 3 months guaranteed for full team.

- **Team Coverage:** All team members covered for 3 months of salary
- **Founder/Core Coverage:** Vrijen, Ilse, and Logan covered for ~6 months minimum
- **Capital Allocation:** Focus on infrastructure costs (API tokens, hosting) rather than hiring or marketing
- **Contingency:** Option to accelerate team member vesting (100% vesting immediately) if acquisition occurs within the year

## Monetization Strategy Decision: Multiple Revenue Streams, Not VC-Backed Growth

**Decision:** Pursue "money through any means" approach rather than building a single VC-backable business model.

- **Primary: Recruitment/Placement Revenue** - Continue headhunting/bounty model (filling recruiter roles)
- **Secondary: VC Subscription Model** - VCs paying subscription for access to pre-vetted talent pool
- **Distribution: Decentralized Talent Network** - Community-focused approach with JD distribution and manual outreach channels
- **Position:** Remain candidate-centric rather than obligated to serve employers exclusively

## Product Pivot Decision: Shift Focus Toward Candidate Experience & Scale

**Decision:** Reduce product's analytical rigor demands in favor of scalability and ease of use.

- **Scope Reduction:** Move from comprehensive analysis to "vibe check" level screening that answers "is this person worth talking to?"
- **Technical Constraint Acknowledgment:** Current API methodology (30-50 cent per candidate with full analysis) is economically unviable at scale
- **Scaling Challenge:** Processing 2,000 users against 50 jobs currently takes 18-22+ hours and hits OpenAI API rate limits
- **Pending Clarification:** Ilse raised concerns about this pivot away from high-rigor product; requested follow-up discussion before team communication

## Go-to-Market Decision: Brand Building Over Direct Sales

**Decision:** Reallocate resources from traditional sales to organic brand building and community embedding.

- **Logan's Focus:** Brand building, social media, grassroots community engagement (approach that created user spikes earlier in the year)
- **Strategy:** Embed with communities and candidate networks rather than negotiate or "wrangle about money"
- **Hiring Manager Leverage:** Recognize hiring managers as primary network advantage and distribution channel
- **Pro-Candidate Positioning:** Open access to jobs on Careerspan, leveraging organic connections and network effects

## Employer Portal Decision: Technical Build for Distribution Efficiency

**Decision:** Build employer portal to enable scalable job distribution and candidate review workflow.

- **Scope:** Allow employers to submit jobs, distribute magic links, and review interested candidates
- **Timeline:** Prioritize this build for upcoming sprint work
- **Note:** Ilse had concerns this conflicts with earlier discussions about candidate-focused positioning; awaiting three-way discussion with Logan

## Next Steps & Contingent Decisions

**Decision:** Hold three-way strategy discussion (Vrijen, Ilse, Logan) before communicating pivot to full team.

- **Rationale:** Align on product direction and resource allocation before team-wide announcement
- **Scope:** Address technical constraints, product strategy conflicts, and ensure buy-in from all leads
