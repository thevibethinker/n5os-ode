# B03 – Stakeholder Intelligence

## Overview

Participants:
- **V** – Founder of Careerspan; selling / partnering, but positioning himself as an advisor and thought partner.
- **Henri Bugnet** – Co‑founder; product / technical founder with Google background, now based in Chile.
- **Ceren Koca** – Co‑founder; product and hiring owner, focused on process, candidate experience, and quality of hires.
- **Ilsa (off‑call)** – Head of AI at Careerspan; expert on rubric design and analysis, referenced as execution owner for configuration tweaks.
- **Alexi (off‑call)** – Mutual friend / trust bridge between V and the founders.

---

## V (Careerspan)

- **Role & power**
  - Founder of Careerspan; primary decision‑maker over product configuration, process design, and go‑to‑market.
  - Controls access to Careerspan, rubric configuration, and support in distribution through his communities.

- **Core interests**
  - Demonstrate that Careerspan is **materially better than ATS + ad hoc interviews** for identifying high‑agency, product‑minded builders.
  - Build a deep, credible partnership with Henri and Ceren that can become a reference case.
  - Protect Careerspan’s brand as a **high‑signal, humane assessment tool**, not just another screening gate.

- **Motivations / values**
  - Strong bias toward **candidate advocacy**: wants tools that help people represent themselves accurately, not game the system.
  - Values **honesty and nuance**: pushes back gently on over‑reliance on psychometric tests and simplistic filtering.
  - Enjoys founder‑to‑founder conversations and is mindful of their emotional context (e.g., previous founder drama).

- **Concerns / anxieties**
  - That friction might be perceived as too high and scare off high‑quality candidates, especially at the compensation level the founders can offer.
  - That the team might underutilize Careerspan (e.g., by only using resume‑only mode) and therefore undervalue its distinctive benefits.

- **Leverage points**
  - Trust from Alexi and prior positive interactions with Ceren.
  - Ability to **tune friction** (story thresholds) and **adjust rubric semantics** to directly address their concerns.
  - Access to communities and candidate pools that can meaningfully increase their inbound.

- **Collaboration style**
  - Warm, explanatory, and story‑driven.
  - Offers concrete next steps and safety valves (“set stories to zero,” “we’ll do the legwork,” “we can tweak the rubric”).

---

## Henri Bugnet

- **Role & responsibilities**
  - Co‑founder and key decision‑maker on the technical / product side.
  - Deeply involved in defining the builder profile and ensuring hires match the company’s way of working.

- **Stated priorities**
  - **Quality over quantity**: would prefer to interview at most ~3 candidates if possible.
  - Avoid being overwhelmed by inbound applications; they have not opened public postings because they cannot filter effectively.
  - Ensure that any hired builder has **true ownership, responsibility, and product thinking**, not just task execution.

- **Concerns**
  - **Faking and AI‑generated responses**: worried candidates might read the tech stack in the JD and fabricate matching stories, possibly using generative AI.
  - Misclassification in rubrics (e.g., cross‑functional collaboration listed as a hard skill).
  - **Gen Z / low‑ownership patterns**: worried about low responsibility and drop‑off tendencies.
  - Friction vs. yield: whether the process will scare away strong candidates before a human interview.

- **Behavioral signals**
  - Asks detailed, scenario‑based questions (e.g., “What if they just mimic the stack?”).
  - Open to nuanced answers; willing to adopt tooling if he trusts the underlying logic.
  - Pragmatic about trade‑offs (e.g., sees that if they cannot filter, they will not open inbound at all).

- **Leverage points**
  - Responds well to **clear mechanisms** (three‑layer BS detector, semantic checks, interview cross‑validation).
  - Values **data‑backed assurances** (Aspire Institute pilot, examples like Donna/Donatella).
  - Motivated by the prospect of **small, high‑confidence shortlists** that save him time.

---

## Ceren Koca

- **Role & responsibilities**
  - Co‑founder leading much of the **hiring and process design**.
  - Primary owner of the JD, rubric, and candidate experience decisions.

- **Core questions**
  - **Process flow**: wants an explicit picture of how Careerspan integrates into their pipeline as a first screener.
  - **Sourcing**: how candidates will be found and funneled into Careerspan; worried about situations where they only have a handful of candidates.
  - **Candidate experience and friction**: wants to avoid losing good people who refuse to jump through too many hoops.

- **Sensitivity & style**
  - Detail‑oriented; notices classification issues (“hard skill vs soft skill”).
  - Concerned with **fairness and usability**: wants the screening to be rigorous but not punitive or unnecessarily heavy.
  - Comfortable challenging assumptions politely (“not to be nitpicky, but…”), which is useful for iterative rubric refinement.

- **Leverage points**
  - Likely to respond positively to **transparent configuration controls** (importance / expertise sliders, category toggles).
  - Will appreciate **visibility into drop‑off and funnel metrics** once the role runs for a while.
  - Values examples and concrete stories; demo accounts and real candidate case studies resonated.

## Relationship dynamics & power map

- **Power**
  - Henri and Ceren hold the purchasing decision and define success for the hire.
  - V holds power over the design and flexibility of the assessment environment and over candidate distribution via his networks.

- **Trust & tone**
  - The call is high‑trust and founder‑to‑founder; there is prior rapport through Saren and Alexi.
  - V’s candid language (e.g., about ATS, psychological tests) lands well; they respond with laughter and continued engagement.

- **Strategic implications**
  - If Careerspan delivers a strong first experience, Henri and Ceren could become **flagship users and advocates** for builder‑hiring use cases.
  - Ongoing collaboration will likely involve:
    - Periodic rubric tuning with Ceren.
    - Sharing interview transcripts back with Careerspan.
    - Joint experiments on story thresholds and candidate friction.
