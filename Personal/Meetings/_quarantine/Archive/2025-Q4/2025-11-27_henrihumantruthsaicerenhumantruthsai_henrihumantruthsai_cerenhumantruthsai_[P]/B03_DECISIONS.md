# B03 – Decisions

## Confirmed decisions

1. **Careerspan as first‑line screener**
   - Henri and Ceren agree in principle that Careerspan will be used as a **first screening layer** for their builder hire, rather than opening unfiltered inbound.

2. **Rubric as shared source of truth**
   - The **role breakdown in Careerspan** (responsibilities, soft skills, later hard skills) will be the canonical representation of what they are hiring for, rather than keeping a separate, static JD.

3. **Use of behavioral, story‑based signals**
   - They will rely on **stories + resume** (not just resumes) to assess candidates’ self‑management, agency, and product thinking, at least for part of the funnel.

4. **Interview transcript feedback loop**
   - If they proceed with Careerspan, **interview transcripts will be fed back** into the system so Careerspan can cross‑check consistency between stories and live interviews.

## Conditional / contingent decisions

1. **Publish vs. hold for rubric tweaks**
   - **If** the current role breakdown feels broadly accurate:
     - Ceren and Henri will **publish the role directly** in Careerspan and begin sending candidates to it.
   - **If** they find significant misalignment (missing skills, bad categorization, major level mismatches):
     - They will **not publish immediately** and will instead ask V to have **Ilsa** adjust the configuration by Monday.

2. **Story threshold (friction level)**
   - **Baseline decision**: start with the current recommended behavior (1–3 stories) to benefit from richer signals.
   - **Fallback decision**: if candidate drop‑off looks problematic or friction feels too high, they will **lower the story threshold to zero** and rely on semantic resume analysis as a first pass.

3. **Use of global sourcing via Careerspan communities**
   - V will post about the opportunity in communities where Careerspan already has presence.
   - Henri and Ceren will treat these communities as **primary early‑stage sourcing channels**, especially for Global South talent.

## Open questions (to be resolved post‑call)

1. **Final rubric boundary decisions**
   - Exactly which attributes are considered **critical vs. important**.
   - Final categorization of ambiguous skills (e.g., cross‑functional collaboration as soft vs. hard skill).

2. **Target friction level**
   - Where to set the long‑term **minimum story requirement** once they have initial data on candidate behavior and drop‑off.

3. **Success metrics**
   - Explicit quantitative targets (e.g., number of candidates entering the funnel, number of interviews run, offer‑to‑interview ratio) were not set on the call and should be defined later.
