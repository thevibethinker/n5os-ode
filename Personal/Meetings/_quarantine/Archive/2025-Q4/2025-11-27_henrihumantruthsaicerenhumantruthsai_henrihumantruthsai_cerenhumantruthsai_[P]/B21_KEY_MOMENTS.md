# B21 – Key Moments

## 1. Framing the need: “You just need a builder”

- V connects Henri’s written JD, his prior call with Ceren, and feedback from Careerspan’s head of AI into a single conclusion: **they need a scrappy builder to frame the product**, not another pure researcher.
- This aligns expectations and clarifies what success for this hire looks like.

## 2. Ceren’s three questions

- Ceren articulates the core things she needs to understand:
  1. How Careerspan fits into their **process flow** as a first screener.
  2. How they will **find candidates** (given low capacity and possibly few total applicants).
  3. What **candidates actually experience** using Careerspan.
- This moment reframes the call around concrete operational questions rather than abstract features.

## 3. “Inbound is dead” and the case against resume‑only funnels

- V’s claim that **inbound is effectively dead / overwhelming** for founders, combined with the observation that resumes are low‑trust, sets up Careerspan as a necessary evolution, not a “nice‑to‑have”.

## 4. Story‑based assessment demo

- V walks through a real candidate example (Donna / Donatella) to show how **stories change the signal**:
  - Resume alone would have filtered her out as a weak match.
  - Stories reveal alignment and potential that are invisible to keyword scans.
- This concretely illustrates the value of asking candidates for reflective narratives.

## 5. Three‑layer “bullshit detector” explanation

- Henri’s concern about candidates gaming the stack leads to V outlining:
  1. A stylistic/semantic **BS detector**.
  2. The **open‑ended reflection structure** that makes faking harder.
  3. **Interview transcript cross‑checks**.
- This directly addresses a key blocker to trusting AI‑mediated assessments.

## 6. Gen Z / ownership concern and self‑selection

- Henri raises worries about low responsibility and follow‑through.
- V reframes Careerspan as a **self‑selection mechanism**: the act of completing reflective stories is itself a filter for agency and diligence.
- This shifts the tool from “extra hoop” to a way to **screen for the exact behavior they care about**.

## 7. Publish vs. hold decision path

- V proposes a clear fork:
  - If the rubric looks broadly right → **publish and start using it**.
  - If it needs more work → **don’t publish**, send feedback, and let **Ilsa** adjust it by Monday.
- This reduces fear of committing to a mis‑configured assessment and clarifies next steps.

## 8. Commitment to distribution support

- V offers to **post the role in communities** where Careerspan is already present and route interest back to Henri and Ceren.
- This expands the scope of collaboration from tooling to **sourcing + filtering**, increasing potential impact.

## 9. Closing on shared ethos

- The call ends with banter about Thanksgiving and working through holidays.
- V’s line about “disrespecting all holidays equally by working on them” and the group’s laughter signals a **shared work ethic and cultural fit**, which supports a deeper, longer‑term partnership.
