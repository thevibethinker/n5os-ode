# Strategic Decisions Made

## Primary Business Decision: Shift from Fundraising to Growth-Focused Acquisition Strategy

**Decision:** Pivot monetization and exit strategy from venture fundraising to organic user growth targeting acquisition by larger HR/recruiting platforms.

**Context & Rationale:**
- V stated: "We're not actually even trying to raise money necessarily. We're just trying to say, hey, can we bring more and more users in?"
- Reasoning: Careerspan has demonstrated strong product-market fit metrics (D30 retention ~20%, 6-month retention ~5-6%, 1,000+ hours of coaching recorded, 3,000 signups)
- Benchmarking: UK competitors (Dex, Jack and Jill) are better capitalized and have founder credibility in HR recruiting space, but Careerspan has superior technology
- Recognition: Traditional HR sales/recruiting credibility approach hasn't yielded expected results; redirecting to roots-based distribution

**Strategic Implication:**
Instead of competing on HR credentials, acquire users through community partnerships and alumni networks, making Careerspan an acquisition target for larger platforms seeking US market entry or expansion.

## Secondary Decision: Community-Based Distribution as Primary Growth Channel

**Decision:** Prioritize relationships with alumni associations and community organizations as primary user acquisition mechanism.

**Examples Cited:**
- Emory alumni association already committed to distributing Careerspan to entire alumni base (tens of thousands)
- Cornell Johnson community positioned as next major expansion target
- CMC (Cornell Career Management Center) integration under consideration

**Rationale:**
- Community distribution aligns with Careerspan's core philosophy (careers as ongoing, community-supported process)
- Addresses monetization constraint: offer premium free product to community partners rather than charging individual job seekers
- Scalable: works across multiple universities and alumni networks simultaneously

## Decision in Process: CMC Integration at Cornell

**Status:** Under consideration/exploration phase

**Action Items:**
- Gather student survey feedback on current CMC tools (specifically vMock weaknesses)
- Identify value exchange for CMC partnership (e.g., better resume analysis, clearer narrative development)
- Leverage Zoe's position to facilitate introductions to CMC leadership
- Position Careerspan as solution to CMC's resource constraints

**Strategic Value:**
- Institutional adoption creates legitimacy and reduces customer acquisition cost
- Direct pipeline to active job seekers (MBA students in recruiting mode)
- Potential leverage for broader alumni network distribution

## Tertiary Decision: Positioning for International Student Segment

**Decision:** Actively position Careerspan toward international student community, leveraging V's personal story as competitive advantage.

**Context:**
- Zoe highlighted: International student population anxious about visa/H1B sponsorship challenges
- Current cohort diversity lower than prior years
- Market recognizes few credible resources addressing international student job search pain

**Action:** V offered to speak directly to international students/groups about recruitment landscape, visa dynamics, and career strategy from unique dual perspective.

**Rationale:**
- Founder story + product create differentiated value for underserved segment
- Distribution opportunity: international student associations, networks, orientation programs
- Revenue model: free/discounted for students, acquisition target appeal for larger platforms

## Summary of Strategic Positioning

Careerspan is moving from a traditional venture-backed approach (raise, acquire customers through sales, sell to enterprises) toward a network-effects model (grow community-rooted user base, become acquisition target for larger platforms, maintain free/premium model focused on job seeker empowerment rather than B2B SaaS).
