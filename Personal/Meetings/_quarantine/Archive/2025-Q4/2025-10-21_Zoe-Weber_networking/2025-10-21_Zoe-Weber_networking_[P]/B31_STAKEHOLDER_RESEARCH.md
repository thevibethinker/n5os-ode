**Perspective:** Speaking as MBA student, Park Fellow at early-stage VC fund, former consulting analyst (PE/M&A)

### Key Landscape Insights

1. **MBA Cohort Composition and H1B Anxiety**
   - **Observation:** International student percentage is increasing year-over-year; second-year class is "less international" than first-year cohort, but first-years are "6% even more international"
   - **Implication:** International hiring uncertainty is top-of-mind for significant portion of MBA class; H1B/visa concerns are material blockers in recruiting
   - **Strategic Value:** H1B anxiety is unmet need in MBA career services; Vrijen's dual perspective (founder + international hiring experience) is rare and credible positioning
   - **Action Signal:** International student community is addressable, high-need segment within Johnson

2. **CMC Capacity and Current Tooling Failures**
   - **Observation:** CMC requires students to use VMock; VMock feedback is often misleading or counterproductive; CMC staff are overloaded with advising requests
   - **Implication:** CMC has *intent* to help but lacks *capacity* to do it well; VMock is failing them (tool problem, not advisor problem)
   - **Strategic Value:** CMC is a potential ally, not a competitor; they want better solutions. Positioning Careerspan as "CMC enabler" (gives advisors better material to work with) is more viable than positioning as "replacement"
   - **Action Signal:** CMC partnership requires understanding their budget cycles and vendor evaluation process; timing and sponsor matter more than product pitch

3. **MBA Career Transitions and Narrative Gaps**
   - **Observation:** MBA students are doing "so much" in two years and "career pivoting" heavily; current resume-building tools don't capture that evolution
   - **Implication:** MBA is unique use case (high-change environment, many pivots, complex narratives); VMock is designed for 1-2 job transitions, not MBA-scale reinvention
   - **Strategic Value:** MBA is proof-of-concept market for heavy-narrative-users; success here creates halo effect for broader job-seeking population
   - **Action Signal:** Position MBA as pilot segment; early success with MBA will be credible with other audiences

4. **Values-Alignment as Decision Criterion for Gen-Z/MBA Professionals**
   - **Observation:** Zoe actively builds business around "values-aligned brands vs. conglomerates"; appreciated Vrijen's positioning ("not chasing easy money"); multiple references to supporting "smaller brands" and mission-driven companies
   - **Implication:** Next-generation professionals (including MBA students) filter for values alignment; "how you make money" is as important as "what you sell"
   - **Strategic Value:** Careerspan's anti-monetization positioning (not charging job seekers; acquisition target thesis) resonates with this cohort
   - **Action Signal:** Frame Careerspan in values-first terms when speaking to MBA audience (mission clarity, not just product features)

5. **Gorgeous Ventures as Early-Stage Investor Lens**
   - **Observation:** Zoe is Park Fellow at Gorgeous Ventures; fund is "smaller," "started last year"; Zoe is actively building business and evaluating other companies
   - **Implication:** Gorgeous Ventures may be potential future investor or partnership channel; Zoe's evaluation criteria (values, narrative, sustainable model) align with Careerspan positioning
   - **Strategic Value:** Understanding Gorgeous Ventures' investment thesis could reveal adjacent opportunities (women in tech, sustainability, values-driven business models)
   - **Action Signal:** Explore whether Careerspan and Gorgeous Ventures' themes overlap; could be strategic investor introduction path
