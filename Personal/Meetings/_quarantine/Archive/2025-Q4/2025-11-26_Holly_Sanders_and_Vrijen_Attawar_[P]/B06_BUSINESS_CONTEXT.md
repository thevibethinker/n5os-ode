# Business Context

- **Matchbox’s position:**
  - Boutique recruiting/marketplace business focused on early‑stage startups, with an emerging ATS product and a curated database of ~700+ companies and ~600 with open roles.
  - Revenue today is dominated by **placement fees and bounties**, not SaaS subscriptions. A co‑founder breakup has created execution drag and emotional fatigue, but the core founder (Holly) still has strong market credibility.

- **Market dynamics:**
  - ATS incumbents (Ashby, Greenhouse, Workday, etc.) are deeply entrenched despite being widely disliked; data migration and process change make switching tools hard even when buyers are unhappy.
  - Many first‑time founders are **overwhelmed by resume spam** and screening load; second‑time founders often bypass inbound entirely and go straight to recruiters. Both patterns leave a gap for tools that restore signal.
  - AI‑generated resumes and low‑quality outbound make traditional keyword filters increasingly unreliable. Some large players (e.g., Zapier) are already experimenting with **making resumes optional** and building their own AI interviewers.

- **Careerspan’s position:**
  - Careerspan has invested heavily in **story‑based candidate assessment** and an employer portal that surfaces rich, structured narrative data. Its edge is in **evaluating and explaining candidates**, not in being an ATS of record.
  - The business is optimising for **cash‑positive operations and eventual acquisition** rather than chasing large, speculative VC rounds. This aligns it with Matchbox’s own bias toward pragmatic revenue.

- **Mutual opportunity:**
  - Combining **Matchbox’s client relationships and recruiting execution** with **Careerspan’s candidate signal and distribution** offers a credible path to:
    - Close near‑term roles and generate meaningful cash quickly.  
    - Produce concrete, referenceable case studies in a crowded, sceptical hiring market.  
    - Test a SaaS‑adjacent model where employers effectively **"rent" Careerspan links** via Matchbox for a modest monthly fee rather than paying extortionate ATS licenses.

- **Risk factors:**
  - Execution bandwidth on both sides: Holly is effectively rebuilding solo; V and his team are already stretched across product, coaching, and GTM.  
  - Market noise: founders and talent teams are inundated with pitches from AI hiring tools; any joint offer must be extremely clear and outcome‑oriented.  
  - Dependence on a small number of high‑value roles (e.g., DeepTune/Yarn) as early proof points; if those searches stall for reasons outside their control, narrative momentum could lag.
