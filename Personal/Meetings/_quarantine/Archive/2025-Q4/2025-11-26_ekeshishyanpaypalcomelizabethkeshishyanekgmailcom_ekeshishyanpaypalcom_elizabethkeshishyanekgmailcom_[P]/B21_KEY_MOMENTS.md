# Key Moments

## KM1 – Reframing Career Identity After Layoffs
- **Moment:** Elizabeth describes former teammates at Microsoft living in constant fear of losing their jobs, while she initially believed she was a protected “investment.”
- **Quote (paraphrased):**
  - "I learned the hard way that when it comes to businesses optimizing for their profit, people are just…"
  - Her conclusion: do not identify with the company; identify with what she is trying to do and achieve.
- **Why it matters:**
  - Reveals a deep mindset shift that aligns directly with Careerspan’s mission of proactive career capital.
  - Positions Elizabeth as someone who has lived through the problem Careerspan is trying to solve.

## KM2 – V’s Framing of Career Capital and Proactive Strategy
- **Moment:** V responds to Elizabeth’s story by normalizing the impersonal nature of layoffs and reframes the core challenge.
- **Quote (paraphrased):**
  - "How do I make sure that my career capital is locked down, it is ready to go? … Especially as an international, how can I at a moment's notice get myself off the ground and be ready to apply as needed?"
- **Why it matters:**
  - Sharp articulation of Careerspan’s value proposition to internationals and vulnerable workers.
  - This language can be reused in product copy and positioning.

## KM3 – Systems and Rich Dad, Poor Dad
- **Moment:** Elizabeth shares that she has been thinking about "systems" for half of the year, inspired by *Rich Dad, Poor Dad*.
- **Quote (paraphrased):**
  - She wants systems “that work for you and you don't need to be present,” starting with finances but extending to other life domains.
- **Why it matters:**
  - Strong conceptual bridge between personal finance systems and career systems.
  - Reinforces that the right framing for Careerspan and Zo with Elizabeth is *systems*, not just tools.

## KM4 – Introduction of Zo as a Leverage Platform
- **Moment:** V explains Zo as “a server in the cloud that you can prompt,” where a set of markdown files can effectively be a program.
- **Quote (paraphrased):**
  - "All it is, is a bunch of markdown files that are called sequentially. So that's a program now."
- **Why it matters:**
  - Demonstrates how V is already using Zo to build leverage (e.g., the job description builder mini-product).
  - Shows Elizabeth a concrete path to building her own systems without deep infrastructure knowledge.

## KM5 – Clarifying Careerspan’s Platform and ICP
- **Moment:** Elizabeth pushes for clarity: “So who are you targeting as of now? Who's your audience?” and later, “Okay, so you're positioning your product as a two-sided platform.”
- **Why it matters:**
  - Forces articulation of the current ICPs (founders, small HR teams, VCs) and the two-sided nature of Careerspan.
  - Sets up the need for a more deliberate platform strategy, which becomes the central purpose of the follow-up session.

## KM6 – Early Revenue and External Validation
- **Moment:** V notes that they just closed their first paying customer that morning (~$200–300), plus active roles with Zo and another company that has a Gates Foundation grant.
- **Why it matters:**
  - Marks a psychological milestone: the market is starting to pay for the B2B offering.
  - Provides early proof points Elizabeth can work with when thinking about go-to-market and platform strategy.

## KM7 – Proposal for a Structured Strategy Session
- **Moment:** Elizabeth proposes a one-hour joint session where they use 1–2 *Platform Revolution* slides to systematically examine Careerspan’s platform and strategy.
- **Quote (paraphrased):**
  - "Maybe we can go through those two slides, think what the strategy says, and then brainstorm about how we could repurpose those strategies for your business."
- **Why it matters:**
  - Converts a friendly catch-up into a concrete next step with high strategic leverage.
  - Signals Elizabeth’s willingness to invest time and cognitive effort into Careerspan’s success.

## KM8 – Commitment to Collaboration Style
- **Moment:** They discuss how each of them does their best thinking—both converge on working best in collaborative, plan-driven contexts rather than alone.
- **Why it matters:**
  - Validates that investing in well-structured, real-time sessions (versus only async notes) is likely to yield better quality thinking.
  - Helps V design future collaboration formats with Elizabeth and similar stakeholders.
