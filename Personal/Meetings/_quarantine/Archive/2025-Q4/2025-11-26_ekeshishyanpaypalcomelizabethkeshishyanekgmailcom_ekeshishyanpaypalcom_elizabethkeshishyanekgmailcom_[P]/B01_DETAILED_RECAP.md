# Detailed Recap

## Participants
- **Vrijen Attawar** – Founder of Careerspan.
- **Elizabeth Keshishyan** – Product manager at PayPal, recently relocated to Austin, on an H1B visa.

## Context
- Catch-up conversation between V and Elizabeth focused on her transition to PayPal/Austin, career mindset after layoffs, and an update on Careerspan’s current product and go-to-market strategy.
- Call scheduled around U.S. Thanksgiving, with some discussion of holiday timing.

## Chronological Narrative

1. **Warm reconnect and personal setup**
   - Light, friendly opening; V is adjusting a new desk setup and cat space; Elizabeth compliments his glasses.
   - Elizabeth shares that she recently moved to Austin and is settling into a new city, community, and job simultaneously.

2. **Elizabeth’s role and environment at PayPal**
   - She is ~5 months into her role at PayPal and describes the environment as unusually healthy: colleagues are kind, want to see her succeed, and remove blockers when they appear.
   - She contrasts this with prior experiences where teammates at Microsoft were in constant fear of layoffs and job loss.

3. **Mindset after layoffs and H1B realities**
   - Elizabeth reflects on her Microsoft layoff experience:
     - Previously assumed she was a long-term “investment” and thus safe; learned the hard way that companies optimize for profit, not individual security.
     - Reframes her mindset: no longer identifies herself with a company, but with her own long-term goals and capabilities.
   - V normalizes this, emphasizing that at big companies layoff decisions are often algorithmic and impersonal.
   - They align on a core principle: individuals must proactively build “career capital” so they can recover quickly when disruption hits, especially as internationals on work visas.

4. **Career strategy and portfolio thinking**
   - V encourages Elizabeth to think about a **portfolio career**:
     - Side projects and hobbies that double as skill-building.
     - Potential side hustles or small businesses.
     - Emphasis on intrinsic motivation: money vs. joy of the work vs. love of challenge.
   - Elizabeth shares that she’s been thinking about **systems** for about half a year, inspired by *Rich Dad, Poor Dad*: building systems that work for her even when she is not actively present.
   - V suggests leaning into systems and productivity tools, and introduces Zo Computer as a high-leverage platform for non-technical builders.

5. **Introduction to Zo Computer and agentic tools**
   - V explains Zo as a “server in the cloud that you can prompt,” with a file system and a conversational interface.
   - He describes how he built a **job description builder** on Zo using sequentially called markdown files, framing it as a mini-product that previously would have required more technical setup.
   - He positions Zo and similar “agentic” tools as a way for non-technical professionals like Elizabeth to experiment, build systems, and de-risk their careers without having to become full-stack engineers.

6. **Careerspan business update**
   - V shares that Careerspan has been through multiple pivots but that things are starting to look positive.
   - Current state:
     - Consumer-facing career coaching experience still exists and is intended to be free; monetization is shifting away from individual job seekers.
     - The core asset is a **matching engine** that analyzes candidate fit for roles and companies using large language models and semantic understanding rather than keyword-based ATS logic.
   - Product behavior:
     - Employers share a job description and a qualitative description of the ideal candidate.
     - Candidates come in via a link (often via a Notion-hosted job description) and complete “stories” on Careerspan.
     - The system produces rich analytics on how well the candidate fits the *role* and the *company*, surfacing only the top 2–5% for the hiring team.
     - Stories vs. resume view: V shows how the same candidate looks much stronger once their stories are factored in.
   - V frames this as a **two-sided platform**:
     - Side 1: Employers (founders, hiring managers, small HR/talent teams, VCs doing long-term talent cultivation).
     - Side 2: Candidates, who get a better way to “apply” via stories.
   - Early traction:
     - First paying customer closed earlier that day (~$200–300, but symbolically important).
     - ~6 active roles across three companies, including Zo and Skillcraft.
     - Validates that there is real interest, but acknowledges the B2B monetization path has been hard and multi-month.

7. **Strategic questions and platform strategy**
   - Elizabeth asks who V is targeting now (clarifying enterprise vs. individual users) and confirms Careerspan is evolving toward an enterprise / platform model.
   - She introduces **Platform Revolution**, a book on multi-sided platforms and network effects, and outlines its relevance:
     - Addresses the “chicken-and-egg” problem of which side of a two-sided marketplace to build first.
     - Provides strategies for whether to go after end users, enterprises, or alternative value paths.
   - She offers to bring some of the book’s ideas and slides into a structured strategy session to help V think through distribution and platform design.

8. **Distribution and community leverage**
   - V highlights potential distribution via immigrant communities, Microsoft alumni, and other networks where Elizabeth is active.
   - He explains how job seekers who use Careerspan can be automatically pushed to the top of an employer’s queue once they complete stories, making it attractive for communities serving ambitious candidates.
   - He notes the quality of early employers (e.g., Zo as a well-backed seed-stage company, another with a Gates Foundation grant), which could be compelling “anchor logos” for Elizabeth’s networks.

9. **Collaborative next steps**
   - They discuss how each of them thinks best:
     - Both find they do strong work in **collaborative, structured sessions** rather than only solo reflection.
   - Elizabeth proposes a dedicated **one-hour strategy session**:
     - She will bring 1–2 slides summarizing key strategies from *Platform Revolution*.
     - Together they will map those strategies to Careerspan’s current state and decide how to apply them to distribution and platform design.
   - V enthusiastically agrees and suggests scheduling it soon after Thanksgiving (Saturday morning or around noon), noting he is back by Friday evening and has calendar availability.
   - Elizabeth will pick a time from his calendar and schedule the session.

10. **Close**
    - They end on a warm, appreciative note, with mutual excitement about collaborating.
    - Elizabeth wishes V a happy Thanksgiving; both reaffirm the plan to meet again for a deeper strategy discussion.
