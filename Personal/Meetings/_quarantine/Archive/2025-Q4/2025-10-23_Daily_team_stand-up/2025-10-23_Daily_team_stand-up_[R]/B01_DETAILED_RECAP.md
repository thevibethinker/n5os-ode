The broader team setting suggested this was a safe space for candid discussion about both personal matters and business developments.

### Part 2: Strategic Announcement - Acquisition Strategy (Minutes ~9-42)

Vrijen introduced the major business development: Careerspan has been approached with **two potential acquisition offers within the last couple of days**. This led to a comprehensive discussion of the company's strategic pivot away from VC funding toward an acquisition strategy.

**Context for the decision:**
- Logan and Vrijen realized that building a business specifically designed to appeal to VCs was neither something they wanted to do nor felt skilled at doing
- Despite strong VC relationships, they determined they're not suited to "selling snake oil" to VCs
- They recognized Careerspan has "an incredible product that people love"
- The priority shifted to: How do we maintain that product and scale it?

**Market opportunity identified:**
- There are at least two companies (including one a16z-funded) operating in the UK trying to do exactly what Careerspan is doing
- One of these companies recently raised $20 million and is specifically looking to acquire companies in Careerspan's space
- Vrijen made contact through an intermediary with one of these potential acquirers

**Assets in play for acquisition:**
- Great technology/product
- Strong (or "budding") brand that can be built upon
- User base (described as "strong user base")
- Various other "saleable assets"
- The founding team's talent and network

**Acquisition levers & mechanisms:**
Logan outlined four primary levers that typically drive acquisition conversations:
1. **Talent**: Not necessarily as a team unit, but the ability to place individuals in roles. Collective bargaining power is stronger than individual IC negotiation
2. **Tech assets**: Technology that the acquiring company can roll into their roadmap, providing competitive advantage or development time savings
3. **Data & user base**: Existing brand recognition and customer base in markets the acquirer doesn't yet have access to
4. **Sales pipeline/customer relationships**: Already-established footholds in specific geographic or vertical markets

Deal structures are varied—some may be $0 cash sales with salary retention bonuses (6-12 months) plus role choice; others may include equity components or cash. The concept of "Aqua Hire" has been rising in frequency in recent tech deals.

**Current status of conversations:**
- Not yet at Letter of Intent (LOI) stage
- Conversations are at the "let me run this up the flagpole" stage
- One contact is "one degree of separation away from the main decision maker"
- Logan has a "war game room" of ~150 companies that could be interested in talent, tech, or distribution/data
- Initial signals are positive enough to provide "confidence to go out and say" these are the types of conversations happening

**Runway & employment commitments:**
- Runway for 3+ months is confirmed
- Everyone will be paid for at least three months
- Job search accommodation available if needed

**Strategic rationale:**
Logan explained the shift: avoiding the VC model (which demands 100X returns "at all costs") and instead finding a "most realistic, responsible and strategic decision." He expressed relief about not having to sell equity stake/soul to VCs while noting that acquisition allows partnership with "somebody bigger who can take this to a bigger market."

The market timing is considered strategic—there's "a shit ton of money and movement in this space right now." Logan specifically mentioned Workday's ventures team has made five acquisitions in the last six months. Logan has existing relationships with decision-makers at major companies, which he framed as a significant advantage.

**Product roadmap considerations:**
A practical question from Ilse explored whether strong sales success in the next two months would change acquisition plans. Logan's response: strong sales would actually increase optionality by providing more runway and demonstrating growth momentum, making the company more attractive to acquirers. The goal remains a sale, but with more leverage. Even free usage with growing adoption (e.g., "zero to 10 to 70 businesses using us") signals traction to potential acquirers.

**Next steps & team considerations:**
- Individual calls will be scheduled to understand each team member's preferences and desired outcomes
- Team members are encouraged to explore options but not aggressively job hunt on LinkedIn
- The company will help facilitate placement opportunities through their networks
- If team members are placed through Careerspan's recruiting relationships, they'll receive recruiting cuts as part of severance
- Maximum optionality is the goal for both the company (acquisition strategy) and individuals (career paths)
- Product focus for December: Getting employer dashboard and other features to a state that maximizes appeal to potential acquirers

**Tone and message from leadership:**
Both Logan and Vrijen emphasized: this is not a "fire sale" or "zombie-style, on the ground bleeding out" situation. They have options and strategic momentum. The three-month heads-up is intentional—they promised this forewarning and want maximum transparency. Logan specifically noted they've been trying to represent the team's interests "as best as we can" despite recognizing that "overall this is like not great for you guys."

## Technical Details & Specific References

The acquisition strategy references specific market players and strategic frameworks:

- **Specific acquirer examples**: UK-based company that raised $20M, a16z-funded player, Workday (major HR platform actively acquiring in this space)
- **Deal structure framework**: Four-lever model (talent, tech, data/users, sales pipeline), with Aqua Hire as increasingly common deal type
- **Equity commitment**: Full vesting of four-year equity grants if company sells within next year, plus equivalent payout
- **Timeline assumptions**: Two-month runway to optimize product for acquisition appeal (target: mid-December for product readiness)
