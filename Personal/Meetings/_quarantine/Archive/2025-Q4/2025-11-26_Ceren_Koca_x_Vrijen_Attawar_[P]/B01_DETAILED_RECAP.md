# Detailed Recap

1. **Opening and rapport**  
   Ceren and V open with light banter about V’s cat and home setup. Logan joins shortly after. They realize they share multiple mutual friends (Alexi and Misha), overlapping time in Singapore, and connections through Google and Harvard, which establishes quick trust and a "small world" feeling.

2. **Backgrounds and origin stories**  
   - Ceren shares her path: IBM → PwC strategy/behavioral economics → six years at Google leading a behavioral insights team in APAC → Harvard (Human Development) → co‑founding a Gates‑funded startup. She is an applied behavioral scientist who has run large‑scale market research and complex mixed‑methods studies.  
   - Logan and V summarize their story: years working together across Asia (Singapore, Shanghai), early edtech and consulting experience, and eventually founding Careerspan. They transitioned from college admissions to career coaching, centered on narrative design and helping people articulate their stories.

3. **Ceren’s current startup and problem statement**  
   - Ceren and co‑founder Luigi won MIT’s venture builder course and subsequently secured ~$680k in non‑dilutive Gates Foundation funding, giving ~12 months of runway.  
   - Their product: an AI‑enabled research and insights platform aimed at doing *serious* market/user research (surveys, interviews, focus groups, statistical analysis, causal and correlational work) rather than shallow "AI interview" summaries. They explicitly want to *amplify* professional researchers, not replace them.  
   - They have strong B2B demand and a working prototype, but lack a production‑ready, robust tech product. The team has: Ceren (CEO, GTM and research), Luigi (Chan School; research/impact), Henry (ex‑Google insights researcher now acting as product‑leaning CTO, very strong technically but not a professional software engineer).  
   - A previous CTO (an overextended MIT engineer) created misalignment and was let go. Now they urgently need: (a) a senior product‑oriented engineer, and (b) a trusted technical advisor who can guide Henry and Ceren on architecture and "unknown unknowns" without demanding an unsustainable rate.

4. **Careerspan’s product and positioning**  
   - V and Logan explain Careerspan’s core: AI‑facilitated storytelling that surfaces a candidate’s experiences and durable skills via structured "stories" (often captured via audio), guided by multi‑agent conversations.  
   - On the employer side, they show how those stories power an employer portal that: flags potential deal breakers, highlights non‑obvious strengths, and gives a more complete view than a resume alone. Candidates with rich stories are scored and profiled very differently from those with empty or low‑engagement profiles.  
   - They stress that the goal is to separate *getting to know someone* from *evaluating* them. Modern hiring over‑indexes on keyword/ATS filters and under‑indexes on values, mindset, and soft‑skills alignment—despite the fact that most 18‑month churn is due to cultural/value mismatch, not pure skill gaps.

5. **How Careerspan could help Ceren’s hiring problem**  
   - V reframes Ceren’s challenge: as non‑technical founders without a deep dev network, they struggle both to *find* strong senior technical talent and to *evaluate* them, especially on product sense and fit with their mission and working style.  
   - Careerspan proposes to help in three ways:  
     1. **JD and role definition support** – V will run a short, high‑signal JD design session with Ceren (and ideally Henry), incorporating both hard‑skill requirements and nuanced cultural/working‑style traits. This uses a new JD‑copilot workflow V has been prototyping on Zo and/or input from Ilsa (Careerspan’s Head of AI).  
     2. **Zero‑touch candidate intake** – Instead of a traditional ATS link, Ceren would post a Careerspan link in job posts and community channels. Candidates go through the Careerspan storytelling experience, producing rich, structured data that Careerspan then uses to surface the 2–5% most promising candidates. This mitigates the "LinkedIn post that attracts only vibe coders" problem.  
     3. **Network amplification and technical‑advisor search** – V and Logan will promote the role into curated communities (e.g., high‑caliber engineering groups such as FOHI and their broader founder networks), and also help Ceren identify a small set of credible technical advisors who have been in early‑stage trenches and can guide Henry.

6. **Technical‑advisor expectations and model**  
   - Ceren describes her ideal: someone who can take Henry from roughly "50" to "80–90" on technical leadership—help him make key architecture decisions, avoid over‑engineering, and grow into a strong product‑oriented CTO, without charging "$700k/hour" or demanding disproportionate equity.  
   - Logan and V share their own experience: they previously engaged a senior technical advisor for ~10 hours (1–2 hours of discovery; creation of interview frameworks; and sitting in on several second‑round interviews). That structure worked well and did not require a large, ongoing commitment.  
   - They also describe Ilsa, Careerspan’s Head of AI, as an example of the kind of deeply aligned, mission‑driven senior technical partner Ceren should be seeking (whether or not Ilsa herself becomes an advisor). Ilsa helped them avoid over‑complex ML for its own sake and focus on "the dumbest system that yields the smartest outputs."

7. **Broader reflections on hiring and research tooling**  
   - V and Ceren agree that current hiring funnels are broken: AI‑generated resumes make ATS filters unreliable; many tools over‑promise "anyone can do research" in ways that insult professional researchers.  
   - Ceren explains that some AI research tools have offended senior research leads at companies like Pinterest precisely because they imply research is easy and generic. Her product is explicitly aiming at harder analytic work and serious methodology, not just pretty AI summaries.  
   - V ties this to Careerspan’s philosophy: tooling should respect the craft (career coaching, research, hiring) and act as an amplifier rather than a replacement.

8. **Commercial terms and product readiness**  
   - V proposes a simple pricing model: Ceren "rents the link" to Careerspan for **$200/month** while the role is open. The expectation is that she will only need it for 1–2 months per hire.  
   - That fee covers the compute and orchestration required to run the multi‑agent coaching, scoring, and employer‑side analysis; Ceren does not need to manage any of the underlying AI infrastructure.  
   - Ceren confirms that this pricing is feasible within their Gates‑funded budget and is eager to move quickly, given strict milestones and timelines tied to the grant.

9. **Concrete next steps**  
   - **JD session** – V will check with Ilsa (currently in Portugal and partially on Thanksgiving break) about joining a short JD‑definition call. Ceren will loop Henry in and aim for a slot within the next 3–4 hours if possible; otherwise they will find the next viable window.  
   - **Account setup** – After the JD session, V will spin up a Careerspan employer account for Ceren’s team and generate a live job link.  
   - **Distribution** – Once the link is ready, Ceren will post it to her own network (e.g., LinkedIn, founder circles), while V and Logan push it into their vetted engineering and founder communities.  
   - **Future case study and collaboration** – If Careerspan helps them land the right senior engineer and/or advisor, Ceren is happy to do a public case study and share feedback on the candidate and employer experience, including UX/UI insights from her applied behavioral science perspective.

10. **Close and relationship building**  
    - They discuss life in Boston, New York, Mexico, and various international upbringings (TCK experiences), reinforcing personal connection.  
    - V offers to connect Ceren with other mission‑aligned founders (including an applied behavioral scientist in Mexico) so she has more peer support as an early‑stage CEO.  
    - They end aligned on a shared view of "serious" research and "serious" hiring, with clear enthusiasm to collaborate quickly on filling Ceren’s critical senior technical role.
