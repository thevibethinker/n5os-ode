# Business Context

## Ceren’s Company

- **Stage**: Early seed‑stage, ~5–6 months into build; funded via **~$680k non‑dilutive grant from the Gates Foundation**, with ~12 months of remaining runway tied to specific technical and impact milestones.
- **Product**: AI‑augmented **market and user research platform** designed for *serious* research teams (e.g., at large tech companies and global orgs).  
  - Goes beyond simple AI interview tools; aims to support: survey design, interviews/focus groups, statistical analyses (correlations, causal inference), and nuanced qualitative analysis.  
  - Philosophical stance: tools should **amplify** professional researchers, not imply "anyone can do research" with a thin AI layer.
- **Target customers**:
  - Enterprise research and insights teams (e.g., Pinterest’s research org, big‑tech research units, UN‑adjacent orgs).  
  - Possibly UN and large NGO stakeholders through Gates/Chan networks focused on economic mobility and development.
- **Team**:
  - **Ceren** – CEO, applied behavioral scientist, ex‑Google behavioral insights lead, strong B2B/GTM network.  
  - **Luigi** – Co‑founder from the Chan School; impact and research‑oriented partner.  
  - **Henry** – Ex‑Google insights researcher, now acting CTO/product lead; very strong analytically and technically but not yet a full‑stack product engineer.  
  - Former CTO (MIT engineer) was mis‑aligned (spread across too many projects, chasing equity without focus) and was removed.
- **Key constraints and risks**:
  - Need to turn a promising prototype into a robust, maintainable product quickly to satisfy grant milestones and convert strong early interest into paying customers.  
  - Limited runway and budget; cannot afford a mis‑hire at senior technical level or an advisor who charges enterprise‑grade rates with little practical value.  
  - As a non‑technical CEO, Ceren is acutely aware of her blind spots on architecture and technical trade‑offs.

## Careerspan

- **Business model**: AI‑driven **career coaching and hiring intelligence** platform.  
  - Candidate‑facing: multi‑agent, story‑driven coaching that helps candidates articulate detailed STAR‑quality stories, primarily via audio.  
  - Employer‑facing: portal that scores and summarizes candidates, highlights dealbreakers, and surfaces deeper behavioral/values signals, rather than relying solely on resumes/keywords.
- **Target customers**:
  - Employers and communities that care about durable skills, values, and mindset—not just stack keywords—especially in competitive technical hiring.  
  - Schools, bootcamps, and communities that want better placement and coaching for their members.
- **Pricing (relevant here)**: simple subscription to "rent the link" at roughly **$200/month per role**, expected to be used for 1–2 months per hire.

## Strategic Fit

- Both companies operate at the intersection of **applied behavioral science** and **AI‑mediated conversations** (for coaching, research, and assessment).
- Ceren’s problem—finding a senior product‑oriented engineer and trustworthy advisor without a deep dev network—is precisely the kind of **high‑stakes, low‑volume hiring** problem where Careerspan’s strengths (rich candidate storytelling + curated networks) matter most.
- Success on this search would create a **mutually valuable case study**:  
  - For Ceren: story of landing a mission‑aligned senior engineer under time pressure.  
  - For Careerspan: proof that their system can solve a complex, non‑commoditized technical search for a sophisticated, research‑driven founder.
