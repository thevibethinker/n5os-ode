# B03_DECISIONS MADE

## Strategic Pivots

### Decision 1: Shift from Growth-at-All-Costs to Acquisition-Ready Positioning
**Status:** DECIDED  
**Timeline:** 6-month execution window to acquisition  
**Rationale:** Founder burnout is unsustainable; product is feature-complete for career coaching; market perception (via Laura's feedback) validates core differentiator but not market expansion potential

**Implication:** All energy allocation decisions now filtered through: "Does this increase acquisition appeal?" rather than "Does this drive unlimited growth?"

### Decision 2: Target 2-3M Exit Valuation
**Status:** DECIDED  
**Baseline:** ~$2M sunk capital (primarily from Vrijen's father's investment)  
**Justification:** Recover invested capital while acknowledging market realities for early-stage SaaS acquisition

**Owner:** Vrijen (financial decision-maker/board alignment)

### Decision 3: Reframe Distribution Strategy—"Personal Brand Development" Focus
**Status:** DECIDED  
**Previous:** Selling "Careerspan" as a product to communities  
**New:** Use Careerspan strategically as a tool within personal brand/coaching practice

**Shift:** Rather than Vrijen pitching "Careerspan to communities," he can offer webinars/coaching services and embed Careerspan. Logan can sell to communities as a coaching enhancement tool.

**Benefit:** More energizing positioning + maintains Careerspan distribution without disproportionate founders' energy drain

### Decision 4: Payroll Restructuring—Move Founders to India Payroll
**Status:** DECIDED  
**Objective:** Bridge 3-month funding gap (Jan-Mar 2026; allowance resets April 1)  
**Mechanism:** Founders move to India payroll (cost reduction); Ilsa remains US payroll (employment compliance requirement)

**Owner:** Logan (execution); Vrijen (approval)

### Decision 5: Prioritize Sales/Marketing Infrastructure Over Product Development
**Status:** DECIDED  
**Rationale:** Product is 90% self-serve complete; distribution infrastructure is severely underdeveloped  
**Actions:** 
- Bring in Ilya (advisor/contractor) to lead sales/marketing loops
- Focus dev budget (~$5-6K/month) on distribution infrastructure, not product features
- Build 10+ repeatable "buyer loops" (sales funnels) before acquisition pitch

**Owner:** Logan (technical lead); Ilya (sales/marketing lead)

## Outcome of Key Debate

**Question Posed:** Should Careerspan stay in "growth mode" with dedicated operational focus, or shift to low-cost "autopilot" maintenance while founders pursue other work?

**Resolution:** Neither pure option adopted. **Hybrid model selected:**
- Careerspan enters "acquisition-focused maintenance mode"
- Founders dedicate energy to sales/marketing loops and buyer identification (not product development)
- Founders pursue personal brand/coaching work in parallel (zero incremental work on Careerspan)
- Company costs fixed at low level (~$5-6K/month dev + payroll) with clear exit timeline

This satisfies founder desire for reduced operational burden while maintaining acquisition trajectory.
