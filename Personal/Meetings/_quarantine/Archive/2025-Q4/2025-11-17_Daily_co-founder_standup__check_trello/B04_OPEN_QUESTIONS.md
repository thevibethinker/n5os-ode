# Open Questions & Unresolved Decision Points

## Partnership & Positioning
1. **Should the Zo integration be centered on Careerspan use or intentionally independent?**
   - Raised concern: Building a Zo instance exclusively around Careerspan may limit broader appeal and reduce strategic flexibility
   - Contrarian position offered: Would a CRM-focused or portfolio-building Zo tool be more effective if positioned as generic career tool?

2. **What is the optimal depth of Zo x Careerspan co-branding?**
   - Two frameworks proposed but decision deferred:
     - Light option: Careerspan as underlying data source, Zo as generic AI career advisor
     - Deep option: Exclusive custom Zo instance with Next Play proprietary training

3. **Does the event itself create necessary value or is it just a distribution mechanism?**
   - Questioned whether hosting an event (with all associated logistics) is worth the effort vs. direct outreach to employers

## Revenue & Scaling Concerns
4. **How do we sustainably grow Careerspan user base without corresponding employer revenue growth?**
   - Core tension: More users without employer demand creates a liability rather than asset
   - Previous conversation with Ilya raised cost implications of user growth misalignment

5. **What is the employer incentive structure that ensures long-term job posting?**
   - Is four-day early access to Next Play members sufficient incentive for founders to post?
   - How much operational burden do we create for employers with dual-round candidate processing?

## Go-to-Market Mechanics
6. **Should we require genuine job openings or can we position with "signal" (employer credibility) alone?**
   - Radical proposal offered: Start with just 6 roles from trusted founder friends rather than requiring large job board
   - Effectiveness concern: Does signaling alone drive adoption or do actual opportunities matter more?

7. **What channels should we use to notify Next Play members of exclusive job access?**
   - Slack vs. email: Current state of email distribution unclear
   - Question raised: Does Slack alone provide sufficient reach if not everyone monitors daily?

## Community & Targeting Strategy
8. **Should we expand geographically or deepen NYC focus?**
   - Proposed benefit of city-focused events: Better employer-candidate matching by location
   - Unresolved: Is NYC test enough to validate model before expanding?

9. **What proportion of Next Play membership consists of hiring decision-makers vs. job seekers?**
   - Assumption made that audience is mixed, but specific split unknown
   - Affects messaging: Community benefit (jobs available) vs. employer benefit (qualified pipeline)

10. **Can Next Play members realistically generate demand for Careerspan as data source?**
    - Ben Lang likely receives compensation for job distribution; would similar incentive apply to talent pipeline concept?
    - How do we turn early-access benefit into long-term adoption without ongoing exclusivity?

## Execution & Testing
11. **What is the minimum viable test for employer willingness to participate?**
    - Should we ask committed founders first (warm outreach) or test with cold Next Play-based employers?
    - Risk of overbuilding if employer buy-in is lower than assumed

12. **How transparent should we be with employers about the multi-round candidate processing?**
    - Question of truthfulness raised: Can we accurately represent "early access" if the mechanics feel opaque?
