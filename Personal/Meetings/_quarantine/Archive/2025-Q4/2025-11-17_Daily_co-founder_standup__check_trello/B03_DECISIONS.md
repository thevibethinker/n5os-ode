# B03: Decisions

## Confirmed Decisions

### 1. Simplified Event Structure (Approved)
**Decision**: Careerspan acts as sponsor (no payment required) for a Zo-hosted event rather than creating integrated product collaboration
- Zo has full creative control over event content (site building, portfolio careers, etc.)
- Careerspan's role limited to: sponsorship visibility + job board announcement
- **Rationale**: Decouples messaging complexity, removes product development burden

### 2. Job Board Announcement at Event
**Decision**: Use event momentum to announce Careerspan job listings to Next Play community
- Present 5-6 curated job openings at event
- Offer jobs as proof points and tangible value to attendees
- **Rationale**: Converts passive sponsorship into concrete user action

### 3. Early Access Mechanic Approved
**Decision**: Next Play members receive 3-7 day (or 72-hour equivalent) head start on job postings before public distribution
- Framing: "Next Play members get first look" or "early access to roles"
- Positions Careerspan as community partner, creates switching incentive
- **Rationale**: Genuine advantage; aligns with economic incentives of job seekers

### 4. Founder Pre-Commitment Strategy
**Decision**: Approach 5-6 founder partners individually for initial job postings
- Offer value to founders: pre-screened candidates from Careerspan + Next Play audience exposure
- Positioned as "favor to me" + business benefit (early eyeballs on hiring)
- **Rationale**: Creates proof points; ensures quality initial listings

### 5. Ben Lang Engagement Path (Conditional)
**Decision**: Set up "gut check" conversation with Jaya or other founders to validate early access messaging before broader outreach
- Test specific framing (e.g., "4-day head start" vs "72 hours")
- Determine founder willingness to participate in early access model
- **Rationale**: Validates assumptions about founder incentives before full execution
