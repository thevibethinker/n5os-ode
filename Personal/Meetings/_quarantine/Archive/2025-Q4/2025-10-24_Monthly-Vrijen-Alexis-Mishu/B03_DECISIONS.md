# B03_DECISIONS

## Primary Decision: Hiring Senior Systems Design Engineer

**Decision:** Hire contractor-based Systems Design Engineer from Malawi at $3,000/month for 1-year term.

**Framework Applied:**
- **Problem defined:** Need someone who can present technical options with clear pros/cons analysis; Mishal makes strategic decisions against 6-month and 5-year timelines
- **Candidate evaluation:** Assessed for backend/systems engineering expertise, communication ability, long-term vision alignment
- **Deal structure:** Contractor (not employee), salary-based (not equity-only), 6-month review checkpoint

**Rationale:**
- Contractor has proven backend infrastructure experience (building logistics systems at scale)
- Shows entrepreneurial trajectory despite non-traditional education background
- Motivated by stability + long-term opportunity vs. commodity contract work
- Pay represents bump from current earnings; removes survival-mode desperation from equation

**Implementation:** Contractor already accepted deal; onboarding proceeds immediately.

**Risk Acknowledgment:** Vrijen flagged potential for this to replicate previous advisory patterns without equity (referencing unpaid Canada candidate). Mishal countered with differentiation: Canada candidate showed early disinterest and was never compensated; this contractor is full-time hired role.

## Secondary Decision: Maintain Weekly Mentor Relationship with Uber Engineer

**Decision:** Continue weekly advisory sessions with Uber senior engineer at no compensation, no equity, framed as exploratory side project.

**Framework Applied:**
- **Context:** Engineer is comfortable in current role but bored; using relationship as low-risk startup exposure
- **Boundaries:** First startup experience; proceeding cautiously without full commitment
- **Value exchange:** Technical guidance + credibility + engineering depth vs. learning opportunity for mentor

**Rationale:**
- Fills immediate gap in technical decision-making depth while contractor ramps
- Mentor has relevant experience (logistics infrastructure, data engineering) matching needs
- Startup-forward background reduces big-tech bias risk vs. pure enterprise engineer

**Implementation:** Weekly standing sessions continue; no formal contract or equity framework established.

**Risk Acknowledgment:** Vrijen surfaced concern: unpaid/non-equity advisory roles often misalign incentives. "A hundred percent of nothing is nothing." Team that knows their worth only commits what they believe they're worth.

**Decision Made:** Acknowledge risk; proceed with 1-month pulse-check evaluation to assess whether advisor's participation is driving sufficient value and if formal equity conversation becomes necessary.

## Tertiary Decision: Avoid Upwork, Use WealthFound for Hiring

**Decision:** Moved from Upwork-based recruitment to WealthFound for contractor sourcing.

**Rationale:**
- Upwork candidates treat work as side projects; lack commitment/focus
- WealthFound candidates show higher seriousness and dedication levels
- Enables full-time dedicated resources vs. juggling multiple gigs

**Implementation:** Posted detailed job spec on WealthFound with explicit tech stack and skill requirements; ran 3-4 interview rounds.

**Outcome:** Found "really good" candidate (the hired Systems Design Engineer).

## Implicit Decision: Reject Pure AI-First Development Model

**Decision:** Officially pivoting away from AI-as-primary-development-tool to hybrid model with human engineering + AI assistance.

**Rationale:**
- AI provides inconsistent architecture guidance; changes recommendations when challenged
- Two-month discovery phase proved complexity too high for AI coordination across interconnected systems
- Confirmed explicit technical requirements exist (backend architecture, systems design) that require human expertise

**Framework Applied:** Vrijen validated this wasn't unique/shameful—every bootstrap founder considers AI-first model. Value = early recognition of silo/coordination problem (caught at 2 months, not 12 months).

**Implementation:** Human-led technical decision-making with AI as supplementary tool for execution/exploration.
