Logan shared that a Forbes freelancer writing about portfolio careers reached out for a quote and promised to link to Careerspan, so the co-founders agreed to reawaken the Career Tech Consortium by inviting the journalist to an open session (aiming for December with a high-energy guest such as Shadat, Adam Alpert, or another marquee founder).

Discussion shifted to guest scouting: the Squarespace chief people officer, the Modern People Leaders podcast hosts, and the Modern People Leaders (MPL) journalist were proposed as leverage points for coverage. Vrijen noted that several people they met at a recent HR event are still warm, and he intends to invite them to a session where an embedded journalist could capture the story; he is also tracking seven-plus advocates who already use Careerspan and can testify about its impact.

The stand-up then leaned into community anecdotes—Logan’s whirlwind week, the energy from dinners, the viral Twitter traction from the Zoe memes, and the plan to keep coordinating with Ben and Robin. They also talked about the Zo contract, Jeff’s employer platform, the demo at 10, and the possibility that Jeff will own his employer relationships while the team keeps a smaller take.

The latter part of the call turned strategic: Vrijen floated a “kamikaze plan” to open-source the candidate-facing part of Careerspan if no acquisition offers materialize, keeping the employer portal in the stack while giving the user experience to the community. Logan agreed that separating the two pieces during an exit could secure both revenue and the public good. The stand-up wrapped with follow-ups on demos, demo cadences (Marvin showcase, Junto Club), and a reminder to log coverage results for the new Forbes article.
