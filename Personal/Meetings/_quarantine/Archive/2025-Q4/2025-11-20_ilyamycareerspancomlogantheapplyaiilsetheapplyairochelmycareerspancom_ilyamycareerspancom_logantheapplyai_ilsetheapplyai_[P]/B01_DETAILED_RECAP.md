# Meeting Recap: Aviato Demo Preparation

## Overview
Internal team meeting between Careerspan co-founders (V, Logan, Ilya) and Applied AI team members (Logan, Ilse/Rochel) to prepare for an upcoming demo with Aviato—a high-growth startup founded by a 17-year-old inspired by the HBO show. The meeting focused on demo strategy, flow, team dynamics, and candidate profile mapping for the demo.

## Key Context
- **Client**: Aviato (led by Conrad Kacharski, Chief Revenue Officer)
- **Call Time**: Friday, 10:30 AM EST (24 hours from meeting time)
- **Participants**: 4 people on call (V leading, Ilsa presenting, Logan making brief appearance, Ilya providing strategic input)
- **Company Profile**: Data-driven, startup culture, believes ability matters more than pedigree, strong focus on candidate quality over volume

## Main Topics Discussed

### 1. Demo Philosophy & Audience Psychology
V articulated the core principle: customers won't remember every detail of the demo, but they will remember:
- Whether the team seemed nice, present, and genuinely passionate
- If the product delivered on its core promise in one key area
- The general impression of dedication and focus

Strategy: Demo should convey two things:
1. "This team is solely dedicated to this one mission"
2. "The product shines in this specific area"

### 2. Demo Flow Structure (Three-Act Play)
The demo is organized as three sequential acts, each 1-2 minutes (potentially 5 minutes with questions):

**Act 1**: Job Description Analysis
- Shows how Careerspan breaks down job descriptions into responsibilities, hard skills, soft skills, and implied requirements
- Demonstrates customization options (importance weighting)
- Explains how this creates assessment criteria for matching

**Act 2**: Resume & Candidate Analysis
- Shows how candidate information (resume + stories) is analyzed against the job profile
- Emphasizes how stories reveal dimensions that resumes don't capture
- Demonstrates the density of data extraction and matching

**Act 3**: Matching & Ranking
- Shows how candidates are ranked based on job fit
- Demonstrates the visualization and filtering of results

### 3. Introductions Strategy
- Keep to 1-2 minutes maximum
- Name, rank, serial number only—no LinkedIn bio readings
- Default: brief intros → immediate screen share
- Alternative: screen share already open with system homepage during intros (satisfies early curiosity)
- Determine audience preference early (some want quick intros, others want deeper context)

### 4. Demo Customization for Aviato
Based on research, Aviato's likely pain point is not volume but scalability of high-touch hiring process:
- They currently accept email applications and manual review
- This allows them to assess "crackedness" (cultural fit/quality indicators) but doesn't scale
- Careerspan positions: "Scalable way to do that deep cultural assessment"

Emphasis areas for Aviato demo:
- Show how system can surface "diamond in the rough" candidates (transferable skills, hidden potential)
- Highlight "Tell your story once, we shape it for every application" approach
- Emphasize how system calibrates importance weightings (competitive advantage vs. Greenhouse)
- Position ability/merit-based signals over pedigree

### 5. Recording & Consent
- Ask permission to record at top of call
- Rationale: Share with their team for review (external-facing benefit); also for Careerspan's refinement (internal benefit)

### 6. Candidate Profile Mapping
Ilsa created three fictional applicant personas for "Chair AI" (fictitious company):
1. **Rockstar**: Resume matches claims, stories match claims—objectively strong
2. **Diamond in the Rough**: Resume understates abilities, stories reveal hidden potential (titled role with outsize impact)
3. **Weak Candidate**: Used as contrast

Used names from sci-fi book *Gideon the Ninth* (coincidentally, also favorite of Aviato founder)—demonstrates cultural attentiveness and breaks up naming bias concerns.

Approach: Filter high-to-low to show top matches immediately, then optionally show applicant portal to reveal how stories filled gaps.

### 7. Team Composition & Roles
**Final lineup for demo**:
- **V** (CEO): Lead narrative, strategic framing, context-setting
- **Ilsa**: Handle live product walkthrough, screen sharing, answer technical details
- **Logan**: Make 1-minute cameo appearance early to establish co-founder credibility, then drop off ("I'm double-booked with a VC event in Boston")

Rationale: V provides strategic context and founder credibility; Ilsa demonstrates product sophistication; Logan's brief appearance adds legitimacy without overwhelming the call.

- V: Sketch out detailed three-act play with screen order and key talking points; create demo account with three applicant personas; prepare "biggest problem" qualifying question
- Ilsa: Finalize removal of PII (names already anonymized using sci-fi references); ensure all demo accounts are locked down and rehearsed
