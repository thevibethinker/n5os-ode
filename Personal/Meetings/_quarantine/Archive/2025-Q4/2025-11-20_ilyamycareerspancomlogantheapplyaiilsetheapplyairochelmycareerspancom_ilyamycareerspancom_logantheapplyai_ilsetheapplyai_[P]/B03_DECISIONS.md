# Decisions Made

## Demo Structure & Flow

**DECISION**: The demo will follow a three-act play format:
- **Act 1**: Job Description Analysis (system breaks down JD into skills, responsibilities, criteria)
- **Act 2**: Candidate/Resume Analysis (shows how candidate data maps to job profile)
- **Act 3**: Matching & Ranking (displays filtered, ranked candidates)
- **Duration**: 1-2 minutes each act ideal; realistically 5 minutes per act with questions
- **Rationale**: Tight structure focuses buyer attention on core value propositions without overwhelming detail

**DECISION**: Introduction will be brief and audience-responsive:
- **Default strategy**: 1-2 minutes max intros, then jump to screen share
- **Personalization**: Take audience temperature early—some prefer formality, others want immediate product view
- **Tactic**: Keep system already visible on screen during intros (homepage/login screen) to satisfy early curiosity
- **Script**: Name, role, and brief welcome only—no biographical deep-dives

## Team Roles & Demo Lead

**DECISION**: V leads the narrative; Ilsa conducts live product walkthrough:
- **V's role**: Strategic framing, context-setting, founder credibility, audience guidance
- **Ilsa's role**: Live system navigation, technical demonstrations, deep-dive answers
- **Reasoning**: V's stream-of-consciousness strategic thinking endears her to tech audiences; Ilsa's product intimacy ensures smooth execution
- **Risk mitigation**: Ilsa avoids over-explanation; V avoids rabbit holes by staying narrative-focused

**DECISION**: Logan makes a 1-minute cameo at demo start:
- **Script**: "Hi, I'm Logan, co-founder. I'm on another call but wanted to say hi and mention I'm a big fan. I'm coming to Boston before Thanksgiving to set up with Aviato. Have a great call."
- **Purpose**: Establish multiple founder presence and credibility without overwhelming the call
- **Timing**: Happens in first minute, then Logan exits

## Aviato Demo Customization

**DECISION**: Emphasize Acts 2 & 3 over Act 1 (lean into candidate/matching problem):
- **Rationale**: Aviato's pain point is likely not job description quality but scalable assessment of candidate quality/cultural fit
- **Current process**: Accept email applications, manual high-touch review (good for assessing "crackedness" but doesn't scale)
- **Value proposition**: "Scalable way to do that deep assessment without losing the human judgment element"

**DECISION**: Lead with "biggest problem" qualifying question early in call:
- **Timing**: After brief intros, before diving into demo
- **Question focus**: "What's your biggest hiring pain point right now?" or similar
- **Purpose**: Locks in demo path customization (which act to emphasize, which examples to highlight)
- **Flexibility**: Demo structure stays same; emphasis areas shift based on answer

**DECISION**: Showcase ability/merit indicators over pedigree signals:
- **Tactic**: Use "diamond in the rough" candidate persona to demonstrate how system surfaces hidden potential
- **Narrative**: "Here's a candidate whose resume understates their impact. When they tell their story, you see the real ability."
- **Connection to Aviato culture**: Aligns with their "ability matters, not pedigree" philosophy (17-year-old founder in bathroom taking meetings)

**DECISION**: Demonstrate customization & weighting controls (Greenhouse competitive advantage):
- **Example**: "You can say 'cultural fit matters more than advanced ML expertise' and the system prioritizes accordingly"
- **Implication**: Answers Ilya's Greenhouse concern—show that Careerspan already supports what Greenhouse claims as new feature

## Candidate Profile Mapping

**DECISION**: Use three fictional applicants for "Chair AI" company demo:
- **Persona 1 (Rockstar)**: Resume and stories both show excellence—control/baseline candidate
- **Persona 2 (Diamond)**: Resume understates impact; stories reveal hidden potential—demonstrates core value
- **Persona 3 (Weak candidate)**: Used as contrast/filtering demonstration
- **Naming**: Use sci-fi names from *Gideon the Ninth* (conveniently also Aviato founder's favorite book)

**DECISION**: Show applicant portal selectively (not in main demo flow):
- **Core demo**: Employer dashboard only
- **Optional reveal**: If time/questions permit, show applicant portal to demonstrate how stories fill profile gaps
- **Rationale**: Keeps demo focused on employer value; applicant side is supporting context

**DECISION**: Finalize PII removal before demo:
- **Status**: Ilsa removing personal information from candidate profiles
- **Approach**: Already using anonymized sci-fi names; ensure all accounts are fully locked-down
- **Plan**: Create dedicated demo account that no one can modify

## Recording & Consent

**DECISION**: Record the demo call with explicit permission:
- **Ask timing**: Very top of call
- **Messaging**: "We're going to record with your permission—this is so you can share it with your team for review, and for us to refine our demo process"
- **Benefit emphasis**: Primary value is for them (team sharing); secondary is internal improvement
- **Format**: Provide link/copy to prospect after call

The team decided to execute a tight, customizable three-act demo with V leading narrative strategy and Ilsa handling product execution. Demo will emphasize Careerspan's ability to scale cultural assessment (Aviato's core pain point) through high-touch customization and "diamond in the rough" candidate surfacing. Team composition (V + Ilsa lead, Logan cameo) balances credibility with focus. Pre-demo prep includes script finalization, PII review, and practice runs.
