## Partnership Strategy: Jeff (Interview Coach)

### Scenario 1: Jeff Owns Client
If Jeff already has candidates as clients:
- Jeff identifies high-performing candidates who aren't getting hired despite strong interviews
- Expands candidate reach by introducing Careerspan platform for profile building and visibility
- Candidates populate profiles with real credentials
- Creates additional value/revenue opportunity for Jeff

### Scenario 2: Careerspan Owns Client (VC Portfolio Model)
The more probable and strategic scenario:
- **Setup**: VC firm with portfolio companies; Careerspan signs primary portfolio company (e.g., Elise) as pilot
- **Execution**: First 2-3 open positions = 9 top Careerspan candidates
- **Jeff's Role**: Interview prep for all 9 candidates, making them "Academy Award winning interviewers"
- **Win-Win**: Candidates perform exceptionally in interviews (Jeff's impact visible), VC firm sees universal candidate quality (Careerspan impact visible)
- **Outcome**: "We're done. Careerspan, you can have the rest of the portfolio"
- **Timeline**: First week December - sit with job descriptions, Jeff preps candidates; second week December - candidates interview; VC firm approves portfolio expansion

### Partnership Philosophy
Jeff's interview coaching product cannot directly drive Careerspan sales. However, he's valuable because:
- Good connections and reputation
- Can amplify candidate success stories
- Creates win-win where both companies benefit from candidate placement success

## Revenue/Business Model Insight
Careerspan's "get rich quick scheme" is NOT profit-sharing on individual candidate prep. The real revenue play is portfolio-level contracts where Careerspan becomes the hiring backbone for entire VC-backed company groups. Interview coaching partnerships amplify this outcome.
