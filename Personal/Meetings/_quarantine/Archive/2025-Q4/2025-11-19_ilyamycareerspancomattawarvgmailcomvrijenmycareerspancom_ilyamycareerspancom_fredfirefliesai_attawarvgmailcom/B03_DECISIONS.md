### Decision 2: Demo Flow Optimization
**Decision**: Minimize introduction time; prioritize rapid transition to product screen.
- **Time allocation**: 30 seconds max for introductions + context setting
- **Rationale**: Customer has specific itch (seeing solution); lengthy introductions delay scratching that itch, causing audience disengagement and irritation
- **Example of what to avoid**: 20+ minute delays before reaching product screen (observed on recent call)

### Decision 3: Distribute Speaker Roles by Background Strength
**Decision**: Assign demo segments to highlight each team member's unique credibility.
- **Logan**: Speaks to pedagogy, methodology, Harvard connections, workforce expertise

- **Vrijen** (CEO): Frames value prop, guides demo flow, handles strategic framing
- **Rationale**: Audience will absorb credibility signals faster when information is delivered by credible messenger (Logan on education strategy, Ilsa on technical execution)

## Marketing Strategy Decisions

### Decision 5: Pursue Persona-Based Video Content Development
**Decision**: Develop role/persona-specific video content for outbound prospecting (in addition to general 60-second commercial).
- **Target personas**: 4-5 B2B hiring personas (hiring managers, sourcing leads, etc.) + candidate personas
- **Video model**: 10-15 second role-specific clips showing how Careerspan solves that persona's specific problem
- **Execution**: "Gangster move" approach (CEO-delivered, authentic, custom-per-prospect)

- **Rationale**: Red Bull model demonstrates that brands with budget/time/tools create persona-specific content paths to conversion; Careerspan has manageable persona count (4-5) making this feasible at scale

## Partnership Strategy Decisions

### Decision 7: Pursue VC Portfolio Model with Jeff as Interview Prep Partner
**Decision**: Position Jeff partnership as optional, project-based collaboration rather than default revenue share agreement.
- **Model**: When Careerspan signs VC portfolio company, offer to bring Jeff in for candidate interview prep to maximize success
- **Revenue**: No automatic profit share; instead, project-based engagement fees or success fees
- **First use case**: Sapphire/Elise pilot in early December
- **Rationale**: Jeff's product doesn't directly close Careerspan deals; however, his coaching amplifies candidate success, making Careerspan results more visible to VC firm, accelerating portfolio expansion opportunity

### Decision 8: Position Careerspan as Portfolio Revenue Play
**Decision**: Shift internal revenue model from per-candidate to per-portfolio.
- **Revenue thesis**: Careerspan's "get rich quick scheme" is portfolio-level contracts, not profit-sharing on individual candidate prep
- **Example**: Sign one VC firm portfolio company as pilot; upon demonstrating candidate quality, expand to entire portfolio
- **Jeff's role**: Amplifies candidate success visibility, removing objections to portfolio expansion
