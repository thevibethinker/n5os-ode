## Careerspan
- Non-technical, founder-led product that helps high-growth companies build candidate pipelines while staying lean on compute. The team is currently validating that Careerspan can be the low-effort way for founders to keep hiring even when they are “too busy to fully activate referrals.”
- The current focus is on generating proof points (Aviato, Dirac) and then showing that the same product works in partnership scenarios, which is why the Next Play experiment is being framed as a two-week, 10-role pilot rather than a permanent product feature.

## Next Play + Ben Guo
- Ben runs a $12/month, invitation-style community of achievers (associate-to-VP, director-level) who are hungry for events and peer wisdom. He is comfortable hosting events and has direct relationships into Lightspeed, Lorimer, and venture-backed founders.
- The community values curated experiences (AI productivity talks, in-person/digital events) and wants partners who bring polished, low-lift collaborations (hence the “we do the heavy lifting” pitch for the “Hack Your Career” series).

## Strategic hypotheses discussed
1. **Events as marketing** – Use Ben + Zoe to run a “hack your career” digital series and follow that with a January event that gives Ben visibility while letting Careerspan show off its tactical/strategic mix.
2. **Passive hiring link** – Treat founder websites (with embedded Careerspan talent calls) as a low-touch recruitment net, surface early access to Next Play members, and see if the team can manually nurture the responses before automating.
3. **Marketing cadence** – Run Instagram/paid campaigns in the weeks before December, secure demos via community introductions, then pivot to candidate messaging for late December to show momentum on both sides of the marketplace.
