# B21 – Key Moments

1. **Framing the monetization problem (Logan raises the paywall idea).**
   - Logan surfaces the tension between wanting to keep Careerspan free and the need to cover costs, explicitly suggesting a paywall for users.
   - This reframes the conversation from abstract financial stress to concrete product and pricing levers.

2. **Alice’s “no passive job seekers” insight.**
   - Logan relays Alice’s view that essentially everyone is actively job‑seeking now, driven by anxiety and uncertainty.
   - This becomes a pivotal justification for candidate‑side monetization and later for the “set it and forget it” subscription model.

3. **Articulation of the “Careerspan Select” / auto‑apply subscription.**
   - Vrijen spells out a model where candidates pay a monthly fee to be automatically checked against jobs and put in front of hiring managers.
   - Logan enthusiastically agrees with the value but flags the multi‑layered product and operational work required, sharpening the build‑vs‑reward tradeoff.

4. **Convergence on sequencing: simple paywall first, subscription later.**
   - After exploring Kickstarter‑style prepayments and subscription mechanics, both founders align on first understanding and possibly implementing a simple paywall.
   - This is a quiet but important decision about sequencing and focus.

5. **Aisha as a bridge between Careerspan, Zo, and wider AI audiences.**
   - Logan shares that Aisha has tapped her to film a LinkedIn reel; Vrijen immediately sees an opportunity to connect Aisha and Zo.
   - Logan agrees to raise this on her next call, marking a potential future distribution and partnership node.

6. **Reframing Getro as a structurally ideal BD target.**
   - When Ilsa’s mention of Getro comes up, Vrijen realizes (and explains) that a VC job board on its own is not obviously VC‑backable, but a conversational sourcing layer on top of it *is*.
   - This crystallizes Getro as a strategically important partner and elevates the urgency around warm intros to CEO Evan Walden.

7. **Recognition of newfound network strength.**
   - Vrijen contrasts the current state (“shitloads” of meaningful contacts who will vouch for him) with where he was two years ago.
   - This marks an inflection point in how the team can think about BD: from opportunistic outreach to systematic leverage of a real relationship graph.

8. **Light‑hearted but revealing discussion about personal intros (Megan, Netta, David).**
   - The joking around Mormon suitors, FIF Collective, and other personalities showcases how intertwined personal and professional networks have become.
   - It underlines that future motion will often hinge on how Careerspan navigates and nurtures these relationships.
