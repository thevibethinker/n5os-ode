# B03 – Stakeholder Intelligence

## Primary Participants

### Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan – Founder)
- **Role in meeting:** Co‑founder, financial decision‑maker, strategic architect.
- **Stated concerns:**
  - Short‑term cash constraints after paying Ilya and Rahul; worried about near‑term burn while confident a solution exists by the new year.
  - Engineering capacity as a scarce resource; skeptical of investing heavily in billing/paywall infrastructure without clear upside.
  - Risk of “death by a thousand cuts” if user growth is not paired with monetization.
- **Goals and interests:**
  - Keep Careerspan solvent and moving forward through year‑end without panic moves.
  - Preserve the product’s accessibility for job seekers while still capturing some value from a population that clearly sees Careerspan as useful.
  - Build a structurally advantaged business by pairing conversational tooling with high‑leverage distribution (VC job boards like Getro, influencers like Aisha).
- **Decision tendencies:**
  - Pragmatic risk manager (e.g., TaskRabbit vs. DIY plumbing, deferring vendor payments where sensible).
  - Prefers options that preserve future strategic flexibility (e.g., exploring simple paywalls first, then more ambitious subscription models once validated).
  - Comfortable with relationship‑driven BD and playing a long game with network.
- **Leverage points:**
  - Strong and growing network in VC, founders, and talent operators (Jamie Farrell, Eric Lavin, Andy Moat, Jacob Asher, Chris Russell, Netta, Megan Curtis McKenna, etc.).
  - Deep understanding of candidate psychology and the current “everyone is an active job seeker” reality.
  - Credibility with both operators and candidates that can be converted into intros, pilots, and co‑marketing.

### Logan Currie (Careerspan – Co‑founder/Growth & Marketing)
- **Role in meeting:** Co‑founder focused on growth, marketing execution, and candidate experience.
- **Stated concerns:**
  - Covering immediate costs and reducing financial anxiety without over‑complicating the product.
  - Avoiding a situation where they accumulate large numbers of active candidates but no monetization, especially during a hiring lull.
- **Goals and interests:**
  - Find realistic, implementable monetization mechanisms (simple paywalls, usage tiers) that do not overburden the team.
  - Keep the product experience attractive and fair for candidates, while testing willingness to pay.
  - Drive distribution via content (Meta ads, LinkedIn reels, Instagram), storytelling, and partnerships.
- **Decision tendencies:**
  - Bias toward shipping pragmatic, lower‑lift experiments (e.g., a basic paywall) before committing to multi‑layered subscription systems.
  - Open to creative funding/launch mechanisms (Kickstarter‑style prepayments, “Careerspan Select” memberships) but wary of future burden.
  - Thinks in terms of narratives and offers that will resonate with job seekers (e.g., “set it and forget it” subscription for being auto‑matched to jobs).
- **Leverage points:**
  - Strong marketing skill set: ad setup in Meta Business Suite, content creation in Canva, social video (Instagram, LinkedIn).
  - Access to influencers like Aisha with large online audiences.
  - Ability to act as connector and validator in intros (e.g., Zo ↔ Aisha, tapping her Radancy contact for Getro).
