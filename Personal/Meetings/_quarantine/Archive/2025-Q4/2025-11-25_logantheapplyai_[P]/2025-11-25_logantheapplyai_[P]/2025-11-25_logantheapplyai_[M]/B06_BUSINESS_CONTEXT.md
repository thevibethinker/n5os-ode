# B06 – Business Context

## Company State

- **Stage:** Early‑stage, founder‑led company (Careerspan) with a working product, active candidates, and a growing network in the VC/startup ecosystem.
- **Financial posture:**
  - Cash is tight in the short term; recent payments to contractors (Ilya, Rahul) have increased pressure.
  - Vrijen expects a guaranteed funding solution by the new year (family support plus possible business loans), but needs to manage the bridge period carefully.
  - They are exploring timing adjustments (e.g., paying some partners like Megan in January) to smooth cash flow.

## Market Conditions

- **Demand side (candidates):**
  - Per Alice’s framing, there is effectively no “passive job seeker” anymore; nearly everyone is anxious and actively open to new roles.
  - This makes the idea of a standing subscription—where Careerspan continuously checks and applies candidates to relevant roles—structurally compelling.

- **Supply side (employers):**
  - Many companies pause hiring and decision‑making around the winter holiday period, leading to slower cycles and fewer immediate closes.
  - This seasonal lull pushes Careerspan to consider shifting some focus toward candidate‑side monetization and engagement, rather than depending solely on employer contracts.

## Product & Monetization Landscape

- **Current product:**
  - A candidate‑centric experience built around stories, curated applications, and a conversational layer that helps job seekers present themselves effectively.
  - Some infrastructure already exists for matching candidates to roles and managing applications, but there is no usage‑based billing or paywall today.

- **Monetization options under discussion:**
  1. **Simple paywall on existing usage**
     - Examples: limited free stories or applications, then a paid tier.
     - Pros: relatively straightforward; aligns with current workflow; could be tested quickly.
     - Cons: engineering effort for billing/entitlements; risk of friction for early‑stage growth.

  2. **“Careerspan Select” / auto‑apply subscription**
     - Monthly subscription where candidates pay to be automatically evaluated against incoming jobs and put at the front of the queue.
     - Clear, emotionally resonant pitch for anxious job seekers who want set‑and‑forget progress.
     - Operationally heavier: requires payments, matching logic, and either manual or automated execution loops.

  3. **Pre‑payment / Kickstarter‑style campaign**
     - Collect money upfront for a year‑long membership or future offering using third‑party payment tools (Buy Me a Coffee, Kickstarter, etc.) rather than building billing into the product.
     - Pros: raises cash quickly without large engineering investment.
     - Cons: creates a future delivery burden and long‑lived expectations; both founders are cautious about over‑promising.

## Distribution & Ecosystem Positioning

- **Influencer and content channels:**
  - Logan is actively building content and ad infrastructure: Meta ads, Canva sales collateral, Instagram videos, and a LinkedIn reel in collaboration with Aisha.
  - Aisha’s audience is potentially a high‑leverage channel for both Careerspan and Zo, especially for AI‑curious, career‑oriented viewers.

- **VC and platform relationships:**
  - Getro (VC job board) is seen as a near‑ideal platform partner: it sits where talent and portfolios intersect, and Careerspan’s conversational sourcing could make it more powerful.
  - The team is better positioned than two years ago, with multiple credible connectors into people like Getro’s CEO, Evan Walden.

## Strategic Tension

- **Short‑term:** Survive the next few months, cover key costs, and avoid desperate product changes driven purely by cash crunch.
- **Medium‑term:** Validate that candidates will pay (through low‑lift experiments like a simple paywall) while continuing to grow the user base and relationship graph.
- **Long‑term:** Build a defensible, subscription‑like business where Careerspan continuously works in the background for job seekers and plugs into high‑leverage ecosystems (VC job boards, influencer communities, AI platforms like Zo).
