# B03 – Decisions

1. **Prioritize exploring a simple paywall before heavy subscription build‑out.**
   - Logan and Vrijen agree that a lightweight, pragmatic paywall on existing functionality (e.g., limited free usage, then paid) is the first monetization lever to investigate.
   - The more ambitious “Careerspan Select” / auto‑apply subscription model remains attractive but is explicitly treated as something to *work toward*, not an immediate build.

2. **Treat candidate‑side monetization as a core strategic pillar.**
   - Both accept Alice’s framing that there are effectively no truly passive job seekers anymore and that candidates will pay *something* for real value.
   - As a result, they implicitly commit to designing revenue experiments that focus on candidates rather than only on employers.

3. **Near‑term financial bridge will rely on external funds plus tactical levers, not drastic product changes.**
   - Vrijen confirms there is a guaranteed solution in place by the new year (family support + potential business loans), and the shared stance is to *bridge to that point* without over‑reacting.
   - They are open to timing‑based shifts like asking Megan to accept payment in January, but there is no decision to radically alter product or pricing immediately just to plug a short‑term gap.

4. **Use warm intros and existing network as the primary path into Getro and similar targets.**
   - They decide that direct outreach to Getro’s CEO should come via trusted connectors (e.g., Andy, Jacob, Radancy contact, others) rather than cold approaches.
   - The group converges on the idea that Careerspan’s position—as a conversational sourcing layer on top of VC job board infrastructure—makes this a strategically important relationship to pursue.

5. **Leverage Aisha’s platform for distribution and potential Zo collaboration.**
   - Logan agrees to raise the idea of connecting Aisha with Zo on her upcoming call, positioning this as an exploratory step toward co‑marketing or events (e.g., AI‑influencer dinner in NYC).
   - Implicit decision: treat Aisha’s audience as a high‑value channel worth cultivating through thoughtful collaboration, not just one‑off content.

> Note: Many other points discussed (e.g., Kickstarter‑style prepayments, detailed subscription mechanics) remain *open explorations* rather than concrete decisions.
