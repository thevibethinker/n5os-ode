# Decisions – Rice x Careerspan

> Note: This meeting produced directional agreements rather than formal contracts. Items below capture what participants implicitly/explicitly aligned on.

## 1. Product–Need Fit
- **Agreed:** Careerspan’s approach (story‑based reflection, skill signalling, AI‑assisted analysis) aligns well with SEE/CSE goals and the needs of Rice engineering students and early‑career alums.
- **Implication:** It is worth investing further time in pilots and distribution experiments rather than treating this as a one‑off demo.

## 2. Initial Distribution Strategy
- **Agreed:** The lowest‑friction next step is to **give Careerspan directly to a subset of Rice students and SEE/CSE participants** via promo codes / links, not to wait for a full university‑wide vendor process.
- **Framing:** "Let the traction speak for itself" – if students and alums derive value, that data and those stories can later support broader institutional adoption.

## 3. Focus on Early‑Career & Equity Use Cases
- **Agreed:** Careerspan is particularly relevant for:
  - Early‑career alums and SEE/CSE participants (especially first‑gen / lower‑income students lacking networks).
  - Undergrads doing work‑based learning (internships, projects) who need help converting experience into narratives and interview readiness.
- **Implication:** Pilots and communications should center these groups and their specific challenges.

## 4. Long‑Term Vision: Rice ↔ Careerspan Hiring Loop
- **Directional agreement:** There is a promising future state where:
  - Rice‑affiliated companies and alumni post roles into Careerspan.
  - Rice students/alums who have done story work can one‑click apply.
  - The system intelligently routes roles to the best‑fit Rice candidates (high‑signal email distribution, high open rates).
- **Status:** Concept approved in principle as "very interesting"; requires further design, but no objections raised.

## 5. Evidence & Pilot Orientation
- **Agreed:** Any deeper integration should be backed by **pilot data and concrete stories**, not just theory.
  - Examples: before/during/after SEE/CSE pilots; case studies akin to UMass Eisenberg.
- **Implication:** Next steps should include designing at least one small, well‑instrumented pilot with SEE/CSE or a comparable cohort.

## 6. Ownership of Follow‑Up
- **Agreed:**
  - **V/Logan** own sending a refreshed Rice promo link, a concise demo asset, and a written proposal for a two‑pronged plan (direct distribution + longer‑term job‑distribution channel).
  - **Felix/Jim** own socializing these materials within REA/SEE and deciding how to roll them out to target students and alums.
