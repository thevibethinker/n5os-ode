# Stakeholder Intelligence – Rice x Careerspan

## Overview
Key people in this meeting and how they show up in relation to Careerspan, Rice students, and the alumni ecosystem.

---

## Felix Campos – Rice Engineering Alumni (REA) Board President

**Role / context**
- President of Rice Engineering Alumni board; co‑leads the Summer Engineering Experience (SEE/CSE) program with Jim.
- Point person who originally engaged with Careerspan and has already advocated internally.

**Goals & priorities**
- Shift SEE/CSE from "we find internships for students" to "we teach students to find and win their own opportunities" ("teach them to fish").
- Equip first‑gen / lower‑income engineering students with tools for self‑advocacy, reflection, and interview preparation.
- Increase alumni engagement by offering meaningful value (not just events / newsletters).
- Maintain credibility with Rice administration and alumni by being selective about any tools he champions.

**Motivations**
- Deep emotional investment in SEE/CSE; this program is why he joined the alumni org.
- Strong belief that reflection, introspection, and connecting experience to value are missing from many students’ toolkits.
- Takes pride in having already tried to open institutional doors for Careerspan.

**Concerns / constraints**
- Institutional drag: Career Development and alumni vendor programs are understaffed or unfilled; he has limited leverage over their timelines.
- Unclear precedent: REA has not previously "promoted a vendor" to alumni; unsure what the right mechanism and governance should be.
- Wants to avoid recommending a tool in a way that creates obligations REA cannot sustain.

**Receptivity to Careerspan**
- Very high. Describes Careerspan as a strong fit for reflection and connecting dots between experiences and roles.
- Validates the product with Rhett’s positive feedback and his own conversations.

**Leverage points**
- Position as board president and co‑owner of SEE/CSE strategy.
- Existing champions (Rhett, students who have used Careerspan successfully).
- Can pilot quietly with SEE/CSE participants and use data and stories to influence the dean, Career Services, and the broader alumni board.

---

## Jim Pyke – REA Leader, Rice + HBS Alum

**Role / context**
- Rice Engineering alumnus and HBS graduate; co‑leads SEE/CSE with Felix.
- Regularly in conversation with the dean of Engineering & Computing.

**Goals & priorities**
- Answer the dean’s persistent question: "What are my graduates’ job prospects?" with real, scalable solutions.
- Support SEE/CSE students and early‑career alums through volatile job markets (e.g., COVID recession, AI disruption).
- Close the opportunity gap between well‑networked students and first‑gen / low‑income students who lack white‑collar connections.

**Motivations**
- Strong sense of responsibility to SEE/CSE participants he has invested time in.
- Deep interest in how AI can be used constructively (mock interviews, question‑asking, resume tailoring) rather than purely as a threat.

**Concerns / constraints**
- Skeptical of naive "AI does everything" narratives; wants clear understanding of how Careerspan uses AI (stack, value add, guardrails).
- Sensitive to resume formatting / output‑quality issues; doesn’t want tools that produce unusable documents.
- Needs a story he can confidently take to the dean and other stakeholders about why this is a good, responsible move.

**Receptivity to Careerspan**
- Positive and intellectually engaged; asks sharp questions about business model, AI architecture, and differentiation.
- Clearly sees alignment with SEE/CSE’s goals (interview prep, storytelling, self‑advocacy).

**Leverage points**
- Access to the dean; acts as a trusted interpreter of early‑career risk and opportunity.
- Networks with high‑influence alumni (e.g., HBS peers) and Rice‑affiliated employers.
- Can frame Careerspan as a proactive response to AI‑driven disruption, not just another GenAI gadget.

## Logan Currie – Careerspan Co‑Founder (Learning & Design)

**Role / context**
- Co‑founder of Careerspan; learning‑design and metacognition lead.
- Bridges pedagogy, product design, and user experience.

**Goals & priorities**
- Build a tool that genuinely improves self‑efficacy and story quality, not just surface‑level resume rewriting.
- Make the experience accessible to:
  - Students who are uncomfortable writing in English.
  - First‑gen / international students with complex stories.
- Design experiences that create durable behavior change (regular reflection, ongoing story logging).

**Motivations**
- Nerdy, research‑driven orientation to learning science (metacognition, self‑efficacy, skill signalling).
- Values concrete pilots and before/during/after data to prove impact.

**Concerns / constraints**
- Doesn’t want Careerspan to become "just another AI resume tool" that strips away specificity.
- Cautious about over‑promising—strong preference for pilots where results can be measured and iterated.

**Receptivity to partnership**
- Highly enthusiastic about co‑designing pilots with SEE/CSE.
- Sees Rice as an excellent testbed for longitudinal work‑efficacy → academic/career‑efficacy studies.

**Leverage points**
- Can design curriculum‑adjacent uses (course assignments, internship reflections, etc.).
- Provides credible learning‑science narrative that will resonate with deans, faculty, and grant funders.

## V – Careerspan Founder / CEO

**Role / context**
- Founder of Careerspan; primary storyteller and relationship owner for institutional partnerships.

**Goals & priorities**
- Position Careerspan as the most pro‑candidate AI hiring solution.
- Build long‑term engagement loops where alumni return monthly to log stories and run quick job checks.
- Create a Rice‑specific hiring loop: Rice alums and companies sourcing Rice candidates via Careerspan.

**Motivations**
- Deeply values fairness in hiring and the ability of good questions to unlock under‑signalled talent.
- Wants to prove value to Felix and Jim first, then earn the right to broader institutional adoption.

**Concerns / constraints**
- Realistic about university slowness and institutional risk aversion.
- Aware that Careerspan’s success with Rice depends on building trust and delivering visible wins quickly (e.g., SEE/CSE outcomes, alum stories).

**Leverage points**
- Strong narrative and examples from other communities (higher‑ed professional networks, tech operator groups).
- Can bring in real job inventory and employer relationships to make the value proposition concrete.

## Institutional Stakeholder – Rice Engineering Alumni (REA) & SEE/CSE Program

**Mandate**
- Support Rice engineering students and alums in their early career journeys.
- Serve as a bridge between students, alumni, employers, and the School of Engineering & Computing.

**Incentives**
- Demonstrate to parents and the dean that Rice engineering graduates are employable in a tough, AI‑shaped market.
- Show tangible, differentiated support for first‑gen and lower‑income students.
- Avoid being swamped by manual internship matchmaking; move toward scalable self‑advocacy.

**Fit with Careerspan**
- Strong: Careerspan can be positioned as the "engine" behind SEE/CSE storytelling, mock interviews, and job targeting.
- Offers a credible story to the dean about modernizing career preparation without over‑promising on what Career Services can deliver.
