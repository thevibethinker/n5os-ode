# Key Moments – Rice x Careerspan

## 1. Re‑anchoring the Relationship
- Early in the call, Felix shares that he **already reached out** to Rice’s Center for Career Development and Development & Alumni Relations on Careerspan’s behalf.
- This signals that he is not just curious; he has already spent political capital trying to make introductions.

## 2. V’s Framing of the Pivot
- V explains that Careerspan **moved from paid candidate coaching to employer‑funded hiring filters** so they wouldn’t need to charge job seekers.
- Key line: the same tools that help candidates understand whether they fit a job also help employers understand if the candidate is a fit "in the other direction."
- This reframes Careerspan from "AI resume helper" to a bi‑directional matching engine.

## 3. Logan on Skill Signalling as the Meta‑Skill
- Logan articulates that the real differentiator in the next 5–10 years is **skill signalling**, not just work‑based learning.
- Quote (paraphrased): work‑based learning will be table stakes; the question is how clearly you talk about and signal your work.
- This resonates strongly with Jim and Felix’s concerns about their students’ communication and storytelling gaps.

## 4. Jim’s Equity & Access Story
- Jim shares the contrast between SEE/CSE students and a peer’s child who had internships every summer since freshman year of high school.
- He explicitly asks how to help students **"close that gap"** in a world where AI is changing hiring.
- This frames Careerspan as a potential *equity* tool, not just a convenience.

## 5. The "Slower and More Expensive than AI" Quote
- Logan relays an MIT robotics grad student’s line: **"How can I help my students be employable when they’re slower and more expensive than AI?"**
- The group treats this as an uncomfortable but clarifying statement about the stakes for early‑career professionals.

## 6. V on Human‑in‑the‑Loop and Honesty About AI
- When pressed about the AI stack, V:
  - Admits Careerspan uses off‑the‑shelf models and that most vendors claiming otherwise are "frankly lying."
  - Emphasizes the importance of a human‑in‑the‑loop design and "cognitive friction" so candidates consciously choose what to put on their resumes.
- This candor builds trust and differentiates Careerspan from hype‑driven tools.

## 7. Vision of a Rice ↔ Careerspan Hiring Loop
- V paints a picture where:
  - Rice alums and companies can drop a link into their channels.
  - Rice candidates who have logged stories can one‑click apply.
  - Emails go out only to best‑fit Rice candidates with very high open rates.
- Jim explicitly connects this to past moments when the dean asked what REA can do in recessions, framing this as a potential future answer.

## 8. Closing Commitments
- V proposes sending a **new Rice‑specific link with more seats** and a follow‑up outlining a two‑pronged plan.
- Felix and Jim agree to **distribute the link** to selected students/SEE participants and to consider broader alumni communication.
- The meeting ends on a note of mutual appreciation and intent to keep building together.
