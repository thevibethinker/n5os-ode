The call started with catching-up energy—muted mic issues, greetings, and some light banter about long days and getting back to routine. Rochel explained that she has been heavily leaning on LinkedIn, running two resumes (Israel vs. New York) to ensure geography-sensitive optics, and had already secured an interview the following Monday. Vrijen asked whether she planned to stay in Israel, and Rochel said she intends to travel back and forth through mid-2025 before resettling in New York despite missing in-person work.

Vrijen pivoted to networking support: he flagged Braze (good culture, remote-friendly), offered to drop her résumé to a contact there, and recommended that she get comfortable sharing the deck/pitch for that company and others. He also suggested keeping tabs on design-focused teams—he called out a stylish founder-led shop that values UX and animation, and he emphasized the value of referrals (McKinsey especially has been referral-friendly). Rochel appreciated the ideas and mentioned a Forerunner VC design director who is actively hiring; she had already sent a cold connect and was excited that the director responded with interest.

The conversation then shifted to resume strategy. Vrijen recommended trimming anything older than 2022–2023, adding more bullet points for the most recent experiences, and showing progression within organizations rather than a long laundry list. He encouraged her to keep newer initiatives (e.g., group feedback facilitator work) and to run the resume through the Careerspan conversion metrics and ATS checks. Rochel agreed to polish her stories and use Careerspan to map the JD she was prepping for.

The call degenerated into wardrobe talk, Black Friday planning, and praise for Rochel’s product portfolio—which Vrijen said convinced him she was the person he was looking for. He also shared optimism about Careerspan’s ability to engage new users and noted that selling the product felt easier than convincing investors. They closed with a reminder that she could use CareerSpan to reflect on next roles, to share updates, and to keep him posted on interviews and referrals.
