# B21 – Key Moments

1. **Reframing the purpose of the call ("not raising yet").**
   - Alexis clarifies they are **not looking to raise immediately**, but want to understand the processes and pitfalls around fundraising before they need capital.
   - This anchors the conversation in **strategic preparation** rather than deal-making.

2. **Gas-stove incident and building reliability.**
   - V’s stove appears to malfunction mid-call, sparking real safety concern and an emergency maintenance request.
   - The issue is resolved quickly; V notes he pays a premium for an apartment where issues are fixed fast and thoroughly.
   - Subtext: V values **reliability and responsiveness**, mirroring his views on good investors and tools.

3. **Deep dive on SAFEs vs. priced rounds vs. convertible notes.**
   - V walks Alexis through:
     - Why SAFEs are structurally attractive in the US.
     - How stacking incompatible SAFEs can blindside founders with unexpected dilution.
     - Why Indian capital forced a priced round and heavy legal fees in his own journey.
   - This segment reframes instruments from jargon into **concrete risks and tradeoffs**.

4. **Misha’s father’s quote on money.**
   - Alexis shares a line from Misha’s father: *"Money is a whore; she comes and goes, she has no loyalty. Anyone you take investment from should offer value beyond that whore."*
   - This becomes a **guiding principle** for which investors they will even consider.

5. **Time-compressed raise playbook.**
   - V outlines a specific, high-leverage strategy:
     - Spend months politely saying "we’re not raising yet" while building a list of interested investors.
     - Open a **2-week window** after a strong revenue/product quarter and force in-or-out decisions.
   - This gives Alexis & Misha a **concrete operating system** for their first raise.

6. **Valuation as forward obligation, not backward scorecard.**
   - V emphasizes that the valuation you pick is the **future you’re committing to deliver**, not a trophy for past work.
   - He walks through simple revenue × multiple logic and notes how an over-ambitious cap can backfire in the next round.

7. **Candid post-mortem on V’s own raise.**
   - V recounts:
     - Taking a $1M check from a family friend in India.
     - The stress that followed due to unclear deployment plans and lack of investor diversification.
     - How later fundraising attempts failed to clearly explain **how investors would get their money back**.
   - This becomes a cautionary tale that Alexis & Misha explicitly want to learn from.

8. **Reframing YC and accelerators.**
   - Alexis voices skepticism about YC/Antler’s practical value for factory buyers.
   - V offers a nuanced view: YC won’t deliver factories, but may deliver **brand, network, and career de-risking**.
   - They land on a stance of **"use only if strategically necessary"**, not by default.

9. **Two-tier investor strategy (non‑US angels now, US VCs later).**
   - V suggests using **non‑US, high-conviction angels** to get early capital at favorable terms.
   - US institutional capital becomes a **later-stage amplifier**, once traction and valuation justify it.

10. **Human Truths AI opportunity surfaces.**
    - Alexis connects dots between Careerspan’s interviewing AI and **Human Truths AI** (Gates-funded, tech-constrained).
    - They see a mutually beneficial path where Careerspan’s engine accelerates Human Truths’ delivery while providing Careerspan with revenue and visibility.

11. **Zo as personal AI computer, not chatbot.**
    - V explains how Zo has effectively replaced Notion, Zapier/n8n, and multiple AI subscriptions for him.
    - Frames Zo as the place Alexis & Misha could eventually **build their CRM and operations tooling**, not just another app.

12. **Closing commitments.**
    - V commits to:
      - Future intros to **XRC Ventures (Darshan)** and **RVC Capital**.
      - Exploring a collaboration with **Human Truths AI** once introduced.
    - Alexis commits to:
      - Formalizing their pipeline and lead systems.
      - Continuing to share letters and updates with V.
