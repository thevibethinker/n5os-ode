# Decisions Made

## Strategic Direction Decisions

### Decision 1: Careerspan Product Review & Testing
**Owner**: Coral  
**Decision**: Test and provide "brutally honest review" of Careerspan product  
**Rationale**: Coral has direct experience as career advisor and understands the space; authentic feedback from someone with domain expertise will be more valuable than generic testing  
**Next Step**: Vrijen to provide trial code/access; Coral to use product and share detailed feedback

### Decision 2: FIU Partnership Introduction
**Owner**: Coral with Vrijen support  
**Decision**: Coral will introduce Vrijen/Careerspan to Ruth (FIU Career Director)  
**Rationale**: FIU is 50k-student institution with current Vmock contract; Careerspan can offer better product + 50% cost savings; Ruth is decision-maker with direct authority; Coral has existing relationship  
**Timeline**: To be followed up; Vrijen to provide supporting materials  
**Success Criteria**: Ruth meeting scheduled and product evaluation initiated

### Decision 3: TikTok Creator Collaboration
**Owner**: Vrijen with Logan (Careerspan co-founder)  
**Decision**: Connect Coral with Logan for potential TikTok content collaboration  
**Rationale**: Logan has proven TikTok growth (5k followers in 2-3 months) and career content expertise; Coral is growing creator in same space; mutual audience and philosophy alignment  
**Medium**: Vrijen to add both to group chat  
**Potential Output**: Joint career content for TikTok; cross-promotion; brand partnership

### Decision 4: Community Monetization Model Implementation
**Owner**: Coral (with Careerspan as backend)  
**Decision**: Use Careerspan as recruiting/monetization engine for Coral's community  
**Structure**:
- Coral promotes Careerspan to her community/followers (with exclusive promo code)
- Community members use Careerspan to optimize careers
- When Coral facilitates job placements through Careerspan, she earns recruiting kickback ($20-40k per placement typically)
- Careerspan handles candidate surface/matching; Coral handles community relationship
**Rationale**: Low-effort revenue stream leveraging existing trust; provides tangible benefit to Coral's community (free quality tool); aligns with mutual philosophy of helping people first  
**Success Criteria**: Community adopts Careerspan; placements begin occurring

### Decision 5: Brand/Positioning Blurb
**Owner**: Vrijen  
**Decision**: Create language/positioning blurb for Careerspan  
**Rationale**: Make it easier for Coral to communicate Careerspan value to her community and to Ruth/FIU  
**Deliverable**: Written positioning document (to be sent post-call)

### Decision 6: Philosophy Commitment
**Owner**: Both parties  
**Decision**: Maintain authenticity and user-first approach in all partnership activities  
**Rationale**: Both parties prioritize trust over aggressive monetization; rushing or cutting corners would undermine the fundamental value proposition  
**Implications**: 
- Coral won't push Careerspan harder than she genuinely believes in it
- Vrijen won't pressure Coral for aggressive promotion
- Any collaboration must genuinely serve the community

## Product/Feature Decisions

### Decision 7: No Immediate Monetization of Coral's Product
**Owner**: Coral (affirmed by Vrijen's coaching)  
**Decision**: Focus on product quality and user feedback before monetizing Fix Your Feed  
**Rationale**: 
- Product not yet at sustainable scale (hosting costs nominal; ~$5/month on Railway)
- Monetization premature when feature requests signal strong product-market fit
- Attention/trust is the asset; monetization comes after scale/stability
- Vrijen reinforced advisor guidance: "focus on making it better based on what people say, then think about monetizing"
**Timeline**: Revisit monetization strategy after reaching 30k+ users (vs. current 3k)
