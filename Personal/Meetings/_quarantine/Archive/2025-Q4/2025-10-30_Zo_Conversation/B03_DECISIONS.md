# Strategic Decisions

## Launch Video Format & Approach
**Decision:** Focus on exploring alternative launch video strategies rather than pursuing the traditional "knock it out of the park" browser company approach.

**Context:** Discussion centered on the tension between Ben's preference for a classic, heavily-produced launch video versus an alternative marketing-first philosophy. The decision was to defer the format choice while prioritizing outbound work.

**Rationale:** Marketing effectiveness relies on catalysts and hype generation rather than cold-turkey production value. Generic launch videos often consume substantial resources without guaranteed returns.

---

## Recipe Economy as Core GTM Model
**Decision:** Position Zo's go-to-market strategy around a dual-sided recipe economy—supply side (recipe creation incentives) paired with demand side (founder engagement and events).

**Context:** Rather than relying purely on features to drive adoption, the strategy emphasizes generating quality recipes and building demonstrated demand for them simultaneously.

**Rationale:** Current architecture supports recipes but lacks clarity on (1) which recipes users want and (2) scalable incentive mechanisms for recipe creation. Founder outreach validates demand while hackathons and student incentives can fuel supply.

---

## Event vs. Hackathon Priority
**Decision:** Commit to executing the November 5th event as a priority, with a secondary evaluation of whether to add hackathons with students or continue focusing on founder touch points.

**Context:** The November 5th event represents a validated demand signal and opportunity to stress-test the recipe economy model. Hackathons emerged as a potential complement but need prioritization clarity.

**Rationale:** Growing upper-funnel users is the immediate priority over retention optimization. A successful November 5th event de-risks the foundational go-to-market model.

---

## Founder Outreach & Relationship Cultivation Strategy
**Decision:** Activate targeted founder outreach focused on high-profile individuals (Bailey Plumfeet, Cal.com founder) to serve as advocacy nodes and early validators.

**Context:** These founders have demonstrated interest in Zo's API integration capabilities and represent significant social reach. The strategy emphasizes slow cultivation rather than transactional engagement.

**Rationale:** Founders combine technical credibility, social influence, and distribution channels. Their adoption and positive positioning can significantly reduce customer acquisition friction for the broader market.

---

## Platform Positioning: Principles Over Workflows
**Decision:** Emphasize Zo's differentiation as a principles-based (rather than workflow-based) automation platform when communicating value.

**Context:** Competitive tools like Zapier and N8N force users into rigid, volume-heavy workflow construction. Zo's flexibility allows principle-driven operation where the system adapts contextually.

**Rationale:** This distinction resonates with sophisticated users and addresses a key pain point: the cognitive burden and maintenance cost of managing hundreds of interconnected workflows. It also provides a clearer differentiation story vs. N8N/Zapier.

---

## N8N Community Engagement as Growth Channel
**Decision:** Explore hosting a Discord community session with the N8N community to position Zo as a complementary platform rather than a direct replacement.

**Context:** N8N users are highly technical and hacker-oriented—a better fit for Zo's current positioning than Zapier users. The openness of N8N's codebase suggests receptiveness to ecosystem approaches.

**Rationale:** Reduced switching costs and positioning Zo as an enhancement to N8N workflows rather than a disruptor could significantly ease adoption among this already-technical audience segment.

---

## Distribution Channel Strategy for November 5th Event
**Decision:** Leverage multiple founder networks and community channels to distribute event awareness, including Cherry Tree community (Tim), direct founder networks, and potential LinkedIn/social outreach.

**Context:** Event success depends on reaching relevant founder audiences. Multiple distribution vectors improve reach and attendee quality.

**Rationale:** Organic community channels (Cherry Tree, Tim's network) have higher credibility and relevance than cold outreach. Dense founder networks create multiplier effects through personal recommendations.
