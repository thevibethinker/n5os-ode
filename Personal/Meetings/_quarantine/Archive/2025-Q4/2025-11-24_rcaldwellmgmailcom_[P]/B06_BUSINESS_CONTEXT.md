# B06_BUSINESS_CONTEXT

- **Product stage:** Careerspan is an early‑stage hiring product focused on turning noisy inbound interest into high‑signal, curated candidates for employers who value deeper context over raw volume. The system decomposes roles into skills, scores candidates, and produces structured narratives for hiring managers.
- **Target customer:** Modern hiring managers and founders (often in tech) who have hired before, care about the candidate experience, and are willing to adopt a higher‑friction but higher‑signal workflow instead of traditional job boards or ATS forms.
- **Differentiation vs job boards:** Unlike LinkedIn or generic job boards, Careerspan emphasizes structured storytelling, candidate agency, and curated matches. Employers receive actionable intelligence (skills fit, deal‑breakers, relative strengths) instead of unstructured resumes.
- **Risk / objection:** Because Careerspan requires candidates to invest in a new type of profile that can’t simply be copy‑pasted from other tools, employers may worry about losing strong candidates who are not yet willing to do that extra work—especially while Careerspan is still building market familiarity.
- **Opportunity:** Rina’s background as both a hiring manager and growth leader validates that, if executed well, Careerspan could become the primary intake channel for employers who want a humane, high‑signal hiring experience—and her feedback offers concrete levers for de‑risking adoption (filters, mid‑funnel education, and product‑embedded reassurance).
