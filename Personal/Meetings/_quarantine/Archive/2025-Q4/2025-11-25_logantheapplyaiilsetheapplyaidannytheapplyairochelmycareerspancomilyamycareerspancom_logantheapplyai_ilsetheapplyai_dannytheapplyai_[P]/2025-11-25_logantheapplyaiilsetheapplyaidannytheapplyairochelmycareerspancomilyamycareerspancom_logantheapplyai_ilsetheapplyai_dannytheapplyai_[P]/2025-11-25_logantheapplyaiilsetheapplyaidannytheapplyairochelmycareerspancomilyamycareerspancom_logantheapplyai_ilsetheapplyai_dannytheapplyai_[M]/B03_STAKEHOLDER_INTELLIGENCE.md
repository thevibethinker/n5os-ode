# B03 – Stakeholder Intelligence

## Vrijen Attawar (V) – Founder / CEO
- **Role & focus**: Overall product and company direction; responsible for making trade‑offs between experimentation, ethics, and commercial traction.
- **Key interests**:
  - Owning critical communication channels (e.g., email via SendGrid) rather than outsourcing them to employers or opaque systems.
  - Turning demos into a predictable revenue engine ("numbers game" framing; 10+ demos every ~2 weeks with ~1/3 conversion).
  - Protecting candidate experience and fairness while still giving employers meaningful control over filters.
- **Beliefs / mental models**:
  - Prefers simple, controllable systems over prematurely complex agent ecosystems, unless agents clearly reduce legwork and surface real insight.
  - Understands that employers will pursue their own selection preferences; sees Careerspan’s role as shaping defaults and guardrails rather than dictating morality.
  - Thinks in analogies and long‑term dynamics (e.g., community, data moats, dopamine, consumer vs B2B behavior).
- **Skepticism / concerns**:
  - Over‑engineering ("not to over‑solutionize") when a simple trigger→email pipeline will do.
  - Scoring systems that are too generous on transferability and misrepresent true readiness to employers.
- **Leverage points**:
  - Responds well to concrete numbers (demo counts, conversion rates, cost per action) and to narratives that connect UX, ethics, and revenue.
  - Likely to back initiatives that strengthen Careerspan’s long‑term data advantage and community value while remaining operationally realistic.

## Ilse Funkhouser – Product / Engineering
- **Role & focus**: Designs and maintains the scoring, vibe check, and analysis systems; deeply involved in technical trade‑offs and cost structure.
- **Key interests**:
  - Keeping large‑scale analyses financially sustainable (awareness that vibe checks cost 8–10¢ each and run in volume).
  - Building robust, interpretable scoring logic that distinguishes direct vs transferable experience without collapsing into simple thresholds.
  - Modernizing the broader conversation system as a near‑term major initiative.
- **Beliefs / mental models**:
  - Values explicit pipelines: preference filters → quick checks → vibe checks → full analysis, with each stage adding cost and precision.
  - Sees scoring as inherently multi‑dimensional and context‑dependent; wary of naive linear scales.
  - Appreciates that transferability is powerful but must be bounded to avoid overstating a candidate’s readiness.
- **Skepticism / concerns**:
  - Quick parameter tweaks (e.g., threshold shifts) as a substitute for the deeper architecture work that’s actually required.
  - Using vibe checks as a blunt filter once candidates have many stories, given observed mis‑alignment between vibe and true scores.
- **Leverage points**:
  - Motivated by clearly framed technical problems with real user impact (e.g., unfair filtering, misleading scores, wasted compute).
  - Responsive to well‑scoped redesign proposals that respect her time and the current priority stack.

## Rochel Polter – CX / Marketing / Product
- **Role & focus**: Bridges product, marketing, and candidate experience; often voices the user’s perspective (especially candidates).
- **Key interests**:
  - Reducing friction for candidates, especially around long‑running flows like full analysis and vibe checking.
  - Using notifications and UX copy to set expectations and turn time investment into a perceived advantage, not a burden.
  - Giving employers meaningful configuration options (e.g., hard vs transferable skill ratios) while keeping defaults humane and inclusive.
- **Beliefs / mental models**:
  - Thinks in terms of flows and funnels (e.g., landing page → CTA → job board/demo) and is highly sensitive to breaks in those flows.
  - Sees messaging and defaults as vehicles for embedding ethics and company values.
- **Skepticism / concerns**:
  - Sluggish, opaque experiences where users wait without feedback.
  - Employer configuration that quietly nudges toward hyper‑elitist filters without any counter‑messaging.
- **Leverage points**:
  - Very responsive to concrete UX improvements (notifications, trackers, clearer CTAs) that she can see and feel herself.
  - A strong partner for testing copy, landing pages, and end‑to‑end flows.

## Ilya Kucherenko – Marketing / Strategy / Copy
- **Role & focus**: Owns much of the narrative, ad concepts, and campaign strategy; also contributes product framing and community thinking.
- **Key interests**:
  - Building experiments rather than hypotheticals: small, well‑guarded campaigns to see real user behavior.
  - Leveraging AI agents where they genuinely reduce manual work and produce digestible summary artifacts.
  - Creating strong, modular messaging systems (slide decks, landing page components, ad variants) that can be remixed and tested.
- **Beliefs / mental models**:
  - Sees agents as a powerful hammer and is explicit about that bias; envisions multi‑sided agents connecting candidates, employers, and Careerspan.
  - Thinks in platform/moat terms: data access, anti‑scraping, and paywalled visibility tiers analogous to LinkedIn and games (XP boosts).
  - Comfortable with behavioral design and nudging, as long as outcomes serve both users and the business.
- **Skepticism / concerns**:
  - Building products based solely on internal intuition without sufficient testing.
  - Under‑leveraging the metadata Careerspan will accumulate over time.
- **Leverage points**:
  - Highly engaged by ideas that combine data, experimentation, and storytelling.
  - Ideal owner for copy‑heavy assets such as candidate time‑commitment pages and campaign concepts.
