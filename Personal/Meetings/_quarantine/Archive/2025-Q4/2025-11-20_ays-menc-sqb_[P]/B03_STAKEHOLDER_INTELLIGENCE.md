**Ilse Funkhouser (Product/Customer Success lead, demo host)**
- *Role & context:* Guides demos for high-intent prospects, narrates the candidate stories, and ensures explainability while keeping the experience live inside the app.
- *Key interests:* Keeping the three-act structure crisp, showing how candidate stories surface real transferability, preventing the demo from devolving into dense Google Docs, and feeding curveball answers (pricing, methodology, one-pagers) that reassure buyers.
- *Skepticism / risk:* Concerned about losing the story flow if too much explanatory material is presented too early or if the candidate segment gets too much airtime before they understand the business problem.
- *Leverage:* Deep knowledge of the narrative section, direct line to users (e.g., Martin Chen, Donatella) who produce stories, and a strong feel for pacing/timing.

**Logan Currie (Sales & community champion)**
- *Role & context:* Runs demos, coaches Ilse, and manages investor and partner relationships (e.g., Silicon Valley Gag company, Cornell prospect, Aqua Hire lead).
- *Key interests:* Ending demos on the “top candidates” payoff, using diagnostic upfront questions, amplifying the recruiter-as-a-service framing, and reinforcing the narrative that Careerspan saves time and avoids ATS drudgery.
- *Skepticism / risk:* Sensitive to legal compliance around ranking and to keeping the call within 30 minutes—he wants to avoid the nightmare scenario of running out of time after a late start.
- *Leverage:* Salescraft to “poke” executives with worry-questions, ability to ground the conversation in actual customer stories (e.g., Autodesk cautionary tale), and access to referral champions (Gunderson, Professor Kniffin).

**Ilya Kucherenko (Engineering / product strategy)**
- *Role & context:* Provides technical guardrails, answers compliance questions, and produces collateral (one-pager, appendices) for demos.
- *Key interests:* Ensuring the demo explains how the assessment criteria/AI breakdown works, delivering the promised PDF immediately after the call, and guiding Ilse/Logan through curveball questions so they sound confident about methodology and pricing.
- *Skepticism / risk:* Worried about legal exposure from stack ranking, wants to keep the candidate score hidden from employers while still giving them explainability, and wants the team to maintain momentum without chasing minor distractions.
- *Leverage:* Confidence in the methodology (assessment criteria, story scoring, deal-breakers), ownership of compliance roadmap, and ability to build collateral and pricing narratives quickly.
