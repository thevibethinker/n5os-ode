## Purpose and Scope

This meeting was a rapid-fire internal alignment session focused on refining the Careerspan product demo structure and delivery strategy ahead of customer presentations. The session was triggered by recent competitive intelligence from a Greenhouse demo and aimed to lock in final decisions on demo pacing, act sequencing, messaging, and legal risk mitigation.

---

## Meeting Outcomes and Decisions

### Decisions Locked In
1. **Demo Act Order**: Maintain original 1-2-3 structure (problem → candidate hoops → solution).
2. **Timing Discipline**: Enforce sub-5 minute per-act pacing; memorize flow to handle late arrivals and early questions.
3. **App-First Demo**: Stay in the Careerspan app; use Google Docs only if customer explicitly demands deeper methodology explanation.
4. **Scoring Legal Safeguard**: Never display candidate scores or enable stack-ranking; use semantic categories instead (deal-breakers, gaps, strengths).
5. **Act 2 Brevity**: Keep candidate experience narrative <1 minute; focus on showing "hoops" and quality output rather than explaining candidate journey depth.

### Unresolved/Ongoing

- Fine-tuning the exact wording of demo CTAs and candidate explanations (team noted "taking notes to do").
- Determining final candidate example details (Vrijen noted V is preparing test candidates; demo will use one with at least one clear deal-breaker).
- Layout and presentation optimizations for the candidate strength/weakness display (team acknowledged UX could improve).

## Key Themes and Tensions

### Information Asymmetry
The core tension: Employers want to understand *why* Careerspan's recommendations are trustworthy, but explaining the full methodology risks overwhelming them or creating new questions. Resolved by adopting a "explain-on-demand" philosophy.

### Time Scarcity and Risk
Hard 30-minute caps with uncontrollable variables (customer lateness, early questions) create cascading time pressure. Mitigation: memorize, simplify, prioritize ruthlessness in pacing.

### Legal Compliance
Competitive differentiation against Greenhouse is partly grounded in legal risk avoidance (no stack-ranking, no final scores). This is non-negotiable and must infuse demo design.

### Candidate vs. Employer Narrative
Internal debate between emphasizing the candidate experience (hoops, stories) vs. employer output (filtered candidates, recommendations). Resolution: Lead with output; keep candidate story brief.

## Competitive Intelligence Captured

- **Greenhouse positioning**: Emphasizes score visibility, stack-ranking, and methodological transparency—all of which create legal exposure.
- **Careerspan differentiation**: Semantic categories, explainability on-demand, and customer fear-mitigation narrative (avoiding "phoned-it-in" hires).
- **Market insight**: Competitors (Greenhouse) are relying on marketing overconfidence (30-40% speed claims without user adoption data); Careerspan should differentiate on rigorous, forensic approach.

---

## Strategic Notes for Execution

- **Emotional recency matters**: End on the CTA ("Click publish today") not on methodology.
- **Sales narrative is customer-centric**: Frame around employer pain (hiring risk, time waste, legal exposure) not product features.
- **Discipline over flexibility**: Tight, memorized, executable flow beats adaptable but loose structure.
- **Demo is a closing tool, not an explanation tool**: Objective is to trigger "yes" decisions, not educate on product depth.
