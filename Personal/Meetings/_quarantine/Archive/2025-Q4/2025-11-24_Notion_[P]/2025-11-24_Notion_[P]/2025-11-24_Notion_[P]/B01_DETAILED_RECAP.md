# B01_DETAILED_RECAP

## Overview
- Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan founder) and Logan Currie (Notion job board owner) met to stabilize the job board share, map David/Zoë role links, and align on candidate communication expectations before publishing the refreshed board.

## Chronological recap
- **Opening glitch (00:00–04:00):** Logan refreshed the newly updated board several times while sharing, reporting, “I feel like I’m being gaslit by Notion,” before the view finally resolved and everyone confirmed the change.
- **Role linking (04:00–10:30):** Logan described how the David Notion page and Zoe roles would link into the board, planned to surface four open roles, and mentioned two closed slots—one even celebrated as a Careerspan hire for social proof.
- **Category strategy (10:30–14:00):** Vrijen and Logan mapped out three categories—coming soon, closed, and open—and introduced a “talent call” bucket for profiles of engineers ready to move quickly while the site builds magic links.
- **Open-role blueprint (14:00–18:30):** They agreed on four open-call slots (NY + SF software, NY + SF GTM), emphasized location signaling, and kept the noises around checking the board as roles spin up.
- **Candidate communication (18:30–29:30):** Logan raised that there is no employer-side functionality to notify applicants (“there’s no black hole meaning candidates”), so they resolved to control the narrative manually, close roles when ready, track which candidates have been informed (via Slack/Linear), and prompt employers to lead follow-up once Careerspan has passed candidates along.
- **Closing tone (29:30+):** The session ended with a reminder that without clearer expectations (“we need to let folks know when they can expect to hear from us”), the platform will continue to feel like a ghosting black hole.

## Themes to watch
- Candidate trust requires visible intent: closed roles demonstrate sorting, open calls signal urgency, and talent calls promise a proactive relationship.
- Notion’s technical quirks still create friction; keep an eye on share stability before making the board public.
- Employer-side communication must be documented so Careerspan can claim the “hand-off” without losing credibility.
