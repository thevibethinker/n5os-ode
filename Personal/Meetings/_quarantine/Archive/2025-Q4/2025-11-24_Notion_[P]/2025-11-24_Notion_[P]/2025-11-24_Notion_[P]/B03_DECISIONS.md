# B03_DECISIONS

1. Surface two closed roles (one can be Careerspan’s own hire) so the board signals traction and dogfooding before the open roles go live.
2. Spin up four open-call roles—software and GTM for New York and San Francisco—with the location baked into the copy so candidates read the right geography at a glance.
3. Formalize a three-part category structure (coming soon / closed / open) plus a “talent call” lane that frames Careerspan as constantly scouting great companies and keeps interested profiles in view.
4. Treat candidate communication as a manual process: close roles manually, track who has been notified in Linear/Slack, and tell employers that after Careerspan passes a candidate along it becomes their job to follow up and keep applicants informed.
