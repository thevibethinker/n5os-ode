# Stakeholder Intelligence

## Jeff Chen — Practice Interviews (Founder and Technical Lead)
- Values data-rich signals: dashboard walkthroughs stress questions attempted, Interview Academy adoption, rolling score averages, and whether users keep practicing ("action speaks louder than words").
- Hands-on operator: demoed admin workflows, explained how Practice Interviews can add users, push notifications, and surface candidate heat maps without leaving the joint admin portal.
- Partnership focus: wants Careerspan to co-design exclusive cohorts, share demo decks, and turn customer engagement into descriptive attribution data ("show they do the work, then reward them").
- Skeptical of vanity metrics—prefers cohorts that have done the work (he repeatedly insisted on a "3-story" threshold) and wants to gate access so only high-signal users move forward.

## Logan Currie — Apply AI / Data Partnership Lead
- Execution custodian: owns the rollout playbook, from email copy to auto-trigger rules; keeps the demo grounded in workflow realities (e.g., who emails approval drafts, what counts as a user story).
- Communicator: wants clear handoffs (“I’ll mock up the user email; I’ll defer to you on how we label intro copy”) and values simplicity for early adopters.
- Leaning into storytelling: wants to position Practice Interviews as a “value-add retention tool,” using exclusive access and exemplar emails as incentives.

## Ilya Kucherenko — Careerspan Partnerships
- Strategic steward: keeps the call on point, reminding the team to stay friendly, present, and mindful of noise or context (background sensitivity, calling quality).
- Bridge builder: pushes the three-act demo structure, ensuring the call covers job description analysis, candidate story amplification, and the joining math that demonstrates ROI.
- Relationship guardrail: emphasizes empathy, suggests scripting introductions, and keeps the team tuned to both the human and product narrative ("do they remember two things?").

## Vrijen Attawar — Careerspan Founder
- Story architect: threads Coaching stories into the partnership narrative (“if someone has 2 careerspan stories, they can already apply smoothly”) so Practice Interviews can advertise ease-of-use.
- Quality evangelist: insists on showcasing engagements that emphasize reducing friction, demonstrating Zo/Practice Interviews synergy, and matching high-intent users.
- Signal translator: frames the partnership as a chance to show how Careerspan users can make non-technical mental models accessible to partners like Practice Interviews (via curated personas and narrated stories).
