# Risks & Opportunities

## Key Risks

### User Adoption & Engagement
**Risk**: Low adoption from Careerspan users despite offering free Practice Interviews access. The partnership depends on users actually engaging with the tool, but "not everybody's going to use it" as Jeff acknowledged.
- **Mitigation**: Implement a quality barrier (e.g., "three stories completed" threshold) to access Practice Interviews, ensuring only motivated users get access. This filters for high-signal users more likely to engage.
- **Impact Level**: High—core to partnership value

### Quality Control & Data Integrity
**Risk**: Admin dashboard permissions system is complex ("permissions and all this crap just takes forever and it's expensive"). Granting admin access to Logan and other Careerspan team members introduces potential for mismanagement or misconfiguration.
- **Mitigation**: Clear training on admin capabilities; start with simple manual processes rather than mass uploads to maintain control.
- **Impact Level**: Medium—operational but manageable

### Attribution & Measurement Ambiguity
**Risk**: Tracking partnership value is not yet fully defined. While future attribution analysis is mentioned ("trace that attribution data"), current mechanisms are unclear. This could make ROI assessment difficult for both parties.
- **Mitigation**: Set baseline cohort-level data collection now; establish clear success metrics around offer attainment for users leveraging both products.
- **Impact Level**: Medium—strategic importance

### User Quality vs. Scale Tension
**Risk**: Jeff's suggestion to grant access to "all people" without restrictions could lead to noise in the data and dilute the partnership's value. Logan correctly advocated for a barrier, but this tension may resurface.
- **Mitigation**: Maintain the agreed quality threshold; communicate to Careerspan users that Practice Interviews is an exclusive benefit for engaged learners.
- **Impact Level**: High—strategic positioning

## Key Opportunities

### Market Differentiation
**Opportunity**: Careerspan can position itself as uniquely supporting end-to-end interview preparation through this partnership. Users get resume building, storytelling, AND interview practice in one ecosystem.
- **Strategic Value**: High—competitive advantage for Careerspan
- **Timeline**: Immediate (rollout can start this week)

### Learning Loop & Behavioral Economics
**Opportunity**: Jeff's "free throws" analogy signals a powerful flywheel: practice → success → motivation → more practice. Once users see results (better interview scores), they'll be incentivized to keep using both products, creating network effects.
- **Strategic Value**: High—drives long-term retention and engagement
- **Timeline**: 4-8 weeks (time to see measurable results)

### Data & Intelligence Asset
**Opportunity**: Cohort-level data on how Careerspan users progress through Practice Interviews creates valuable intelligence. This can inform product development, content strategy, and enable measurement of training effectiveness.
- **Strategic Value**: High—internal insights + potential monetization angle
- **Timeline**: Ongoing, foundation laid in v1

### Monetization & Upsell Path
**Opportunity**: The exclusive access tier ("only for our most engaged users") creates a natural upsell funnel. Users who hit the quality threshold and see value may later upgrade to premium tiers or paid services.
- **Strategic Value**: Medium–High—revenue expansion potential
- **Timeline**: 3-6 months (after establishing baseline engagement)

### Employer Intelligence & Placement Optimization
**Opportunity**: If attribution tracking works, Careerspan can identify which employers see the highest concentration of successful Practice Interviews users and target those employers for direct placement opportunities.
- **Strategic Value**: High—closes the loop from training to employment
- **Timeline**: Medium-term (6+ months)
