1. **Warm handoff.** Logan Currie stepped into Vrijen and Marianne’s call for a quick hello, noted the chemistry he expected between them, ensured Marianne had context, then left the conversation to run long form of the intro.

2. **Opening framing.** Vrijen framed the conversation as a listening tour: he is spending time with trusted career voices to understand pain points that candidates and mentees are reporting, especially around bridging candidates to companies in a market where the 3- to 10-year experience band is still moving while early-stage and senior roles remain sparse.

3. **Market diagnosis.** Marianne described the scramble she sees from Tufts mentees and other folks: entry-level slots are rare, senior technical management openings have dried up, and everyone in the 3–10 year window competes against waves of displaced people. She also raised the broader career-switcher opportunity and the degree to which existing systems ignore identity, story, and self-advocacy.

4. **Careerspan product narrative.** Vrijen walked through the conversational intake that powers Careerspan: instead of filling forms candidates talk with the AI, reflect on past experiences, and get resume bullets, interview answers, and “vibe check” gap analysis. The tool not only surface uses qualitative voice notes and job descriptions to profile what hiring teams really need, it also creates traceable signals so employers know when a candidate cares about their company.

5. **Representation + noise.** Marianne shifted the tone toward representation, noting the disproportionate negative chorus online about women in tech, her concern about bots amplifying discouraging narratives, and the urge to expand access for people who are disconnected. Vrijen added context about bot farms, phantom students used to harvest identities, and the challenge of pushing a more nuanced message when misinformation cascades.

6. **Systems thinking and action.** Marianne described her manual job-tracking system—spreadsheets, Notion, notes, AI-generated summaries—and asked whether Careerspan could capture that workflow. Vrijen shared that they are building employer views to mirror candidates’ deal breakers and gaps, and the conversation closed with mutual offers: Marianne will test Careerspan and share feedback, Vrijen will keep an eye on Zo Computer (infra/backend) and Elise AI roles, and both remain open to keeping the channel alive for introductions and future context.
