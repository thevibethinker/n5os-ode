Careerspan launched as a career coaching companion and, in this conversation, presented itself as a hiring tool that layers the original coaching workflow on top of employer intelligence. Vrijen described how candidates engage in ~15-minute conversations with the AI to translate personal stories into resume bullets, interview answers, and a “vibe check” that exposes gaps versus a target role. That data is paired with employer-provided job descriptions plus qualitative voice notes so the system can articulate the combination of hard and soft skills employers truly need.

The business remains free for candidates because the focus is on leveling the field for career changers and underrepresented voices; the commercial motion now targets startups through Series B as a smarter alternative to suffering ATS systems. Employers pay for the traceability and signal amplification (e.g., a 40-page report per fully processed candidate) that prove Careerspan can show whether a candidate cares about the company and where their strengths/weaknesses lie. The product team continues to invest in employer-facing features that mirror the candidate tracker Marianne described (in-progress, waiting, take-homes, etc.), reinforcing the hypothesis that self-advocacy is the leverage point behind better matching.
