# Meeting Recap: Careerspan <> Sam | Partnership Discovery Call

**Date:** October 24, 2025 | **Duration:** ~45 minutes (Max left early) | **Attendees:** Saul, Ash, Max (Sam team) + Vrijen (Careerspan)

---

## Executive Summary

Discovery call with Sam (acquisition/partnership target) introduced Careerspan's candidate sourcing platform to Sam's founder team. Meeting established strong product-market fit alignment, mutual strategic interest in partnership, and preliminary acquisition discussion framework. Two of Sam's three founders (Saul & Ash) gave thumbs up on proceeding with partnership exploration. Next call scheduled for following week to formalize partnership structure and acquisition terms.

---

## Meeting Flow & Key Discussion Points

### 1. **Context Setting & Introduction** (0-5 min)
- Saul (misheard as "Reggie" initially) explained Sam's background: met Vrijen at founder event on rooftop where he was building Careerspan
- Sam's positioning: AI-forward platform capturing candidate data similarly to Careerspan
- Sam has existing partnership with Full Circle (intro from mutual connection)
- Sam seeking partnership to access Careerspan's talent network (1,500+ candidates, predominantly ex-startup/ex-TA)
- Key agreement: **5% kickback model** - Sam gets paid when they place candidates, up to 12 months. Early termination resets payment window.

### 2. **Careerspan Product Presentation** (~15-25 min)
Vrijen walked through core product capabilities with live demo:

**Core Functionality:**
- Conversational AI coach for candidates to reflect on experiences in depth
- Generates substantive understanding of candidate capabilities beyond surface resume review
- AI-generated insights from coaching conversations

**Fit Scoring Assessment:**
- Intersects resume data + coaching conversation insights to create role-specific fit scores
- Identifies gaps: what candidate has on resume but hasn't discussed (gap in communication), vs. missing skills entirely
- Semantic positioning analysis: "How well do you cover the gaps this role implies?"

**Magic Link Feature:**
- Employers/partners distribute link; candidates get filtered through Careerspan's assessment
- Partners can send voice notes with real vs. official job requirements; AI evaluates semantically against both
- ATS-like functionality: surfaces only qualified candidates, enables silent screening anonymously

**Candidate Retention & Engagement:**
- Day 30 return rate: 20-40% (cohort/month dependent)
- Candidates recommended for jobs show ~40% return rate at 1+ months later
- Average 2 conversations per engaged candidate = 40+ minutes of AI interaction
- Recent UX win on "vibe checks": 30% of sign-up completers now do vibe check; 50% do 2+ checks
- Organic monthly reengagement: 2-3% of inactive users (even 10-12 months dormant) return unprompted

**Custom Resume Generation:**
- Semantically optimized resumes (30-step process, ~50 cents compute per resume)
- AI rethinks candidate positioning for specific role

**Data Quality & Bias Mitigation:**
- "Bullshit detector" on both JD and candidate sides
- JD detector flags unreasonable expectations, clarity issues
- Candidate detector benchmarks self-reported skills against LLM understanding (eventually against largest reservoir of professional self-descriptions)
- Accent bias mitigated: transcripts capture content, not intonation
- PII scrubbed (deployed or on roadmap)
- Manual QA: ILSE (AI engineer) does 2+ hours daily manual review for consistency & bias
- Vibe check feedback loop driving additional engagement and conversation completion

### 3. **Careerspan Strategic Positioning** (~25-30 min)
- Originally built as career coaching product; pivoted to talent sourcing
- Now positioning explicitly as **sourcing platform** (not dual-sided marketplace)
- Has tactical integrations with 2 job boards + semi-recruiting agency (smaller scale)
- In discussions with Marvin Ventures (McKinsey alumni group) for job board partnership
- Philosophy: Best candidates are passive (not on LinkedIn, not updating). Goal = surface "invisible amazing ones" who just need opportunity trigger
- **Not pursuing VC playbook:** Raising minimally, focusing on network growth at low cost, considering acquisition as exit
- Open to acquisition, partly because VCs don't align with team's values

### 4. **Sam's Product Overview & Sourcing Approach** (~30-35 min)
- Sam dual-sided marketplace: candidates + employers
- Capturing similar data to Careerspan but on employer side
- Founder network = employer advantage; Careerspan's candidate access = Careerspan advantage
- Suggested division of labor: **Careerspan handles sourcing side, Sam handles placement/employer relationships**
- Sam's current challenge: reengaging candidates who haven't converted to placements
- Careerspan's organic reengagement (2-3% monthly) differentiates Sam's offering

### 5. **Acquisition Discussion** (~35-45 min)

**Careerspan Positioning:**
- Timescale: 3-6 months minimum before considering acquisition
- Want to enter "low power mode," build network, avoid new VC money
- **Key point:** Team acquisition preference - wants ILSE included (reliability engineer)
- Philosophy on acquisition: "Rather make less money and work with you guys 100 years" vs. other options (e.g., ETS)
- Prioritizes team cohesion & cultural fit over maximum valuation
- Willing to be transparent and non-Machiavellian about terms

**Sam's Acquisition Framework:**
- Has done similar acquisition structure (referenced Neighborly deal from prior venture)
- Model: IP acquisition (partial), asset purchase (cash exchange), equity stake, remainder open for partner sale
- Interested in acquisition with founding team knowledge + ability to continue scaling sourcing side
- **Tradeoff:** Narrowing focus to startup/entry-level talent (eliminating super-senior/executive placement category)
- Proposed structure: Keep Careerspan as low-cost asset for user retention + love (strong LinkedIn sentiment)

**Next Steps Agreed:**
- Call next week with full founding teams (Logan + Vrijen from Careerspan)
- Both sides to prepare: acquisition proposal framework + partnership structure details
- Sam closing funding checks (targeting 2M raise, but may close earlier like Edmund's 1.2-1.6M close)

## Tone & Chemistry Assessment

**Very positive:** High rapport established despite being first live meeting. Sam team impressed with product depth and Vrijen's philosophy. Mutual appreciation for founder-to-founder dynamics (understanding of VC skepticism, team preference over money, user-centric building). Max had to leave early but left on high note.

**Saul's response:** Full thumbs up on proceeding; enthusiastic about team acquisition structure.

**Ash's response:** Full thumbs up on proceeding.

**Democratic founders model:** Sam operates on unanimous/2-of-3 (Saul, Ash, Max) thumbs up = yes. Two thumbs up achieved = green light for partnership exploration.

---

## Key Insights & Implications

1. **Product-Market Fit Alignment:** Sam values Careerspan's candidate depth + retention mechanics; Careerspan values Sam's employer network
2. **Cultural Compatibility:** Shared skepticism of VC playbook, prioritize team continuity and ethical AI deployment
3. **Acquisition is Secondary Exit:** Both parties exploring partnership as primary path; acquisition as fallback if funding changes dynamics
4. **ILSE is Critical Asset:** Reliability engineering + AI bias auditing is the true differentiator Careerspan is selling (not just the theory)
5. **User Love is Defensible:** Careerspan's organic reengagement + positive LinkedIn sentiment is strategic moat worth preserving even in acquisition scenario
