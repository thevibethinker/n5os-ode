# Commitments & Terms: Careerspan <> Sam Partnership

---

## Partnership Economics & Revenue Sharing

### **5% Kickback Model** (AGREED)
- **Mechanism:** Sam pays Careerspan when candidates placed successfully
- **Payment Window:** Up to 12 months from placement
- **Early Termination:** If contract ends before 12 months, payment window resets
- **Rationale:** Aligns incentives (Sam gets paid by employers, then pays Careerspan)
- **Status:** Already understood by Sam team before call; reinforced as core term

### **Revenue Trigger:** Placement completion
- No upfront platform fees discussed
- No per-candidate screening fees
- Payment model entirely contingent on successful Sam-to-employer placement

## Partnership Structure: Division of Labor

### **Careerspan's Role: SOURCING SIDE**
- Provides access to talent network (1,500+ candidates currently)
- Candidate base: predominantly ex-startup, ex-TA backgrounds
- Delivers candidates pre-screened through AI coach framework
- Maintains candidate engagement through Careerspan platform
- Responsibilities:
  - Keep candidate data current (via coaching conversations)
  - Surface candidates matching Sam-provided job specs
  - Provide fit assessments and positioning materials for candidates

### **Sam's Role: PLACEMENT/EMPLOYER SIDE**
- Manages employer relationships and job distribution
- Handles contract negotiations and placement logistics
- Leverages Sam's founder network for job opportunities
- Manages dual-sided marketplace dynamics with employers
- Responsibilities:
  - Source high-quality jobs aligned with candidate base
  - Manage employer expectations and feedback loop
  - Handle placement execution and confirmation

### **Why This Division Works**
- **Avoids market conflict:** Careerspan + Sam both collect candidate data similarly, but staying on opposite sides prevents competition
- **Leverages asymmetric advantages:** Careerspan has candidate depth/volume; Sam has employer network depth/trust
- **Reduces marketplace muddy dynamics:** Sam already operates dual-sided; adding Careerspan on sourcing = complementary not conflicting

## Acquisition Framework (Preliminary Terms)

### **Careerspan Stance: Willing to Discuss Acquisition Under Conditions**
- **Timescale:** 3-6 months minimum before serious acquisition consideration
- **Rationale:** Needs time to build network momentum in "low power mode" before exiting
- **Team Preference:** Acquisition with team intact preferred over maximum valuation
- **Key Personnel Requirement:** ILSE (AI reliability engineer) non-negotiable in team acquisition
- **Philosophy:** "Rather make less money and work with you guys 100 years" vs. other acquisition paths

### **Sam's Acquisition Model** (Referenced from Neighborly deal)
**Structure with three components:**

1. **IP Acquisition (Partial)**
   - Careerspan tech stack, algorithms, ML models
   - Not necessarily all IP; can be segmented

2. **Asset Purchase (Cash Exchange)**
   - Physical/digital assets at fair valuation
   - Cash amount TBD based on funding close

3. **Equity Stake**
   - Sam takes equity position in Careerspan (or equivalent value exchange)
   - Team member retention may be structured as equity/employment package

4. **Remainder Open for Partner Sale**
   - Portions of IP/assets Careerspan retains can be sold to other companies if desired
   - Flexibility for founding team on non-core assets

### **Careerspan Asset Preservation in Acquisition**
- **User Platform:** Intent to keep Careerspan accessible to end users at low cost (even if acquisition occurs)
- **Rationale:** Strong user love, organic reengagement (2-3% monthly), defensible moat through product sentiment
- **Narrower Focus Post-Acquisition:** Would shift from broad talent (entry-to-executive) to startup/entry-level focus per Sam's narrowing
- **Acceptable Trade-off:** Team willing to accept narrower market in exchange for working with Sam
