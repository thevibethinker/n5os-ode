# B03_DECISIONS

## Key Decisions and Agreements

• **Trial Launch Strategy** – Pursuing TWO parallel trial paths to validate go-to-market approach: (1) Direct marketing campaign targeting ~5,000 companies with landing page and email/ad assets, (2) Platform testing to ensure 10-20 employers can have meaningful user experiences. Rationale: Want to de-risk the campaign by testing both messaging AND product capability before full-scale rollout. Why it matters: This dual-path approach ensures we don't invest heavily in marketing a product that can't deliver the experience we're promising.

• **"Better Filter" as Bare Minimum Value Prop** – Agreed that for the initial trial, the minimum viable promise to employers is a "better filter" than existing ATS systems or manual processes (e.g., Notion boards). Bonus layer: Access to Careerspan's talent pool for supplementary candidates. Rationale: Recognition that "better filtering" alone isn't compelling enough for adoption (competitors already do this), but it's the foundational layer. Psychological motivators (FOMO on unique talent + Speed advantage) drive the actual conversion. Why it matters: This clarifies what we can ship TODAY vs. what we're positioning as differentiators.

• **Story Requirements Policy – Internal Alignment Needed** – Decision that every employer using magic link will receive a "story requirement" when they submit (prompting them to write about why they're hiring). Reason: Protects Careerspan's brand by ensuring we get quality stories to feature and preventing spam/low-quality job postings. However: Ilya raised concern about friction—a story requirement could reduce trial adoption if employers see it as friction. Resolution: Agreed to test this in trial but flagged as potential conversion blocker. Why it matters: This is a policy decision that affects both brand quality AND trial success metrics, and we're intentionally testing its impact.

• **ICP Prioritization Matrix** – Discussed three dimensions for prioritizing which employers to target first: (1) Vertical focus (e.g., tech, finance, operations), (2) Company stage (early-stage, growth, mature), (3) Hiring urgency/scale. Agreed to develop a clear prioritization matrix rather than spray-and-pray approach to the 5,000-company sample. Rationale: Want signal from trial data about which segment responds best to our messaging. Why it matters: This determines both campaign targeting AND what we learn from the trial (which segments show highest engagement, conversion, retention).

• **Marketing Channel Strategy – Job Boards + Email + Ad Targeting** – Confirmed three channels for initial campaign: (1) Direct targeting of companies via ad platforms (LinkedIn, job board integrations), (2) Email outreach (warm intro + cold), (3) Possibly organic/referral (TBD). Decided NOT to go wide across all channels immediately; instead, test highest-ROI channels first. Rationale: Resource constraints and signal learning—want to understand which channel brings highest-quality employers. Why it matters: This shapes both the messaging and asset requirements (landing page, email template, ad creative).

## Strategic Context

This meeting occurred at an inflection point for Careerspan's go-to-market strategy. The core tension is between **speed of adoption** and **brand protection**. Ilya has been brought in as commercial advisor and is pushing on what it means to actually execute a marketing campaign that will put Careerspan in front of thousands of employers—this is different from organic growth or small warm intros.

The decisions above reflect a careful balancing act:
- **On positioning**: Move beyond "we're a better filter" to "we have access to talent you won't find elsewhere + we're faster"
- **On execution**: Run a trial that tests BOTH the marketing messaging AND the product capability to deliver on promises
- **On brand**: Protect quality by requiring stories, but be prepared that this might be a friction point in early adoption
- **On data**: Use the trial to get signal on which employer segments respond best

There's underlying anxiety about **system capacity**: The team mentioned concerns about whether infrastructure can handle a spike in employer signups. This isn't a technical blocker being discussed here, but it's a constraint on how aggressive the marketing can be.
