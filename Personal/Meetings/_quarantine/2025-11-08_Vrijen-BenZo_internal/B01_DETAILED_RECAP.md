## Strategic Context

This meeting operated at the intersection of **product storytelling** (how to talk about Zo), **brand narrative** (freedom/lifestyle positioning), and **go-to-market mechanics** (funnel design for early-stage adoption). The team is balancing three competing concerns:

1. **Appeal to diverse personas:** Founders want to see technical depth; operators want to see lifestyle benefits; VCs want to see traction momentum
2. **Overcome category education gap:** Most attendees likely don't understand what Zo *is* (AI computer vs. AI chatbot vs. workflow tool), so narrative framing is critical
3. **Drive delayed conversion:** Can't download/install Zo at event, so must create persistent engagement hook (QR → email → Discord → documentation)

The **thematic approach** (freedom) rather than feature dump signals mature product storytelling. The **personal workflow framing** (not "look at Zo's features") suggests the team understands their true differentiation: lifestyle product, not productivity tool.

**Critical tension:** Vrijen wants visuals + stinger (emotional landing), while practical execution requires email funneling (mundane but necessary). Team resolved this by making email funnel itself invisible to attendee experience.
