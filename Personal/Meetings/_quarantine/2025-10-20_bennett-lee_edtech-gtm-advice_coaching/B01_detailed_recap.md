# B01: Detailed Recap

## Context & Opening

Bennett Lee reached out for advice on navigating the EdTech space, specifically around selling to universities. He's building an AI-powered grading tool and is 1.5 years into his journey. Both founders immediately bonded over the shared pain of selling to higher education institutions.

## Bennett's Product & Journey

Bennett teaches computer science at University of Southern California (10 years teaching experience) and built an AI grading system out of personal pain. His initial goal was simple: eliminate the manual grading burden where teachers spend 1/3 of their time grading on nights and weekends.

**Product Evolution**: What started as "throw it into ChatGPT and call it a day" turned into 1.5 years of hard engineering work. The key differentiation: while competitors can only grade typed essays, Bennett's system uses OCR and computer vision to grade handwritten work, including mathematical symbols, charts, and graphs. They use off-the-shelf commercial models layered with custom orchestration and fine-tuning.

**Current Traction**: 50 pilot users across K-12 and higher ed, concentrated in Southern California (USC, UCLA, other local universities). Teachers are using it and trying to convince their departments to purchase larger licensing agreements. Progress is "slow and steady" rather than exponential velocity.

**Founder Insight**: Bennett acknowledged the classic engineer trap: "We sort of think like every solution is an engineering solution. So we like over index on that and then we realize at the end of the tunnel we're like wait, is anybody going to buy this?" Now transitioning from product-building mode to go-to-market mode.

## Vrijen's EdTech Experience

V shared Careerspan's pivot story: started as pure-play career tech selling to university career centers ("of the many university stakeholders perhaps some of the worst to sell to"). Had "quite a bit of success" with over a dozen pilots and a couple of contracts, but realized it wasn't lucrative enough given career center dysfunction. Pivoted to building a decentralized talent network, no longer seeking venture backing, focused on growth through free distribution to job seekers and hiring managers.

**Key Insight Shared**: "We saw a dramatic change in how we were greeted by universities saying we've been around for a year versus being around for two years... they've seen so many tools, they've seen so many products come and go. They've seen so many folks make empty promises. To that extent they are jaded by both necessity and by their own stubbornness."

## Technical Discussion

Brief technical exchange where V and Bennett bonded over the challenges of working with LLMs:

- V: "LLMs are an absolute nightmare to engineer with"
- Bennett: "It's crazy... you have to do this thing called role playing. You have to say, like, imagine you're the best AI grader in higher ed university. I'm like, what the heck is like role playing?"
- Discussion of how LLMs require fundamentally different engineering approaches than traditional deterministic software

V correctly identified Bennett's architecture involves computer vision/OCR, which Bennett confirmed. They discussed fine-tuning as critical for accuracy improvements, with Bennett mentioning College Board's historical AP exam data as potential training gold mine.
