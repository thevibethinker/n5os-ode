## Product/Feature Ideas Discussed

| Idea | Description | Rationale | Source | Confidence | Status |
|------|-------------|-----------|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Programmatic Role Distribution to Community** | Create simplified flow where Careerspan roles can be "bulk distributed" to communities without manual curation; system auto-analyzes role against community skills | Darius wants to send roles to his community with minimal overhead; mentioned "drop in job description" and "system breaks it down and analyzes it with the same level of rigor as rocket science" | Vrijen (Careerspan proposal) | HIGH | Existing feature (needs to be showcased as primary value in walkthrough) |

| **Real-time Revenue Dashboard for Partners** | Partner dashboard showing placements, commission owed, real-time tracking of roles sent and outcomes | Darius wants to see ROI from his end; transparency builds trust | Darius (implicit, when discussing reporting) | MEDIUM | Important for partnership health; low build complexity |
| **Healthcare Vertical Expansion** | Specific focus on medical coding, medical billing, IT roles in hospital systems (implied: could use Darius's community or separate healthcare-trained community) | Darius mentioned hospital systems as high-volume hiring (dozens needed, not one engineer); suggested as expansion but not priority | Darius (suggestion) | LOW | Speculative; requires healthcare-specific training supply |

## High-Confidence Ideas for Development

**Ideas 1-2 should be prioritized for demo:**
1. Show how programmatic role distribution works (existing feature)
2. Clarify UI distinction between community-distributed roles (free) vs. employer roles (freemium)

These directly unblock Darius's concerns and are low-effort to showcase.

**Idea 3 (subsidy model)** is worth scoping out (Q: Can a partner subsidize freemium for their community?) but not urgent for pilot.

**Idea 4 (partner dashboard)** is worth building if partnership scales, but can start with manual reporting for pilot.

**Idea 5 (healthcare)** is a future expansion; not actionable until healthcare-trained supply is available.
