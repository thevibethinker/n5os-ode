## 1. "Playing Card" Candidate Presentation System

**Description**: Visual candidate summary cards (similar to sports trading cards or playing cards) with picture, name, 2-3 relevant experience bullets, and 1-2 story highlights. Packaged as URL bundles that hiring managers can flip through quickly.

**Rationale**: Solves hiring manager fatigue from reviewing long resumes; provides faster time-to-decision (5 minutes to review bundle vs. hours for traditional resume review); differentiates from traditional recruiters who only filter and send resumes.

**Source**: Alex Caveny - "I'm almost picturing like playing cards or like sports collector cards. You've got the picture and then like the hot stats... if you had something like here's the picture of the person, their name and then like there are two pieces of relevant experience and then they're from their pulling from their stories, like what are two interesting things they've done would be potentially like a really interesting way to present that information." (~26:32)

**Confidence**: HIGH - Alex elaborated extensively, workshopped specific format details, and called this "unprecedented" in recruiting space; directly addresses validated hiring manager pain point.

## 2. URL-Hosted Candidate Bundles with Engagement Tracking

**Description**: Host candidate cards on unique URLs (one per employer/job) that track engagement metrics: click-through rate, number of profiles viewed, time spent per profile, which candidates they viewed. URL itself becomes the sales tool.

**Rationale**: Allows Careerspan to measure actual employer engagement without requiring explicit opt-in; provides data for optimizing presentation format; creates frictionless review experience ("just click this link"); enables A/B testing of different card formats or candidate groupings.

**Source**: Alex Caveny - "I think just send the info and then if they read it or not, up to them. And that's why I think like hosting it on a URL might better than sending like a PDF or a presentation, because you can also glean metrics. Because if each URL is unique because you're sending them their special bundle, then you can see like what the click through rate is, how many profiles they browsed, if they just totally ignored it." (~39:49)

**Confidence**: HIGH - Solves technical problem (how to measure engagement) and marketing problem (how to deliver value without friction); Alex has direct experience with this type of product analytics.

## 3. Thematic Candidate Categorization ("Clear Fits" vs. "Hidden Gems")

**Description**: Group candidates into themed categories like "Clear Fits" (obvious matches on paper), "Hidden Gems" (non-obvious matches worth second look), "Career Transitioners" etc. instead of percentage fit scores.

**Rationale**: Feels curated and human rather than algorithmic; builds trust by acknowledging trade-offs explicitly; helps hiring managers understand *why* to consider each candidate rather than just ranking them; differentiates from "corporate and boring" HR tech tools.

**Source**: Alex Caveny - "I think giving them category, categorizing them could be an interesting way to present that info. So you could have like clear fits for example... And then another section could be like hidden gems or whatever... These are the people that if you're not thinking carefully that you're going to miss out on, like you're not going to think about them. And we are surfacing that they can do X, Y and Z really well actually." (~51:32)

**Confidence**: MEDIUM-HIGH - Strong product intuition from someone with hiring experience, but not yet validated through user testing; needs iteration to find right category labels and groupings.

## 4. Candidate "Trade-Off" Presentation Format

**Description**: For each candidate, explicitly show 3-5 core strengths relevant to the role AND 3-5 weakest areas or gaps, with AI-generated confidence levels for each judgment. Frame as trade-offs rather than pure advocacy.

**Rationale**: Builds trust with hiring managers by being honest about shortcomings; positions Careerspan as honest broker rather than pure candidate advocate; hiring managers can make informed decisions rather than feeling sold to; addresses concern about "are you just being blatantly biased?"

**Source**: Vrijen - "And that's such an easy, quick thing to test... When you're looking at this piece of paper about a candidate, is it more or less compelling to have you be pitched the candidate with an obvious advocate or totally neutral description?" Alex response: "I think you want to sort of give the impression that it's neutral while being a little bit salesy... If you can kind of surface those flaws too... that can build a little bit of trust." (~34:21, ~35:27)

**Confidence**: MEDIUM - Both parties agreed on principle of transparency, but exact format and level of negative information needs testing to avoid undermining candidates.

## 5. Signing Bonus for Scouted Candidates (Recruiter Fee Reallocation)

**Description**: Instead of employer paying recruiter fee (typically 20-30% of salary), redirect that money to candidate as signing bonus. Pitch to candidates: "Get scouted like in sports, get paid for being recruited."

**Rationale**: Eliminates third-party fee friction for employers; creates candidate incentive to be recruited through Careerspan; aligns with labor organizing/guild concept; differentiates from traditional recruiter dynamics.

**Source**: Vrijen - "That would be interesting if one day we could make that a signing bonus for candidates. Right. And cut recruiters out of that loop altogether where you know, hey, if you get like scouted, you know, just like in sports, you get a signing bonus." (~48:08)

**Confidence**: LOW - Speculative idea without immediate implementation path; would require different business model and employer buy-in; interesting long-term positioning but not MVP feature.

## 6. Professional Guild Model for Job Security

**Description**: Create professional communities (PM Guild, Engineering Guild, etc.) where members pay dues that fund collective job security services—placement assistance, career coaching, professional development.

**Rationale**: Organizes labor to create sustainable funding model beyond transactional placement fees; creates network effects (more members = better placement outcomes); aligns with long-term Careerspan vision of career agent.

**Source**: Vrijen - "There was another sort of idea that we had that was like almost having professional guilds, like having all the PMs sort of have a career span like PM Guild and effectively put money towards long term Job security, like splitting that amongst everyone." (~48:59)

**Confidence**: LOW - Long-term strategic vision rather than near-term product feature; requires critical mass of users and different business model; interesting direction but not actionable in current GTM phase.
