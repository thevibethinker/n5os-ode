## Strategic Context

### The AI/LLM Paradox

Vrijen articulated a critical insight: LLMs are pseudo-democratizing, not truly democratizing. They create *advantage for people who can wield them*, not for everyone. This is actually perfect for founders like Logan/Vrijen because:
- Requires linguistic reasoning capability (their strength)
- Computationally expensive (they have discount compute access)
- Still requires human judgment to implement effectively
- Creates 18-24 month window before commoditization

**Implication:** The company's AI-driven matching/verification tech is not commoditized; it's actually defensible because it requires the exact skill profile they have.

### The Runway Reality Check

Logan surfaced the core tension: "Don't want to put money into a leaky bucket" (from Jeff Busking's workshop). Current path is high-burn without clear monetization path. Vrijen's thesis: if you're selling within a year, stop optimizing for revenue and optimize for traction/distribution/tech proof.

**The Pivot:** Don't chase revenue; chase growth. Convert runway to proof points.

### Distribution-First Narrative

Vrijen proposed a community-centric narrative: instead of "we built a platform," say "we've embedded ourselves in X communities representing Y hundred thousand candidate reach." This reframes from product/revenue metrics to network penetration metrics.

**Why it works:** 
- Quantifiable immediately (community contracts, LOIs)
- Leverages their strongest asset (relationships)
- Easier to prove than tech
- Reduces dependency on AI marketing (which is difficult)
