# STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH: Landscape Insights from Career Tech Founders

**Perspective:** Speaking as bootstrapped career tech founders (CareerSpan) navigating AI, funding, and exit strategy in 2025

---

## Key Landscape Insights

**1. Enterprise M&A is Shifting from Software Budget to "Automatable Payroll"**  
- **Observation:** Jeff Busking's workshop highlighted that enterprise procurement is moving beyond software spend to labor cost replacement
- **Implication:** Career tech companies are not being evaluated as "software tools" but as "labor cost reduction vectors"
- **Strategic Value:** Changes positioning—stop saying "we're a platform," start saying "we reduce your recruiting headcount by 40%"
- **Window:** This shift is early-stage; incumbents haven't adapted narratives. Founder advantage to move fast.

**2. Roll-Up Plays Are Hot, Especially in "Unsexy" Industries**  
- **Observation:** Google Ventures + Compadre Capital showcased three roll-up companies (accountants, business automation, and recruiting adjacent). All PE or operations-background founders.
- **Implication:** Roll-ups in fragmented industries (recruiting, staffing, freelance platforms) are attractive to growth VCs and PE firms
- **Specific to CareerSpan:** "Recruiting firms are a dying breed with only network advantage left. We automate their workflow and take their network."
- **Window:** This strategy works for 18-24 months before market consolidates. Need to move fast.

**3. Verifications/Background Checks Are Hot M&A Territory**  
- **Observation:** Checker just acquired another company for ~B. Background check/verification is undergoing massive consolidation
- **Implication:** If CareerSpan can credibly position resume-to-interview consistency verification, it enters a B+ market
- **Specific Opportunity:** Ilsa's tech (resume consistency validation across transcripts) is exactly what acquirers in this space need

**4. LLMs Create Temporary Founder Advantage, Not Permanent**  
- **Observation:** Language-based AI rewards articulate, linguistic thinkers who can wield it effectively
- **Implication:** 18-month window where implementation requires human judgment + domain expertise. After that, commoditized.
- **Strategic Implication:** Exit now, not in 3 years. The moat erodes faster than traditional software businesses.

**5. Revenue is Not a Filter for Exit—Traction and Network Are**  
- **Observation:** Go Ahead Ventures explicitly states "revenue wasn't an issue" for their consumer product portfolio
- **Implication:** Traditional VC metrics (MRR, LTV, CAC) matter less for exits. Distribution (users, communities, network reach) matters more.
- **Specific to CareerSpan:** 50-70K user base + community relationships are worth more than 0K MRR from wrong channels

**6. Deal Structure Timing: Asset Purchase >>> Acquihire**  
- **Observation:** Both founders want asset purchase (not acquihire) to preserve Ilsa + tech
- **Market Signal:** This is harder to execute (more diligence required) but signals company value vs. people value
- **Implication:** Prepare cleaner books, IP ownership, tech stack clarity. Harder now = better exit terms later.

---

## Market Dynamics

**Founder Advantage Window:** 18 months before LLM implementation becomes commodity  
**M&A Activity:** Hot in career tech (recruiting, staffing, labor marketplaces). Especially work-tech and HR-tech focused buyers  
**Deal Sizes:** -10M range for asset purchases of early-stage companies with proven tech + traction  
**Preferred Structure:** Asset purchase with founder optionality, not acquihire with locked-in employment  
**Buyer Types:** HR/ATS (buying tech), Consumer work-tech (buying distribution), Recruiting roll-ups (buying both)
