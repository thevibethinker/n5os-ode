# Transcript: Why Might Doesn't Make Right, But Talent Makes Possible

Why "might does not "make right" , but talent does "make possible"

Talent + hard work are the prosocial inputs to achievement.

When your ambitions exceed your talent, people resort to antisocial behaviors (cheating, stealing, cutting corners) to close the gap.

Talent = encountering the world unafraid

When you're talented, you can take honest swings at life. You can compete without manipulation because your baseline capability gives you a real shot.

Under-talented = desperate = antisocial shortcuts

The person who lacks talent but has ambition gets cornered into antisocial tactics:
- Lies about credentials
- Steals credit
- Manipulates relationships for gain
- Takes shortcuts that hurt others

Anti-social people can still be high-achievers

High-talent people can succeed prosocially, but antisocial tactics also provide a "buff to an already buffed capability"

Life as tactical execution

Navigating pro/antisocial tactics based on what you can get away with

Some people choose antisocial paths because:
1. They don't have the talent to compete fairly
2. They have talent but want an even bigger edge
3. They exist in environments where antisocial behavior is normalized

The moral dimension

This isn't moral relativism — it's recognizing that antisocial behavior often stems from capability gaps, not inherent evil. The most talented can afford to be good.

"Might makes right" vs "Talent makes possible"

Traditional view: Power justifies itself
V's view: Talent creates genuine optionality — the talented person CAN choose the prosocial path because they have a real shot at winning fairly

Implications for Careerspan

If talent creates prosocial optionality:
- Help people discover and develop genuine talent
- Reduce the gap between ambition and capability
- Create environments where prosocial paths are viable

The talented person encounters the world unafraid. The under-talented person compensates with manipulation.
