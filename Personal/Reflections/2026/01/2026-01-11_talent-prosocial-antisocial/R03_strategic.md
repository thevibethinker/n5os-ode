### Strategic Analysis

This reflection articulates a foundational premise for Careerspan's reason-for-being: the gap between what people want and what they can actually do drives antisocial behavior. When talent + hard work can't close that gap, people cheat, steal credit, and manipulate. 

The strategic implication is that Careerspan's value proposition isn't just "better matching" or "career advancement" — it's **creating the conditions for prosocial success**. By helping people discover and develop genuine talent, we reduce the desperation that drives manipulation. This positions Careerspan as more than a career tool; it's a moral infrastructure play.

This is a bet that the market will eventually reward authenticity enablers over manipulation tools. In a world where AI makes credential-faking easier, the value of genuine capability assessment increases.

2. **Lean into credential verification** — catch the cheaters

3. **Build talent development + matching** — reduce the gap
   - Preferred because: Creates long-term defensibility, mission-aligned
