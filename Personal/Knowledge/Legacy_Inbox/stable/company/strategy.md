# Company Strategy — Careerspan

**Last Updated:** 2025-10-09

---

## Target Market

### Product Manager Staffing Focus

**Why Product Managers First:**

Product manager roles attract "insanely varied backgrounds" - career transitions, IC contributors, diverse career paths - making hiring evaluation significantly harder than credential-based roles like engineering. An engineer with 15 years experience is obviously skilled; a PM could be a career changer requiring deep contextual evaluation.

**Strategic Fit:**

This hiring chaos is a perfect match for Careerspan's core strength: surfacing atypical profiles with rich contextual information. Where traditional resume screening fails (non-linear careers, unconventional backgrounds), Careerspan's AI coaching provides the context hiring managers need.

**Initial Geography:** New York, San Francisco, Boston

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Hiring Manager Targeting (Not HR)

**Target Persona:** Engineering managers, team leads, hiring managers with direct authority

**Why Not HR:**
- HR/talent departments resist disruption because their job security depends on maintaining existing ATS/resume review processes
- HR sees new recruiting approaches as competition to their role
- Traditional gatekeepers who "like their normal process"

**Why Hiring Managers:**
- Burned out from resume review ("most mind-numbingly boring process")
- Want digestible, human-centric information
- Have authority to skip people from ATS directly
- More receptive to solutions that make their life easier

**Positioning:** Frame Careerspan as "hedge on sourcing" - complement to internal talent team and external recruiters, not a replacement.

**Source:** Validated through Alex Caveny (former hiring manager) coaching session, 2025-09-24. See hypothesis H-GTM-009.

### "Goods Upfront" Outreach Approach

**Core Principle:** Show candidate bundles immediately in first contact, don't establish relationship first.

**Why It Works:**
- Hiring managers: "I don't want to start a conversation with every recruiter. I want to see your candidates."
- Trust built through candidate quality, not relationship building
- Market gap: "Literally no one has ever done this" - immediate differentiation

**Outreach Format:**
- Minimal email copy + URL to candidate bundle
- URL tells full story (what Careerspan is, why these candidates, CTA)
- Bundle engagement metrics drive follow-up conversation

**Anti-Pattern to Avoid:**
- Long introductory emails about Careerspan
- Scheduling discovery calls before showing candidates
- "Establishing relationship" before demonstrating value

**Implementation:**
- URL-based candidate bundles (10-30 candidates per URL)
- Track engagement: click-through rate, profiles viewed, time spent
- Follow up only with engaged viewers

**Source:** Validated through Alex Caveny direct feedback, 2025-09-24. See hypothesis H-GTM-008.

## Pricing Model

### Current: Warm Intro Fee → Future: Subscription

**Warm Intro Pricing:** $300-400 per warm introduction

**Strategic Constraint:**
Companies express subscription interest BUT explicitly require: **"Show me at least one good candidate first."**

This blocks a direct-to-subscription model. Companies are skeptical, jaded by failed HR tech, and need proof before committing to recurring payments.

**Sequencing:**
1. **Phase 1 (Current):** Warm intro model to establish trust and proof
2. **Phase 2 (After 2-3 placements):** Pitch subscription from position of demonstrated success
3. **Phase 3 (Target):** Recurring revenue subscription model

**What Unlocks Subscription:**
- 5-10 successful warm intro → hire conversions
- Consistent quality demonstrated over time
- Geographic candidate pool built (NY/SF/Boston)

**Source:** Multiple customer conversations validated in Alex session, 2025-09-24

## Distribution Channels

### Two-Track Strategy

**Track 1: Communities (Candidate Pool Building)**
- Mission-aligned, fast yes, low risk
- Provides volume and engagement
- Premium perk positioning for paid members
- Examples: Remotely Good, other micro-communities

**Track 2: Companies (Revenue Generation)**
- Profit-driven, require proof, slow yes
- Higher burden of proof but pays
- Only pitch after candidate pool established
- Target: Hiring managers in NY/SF/Boston with product roles

**Why Both:**
Different value props, different timelines. Communities provide talent supply; companies provide distribution and revenue.

## Key Strategic Bets

1. **Product staffing focus:** Vertical specialization in chaotic hiring market (PMs)
2. **Hiring manager targeting:** Bypass HR gatekeepers, go to decision makers
3. **Goods upfront approach:** Differentiated sales motion showing value first
4. **Community partnerships:** Alternative talent pool vs. traditional sourcing
5. **Proof before subscription:** Accept warm intro phase as trust-building
