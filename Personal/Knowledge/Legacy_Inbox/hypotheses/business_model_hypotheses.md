## H-BIZ-001: Placement Fee Model Works for Community Partnerships

**Stakeholders:** `[community_partners]` `[employers]`

**Statement:**
Revenue share on placement fees (vs. subscription or per-posting fees) aligns incentives between Careerspan and community partners, making partnerships easier to close and sustain.

**Why it matters:**
- If true: Use placement fee model for all community partnerships
- If false: Need alternative revenue model (subscription, per-license, etc.)
- Economics: Success-based pricing vs. upfront commitment

**How to test:**
- Track: Community partner acceptance of placement fee model
- Measure: Revenue per partnership vs. projected subscription revenue
- Compare: Partner satisfaction with aligned incentives
- Timeline: 5+ community partnerships with revenue data

**Status:** `TESTING` (Remotely Good accepted model, awaiting revenue data)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- **[Sep 29, 2025]** Remotely Good: Partnership structured as revenue share on placements. Theresa accepted without pushback. Model positions career services as "passive income" for community, not operational burden. (Meeting: 46a9c7)

*Contradicting:*
- None yet (may emerge if communities want predictable subscription revenue instead)

## November 2024 Update: Data Moat Hypotheses

**Added:** November 4, 2025  
**Source:** Strategic Intelligence Report (con_kSq36OtO8rmECYtW)

### H-BIZ-010: Longitudinal Behavioral Data Creates Defensible Moat

**Stakeholders:**   

**Statement:**
Longitudinal behavioral data captured through sustained coaching engagement creates a defensible moat that competitors cannot replicate without fundamentally changing their business models. This data advantage compounds over time as coaching quality improves matching outcomes.

**Why it matters:**
- If true: Sustainable competitive advantage, high barriers to entry
- If false: Competitors could catch up quickly, need different moat strategy
- Valuation: Data moats command premium multiples

**Moat Components:**

**1. Product Moat (Engagement):**
- 20% D30 retention = 2x industry average
- 50%+ multi-session engagement rate
- High session depth (multi-turn conversations)

**2. Data Moat (Signals):**
- Communication patterns, self-awareness, learning agility
- Persistence, goal clarity, response to feedback
- Longitudinal behavioral fingerprints

**3. Network Moat (Marketplace):**
- More candidates → Better data → Better employer ROI
- Better ROI → More employers → More opportunities
- Flywheel effects compound over time

**How to test:**

**Phase 1: Engagement sustainability (6 months)**
- Track: D30 retention holds at 15-20% as scale
- Measure: Session depth and quality over time
- Validate: Repeat usage drives data accumulation

**Phase 2: Predictive validity (12 months)**
- Track: Placement success rates vs. engagement levels
- Measure: Employer-reported retention at 90 days
- Prove: Enriched profiles → Better hires (measurable)

**Phase 3: Competitive moat test (18 months)**
- Track: New entrants attempting similar model
- Measure: Time/capital required to reach our engagement levels
- Validate: 18-24 month replication barrier

**Status:**  (engagement metrics validated, predictive validity pending)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- **[Nov 2024]** 20% D30 retention demonstrates sustained engagement (product moat validated). 50%+ multi-session rate shows behavioral data capture is working. (Source: Metrics analysis)
- **[Nov 2024]** Competitive analysis shows structural inability for job boards, recruiting platforms, assessment tools, and coaching platforms to replicate without changing core business models. (Source: Competitive landscape analysis)

*Contradicting:*
- **[Pending]** Have not yet proven enriched profiles → measurably better hires (need employer retention data)
- **[Risk]** If AI coaching quality degrades, engagement drops → Data moat erodes
- **[Risk]** Large players (LinkedIn, BetterUp) could attempt similar model with capital advantage

**Last updated:** 2025-11-04

## H-BIZ-011: Engagement Itself is a Quality Signal

**Stakeholders:**  

**Statement:**
The act of engaging with Careerspan (returning for multiple coaching sessions) is itself a predictive signal of candidate quality, independent of the behavioral data captured. High-engagement candidates demonstrate seriousness, self-awareness, and growth orientation.

**Why it matters:**
- If true: Engagement creates immediate employer value (not just long-term data value)
- If false: Value depends entirely on AI analysis accuracy
- Product strategy: Justifies engagement optimization as top priority

**Hypothesis mechanics:**

**Engagement as screening:**
- Returning for coaching = demonstrates career seriousness
- Implementing feedback = shows coachability and learning agility
- Multi-session users = higher commitment, lower flake rate

**Employer value:**
- Pre-screened for engagement level (not just keywords)
- Lower no-show rate for interviews
- Higher offer acceptance (candidates who've invested in process)

**Competitive advantage:**
- Job boards have no engagement filter (anyone can apply)
- Our candidates self-select through coaching investment
- "Skin in the game" effect without financial barrier

**How to test:**

**Phase 1: Engagement correlation (6 months)**
- Track: Interview show rates by engagement level
- Measure: Offer acceptance by session count
- Compare: High-engagement vs. low-engagement candidates

**Phase 2: Employer perception (6 months)**
- Track: Employer feedback on candidate quality
- Measure: Repeat hiring rates
- Compare: Engagement-screened vs. traditional candidates

**Phase 3: Retention prediction (12 months)**
- Track: 90-day retention by candidate engagement level
- Measure: Employer-reported performance ratings
- Prove: Engagement level → Job success correlation

**Status:**  (logical but not yet measured)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- **[Nov 2024]** High engagement demonstrates behavioral patterns (persistence, self-direction) that correlate with job success. Logical inference but not yet proven with data. (Source: Positioning analysis)
- **[Anecdotal]** Employers value "pre-screened" candidates who've demonstrated seriousness. Engagement is tangible proxy for commitment.

*Contradicting:*
- **[Pending]** No empirical data yet on engagement → job success correlation
- **[Risk]** High engagement could also indicate desperation (not always positive signal)
- **[Risk]** Self-selection bias (engaged users may differ systematically in other ways)

### H-BIZ-012: Closed-Loop Feedback Creates Compounding Advantage

**Stakeholders:** investors, employers

**Statement:**
Closed-loop feedback system (self-representation → hiring → performance → progression) creates compounding advantage as each placement enriches prediction model. This feedback loop is defensible because competitors can't access post-hire performance data.

**Why it matters:**
Explains how data moat compounds over time (not just linear growth)

**Status:** TESTING (early placements starting to generate feedback)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- [Nov 2024] Live placements: Bonfire case study, Remotely Good partnership (Source: SHRM application)
- [Nov 2024] Employer partnerships can provide performance data
- [Nov 2024] Each placement = validation or correction of behavioral signals

*Against:*
- Limited placements to date (small dataset)
- Employer willingness to share performance data unclear at scale
- Time lag: performance signal takes 6-12 months minimum

**Implications if true:**
- First-mover advantage compounds exponentially
- Placement volume becomes strategic priority (even low-margin initially)
- Data moat deepens with every successful hire

**Test plan:**
- Track prediction accuracy improvement over time
- Measure signal strength: early indicators vs. actual performance
- Calculate data value: how many placements needed for statistically significant model?
