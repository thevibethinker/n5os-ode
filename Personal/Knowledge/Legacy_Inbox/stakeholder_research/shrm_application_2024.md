## Application Strategy & Tactical Guidance

### Key Questions to Address

**Q: What problem are you solving?**

Framework: Problem → Impact → Why Now → Why Us

"Mid-market employers (95% of SHRM members) struggle to attract talent because they can't compete on compensation. Meanwhile, candidates evaluate opportunities primarily on salary because traditional tools (job boards, resumes, interviews) don't surface mission, culture, and development opportunities effectively.

This mismatch costs employers (high turnover, poor retention) and candidates (accepting wrong-fit roles, leaving within 12 months).

Careerspan solves this by helping candidates discover and evaluate opportunities on dimensions beyond compensation through sustained career coaching, while providing employers with behavioral data that predicts retention and performance."

**Source:** con_kSq36OtO8rmECYtW, Q6 problem statement development

**Q: How does it work?**

Framework: User Journey → Mechanism → Output → Value

"Candidates sign up for free AI career coaching through community partnerships (alumni associations, professional groups) or directly. Through ongoing coaching conversations, Careerspan captures behavioral and longitudinal career data. Employers access enriched candidate profiles with predictive signals about retention and culture fit. Payment happens per placement (00-1K), making it low-risk for employers."

**Source:** con_kSq36OtO8rmECYtW, Q7 mechanism description

**Q: What's your traction?**

Framework: Engagement → Partnerships → Differentiation

"50%+ of users engage in multiple coaching sessions. 20% D30 retention (2x industry average for career tools). Live partnerships with Columbia and UMass alumni associations. Pipeline includes HBS and Cornell. Early employer placements include Bonfire (education software) and Remotely Good case study.

What matters: Unlike every career tool competitor (LinkedIn <10% engagement, Indeed episodic, assessment tools one-time), we've achieved sustained candidate engagement that enables longitudinal behavioral data capture."

**Source:** con_kSq36OtO8rmECYtW, traction synthesis + competitive context

**Q: Why are you uniquely positioned to solve this?**

Framework: Team + Insight + Timing + Moat

"Vrijen Attawar (founder, 10+ years career coaching experience) + Logan Byers (technical co-founder) built engagement-first coaching that candidates actually want to return to. The insight: engagement pattern itself is a quality signal, and longitudinal behavioral data predicts retention better than resumes or interviews.

Structural moat: Competitors are locked into incompatible business models (job boards = transactional, ATS = employer-side only, coaches = confidential, assessments = one-time). We have 2-3 year head start on longitudinal data accumulation that compounds our advantage."

**Source:** con_kSq36OtO8rmECYtW, Q27-Q28 competitive positioning

## Risks & Mitigation

### Application Risks

**Risk: Foundation may not see tech solution as mission-aligned**
*Mitigation:* Lead with democratized access story, free coaching for underserved populations

**Risk: May seem too early-stage**
*Mitigation:* Emphasize live partnerships and engagement metrics, not just concept

**Risk: Data usage in hiring may raise concerns**
*Mitigation:* Lead with transparency, consent, candidate benefit; contrast with assessment tools (helping IN not screening OUT)

### Partnership Risks

**Risk: Foundation programs move slowly**
*Mitigation:* Set realistic timelines, have alternative GTM paths

**Risk: Member engagement ≠ customer conversion**
*Mitigation:* Pilot with small cohort, measure conversion metrics

**Risk: Enterprise requirements we're not ready for**
*Mitigation:* Start with SMB segment within mid-market, prove model before scaling up
