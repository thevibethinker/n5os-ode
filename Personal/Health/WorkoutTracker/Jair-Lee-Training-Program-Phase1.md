## 🧬 V's Genetic Context for This Program

> *Reference: `file 'Personal/Health/V_GENETIC_PROFILE.md'`*

Your genome has specific implications for how you should approach this training:

### Endurance-Oriented Muscle Fiber Type (ACTN3 TT)
- You lack functional alpha-actinin-3 → genetically suited for **sustained effort over explosive power**
- **Implication:** The Zone 2 cardio days (Day 3, Day 6, Day 7) are where your body naturally excels. Don't skimp on these.
- **Implication:** Power movements (box jumps, medicine ball slams) may require more deliberate practice — they won't come as naturally.

### Warrior COMT (GG) — Recovery Needs
- You perform well under acute training stress but need **more deliberate recovery** between intense sessions
- **Implication:** Day 6 (Restorative/Active Recovery) is not optional. Your dopamine system clears fast, which is great for performance but means you deplete faster during prolonged high-intensity blocks.
- **Implication:** If you're stacking multiple hard training days, watch for elevated resting HR on Fitbit — that's your signal to back off.

### Caffeine Timing (CYP1A2 AC)
- Intermediate caffeine metabolism means afternoon caffeine may disrupt sleep
- **Implication:** Pre-workout caffeine is fine for morning sessions. For afternoon/evening workouts, skip the stimulant — your recovery sleep matters more than the performance boost.

### Lactose Intolerance (MCM6 GG)
- You carry the intolerance genotype
- **Implication:** If you're using whey protein or dairy-based recovery shakes, monitor for GI issues. Consider plant-based alternatives (pea, rice, hemp) if you notice discomfort.

## Overview

This is a comprehensive 7-day training program focusing on building strength, endurance, and mobility for marathon/half-marathon preparation. The program balances upper body, lower body, core work, and cardiovascular conditioning.

**Important Notes:**
- Track quantitative measurements for each exercise (Reps, Sets, Tempo, Rest, Weight used, %of Max weight, Comments, how did it feel before/during/after, any pain or discomfort)
- For exercise demonstrations: Search YouTube for proper form videos
- Prioritize movement quality above all, then volume and intensity
- Document as much detail as possible

## Week 1 Schedule

### **Day 1: Upper Body Focus**

**A. Warm-up (10-15 minutes)**
*Before deep breathing exercises, Tai Chi practice, or 5-10 min slow-to moderate walking/jog*
- Around the worlds: 1x10 each direction
- Band pull aparts: 1x10
- Cat cows: 1x10
- Thread the needle: 1x8 per side
- Thoracic spine rotation: 1x8 per side

**B. Strength Circuit (perform as circuit, 1-3 rounds)**

1. **Bench Press or alternatives**
   - Push-ups, standard [3x8-10]
   - Barbell bench press: [3x8 at 65%]

2. **Chest Dips** (if available) OR substitute with close grip push-ups [3x8]
   - Alternate: Dumbbell Chest Press 3x12 with 30° bench incline

3. **Seated Cable Row** (V-Bar close) Pull to lower abdomen [3x10 (light weight)]
   - Alternative: Scapular retraction hold for 10 seconds then release

4. **Single Arm Dumbbell rows** or similar (if dumbbells available)
   - Alternative: Wide grip inverted row (if access to bar and suspension straps) [3x8]

5. **TRX Row** or [3x10] - as many as you can

6. **Band Pull-Aparts (20 reps)**

7. **Hammer Curls (Dumbbells)** [3x10]

8. **Plank** (30 seconds or failure), **side planks** (20 sec dynamic) [3 sets]

9. **Half-kneeling wood chops with medicine ball** or 3x10 stack rope swing

10. **Dead bug** [3x10]

**C. Conditioning (optional)**
+ 15-20 min steady state moderate cardio (Zone 2: 65%-75% HR max)
- Concept2 rower is fine

**D. Cool Down**
+ Stretching 5-10 minutes (static stretch 20-30sec hold, muscle specific)

---

### **Day 2: Lower Body Focus**

**A. Warm-up + Activation (10-15 minutes)**

- March in place or light jog 2-3 minutes
- Leg swings front to back and side to side [10 each leg]
- Glute Bridges 1x10
- Side lying leg raises (fire hydrants) [1x10 per side]

**B. Strength Circuit (perform as circuit, 1-3 rounds)**

1. **Goblet Squats or Front Squat** (use dumbbell/kettlebells) [3x12]
   - If unavailable: Goblet squat reps to technical failure/fatigue

2. **Romanian Deadlifts (RDLs)** - 3x8 (use dumbbells) at 2-3 tempo

3. **Walking Lunges** - 2 sets x 20 steps (not alternating per leg count)
   - Or reverse lunges

4. **Box step ups** or [3x10 each leg]
   - Single leg step to knee other leg [modified pistol squats as alternative]

5. **Glute Bridges** [3x12] or 3x12 hip thrust

6. **Calf Raises** (standing) - [3x15], vary tempo 2-0-2-0

7. **Pallof press** (resistance machine or cable) [3x12] or 3x8 per side

8. **Reduced Range of Motion Split Squats** - 3x8 (as needed)

9. **Bear Crawl** (20 yards forward/back)

**Note: Accessory/Coordinative work** as needed, for fun!

---

### **Day 3: Core + Cardio + Mobility**

**A. Warm-up**

- Easy bike ride, elliptical or rowing machine for 10 minutes
- Activation sequence:
  - Glute bridges 1x10
  - Dead bugs 1x10
  - Bird dogs 1x10

**B. Circuit (1-3 rounds)**

1. **Plank Variations** (30 sec front/30 sec left side/30 sec right side) [3x60 sec total]

2. **Russian Twists** (use ball 5-15lbs) 3x40 total twists

3. **Hanging Knee/Leg Raises** or Lying knee raises [3x10]

4. **Reverse Crunches** 3x15

5. **Mountain Climbers** 3x30 seconds

6. **Medicine Ball Slams** 3x15

7. **Dead bugs** 3x10 per side

**C. Cardio (20-30 minutes)**

Either:
- 20-25 min easy, Zone 2 cardio
- Intervals (30 sec fast: 90 sec recover) 6-8 rounds
- Fartlek style

Or:
- Conditioning finisher: As many rounds in 10 minutes: (Jump rope 50 skips, 10 step jacks, 10 jumping jacks)

**D. Cool Down (5-10 minutes)**
- Stretching & mobility: focus on hip flexors, hamstrings, glutes, upper traps, and lats

**E. Meditation** 5 min at the end

---

### **Day 4: Upper Body Focus #2**

**A. Warm-up** (same as Day 1)

**B. Strength Circuit (perform as circuit, 1-3 rounds)**

1. **Pull-ups or Lat Pulldowns** (if gym) (3x6-8) or wide overhand pull-ups

2. **Seated Cable Row or face pulls** 3x12 at moderate weight

3. **Chest Dips or Decline pushups** 3x10/9

4. **Farmers Carries** (30 seconds per arm) add: Koran walks (optional)

5. **TRX rows** (as many as you can) 3 sets

6. **Wall angels** (10 reps)

7. **Shoulder complex** - lateral raises, front raises, upright rows 2x10 each

8. **Half-kneeling wood chops** 3x15 per side

9. **Dead bug** 3x10-15

10. **Quadriped opposite arm/leg lifts** 5 reps

**C. Conditioning** – 12-15 minutes moderate cardio

**D. Cool Down** + stretching

---

### **Day 5: Lower Body Focus #2**

**A. Warm-up + Activation (10-15 minutes)**

- March in place or light walk/jog 5 minutes
- Activation as before

**B. Strength Circuit (perform as circuit, 1-3 rounds)**

1. **Deadlifts** (barbell or trap bar, 3x8-10) - substitute Single leg romanian deadlifts if limited equipment

2. **Walking lunges with rotation** - 3x16 steps (using medicine ball or plate)

3. **Kettlebell swings** or broad jumps (3x15-20)

4. **Step up to reverse lunge** - per leg 3x5

5. **Glute bridges** (or hip thrusts) [3x12]

6. **Single leg stability ball curls** 3x8 per leg

7. **Single leg calf raises** - 3x12 per leg

8. **TRX single leg squat to row** - 3x8 per leg

9. **Rotational ball throws** against wall 3x8/side

**C. Cool Down (10 min)**

---

### **Day 6: Restorative / Active Recovery**

*Recovery walk 30-45 min or Yoga flow. Meditation & stretch + drink water.*

- Optional: 3-5 miles easy hike/jog if desired
- Core work: breathing exercises + plank variations
- Goal: prep for tomorrow

---

### **Day 7: Long Run or Long Hike + Mobility**

**Run/Hike:**
- Easy distance — 10-15 miles (Zone 2/3) or hike 2-4 hours

**Or**

**Fartlek run** 30 minutes — 4x3 minutes @half marathon pace/ recover 2 minutes; 5 min cool down

**And**

- Spend 10-20 minutes: stretching, mobility, roll out or NormaTec if available
- Consistent hydration and fueling practices relevant to race day
