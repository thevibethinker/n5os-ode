### Critical UX/Product Feedback

**Hiring Manager Experience - The "Playing Card" Approach**:
- Alex strongly recommended a visual "playing card" or "sports card" format for presenting candidates
- Show only the most relevant data points needed to decide if worth digging deeper
- **NOT** long text descriptions - those are "too long" and won't be read
- Components of each card:
  - Picture (smaller, less prominent)
  - Name
  - 2-3 pieces of relevant experience
  - 2-3 interesting stories (NOT boring resume bullet points)
  - Keep to one sentence each

**URL-Based Delivery System**:
- Host candidate "packs" on unique URLs (not PDFs or email attachments)
- Benefits:
  - Track metrics: click-through rate, profiles browsed, engagement
  - Tell entire story in one webpage
  - Sandwich approach: intro at top, candidate cards in middle, CTA at bottom
- Send 10-30 candidates per URL depending on quality/fit

**Categorization over Percentage Scores**:
- **Don't use**: "75% fit" or percentage match scores - feels algorithmic and corporate
- **Do use**: Thematic categories like:
  - "Clear Fits" - obvious matches on paper
  - "Hidden Gems" - career transitions, non-obvious but strong fits
  - Show trade-offs: 3-5 core strengths + 3-5 weaknesses for each candidate

### Go-To-Market Tactics

**Target Hiring Managers, Not HR**:
- HR likes their normal process - it's their job security
- Hiring managers (engineering managers, team leads) are:
  - Burned out from ATS review
  - Want digestible, human-centric info
  - Have authority to skip people from ATS
  - More receptive to this approach
- Look for job postings that list the actual hiring manager

**Cold Outreach Approach**:
- **Anti-pattern**: Long emails establishing relationship before showing candidates
- **Alex's experience**: Ignored all recruiters who tried to "establish relationship first"
- **Recommended approach**:
  - Just send the URL with candidates upfront
  - "Here are candidates. Do you want to pay for any? If not, no problem."
  - Goods up front - show the diamonds in first 5 minutes

**Timing Consideration**:
- Unclear whether newly posted jobs (engaged manager) or month-old jobs (burned-out manager) are better targets
- Needs testing

### Anonymization Strategy
- Keep candidates anonymous in initial bundle (no name/contact)
- Can show: first name, picture, blurb
- Only reveal full info after employer signs on or pays
- Protects both candidates and creates conversion funnel

### Product Role Positioning
- PM roles are interesting target because:
  - Less common than designer/engineer roles
  - "Insanely varied backgrounds" - chaotic to hire for
  - Engineer with 15 years experience = obviously skilled
  - PM could be career transition, IC contributor, varied paths
  - Perfect for Careerspan's strength: surfacing atypical profiles with context

### Pitch Event Advice
- Most free pitch events = low value (other founders, mostly practice)
- Value is in: overcoming anxiety, timing practice, projecting confidence
- $2k for pitch event is "a lot" - only worth it if high-value audience
- "Projecting confidence is half the battle" - even with best pitch/numbers
