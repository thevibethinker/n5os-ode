## Be Ready Platform Details

### Platform Mechanics
- **Talent Side**: 
  - Resume upload
  - 1-minute video answering critical thinking question
  - 500-word pitch
  - No job applications (passive talent pool)
- **Employer Side**:
  - Search database using job description
  - Green/yellow/red percent match algorithm
  - Names scrubbed (anonymized profiles)
  - Can click to interview from platform
  - Accept/decline workflow

### Current Traction (After 1 Year)
- **Talent**: 1,200 candidates on platform
- **Employers**: 145 companies
- **Placements**: 45 hires
- **Assessment**: "Very modest numbers" but meaningful for first year in university setting

### Target Market
- **Geography**: Ontario, Canada (expanding)
- **Company Size**: Under 200 employees (SME space, not just startups)
- **Industries**: Manufacturing, food processing, not just tech
- **Roles**: Broad - IT, marketing, various functions (no union or fractional roles)

### Talent Focus: Canadian Immigrants
- **Entry Routes**: Express Entry visa, international students, startup visa program
- **Challenge**: Perception that prestige = large companies (Google, banks)
- **Need**: Mindset shift to see SMEs as opportunity
- **Pain Point**: New to country, don't understand opportunity landscape

### Value Proposition Focus
> "For us, it's not about the technical skills, it's more. They already have the technical skills. What we focus on more on is their soft skills."

**Soft Skills Training**:
- Interview skills
- Language challenges
- Business etiquettes in Canada
- Cultural adaptation

**Explicitly NOT**:
- "One more AI course and one more cybersecurity course" - they have technical skills

## Careerspan Business Model Deep Dive

### Evolution: Coaching → Employer Sales
> "Providing advice is good. Giving someone a new resume is great. What is best or sort of what is ideal is actually taking them by the hand and saying, hey, this is the job for you."

**The Pivot**: From resume optimization (coin toss) to structural improvement of job-getting chances

### Current Model
1. **Target**: Communities with high-signal talent
2. **Offer**: Free coaching tool (guided self-reflection)
3. **Data**: Collect structured candidate information
4. **Match**: Use data to match to employers
5. **Monetize**: Charge employers for access to talent pool

### Positioning
> "In many ways we're like a virtual in house recruiter for these communities"

### Technical Capabilities Highlighted

**Multilingual Support**:
> "Accepts 50 different languages, by the way, and outputs English. So it's very sort of inclusive in that regard, especially for immigrants"

**Traceability**:
> "You can actually say, hey, this person at this trait because this situation where they did this thing that exhibited that quality. So that's the level of traceability that our system sort of has"

**Semantic Analysis**:
> "It actually breaks down the role and tries to figure out what kind of individual or kinds of individual would be suited for this role as sort of a composite of qualities. And then that's what it sort of scans you against on a semantic basis"

**Outputs Generated**: Resume bullets, interview answers, comprehensive preparation

### Fundraising Status
- **Raised**: $1M pre-seed
- **Investor**: Family office in India (still supportive)
- **Current**: Paused bridge round to focus on GTM and revenue
- **Next**: Plan to resume in ~3 months with revenue traction
- **Seeking**: "Smart capital"
