# SHRM WorkTech Accelerator — Careerspan Strategic Positioning

## Executive Strategic Assessment

**Bottom line:** Careerspan fills a **critical gap** in SHRM's WTA portfolio and aligns with their **2025 organizational pivot** from recruiting-volume to engagement-quality. Position as the **continuous engagement layer** that solves both recruiting (persistent pain) and employee development (2025 priority).

---

## I. Market Context Analysis

### SHRM's 2025 Strategic Shift (Confirmed via Research)

**What Changed:**
- **2024 Priority #1:** Recruiting (43% of HR professionals)
- **2025 Priority #1:** Leadership & Manager Development (41%)
- **2025 Priority #2:** Employee Experience (37%)
- **2025 Priority #3:** Learning & Development (25%)

**Why It Matters:**
SHRM is actively moving their membership **away from reactive hiring** toward **proactive talent development and engagement**. But recruiting challenges persist:
- 51% report too few qualified applicants [^1]
- 50% lose talent to competitors [^1]
- 41% cite candidate ghosting [^1]
- 75%+ difficulty filling full-time roles [^2]

**The Paradox:** HR leaders want to focus on development, but they're still drowning in recruiting fires.

**Careerspan's Opportunity:** Solve the recruiting problem *through* development/engagement infrastructure.

4. ✗ Alumni/community-based distribution models (no one leveraging verified professional networks)
5. ✗ Continuous engagement that transcends job search (all tools are job-search-triggered)

**Careerspan fills the "quality talent pipeline that doesn't go cold" gap.**

## VI. Messaging Framework for Application

### Core Narrative Arc:

**1. The Problem (Align with SHRM Research):**
> "SHRM's 2025 State of the Workplace research reveals a paradox: HR leaders want to shift from reactive recruiting to proactive employee development, but they're still drowning in talent shortages (51% report too few applicants), ghosting (41%), and competition for quality candidates (50% lose talent to competitors). Traditional recruiting tools optimize for *volume and speed*, but HR's real problem is *quality and continuity*—how do you build relationships with top talent before you need to hire them?"

**2. The Insight (Novel Angle):**
> "We realized the recruiting problem is actually an *engagement* problem. The transactional job search model (job posting → application → interview → hire) creates a feast-or-famine cycle where talent pipelines go cold the moment someone accepts an offer or stops actively job hunting. Meanwhile, the best candidates—the ones with high agency and conscientiousness—are cultivating their careers *continuously*, not just when they're job searching. The question is: where do they go for ongoing development, and how can employers maintain relationships with them?"

**3. The Solution (Careerspan's Unique Approach):**
> "Careerspan is a two-sided marketplace that solves both sides of this problem. On the supply side, we partner with alumni associations and professional communities (Rice, Emory, Future Higher Education Group with 5,000+ members) to offer continuous career coaching to their members—creating ongoing engagement that transcends job search. On the demand side, employers replace their traditional application link with our 'magic link,' gaining access to a warm pipeline of pre-qualified, continuously-engaged professionals who demonstrate high agency and conscientiousness through their usage patterns. Instead of ghosting, we maintain continuity. Instead of cold outreach, we cultivate warm relationships. Instead of resume screening, we provide behavioral signals of candidate quality."
