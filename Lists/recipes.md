### Ingredients



### Pasta

- 400g spaghetti (preferably Italian brand)
- Salt for pasta water

### Sauce

- 150g pancetta or guanciale, diced
- 4 large egg yolks
- 2 whole eggs
- 100g Pecorino Romano, freshly grated
- 50g Parmesan, freshly grated
- Freshly ground black pepper (generous amount)

### Optional

- Fresh parsley for garnish
- Extra Pecorino for serving

### Instructions

1. **Prepare the pasta water**: Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. The water should taste like the sea.

2. **Cook the pancetta**: While water heats, cook the diced pancetta in a large skillet over medium heat until crispy and fat is rendered (about 6-8 minutes). Remove from heat and set aside, keeping the rendered fat in the pan.

3. **Make the egg mixture**: In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, whole eggs, grated cheeses, and a generous amount of black pepper. The mixture should be thick and creamy.

4. **Cook the pasta**: Add spaghetti to boiling water and cook to al dente (usually 1-2 minutes less than package instructions).

5. **Reserve pasta water**: Before draining, reserve 1 cup of hot pasta water.

6. **Combine**: Drain the pasta (do not rinse) and immediately add to the skillet with the pancetta. Remove from heat completely.

7. **Add egg mixture**: Pour the egg mixture over the hot pasta and toss vigorously. The residual heat will create a creamy sauce. If sauce is too thick, add reserved pasta water a tablespoon at a time until silky.

8. **Serve immediately**: Plate immediately with extra cheese and black pepper.

### Notes

- **Critical**: Remove the pan from heat before adding eggs to prevent scrambling
- **Authentic**: No cream in traditional carbonara—the eggs and cheese create the sauce
- **Guanciale vs. Pancetta**: Guanciale (cured pork cheek) is traditional and has a deeper flavor; pancetta is a good substitute
