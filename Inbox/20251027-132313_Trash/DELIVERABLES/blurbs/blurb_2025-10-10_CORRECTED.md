## The Blurb

**Careerspan** is an AI-powered career platform that builds richer candidate profiles through coaching-based interactions. Unlike traditional job boards that rely on keyword matching, we engage professionals through guided AI coaching sessions (20+ minutes minimum) that capture motivations, values, and fit factors beyond what a resume shows.

What makes our user base valuable for Lensa:

**Quality Over Volume**
- 75-80% email open rates, 15-16% click-through rates on job distributions
- 2-3% monthly reactivation rate of dormant users (organic engagement)
- Users who complete coaching sessions are highly activated and conscientious job seekers

**Fresh, Engaged Job Seekers**
- Not bouncing between platforms - Careerspan offers career development beyond job search
- Users return for ongoing career reflection and profile updates, not just when actively searching
- Target audience: career-conscious professionals focused on fit and growth, not just any job

**Distribution Partnership Alignment**
- Current scale: 700-1,000 activated users (growing 200-400/week starting October 2025)
- Focused initially on product roles in Boston, SF, NYC with plans to expand
- Strong geographic and role filtering capability ensures relevant job matching

**Technical Integration**
- Clean, structured data capture (no messy scraping on your end)
- Ability to route directly to Lensa registration/application flows
- AppCast integration for seamless tracking and optimization

Careerspan partners with high-signal professional communities (Ivy League alumni, McKinsey alumni networks) to distribute our coaching platform as a member benefit. This community-first approach means our users are vetted, engaged, and serious about their careers - exactly the "fresh job seekers" Lensa values for sustainable user acquisition.

We're excited to test organic, syndicated, and direct agency content through Lensa's feeds, with dedicated analyst support to optimize toward mutual ROI. Starting small at 15¢ CPC with $2,500 test budgets allows us to prove value before scaling.
