# Meeting Detailed Recap

**Meeting**: Greenlight Partnership Discovery Call  
**Date**: September 12, 2025  
**Attendees**: Allie Cialeo (Recruiter, Greenlight), Paul Lee (Product, Greenlight), Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan), Logan Currie (Careerspan)

## Context
Partnership discovery call with Greenlight, referred by James (Careerspan advisor). Allie joined Greenlight in mid-July and immediately faced 22 open roles across the company. Greenlight recently paused hiring for Series B fundraising, which is announcing Monday (Sept 15).

## Meeting Flow

### Careerspan Introduction (Vrijen)
- Origin story: Started as career coaching tool for thousands of hours helping people build better careers
- Core insight: The big gap is how people present themselves - alignment and relevance matter more than pure qualifications
- Evolution: Realized the coaching process created deep candidate profiles that go beyond achievements to show *how* skills were developed
- Current state: Decentralized talent network drawing from best professional communities (Lenny's, ex-McKinsey, Stripe alumni, etc.)
- Free for candidates, paid by employers for matches

### Greenlight's Hiring Challenges

**Allie's Context:**
- Joined mid-July, immediately hit with 22 open roles
- Initial focus on sales and operations (most urgent), just started tech recruiting a week ago
- Current approach: Agency partners only (no bandwidth for direct sourcing)
- Tech priorities through EOY: Site reliability engineer, ML engineer (senior/staff/principal), 1-2 full stack engineers
- Also have mechanical engineers (multi-disciplinary company)
- In-person 4 days/week in New York

**Paul's Engineering Hiring Experience:**
- Biggest challenge: People look strong on paper but weak technically
- Not outright deception, but market full of people reaching above current competence
- Example: Staff engineer from Autodesk (8+ years) - worst technical screen ever, likely coasting
- Referrals are king - hard to find amazing random candidates
- Many experienced people either spoken for, happy where they are, or expensive
- Seeing more people cut from big companies now appearing in interviews

### Signal vs. Noise Problem (Allie's Question)
Allie raised concern about Lenny's List: Used it at previous role in agency capacity, but quality dropped as it scaled. How does Careerspan prevent saturation?

**Vrijen's Answer:**
- Matching system is semantic, not keyword-based
- Decomposes JD + subjective preferences into what's actually being sought
- Scans every resume and story told by candidates
- Gives candidates head start to fill gaps in their stories
- Updates employer when candidates improve fit
- Problem isn't always lacking something - often just haven't highlighted it properly

**Logan's Take:**
- Focusing on micro-communities (100-300 person communities)
- Higher signal than mega-communities like Lenny's
- Working with niche community founders directly
- Secret sauce: Combination of scanning segments within large communities + 2-3 niche communities

### Proposed Partnership Structure

**What Careerspan Offers:**
- Provide JDs + subjective context from Ed (head of engineering) or Allie
- Scan 200-400 new candidates weekly (emphasis on product + engineering)
- Weekly cadence (or preferred): List of names + analysis + resumes
- Feedback loop: "These were lacking X" → add to algorithm for next scan
- **All scanning free - only pay for intros to candidates they like**
- Pricing: ~00 for warm intro (proof-of-concept pricing)
- 15 minutes/week max time commitment from Greenlight

**Distribution Method:**
- "Magic Link" system: JD embedded in link
- Candidates signing up with Magic Link auto-create Greenlight application
- Can keep company name stealth/confidential
- Careerspan manually distributes to top communities and micro-communities
- Candidates go through Careerspan (not flooding Greenlight ATS)
- White glove follow-up: If interested in someone, pass questions, candidate updates profile, Careerspan provides incremental analysis

**Additional Value:**
- Passive talent pipeline: People not actively looking
- Long-term matching: "In January I have a role, want great candidates, zero effort"
- Prevent future "22 roles in July" situations

### Greenlight's Response
**Allie:**
- Open to test run
- Wants honesty if it becomes too much to manage alongside other agencies
- Loves idea of pre-vetted, higher funnel candidates
- Excited about passive talent even if they need convincing over time
- Confirmed: Stealth/confidential approach better (prevents rejected candidates from applying directly)

**Paul:**
- No pressing product openings currently (just hired designer)
- Likes keeping compelling people "on the bench" for future growth
- Not P0, but open to expanding network
- Example: Engineer from 6 months ago recently re-emerged
- Worth cultivating relationships with sharp people

### Next Steps
- Allie will send Series B announcement when it goes live Monday (for outreach messaging)
- Start with lightweight, low-effort partnership
- Focus on tech roles: SRE, ML engineer, full stack engineers
- Geographic focus: New York (in-person 4 days/week)
- Stealth/confidential distribution

## Key Insights

1. **Timing**: Greenlight just closed Series B, has 22 open roles, and Allie is underwater - perfect timing for partnership

2. **Pain Points Validated**:
   - Selection problem (people not as strong as they appear)
   - Volume problem (too many roles, not enough time)
   - Quality drop in large communities (Lenny's example)
   - Referrals still king but not scalable

3. **Strategic Fit**:
   - Greenlight wants pre-filtered, higher-quality candidates
   - Willing to trade slightly more effort for better signal
   - Values passive talent cultivation
   - Appreciates low time commitment (15 min/week)

4. **Risk Mitigation**:
   - Stealth approach prevents rejected candidates from direct applying
   - Low/no upfront cost reduces risk
   - Test-and-learn approach with honest feedback expectation

5. **Future Potential**:
   - Product roles in 2026 (Paul)
   - Long-term passive pipeline cultivation
   - Potentially expand beyond tech to other functions
