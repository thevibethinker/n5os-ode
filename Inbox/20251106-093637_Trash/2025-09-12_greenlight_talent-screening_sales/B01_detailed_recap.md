# B01 - Detailed Recap

## Context

Discovery call between Careerspan (Vrijen, Logan) and Greenlight (Allie, Paul) on September 12, 2025. James (shared advisor) facilitated introduction. Greenlight is construction tech company that recently closed fundraising round and opened hiring for 22 roles across organization, with tech priorities being SRE, ML engineer, and full stack engineers.

## Opening

Brief pleasantries. Allie in Cape Cod for wedding (delayed flight, arrived post-midnight). Paul in Greenpoint, Brooklyn. Vrijen mentioned Brooklyn bathhouses as weekend recommendation. Acknowledges post-fundraise timing is busy for startups.

Logan introduced as "Logs" - James apparently uses same nickname. Casual rapport established quickly.

## Company Introduction (Vrijen)

**Career Coaching Origins**: Careerspan started as career coaching tool. Both founders are career coaches with thousands of hours helping people build better careers. Core insight: biggest gap for most people is how they present themselves, not their qualifications. Built coaching tool to help candidates align their story and highlight relevant background.

**Evolution to Talent Matching**: Along the way, realized they'd built a powerful way to get to know people deeply - not just achievements or skills, but traceability around *how* they developed those skills. This enables comparison across different contexts.

**Current Model**: Tool is free for candidates (everyone gets chance to build best possible story). Use that data to match candidates to employers looking for specific roles. Built decentralized talent network drawing from best professional communities online: Lenny's product community, ex-McKinsey, Stripe alumni, etc.

**Value Prop**: Employers can quick-scan against onboarded candidates and identify great matches based on semantic understanding, not just keywords.

## Allie's Challenge: Community Quality Degradation

**Past Experience**: At previous role, used Lenny's List in agency capacity to get intros to candidates through professional network. Initially excellent quality.

**Degradation Pattern**: "the bar for talent slipped quite a bit from like... the earlier stages of Lenny's. I think it just got almost too big."

**Question Posed**: How does Careerspan navigate maintaining exceptional talent quality while scaling network reach? How prevent saturation with wide network?

## Vrijen's Response: Semantic Matching System

**Not Keyword/Taxonomy Based**: System decomposes job description + subjective requirements (what you want but wouldn't put on JD) into semantic understanding of what's being looked for.

**Deep Candidate Analysis**: System goes through every resume and every story each shortlisted candidate has told, comes up with explanation of fit.

**Candidate Head Start Feature**: System identifies gaps in candidate's story, gives them time to fill gaps if possible. If they can fill gaps, system updates employer: "actually this candidate is more fitted."

**Two-Part Problem Solution**:
1. Some people genuinely lack qualification (system filters these)
2. Most people *have* qualification but haven't highlighted it properly (system surfaces this by giving candidates structured way to demonstrate depth)

**Analogy**: "as though they spend a month filling out a Workday profile, right? But you get to scan that in a second" - candidates put in significant effort upfront, employers get instant comprehension.

## Paul's Pain Point: Technical Screen Failures

**Current Challenge**: "biggest challenge was there's a lot of even people who have experience, they're just not as technically strong as they seem to be on paper."

**Pattern**: Many candidates look qualified, pass initial phone screen, but "once you got them on like a technical round became pretty obvious pretty fast."

**Specific Example**: Staff engineer at Autodesk, 8+ years tenure, AEC tech adjacent to Greenlight's domain, checked many boxes. Paul: "our engineers really was, like, the worst technical screen they've ever done in their life. This person's probably just been coasting there."

**Referral Preference**: "for me, I'm always, like, always referral as king... it's definitely been hard. Just, like, meet somebody completely random who's, like, amazing, and it doesn't come through some network or at least has some level of backlink."

## Vrijen's Follow-Up Question

**Root Cause Inquiry**: Is the resume-vs-reality gap due to (a) outright deception, or (b) insufficient pre-filtering systems?

**Paul's Response**: "I'll hope for the best in people's intentions" (leans toward #b). Notes it's a lot to ask someone to complete technical screening with no guarantee of moving forward or talking to actual person.

**Market Dynamics Shift**: Post-layoffs, many places (including Greenlight) looking for experienced candidates. Those candidates are either (a) spoken for / happy / expensive, or (b) were potentially coasting at big companies and now getting exposed in startup interview processes where "we need high quality."

**RIF Context**: "there's a lot of just, like, cutting of unnecessary most probably. So now I feel like those people are kind of starting to bubble up more interviews and like, kind of be exposed."

## Vrijen's Strategic Question: Current Funnel

Asked Paul and Allie: Based on these challenges, what are your current pipelines? What strategies have you concluded are most viable? How has this affected approach to sourcing/staffing?

## Allie's Current State

**Tenure**: Joined mid-July 2025 (very new to Greenlight)

**Hiring Pause Context**: Upon arrival, hiring was paused due to fundraising focus - all efforts on due diligence. As soon as fundraise closed, "floodgates of recruiting open: 22 across the company."

**Prioritization**: James and Ben (leadership) directed her to focus first on sales and operations/client services teams (most urgent). Just started digging into tech hiring about a week ago.

**Current Sourcing**: "my only funnel right now, because I just don't have bandwidth still to source across all these roles is agency partners." Ed (head of engineering) brought on agencies himself; Allie brought on others from her past relationships. Started late last week, very early in funnel.

**Agency Process**: Agencies pre-vetting candidates before Allie even sees them. Candidates moving directly to hiring manager (Ed) calls after agency screen.

**Tech Priorities**: As of today (Sept 12):
1. Site Reliability Engineer
2. Machine Learning Engineer (senior/staff/principal level)
3. 1-2 Full Stack Engineers (senior level preferred, might consider exceptional mid-level after senior hire)

These are end-of-year headcount targets.

**Future Hiring Plans**: Goal is to bring on tech recruiter or recruiter with technical lean who could help both tech hiring AND non-software technical roles (mentioned mechanical engineers on team).

## Vrijen's Process Question

**Filter Preference Inquiry**: Would Allie prefer (a) stronger pre-filter with higher threshold, then more open to screening people who pass, or (b) lower threshold for getting into screening, but screening is where real filtering happens?

**Allie's Response**: "early in the funnel to see to know what we are today" but leans toward **strong upfront screening being critical**.

**Current Process**: Ed reviews resumes after agencies submit, tells Allie who he wants to speak with. He sends feedback on why he likes certain candidates. Allie is "mental mapping" his preferences to eventually help with screening herself as things wind down on sales/ops side.

**Value of Recruiters**: Helpful for volume perspective, seeing diverse candidate sets at top of funnel. Then need to figure out "a little deeper in the funnel, like after Ed's call, like where we need them to hit... more deeply."

## Product Clarification

**Allie's Question**: Is Careerspan only for product roles?

**Vrijen's Answer**: Starting talent pool filling with product and engineering. Tool is embedded in generalist communities and open for anyone to join. Hope is pool will fill out other functions over time.
