# SHRM Application Writing Framework

**Purpose:** Systematic approach for transforming Careerspan information into compelling accelerator application responses

---

## SHRM Evaluation Lens

### What SHRM is Really Assessing

1. **Investment Quality**
   - Business viability (can they scale?)
   - Exit potential (is there a path to liquidity?)
   - Founder execution capability (can V deliver?)
   - Market timing (is now the right time?)

2. **Strategic Alignment**
   - HR/workforce innovation impact
   - Alignment with SHRM research themes
   - Fills gap in current cohort (we solve engagement, not just volume)
   - Showcaseable (can SHRM use us as proof of their thesis?)

3. **PR Value**
   - Can they point to us as exemplar of HR tech innovation?
   - Do we make them look smart for choosing us?
   - Will we generate positive visibility for SHRM?

### The "Outstanding" Application

SHRM wants to see:
- **Confidence without arrogance** - "We do X" not "We hope to do X"
- **Evidence over claims** - Every assertion backed by data
- **Differentiation clarity** - Obvious why we're different, not "better"
- **Narrative coherence** - Full application tells one clear story
- **Founder authenticity** - Sounds like a real human, not corporate deck
- **Strategic awareness** - We know their research, their gaps, their priorities

## Voice Transformation Framework

### Source Material Characteristics
**Core Brief voice:** Comprehensive, factual, organized, metric-dense, strategic

### Target Application Voice
**Accelerator application voice:** Direct, confident, human, evidence-based, compelling

### Transformation Rules

#### 1. Convert Metrics → Proof Points
**Before:** "47% of candidates who engaged with a coach completed onboarding"  
**After:** "Candidates who work with a coach complete onboarding at 47% vs. 35% industry average—proof that engagement, not just volume, drives outcomes."

*Rule:* Always contextualize metrics. Show *why* the number matters, what it proves.

#### 2. Convert Features → Business Impact
**Before:** "Magic link integration replaces traditional application submission"  
**After:** "One client cut time-to-apply from 12 minutes to 90 seconds with our magic link integration. Candidates don't fill forms—they start conversations."

*Rule:* Lead with outcome, then mechanism. "What happened" before "how it works."

#### 3. Convert Strategy → Confident Positioning
**Before:** "We believe AI recruiting should focus on engagement rather than volume"  
**After:** "AI recruiting solves the wrong problem. Everyone chases more applicants. We convert the ones you already have."

*Rule:* Replace "we believe" with declarative statements. State the world as it is, not as opinion.

#### 4. Convert Technical Details → Human Outcomes
**Before:** "LLM-powered coaching workflow with structured data capture"  
**After:** "Candidates get AI coaching that feels human. Employers get structured insights they can act on."

*Rule:* Technical complexity lives in the brief. Applications explain outcomes, not architecture.

#### 5. Convert Differentiation → Market Positioning
**Before:** "Unlike other platforms that focus on ATS integration, we prioritize candidate experience"  
**After:** "Every competitor solves for employers first. We're the only platform that improves both candidate conversion and employer efficiency simultaneously."

*Rule:* Don't say "unlike X we do Y." Say "everyone does X. Only we do Y."

## Sentence-Level Style Guide

### Do This
- **Short sentences:** 10-20 words average
- **Active voice:** "We built" not "was built by us"
- **Concrete nouns:** "Candidates" not "users"
- **Strong verbs:** "Cut" not "reduced"
- **Specific numbers:** "47%" not "nearly half"

### Don't Do This
- **Corporate speak:** leverage, synergy, ecosystem, stakeholders
- **Hedge words:** very, really, quite, somewhat, fairly
- **Qualifier phrases:** "It's important to note that..." / "In order to..."
- **Passive constructions:** "The decision was made..."
- **Vague claims:** "significant improvement" / "strong results"

## Strategic Repetition Rules

### Good Repetition (Reinforce Core Themes)
- **What:** Key differentiators (engagement over volume, both sides win, magic link)
- **How:** Different angles each time
- **Example:**
  - Q1: "Magic link replaces application submission"
  - Q3: "Magic link integration drove 67% engagement rate at Client X"
  - Q7: "Magic link became client's #1 requested feature"

*Each mention adds new information or perspective*

### Bad Repetition (Redundant Explanation)
- **What:** Explaining same mechanism with same words
- **How:** Copy-paste paragraphs across answers
- **Example:**
  - Q2: "Magic link allows candidates to bypass traditional application forms by generating a unique URL that..."
  - Q4: "Magic link allows candidates to bypass traditional application forms by generating a unique URL that..."

*No new information, wastes word count*

### Information Budgeting
Track deployment of:
1. **Metrics** (engagement %, completion %, time savings)
2. **Client stories** (specific use cases)
3. **Product features** (magic link, AI coaching, structured insights)
4. **Market positioning** (engagement vs. volume thesis)

Deploy strategically across questions. Save best evidence for questions where it has maximum impact.

## Pre-Answer Rubric Template

For each question, create rubric defining "outstanding" using this structure:

### What SHRM is Really Asking
*(Surface question vs. deep evaluation intent)*

### Outstanding Answer Includes
1. **[Criterion 1]** - What excellent looks like
2. **[Criterion 2]** - What excellent looks like
3. **[Criterion 3]** - What excellent looks like

### Outstanding Answer Avoids
- **[Anti-pattern 1]** - What to never do
- **[Anti-pattern 2]** - What to never do

### Evidence Requirements
- What specific metrics/proof points needed
- What level of detail appropriate

### Strategic Context
- How this answer fits in overall narrative
- What to reinforce from previous answers
- What to introduce fresh

---

## Multi-Step Answer Generation Process

### Step 1: Create Rubric
Use template above. Define "outstanding" from SHRM's lens.

### Step 2: Map Core Brief
- Which sections of Core Brief address rubric criteria?
- What metrics/stories best prove our points?
- What's the hierarchy (what matters most)?

### Step 3: Check Repetition Context
- What have we already said?
- What needs reinforcement (different angle)?
- What's fresh deployment?

### Step 4: Draft Answer
- Lead with impact, not mechanism
- Use transformation rules (metrics → proof points, etc.)
- Apply voice guidelines (short sentences, active, concrete)
- Self-assess against rubric

### Step 5: Present with Rationale
- Show draft
- Explain strategic choices
- Note what's deployed vs. reserved
- Highlight any trade-offs made

## Voice Exemplars

### Bad (Corporate/Hedging)
"We believe that our innovative platform has the potential to significantly improve candidate engagement through our proprietary AI-powered coaching solution, which we hope will help transform the recruiting landscape."

**Problems:** believes, potential to, significantly, proprietary, hope will, transform landscape (buzzword)

### Good (Direct/Confident)
"Candidates who work with our AI coach convert at 47%—12 points higher than the industry. We prove engagement beats volume."

**Why:** Specific metric, clear claim, short sentences, no hedging, stakes out position
