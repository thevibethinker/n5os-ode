# B31: Stakeholder Research

**Perspective:** Speaking as Director of Career Services at liberal arts college with 15+ years experience across multiple institutions (Colby, Bowdoin, Bennington)

---

## Insight 1: Career services professionals manually vet every employer relationship because incumbent platforms (handshake) provide no filtering - creating unsustainable workload that prevents scaling

**Evidence:**
> "So I hand it over to Davis Connects... The lazy thing to do is to stick it all on handshake or to give them a link... I think it requires a fair amount of stewardship and qualifying... my feeling is great opportunities come in all sizes... I take the time, you know, I really do, to vet them and to have proper conversation and to fall in love with them and to understand, you know, what is reasonable."

**Why it matters:** Career services directors will champion platforms that maintain quality standards while reducing manual vetting time - this is the core value proposition for higher ed partnerships, not just student experience.

## Insight 2: Alumni career transition support (especially classes 2017-2022) is becoming higher priority than undergraduate placement for career services offices - established professionals seeking pivots have immediate needs and higher urgency

**Evidence:**
> "I actually think there's a massive opportunity to do that with alumni networks, you know... a lot of my former students are reaching out to me. The class of 2017 to 2022, they're all pivoting right now... If you haven't chosen an MBA, they're looking seriously at what's next. And it's not a great market out there."

**Why it matters:** Go-to-market strategy should prioritize alumni job distribution over student pilots for higher ed partnerships - faster time-to-value, more immediate pain point, less institutional friction than changing undergraduate curriculum.

## Insight 3: Founders have completely abandoned reviewing inbound applications - they post jobs for legal compliance but exclusively hire through warm referrals with significantly raised bars, making traditional job boards and ATS systems obsolete

**Evidence:**
> "From founders that they don't even look at inbound... They don't even look. They post for legal reasons. They don't... They really qualify people in the community that have been their networks."

**Why it matters:** This explains why handshake and traditional career services platforms are failing - they're built around "post and collect applications" model that no longer reflects hiring reality, creating urgency for alternative approaches like Careerspan's filtering and warm intro facilitation.

## Insight 4: Top-tier MBA programs and consulting firms are experiencing unprecedented hiring contraction (Columbia MBAs still unemployed 5+ months post-graduation, Bain summer analysts not receiving return offers) - first time career services directors are seeing this breakdown

**Evidence:**
> "I have three former Colby students of mine who wrapped up at Columbia Business School this May, and they are still looking for work. That is the first time I've ever had that happen... top MBA programs equals employment immediately. And it's been rough... for the first time ever, I have, you know, top students who spent their junior summer at Bain did not get return offers for the first time."

**Why it matters:** This validates the severity of current job market dysfunction and demonstrates that traditional "gold standard" pathways (top MBAs, MBB internships) are no longer reliable - creating urgency for alternative career development approaches and increasing receptivity to new platforms like Careerspan.
