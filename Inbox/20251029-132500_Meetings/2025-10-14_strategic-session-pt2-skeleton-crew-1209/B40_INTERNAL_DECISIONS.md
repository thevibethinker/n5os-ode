### Key Strategic Decisions

#### 1. **Pivot to "Career Agent" Passive Hiring Model**
- **Decision**: Shift from active job search platform to passive hiring agent that works on behalf of top talent
- **Rationale**: Aligns with "slow hiring" / passive candidate market rather than competing in active job search space; leverages existing tech with lower maintenance burden
- **Supporting Evidence**: 
  - "We're going straight out for the passive hiring... that's what the career agent is"
  - "You don't want an agent you want [one] running on your behalf"
- **Implementation Status**: Concept validated, needs product simplification + messaging overhaul

#### 2. **Adopt "Skeleton Crew" Operating Model**
- **Decision**: Reduce to minimal viable team (V + Logan + part-time Ilsa) by end of year
- **Rationale**: Dramatically reduces burn to ~$20-25K/month, makes business sustainable without VC funding, reduces pressure
- **Supporting Evidence**:
  - "What is the skeleton crew crop product that you and I can then... with less pressure... just you and me, like, kind of get out to people"
  - "All of our costs to go, so fucking low... 50 percent of one person's salary for part-time, or like, 75 of one Ilsa salary for part-time"
- **Target Date**: January 1, 2025 ("hibernation day")

#### 3. **Target Agency Fee Revenue Model (Not VC Funding)**
- **Decision**: Monetize via placement fees (20% of first-year salary model), split between candidate incentive + employer discount + Careerspan cut
- **Rationale**: Proven recruiting model, aligns incentives, doesn't require VC dilution/control
- **Supporting Evidence**:
  - "We get a [cut]... we should capture at least like 50, if not more, of the value... 20 grand, we got to give the employer back 10 grand... split... Seven Grand US three grand to candidate"
- **Implementation**: Requires building out hiring manager network + placement infrastructure

#### 4. **Prioritize Personal Brand Building Over Company Scaling**
- **Decision**: Focus V + Logan effort on content/thought leadership rather than operational scaling
- **Rationale**: Builds optionality (consulting/advisory work), drives inbound for agent product, leverages natural strengths
- **Supporting Evidence**:
  - "Personalities... we almost don't leverage it as much as we could... we don't do a maximum extent. That's also because we're busy trying to do something we're not good at, which is around a startup company"
  - "We having proven it out. We just need like little bits of money to help us scale it make a perfectly great lifestyle business"

#### 5. **Abandon Active VC Fundraising**
- **Decision**: No more investor pitches or raise attempts; accept bootstrapped/lifestyle trajectory
- **Rationale**: VC ownership = loss of control, exhaustion from 3-year grind, preference for autonomy
- **Supporting Evidence**:
  - Logan: "I have found this like instinctive revulsion to freezing BC [VC] money"
  - "The problem with going to BC route... somebody fucking owns you. You have to work for somebody else"
- **Philosophical Shift**: From "unicorn or bust" to "sustainable lifestyle business with exit optionality"

#### 6. **Team Wind-Down Plan with Selective Retention**
- **Decision**: Wind down team by end-of-year, with special retention offer for Ilsa through February
- **Rationale**: Reduces burn while preserving critical operational knowledge; treats key contributors fairly
- **Supporting Evidence**:
