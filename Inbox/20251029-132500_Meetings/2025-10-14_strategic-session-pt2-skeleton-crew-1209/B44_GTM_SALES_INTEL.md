### Go-to-Market Strategy

#### Core GTM Positioning Shift

- **Old Positioning**: Job search platform for active candidates
- **New Positioning**: Talent agency with proprietary tech for passive hiring
- **Key Quote**: "We are a talent agency. We have proprietary Tech, and this is what we do"
- **Messaging Hierarchy**: Agency first, tech second (not "tech company that also does recruiting")

#### Target Customer Segments

**Primary: High-Quality Passive Candidates**

- **Profile**: Top talent, currently employed, not actively looking
- **Pain Point**: Miss opportunities because not actively searching
- **Value Prop**: "You've got this, like working on your behalf" - automated opportunity surfacing
- **Acquisition**: Personal brand content, alumni groups, community partnerships
- **Economics**: Earn $7K placement bonus when hired

**Secondary: Hiring Managers (Supply Side)**

- **Profile**: Department heads with hiring authority, frustrated with HR bottlenecks
- **Pain Point**: Want to tap networks directly, bypass slow HR processes
- **Value Prop**: "Surface things directly to the hiring manager" from vetted talent pool
- **Acquisition**: Direct outreach from V/Logan's networks, referrals from successful placements
- **Economics**: Pay \~$10K less than traditional 20% agency fee

#### Distribution Channels

**Channel 1: Founder Personal Brands**

- **Approach**: Content/thought leadership → Coaching/advisory → Tool adoption
- **V Quote**: "We can actually be liberated to then just build our personal brands in a way that's aligned with the product"
- **Advantage**: Zero acquisition cost, high trust, attracts ideal customers
- **Timeline**: Q1 2026 onward (post-skeleton crew)

**Channel 2: Direct Network Outreach (Hiring Managers)**

- **Approach**: V/Logan reach out to hiring manager friends directly
- **Script**: "Would you be willing to do this, like, do you have any hiring coming up in the next six months?"
- **Target**: 5-10 initial HMs from warm network
- **Scale**: Each successful placement generates 1-2 referrals
- **Timeline**: Q1 2026 pilot

**Channel 3: Alumni Groups & Communities**

- **Approach**: "Hand out to universities. Hand out to alumni groups"
- **Value Prop**: Free tool for members, community gets placement success stories
- **Scalability**: High - once integrated, self-serve adoption
- **Timeline**: Q2 2026 (after pilot phase proves model)

**Channel 4: First Customer Testimonials**

- **Strategy**: Place Danny + Rockwell via Careerspan as first customers
- **Quote**: "We fucking pay people when they get hired"
- **Use Case**: Proof of concept, testimonial content, iron out UX bugs
- **Timeline**: Q4 2025 / Q1 2026

### Sales Process & Conversion

#### Candidate "Sales" Process

1. **Awareness**: Personal brand content, alumni group partnership
2. **Interest**: "Passive hiring agent" positioning resonates
3. **Setup**: Create profile, set "what would make me move" criteria
4. **Activation**: Tool runs in background, surfaces opportunities automatically
5. **Conversion**: Accept match, interview, get placed, earn $7K bonus

**Key Insight**: No traditional "sales" needed - self-serve setup, automated matching

#### Hiring Manager "Sales" Process

1. **Outreach**: Direct ask from V/Logan ("Do you have hiring coming up?")
2. **Value Prop**: "We'll surface vetted candidates from your network, you only pay on placement"
3. **Privacy Assurance**: "We won't share your company \[until match\]"
4. **Setup**: Share role description, ideal candidate profile
5. **Engagement**: Review matches, accept/pass, interview, hire
6. **Payment**: Pay reduced agency fee (\~50% of standard)

**Key Insight**: Requires human trust-building initially, then becomes self-serve dashboard

### Pricing & Unit Economics

#### Revenue Model: Agency Fee Split

- **Industry Standard**: 20% of first-year salary (\~$20K for $100K role)
- **Careerspan Split**: 
  - **Employer Savings**: \~$10K (50% discount from standard fee)
  - **Candidate Bonus**: \~$7K (unprecedented in recruiting)
  - **Careerspan Cut**: \~$3K (15% margin)
- **Value Prop**: Win-win-win vs. traditional recruiting (employer saves, candidate earns, Careerspan profits)

#### Operating Cost Target (Skeleton Crew)

- **Monthly OpEx Target**: $20-25K 
  - Founders (2): \~$15-20K total (implied minimal salaries)
  - Infrastructure: \~$5K (hosting, AI inference, tools)
- **Break-Even**: 7-8 placements/month at $3K margin each
- **Lifestyle Business Threshold**: 10-15 placements/month = $30-45K/month revenue

#### Scalability Without VC

- **V Quote**: "If somebody just need like little bits of money to help us scale it make a perfectly great lifestyle business"
- **Growth Mechanism**: Placements → Testimonials → Referrals → More hiring managers → More placements
- **Capital Needs**: Minimal - no paid acquisition, no sales team, no office

### Competitive Differentiation

#### vs. Traditional Recruiting Agencies

- **Cost**: 50% cheaper for employers
- **Candidate Value**: $7K bonus vs. nothing
- **Speed**: Direct to hiring manager vs. HR bottleneck
- **Tech**: Automated matching vs. manual recruiter work

#### vs. Job Search Platforms (LinkedIn, Indeed)

- **User Effort**: Passive vs. active job search
- **Quality**: Vetted matches vs. spray-and-pray applications
- **Economics**: Earn money vs. free (but low success rate)

#### vs. Referral Platforms (Rill)

- **Focus**: Candidate earnings vs. referrer earnings
- **Automation**: AI matching vs. manual referrals
- **Scale**: Can operate with skeleton crew vs. requires growth

### GTM Risks & Mitigations

**Risk 1: Hiring Managers Don't Have Budget/Authority**

- **Mitigation**: Target mid-size companies where HMs have discretion, validate budget in initial outreach

**Risk 2: Candidates Don't Trust "Passive" Tool**

- **Mitigation**: Founder personal brands build trust, first placements create social proof

**Risk 3: Unit Economics Don't Scale**

- **Mitigation**: Start with high-margin placements ($100K+ roles), optimize over time

**Risk 4: Can't Reach Break-Even Before Runway Ends**

- **Mitigation**: V's family capital as bridge financing, skeleton crew keeps burn low
