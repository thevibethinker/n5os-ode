## Executive Summary

Careerspan is pivoting from VC-backed job search platform to bootstrapped "talent agency with proprietary tech" operating as a skeleton crew lifestyle business. The shift is driven by founder exhaustion, VC fundraising revulsion, and recognition that the passive hiring agent model can be sustainable with minimal resources.

**Core Thesis**: High-quality passive candidates + hiring managers who want to bypass HR + AI-powered matching + placement fee split = profitable lifestyle business that can operate on <$25K/month burn with just V + Logan.

**Success Criteria (6 months)**: 7-10 placements/month at ~$3K margin each = break-even. If achieved, optionality to scale, raise, or sell. If not achieved, shutdown or pivot again.

## The Pivot: What Changes

### Positioning Shift

**FROM**: Job search platform for active candidates  
**TO**: Talent agency with proprietary tech for passive hiring

**Key Messaging**:
- "We are a talent agency. We have proprietary Tech, and this is what we do"
- "You don't want an agent you want [one] running on your behalf... Servicing opportunities"
- "Unlike a normal agent relationship, we should capture at least like 50, if not more, of the value"

### Operating Model Shift

**FROM**: VC-backed team of 5+ racing to scale  
**TO**: Bootstrapped skeleton crew (V + Logan) with personal brand leverage

| Dimension | Old Model | New Model |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Team** | 5+ people | 2 (V + Logan) + Ilsa through Feb |
| **Burn** | ~$50K+/month | $20-25K/month |
| **Growth** | Venture scale | Lifestyle profitability |
| **Distribution** | Paid acquisition | Personal brand + network |
| **Product** | Feature-rich platform | Stripped to matching core |
| **Customer** | Active job seekers | Passive candidates + HMs |

### Business Model Shift

**FROM**: Free for candidates, charge employers standard rates  
**TO**: Agency fee split (employer saves + candidate earns + Careerspan profits)

**Unit Economics**:
- Industry standard: 20% of first-year salary (~$20K for $100K role)
- Careerspan split:
  - Employer saves: ~$10K (50% discount)
  - Candidate earns: ~$7K bonus
  - Careerspan keeps: ~$3K (15% margin)

**Break-Even**: 7-8 placements/month  
**Lifestyle Target**: 10-15 placements/month = $30-45K revenue

## Strategic Philosophy

### Why This Might Work

1. **Market Gap**: Passive hiring agent model under-served vs. active job search
2. **Unit Economics**: Win-win-win fee split creates better value than traditional recruiting
3. **Distribution Advantage**: Personal brand + network = zero acquisition cost
4. **Founder Fit**: V + Logan want coaching/advisory work; this amplifies vs. replaces
5. **Low Burn**: $20-25K/month = sustainable with minimal revenue
6. **Optionality**: If works → scale/raise/sell; if doesn't → clean shutdown

### Why This Might Fail

1. **Revenue Lag**: 6 months may not be enough to prove model
2. **Hiring Manager Friction**: HR gatekeeping stronger than expected
3. **Candidate Passivity**: Tool requires some engagement; truly passive users churn
4. **Competitive Response**: Incumbents (LinkedIn, agencies) copy model
5. **Founder Discipline**: Complexity creep ("let's do that because it would be cool") undermines simplicity

### The Bet

**V + Logan are betting**:
- 3-6 months of runway (plus potential family capital bridge)
- Opportunity cost of not shutting down or selling now
- Emotional energy to execute one more pivot

**To validate**:
- Passive hiring agent model has product-market fit
- Personal brand distribution can drive growth
- Lifestyle business (10-15 placements/month) is achievable
- If validated → optionality to scale, raise, or sell at better terms

**Downside if wrong**:
- 6 months "wasted" (but founders get break from VC treadmill)
- Shutdown from weaker position than today
- Disappointment of stakeholders (parents, investors)

**Upside if right**:
- Profitable lifestyle business ($30-45K/month revenue)
- Autonomy and sustainable pace for founders
- Option to scale or sell from position of strength
- Alignment with personal brand / coaching work

## Conclusion: Why This Pivot Makes Sense

This isn't a pivot born of excitement—it's born of exhaustion, pragmatism, and hard-won self-awareness. V + Logan recognize:

1. **They don't want to be VC-backed founders**: "I have found this like instinctive revulsion to freezing BC money"
2. **They overcomplicate things**: "My best attribute is making a plan that's more complicated than it needs to be with less money than you would want"
3. **They're better as coaches/advisors with tech leverage**: "The irony of this is we started this. Kind of saying that we were career coaches, and we're going to end this saying the full confidence that we are career [coaches]"
4. **They have 3-6 months to validate a simpler path**: Passive hiring agent as lifestyle business

The pivot strips away everything that doesn't serve this simpler vision:
- ❌ No more VC pressure
- ❌ No more feature bloat
- ❌ No more team management overhead
- ✅ Just two founders, a focused product, and a bet that they can make 10 placements/month

If it works, they've built a profitable lifestyle business with optionality to scale or sell. If it doesn't, they shut down from a position of honesty and self-knowledge rather than grinding another 12 months on the VC treadmill.

**The strategic clarity is this**: Sometimes the right pivot isn't to a bigger vision—it's to a simpler, more sustainable one that aligns with who you are and what you actually want.
