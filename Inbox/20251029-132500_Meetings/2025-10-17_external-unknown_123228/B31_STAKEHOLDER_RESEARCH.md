**1. "Superhuman dynamic" applies to AI productivity tools - simplify for mass market OR go deep with tastemakers who influence discourse**

- **Evidence:** "It's the superhuman dynamic right where you have an incredible product but it is intense... the effort investment right are you going to get the product UX UI wise so good for mass market adoption or is a better strategy to achieving your goals getting tastemakers and important people and folks that like set the discourse"
- **Why it matters:** Most AI tools try to be accessible to everyone and lose power users; Zo's flexibility is a feature for obsessives, not a bug to fix. Suggests tastemaker strategy (VCs, founders, builders) will drive more value than UI simplification at this stage.

**5. Productivity tools increasingly need to be "scaffolded self-reflection" rather than "directive advice" - users want tools that help them think, not tell them what to do**

- **Evidence:** "Users don't want directive advice anymore—they want tools that help them think through their own decisions" (in context of career guidance); "alignment-first approach is ahead of the curve; this is where the market is going"
- **Why it matters:** Philosophical shift in how people want to interact with productivity/decision tools. Suggests AI tools should be "thought partners" that surface insights and let users decide, rather than automated decision-makers. Relevant for Zo's positioning - it gives users control and agency, not prescribed workflows.
