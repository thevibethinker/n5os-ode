**Perspective:** Speaking as founder/operator struggling with workflow automation

### Industry Landscape Insights

#### 1. Workflow automation tools fail because work isn't sequential

- **Evidence:** "For many of us, our workflows are not sequential. This, then that. It can be. I'm doing this and then it goes here and maybe here and here, and then it comes back here and then there and then this and that. And it's all in my brain."

- **Why it matters:** Traditional automation tools (Zapier, Make, etc.) are built on sequential trigger-action paradigms, but knowledge work is actually graph-shaped, context-dependent, and state-based - this explains low adoption despite high interest.

#### 2. The "what to automate" question is the real blocker, not "how to automate"

- **Evidence:** "I have not been able to really figure out yet where would I automate the best workflows. Like, what for me would be the best spot to do this." (04:34)

- **Why it matters:** Automation adoption is blocked by prioritization and discovery problems, not technical capability - users need help identifying automation opportunities before they need automation tools.
