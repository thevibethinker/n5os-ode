"We're converting our pre-seed equity into a debt instrument, which gives my co-founder and me full control of the company. ### 2. Product-Market Fit Exists, Monetization Model Was Wrong (For product/founder conversations)

"The product is great and users like it—we have strong engagement and organic growth. The challenge wasn't building something people want; it was finding the right monetization model. We tried selling to employers and it didn't work fast enough. Now we're funding it differently while we figure out the right business model."

**Usage context**: When discussing with other founders, product people, or potential partners  
**What resonated**: Separating product quality from business model challenges is honest and relatable

**What resonated**: Acknowledging you haven't figured out monetization while emphasizing real product value

### What Resonated Analysis

**Tony's framing**: Immediately asked about equity raise, runway, and customer revenue—classic investor diagnostic. This reveals what investors care about first: **survival fundamentals before growth metrics**.

**Careerspan's positioning opportunity**: The pivot narrative works because it acknowledges reality (employer sales didn't work) while preserving value (users love the product, organic growth continues). This is a mature, pragmatic frame that sophisticated investors and founders will respect more than forced optimism.

**Market signal**: Tony's question about "incoming paying customers to scale out without raising" shows that **alternative funding paths are increasingly acceptable** in the current market. The "grow at all costs" VC mentality has shifted post-2021.
