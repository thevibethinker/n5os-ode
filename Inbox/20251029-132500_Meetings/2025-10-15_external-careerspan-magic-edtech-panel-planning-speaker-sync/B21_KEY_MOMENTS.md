### Memorable Quotes

1. **"Thank God for you [Careerspan]. People don't have to rely on my Harry Potter sorting hat tools that are all in my head."**
   - **Who said it**: Lisa Noble
   - **When**: ~25:40, during discussion of career services scalability challenges
   - **Context**: Lisa has vocabulary for diverse roles from 17-job career but can't deliver that at scale; Careerspan solves this by democratizing career clarity. This is a spontaneous, authentic endorsement from a career services insider—the exact ICP Careerspan is trying to reach. Lisa immediately recognized the value proposition without prompting.

2. **"Basically every career tech tool sucks because they presuppose that you know yourself to the extent that you can use the tool. That's the equivalent of giving someone a sword with instructions: 'swing it around and you'll figure it out.'"**
   - **Who said it**: Vrijen
   - **When**: ~34:00, discussing student self-representation challenges
   - **Context**: Core differentiation message for Careerspan. Lisa called it "genius"; Eric noted it's clearly a refined talking point. This analogy clarifies why tools like StrengthsFinder fail (they give you "woo" but not "what to do with woo"). It frames Careerspan as the missing cognitive scaffolding, not just another assessment tool.

3. **"If AI is enabling anything, it's a lot more autonomy, agency, independence. How can we equip folks with cognitive tools and emotional tools to find those answers themselves? That's a more scalable way for career services to have that impact."**
   - **Who said it**: Vrijen
   - **When**: ~24:00, responding to Lisa's concern about alienating career services in messaging
   - **Context**: This is the precise value proposition that bridges B2C (student empowerment) and B2B (career services scalability). Lisa immediately agreed ("I love it"). This positions AI not as replacement but as enabler of autonomy when resources are scarce. It's the diplomatically calibrated version of the hot take.

4. **"The people who win through those [hiring] systems are the ones who've been able to AI their way into a perfectly aligned resume."**
   - **Who said it**: Lisa Noble
   - **When**: ~39:35, discussing Goldman Sachs screening tens of thousands of applications
   - **Context**: Reveals systemic problem: authenticity loses to keyword optimization. This validates Careerspan's premise that current hiring tech selects for gaming ability, not actual fit. Lisa (insider) is identifying the brokenness that Careerspan can solve: helping people articulate transferable skills authentically, not just match keywords.

5. **"Career centers don't cop to being in the placement business. They're teaching the fish, but they're really not doing a solid job of that."**
   - **Who said it**: Lisa Noble
   - **When**: ~27:48, discussing incentive misalignment in higher ed
   - **Context**: Insider critique from someone who works in the system. Career services claims to "teach fishing" but lacks resources/incentives to actually do it well. Only MBA programs (where outcomes → rankings → funding) invest properly. This explains why B2C is the right model: the institution can't solve the problem, so empower students directly.

### Salient Questions

1. **"Are we even giving people the chance to show their full selves, or are we judging them based on forms that don't allow function?"**
   - **Why it matters**: Reframes the problem from "students can't articulate strengths" to "systems don't create space for showing strengths." This is a strategic positioning question: is Careerspan a tool to help students play the broken game better, or a tool to change the game entirely?
   - **Who raised it**: Vrijen (~38:47)
   - **Action hint**: Explore whether Careerspan's roadmap includes B2B hiring manager tools (beyond just student-facing) to change how employers evaluate candidates

2. **"Is higher ed a potential target? Frenemy? Enemy?"**
   - **Why it matters**: Strategic GTM question with direct revenue implications. Lisa is probing whether Careerspan's messaging will alienate potential institutional buyers. Answer revealed: higher ed undergrad is "friend" for distribution/thought leadership but not economically viable B2B target.
   - **Who raised it**: Lisa Noble (~21:49)
   - **Action hint**: Clarify in future conversations: Careerspan partners with career services on thought leadership (webinars, panels) but sells to students or MBA programs only

3. **"How do people represent their strengths differently now vs. traditional career ladders?"**
   - **Why it matters**: This is Olivia's webinar question that sparked deep discussion. The answer revealed isn't "they represent differently" but "they can't represent effectively because tools presuppose self-knowledge and systems filter for keyword optimization, not authenticity."
   - **Who raised it**: Olivia Lara-Gresty (topic outline), discussed by group (~32:00+)
   - **Action hint**: Reframe for webinar as "What would need to change for people to represent their FULL strengths?" (shifts from descriptive to prescriptive)

4. **"Is there not some movement where funding comes from transferring students into successful employment?"**
   - **Why it matters**: Eric is testing whether career services has economic incentive to buy tools like Careerspan. Answer: Only MBA programs (outcomes → rankings → revenue). Undergrad career centers don't have this incentive structure, validating B2C strategy.
   - **Who raised it**: Eric Stano (~26:26)
   - **Action hint**: Use this as positioning in future higher ed convos: "We know undergrad career centers are constrained, so we empower students directly"

5. **"Do we want to insult all my colleagues in career services everywhere?"**
