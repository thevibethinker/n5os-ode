### Trade-offs Under Consideration

**Trade-off 1: Integration Complexity vs. Adoption Friction**
- **Dilemma:** Deep ATS integration reduces adoption friction but increases technical complexity and time-to-market
- **Option A:** Shallow integration (API for data exchange only) - faster but less seamless
- **Option B:** Deep integration (embedded in ATS UI) - better UX but much longer timeline
- **Related Decisions:** [B40.T5] "Filter Plus" positioning
- **Resolution Path:** Research actual integration requirements (Logan, Week 2). Let feasibility drive positioning.

**Trade-off 2: Broad Market vs. Elite Focus**
- **Dilemma:** Verification product targets broad hiring manager market; top candidate + signing bonus targets elite narrow segment
- **Option A:** Verification = larger TAM, more acquisition appeal, but commoditizable
- **Option B:** Elite candidates = differentiated positioning, missionary brand, but smaller TAM
- **Related Decisions:** [B40.D2] [B40.D4] Product strategy
- **Resolution Path:** Parallel field testing. Verification tests Week 1-2, top candidate prototype Month 1. Data-driven decision.

**Trade-off 3: Sale Timeline vs. Proof Point Quality**
- **Dilemma:** 3-month timeline forces speed but may sacrifice proof point quality that maximizes valuation
- **Option A:** Sprint to acquisition (3 months) - respects team notice period, forces discipline
- **Option B:** Extended proof building (6 months) - stronger case studies, potential revenue, but requires bridge funding
- **Related Decisions:** [B40.D3] Team downsize, [B40.D1] Sell vs. raise
- **Resolution Path:** Assess after Week 2 field tests. If strong traction emerges, revisit timeline + funding needs.

**Trade-off 4: Manual Scaling vs. Product Development**
- **Dilemma:** Ilse can do manual verification NOW but limits scale; building automation takes time but enables growth
- **Option A:** Manual service during sale process - immediate customer traction, no engineering needed
- **Option B:** Build automation first - longer timeline but more scalable/valuable for acquirer
- **Related Decisions:** [B40.T6] Ilse's manual process
- **Resolution Path:** Hybrid - manual for proof-of-concept customers (Month 1), automation roadmap for acquisition pitch. Use manual learnings to inform automation design.

### Unresolved Philosophical Questions

**Philosophy 1: Are we building to sell or selling because we're stuck?**
- **Tension:** Language oscillates between "this would be a fucking good outcome" and "preparing for the worst"
- **V's framing:** Sale as strategic choice, dignity preservation, team care
- **Logan's concern:** Implicit admission of failure if we can't raise
- **Why it matters:** Affects pitch narrative to acquirers - acquisition of choice vs. distress sale
- **Related to:** [B40.D1] Sell vs. raise decision, [Q3] Timeline question

**Philosophy 2: Is Careerspan's value in the tech or the mission?**
- **Tech value:** Story-based verification, consistency checking, filtering algorithms
- **Mission value:** "Outstanding and authentic," populist brand, anti-recruiter positioning
- **Tension:** Different acquirers value different aspects. Verification companies want tech. Workforce platforms want mission/brand.
- **Why it matters:** Determines which acquirers to prioritize, how to position company value
- **Related to:** [Q1] Value prop question, [Q5] Populist angle question

**Philosophy 3: Do we believe in the verification product or just pitching it for sale?**
- **Evidence of belief:** Enthusiastic discussion of market timing, unique moat, field test eagerness
- **Evidence of doubt:** Immediate pivots to alternatives (top candidate model), trade-off debates, lack of conviction statements
- **Why it matters:** Acquirers can sense lack of conviction. Need to fully commit to primary narrative.
- **Related to:** All strategic questions - lack of focus is a signal
