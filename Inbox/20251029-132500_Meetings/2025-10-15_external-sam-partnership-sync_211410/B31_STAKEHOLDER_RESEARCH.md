**2. Charging job seekers is VC-investor default assumption despite founder/market resistance**
- **Evidence:** "I can't tell you how many times someone has said, like, oh, so are you charging the job seekers... - **Why it matters:** VCs systematically misunderstand recruiting tech business models and push consumer monetization despite philosophical and practical problems (unemployed people are broke), creating friction in fundraising and potential misalignment if founders take VC money with this expectation.

**3. Partnership economics shifting from basic job boards to revenue-sharing integration models**
- **Evidence:** "We want to move away from originally it was like job boards and like very basic partnerships. We want to move into more like smarter partnerships with smarter systems similar to yours, where we give the kickback once they've been placed and that kickback up to like 12 months... we have a similar partnership with like a couple of other partners on the sales side" (Ash, 09:41-10:32)
- **Why it matters:** Recruiting platforms are moving from low-value commodity partnerships (pay-per-post job boards) to integrated revenue-share models with complementary systems, creating opportunities for products like Careerspan that provide differentiated candidate quality rather than distribution volume.
