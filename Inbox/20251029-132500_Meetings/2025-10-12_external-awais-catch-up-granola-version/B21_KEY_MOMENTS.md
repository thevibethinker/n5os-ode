### SALIENT QUESTIONS

- Why it matters: Cuts to core product-market fit question - who has pain severe enough to pay meaningfully? Challenges Vrijen's community-distribution strategy by pointing out conversion will be weak if pain isn't acute.
- Action hint: Before pursuing community partnerships, identify and interview the 5-10 users who would be devastated if Careerspan shut down. Understand what makes them different, build for them specifically.

**2. "Do you think I could hire someone and just pay them commission?"**
- Who asked: Awais (exploring lead generation solution)
- Why it matters: Reveals capital-constrained thinking and desire to de-risk hiring experiments. Commission model aligns incentives and preserves runway but requires finding genuinely motivated salesperson.
- Action hint: Test commission-only BD role through Tim He's founder community, offering $500 per qualified O-1 candidate that signs. If it works, proves model; if not, minimal cost.

**3. "How can we be a little more clout-y?"**

- Why it matters: Recognition that attention/brand might be more valuable than traditional metrics at this stage. Questions whether boring B2B approach is right or whether provocative positioning could work better.
- Action hint: Identify 3-5 founders in career/hiring space who are "loud and obnoxious" - study their playbooks, test controversial takes on hiring/AI breaking inbound, measure engagement response.

**4. "Is it easier to spread to 20 communities by end of year than sell to 20 employers?"**
- Who asked: Vrijen (strategic question)
- Why it matters: Resource allocation decision - community distribution vs. direct B2B sales. Communities feel more accessible but Awais's challenge suggests conversion will be weak. Forces prioritization.
- Action hint: Run 30-day test - pick 2 communities and 2 direct employer targets. Measure effort-to-conversion for each channel. Let data decide, not gut feel.

**5. "Where does the tech get good enough that if I get five candidates a week, I can handle it?"**
- Who asked: Awais (implied, describing his roadmap)
- Why it matters: Capacity planning question that reveals chicken-and-egg problem. Won't market until tech scales, but tech won't improve without real usage volume. Classic founder trap.
- Action hint: Set "good enough" bar concretely (e.g., "80% first draft quality, 2 hours review time per case"), then commit to getting 5 candidates even if tech isn't ready. Only real volume will force breakthroughs.
