### Strategic Debates & Tensions

#### Debate 1: Placements vs. Platform Growth - Which Comes First?

**Position A: Placements Unlock Everything (Logan)**
- **Logic**: "Having placements drives everything else...we can flog the shit out of those case studies"
- **Evidence**: Need proof that Careerspan method works
- **Approach**: Focus on placing 10 people manually first → case studies → user growth
- **Risk Mitigation**: Proven model before scaling

**Position B: Platform Growth Enables Placements (V)**
- **Logic**: "We just need a shitload of each side in the system and let them match"
- **Evidence**: "We haven't tried just letting people find people and see what emerges"
- **Approach**: Make job delivery cheap → scale users → organic matches will happen
- **Risk Mitigation**: Don't waste energy on manual placements that don't scale

**Resolution**: DO BOTH
- Manual placements (Careerspan Select) prove model + generate revenue
- Platform improvements (cheaper job delivery) enable scale
- 50/50 time split between services and platform growth
- Key Quote: "We can do both...both of those amplify"

**Remaining Tension**: 
- How much time on each?
- What if manual placements don't translate to platform success?

#### Debate 2: Charge Candidates or Employers?

**Position A: Charge Candidates**
- **Pro**: Market precedent (Andy Moa, $10k career coaches, Wall Street Journal article)
- **Pro**: Easier collection (one party, upfront payment)
- **Pro**: Skin in the game (committed candidates)
- **Con**: "I don't love charging job Seekers" (Logan's hesitation)
- **Con**: Brand misalignment (advocate for workers, not monetize them)

**Position B: Charge Employers**
- **Pro**: Aligns with "we're on candidate's side" messaging
- **Pro**: Larger willingness to pay ($20k agency fee baseline)
- **Pro**: Candidate loyalty (free service from their perspective)
- **Con**: Harder to collect (corporate payment processes)
- **Con**: Hiring manager relationships at risk if fees too high

**Position C: Split Model**
- **Pro**: Both parties have skin in the game
- **Pro**: Lower price point for each ($1k vs. $2k)
- **Pro**: Shared success incentive
- **Con**: More complex sales process
- **Con**: Potential for disputes

**Current Resolution**: Start with $2k from candidate (easier, faster)
**Remaining Question**: Revisit after first 5 placements - adjust based on learnings

### Decision-Making Framework Emerging

When stuck, ask:
1. **Does this help us hit $25k/month?** (Sustainability filter)
2. **Does this grow Careerspan user base?** (Exit value filter)
3. **Does this build our personal brands?** (Long-term filter)
4. **Is this actually fun/energizing?** (Founder wellbeing filter)

If no to all four → Don't do it.
