### Industry Landscape Insights

**1. Workforce development intermediaries face clearinghouse problem in down markets**

- **Evidence:** "When there aren't a lot of roles to hire for then we can't really become as much of a clearinghouse... there's not much to clear."
- **Why it matters:** Intermediary business models predicated on transaction volume collapse when hiring freezes; organizations must pivot to value creation during downturn (employer education, infrastructure building) or become irrelevant until market recovers.

- **Evidence:** "Even between companies they don't have the same taxonomy on skills."
- **Why it matters:** Industry-specific matching solutions that rely on rigid taxonomy will fail in life sciences because no common language exists; need contextual intelligence layer that can translate between employer-specific frameworks rather than assuming standardization.

**3. Community college career services infrastructure gap creates talent presentation barrier**

- **Evidence:** "Bunker Hill Community College... they don't have a lot of career services. And so we're trying to offer... some sort of meeting ground... if you need support in this direction, we can provide some of that additional coaching."
- **Why it matters:** Non-traditional talent pipelines (community colleges, alternative credentials) systematically disadvantaged not just by employer bias but by lack of infrastructure to help candidates articulate and demonstrate transferable skills; intermediaries must provide "career services in a box" to level playing field.

**4. Matching vendor landscape has life sciences specialists OR transferable skills generalists, but not both**

- **Evidence:** "There are some who are specifically focused on the life sciences... they're trying to streamline that taxonomy with skills, but may not have as much of the general skills-first language and expertise on how do you actually help with transferable skills from folks who are coming from non-traditional backgrounds."
- **Why it matters:** Market gap exists for solution that combines industry-specific credibility with transferable skills intelligence; vendors optimize for either industry depth or generalist breadth, but workforce development organizations need both to serve non-traditional talent into specialized industries.
