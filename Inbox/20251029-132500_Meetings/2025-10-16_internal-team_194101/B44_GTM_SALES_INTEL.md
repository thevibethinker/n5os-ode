**Core Positioning Shift**: [B40.D5] FROM career search platform TO talent agency with proprietary tech

## Revenue Model

**Primary Revenue**: [B40.T8] Talent agency placement fees

**Fee Structure** (V's proposed model):
- Typical recruiter fee: ~$20k per placement
- Employer savings: ~$10k (50% discount from recruiter)
- Candidate signing bonus: ~$7k (redistributed recruiter fee)
- Careerspan take: ~$3k per placement

**V Quote**: "We should capture at least like 50, if not more, of the value of the actual... recruiter [commission]... if we think of it as like 20 grand, we got to give the employer back 10 grand... and then we split, you know, seven grand [to candidate], three grand to [us]."

**Value Proposition**:
- Employer: Save 50% on recruiting costs
- Candidate: Get signing bonus (unusual/compelling)
- Careerspan: Scalable revenue without platform overhead

**Sustainability Target**: [B40.T2]
- Operating costs: <$20-25k/month (Ilse part-time + maintenance)
- Placements needed: 7-8 per month at $3k each = $21-24k revenue
- Achievable with passive model + community distribution

## Sales Process (Passive Hiring Model)

**Candidate Side** (Self-service):
1. Candidate signs up via community partnership
2. Profile and preferences captured
3. Career agent monitors opportunities passively
4. Matches surface to hiring managers automatically
5. Candidate notified when hiring manager expresses interest

**Hiring Manager Side** (Direct access):
1. Hiring manager describes role/ideal candidate
2. Receives passive candidate matches from Careerspan
3. Reviews candidates, expresses interest
4. Candidate notified, interview process begins
5. Placement fee triggered on hire

**V Quote**: "We give people the ability to surface things directly to the hiring manager... you just monitor them... and we can set up an interest threshold."

**Key Differentiation**: Direct-to-hiring-manager vs. HR/ATS gatekeepers
