# Pav Bhaji (Mom's Style)

## Ingredients

### Vegetables

- 2 big fat tomatoes + 3-4 small tomatoes
- 2 big white onions
- 1 cauliflower head
- 1 (16 oz) bag sweet peas
- Half a medium bag of shredded carrots
- 3 russet potatoes (2 large, 1 medium)

### Aromatics & Spices

- 1 bulb of garlic
- 1 finger's worth of ginger (mature, not raw - buy beforehand)
- Half packet MDH pav bhaji masala (\~10 servings worth)
- 2 pinches fenugreek seeds
- Hing (asafoetida) to taste
- Salt to taste (needs a good amount)

### Fat

- 1 stick of butter

### Liquid

- 1.5 cups water (max) for pressure cooker

## Instructions

### 1. Pressure Cook Vegetables

1. Add vegetables to pressure cooker with 1.5 cups water maximum
2. Pressure cook until tender
3. Once cooked, mash the vegetables

### 2. Prepare Tomatoes

1. Blend all tomatoes until smooth
2. Set aside

### 3. Sauté Aromatics (Critical Step - Have Everything Ready!)

**IMPORTANT:** Confirm pressure cooker is started before beginning this step. Have blended onions ready to add immediately after garlic browns.

1. Heat oil in large pan
2. Add fenugreek seeds, let them crackle
3. Add garlic, sauté until just browned (don't overcook - be ready with onions!)
4. Immediately add blended onions when garlic is browned
5. Cook onions until they stop bubbling (about 15 minutes)
6. Halfway through onion cooking (around 7-8 minutes), add the 3 green bell peppers

### 4. Add Tomatoes

1. Add blended tomatoes to the onion mixture
2. Cook until the water on the surface stops bubbling

### 5. Combine Everything

1. Add mashed vegetables to the tomato-onion base
2. Add butter (entire stick)
3. Add half packet MDH pav bhaji masala
4. Add hing to taste
5. Add salt to taste (needs a good amount)
6. Mix everything together thoroughly
7. Cook until ready (mixture should be well combined and heated through)

## Important Notes & Tips

- **Ginger:** Must be mature, not raw. Buy it beforehand and let it age
- **Garlic:** Should be browned but not overcooked. Have your blended onions ready to add immediately
- **Fenugreek:** Use 2 pinches - don't overdo it
- **Salt:** This dish needs a good amount of salt, don't be shy
- **Masala:** Half packet MDH works better than full (full is too much for typical serving sizes)
- **Water:** Don't exceed 1.5 cups in pressure cooker
- **Bell Peppers:** Add halfway through onion cooking, not at the start
