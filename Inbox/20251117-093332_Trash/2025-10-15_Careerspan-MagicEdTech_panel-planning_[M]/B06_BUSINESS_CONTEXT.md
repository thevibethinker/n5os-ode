# Business Context: Careerspan Pricing & Talent Sourcing Model

## Pricing Structure

Careerspan is positioning two primary pricing models for enterprise clients:

1. **Pay-Per-Scan Model**: $500/month per specific role
   - Clients pay for individual scans of the talent pool against specific job requirements
   - Refund policy: clients only pay for candidates who accept introductions
   - Maximum flexibility for organizations with variable hiring needs

2. **Unlimited Subscription Model**: $2,000/month
   - Unlimited scans across unlimited roles/positions
   - Candidates presented as short lists within hours instead of days
   - Designed for organizations with steady hiring velocity

## Business Model: "Self-Hiring" Innovation

Careerspan frames its value proposition around "self hiring"—enabling companies to:

- **Avoid the application flood**: Post jobs invisibly or limit exposure instead of flooding the system
- **Cultivate proactive pipelines**: Build relationships with candidates before urgent hiring needs arise
- **De-risk hiring decisions**: Access pre-vetted talent from high-signal communities of professionals (initially focused on product roles)
- **Reduce time-to-first-viable-candidate**: Historical industry pain point that Careerspan directly addresses

## Market Problem Validation

Whitney and Vrijen discussed critical HR infrastructure gaps:

- **ATS dysfunction**: Existing applicant tracking systems are technologically outdated; resumes provide weak signal
- **Candidate inflation**: Widespread resume fraud and false signals in the job market
- **Recruiter overwhelm**: High volume filtering requires significant manual effort despite ATS automation
- **Hiring mode inflexibility**: Current systems penalize companies for hiring in high-growth mode vs. planful recruitment phases

## Product-Market Fit Signal

The conversation confirms strong resonance with enterprise buyer (Whitney) on:
- Problem-solution fit: Her acknowledgment that "the volume is high" and vetting is difficult validates the core problem
- Use case multiplicity: Model works for both "wartime" (urgent hiring) and "peacetime" (proactive pipeline) modes
- Operational fit: Weekly batch scans align with realistic hiring cadence without disrupting operational workflows

## Revenue Model Sustainability

The subscription approach with "no payment unless hired" mechanics creates:
- **Low buyer risk**: MagicEdTech only pays when value is realized (introduction accepted)
- **Incentive alignment**: Careerspan directly benefits from higher quality matching
- **Scalability**: Per-role fees scale as hiring needs grow; unlimited model offers predictable cost control
