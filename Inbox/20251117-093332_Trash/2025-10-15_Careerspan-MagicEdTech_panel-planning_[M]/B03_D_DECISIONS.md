# Decisions: MagicEdTech Hiring Mode & Pipeline Strategy

## Context
Whitney Jones articulated a critical tension in MagicEdTech's hiring approach: the trade-off between **proactive relationship cultivation** (peace time) and **urgent hiring needs** (war time/high growth mode). This decision point directly determines whether Careerspan's model fits MagicEdTech's operational rhythm.

## Key Decision: Hiring Mode Flexibility

**Decision Point**: Can MagicEdTech balance planful, relationship-based sourcing during stable periods with rapid deployment during high-growth cycles?

**Whitney's Position**: 
- MagicEdTech operates in high-growth mode, where urgency dominates
- They know predictable hiring needs but can't always forecast timing precisely
- When growth accelerates, they shift to "I need this job now" mode
- Stealth hiring is only used for senior positions; most roles are non-confidential
- The company struggles to maintain relational hiring practices during growth spurts

**Careerspan's Positioning**: 
- Careerspan designed to work in **both modes** through batch processing
- Weekly scans against the talent pool (recurring, low-touch)
- Pay-per-scan model allows continuous pipeline touch without active job postings
- Candidate tracking capabilities enable "pre-hiring funnel" before urgent needs materialize

## Secondary Decision: Stealth vs. Open Hiring

**Decision**: Limited application of stealth hiring at MagicEdTech

**Finding**: Whitney indicated stealth hiring only applies to senior positions. For analyst roles and most other positions, open job posting is standard practice. This means Careerspan's stealth/proactive pipeline model has less applicability than for companies with broader confidentiality requirements.

**Implication**: Careerspan's value proposition may be strongest during MagicEdTech's "peace time" periods when they can afford to be planful, but needs validation that they have regular windows for this mode.

## Decision Status: UNRESOLVED

Whitney did not commit to either hiring philosophy—she presented both as operational realities. The fundamental question remains: **Will MagicEdTech's growth trajectory and operational model allow them to dedicate resources to proactive, relationship-based talent cultivation using Careerspan?** Or will they remain stuck in reactive hiring mode?

This is a critical decision gate for partnership viability.

- Trade-off: relationship cultivation vs. immediate hiring pressure—decision depends on industry and MagicEdTech's specific growth patterns
