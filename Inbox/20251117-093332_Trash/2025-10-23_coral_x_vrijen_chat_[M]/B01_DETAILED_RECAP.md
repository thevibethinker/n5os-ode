# Detailed Recap

## Meeting Overview
Networking call between Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan founder) and Coral, a CS/Film student at FIU building "Fix Your Feed"—a quiz-based career content discovery platform for students.

## Key Discussion Points

### Coral's Product: Fix Your Feed
- **What it is**: A quiz matching students to relevant career content creators in under 20 seconds on LinkedIn
- **Current traction**: 3,000 visits, viral TikTok (60K views, 500 new followers)
- **Validation**: Caught attention of LinkedIn's Director of Product who praised the concept of human-vetted content
- **Core philosophy**: Database curated by student feedback and Coral's experience as a career advisor for engineering students—not just algorithmic ranking
- **Expansion challenge**: Students requesting content creators in humanities, law, medicine, but Coral only knows tech industry creators
- **Key insight**: Students want authentic, humanized alternatives to algorithmic content feeds; they want to choose their teachers/role models rather than have AI dictate what to watch

### Vrijen's Strategic Advice on Product Development
- **Product-market fit first**: Focus on making the product better based on user feedback before monetizing; worry about scaling and revenue later
- **Infrastructure timing**: Only seek external funding when hosting/infrastructure costs become prohibitive (currently $5/month on Railway; Coral could theoretically bootstrap if she invests personal engineering time)
- **Attention as asset**: The goal is building stable user trust and eyeballs—this is a position of strength. Once product-market fit is achieved and attention is locked in, Coral will be in a powerful position to negotiate from strength
- **Avoid premature monetization**: Risk of compromising authenticity by adding sponsored creators or ads; user trust is the most defensible moat
- **Scaling strategy**: Volume-first approach—reach 30K-300K users while keeping infrastructure simple before raising capital

### Careerspan Product Positioning
Vrijen used this as a teaching moment about career storytelling:
- Existing tools (Overleaf, ChatGPT, Teal, Vmock) are insufficient because they lack structured understanding of the candidate
- **Careerspan's differentiator**: Guided coaching conversations that help users understand their own strengths, then AI-generates personalized resume bullets and job-specific positioning
- **Proof**: Diverse user base from career coaches to teachers to Netflix Chief of Staff; 1,000+ hours of coaching recorded
- **Business model**: Free for job seekers; revenue from employers through talent placement (taking 20-40K cuts per placement)
- **Why free**: Team value-aligned around helping candidates; not willing to charge people seeking career help

### Diversity & Inclusion Focus
Coral emphasized gap she sees:
- Latina women disproportionately suffer from confidence gaps vs. salary negotiation boldness of male peers
- Women historically underestimate their accomplishments; men naturally inflate them
- Impact of systemic underencouragement on ability to self-advocate
- Careerspan discovered similar pattern: 80-90% early user base was women/educators who had never considered their own project management skills as valuable achievements
- Vrijen noted: People from historically marginalized groups lack the baseline "white boy audacity" needed to succeed in job market—not a character flaw, but systemic under-encouragement

### Partnership & Collaboration Opportunity
- **FIU connection**: Coral has direct relationship with Ruth, career director at FIU (50K students, largest Hispanic-serving institution in US)
- **Careerspan opportunity**: Currently in discussions with Granola to replace Vmock (problematic tool colleges are forced to use); FIU could be major deployment
- **Fix Your Feed + Careerspan synergy**: Potential collaboration—Coral could promote Careerspan to her audience; Careerspan could promote Fix Your Feed to its users
- **TikTok collaboration**: Logan (Careerspan co-founder) has 5K TikTok followers; both building career-focused communities; potential for coordinated content
- **Recruitment model innovation**: Coral's community could use Careerspan as internal recruiting tool—if she connects community members to employers, Careerspan surfaces candidates and splits recruiter fees (20-40K per placement)

## Emotional Tone & Dynamics
- Warm, mutual enthusiasm; genuine intellectual alignment
- Vrijen positioned as mentor; Coral as emerging founder with validated product-market signals
- Key moment: Vrijen called Coral a "founder" for first time; Coral's surprise revealed she still thought of it as side project (identity shift)
- Both shared commitment to authenticity, helping underrepresented groups, and avoiding compromise on values
- Coral immediately trusted Vrijen's judgment; requested product trial and offered FIU connection
