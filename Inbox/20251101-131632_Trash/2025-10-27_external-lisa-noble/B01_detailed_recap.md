## Strategic Context

### Career Services Pain Points
Lisa manages a challenging trifecta at Colby: (1) inbound job requests from employers she must qualify and vet manually, (2) handshake platform that both students and colleges hate, and (3) growing alumni population (2017-2022 cohorts) actively seeking career transitions in difficult market. The manual vetting workload is significant - she emphasized taking time to "vet them, have proper conversation, fall in love with them" for startups and non-traditional employers.

### Market Timing
The conversation revealed acute hiring market dysfunction from both sides. Founders aren't even looking at inbound applications anymore - they post for legal reasons but exclusively hire through warm referrals with dramatically raised bars. Meanwhile, top MBA graduates from Columbia are still unemployed months after graduation (first time Lisa has seen this), and Bain summer analysts aren't getting return offers. AI is being used as cover for layoffs more than genuine transformation.

### Partnership Value Proposition
Careerspan could solve three problems simultaneously: (1) automated employer vetting and job matching, (2) handshake replacement with better student experience, (3) alumni career transition support. Lisa was particularly interested in the ability to distribute jobs to matched students automatically and the filtering capability that lets employers describe ideal co-workers via voice message.
