# B03_DECISIONS

| Decision | Rationale | Implications |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pursue a **club‑based growth program** built around hand‑picked leaders rather than a generic referral scheme | Zo already has a small set of extremely passionate users; concentrating on them as club leaders is more authentic and scalable than trying to recruit a broad, undifferentiated base | Sets direction for designing Zo Clubs as the primary grassroots growth experiment; future GTM work will assume a leader‑centric model (names, crests, badges, small budgets) rather than a simple discount‑link referral system |
| Make **status and lore** core to the program ("tapped on the shoulder" leadership) | Crypto communities demonstrate that people value status conferred by the group; hand‑selecting leaders and framing leadership as a privilege should reduce gaming and create pride instead of obligation | Club leadership will likely be invite‑only; onboarding, messaging, and badge design must reinforce that being a leader is something earned and granted, not something extracted by chasing metrics |
| Treat v1 of clubs as **community + learning first, monetization later** | Introducing strong financial incentives too early risks skewing behavior and attracting the wrong participants; the team wants to first prove that people naturally want to build on Zo together | Early experiments will optimise for engagement, learning, and successful projects rather than revenue; ideas like “Zo VC” or prompt monetization are explicitly framed as v2 once healthy patterns emerge |
| Use clubs to generate **structured feedback and content** (surveys, check‑ins, showcases) | Weekly check‑ins and quick surveys are a lightweight way to capture what members are building, what’s working, and what’s broken, while also producing stories Zo can amplify | Club rituals (Monday check‑ins, Friday surveys, project submissions) become part of the design spec; Zo can turn those streams into newsletters, hall‑of‑fame lists, and product‑shaping insights |
| Begin scoping a **clout‑hacking / personal‑CRM themed club** as a strong candidate for Vrijen’s first experiment | The job market and opportunity landscape are increasingly network‑driven; a club focused on systematically using Zo for networking, personal CRM, and reputation building fits both Zo’s strengths and V’s expertise | V will think through initial member candidates and structures for such a club; Zo leadership can assume this is one of the leading testbeds when they model program outcomes and support needs |
| Start collaboration by **posting Ben’s roles and leveraging his channels**, once V shares jobs and a concise blurb | Ben is willing to promote V’s work in trusted founder communities if given something concrete and on‑brand to share; job postings are an immediate, low‑friction start | Creates a near‑term dependency on V to provide job postings and a short description; once delivered, Ben can route attention to V’s work and the broader Zo ecosystem, seeding future club members and collaborators |
