# B03 – Decisions

1. **Upwork expense classification**  
   - **Decision:** Upwork spend for this period is confirmed as **video production / marketing** and should be categorized as a **marketing/promo expense**, not generic contractor services.
   - **Implication:** Aligns cost with go-to-market efforts; simplifies later gross margin analysis.

2. **Treatment of Ilsa’s laptop purchase**  
   - **Decision:** The large hardware purchase (~$2.3k) is confirmed as **Ilsa’s laptop**, to be treated as a **computer asset** (capitalized) and depreciated over time.  
   - **Implication:** Recorded under fixed assets; not expensed immediately.

3. **Loan from founder (capital structure)**  
   - **Decision:** Prior founder funding from Vrijen will be treated as a **non–interest-bearing loan from officer**, not as informal equity or unstructured advances.
   - **Implication:**
     - Balance sheet will show a formal **loan from officer (Vrijen)** liability.
     - Zeni will rely on a signed loan agreement for documentation.

4. **Audited financials next steps**  
   - **Decision:** Before committing to an audit, Vrijen will **speak with Shaker at RAB** to clarify:
     - Exactly **which period(s)** need audited financials (e.g., year ended December 2024).
     - Whether they truly require a **CPA audit** vs. standard financial statements.
   - **Implication:** Prevents over-committing to an audit that may not be strictly required; gives Zeni time to plan if an audit is needed.

5. **Ownership of 1099 filings**  
   - **Decision:** Zeni will **again handle 1099 preparation and filing** (as in the prior year), including filings for contractors such as Brady.
   - **Implication:** Centralizes compliance with one provider; Careerspan avoids internal 1099 admin.

6. **Tax preparation provider (short term)**  
   - **Decision:** For personal / joint returns, Logan and Vrijen expect to continue using **Mel** and an existing firm for this year, while remaining open to Zeni’s tax services in the future.
   - **Implication:** No immediate migration of personal tax prep to Zeni; Zeni remains primary for company bookkeeping, 1099s, and potentially corporate taxes.

7. **Financial reporting cadence**  
   - **Decision:** Zeni will **finalize November books and send completed financial statements by the next day**, with a standing expectation that recurring calls include a structured review of balance sheet and P&L.
   - **Implication:** Provides timely visibility heading into year-end and any investor requests.
