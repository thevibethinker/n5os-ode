# B03 – Stakeholder Intelligence

## 1. Careerspan leadership

### Vrijen Attawar (Founder / CEO)
- **Role in meeting:** Co-host of the call; primary decision-maker on capital structure, investor communications, and relationship with Zeni.
- **Interests & goals:**
  - Keep the company’s books accurate and audit-ready ahead of year-end and investor requests.
  - Ensure founder funding is represented cleanly (loan vs equity) without unnecessary complexity.
  - Avoid surprises from investors (e.g., RAB) by clarifying requests early.
- **Motivations / leverage points:**
  - Values clarity, correctness, and thoughtful structuring more than optimization tricks.
  - Comfortable taking on relationship work (e.g., calling Shaker) and drafting agreements when it unblocks the system.
- **Concerns / sensitivities:**
  - Wants to avoid misclassification of expenses and liabilities that might confuse investors or future auditors.
  - Sensitive to time cost—prefers concise, well-prepared finance reviews rather than meandering walkthroughs.

### Logan Currie (Co-founder / Operator)
- **Role in meeting:** Co-host; operational counterpart ensuring execution on invoices, 1099s, and internal follow-through.
- **Interests & goals:**
  - Practical, execution-focused handling of items like 1099 filings, contractor invoices, and day-to-day bookkeeping.
  - Maintain a simple, reliable workflow with Zeni that minimizes surprises and rework.
- **Motivations / leverage points:**
  - Responsive and collaborative; quickly confirms categorization (e.g., Upwork as marketing) and takes ownership of document gathering.
  - Open to Zeni handling operational finance work when it reduces friction for the team.
- **Concerns / sensitivities:**
  - Wants confidence that recurring obligations (1099s, contractor pay, etc.) are handled correctly and on time.
  - Prefers that complexity (like audited financials vs standard statements) be clarified early so the team can plan.

## 2. Zeni team (accounting/bookkeeping partner)

### Ankit Mittal (Senior finance lead / relationship owner)
- **Role in meeting:** Lead on financial review, balance sheet/P&L walkthrough, and strategic finance guidance.
- **Interests & goals:**
  - Maintain accurate, accrual-based books that can withstand investor and auditor scrutiny.
  - Expand the scope of services (1099 filings, potential audit coordination, possibly tax work) while remaining transparent.
- **Motivations / leverage points:**
  - Positions Zeni as a long-term, trusted finance partner rather than a commodity bookkeeping vendor.
  - Uses recurring reviews of financial statements as a way to build trust and highlight value.
- **Concerns / sensitivities:**
  - Clear about the boundary between bookkeeping and CPA audit work; does not want Zeni mis-positioned as the auditor of record.
  - Wants documented agreements (e.g., loan from officer) to support how items appear in the ledger.

### Alphonse George (Bookkeeper / operations lead)
- **Role in meeting:** Day-to-day owner of the open-items sheet, invoice gathering, and classification details.
- **Interests & goals:**
  - Close open questions on invoice classification (Upwork, Braze, Gunderson, etc.) so the books are clean.
  - Ensure prepaids, accruals, and contractor expenses are recorded to the correct period.
- **Motivations / leverage points:**
  - Detail-oriented and methodical; uses structured spreadsheets and systematic walkthroughs.
  - Appreciates prompt responses and document sharing from the Careerspan side.
- **Concerns / sensitivities:**
  - Wants to avoid carrying unresolved open items across multiple months.
  - Needs clarity on service periods and contract terms to avoid mis-accruals.

## 3. External stakeholders

### RAB / Investor-side finance contacts
- **Role in background:** Party requesting **audited financials** (likely for year-end 2024) as part of their diligence or internal processes.
- **Interests & goals:**
  - Obtain a trustworthy, CPA-signed view of the company’s financial position.
  - Ensure the numbers they rely on for decision-making are independently verified.
- **Motivations / leverage points:**
  - As an investor or financing partner, can create time-sensitive requirements that drive priorities on the finance side.
- **Concerns / sensitivities:**
  - Likely concerned with compliance, risk management, and having consistent documentation in their portfolio.

## 4. Relationship dynamics

- The relationship between Careerspan and Zeni is **collaborative and collegial**:
  - Friendly rapport (small talk about Thanksgiving, LinkedIn posts) indicates trust and continuity.
  - Zeni is proactive in reminding the team about 1099 timelines and early-bird pricing.
- Decision-making is **shared but with clear domains**:
  - Vrijen owns investor-facing decisions and capital-structure choices.
  - Logan owns much of the operational follow-through and contractor/vendor touchpoints.
  - Ankit and Alphonse own the accounting treatment, controls, and statement preparation, while being transparent about the limits of their remit (audit vs bookkeeping).
