# Internal Decisions - Pricing Strategy Discussion

**Meeting Date**: November 5, 2025  
**Type**: Leadership Strategy Meeting  
**Participants**: V (CEO), Alex (Head of Product), Maya (VP Sales)

---

## Decision Context

**Problem**: Current pricing ($295/person/month) is causing deal losses in mid-market segment. Competitors (BetterUp) offering similar services at $200/month.

**Evidence**:
- Maya lost 2 deals in past month due to price
- Prospects love product but can't justify premium pricing
- Competitive pricing at $200/month (BetterUp)

**Strategic Importance**: 
- Critical for Q1 budget season capture
- Need competitive response to avoid continued deal losses
- Balance between market competitiveness and margin protection

---

## Decision Options Considered

### Option 1: Status Quo
- **Description**: Maintain $295/month pricing
- **Pros**: Protects current margins, maintains premium positioning
- **Cons**: Continued deal losses to lower-priced competitors
- **Outcome**: ❌ Rejected - Losing too many deals

---

### Option 2: Tiered Pricing Model (Proposed Solution)
- **Description**: Create two tiers with different service levels

**Premium Tier ($295/month)**:
- Monthly 1:1 coaching sessions
- Full platform access
- Manager support and training
- Current offering maintained

**Standard Tier ($200/month)**:
- Quarterly 1:1 coaching sessions (vs monthly)
- AI-assisted coaching between sessions
- Platform access
- No manager support/training

**Pros**:
- Competitive with BetterUp pricing
- Provides upsell path to premium
- Maintains margin on premium tier
- Gives sales a tool to compete on price

**Cons**:
- Risk of cannibalization (existing customers downgrading)
- Quarterly coaching may not drive same outcomes as monthly
- AI assistant needs to be high quality to deliver value
- Complexity of managing two tiers

**Key Innovation**: AI-assisted coaching between quarterly sessions to maintain engagement and value

**Concerns Raised**:
- V: Margin risk if AI doesn't reduce coach costs enough
- V: Quarterly coaching effectiveness compared to monthly
- Maya: Positioning risk if standard tier feels like downgrade
- Alex: AI quality needs to be high enough to deliver real value
