# B27 - KEY_MESSAGING Generation Prompt

You are generating a KEY_MESSAGING intelligence block.

## Core Principle

Extract the 3-5 CORE MESSAGES that resonated with the stakeholder. What should we EMPHASIZE in future conversations? What should we AVOID?

## Output Structure

### MESSAGES THAT LANDED ✅

### [Number]. [Message Theme]
**What we said**: [Specific point/angle/story]  
**Their reaction**: [Quote or behavior showing positive response]  
**Why it worked**: [Analysis of resonance]  
**Amplify in future**: [How to lean into this further]

### MESSAGES THAT MISSED ❌

### [Number]. [Message Theme]
**What we said**: [Specific point that didn't land]  
**Their reaction**: [Silence, redirection, polite acknowledgment without follow-up]  
**Why it missed**: [Analysis - wrong priority? Wrong framing? Wrong timing?]  
**Adjust for future**: [How to reframe or drop]

### KEY LANGUAGE TO MIRROR 🔄

- **"[Their distinctive phrase]"**: [Use when discussing X topic]
- **"[Their framing]"**: [Adopt this perspective when talking about Y]

## Selection Criteria

### Messages That Landed:
- Sparked follow-up questions
- Elicited enthusiasm/energy shift
- Got quoted back to you later
- Led to deeper exploration
- Generated "tell me more" response

### Messages That Missed:
- Met with silence or topic change
- Required re-explanation
- Elicited confusion
- Got surface acknowledgment but no depth
- They never circled back to it

## Extraction Rules

### Look For:
1. **Energy shifts**: When did they lean in? When did energy drop?
2. **Questions sparked**: What made them curious?
3. **Objections**: What did they push back on?
4. **Quotes back**: What did they reference later in conversation?
5. **Time spent**: What topics did they want to explore vs gloss over?

### Analyze:
- **Framing success**: Was it the content or the WAY we said it?
- **Timing issues**: Right message, wrong moment?
- **Priority mismatch**: We cared, they didn't?
- **Language barriers**: Did our jargon confuse?

## Quality Standards

✅ **DO:**
- Ground observations in specific moments
- Explain WHY messages landed/missed
- Provide actionable guidance for future conversations
- Note their distinctive language to mirror
- Balance positives and misses (both are valuable)

❌ **DON'T:**
- List everything discussed (focus on HIGH-SIGNAL moments)
- Ignore obvious misses (learning opportunity)
- Miss language patterns to adopt
- Forget to explain the "why" behind reactions
- Overindex on positives (honest assessment needed)

## Example Quality Indicators

**HIGH QUALITY:**
### 1. AI Democratizing Software Development

**What we said**: Mentioned how Cursor empowers non-technical people to build real software  
**Their reaction**: "Cursor has been really empowering where I can actually develop software" (15:55) - energy spike, personal story shared  
**Why it worked**: Validated her experience, positioned her transformation as part of larger trend  
**Amplify in future**: Lead with democratization narrative when discussing Careerspan's positioning tools

**LOW QUALITY:**
### 1. Product demo

**What we said**: Showed product  
**Their reaction**: They liked it  
**Why it worked**: Good product  
**Amplify in future**: Keep showing product

## Edge Cases

**All messages landed**: Great, but look harder for nuances—did some land BETTER than others?

**All messages missed**: Rare, but signals fundamental misalignment. Note for future prioritization.

**Mixed signals**: Note ambiguity: "UNCERTAIN - responded positively but didn't follow up with questions or action"
