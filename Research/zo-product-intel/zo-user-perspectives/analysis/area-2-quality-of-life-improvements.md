# Area 2: Quality of Life Improvements — Evidence Review

## Inquiry Question
What evidence exists that Zo delivers "quality of life" improvements vs. productivity gains? What are users actually valuing?

---

## Direct Evidence

### What Users Actually Said

**Gabi (founder):**
> "Core problem is **context and state** — how to maintain right information in head and right state of all information." [source: gabi-founder.md]

Interpretation: Framing problem as cognitive load, not output volume.

**Logan Currie (Friends Next workshop):**
> "Using AI during passive moments — waiting, transit — to save articles, summarize content, execute tasks." [source: logan-currie.md]

Interpretation: "Ambient productivity" = reclaiming dead time, not doing more work.

**Ben Guo (Zo co-founder, via V):**
> "Ambient productivity — using AI in passive moments when waiting, in cab, boarding plane." [source: ben-guo.md]

Interpretation: QoL framing originates from Ben, reinforced by V.

---

## Indirect Evidence

### What Users Did/Demonstrated

| Behavior | Source | QoL Indicator |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| LinkedIn data analysis for "lost opportunities" | Logan | Peace of mind (not missing DMs) |
| Daily digest workflow | V (describing his use) | Reduced cognitive overhead |
| "Magic" framing in demos | Multiple | Delight, not efficiency |

---

## Counter-Evidence / Tension

**Mihir Makwana:**
> "Pricing: $30-35/month for 'ambient' feels high." [source: mihir-makwana.md]

Interpretation: If QoL is the value prop, pricing anchoring is unclear. Users compare to productivity tools ($10-20), not QoL services.

**Gabby:**
> "Generated sources may not be real." [source: gabby-workshop.md]

Interpretation: Trust issues undermine QoL benefits. Can't relax if verifying AI output.

---

## What's Claimed vs. What's Evidenced

| Claim | Evidence Source | Quality |
|-------|-----------------|---------|
| "Ambient productivity" reduces cognitive load | Ben → V framing | Secondary (V's paraphrase) |
| Reclaiming "dead time" is valuable | Logan's demo | Secondary (observed behavior) |
| Daily digest reduces mental overhead | V's own use | Tertiary (self-reported) |
| Users feel less overwhelmed | None captured | Absent |

---

## Gaps (What We Don't Know)

1. **Retention correlation:** Do users who experience "QoL benefits" retain longer than "productivity gains" users?
2. **Pricing willingness:** Would users pay $30+ for QoL vs. $10 for productivity?
3. **Competitive comparison:** How do users compare Zo QoL to Apple Intelligence, Notion AI, etc.?
4. **Failure impact:** When AI fails, does QoL framing make it worse ("you failed me when I was relaxing")?

## Open Questions

1. Is "ambient productivity" a real category or marketing framing?
2. Do users experience QoL benefits, or is this aspirational?
3. How does QoL framing affect pricing tolerance?
