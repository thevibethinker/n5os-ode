## QoL Categories with Evidence

### 1. Cognitive Load Reduction
**What users said:**
> "Context switching is the killer. If Zo can handle the mental overhead of remembering what I was doing across tools..." — Ben, discussing ambient AI

**Your hypothesis:** QoL improvements should target the *coordination tax* — the mental effort of keeping state across apps.

**Evidence strength:** Strong (recurring theme)

### 2. Failure Recovery
**What users said:**
> "The AI will do things you don't want... but you can roll back" — Ben, on self-healing

**Pattern:** Users who experience graceful failure → higher trust → deeper usage.

**Your insight:** QoL improvements should include *deliberate* failure paths that feel safe, not catastrophic.

### 3. Progressive Disclosure
**Mihir's tension:**
> "Is the learning curve a moat or liability?"

**Your position:** QoL improvements can bridge the gap — surface simple capabilities first, reveal complexity as users demonstrate readiness.

**Evidence:** No user complained Zo was "too simple" after onboarding. Risk is in the other direction.

### 4. Context Preservation
**Logan's LinkedIn analysis:**
18 years of data processed, but value came from *context* — not just the analysis, but the narrative frame he provided first.

**Your hypothesis:** QoL improvements should help users *externalize* their thinking (notes, plans) so AI can build on it, not just access it.

## Messaging Implications

**Frame QoL improvements as:**
- NOT: "Zo automates tasks"

**Ben's articulation:**

**Your refinement:**
> "Ambient productivity" — Zo doesn't demand your attention; it protects it.
