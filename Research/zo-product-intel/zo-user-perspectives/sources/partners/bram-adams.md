## Direct Interactions About Zo

### Bram's Critique of V's Approach

**Core critique:**
> Building AI automation tools (GPT pipelines, Zo workflows) before establishing information governance philosophy is "spinning wheels"

**The "Complexity Snowball":**
- V spent $300 on compute building Zo system
- Became "too unwieldy" 
- Had to wipe and restart
- Pattern: Tool-building outpacing governance maturity

### Zo vs. ChatGPT vs. Notion

**V's assessment:**
> "ChatGPT: Functionality is very reliable but state management and file management is fucking non-existent"

> "Zo Computer: Lets me be somewhat technical without having to be technical" but "hasn't nailed" routines/agents UX yet

**Bram's alternative:**
- YCB boilerplate (open-source knowledge system)
- "Master ledger" pattern: curated, highest-quality knowledge base
- Shift from "capture all + AI filter" to "curate manually + AI augment"

### Bram's View on AI Computers

> AI computers are "Minecraftian" or Roblox-like environments—programmable spaces where everything is text in and text out

> "Despite sophistication, models still only transform text, which is both powerful and limiting"

### Philosophical Tension

**Bram's warning:**
> Over-engineered prompts can produce mediocre, self-reinforcing artifacts
> AI-generated documents become training substrate for future prompts, progressively degrading quality

**Bram's proposed pattern:**
> Shorten meetings slightly, use saved time for participants to review transcript and highlight key lines themselves
> Different people highlight different things—valuable signals about perspective

**V's realization:**
> "I have often delegated judgment to AI—e.g., asking it to 'name all the decisions'—and seemingly neutral instructions still encode model assumptions"
