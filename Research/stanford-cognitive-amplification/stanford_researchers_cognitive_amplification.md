# Stanford Researchers Aligned with V's Cognitive Amplification Thesis

## V's Thesis (Summary)

1. **Three-stage model**: (1) Know thyself → (2) Compensate for weaknesses → (3) Draw in data from new domains for actual cognitive enhancement
2. **The aspiration**: "Give everyone 30 more IQ points" — AI not as replacement but as genuine intelligence amplification
3. **ADHD angle**: Using AI to not just normalize but *overpower* — amplify strengths, compensate weaknesses
4. **The human seed**: AI needs irreducible human knowledge/input to work from — cognitive amplification, not cognitive abdication
5. **Cognitive profiling**: AI that learns your cognitive profile — biases, strengths, where to be careful vs. where to reduce load
6. **Learning model**: AI optimized for learning outcomes (not satisfaction) — long feedback loop training cycles

## Tier 1: Highest Alignment — Direct Conversation Partners

These researchers are working on problems that map directly onto V's thesis. Each one would likely find a conversation with V substantive and mutually interesting.

### 1. Khizer Khaderi, MD, MPH
**Title**: Clinical Associate Professor, Byers Eye Institute, Stanford Medicine
**Lab**: Founding Director, Stanford Human Perception Lab (HPL)
**Also**: Faculty, Stanford HAI; Founding Director, Stanford Vision Performance Center

**Why he matters for V's thesis**: Khaderi developed the discipline of **"Symbiotics"** — the convergence of human and computer sciences to design systems where machines perceive, understand, and respond to optimize the human experience. His lab studies how human sensory and cognitive systems work, then applies those principles computationally to build human-centric technologies. He's literally building the science of human-machine cognitive symbiosis.

**Alignment with V's thesis**:
- **Stage 3 (cognitive enhancement)**: His work on interpreting biosignals from everyday activities to improve human-machine interactions is the technical substrate for cognitive amplification
- **Cognitive profiling**: The HPL's focus on understanding individual human perception and performance maps directly to V's vision of AI that learns your cognitive profile
- **Human seed**: Symbiotics is fundamentally about starting from human systems and extending them — not replacing them

**Connection angle**: Khaderi is a surgeon-turned-technologist-futurist who bridges medicine and AI. V's non-technical but deeply thoughtful perspective on cognitive amplification from the *user's* side would complement Khaderi's *builder's* perspective. The Symbiotic Summit (his annual event) could be a venue for V to present.

**Contact**: Stanford Medicine profile — khizer-khaderi; Stanford HPL

### 2. Manish Saggar, PhD
**Title**: Associate Professor of Computational Neuropsychiatry, Stanford Medicine
**Lab**: Brain Dynamics Lab
**Also**: Wu Tsai Neurosciences Institute

**Why he matters for V's thesis**: Saggar's lab develops computational methods to characterize and model temporal dynamics of brain activity — specifically studying **creativity, contemplative practices, and cognitive flexibility**. He uses machine learning, signal processing, and hyperscanning (studying neural correlates of social interaction) to understand how brains generate creative solutions and adapt.

**Alignment with V's thesis**:
- **Stage 1 (know thyself)**: His computational brain dynamics work is essentially building the scientific toolkit for cognitive self-knowledge — understanding how YOUR brain generates ideas and solves problems
- **Cognitive profiling**: His work on individual differences in brain dynamics is the neuroscience foundation for personalized cognitive profiles
- **ADHD connection**: Computational neuropsychiatry includes studying how neurodivergent brains differ in their dynamics — directly relevant to the ADHD × AI angle

**Connection angle**: Saggar is interested in contemplative practices AND creativity — two domains where V's "human seed" philosophy resonates. V could discuss how his personal AI system (Zo) functions as an external cognitive tool that extends creative capacity, providing Saggar with a real-world case study of what his lab studies computationally.

**Contact**: med.stanford.edu/profiles/23965; braindynamicslab.github.io

### 5. Patrick Bissett, PhD
**Title**: Senior Research Scientist, Department of Psychology, Stanford
**Lab**: Works with Russ Poldrack in Poldrack Lab

**Why he matters for V's thesis**: Bissett studies **cognitive control and decision-making** — how people control their thoughts and actions, and how they make decisions. He uses experimental tasks, functional brain imaging, and computational modeling. His work on response inhibition and cognitive control is foundational to understanding how AI could help people make better decisions.

**Alignment with V's thesis**:
- **Cognitive profiling**: His computational modeling of individual differences in cognitive control is the scientific basis for understanding someone's decision-making profile
- **Stage 2 (compensate)**: Understanding where and how cognitive control fails (impulse control, ADHD-adjacent executive function) is the foundation for AI compensation
- **Stage 3 (enhance)**: If you can model someone's cognitive control profile, you can theoretically design AI interventions that enhance their decision-making

**Connection angle**: V's interest in AI that understands cognitive biases and strengths is the applied version of what Bissett studies experimentally. V could offer a practitioner's perspective on what cognitive control support looks like in practice through personal AI.

**Contact**: bissettp.github.io; psychology.stanford.edu/people/patrick-bissett
