# TeamWork Online — Partnership / Acquisition Dossier

## Parameters
- **Primary contact:** Davis Filippell (President) [^1]
- **Objective:** Position Careerspan for (a) partnership and (b) acquisition; pursue both in parallel.

## What we know (high-signal, acquisition-relevant)
1. **They position themselves as a vertical recruiting system for sports + live events**, and explicitly contrast themselves with “traditional ATS” (their framing: ATS ≠ recruiting) [^2].
2. **They sell an “Online ATS” as an annual license** (employer-facing) [^3].
3. **They also monetize the candidate side via paid subscription products (e.g., “MVP Access”) and an activation fee** (useful wedge for bundling Careerspan into an existing billing event) [^4].
4. **They appear to function both as a destination network and as a distribution channel into other ATS products** (e.g., Pinpoint describes a TeamWork Online integration that syndicates jobs to TeamWork’s candidate network while keeping workflow inside Pinpoint) [^5].

## Davis Filippell — bio signals (for stakeholder strategy)
- Public bio emphasizes he has led product/sales/ops teams and has a mission around “building vibrant communities through technology.” [^1]
- Prior background: investor at an incubation/VC firm; started career as a management consultant (useful for how he will evaluate ROI + distribution logic). [^1]

## Partnership→Acquisition thesis (your wedge)
**Thesis:** TeamWork Online already has (1) a paid “activation” moment and (2) stored billing infrastructure; Careerspan can be positioned as the *highest-value add-on* to convert that moment into materially higher uptake and better outcomes (engagement, conversion, retention, hiring quality). [^4]

### What we need to validate next (MECE research agenda)
1. **Business model & monetization map**
   - Employer licensing: what exactly is included in “annual license” (ATS + recruiting tools + EEO suite + TextTender)? [^3]
   - Candidate monetization: what % converts to MVP Access? why activation fee exists? [^4]
   - Unit economics hypotheses: where is the highest-margin product line?

2. **Customer base & distribution power**
   - Confirm: leagues/teams penetration; # employers; what segments are growing fastest. [^2][^5]
   - How much value is “network effects” vs “workflow lock-in”?

3. **Product roadmap pressure (AI gap = opportunity)**
   - They already sell job-description text tooling (“TextTender”)—adjacent to Careerspan’s “story/translation” layer. [^3]
   - Identify whether they’re investing in deeper AI for candidate profiles/applications (signals: job postings, blogs, product pages).

4. **Integration surface area**
   - Map where Careerspan can attach first (fastest wedge): candidate profile building, application questions, recruiter review UX.
   - Inventory existing partners; understand whether they prefer “attach to ATS” vs “be ATS.” [^2][^5]

5. **Ownership / corporate structure / acquisition appetite**
   - Private vs investor-backed; any prior transactions.
   - Who besides Davis decides? (Founder Buffy Filippell is prominently credited on the site.) [^2][^3]

6. **Risks & objections (steel-man)**
   - “Candidate-side value doesn’t monetize” → respond with activation-fee conversion lift + retention + employer NRR.
   - Data/privacy/compliance risks in sports/live events hiring.

## Immediate next-step research tasks (concrete)
1. Pull and summarize: employer-facing “Hire Talent / For Employers” pages and any pricing/FAQ pages (to infer packaging + sales motion).
2. Compile integration list (ATS partners, HRIS, job board syndication) and categorize by: *source of applicants* vs *workflow-of-record*.
3. Find public interviews/podcasts with Davis and Buffy that reveal strategy, constraints, and expansion appetite.
