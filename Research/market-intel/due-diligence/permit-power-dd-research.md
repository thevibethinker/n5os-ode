## 1. Mission & Approach

### Mission Statement
> "Making it cheap and easy for American families to power their lives."

### The Problem They Address
- American families pay **up to 7x more** for rooftop solar than families in Australia
- Key cost driver: burdensome red tape from local government permitting and utility interconnection processes
- Bureaucracy adds significant "soft costs" that prevent solar adoption

### Their Theory of Change
1. **Research:** Quantify the costs of red tape and benefits of reform
2. **Education:** Help policymakers understand the problem
3. **Advocacy:** Push for state-level policy fixes to streamline permitting and interconnection

### Impact Claims (from "As Cheap as Our Peers" report)
If states cut red tape and brought solar costs in line with peer countries:
- **18.2 million more families** would install solar by 2040 (155% increase)
- **$1.2 trillion** in cumulative savings for American families
- **$1,600/year** average savings per family installing solar
- **61% cost reduction** possible through streamlined permitting

## 3. Research & Publications

### Flagship Report: "As Cheap as Our Peers" (October 2025)
**Core Findings:**
- US rooftop solar costs 2-7x more than peer countries (Australia, Germany, EU)
- Primary driver: permitting, inspection, and interconnection bureaucracy
- With reform: 18.2M more families → solar by 2040
- $1.2T total savings over system lifetimes
- Uses NREL's Distributed Generation Market Demand (dGen) model

**State-by-State Red Tape Examples:**
- **Illinois:** Paper submissions, sequential approvals, inconsistent requirements
- **Minnesota:** In-person submission, monthly township meetings for zoning
- **New York:** Survey requirements, architectural reviews, restrictive battery rules
- **Colorado:** Bespoke file-naming, mixed paper/digital formats
- **New Jersey:** Sequential approvals, slow processes leading to cancellations

### Other Research
- **Virginia Report** (Sep 2025): "A 21st-Century Permitting Regime for Rooftop Solar and Home Batteries in Virginia"
- **Arizona Case Study** (May 2024): "Automated Permitting Leads to More Rooftop Solar in Tucson and Pima County" — documents 2x installation growth after SolarAPP+ adoption

## 11. Industry Trends & Interview Talking Points

This section captures macro-level patterns and emerging trends that would make for compelling interview conversation, demonstrating strategic awareness beyond Permit Power's immediate work.

### A. The Tax Credit Cliff (January 2026)

**What happened:** The 30% federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) for residential solar and batteries expired December 31, 2025. [^14]

**Implications:**
- Residential solar installations now lack the incentive that saved homeowners thousands
- Tax credits remain for **leased solar systems through end of 2027** — installers shifting to "lease-to-own" models
- New financing innovations emerging: prepaid leases where homeowner pays upfront, installer passes on ITC savings as discount
- Makes Permit Power's cost-reduction mission **more urgent than ever** — without tax credits, permitting soft costs become proportionally larger barrier

**Interview angle:** "With the ITC gone, your work on soft costs moves from 'nice to have' to 'essential.' How does this change your strategy or priorities?"

---

### B. The SPEED Act & Federal Permitting Reform Chaos

**Current situation (December 2025-January 2026):**
- House passed the **SPEED Act** (H.R. 4776) — bipartisan permitting reform bill
- BUT: Senate Democrats **froze negotiations** after Trump administration halted all offshore wind construction
- Conservative amendments targeted solar/wind specifically
- SEIA warning: "unequal treatment of solar" will continue to stall projects [^15]

**Key tension:**
- Republicans want permitting reform **only for fossil fuels**
- Democrats walked away after wind project cancellations
- Energy Secretary Chris Wright says admin "wants bipartisan permitting reform done"
- Stalemate means **state-level solutions like Permit Power advocates remain the viable path**

**Interview angle:** "Given federal gridlock, do you see state-level permitting reform as the de facto strategy for the next 2-4 years? How do you think about sequencing states?"

---

### C. AI Data Centers & The Power Crisis

**The numbers are staggering:**
- AI data centers currently draw ~4 GW — enough for 3 million homes [^16]
- BNEF projects US data center demand could reach **106 GW by 2035** (36% higher than April estimates)
- Data centers expected to account for **55% of projected demand growth** through 2030
- Transformer lead times: **4-5 years**; gas turbines sold out through end of decade
- US commercial electricity rates up **~30% over five years**

**Why this matters for Permit Power:**
- Grid strain means **distributed energy (rooftop solar + batteries) becomes grid resilience asset**
- VPPs (Virtual Power Plants) are scaling — aggregating residential batteries for grid services
- Illinois, Michigan, Texas all advancing VPP legislation
- NRG/Sunrun partnership in Texas for residential VPP

**Interview angle:** "Data center demand is creating a grid crisis. Does this change how you frame rooftop solar — from 'cost savings' to 'grid resilience'? Are you tracking VPP opportunities?"

---

### D. SolarAPP+ Momentum: 335+ Jurisdictions and Counting

**Updated stats (January 2026):**
- **335+ jurisdictions** now using SolarAPP+ [^17]
- **110,000+ permits issued**
- **105,000+ staff hours saved**
- Projects permitted **13 business days faster** than traditional process
- **29% fewer failed inspections** vs. traditional permitting
- Now managed by **SolarAPP Foundation** (nonprofit) with UL Solutions as long-term host

**State mandates:**
- California: SolarAPP+ mandated statewide
- Maryland: Mandated + incentive program renewed
- New Jersey: A-5264 passed December 2025 — streamlines residential solar permitting [^18]

**Interview angle:** "SolarAPP+ is at 335 jurisdictions now. What's the path to 1,000? What are the most common adoption barriers you see?"

### E. Net Metering Wars Continue

**California NEM 3.0 fallout:**
- Payments for exported solar cut by ~75%
- Residential solar market dropped significantly in CA
- California Supreme Court (August 2025) ordered lower court to reconsider NEM 3.0 approval [^19]
- Other states watching closely

**Broader pattern:**
- Utilities arguing net metering "unfair" to non-solar customers
- Storage becoming **essential** for rooftop solar economics (use power when you generate it)
- AB 942 (California) would shorten net metering term from 20 to 10 years for existing customers

**Interview angle:** "As net metering erodes, does your strategy shift toward storage-paired systems? How do you think about the interconnection piece vs. permitting piece?"
