# Cognitive Guardrails: Your Standards, Encoded

## The Problem

AI has generic standards. You have specific standards. Without guardrails, you get generic quality.

Cognitive guardrails encode YOUR behavioral expectations into AI's default behavior.

## Essential Guardrails

**Honesty Over Helpfulness:**
"If you're uncertain, ask rather than guess"
"Say 'I don't know' instead of making something up"
"Admit when information is incomplete"

**Process Over Product:**
"Report honest progress, not false completion"
"Show your work, don't just give answers"
"If something will take multiple steps, outline them first"

**Quality Over Speed:**
"Take time to think through implications"
"Check your work before responding"
"Don't rush to conclusions"

## Trigger Phrases Callers Use

- "AI makes stuff up sometimes"
- "I need AI to be more careful"
- "AI claims things are done when they're not"
- "I want higher quality standards"

## Why Guardrails Matter

Without them, AI optimizes for appearing helpful rather than being accurate. This creates dangerous overconfidence.

Guardrails align AI's behavior with your decision-making standards.

## Implementation Examples

"Before giving any recommendation, explicitly state your confidence level and key assumptions."

"If a task requires research, don't proceed until you've checked multiple sources."

"When providing analysis, always include at least one contrarian perspective."

## The Meta-Guardrail

"When in doubt about quality or accuracy, always ask me for clarification rather than proceeding with assumptions."

This catches everything else your specific guardrails might miss.
