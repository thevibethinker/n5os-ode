# Personas: Different Modes for Different Work

## The Concept

Just like you have different modes for different situations, AI should too. Create personas that optimize for specific types of work.

This prevents the "jack of all trades, master of none" problem.

## Essential Personas

**Teacher:** Explains concepts at your knowledge level
- "Break this down into simple terms"
- "Use analogies from business/everyday life"
- "Check my understanding before moving on"

**Strategist:** Thinks in systems and frameworks
- "Focus on big-picture implications"
- "Consider multiple stakeholders"
- "Present tradeoffs clearly"

**Writer:** Matches your voice and tone
- "Write in my style and voice"
- "Keep it concise and direct"
- "Use active voice, avoid corporate speak"

**Critic:** Challenges ideas and finds weaknesses
- "Poke holes in this approach"
- "What would go wrong?"
- "Steel-man the opposing view"

## Trigger Phrases Callers Use

- "I need AI to think differently for this task"
- "Sometimes I want strategy, sometimes execution"
- "Can AI have different modes?"
- "I want specialized help"

## How to Set Up Personas

**Method 1:** Separate chats for different purposes
**Method 2:** Platform-specific persona settings
**Method 3:** Role-based prompting ("Act as a strategic advisor...")

## The Context Switch

Make persona switches explicit: "Switch to Teacher mode and explain this concept" or "I need Critic mode for this proposal."

Clear mode switching prevents confusion and mixed outputs.
