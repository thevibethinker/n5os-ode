# Delay the Draft: The 80/20 Rule for AI Context

## The Problem

AI wants to collapse to an answer immediately. You ask for a proposal, it spits out generic bullet points. You ask for analysis, you get surface-level thoughts.

The default behavior is premature convergence. Your job is to delay that collapse until sufficient context exists.

## The 80/20 Rule

Spend 80% of your conversation building context. Request the deliverable only in the final 20%.

**Wrong Way:**
"Write a marketing proposal for my consulting practice."

**Right Way:**
"I need to build a marketing proposal. Before you write anything, ask me 5 questions about my target audience, current positioning, and biggest challenges."

## Trigger Phrases Callers Use

- "AI gives me generic answers"
- "The output feels too surface-level"
- "I get the same template every time"
- "AI doesn't understand what I really need"

## How to Delay the Draft

1. **Front-load the context:** "I'm going to give you background first..."
2. **Require questions:** "Ask me 5 clarifying questions before responding"
3. **Build in stages:** "First understand my situation, then we'll discuss options"
4. **Use progressive disclosure:** Share information piece by piece

## Next Steps

- Try one conversation this week where you spend 10 minutes building context before asking for output
- Use the phrase: "Ask me questions before answering"
- Notice the quality difference in what AI produces
