# How to Design Rules and Personas

The key insight: rules and personas aren't settings — they're teaching. You're training your AI how to think for you.

## Rules: Think "If-Then" for Your Life

Start with frustrations. Every time you think "I always have to remind it to..." — that's a rule. Good rules are specific and conditional:
- "When I send a food emoji, log it as a meal" — not "be helpful with food."
- "Always use Eastern Time" — not "be aware of timezones."

Start with 3-5 rules. Add more as friction reveals itself.

## Personas: Think "Specialist Colleagues"

A persona isn't a character — it's a lens. Ask: "If I hired someone for this, what would their brief say?"
- What domain do they own?
- What should they always do first?
- What should they never do?

Your default persona handles routing. Specialists handle depth.

## The Meta Principle

The best Zo setups evolve. Start simple, notice what breaks, fix it with a rule or persona. Your system gets smarter because you get clearer about what you actually need.
