Rules customize how Zo behaves. They're persistent instructions that apply across conversations.

A rule has two parts. An optional condition that determines when the rule applies, and a prompt that tells Zo what to do.

You can use rules to set tone and style. For example, "always be concise" or "never use emojis." You can make Zo more critical by telling it to "always critically assess my ideas."

Rules are great for preferences that should apply consistently. Think of them as standing orders for your AI.
