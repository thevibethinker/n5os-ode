The Basics
Careerspan: AI-Powered Candidate Assessment
Transform your hiring process with interviews before resumes—equitable, efficient, and insightful.
The Problem We Solve
Traditional applicant screening relies heavily on resumes, which can be fabricated, AI-generated, or fail to reveal candidates' true capabilities. Most ATS and AI screening tools offer surface-level keyword matching or basic filters that don't provide actionable insights for decision-making. Careerspan goes deeper—delivering decision-ready candidate intelligence that reveals true capabilities, detects inconsistencies, and ensures fair, equitable evaluation. We provide the kind of comprehensive insights you'd normally only get after multiple interview rounds, but before you review a single resume.
How It Works
For Candidates
Applicants create reusable career stories with our AI career coach. These stories become a portable professional narrative they can use across all future job applications, helping them present their best selves consistently.  Once they’ve told enough stories, applying is as simple as LinkedIn’s Easy Apply, but with deep, meaningful, and accurate insights and more transparency for both candidate and employer. 
For Employers
1. Deep Job Analysis We analyze your job description and generate 30+ assessment criteria including:


* Key responsibilities
* Hard skills
* Soft skills


… with clear assessment criteria for how the AI will assess whether an applicant satisfies those needs.  Example


2. Optional, Customizable Assessment Framework Tailor the assessment criteria and assessments to your needs. Add specific requirements, modify assessment standards, or emphasize particular skills that matter most to your organization. 


3. Transparent Candidate Experience Candidates see exactly what criteria they're being assessed against, allowing them to provide relevant stories and examples that demonstrate their qualifications. This transparency helps both parties: candidates can "fill in the gaps" by sharing experiences that showcase relevant skills they might not have thought to highlight otherwise, while you discover diamonds in the rough—talented individuals whose resumes might not scream "perfect fit" but whose actual experience and capabilities align beautifully with your needs. By prompting candidates to draw parallels between their background and your requirements, we help surface non-obvious matches that traditional screening would miss.




4. Comprehensive Results Receive a stack-ranked list of applicants with:


* Overall scores and category breakdowns (Background, Uniqueness, Responsibilities, Hard Skills, Soft Skills)
* "Bottom line" executive summary for each candidate
* Key strengths and development areas
* Career trajectory context (promotion, lateral move, or career change)
* And more!

The Application Process
Once we've agreed on the assessment criteria for your position, we provide you with a unique application link to share with candidates. Applicants use this link to create a Careerspan account and begin their application for your specific role. From there, our AI career coach guides them through telling their professional story, ensuring they have opportunities to demonstrate how their experience aligns with your needs. The entire process is streamlined and candidate-friendly, while generating the deep insights you need to make informed decisions.


Built-In Quality Assurance
Full Transparency: Available upon request, access detailed analysis showing exactly how each assessment was made—approximately 50 pages of analysis per candidate, per position—ensuring consistent and accurate scoring. We don't do this as window-dressing, it's the full internal logic and reasoning the AI used to assess the applicant. You likely won't want it except to build trust in Careerspan's offerings.


Pre-Interview Intelligence: For candidates advancing to interviews, we provide a one-page summary highlighting the applicant's key strengths, development areas, and recommended discussion topics to help you conduct more targeted, productive conversations.


Post-Interview Fraud Detection: Provide us with a transcript or recording of your interview, and we'll automatically cross-reference it against everything we know about the candidate from their application. Our system flags inconsistencies, contradictions, and suspicious patterns that may indicate:


* AI-assisted interview responses
* Detect if it seems to be the same person based on speech patterns
* Fabricated backgrounds or embellished resumes
* Discrepancies between claimed experience and actual knowledge
* Coaching or proxy interviewing


This deep analysis protects you from sophisticated fraud that traditional screening misses entirely, giving you confidence in your hiring decisions.


Bias Mitigation: We're actively developing tools and case studies to ensure cultural and gender-neutral assessments, making hiring more equitable.
Custom Analysis Capabilities
Beyond our standard assessment framework, we offer fully customized analyses tailored to your unique needs. Whether you're looking for specific technical competencies, cultural fit indicators, leadership potential, or even creative requests like evaluating how much a candidate might improve your company softball team—we can design bespoke scoring and analysis to answer the questions that matter most to your organization. Our AI can evaluate virtually any criterion you can articulate, giving you fast, direct insights into exactly what you want to know about your applicants.




Flexible Integration
Careerspan adapts to your hiring workflow. Use us as a standalone solution exclusively for managing referrals—keeping your existing ATS while adding powerful AI-driven insights for referred candidates. Or implement us as a complete drop-in replacement for traditional ATS applicant filtering across all your open positions.  Since we’re building case studies, we’re also willing to manually transfer any and all applicants from Careerspan into your current ATS system as needed. 

The choice is yours, and we can scale with your needs as they evolve.

Hard Skills
1. Product Management
Level Required: Intermediate (practical application)


Importance: 10/10


Why Needed: Example Company needs strong product management to ensure its disaster relief platform delivers faster, more effective assistance through clear problem definition, data-driven decisions, regulatory compliance, and impactful feature releases for vulnerable users in crisis.


Assessment Criteria:


* overall_success_criteria: A candidate has demonstrated the practical ability to manage workflow-heavy products by gathering user needs, defining metrics, prioritizing features, collaborating cross-functionally, using data to inform decisions, and successfully launching and iterating on impactful features, with observable results in process improvement or user experience. Transferable evidence can be drawn from similar work in platforms requiring complex stakeholder alignment, rapid cycles, and outcome measurement.
* why_needed: Example Company needs strong product management to ensure its disaster relief platform delivers faster, more effective assistance through clear problem definition, data-driven decisions, regulatory compliance, and impactful feature releases for vulnerable users in crisis.
* na_criteria: No evidence of product management responsibilities (e.g., no stories about managing a product, gathering requirements, defining features, shipping releases, or using metrics), even in adjacent or transferable settings. For career changers: No examples of owning or driving solutions, user problem-solving, or cross-functional project delivery. For career progression: No sign of exposure to key product tasks.
* fundamental_awareness_criteria: Basic theoretical knowledge of the product management process (e.g., awareness of what PMs do, familiarity with product lifecycle or requirements writing) but no direct or indirect ownership of product outcomes. For career changers: Can articulate what product management is, maybe from following industry news or coursework, but lacks hands-on participation. For career progression: Has observed or supported product processes but not directly contributed beyond administrative tasks.
* novice_criteria: Has participated in some product management tasks under supervision (e.g., helped collect requirements, reviewed user feedback, contributed to a feature launch as part of a team), but did not lead efforts or make key decisions. For career changers: Led or coordinated multi-stakeholder projects or processes (outside digital products), showing ability to define problems and facilitate delivery. For career progression: Actively contributed to PM work (e.g., user stories, bug triage, feature testing) but not yet accountable for roadmap or metrics.
* intermediate_criteria: Shows practical, hands-on experience owning product features or releases—defining problems, engaging with users/stakeholders, setting and measuring success metrics, and collaborating to deliver improvements—with evidence of decision-making and iterations based on results. For career changers: Led end-to-end delivery of tools/processes/platforms (even outside traditional tech), using data to assess impact and iterate, with clear stakeholder feedback loops. For career progression: Progressed from supporting to independently managing substantial product workstreams, with measurable results and increasingly autonomous decision-making.
* advanced_criteria: Has repeatedly managed multi-feature or platform-scale products, often in regulated, high-stakes, or workflow-heavy environments, demonstrating advanced ability to balance user needs, technical feasibility, compliance, and impact; mentors peers and influences strategy beyond their scope.
