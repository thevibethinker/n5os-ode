## Final Draft

I spent 10 years giving career advice.

Then I became a founder and forgot how to follow my own.

---

The irony wasn't lost on me. 

I built Careerspan—a career companion to help people stay organized, confident, and clear about their path.

Meanwhile, I was drowning.

---

Context-switching between investor decks, product decisions, customer calls, ops work.

Losing track of conversations. Missing follow-ups. Forgetting what I promised to whom.

Watching other founders who seemed to have it all together.

And feeling like absolute garbage about myself.

---

For most of my career, I was the person *with* the answers.

The coach. The strategist. The one people came to when they felt lost.

So feeling lost myself? That hit different. 🥴

---

I kept thinking: "If I can't even manage *my own* work, how am I qualified to help anyone else manage theirs?"

The shame was real.

The self-doubt was louder than any investor pitch.

---

So I did what founders do when they're drowning: I built a system.

N5 OS.

Not as a product. As a lifeline.

A way to stop losing track. To follow up. To remember. To reclaim some agency over the chaos.

---

It didn't fix everything overnight.

But it gave me something I'd lost: confidence in my process.

Not confidence that I'm perfect.

Confidence that I have a system that catches what I miss.

That I can trust myself to follow through.

That the quality of my work reflects who I actually am—not just who I am on my worst, most scattered day.

---

I'm still learning.

Still building.

Still comparing myself to founders who seem to have it together (they probably don't).

But I'm not drowning anymore.

And that feels like winning. ✨

---

If you're a founder who's struggling with self-image, productivity, or just... keeping your head above water—you're not alone.

You're not failing.

You're just human.

And that's enough.
