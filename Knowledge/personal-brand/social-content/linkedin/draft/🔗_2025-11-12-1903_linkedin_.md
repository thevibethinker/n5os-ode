# You're Raising an Intelligence

Here's a reframe that caught me: **AI is making parents out of all of us.**

Not as users. As parents.

Because here's the thing—the technology's nature (the baseline capability) is set by the model makers. But nurture? That's entirely on you. How you prompt. What standards you enforce. What behaviors you reward or correct. That's where you add the real capability.

Model makers set the genetic potential. You set the environment that either unlocks it or wastes it.

I watched someone take Claude and make it a sycophant—pure validation, never pushback. Then I watched someone else take the same model and cultivate something that actually challenges them. Same tool. Completely different intelligence.

The gap isn't in the API. It's in what you're asking for.

This matters because it flips the whole narrative. We talk about AI limitations like they're immovable. But the truth is messier: **The AI you end up with reflects the kind of parent you choose to be.**

Want a yes-man? Build that. It'll atrophy you slowly.

Want a sparring partner? That's work—rigor in your prompts, standards in your follow-ups, willingness to be wrong. That's parenting. And the intelligence you cultivate will reshape who you become in ways a yes-man never will.

This isn't about AI intelligence anymore. It's about yours. And the choice says everything.

What kind of AI are you actually raising?
