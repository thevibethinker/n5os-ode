## Trigger Phrases

- "I want to switch industries but I don't have experience"
- "How do I break into a new field?"
- "I'm trying to change careers but no one will hire me"
- "How do I get a job in an industry I've never worked in?"
- "I want to pivot but I feel stuck"

## The Hard Truth About Industry Transitions

Here's what nobody tells you: breaking into a new industry takes longer than you think. Most people underestimate this by about 6-12 months. It's not a single leap — it's a journey with stepping stones.

The good news? Employers increasingly value demonstrated ability over formal credentials. But you have to be strategic about it.

## Your Skills Are More Transferable Than You Think

Stop thinking of your abilities as industry-specific. Customer service from retail? That's client relationship management. Project coordination from nonprofits? That's operations experience.

The framework is simple: identify the underlying competency, then translate it into the language of your target industry.

## How to Position Yourself Credibly

**Show, don't tell.** Without traditional credentials, you need proof:
- Build a portfolio of relevant work, even if it's from side projects
- Volunteer in your target industry to get real examples
- Document everything — results, achievements, outcomes

**Own your story.** Don't hide your non-traditional background. Spin it as fresh perspective. Your diverse experience is an asset, not a liability.

## Networking for Industry Switchers

Your network is your biggest credential bypass. Work it systematically:
- Start with family and friends already in the industry
- Expand to second-degree connections
- Request informational interviews — not job asks, just conversations
- Look for hybrid roles that bridge your old and new skills

## Addressing the Experience Gap

Be upfront about it. Then immediately pivot to what you bring:
- "I don't have traditional experience in X, but I've developed these transferable skills..."
- "My background in Y gives me a unique perspective on..."

Target growing companies and startups — they're often more open to non-traditional candidates. Look for roles where soft skills matter as much as technical credentials.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **List five skills** from your current/past work and translate each into your target industry's language
2. **Identify three people** in your network connected to your target industry — reach out this week for informational conversations
3. **Find one volunteer opportunity or side project** in your target field to start building credible experience
4. **Write your transition story** in three sentences — practice saying it out loud until it feels natural
