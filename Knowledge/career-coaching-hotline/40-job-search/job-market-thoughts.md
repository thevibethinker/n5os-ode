## V's Take on the Job Market

Here's the uncomfortable truth: the job market isn't a rational matching system. It's more like trying to fit oddly-shaped pegs into holes that keep shifting.

**The Paradox of Modern Hiring**

Companies are processing more candidates than ever, but using increasingly reductive tools to do it. Applicant tracking systems focus on narrow keyword matches while missing the bigger picture. The result? You might get eliminated because of minor differences that have nothing to do with whether you'd actually succeed in the role.

**Why Resumes Fail You**

Resumes are semantically limited. They can't capture your soft skills, your values, or your mindset. They definitely can't show your potential. If you lack the social networks to bypass formal screening, this limitation hits you hardest.

**The Talented Beginner Problem**

The market overvalues experience and undervalues potential. Companies obsess over "years of experience" while overlooking promising people who could excel with proper development. This isn't just bad for you — it's bad for them. They're missing out on adaptable talent while chasing credentials.

**The Psychological Weight**

Knowing you might get rejected based on arbitrary keyword differences creates real psychological burden. That weight is real. Acknowledge it.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Stop tailoring to ATS alone** — Yes, keywords matter, but don't reduce yourself to a keyword list. Human eyes eventually see your materials.
2. **Find ways around formal screening** — Networking isn't optional. It's how you bypass systems designed to filter you out.
3. **Reframe "lack of experience"** — Lead with what you bring: adaptability, fresh perspective, hunger to prove yourself.
4. **Protect your psychology** — Rejection often reflects broken systems, not your value.
