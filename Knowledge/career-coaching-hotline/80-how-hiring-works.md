# How Hiring Actually Works

## The Uncomfortable Truth

Hiring is not a meritocracy. It is a pattern-matching exercise conducted by humans with limited time, imperfect information, and misaligned incentives. Understanding this is not cynicism — it is strategic intelligence.

## Pattern Matching

Hiring managers are looking for the candidate who matches the pattern of "person who has succeeded in this role before." This means:

- Similar job titles and industry experience are weighted heavily, even when they are poor predictors of success.
- Unconventional backgrounds face higher scrutiny, regardless of capability.
- First impressions (resume format, LinkedIn photo, interview presence) disproportionately influence outcomes.

This is why tailoring your materials to match the expected pattern is not dishonest — it is necessary communication.

## Recruiter Incentives

- **Internal recruiters** are measured on time-to-fill and quality-of-hire. They want candidates who are easy to present to hiring managers — minimal risk, clear fit.
- **External recruiters** (agencies) earn a fee (typically 15-25% of first-year salary) when a placement is made. They are motivated to fill roles fast and will prioritize candidates who are easy to place.
- Neither type is your advocate. They are the employer's agent. Be helpful and professional, but manage the relationship accordingly.

## The ATS Reality

ATS exists because humans cannot review 250 applications per role. It is a filter, not an evaluator. The system is designed to reduce volume, not to find the best candidate. Your goal is to pass the filter, not to impress it.

## HR as Middleman

In most organizations, HR screens candidates before the hiring manager sees them. HR often lacks deep technical understanding of the role. This means:

- Your resume must communicate value to a non-expert reader.
- Jargon without context will be misunderstood or ignored.
- Clear, outcome-oriented language serves you better than technical depth.

## The Referral Advantage

Referred candidates skip or bypass many of these filters. A referral from a trusted employee tells the hiring manager: "Someone I trust has pre-vetted this person." This is why networking is not optional — it is the most effective strategy for navigating a system that is stacked against cold applicants.
