## The Numbers You Need to Know

Let me give you the real picture. These stats aren't meant to discourage you—they're meant to wake you up to what you're actually dealing with.

### The Application Pile

- **250 applications** arrive for every corporate job posting
- Only **2% of resumes** result in an interview
- Fewer than **1 in 10 resumes** actually reach a hiring manager
- **98% of Fortune 500 companies** use ATS systems to filter candidates

### Recruiter Reality

- Recruiters spend **6-7 seconds** on initial resume review
- **1 in 5 recruiters** reject candidates before finishing the resume
- **63% of recruiters** expect resumes tailored to the specific job
- **77% cite typos** as the top deal-breaker

### Where Job Seekers Go Wrong

- **54% don't tailor** their resume for each application—recruiters see this as a red flag
- **84% rejection rate** for impersonal applications
- Only **35% of job seekers** are adequately prepared for the search

### Timeline Expectations

- Average job search: **5 months**
- Actual time-to-hire: **6 weeks** (but candidates expect 3 weeks)
- Applying before 10 a.m. increases visibility by **90%**
- Applying in the first week increases visibility by **60%**

### The Bigger Picture

- Only **27% of college seniors** secure a good job before graduation
- **50% of graduates** end up underemployed
- **46% of new hires fail** within 18 months—and 89% of those failures are attitude, not skills

### Career Well-Being Matters

Here's the upside: people with high career well-being are **twice as likely** to be thriving in life overall. This work matters.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Tailor every single application.** You're competing against 250 people. Generic doesn't cut it.

2. **Apply early.** First week, before 10 a.m. The math favors the fast.

3. **Fix obvious errors.** Typos kill 77% of your chances before anyone reads your experience.

4. **Prepare differently.** 65% of job seekers aren't ready. Being prepared is a competitive advantage.
