## Trigger Phrases

- "I feel uncomfortable talking about myself"
- "How do I brag without sounding arrogant"
- "I don't know what to say about myself in interviews"
- "How do I sell myself for a job"
- "I'm bad at self-promotion"

## The Peacock Principle

Here's the truth: in nature, it's the birds with the brightest plumage that find mates. Peacocks are the ultimate braggers and we think they're beautiful for it.

If you want your dream opportunity, you need to talk about why you're the dream candidate. The only way to do that is by bragging. For most people, just talking about yourself positively feels like bragging—even when you're just stating facts. Lean into that instinct.

## The Three Pillars

**1. Introspection** — You can't brag if you don't know what to say about yourself.

**2. Packaging** — Frame your bragging so it serves a purpose.

**3. Delivery** — Put yourself forward without sounding like an asshole.

## Introspection: The Anecdote Method

Skip the overwhelming "What am I good at?" questions. Start with stories from your life where you were the protagonist solving a problem or achieving a goal.

For each anecdote, answer these six questions:
1. What was the main problem or challenge?
2. What were the stakes if it went unresolved?
3. How did I figure out what to do?
4. What specific actions did I take?
5. What was the result—for the situation and the people involved?
6. What lessons did I learn?

## Packaging: You're a Composite

No single thing about you is unique. We're all different combinations of traits, skills, and experiences. It's only when you bring it all together that you have something special.

Your job for every application:
- **Figure out what they care about** (read the job description closely—top items matter most)
- **Match what you have to offer** to what they want
- **Communicate through the right channels**

Focus on 5-10 of your strongest, most justifiable qualities. Don't spread yourself thin trying to hit everything. Better to show the best of what you bring in spades.

## The Talking Point Formula

**Trait + How You Cultivated It + Why It Helps in This Role**

Example: "I'm highly organized—I developed systems managing a team of twelve—which means I can keep complex projects on track from day one."

And here's a freebie: **growth mindset** is universally valuable. If you're missing a few things they want, show you're the person who learns and adapts.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **List 5-7 anecdotes** where you solved a problem or hit a goal—don't overthink it
2. **Run one anecdote through the six questions** to build your first "brag story"
3. **Draft one talking point** using the formula: trait + how cultivated + why it helps
4. **Practice saying it out loud** until it feels natural, not boastful
