# Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)

## The Reality

Approximately 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS before a human ever sees them. Large companies receive 250+ applications per open role. ATS is the first gate, and most candidates fail it unknowingly.

## How ATS Works

ATS software parses your resume into structured data fields — name, contact info, work history, education, skills. It then scores your application against the job description using keyword matching, recency, and relevance algorithms. Recruiters see a ranked list and typically review only the top 10-20%.

## Formatting Rules

- **Use standard section headers:** "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills" — not creative alternatives like "My Journey" or "Toolkit."
- **Avoid tables, columns, headers/footers, and text boxes.** ATS parsers often skip or garble these.
- **Submit as .docx or .pdf** depending on the system's preference (when in doubt, .docx parses more reliably).
- **No images or icons.** ATS cannot read them.

## Keyword Optimization

- Extract exact phrases from the job description and incorporate them naturally into your resume.
- Include both acronyms and spelled-out forms (e.g., "Project Management Professional (PMP)").
- Place critical keywords in your skills section AND within your experience bullets — some ATS weight contextual usage higher.
- Do not keyword-stuff. Modern ATS and the humans who follow can detect it.

## The Uncomfortable Truth

ATS is a blunt instrument. It exists because employers are overwhelmed with volume, not because it is good at identifying talent. Understanding how it works gives you an unfair advantage over the 75% who do not.
