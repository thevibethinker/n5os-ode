## The Core Truth About Cover Letters

Your resume is about YOU. Your cover letter is about THEM.

If your cover letter just repeats what's on your resume, you've wasted everyone's time. A cover letter should contain *new information* — specifically, why you want THIS job at THIS company, and proof you can deliver.

## The Three-Part Structure

**Paragraph 1: The Hook**
Open with your name, the position, and *why this company*. Not generic praise — a specific connection. Did their recent project excite you? Does their mission align with your background? Make it personal, but don't overshare.

Bad: "I've always been fascinated by complex political developments."
Good: "Coming from a rural ethnic minority in Myanmar that experienced human rights abuses, I understand why ethics in international affairs matters."

**Paragraphs 2-3: The Evidence**
Prove you're qualified with *specific examples*, not claims. Don't walk chronologically through your resume — pick the 2-3 most relevant experiences and go deeper.

Bad: "I have strong research and multitasking skills."
Good: "As managing editor, I led editorial meetings, coordinated with twelve writers, and used InDesign to finalize layouts on weekly deadlines."

Treat the job description like a checklist. If they say "data analysis," and you've done it, use their exact phrase.

**Conclusion: The Close**
Thank them. State your availability. One sentence summarizing your fit. Done.

## Common Mistakes

- **Generic letters** — Hiring teams spot these instantly
- **Quoting random famous people** — Unless they founded the company, skip it
- **Repeating their mission statement back to them** — They know their mission
- **Forgetting to change the company name** — This one's a killer

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Pull up your last cover letter** — Circle anything that's just restating your resume. Cut it.
2. **Research the company for 15 minutes** — Find one specific thing about their work that genuinely interests you. Lead with that.
3. **Turn one "I have X skill" sentence into a concrete example** — Show, don't tell.
4. **Triple-check the company name and position title** — Seriously. Do it now.
