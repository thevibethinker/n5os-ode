## Trigger Phrases
- "Can I use AI to write my cover letter?"
- "How do I get ChatGPT to write a good cover letter?"
- "My AI cover letters sound generic"
- "Is it okay to use AI for cover letters?"
- "How do I prompt AI for job applications?"

## The 3-Step Workflow

AI isn't magic. It doesn't know your life story. You have to set it up for success. Here's my method:

### Step 1: Framing
Tell the AI what role it's playing. This dramatically improves output quality.

Specify: who the AI is emulating, who you are, what you need, and what success looks like. Think of it like briefing a new assistant on day one.

### Step 2: Digestion
Feed the AI three things:
- The job description
- Your resume or summary of relevant experiences
- Your thoughts on the company and role

Ask it to digest this material before generating anything. The less you share, the more it fabricates or goes generic.

### Step 3: Generation
Now tell it to write the cover letter with specific instructions on:
- Structure (intro, qualification paragraphs, conclusion)
- Writing style and tone
- Any constraints or considerations

## Refining the Output

Your first draft is rarely your final draft. Use refinement prompts like:
- "Make paragraph two more succinct"
- "Use a different example in the conclusion"
- "Make the tone less formal"

You can also feed the AI examples of your past writing so it learns your voice.

## The Litmus Tests

Run these before submitting:

**Name-Blind Test:** Remove the company and role name. Can you still tell exactly what job it's for? Do the same with your name—could anyone have written this, or only you?

**Resume Repetition Test:** Read your resume first, then the cover letter. Does the letter just repeat bullet points, or does it extend and add context?

## Words of Caution

AI has a limited context window. It starts forgetting details over long conversations. Use a fresh chat for each cover letter.

**Always proofread for hallucinations.** The AI will confidently invent details about your experience. Read everything before you hit submit.

## Bonus: Building Company Talking Points

If your "why this company" feels weak, start a separate chat. Ask the AI to interview you about why you're interested. Answer its questions, do some research, then have it summarize your responses into talking points. Use those in your main cover letter workflow.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Open a new chat** and practice the framing step—describe the AI's role before asking for anything
2. **Gather your three inputs:** JD, resume, and 3-4 sentences on why this company interests you
3. **Generate a draft,** then run both litmus tests before finalizing
4. **Read every word out loud** to catch hallucinations and awkward phrasing
