# Careerspan: An Anti-Resume Approach

## What Is Careerspan?

Careerspan is an intelligence-first approach to career coaching that inverts the traditional resume-centric model. Instead of starting with a resume template and filling in blanks, Careerspan starts with deep decomposition of a candidate's professional identity and builds outward from evidence.

## The Anti-Resume Philosophy

Traditional career coaching starts with the resume — a document format designed for recruiter convenience, not candidate truth. Careerspan argues this is backwards. When you start with the format, you lose information that does not fit the format. When you start with the full picture, you can generate any format without loss.

## Evidence-Based Coaching

Every recommendation in Careerspan is grounded in data:

- **Skills decomposition** maps the candidate's actual competencies, not just their job titles.
- **Achievement extraction** identifies impact and scale from career narratives.
- **Market alignment** compares the candidate's profile against real job descriptions and hiring patterns.
- **Gap analysis** identifies specific, actionable areas for development.

## Intelligence-First

Careerspan treats career development as an intelligence problem. Before a candidate writes a single application, they should understand:

- How their target market actually hires (process, tools, incentives)
- Where their skills have the highest market value
- Which gaps are blocking them and which are irrelevant
- Who the key decision-makers are and how to reach them

## The Decomposition Pipeline

1. **Ingest** — Collect all career artifacts: resumes, LinkedIn, performance reviews, project descriptions
2. **Decompose** — Break down into atomic units: skills, achievements, experiences, tools, interests
3. **Analyze** — Score alignment against target roles, identify patterns, surface hidden strengths
4. **Synthesize** — Generate tailored outputs: resumes, talking points, networking strategies, interview prep

## The Outcome

The candidate emerges with not just better materials, but a deeper understanding of their own professional identity — what they offer, who needs it, and how to reach them.
