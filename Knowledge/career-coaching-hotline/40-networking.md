# Networking

## Networking Is Intelligence Gathering

Effective networking is not about asking for jobs. It is about gathering intelligence — understanding how industries work, what companies value, where opportunities are forming, and who makes decisions. The job leads follow naturally from genuine professional relationships.

## Quality Over Quantity

One meaningful 20-minute conversation with someone inside your target company is worth more than 500 LinkedIn connection requests. Depth beats breadth. A small network of people who know your work and would vouch for you is more powerful than a large network of acquaintances.

## Value-First Outreach

Before asking for anything, offer something. Share a relevant article. Make an introduction. Provide a useful perspective on their industry. The principle is simple: give before you ask.

## The Outreach Framework

1. **Research the person.** Read their LinkedIn, their writing, their company's recent news. Find a genuine connection point.
2. **Lead with specificity.** "I read your article on supply chain resilience and found your point about regional diversification compelling" beats "I'd love to pick your brain."
3. **Make a small, specific ask.** "Would you have 15 minutes to share your perspective on how [company] approaches [specific topic]?" Not "Can you help me find a job?"
4. **Follow up with gratitude and value.** After the conversation, send a thank-you note that references something specific you discussed.

## Informational Interviews

These are the most underused tool in job searching. Most people are willing to talk about their work for 15-20 minutes. The conversation gives you insider knowledge, and it builds a relationship that may lead to referrals. Referrals are how 60-80% of jobs are actually filled.

## The Long Game

Networking is not something you do when you need a job. It is something you maintain continuously so that when opportunities arise — on either side — the relationship already exists.
