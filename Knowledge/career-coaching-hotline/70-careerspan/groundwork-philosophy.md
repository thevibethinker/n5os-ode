- "I'm not sure what to say in interviews"
- "How do I figure out what I'm good at?"

## The #1 Mistake Job Seekers Make

Most people jump straight into applications without doing the groundwork. They polish their resume, blast it to 50 companies, and wonder why nothing sticks.

Here's the hard truth: **you can't market a product you don't understand.** And right now, you are the product.

## Why Self-Understanding Comes First

You are an amorphous cloud of skills, experiences, and competencies. Before you write a single bullet point, you need to understand what's actually in that cloud.

Most people struggle to:
- Identify their best attributes
- Decide which experiences matter
- Articulate what makes them valuable
- Tell their professional story coherently

This isn't a character flaw — it's genuinely hard to be objective about yourself. But skip this step, and everything downstream fails.

## The Career Services Onion

Think of career support in layers:
- **Outer layer**: Self-service tools, job boards, templates
- **Middle layer**: Workshops, group coaching, general advice
- **Inner layer**: High-intensity 1:1 coaching with real feedback

Most people only access the outer layers. The problem? Those layers assume you already know your story. They can't teach you who you are.

## You Literally Can't Brag If You Don't Know What to Say

Here's what I tell my clients: the best resume in the world can't save you if you don't know your own story. Interviewers smell uncertainty. They can tell when you're reciting bullet points versus speaking from genuine self-knowledge.

The connection is direct: **introspection drives interview performance.** When you deeply understand your value, you communicate it with confidence. When you don't, you fumble.

## The Inside-Out Approach

We build from the inside out:
1. Collect your stories first (specific situations, actions, results)
2. Extract patterns and themes
3. Only then craft external materials

This isn't optional prep work — it's the foundation everything else rests on.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Start a story bank**: Write down 5 specific professional challenges you've overcome, with concrete details and outcomes
2. **Ask the meta-question**: For each story, ask "What does this say about how I work?"
3. **Get an outside perspective**: Find someone who can ask you probing questions — a mentor, coach, or trusted colleague
4. **Stop applying** until you can clearly articulate your value in 60 seconds
