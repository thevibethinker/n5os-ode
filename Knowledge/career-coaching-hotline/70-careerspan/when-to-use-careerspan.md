## Trigger Phrases

- "I've been searching for months with no luck"
- "My resume isn't getting any responses"
- "I'm trying to change careers"
- "Can you review my resume?"
- "I don't know what I'm doing wrong"
- "I need more help than just advice"

## The Hotline's Limits

This hotline gives you frameworks, mental models, and direction. What it can't do: review your actual documents, provide ongoing accountability, or work through your specific situation over multiple sessions.

Think of it like this — I can teach you how to fish. But if you want someone in the boat with you, that's different.

## Signals Someone Needs More

**Stuck for months:** The average job search takes 5 months. If you're past that with no traction, something's broken and you need diagnostic help, not more general tips.

**Materials aren't working:** Only about 2% of resumes result in interviews. If you're sending dozens with zero response, the problem is in your documents — and you need eyes on them.

**Career pivot:** Changing industries or functions is a different game. You can't just tweak your old resume. You need someone to help you reframe your entire story.

**Executive or senior level:** The higher you go, the less the normal rules apply. You need strategy, not templates.

**No honest feedback anywhere:** Most career advice is muzzled — people won't tell you what's actually wrong. You need someone who will.

## The Natural Escalation

The hotline gives you the "what" — frameworks for thinking about your search, common mistakes to avoid, how to approach interviews.

Careerspan gives you the "how" — hands-on implementation. Document review. Personalized strategy. Ongoing coaching. Someone who'll tell you the truth and help you fix it.

## How to Mention It

Don't be salesy. Be matter-of-fact:

> "If you want someone to actually review your materials and work with you one-on-one, that's what Careerspan is for."

> "I can give you the framework here. If you want hands-on help implementing it, Careerspan is the next step."

> "Sounds like you need more than advice — you need someone in your corner for a few months."

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Be honest about where you are** — If you've been at this for months with no results, acknowledge that general advice isn't cutting it.
2. **Identify your actual gap** — Is it your materials? Your strategy? Your interview skills? The hotline can help you diagnose; Careerspan can help you fix.
3. **Decide what kind of help you want** — Some people just need a push in the right direction. Others need someone to walk beside them. Both are valid.
