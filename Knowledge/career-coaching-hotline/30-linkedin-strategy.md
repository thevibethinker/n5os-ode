# LinkedIn Strategy

## Follow the Money

LinkedIn generates approximately $7 billion from employer/recruiter products (Talent Solutions) and only $1.7 billion from job seekers (Premium subscriptions). The platform is built to serve employers and recruiters first. Understanding this changes how you use it.

## What This Means for You

Recruiters search LinkedIn using keywords, filters, and Boolean queries. They are looking for candidates who match specific criteria. Your job is to be findable and compelling when they search.

## Profile Optimization

- **Headline:** Do not just list your current title. Include keywords recruiters search for. "Senior Product Manager | B2B SaaS | Growth Strategy | Data-Driven Decision Making" beats "Product Manager at Acme Corp."
- **About Section:** Write in first person. Tell your professional story with keywords woven naturally throughout. This is your elevator pitch, not your biography.
- **Experience:** Mirror your tailored resume but optimize for searchability. LinkedIn allows more detail than a one-page resume.

## The Skills Section Is Critical

LinkedIn's algorithm heavily weights the Skills section for search ranking. Add the maximum 50 skills. Prioritize hard skills and industry-specific terms that recruiters actually search for. Get endorsements for your top skills — they boost search visibility.

## Activity Signals

Recruiters notice candidates who are active on the platform. Comment thoughtfully on industry posts. Share relevant content. Engage with companies you are targeting. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards engagement with visibility.

## The 1% Rule

Only about 1% of LinkedIn users create content regularly. Even modest, consistent activity puts you ahead of 99% of the platform in visibility.
