# Career Direction

## Five Questions Before the Job Search

Before writing a single resume bullet, answer these questions honestly:

1. **What do I want to do?** Not what you have been doing, not what you think you should do — what genuinely interests and motivates you.
2. **What am I good at?** Skills fingerprinting reveals your actual strengths, which may differ from your job title.
3. **What do I value?** Compensation, autonomy, mission, stability, growth, location, team culture — rank them honestly.
4. **What am I willing to trade?** Every job involves trade-offs. Knowing yours in advance prevents accepting roles you will regret.
5. **What is my timeline?** Urgency shapes strategy. A search with financial runway looks different from one driven by immediate need.

## The Career Inventory

Take stock of where you actually are:

- **Skills:** Technical, interpersonal, domain-specific — the full inventory, not just what is on your current resume.
- **Experience:** Roles, projects, industries, and the patterns across them.
- **Network:** Who do you know? In which industries? At which companies? This is infrastructure, not just contacts.
- **Resources:** Financial runway, time availability, geographic flexibility, educational credentials.
- **Constraints:** Family obligations, visa status, geographic limits, financial minimums.

## Direction vs. Destination

You do not need to know your dream job to conduct an effective search. You need a direction — a set of criteria that helps you evaluate opportunities and filter out noise. Direction can be refined through action; waiting for perfect clarity leads to paralysis.

## The 70% Rule

If a role matches 70% of your criteria, it is worth pursuing. No job will be 100%. Holding out for perfection is a form of avoidance disguised as strategy.
