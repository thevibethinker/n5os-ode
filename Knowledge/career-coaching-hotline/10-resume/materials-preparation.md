## The Foundation: Prepare Before You Apply

Here's what most people get wrong: they start applying before their materials are ready. Then they're scrambling, making rushed edits, and sending out half-baked resumes. Don't be that person.

A systematic approach beats a scatter-shot approach every single time. Get your house in order first.

## Your Materials Toolkit

You need four core assets:
- **Master Resume** — A multi-page document with everything you could ever say about yourself
- **Cover Letter Framework** — Templates you can customize (only when needed)
- **LinkedIn Profile** — Aligned with your target roles
- **Networking Messages** — Ready-to-send templates for outreach

## The Master Resume Concept

This is your central hub. It contains every bullet, achievement, skill, and experience you might ever use. When you apply for a job, you *subtract* irrelevant content to create a tailored version — you don't build from scratch each time.

Keep it organized:
- Store everything in one cloud folder
- Label files clearly: date, job title, company
- Keep both .docx and PDF versions
- Track which variations you've created

## Your Evidence Bank

You need proof for your claims. Start collecting now:
- **Impact metrics** — Numbers showing your results (sales closed, efficiency gained, growth achieved)
- **Scale metrics** — Numbers showing the level you operated at (clients managed, projects delivered, team size)
- **Positive feedback** — Emails, reviews, testimonials from managers and colleagues
- **Certifications and awards** — Anything that validates your capabilities

This isn't bragging. It's preparation. When someone asks "tell me about a time you..." you'll have the receipts.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Create your master resume folder** in cloud storage. Set up subfolders for master documents, tailored versions, and supporting materials.

2. **Start your evidence bank** — Open a document and brain-dump every metric, compliment, and achievement you can remember. You'll organize it later.

3. **Audit what you have** — Do you have a current resume, LinkedIn profile, and at least one cover letter draft? Identify the gaps before you apply anywhere.
