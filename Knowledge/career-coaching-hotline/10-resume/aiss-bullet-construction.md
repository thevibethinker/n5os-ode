## The AISS Framework

Most resume bullets are forgettable. They list tasks instead of telling a story. AISS fixes that.

AISS stands for **Action, Impact, Scale, Skill**. Every bullet you write should have all four components. Here's what each one does:

**Action** — Start with a strong verb. "Led," "developed," "implemented." Not "responsible for." Not "helped with." You *did* something. Say what.

**Impact** — Why did it matter? What changed because of your work? This is the outcome, the result. Without impact, you're just describing a job duty.

**Scale** — Put a number on it. Percentages, dollar amounts, team sizes, timeframes. Numbers make your achievements real and comparable. "Improved efficiency" means nothing. "Improved efficiency by 30%" means something.

**Skill** — Name the tool, methodology, or competency you used. This gets keywords into your resume for applicant tracking systems, and it shows *how* you achieved the result.

## The Formula in Action

Here's the pattern: **[Action] + [Impact] + [Scale] + [Skill]**

Example: "Led a process improvement that increased efficiency by 30% across 8 departments using Lean methodologies."

Every piece is there. Strong verb. Clear outcome. Quantified scope. Named skill.

## Why This Works

Generic bullets describe job duties. AISS bullets tell achievement stories. Hiring managers skim resumes in seconds — they're looking for proof you can deliver results. AISS gives them that proof in one line.

It also makes your resume modular. You can swap bullets in and out based on what each job needs. Different role emphasizes different skills? Adjust which AISS bullets you include.

## Operating Principles

- **Keep it brief** — Two lines maximum per bullet
- **Active voice only** — No "was responsible for"
- **Every word earns its place** — Cut filler ruthlessly
- **Customize for each application** — Match your skills to the job description's keywords

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Audit your current bullets** — Pick three. Do they have all four AISS components? If not, rewrite them.

2. **Build a number bank** — List every metric you can think of from your work: revenue, percentages, team sizes, timeframes, volumes. You'll use these for Scale.

3. **Match skills to target roles** — Pull keywords from job descriptions you want. Make sure your Skill components include those terms.

4. **Test the "so what?" filter** — Read each bullet and ask "so what?" If you can't answer with a clear impact, rewrite it.
