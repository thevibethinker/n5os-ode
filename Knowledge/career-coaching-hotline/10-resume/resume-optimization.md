## The Truth About ATS

Here's the reality: ATS systems reject 75% of resumes before a human ever sees them. But the solution isn't what most people think.

Modern ATS systems use natural language processing—they're smarter than simple keyword matching. They can recognize synonyms and context. But here's the catch: you don't know which ATS you're dealing with. Some are sophisticated, some are dumb as rocks. So you optimize for the lowest common denominator.

**The diminishing returns problem:** Using a keyword 2-3 times is ideal. Beyond that, you're not helping your score—you're just making your resume sound robotic. Keyword stuffing can actually hurt you.

## Section-by-Section Optimization

**Executive Summary:** Two to three sentences max. Integrate keywords naturally while showing quantifiable impact. "Strategic marketing leader who drove 40% revenue growth" beats "results-driven professional" every time.

**Bullet Points:** Use the formula: Action + Context + Result. Start with strong verbs—"led," "optimized," "developed." Include numbers. "Managed client projects" becomes "Led 5 simultaneous client projects ($2M+ total value) with 100% on-time delivery."

**Education:** Use exact degree names as they appear on your diploma. Keep dates consistent—pick either "March 2025" or "03/2025" and stick with it throughout.

**Skills Section:** Pull exact phrases from the job description. Organize by category (Technical Skills, Tools, etc.). If the posting says "project management," don't write "managing projects."

## Why Clean Formatting Matters

Submit in .docx format when possible—it parses better than PDF. Use standard section headers: "Experience," "Education," "Skills." Avoid tables, columns, graphics, and fancy bullet symbols. Keep it boring visually. The ATS doesn't care that it looks pretty—it cares that it can read it.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Pull up a target job description** and circle every repeated skill or requirement—those are your priority keywords
2. **Rewrite your top 3 bullet points** using the Action + Context + Result formula with actual numbers
3. **Check your formatting**—remove any tables, columns, or graphics and switch to simple bullet points
4. **Save as .docx** and test it by copying all the text into a plain text editor—if it looks scrambled, the ATS will struggle too
