## Trigger Phrases

- "Should I customize my resume for each job?"
- "How do I tailor my resume?"
- "Is it okay to use the same resume everywhere?"
- "How much should I change my resume for different jobs?"
- "What's a master resume?"

## The Hard Truth About Customization

Here's something that should get your attention: 61% of hiring managers say resume customization is the number one thing that makes a candidate stand out. Not your fancy degree. Not your impressive title. Whether you bothered to tailor your resume to *this specific job*.

Submitting the same generic resume everywhere? That's extending your job search by weeks or months. Recruiters make initial judgments in about five seconds. If it's not immediately obvious your resume was prepared for this role, you're done.

## The Master Resume Approach

Stop thinking of yourself as a one-page document. You're an amorphous cloud of skills, experiences, and competencies. Different jobs need different slices of you.

Here's the method: Build a master resume — a comprehensive document containing every bullet point you could ever write about yourself. This isn't for submitting. It's your career inventory.

When a job comes up, you don't *add* to fit the role. You *subtract* the irrelevant. Start comprehensive, then curate down to what matters for this specific position.

## The 70% Rule

Your customized resume should demonstrate evidence of at least 70% of the required capabilities in the job description. If you can't hit that threshold, the role might not be the right fit.

Your skills section specifically should cover 70% or more of the hard and soft skills mentioned in the JD. This isn't about keyword stuffing — it's about showing genuine alignment.

## Make It Obvious

Every bullet point must earn its place. If an achievement doesn't directly relate to what they're looking for, cut it — even if it's impressive. Relevance beats prestige every time.

The goal: anyone glancing at your resume should immediately think, "This person prepared this for us."

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Start your master resume today.** Open a document and dump every bullet point you've ever written, plus new ones you haven't captured yet. This becomes your source material.

2. **Pull up a job you're interested in.** Highlight every requirement, skill, and responsibility. Now search your master resume for matches.

3. **Do the 70% check.** Before submitting, verify your resume shows evidence of 70%+ of their requirements and your skills section covers 70%+ of their listed skills.

4. **Apply the five-second test.** Hand your customized resume to someone unfamiliar with the job. Can they guess what role it's for in five seconds? If not, keep editing.
