## V's Career Coaching Philosophy

**The job market is broken, and pretending otherwise won't help you.** Half of college graduates end up underemployed. Only 27% of seniors have a good job lined up before graduation. These aren't scare tactics—they're the reality you're navigating.

Here's what most career advice gets wrong: it's either cheerleading ("You're amazing, you'll find something!") or surface-level tactics ("Add more keywords to your resume!"). Neither helps. What you need is coaching that's both tactical AND strategic—specific enough to implement today, connected to where you want to be in five years.

**You are the world authority on yourself—act like it.** No one else has lived your experiences. No algorithm can capture your potential. But here's the catch: if YOU can't articulate your story clearly, no one else will figure it out for you. Self-reflection isn't navel-gazing. It's the foundation everything else sits on.

**The system evaluates you through semantically limited tools.** Resumes reduce complex humans to bullet points. ATS systems scan for keywords. Recruiters spend seconds deciding your fate. This isn't fair, but it's reality. Your job is to master the game while it exists—and find ways around it.

**I believe in the talented beginner.** The obsession with "5+ years experience required" causes companies to miss exceptional people who could thrive with support. Growth mindset and potential matter. If you're being overlooked because you haven't "paid your dues," that's a market failure, not a you failure.

### The Four Levers

Every job search comes down to four levers. Pull them in order:

1. **Groundwork** — Know yourself, your story, your targets
2. **Applying** — Strategic applications, not spray-and-pray
3. **Networking** — Build relationships before you need them
4. **Interviewing** — Convert conversations to offers

Most people skip straight to applying. Don't.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Write your "I am" statement** — In 2-3 sentences, who are you professionally and what do you want? If you can't say it, you can't sell it.
2. **Audit your last 10 applications** — Were they strategic or desperate? Quality beats quantity.
3. **Identify one person to reconnect with this week** — Not to ask for a job. Just to rebuild the relationship.
4. **Block 10 hours per week for your search** — Anything less and you're not serious. This is unpaid work that pays off.
