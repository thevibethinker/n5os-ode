# The Five Career Stages

## A Framework for Career Development

Career development is not a single activity — it is a progression through distinct stages, each with its own focus, tools, and challenges.

## Stage 1: Groundwork (Self-Reflection)

**Focus:** Understanding who you are professionally before you market yourself.

Activities: Skills fingerprinting, anecdote collection, values clarification, career inventory. This stage builds the foundation for everything that follows. Most people skip it and wonder why their materials feel generic.

## Stage 2: Materials (Resume, LinkedIn, Cover Letter)

**Focus:** Creating targeted marketing documents that communicate your value.

Activities: Master resume creation, LinkedIn optimization, cover letter templates, portfolio development. Materials should be tailored for each application — a single generic resume is not a strategy.

## Stage 3: Outreach (Networking, Applications, Recruiter Relationships)

**Focus:** Getting in front of the right people and opportunities.

Activities: Networking conversations, direct applications, recruiter outreach, informational interviews. This is where most of the work happens and where most people underinvest.

## Stage 4: Performance (Interviews, Negotiation)

**Focus:** Converting opportunities into offers.

Activities: Interview preparation, story banking, salary research, negotiation practice. Performance is a skill that improves with deliberate practice.

## Stage 5: Transition (First 90 Days)

**Focus:** Succeeding in the new role to make the move stick.

Activities: Stakeholder mapping, quick wins, learning the culture, building internal relationships. The job search is not over when you accept the offer — it is over when you have established yourself.

## Using the Framework

Diagnose which stage you are in. Most people try to do Stage 3 (outreach) without completing Stage 1 (groundwork). The stages build on each other. Rushing ahead creates weak foundations that collapse under pressure.
