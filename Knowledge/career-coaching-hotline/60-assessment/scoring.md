## Scoring Method

Your assessment score tells us where to focus your energy. Here's how it works:

- **A = 1.0** (Strong in this area)
- **B = 1.5** (Solid foundation)
- **C = 2.0** (Needs development)
- **D = 3.0** (Priority area)

We average your responses to determine your career readiness stage.

## The Three Stages

### Groundwork Stage (Score < 1.5)

You're not ready to apply yet — and that's actually good news. Most people rush into applications unprepared and wonder why nothing sticks.

**Focus areas:** Self-reflection, skills fingerprinting, building your story bank

**Coaching note:** You are the world authority on yourself — act like it! Before you can sell yourself, you need to know what you're selling.

**Follow-up questions:**
- Can you tell me three specific problems you've solved at work?
- What skills would a former boss say you're known for?

**Quick wins:** Write down five professional stories using the challenge-action-result format. This alone will transform your interviews.

### Application Stage (Score 1.5–2.5)

You know yourself. Now it's about translating that into materials that get past the gatekeepers.

**Focus areas:** Resume optimization, cover letter strategy, beating the ATS

**Coaching note:** The job search isn't about volume — it's about precision. Ten targeted applications beat a hundred spray-and-pray submissions.

**Follow-up questions:**
- How are you tailoring your resume for each application?
- What's your system for tracking where you've applied?

**Quick wins:** Pull three job postings you want and highlight the repeated keywords. Those go in your resume — verbatim.

### Advanced Stage (Score ≥ 2.5)

Your foundation is solid. Time to work the human side of hiring.

**Focus areas:** Networking strategy, interview preparation, career positioning

**Coaching note:** Most jobs aren't posted. The best opportunities come through relationships, not job boards.

**Follow-up questions:**
- Who in your network works at companies you're targeting?
- How many informational conversations have you had this month?

**Quick wins:** Reach out to three people this week — not to ask for a job, but to learn about their work. Curiosity opens doors that applications can't.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Know your stage** — Don't skip ahead. Each stage builds on the last.
2. **Pick one quick win** — Start with the action for your stage today.
3. **Set your weekly hours** — Job searching is work. Budget 10+ hours weekly if you're serious.
4. **Get honest feedback** — Most people won't tell you the hard truths. Find someone who will.
