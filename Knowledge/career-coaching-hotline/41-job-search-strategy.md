# Job Search Strategy

## It Is a Sales Funnel

A job search is a numbers game with a conversion funnel. You need volume at the top (applications and outreach), conversion in the middle (interviews), and closure at the bottom (offers). Understanding this prevents the emotional spiral of rejection.

## The Numbers

- The average corporate job posting receives **250 applications.**
- The average job search takes **5 months** (longer for senior roles, shorter in high-demand fields).
- Only **2-5%** of online applications result in an interview.
- Referrals are **5-8x** more likely to result in a hire than cold applications.

## The Hidden Job Market

An estimated 60-80% of jobs are filled without ever being publicly posted. They are filled through internal referrals, recruiter networks, and direct outreach. If your entire strategy is applying to posted jobs online, you are competing for a fraction of available opportunities.

## The Three-Channel Strategy

1. **Direct Applications** (20% of effort) — Apply to posted roles. Tailor each resume. Focus on roles where you meet 70%+ of requirements.
2. **Networking and Referrals** (50% of effort) — Informational interviews, alumni networks, industry events, warm introductions. This is where most hires actually happen.
3. **Recruiter Relationships** (30% of effort) — Build relationships with recruiters in your industry. They have access to roles you will never see posted.

## Pipeline Management

Treat your job search like a CRM. Track every application, conversation, follow-up, and status. Know where each opportunity stands. Follow up systematically — most candidates give up after one touchpoint, but most hiring processes require three to five.

## Emotional Management

Job searching is psychologically brutal. Build structure into your week. Set daily targets (applications sent, people contacted). Celebrate process metrics, not just outcomes. Take breaks. The search is a marathon, not a sprint.
