## The Reality Check

Here's what you're up against: 250 applicants per corporate role on average. 75% get filtered out by applicant tracking systems before a human sees them. When a recruiter does look at your resume? Ten seconds. That's it.

It's not your imagination — the job market is brutal. But there's a system that works.

## The 4 Levers Framework

Every successful job search pulls four levers in this order:

1. **Groundwork** — Know what you offer and what you want
2. **Applying** — Targeted applications with tailored materials
3. **Networking** — Real human connections that open doors
4. **Interviewing** — Prepared, polished, and flexible

Most people skip straight to applying. That's mistake number one.

## What Kills Your Job Hunt

- Jumping into applications without doing groundwork
- Prioritizing quantity over quality
- Submitting the same resume everywhere
- Avoiding networking and cold outreach
- Waiting until you get an interview to prepare

## The Groundwork Mandate

You are the world authority on yourself — act like it!

Before you apply anywhere, document your experiences, impact, and skills. What responsibilities have you fulfilled? What measurable results have you delivered? Take detailed notes. This becomes everything.

## Application Rules

Use the 70% threshold: Does your resume show 70% of required capabilities? Do 70% of your bullets include impact metrics? Does your skills section cover 70% of what the job description asks for?

Start with a master resume and subtract what's irrelevant for each role. If a recruiter can't tell you customized it, you haven't.

## Networking That Works

Research the person. Be friendly but professional. Make a clear ask — a 10-minute conversation, not a referral request. And always, always send a thank-you the next day.

## Interview Prep: The START Method

Structure every answer: Situation, Task, Action, Result, Takeaway. Have a polished 1-2 minute "tell me about yourself." Know your strengths, weaknesses, and key achievement stories cold.

The best interview prep is knowing yourself inside and out.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Document 5 achievements** with specific metrics before applying to anything else
2. **Audit your current resume** against the 70% rules for a job you want
3. **Identify 3 people** to reach out to this week — research them first
4. **Write your "tell me about yourself"** answer and time it
