## The Anti-Spray-and-Pray Approach

Here's the uncomfortable truth: 75% of applications never get seen by a human. So if you're blasting out hundreds of applications, you're mostly just exhausting yourself.

Quality beats quantity every time. You have about 5 seconds to grab attention and 30 seconds to make your case. That's it. Better to apply to 10 roles thoughtfully than 100 carelessly.

## Resume Tactics That Actually Work

**Keep a master resume** — a comprehensive document with every accomplishment you've ever had. Add to it as things happen. When you apply somewhere, pull the relevant bullets and leave the rest behind.

**Match one-to-one.** Look at the job requirements. Make sure your resume has something addressing each major one. If a requirement isn't covered, you're giving them a reason to pass.

**Make every word count.** Recruiters will hold unnecessary content against you. If it's not relevant to this specific role, cut it.

## Cover Letters: Less Is More

Only use a cover letter when you have something the resume can't say — career gaps, non-traditional experience, a genuine personal connection to the company.

Keep it under 300 words. Two or three short paragraphs. Don't repeat your resume. Draw one authentic connection and get out.

## Networking Without Being Weird

Start with people who already know you — family, friends, former colleagues. Then expand to second-degree connections.

For cold outreach, do your homework first. Find a genuine connection point. Write a short message with a clear ask. Expect a 10-20% response rate. That's normal — don't take silence personally.

Remember: you're building relationships, not collecting favors. Network before you need it.

## Virtual Interview Setup

Lighting. Camera angle. Clean background. Sticky note under your camera to remind yourself where to look. These basics matter more than people think.

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Start your master resume today.** Open a doc and dump every accomplishment you can remember. You'll thank yourself later.

2. **Pick your next application and audit it.** Does your resume address every major requirement in the job description? If not, fix it before you hit submit.

3. **Message one person in your network this week.** Not to ask for a job — just to reconnect. Plant seeds before you need to harvest.

4. **Do a test video call.** Check your lighting and camera angle. Fix the obvious stuff now, not five minutes before your interview.
