# Interview Preparation

## The START Method

START is a structured approach for answering behavioral interview questions:

- **S**ituation — Set the scene briefly. Where were you? What was the context?
- **T**ask — What was your specific responsibility or challenge?
- **A**ction — What did you do? Be specific about YOUR actions, not the team's.
- **R**esult — What was the outcome? Quantify when possible.
- **T**akeaway — What did you learn? How did it shape your approach going forward?

The Takeaway differentiates START from STAR. It shows self-awareness and growth mindset — two things interviewers consistently rate highly.

## Story Banking

Before any interview, prepare 8-12 stories from your career that cover common behavioral themes: leadership, conflict resolution, failure and recovery, collaboration, innovation, handling ambiguity, and delivering under pressure. Each story should be adaptable to multiple question types.

## Proof Points

Every claim you make in an interview should be backed by a proof point — a specific example, metric, or outcome. "I'm a strong communicator" means nothing. "I presented our Q3 strategy to the board, secured $2M in additional funding, and aligned three departments on a shared roadmap" is a proof point.

## Counter-Interviewing

An interview is a two-way evaluation. Prepare thoughtful questions that demonstrate your understanding of the role and company while genuinely assessing whether this is the right fit for you:

- "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
- "What is the biggest challenge the team is currently facing?"
- "How does this role contribute to the company's strategic priorities?"

## The Preparation Formula

1. Research the company (recent news, earnings, strategy, culture)
2. Map the JD to your story bank
3. Practice out loud — speaking an answer is different from thinking it
4. Prepare questions that show you have done your homework
5. Know your numbers: metrics, dates, team sizes, budgets
