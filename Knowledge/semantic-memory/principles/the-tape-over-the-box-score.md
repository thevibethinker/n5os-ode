# Principle: The "Tape" Over the "Box Score"

## Definition
Hiring typically relies on "box scores"—lagging indicators like job titles, company logos, and years of experience. To find elite talent, we must evaluate the "tape"—the concrete artifacts, mental models, and longitudinal behavior of a candidate.

## Key Insights
- **Box Scores are Lagging:** A title at a prestige firm indicates you were hired by that firm, not necessarily that you were the driver of success. [^1]
- **The Tape is Immutable:** Mental models expressed in real-world artifacts (code, strategy memos, video walkthroughs) are significantly harder to fake than a resume. [^1]
- **Longitudinal Consistency:** Cross-referencing self-reflection with peer feedback over several years reveals the "Superunicorn" signal. [^2]

## Semantic Anchor
This principle moves the objective from "Filtering Candidates" to "Scouting Mental Models."
