## Positioning by Stakeholder

### For Community Partners

**Lead with:**
- "Micro-community" framing (emphasizes intimacy/engagement vs. just "community")
- Partnership language ("built around," "ground-up," "case study," "pilot")
- Member value and mission alignment

**Resonant messaging:**
- "Underserved/undiscovered niche" (positions them as strategic, not small)
- "Premium perk" or "member benefit" (monetization angle)
- "Passive revenue" (low operational burden)
- Non-transactional language (avoid "vendor," "client," "contract")

- Transactional framing
- Heavy compliance/legal language

## Product Positioning

### What We Are

**Primary:** "Virtual recruiter powered by AI"
- Managed service feel with tech efficiency
- Not a job board, not an ATS
- Curated, vouched introductions

**Secondary:** "Community-first recruiting platform"
- Distribution through trusted micro-communities
- Trust transfer from community leaders
- Values-aligned matching

---

### What We're Not

- ❌ Traditional job board (passive listings)
- ❌ ATS (we don't replace internal systems)
- ❌ Freelance recruiter marketplace
- ❌ Enterprise-only solution

---

## Competitive Positioning

### vs. Job Boards (LinkedIn, Indeed)

**Their strength:** Scale, brand, network effects  
**Their weakness:** Keyword matching, noise, no guidance, black hole applications  
**Our edge:** AI coaching → richer profiles, warm intros, fit-focus not keyword-focus

**Messaging:** "We're not another place to post your resume. We're AI-powered coaching that helps you get matched based on who you are, not just what you've done."

---

### vs. Traditional Recruiters

**Their strength:** White-glove service, deep networks, proven track record  
**Their weakness:** Expensive (20-30% fees), slow, limited to specific sectors  
**Our edge:** Tech-enabled scale, broader access, placement-fee model competitive but faster

**Messaging:** "Recruiter-quality curation at platform speed and economics."

## Strategic Positioning Pivots

### Considered but NOT Pursuing (Yet)

- **Enterprise-first:** Too slow, too complex for early stage
- **Consumer D2C:** High CAC, need volume we don't have
- **Industry-specific:** Want to stay horizontal initially
- **Freelancer/gig focus:** Different model, different problems

### May Pivot To (Future)

- Platform play (API for any community)
- Vertical specialization (if one segment dominates)
- White-label for large orgs
