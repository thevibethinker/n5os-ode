# The Auto-Applycolypse Will Doom Us All

The Auto-Applycolypse Will Doom Us All

The Auto-Applycolypse Will Doom Us All

Created on 2025-06-24 15:25

Published on 2025-06-24 15:45

Auto-apply this, auto-apply that....why don’t you stop auto-f*cking your chances of getting a job by using terrible AI tools that don’t have your best interests at heart. 

Don’t take it from me – The NYT thinks the same (article in the comments), as do the dozens of Chief HR Officers and Talent leaders I’ve spoken to in the last two months. 

All auto-apply tools offer is the false comfort of "number go up". They exploit your desperation with vanity metrics. "Congrats! You've applied to 847 jobs this week!". 

What these companies aren’t telling you - because they know you won’t check - is that their AI agents are just as likely to put in an app for a project manager at Meta as they are forklift operator in Fargo. 

They rely on a well-known bias call "metric fixation" and boy do they know you'll fixate. 

Never mind that you're getting a 0.2% response rate - because quantity is obviously ≠ quality - and then internalizing that as you sucking when it’s just your approach that sucks. 

Auto-apply companies know this - and they literally don't care. 

You’re playing the wrong “numbers game”

“But V - it’s a numbers game…right?”, you ask uncomfortably as the realization that you were just repeating a meaningless cliche dawns on you. 

Well, yes and no.

Yes - the number of shots on goal generally correlates with your likelihood to score EXCEPT if all you’re doing is yeeting the ball in any given direction 

A 2023 academic study (Campero & Kacperczyk) involving over 140,000 applications found that even minor tailoring (adjusting job titles or keywords) produced measurable improvements in interview rates—outperforming even the effect of internal referrals. Our own internal study found that semantically tailored resumes averaged an ATS score lift of 18% over generic resumes. 

You’re not sticking it to the system, you’re gumming up the works

Also, and I hate to be that guy, but you (yes, you specifically) using auto-apply is making it worse for EVERYONE

Every spammed application gives employers another excuse to raise barriers. To add more hoops. To retreat further behind referral walls. Heck, 70% of jobs never even end up on a public job board—they're filled through the "hidden job market" of connections and word-of-mouth, now more than ever. 

One last thing: Employers, you brought this upon yourselves 

You spent decades filtering candidates through tired signals—prestigious degrees, brand-name employers, keyword bingo—rather than seeking deeper understanding of the humans worth considering. 

You set up a candidate evaluation paradigm that that is superficial at best and outright inequitable at worst because “there’s plenty of fish in the sea” – why get better at picking when you can just be pickier. 

You automated away humanity, then made the surprised pikachu face when candidates fight fire with fire. 

Don’t expect sympathy from candidates - instead, I advise you to get better at selecting for human traits and not the presence of keywords.  

So where do go from here? Here’s my two cents. Candidates - Tell authentic stories. Make real connections. Don’t come off as a bot; show up as a real person. And for the love of all that is holy, stop spamming your resume. Because auto-apply isn't your secret weapon—it's career self-sabotage wrapped in Silicon Valley marketing.

Employers - take a long, hard look at your hiring process. Find ways to separate the wheat from the chaff on a more nuanced, human-centric basis. 

Because avoiding mutually assured exasperation will require both sides to de-escalate.
