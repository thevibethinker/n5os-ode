# There are no end of articles like this one that all belabor the same basic po...

There are no end of articles like this one that all belabor the same basic point, and yet the point still doesn't seem to have gotten through to most people: Using ChatGPT poorly is just as bad for your job prospects - if not worse - than not using it at all. "
""
"People don't seem to get that AI isn't going to become magically creative on your behalf or figure out things about you out of the blue. "
""
"It is by definition constrained by what it knows (i. e. What you tell it). "
""
"What most people do is give it two pieces of info: the same job description as everyone else and their resume. Now granted everyone's resume is different, but as far as stores of information about you go, they aren't all that deep because all of your experiences are summarized into a dozen or so lines line. In the end, the bulk of what the AI has to draw from is still the JD. "
""
"So when you ask it to generate a cover letter or come up with new resume bullets, your stuff will come out sounding the same as everyone else's. "
""
"This problem is made worse when people give ChatGPT basic instructions that don't tell it what information to prioritize and or what structure to use, so it once again resorts to its base understanding of what to do. "
""
"The less context you give - for yourself, for your output, for your task - the more painfully generic the results. "
""
