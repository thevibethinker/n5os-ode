# In the 1998 Tour de France, you had to go 18 riders down the finisher's list ...

In the 1998 Tour de France, you had to go 18 riders down the finisher's list until you found one that *wasn't* doping."
""
"Was it because most cyclists are bad people?"
""
"While I'm tempted to say so as a New Yorker who's almost been run over a few times, the more honest answer is that they were part of a system that incentivized cheating."
""
"Sound familiar? Today's job market is failing the same way."
""
"When companies filter candidates by the hundreds using blunt instruments like ATS systems and proxies like pedigree, what do you expect candidates to do?"
""
"Juice their applications, GPT their cover letters, and inflate their backgrounds."
""
"Not because they're dishonest—rather, it's because they're being rational when faced with a flawed system, even if they're also inadvertently making things worse for everyone."
""
"Because when trust collapses, companies retreat further to "safe" signals like Ivy degrees and FAANG work experience."
""
"These proxies may signal competence, but competence alone does not a great hire make!"
""
"Put it this way: if you're starting a band, it's not always about finding the most technically gifted instrumentalist; rather, it's about finding someone with whom everyone jives musically."
""
"The best hires can do the job, but also so much more: they're the ones who step up when teammates struggle. Who mentor during coffee breaks. Who herald company values."
""
"That stuff doesn't show up on the resume -- but they are actually the overriding factors in job success. "
""
"46% of hires churn by the 18-month mark. 90% of the reason why is a values, mindset, or soft skills mismatch."
""
"So we have two roads ahead of us as employers."
""
"Path 1: Add more hoops. More tests. More paranoid screening. Watch as candidates get better at gaming those too."
""
"Path 2: Seek validated signals that actually matter - the stuff that can't be faked by ChatGPT or BSed in an interview."
""
"At Careerspan, we're betting everything on making Path 2 viable and scalable."
""
"Because when you assess people on the totality of what they bring to the table, square pegs end up in square holes, and everybody wins.
