# The AI job application "arms race" is perpetuating a hiring doom loop that hu...

The AI job application "arms race" is perpetuating a hiring doom loop that hurts EVERYONE."
""
"I just read a fascinating piece (link in the comments) by Michael Horn and Bob Moesta that perfectly captures what I've been seeing in the job market - we're trapped in a vicious cycle where AI tools are making things WORSE, not better."
""
"Here's the toxic pattern they've identified:"
"• Job seekers use AI to blast out hundreds of generic applications"
"• Employers get flooded with thousands of AI-generated resumes"
"• Companies either double-down on their own AI screening or abandon it entirely"
"• Hiring managers retreat to the safety of personal referrals"
"• Qualified candidates without connections get left behind"
""
"It's essentially mutually assured destruction for both sides of the hiring equation."
""
"In my opinion, the current generation of candidate-side AI tools are primarily fueling this problem because they focus on brute-force volume rather than meaningful personalization. "
""
"LazyApply, JobCopilot and their ilk are selling false hope - tricking job seekers into thinking they're making progress when they're actually just contributing to the noise."
""
"But here's where I think Careertech founders need to listen up: We need AI tools with INTEGRITY. Not tools that help you spam 500 employers, but tools that help you create genuinely tailored, authentic applications for roles you're actually qualified for."
""
"Until then, I'm with Horn and Moesta - invest your time in building real relationships instead of feeding the application machine."
""
