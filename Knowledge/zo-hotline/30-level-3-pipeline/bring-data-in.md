# Bring Data In: Creating Information Flows

## The Vision

Instead of manually feeding AI information every time, create pipes that bring data in automatically.

Your AI environment should know what's happening in your world without you telling it.

## Types of Data Flows

**Calendar Integration:** AI knows your schedule and can suggest optimizations
**Email Processing:** Important emails get analyzed and routed automatically  
**Financial Data:** Regular reports on key business metrics
**Meeting Transcripts:** Every conversation becomes searchable intelligence
**Document Updates:** Changes to important files trigger AI review

## The Building Blocks

**APIs:** Direct connections between systems (think "pipes")
**Webhooks:** Triggers that fire when something happens (think "motion sensors")
**Scheduled Syncs:** Regular data pulls (think "daily newspaper delivery")
**File Watchers:** Monitors for changes in specific locations

## Trigger Phrases Callers Use

- "I want AI to know about my business automatically"
- "Can AI monitor things for me?"
- "I'm tired of manually updating AI on what happened"
- "How do I connect my data to AI?"

## Start Simple

Begin with one data source that updates regularly:
- Daily calendar review
- Weekly email summary  
- Monthly business metrics
- Quarterly goal check-ins

## The Translation

You don't need to learn APIs or webhooks. You need to learn that:
- APIs are pipes between systems
- Webhooks are automatic triggers
- Scripts are recipes for processing data

Ask AI: "How do I automatically bring [data type] into my AI environment?"

## Implementation Path

1. Identify your most valuable recurring data
2. Ask AI how to create an automatic flow
3. Build the flow one piece at a time
4. Test and refine until it runs smoothly
