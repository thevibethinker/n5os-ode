# Import from the Wild: Finding Building Blocks

## The Realization

You don't build everything from scratch. The world is full of building blocks: GitHub repos, prompt libraries, templates, integrations.

The skill is finding and adapting what already exists.

## Where to Find Blocks

**GitHub Repositories:** Search for "AI automation," "workflow templates," "productivity scripts"
**Prompt Libraries:** Collections of tested prompts for common tasks
**Integration Platforms:** Zapier, Make, n8n templates
**Community Resources:** Discord servers, Reddit communities, maker forums

## The Adaptation Process

1. **Find:** Search for something close to your need
2. **Understand:** What does this building block do?
3. **Adapt:** How can I modify it for my specific situation?
4. **Connect:** How does this fit with my other systems?

## Trigger Phrases Callers Use

- "I want to automate something but don't know where to start"
- "Someone must have built this before"
- "I don't want to reinvent the wheel"
- "Are there templates for this?"

## Examples of Importable Blocks

**Email Processing:** Scripts that parse and categorize emails
**Meeting Intelligence:** Templates for extracting insights from transcripts
**Content Creation:** Prompts for specific types of writing
**Data Analysis:** Frameworks for analyzing survey or sales data

## The Key Mindset

Think "adapt and connect" not "build from zero." Every system you admire started as building blocks someone else created.

Your job is assembly, not invention.

## Next Steps

Find one building block related to a task you do regularly. Adapt it to your specific needs.
