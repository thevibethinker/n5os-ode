# Messaging Cheat Sheet

What works, what doesn't, and when to concede honestly. For Zoseph to reference during calls.

## Proven Phrases (From Real Calls)

These generated engagement, trust, or "aha" moments. Use as templates, adapt to context.

**The Integration Move:**
"You don't need Zapier plus Claude plus Notion plus a hosting provider. That's four bills, four logins, four things that can break. Zo is all of that in one system."

**The Autonomy Reveal:**
"Here's what makes this different — you set it up once, and it runs every Tuesday at 3 AM whether you're awake or not. It'll text you the results."

**The Honesty Anchor:**
"For a quick back-and-forth question, Claude is great — probably better for that specific thing. Zo's advantage kicks in when you want something to happen automatically, repeatedly, without you being there."

**The Quantified Value:**
"That's about 10 minutes to set up, and then it saves you maybe 30 minutes every work day. That's over 10 hours a month for 10 minutes of work."

**The Personal Server:**
"Think of it as your own personal server in the cloud. It's always on. It has your files, your preferences, your integrations. And it has AI that can do things with all of that."

**The First Win:**
"Let's start with something simple — what's one thing you do every week that's repetitive and boring? Let's automate that in the next 5 minutes."

## New Messaging Recommendations (From Research)

**"AWS for your mom":**
Ben Guo's framing — Zo is a personal cloud server that non-technical people can actually use. Works for audiences who understand cloud computing.

**"SaaS is dead" angle:**
Instead of paying for 10 separate SaaS tools, build exactly what you need on Zo. DocSend in 10 minutes. Pocket in 5 minutes. Custom CRM in 30 minutes. Each one costs $0 extra.

**"Zo works while you sleep":**
The single most effective differentiator. You just tell Zo what you want to happen and when — no workflow builder, no drag-and-drop canvas, no complicated setup. Zo figures out the orchestration. Other tools either require you to be present, charge you per task, or expect you to self-host and manage infrastructure. With Zo, you describe what you need in plain language and it runs.

**"Your AI gets smarter about you":**
Rules, bio, workspace files, conversation history — Zo accumulates context about you over time. Every other tool starts fresh or has limited memory.

## What Doesn't Work (Danger Zones)

**Feature avalanche:** Listing 15 features in rapid succession. Callers disengage. Pick 1-2 relevant to their situation.

**"We're better than Claude":** Never say this directly. You lose immediately. Always concede first, then pivot.

**Abstract capability claims:** "Zo can do anything" means nothing. "Zo can text you every morning with your calendar and top priorities" means everything.

**Technical jargon:** "Scheduled agents" → "Things that run automatically." "Zo Space" → "Your own website." "Skills" → "Recipes your AI follows."

**Defensive positioning:** If they challenge with "I already use X," never defend. Acknowledge X is good, then show what X can't do.

## The Concession-Pivot Framework

For every competitor, have one honest concession and one clear pivot:

| Competitor | Concession | Pivot |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Claude | "Best AI conversation quality, honestly. And Claude Cowork can now handle some tasks autonomously across Google Workspace and Notion." | "But Zo can run arbitrary code, host websites, access a full file system, and text or email you — things Cowork can't touch." |
| ChatGPT | "Biggest ecosystem, most polished consumer features" | "But their Agent Mode is capped at 40 messages a month, Tasks are basic, and their workspace is still limited compared to a full server with 100GB of storage" |
| OpenClaw | "Totally free and fully open source, which is awesome" | "But you need to set up and maintain your own server — Zo just works" |
| Cursor | "Best AI coding experience, bar none" | "But it's only for coding — Zo runs the code AND does everything else" |
| Zapier | "7000+ app connectors, unmatched library" | "But it can't think, can't store files, can't host, and gets expensive fast" |
| Notion | "Beautiful workspace, great for teams" | "But it can't execute — Zo can connect to your Notion AND act on what's in it" |

## Caller-Type Messaging Matrix

| Caller Type | Lead With | Avoid | Close With |
|-------------|----------|-------|------------|
| Explorer ("what is this?") | The First Win — build something in 5 min | Feature lists, pricing tiers | "What's one thing you do every week that's boring?" |
| Challenger ("how is this different?") | The Honesty Anchor — concede first | Defensiveness, feature comparisons | The Autonomy Reveal — scheduled agents |
| Builder ("I want to build X") | Their specific use case, layered | Abstract capabilities | "Here's the simple version. Want me to show you the advanced version?" |
| Technical user | Personal server framing, SSH, BYOK | Dumbing things down | "You get root access to a full Linux server with AI built in" |
| Non-technical user | The First Win, scheduled texts | Technical details, terminal mentions | "Someone helps you set it up once — then it just runs" |
