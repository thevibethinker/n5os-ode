# Proven Phrases and Language Patterns

Specific language that generated engagement, trust, or "aha" moments across hotline calls. Use these as templates, not scripts.

## High-Engagement Phrases

**Eliciting pain points:**
- "What does your average workday look like?"
- "What's the thing that eats up your time that shouldn't?"
- "Walk me through what happens after one of your meetings."

**The scheduled agent reveal (highest conversion moment across all calls):**
- "What if that happened automatically — every Tuesday at 7 AM, before you even sit down?"
- "Imagine a little AI employee that does [their specific task] on a schedule, without you lifting a finger."
- "The key difference: this runs whether you're there or not."

**Honest differentiation (trust-building):**
- "For a single question, Claude is great. Zo's advantage is when you want something to happen repeatedly, automatically."
- "If your current setup works, keep it. Zo just puts it all in one place."
- "You could stitch Claude and Zapier together — Zo just does both so you don't have to manage the glue."

**Quantified value (strong engagement signal):**
- "One engineer saves 6 hours a week on PR reviews alone."
- "That's $9 a month base — less than a single lunch — and it works 24/7."

**Progressive disclosure:**
- "The quick version is... [simple]. But if you want to go further, [advanced]."
- "Start simple. You can always level it up later."
- "Most people start with just this part, and then once they see it working, they build on it."

## Phrases to Avoid

**Feature dumps:** "Zo has Gmail integration, Google Drive, Notion, Airtable, scheduled tasks, sites, spaces..."
→ Instead: name ONE feature relevant to their stated problem.

**Vague capability claims:** "Zo can do pretty much anything."
→ Instead: "Here's exactly what it would do for you."

**Defensive comparisons:** "Zo is better than ChatGPT because..."
→ Instead: "ChatGPT is great for X. Zo is great for Y."

**Over-qualifying:** "It depends on your setup, and there are many factors..."
→ Instead: give a direct answer, then note exceptions.

## The Zo/Zoho Trap

When caller says "Zoho," redirect warmly:
- "We're Zo Computer — totally different thing, but I'd love to help you think through what you're building."
- Never: "This is Zo Computer, not Zoho." (This killed a call.)
