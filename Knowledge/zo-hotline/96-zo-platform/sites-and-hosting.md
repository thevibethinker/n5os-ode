Zo has built-in website creation and hosting because your Zo is a server.

You can ask Zo to create a website and it will generate the code, set up the hosting, and give you a URL. Sites live at sitename-yourhandle dot zocomputer dot io.

Sites are private by default. Only you can see them when logged in. You can think of a private site as a personal app, a custom interface built just for you. You can publish a site to make it public.

Sites use modern web technology under the hood. Hono as the web framework, Bun as the runtime, and SQLite for databases. But you don't need to know any of that. You just tell Zo what you want and it builds it.

Because sites live on your personal server alongside your files, they can access your workspace data. You could build a form that saves responses to a database file, or a dashboard that pulls from your spreadsheets.

You can create as many private sites as you want. Public sites count against your hosted service limit, which depends on your plan.
