Scheduled tasks are the killer feature of Zo. They let you automate anything on a schedule using AI.

A scheduled task is like hiring a virtual employee who shows up at the same time every day and does exactly what you told them. Except it's AI, so it never forgets and it works at machine speed.

You create a scheduled task by telling Zo what to do and when to do it. It can run once in the future, or on a recurring basis. Some examples:

A daily text message reminder for a habit you're building. A morning email pulling together your calendar, emails, and to-do items. Automatic follow-up emails after meetings, using your calendar and notes for context. Checking a website for price drops or product releases and texting you when something changes.

Tasks can use all of Zo's tools and integrations. They can read your files, check your email, browse the web, and send you notifications via text or email.

The key insight is this: scheduled tasks turn Zo from a chat assistant into an autonomous worker. This is where the "works while you sleep" promise becomes real.
