Good prompts make a huge difference in what you get from Zo. Here are the best practices.

Be specific. Give examples of what you want and what you don't want. Define what done looks like.

Provide context. Use the at symbol to mention specific files in your workspace. You can also mention specific tools you want Zo to use. Remember Zo can read URLs and browse the web.

Break down complex tasks. For big projects, ask Zo to make a plan first. You can have it write the plan as a note, then follow the plan step by step.

These same principles apply when writing scheduled tasks, rules, personas, and skills.
