Zo Skills Overview

Zo has a Skills system — reusable packages that give your AI new abilities.
Think of skills like apps you install on a phone, except they teach Zo how to
do specific things.

There are over a hundred skills in Zo's registry, organized into four categories.

Official skills are made by the Zo team. They cover common tasks like creating
sites, organizing files, setting up automations, making slide decks, and
generating PDFs.

Community skills are made by Zo users and shared publicly. These include things
like making Spotify playlists from Pitchfork reviews, creating dating profiles,
setting up flashcard study systems, and optimizing Claude Code usage.

External skills come from third-party developers. The biggest collection covers
marketing and growth — SEO audits, copywriting, pricing strategy, A/B testing,
email sequences. There are also skills for Three.js 3D graphics, Stripe best
practices, Manim video creation, and more.

Connection skills let Zo use your accounts on platforms like Twitter and LinkedIn.

You can browse and install skills right inside Zo. Skills are open source and
anyone can contribute new ones to the registry on GitHub.
