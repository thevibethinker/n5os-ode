# Threshold Rubrics: Engineering When AI Answers

## The Control Mechanism

Don't just engineer what AI says—engineer when it's allowed to say it.

Set explicit conditions that must be met before AI collapses to an answer.

## Examples of Threshold Rubrics

**For Recommendations:**
"Don't recommend anything until you understand my budget, timeline, and success metrics."

**For Analysis:**
"Don't conclude anything until you've considered at least three different perspectives."

**For Problem-Solving:**
"Don't propose solutions until you've identified the root cause."

## Why Thresholds Matter

Without thresholds, AI jumps to answers with insufficient information. This feels productive but produces poor outcomes.

Thresholds force thoroughness before synthesis.

## Trigger Phrases Callers Use

- "AI rushes to conclusions"
- "I need more thorough analysis"
- "AI doesn't dig deep enough"
- "The answers feel superficial"

## Common Threshold Types

1. **Information Thresholds:** "Don't answer until you have X pieces of data"
2. **Question Thresholds:** "Don't conclude until you've asked Y questions"
3. **Perspective Thresholds:** "Don't recommend until you've considered Z viewpoints"
4. **Validation Thresholds:** "Don't finalize until you've checked for contradictions"

## Implementation Template

"Don't [action] until you've [condition]. Only when that's complete, proceed with [deliverable]."

This gives you control over AI's process, not just its output.
