# Adversarial Probing: Stress-Test AI Output

## The Reality Check

AI's baseline rigor is average human rigor. That's not good enough for important decisions. You must explicitly raise the bar.

After AI gives you output, immediately stress-test it.

## Power Phrases for Probing

- "What are the three biggest weaknesses in this approach?"
- "What would a skeptical investor say about this?"
- "What am I not considering?"
- "Where is this most likely to fail?"
- "What evidence contradicts this conclusion?"

## Why This Matters

AI optimizes for coherence and completion, not accuracy. It will confidently present flawed reasoning if it sounds good.

Adversarial probing forces AI to engage its critical thinking rather than just its pattern completion.

## Trigger Phrases Callers Use

- "AI seems overconfident"
- "I need to check if this is actually good"
- "How do I know if AI is right?"
- "The answer sounds too good to be true"

## The Two-Pass Method

**Pass 1:** Get AI's initial response
**Pass 2:** "Now attack your own answer. What's wrong with it?"

The second pass often reveals critical flaws or missing considerations.

## Advanced Probing

- **Role Reversal:** "Argue against your own recommendation"
- **Scenario Planning:** "What if your key assumption is wrong?"
- **Third-Party View:** "What would an expert critic say?"

## Implementation

Never accept the first answer. Always follow up with at least one adversarial question.
