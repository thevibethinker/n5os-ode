# Memory & Preferences: Context That Compounds

## The Memory Strategy

Use AI memory aggressively. Every recurring need, every communication preference, every piece of domain context—store it.

This creates context that compounds over time rather than starting from zero each conversation.

## What to Remember

**Recurring Needs:**
"Remember that I run weekly team meetings on Mondays"
"Remember that I need quarterly investor updates"
"Remember that I travel frequently to NYC and SF"

**Communication Preferences:**
"Remember that I prefer data before opinions"
"Remember that I like concrete next steps"
"Remember that I work best with visual frameworks"

**Domain Knowledge:**
"Remember that our product serves small businesses"
"Remember that compliance is critical in our industry"
"Remember that our biggest competitor is [company]"

## Trigger Phrases Callers Use

- "I keep explaining the same context"
- "AI forgets what we discussed"
- "I wish AI remembered my situation"
- "Can AI learn about my business?"

## How to Build Memory

**Active Storage:** Explicitly tell AI to remember key facts
**Confirmation:** Ask "What do you remember about my situation?"
**Updates:** "Update what you know about my role—I got promoted"
**Corrections:** "Actually, remember that we decided against that approach"

## The Signal-to-Noise Balance

More context ≠ better. You can drown AI with irrelevant information. The art is storing what matters.

Ask yourself: "Will this be relevant in future conversations?"

## Implementation

Start each week by having AI summarize what it remembers about you. Fill gaps and correct misconceptions.
