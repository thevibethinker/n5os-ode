## H-GTM-008: Hiring Managers Prefer "Goods Upfront" Over Relationship Building

**Stakeholders:** `[employers - hiring_managers]` `[recruiters]`

**Statement:**
Hiring managers prefer recruiters who show candidate profiles immediately ("goods upfront") over those who try to establish a relationship or schedule discovery calls first. Trust is built through candidate quality, not relationship building.

**Why it matters:**
- If true: Restructure outreach to lead with candidate bundles, not intro calls
- If false: Traditional recruiter relationship-building approach is correct
- Affects: Sales motion, email templates, conversion funnel design

**How to test:**
- A/B test: "Goods upfront" email (candidate URL) vs. "Let's talk" email (meeting request)
- Measure: Response rate, engagement rate, conversion to paid intro
- Track: Qualitative feedback from hiring managers
- Timeline: 20+ outreach attempts per approach

**Status:** `VALIDATED` (High confidence from hiring manager direct feedback)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- **[Sep 24, 2025]** Alex Caveny (former hiring manager): Direct quote "I don't want to start a conversation with every single recruiter that reaches out. I want to see your candidates." Behavioral validation: ignored all recruiters who tried relationship-building first, only engaged with those showing candidates. "I have no idea who you are and once I start talking you'll never leave me alone." Market gap: "I've never experienced that. Literally no one has ever been like, here's cool candidates I have, do you want to pay for them?" (Meeting: alex-coaching-2025-09-24) [APPROVED]

*Contradicting:*
- None yet

**Implications:**
- Change outreach approach: Send candidate URL in first contact
- Email structure: Minimal copy + link to bundle
- Differentiation: This alone stands out in crowded recruiter market
- Conversion funnel: Bundle engagement drives follow-up, not upfront commitment

**Last updated:** 2025-10-09

## H-GTM-009: HR Resists Disruption Due to Job Security, Target Hiring Managers Instead

**Stakeholders:** `[employers - HR]` `[employers - hiring_managers]`

**Statement:**
HR/talent departments resist new recruiting approaches because their job security depends on maintaining the existing ATS/resume review process. Hiring managers (engineering managers, team leads) are more receptive because they're burned out from resume review and want better solutions.

**Why it matters:**
- If true: Target hiring managers directly, not HR departments
- If false: Continue targeting HR/talent acquisition leads
- Affects: ICP definition, outreach targeting, positioning/messaging

**How to test:**
- Compare: Response rates HR vs. hiring managers
- Track: Objections raised by each persona
- Measure: Deal velocity HR vs. hiring manager initiated
- Timeline: 30+ outreach attempts (15 HR, 15 hiring managers)

**Status:** `VALIDATED` (High confidence from hiring manager insight)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- **[Sep 24, 2025]** Alex Caveny insight: "HR likes their normal bullshit resume review process. That's their job security." Hiring managers are burned out and want solutions, have authority to skip people from ATS directly. "The main purpose of HR/hiring department is to take the fire hose and give filtered candidates. You're doing that [so go around them]." Incentive misalignment: HR sees disruption as competition to their role. (Meeting: alex-coaching-2025-09-24) [APPROVED]

*Contradicting:*
- None yet

**Implications:**
- **Target change**: Shift ICP from HR/talent teams to hiring managers (engineering managers, team leads)
- **Channel strategy**: Find job postings with hiring manager contact info listed
- **Positioning**: Frame as "hedge on sourcing" not replacement for talent team
- **Messaging**: Emphasize making hiring manager's life easier, not disrupting HR
