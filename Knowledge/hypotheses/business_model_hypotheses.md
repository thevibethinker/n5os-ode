## H-BIZ-001: Placement Fee Model Works for Community Partnerships

**Stakeholders:** `[community_partners]` `[employers]`

**Statement:**
Revenue share on placement fees (vs. subscription or per-posting fees) aligns incentives between Careerspan and community partners, making partnerships easier to close and sustain.

**Why it matters:**
- If true: Use placement fee model for all community partnerships
- If false: Need alternative revenue model (subscription, per-license, etc.)
- Economics: Success-based pricing vs. upfront commitment

**How to test:**
- Track: Community partner acceptance of placement fee model
- Measure: Revenue per partnership vs. projected subscription revenue
- Compare: Partner satisfaction with aligned incentives
- Timeline: 5+ community partnerships with revenue data

**Status:** `TESTING` (Remotely Good accepted model, awaiting revenue data)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- **[Sep 29, 2025]** Remotely Good: Partnership structured as revenue share on placements. Theresa accepted without pushback. Model positions career services as "passive income" for community, not operational burden. (Meeting: 46a9c7)

*Contradicting:*
- None yet (may emerge if communities want predictable subscription revenue instead)

**Last updated:** 2025-10-09

## H-BIZ-002: 20% Revenue Share is Sustainable

**Stakeholders:** `[community_partners]`

**Statement:**
20% revenue share to community partners (from placement fees) is attractive enough to close partnerships while maintaining healthy margins for Careerspan.

**Why it matters:**
- If true: Standard pricing for community channel
- If false: Need to adjust split (higher for partners? lower for us?)
- Economics: Determines community channel profitability

**How to test:**
- Track: Partner acceptance at 20% (negotiate up or down?)
- Measure: Careerspan margin after costs at 20% share
- Benchmark: What do competitors/alternatives offer?
- Timeline: 5+ partnerships to validate pricing

**Status:** `PROPOSED` (20% mentioned as target, not yet deployed)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- Industry standard for community partnerships: 15-25% (research TBD)

*Contradicting:*
- None yet

**Last updated:** 2025-10-09

## H-BIZ-003: White-Label Code Model Scales

**Stakeholders:** `[community_partners]`

**Statement:**
Custom access codes (e.g., REMOTELYGOOD) with license limits can scale to 50+ community partnerships without breaking operationally.

**Why it matters:**
- If true: Current tech infrastructure is sufficient for near-term
- If false: Need to build API or self-service platform earlier
- Investment: When to allocate eng resources to automation

**How to test:**
- Track: Manual effort per partnership (code creation, management, support)
- Measure: Operational cost per partner as volume grows
- Identify: Breaking point (at what # of partners does manual fail?)
- Timeline: 10-20 partnerships to see strain

**Status:** `TESTING` (First code deployed, no strain yet)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- None yet (too early)

*Contradicting:*
- None yet

## H-BIZ-004: B2B2C Model Reduces CAC

**Stakeholders:** `[community_partners]` `[job_seekers]`

**Statement:**
Acquiring candidates through community partnerships (B2B2C) results in lower CAC than direct-to-consumer marketing because of trust transfer and targeted reach.

**Why it matters:**
- If true: Prioritize community channel over D2C marketing
- If false: Community is nice distribution but not CAC advantage
- Economics: Channel ROI comparison

**How to test:**
- Measure: CAC for candidates acquired via communities
- Compare: CAC for direct signups (ads, content, SEO)
- Track: LTV by acquisition channel
- Timeline: 3+ community partnerships with volume data

**Status:** `PROPOSED` (Need data from both channels)

**Evidence:**

*Supporting:*
- None yet (awaiting activation and direct channel data)

*Contradicting:*
- None yet
