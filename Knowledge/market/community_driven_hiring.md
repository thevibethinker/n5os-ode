# Community-Driven Hiring

**Last Updated:** October 9, 2025  
**Status:** Template - ready for data population

**Thesis:** Micro-communities represent an underdeveloped distribution channel for recruiting, offering higher-quality, pre-vetted talent pools with built-in trust mechanisms.

---

## Market Overview

### What is Community-Driven Hiring?

**Definition:** Recruiting that leverages trusted communities (professional groups, alumni networks, affinity groups, online communities) as distribution channels and trust mechanisms.

**Models:**
- Job boards embedded in communities
- Community leader as curator/voucher
- Membership benefits (career services as premium perk)
- Referral networks formalized
- Cohort-based hiring (bootcamp → employer pipelines)

## Evidence for the Thesis

### What We Know

### Case Study #1: Remotely Good (September 2025)

**Evidence supporting micro-community distribution thesis:**

**Community characteristics:**
- Size: Sub-10K members (micro-community definition)
- Focus: Social impact careers, SF-based
- Engagement: 100+ curated events on calendar
- Leadership: Founder-led (Theresa Anoje)
- Monetization: Free + paid premium tiers with Perks section

**Partnership dynamics:**
- **Speed:** First contact to meeting in 12 days (warm intro via Jessica Greenwald)
- **Enthusiasm:** "Very excited to push ahead" (email Sep 23)
- **Proactive action:** Created Perks section infrastructure before partnership finalized
- **Values-first:** Focused on mission alignment, deferred commercial terms discussion
- **White space:** No existing career solution integrated

**Initial validation:** Community leader immediately recognized value, acted decisively, no friction

**Status:** Single validation point. Need 2-3 more micro-community partnerships to confirm pattern.

**Source:** Meeting 46a9c7 + email thread Sep 17-29

**Next test:** Observe if pattern holds with different community types (non-impact-focused, different geography, different size within micro range)
