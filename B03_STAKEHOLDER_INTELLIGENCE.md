# B31: Stakeholder Research

**Perspective:** Ray, the founder who walked a Shift Up earnout to near-zero and is now coaching this sale with only a couple of quarters of runway left.

## Insight 1: Treat the acquisition hunt as a sales funnel, not a headline signal

**Evidence:** "I had, you know, organic interest from a few and then I had a mid target list of like 30 or 40 that basically whittled down... It's like a sales process and it basically came like five or six that seem to have some level of real interest. And then of that five or six, it was like two to three that sort of got to the point where they were, like, seriously interested. And then only one of those actually ended up making any sort of move."

**Why it matters:** The mere existence of a connection or pilot means nothing until it survives the rigorous funnel Ray described—V needs to keep the outreach volume high and treat every call as a qualification step.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)
- Rating rationale: Ray has lived through a comparable process and is pointing to concrete numbers, but we are still relying on a single anecdote from a past exit.

## Insight 2: You need at least two legitimate bids to keep any leverage once runway is tight

**Evidence:** "If you're backs against the rope and you only have one person that wants to do a real thing, they're not going to pay you anything, basically because you don't have any better options generally, right?"

**Why it matters:** One bidder is a pricing trap—without a second offer, acquirers know you have no alternatives and will push the price toward yield rather than value.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)
- Rating rationale: Insight comes from Ray’s painful numeric experience and applies directly to V’s three-month runway, so it is specific and actionable.

**Category:** Founder Pain Points

**Source Credibility:**
- **Stakeholder:** Ray → See B08 Domain Authority
- **Relevant experience:** Veteran of a symbolic sale where a single offer produced pennies, so he now resists being captive to a lone bidder.
- **Source type:** PRIMARY
- **Weight justification:** Direct recall from his last exit gives the lesson vividness; he is speaking from the downside of limited leverage.

## Insight 3: Pace outreach like a job offer cycle—slow-play some conversations while accelerating others so offers land together

**Evidence:** "You got to be smart with your timing on outreach, such that you can kind of... slow play some conversations and sort of try to speed up other conversations such that they end up getting to roughly the same point in time in any negotiations around the same time... so that you can potentially play them off against each other."

**Why it matters:** Synchronizing negotiation stages keeps multiple buyers credible at once, which is the only way to generate the bilateral pressure needed for a decent price.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)
- Rating rationale: Tactical guidance that mirrors standard deal flow management but comes with Ray’s own war stories, so it feels grounded yet still anticipatory.

**Category:** Fundraising & Business Model

- **Source type:** PRIMARY
- **Weight justification:** The counsel stems from his direct negotiation experience and is being applied to the exact pipeline V is currently managing.

## Insight 4: Earnouts usually net founders pennies unless the acquirer executes exactly as promised

**Evidence:** "If everything went right, I could have been... I walked away in my earnout with, like, basically nothing. So I didn't really make that much... And so I walked away in my earnout with, like, basically nothing... It reduces the risk on their side."

**Why it matters:** Ray’s experience shows that earnouts are acquirer protection, not a guarantee of upside—V needs to cap the earnout timeline, quantify milestones, or push for a cleaner asset purchase so he isn’t paid only if the buyer behaves perfectly.

**Signal Strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○ (3/5)
- Rating rationale: This is a vivid example of a common earnout trap; the emotional weight of the story boosts credibility even though it is one founder’s outcome.

**Category:** Fundraising & Business Model

**Source Credibility:**

- **Source type:** PRIMARY
