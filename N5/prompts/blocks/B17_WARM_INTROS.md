# B17: Warm Introduction Opportunities

## Objective

Identify opportunities for warm introductions, networking connections, and relationship-building that emerged during the meeting.

## What to Capture

**Introduction Offers:**
- Who offered to make an introduction
- To whom
- For what purpose
- Timeline or conditions

**Connection Opportunities:**
- People mentioned who could be valuable to connect with
- Shared connections identified
- Network overlaps discovered

**Referral Possibilities:**
- Who might benefit from knowing each other
- Business development opportunities
- Partnership potentials
- Talent connections

**Context & Value:**
- Why the introduction matters
- What could come from it
- How to position the introduction
- What to emphasize

## Output Format

### Introduction: [Person A] → [Person B]

**Connector:** [Who will make the intro]

**Purpose:**
[Why this introduction matters, what the goal is]

**Context:**
[Relevant background, how they know each other, why good fit]

**Value Proposition:**
- For [Person A]: [What they gain]
- For [Person B]: [What they gain]

**Next Steps:**
- [ ] [Action needed to facilitate]
- [ ] [Timing or conditions]

**Positioning Notes:**
[How to frame the introduction, key points to emphasize]

- Who mentioned: [Name]

## Generation Guidelines

1. Listen for introduction language:
   - "I can connect you with..."
   - "You should meet..."
   - "Let me introduce you to..."
   - "I know someone who..."
   - "You'd love talking to..."

2. Capture the value:
   - Why does this introduction matter?
   - What's in it for each party?
   - What could come from it?

3. Note the context:
   - How does connector know both parties?
   - What's the relationship basis?
   - Any relevant shared interests?

4. Document next steps:
   - Who needs to do what?
   - Any prerequisites?
   - Timing considerations?

5. Include positioning:
   - How to frame the intro?
   - What to emphasize?
   - Any sensitivities to consider?

## When to Generate

Generate B17 when meeting includes:
- Explicit offers to make introductions
- Discussion of who knows whom
- Network mapping conversations
- Business development opportunities
- Partnership exploration
- Hiring/talent discussions

Skip for internal-only meetings with no external connections discussed.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check:
- [ ] All introduction offers captured
- [ ] Clear who/what/why for each
- [ ] Value proposition articulated
- [ ] Next steps actionable
- [ ] Positioning guidance included
- [ ] Timeline or conditions noted
- [ ] Names and companies correct
- [ ] Sensitive to relationship dynamics
