# B25: Deliverables & Follow-Up Communications

## Objective

Map out tangible deliverables discussed and draft framework for follow-up communications (emails, proposals, documents).

## What to Capture

**Deliverables Mentioned:**
- Documents to create/send
- Presentations or decks needed
- Proposals or agreements to draft
- Data or analysis to provide
- Designs or mockups to share
- Reports or summaries expected

**Follow-Up Communications:**
- Thank you emails needed
- Recap emails to send
- Proposals to write
- Introduction emails to make
- Calendar invites to send
- Documents to share

## Output Format

### Deliverables Map

**[Deliverable Name]**
- **Type:** [Document, deck, proposal, analysis, etc.]
- **Owner:** [Who creates this]
- **Audience:** [Who receives/reviews]
- **Deadline:** [When needed]
- **Key Contents:** [What must be included]
- **Dependencies:** [What's needed to create this]

### Follow-Up Email Framework

**To:** [Recipients]  
**Subject:** [Suggested subject line]  
**Purpose:** [What this email accomplishes]  

**Key Points to Include:**
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

**Attachments/Links:**
- [Documents to include]

**Call to Action:**
- [What you need from recipient]

## Generation Guidelines

1. Listen for deliverable language:
   - "I'll send you..."
   - "Can you put together..."
   - "We need to create..."
   - "I'll follow up with..."
   - "Let me share..."

2. Map dependencies:
   - What's needed to create this?
   - Who else is involved?
   - What's the sequence?

3. Draft frameworks, not full content:
   - Provide structure for follow-up
   - List key points to cover
   - Note tone and approach
   - Don't write full emails (that's separate task)

4. Organize by priority:
   - What's urgent?
   - What blocks other work?
   - What's quick wins?

5. Note audience considerations:
   - Who's the primary recipient?
   - What do they need to know?
   - What tone is appropriate?

## Edge Cases

**Multiple Follow-Ups:**
If multiple communications needed, provide framework for each

**Internal vs External:**
Distinguish between internal deliverables and external-facing materials

**Series of Deliverables:**
If deliverables build on each other, note the sequence and dependencies

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check:
- [ ] All deliverables from meeting captured
- [ ] Owners and deadlines noted
- [ ] Follow-up communication frameworks provided
- [ ] Key contents/talking points identified
- [ ] Audience and tone considerations noted
- [ ] Dependencies and blockers surfaced
- [ ] Organized logically (by priority or timeline)
