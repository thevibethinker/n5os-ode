# BUILD PLAN: Careerspan Acquisition Collateral

## Objective
Generate three internal positioning documents that build the strongest possible argument for each acquisition angle. These are NOT polished external collateral—they're internal strategic scaffolding that workers will develop into thinking frameworks.

## Output
Three internal one-pagers, each constructing the strongest argument for one positioning:
1. **Internal Mobility Tool** — Career development, promotions, internal talent marketplace
2. **Sourcing Tool** — Talent pipeline building for recruiters, Handshake-type platforms, agencies
3. **Matching/Assessment System** — ATS replacement, evaluation layer, vetting enhancement

## Core Context (All Workers Must Have)

### The Team
- **V (Vrijen Attawar)** — 10+ years career coaching, second-time founder, domain expertise in career development
- **Logan Currie** — Harvard Ed School graduate, student leader of Project on Workforce at Harvard, formidable founder, domain expertise in workforce/education
- **Ilse** — CTO, technical execution velocity, builder

### The Metrics (Current)
- 4,000 signups
- 1,500 fully activated users
- Average activated user: 2 stories (~40 min of deep self-reflection)
- 35% completed 3+ stories
- ~$1M pre-seed raised Q1 2024

### What's Been Cracked
1. **UX** — Users actually do deep self-reflection (40 min avg)
2. **Matching Tech** — Multi-agent GPT-4 architecture, AISS framework, story-based profiles
3. **Community Embedding** — Proven partnerships: Peace Corps, Emory University, Columbia, humanUPtions

### Technical Architecture
- Multi-agent system (GPT-4): input processing, evaluation, direction, extraction, QC
- AISS Framework: Action-Impact-Scale-Skill for structured output
- Story-based profiling: qualitative, narrative data (not just keywords)
- Semantic matching: context-aware, beyond keyword matching

### HR Pipeline Positioning
**We target:** Vetting, Matching, Sourcing
**We do NOT target:** Validating/Verifying, Onboarding, Negotiation

### Key Differentiators
- Pro-candidate (vs. employer-first)
- Signal capture through coaching interactions (soft skills, follow-through, self-awareness)
- Longitudinal behavioral data (users return, profiles grow)
- Story-based profiling captures what assessments miss
