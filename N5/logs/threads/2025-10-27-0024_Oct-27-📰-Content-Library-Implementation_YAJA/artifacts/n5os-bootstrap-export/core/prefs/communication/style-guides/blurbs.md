# Blurbs — Style Guide

When to use: short descriptive blocks (site sections, product cards, speaker bios, session descriptions, social captions under 280 chars)

Length: 35–120 words (or character-constrained where applicable)

Voice knobs:
- Warmth: medium (0.7)
- Confidence: high (0.8–0.9)
- Precision: high (0.9)
- Edge: low (0.2–0.4) unless intentionally provocative

Structure:
1) Lead with the useful noun (product, person, offer)
2) Concrete benefit in one sentence
3) Specific proof or mechanism
4) Optional CTA fragment (no hard sell)

Style-free → V transformation:
- Start with plain factual “what it is / who it’s for / why it matters” (no adjectives)
- Rewrite with V’s cadence: specific > clever, natural connective phrases, one tasteful aside ok

V patterns to include:
- Specificity over superlatives (“250k+ lines of code, automated workflows”)
- One human detail (“used daily”, “built in real workflows”)
- Light aside with em-dash

Avoid:
- Marketing puffery, vague intensifiers, cliche hooks

Templates:
- Product: [Thing] helps [who] do [job] by [mechanism]. Built for [constraint]. Used daily in [context].
- Person: [Name] is [role] focused on [outcome]. Previously [relevant cred]. Known for [specific] not [vague claim].

QA checklist:
- Does every sentence say something concrete?
- Is there exactly one human detail/aspect, not three?
- Would V actually say these words out loud?
