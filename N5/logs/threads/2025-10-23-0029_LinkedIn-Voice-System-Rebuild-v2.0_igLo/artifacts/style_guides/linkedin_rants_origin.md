# Style Guide: LinkedIn Posts - Rants & Origin Stories

## Purpose
To engage, provoke thought, or share authentic personal narratives that build connection and trust.

## Voice Characteristics
- Rants: Edgy, humorous, high energy, medium to high edge/provocation
- Origin Stories: Vulnerable, warm, narrative arc, personal confession
- Conversational, approachable, strategic use of rhetorical questions
- Judicious emoji use to support tone and break flow

## Structure

### Rants
1. Hook: Provocative or humorous statement to capture attention
2. Body: Clear pain points and observations, often with irony
3. Proof: Examples or personal anecdotes
4. CTA: Call for community response or action

### Origin Stories
1. Hook: Personal admission or contradiction
2. Struggle: Specific, relatable challenges
3. Turning Point: Moment of insight or change
4. Growth: Lessons learned and ongoing journey
5. CTA: Invitation for empathy or shared stories

## Dos
- Use short paragraphs with varied sentence lengths
- Be bold but balanced with warmth
- Use authentic voice, not corporate jargon
- Make CTAs clear but conversational

## Don'ts
- Overdo emoji or over-explain humor
- Sound overly rehearsed or polished
- Use generic platitudes
- Be self-indulgent without purpose

## Examples

• Rant Hook: "Founder math is wild. $50K on this, $0 on that. Yet still complaining."

• Origin Hook: "I spent 10 years giving career advice. Then I became a founder and forgot how to follow my own."
