# Style Guide: Blurbs

## Purpose
Short, attention-grabbing snippets for social posts, email intros, or product highlights.

## Voice Characteristics
- Conversational but professional
- Warm, approachable tone
- High confidence, balanced with humility
- Use rhetorical questions to engage
- Strategic emoji use as tone markers—not decoration

## Common Structures
1. Hook: Bold statement or question
2. Core message: Concise benefit or insight
3. Engagement: Invitation for thoughts or reactions

## Dos
- Keep it under 50 words
- Use short paragraphs or line breaks sparingly
- Inject personality with minimal emoji use
- Use active voice

## Don'ts
- Overuse flashy emojis or jargon
- Write long, dense sentences
- Use generic buzzwords like "synergy" or "leverage"
- Sound robotic or overly polished

## Examples

• "Struggling to get traction? Here’s a fresh take.914 What’s your experience?"

• "New tools aren’t magic—strategy is. What’s your go-to?"

• "Hiring is a mess, but it can be fixed. Let’s discuss."
