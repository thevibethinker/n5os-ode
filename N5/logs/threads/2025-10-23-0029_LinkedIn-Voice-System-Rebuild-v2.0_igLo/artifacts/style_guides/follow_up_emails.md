# Style Guide: Follow-Up Emails

## Purpose
Maintain professionalism while being warm and approachable. Drive action and clear next steps.

## Voice Characteristics
- Warm and conversational when appropriate
- Clear, concise language
- Polite reminders with urgency balanced
- Avoid jargon or overly formal tone
- Close with personalized, thoughtful signoffs (e.g., "Warmly")

## Common Structures
1. Greeting: Friendly yet professional
2. Context reminder: Briefly restate topic
3. Call to action: Clear next step or request
4. Closing: Polite, warm signoff

## Dos
- Use complete sentences
- Add personal touches for relationships
- Address potential concerns proactively
- Be concise but thorough

## Don'ts
- Sound robotic or generic
- Use unclear or vague requests
- Overuse exclamation points or emojis
- Overload with details, keep focused

## Example

Hi [Name],

Just following up on our conversation about [topic]. Let me know if you need any more info or if we're good to proceed.

Looking forward to your thoughts!

Warmly,
[Your Name]
