# LinkedIn Exemplars (v2.1 Transformation-Based)

Note: Each draft assembles pattern primitives and is grounded in transformation pairs; keep human edit pass.

---

## 1) Origin Story (Vulnerable)

Hook (Contrast): I spent a decade helping people find their footing. Becoming a founder knocked mine out from under me.

Show-Then-Name: For months, my day looked like 40 tabs open in my mind. Context switching. Missed follow-ups. The sinking feeling that I was always late to the thing that mattered.

Lived Specifics:
- I’d start an email, get pulled into a DM, “quickly” open a doc, and lose the thread.
- On Tuesday, I apologized to the same person twice in one afternoon.

Quiet Resolution: N5O didn’t give me more hours—it gave me state back. Same brain, fewer dropped stitches. I can see the work, trust my process, and the quality followed.

One-Ask CTA: If you’ve been here, how did you rebuild your confidence? I’ll share what worked for me.

## 2) Product Explainer (Show-Then-Name)

Hook (Contrast): Your first five hires set culture, but most founders spend more on tools they won’t use than on hiring well.

Example first: We see teams burning $8k/mo across tools, then try to "DIY" recruiting for roles 1–5.

Mini-Framework:
- Define the bar: what “great in this seat” actually means
- Evidence-first signals: work samples > vibes
- Close well: clear runway, honest scope, feedback loop

Quiet Resolution: Careerspan is built for that reality—evidence over theater.

One-Ask CTA: What’s the one signal you trust most for early hires?

## 3) Rant (Edge, with receipts)

Hook (Contrast): Founder math: $25k for a booth, $0 for the first hires who will run the company with you.

Receipt: Last month I met a team running 12 tools, zero structured hiring. Three great candidates ghosted. The team blamed “the market.”

Lived Specific: One week of structured work fixed the calendar and the close rate.

Quiet Resolution: It’s not more effort. It’s fewer decisions.

One-Ask CTA: If you’ve made great early hires, what did you cut to make room for it?

## 5) Thought Lens (Mini-framework)

Hook (Contrast): Most founders don’t lose time—they lose state.

Lens: Time disappears when state is fragmented; you pay the tax in quality.

Mini-Framework:
- Reduce surfaces: fewer lists, clearer queues
- Preserve intent: decisions travel with the work
- Verify state: reality checks you can’t skip

One-Ask CTA: What’s one surface you could remove this week?

## 6) Hiring Teardown (Practical)

Hook (Specific): Here’s how I’d fix an early-stage hiring funnel in 7 days.

Day 1–2: Define the bar. Write 5 must-have behaviors tied to the actual work.
Day 3: Replace the 45-min screen with a 15-min work sample prompt.
Day 4–5: Batch interviews; one shared scorecard; no free-form.
Day 6: One written exercise; one reference focused on “How do they learn under pressure?”
Day 7: Close—clear runway, honest scope, what “great” looks like in 30/60/90.

CTA: Want the templates? Happy to share the docs I use.

## 10) Mistake → Correction (Honest)

Hook (Contrast): I was optimizing for speed and ended up optimizing for rework.

Specific: I skipped writing the bar for a role. Two rounds later we were arguing preferences, not evidence.

Correction: We wrote the bar, redid the screen, and the path got obvious.

CTA: What’s one thing you stopped doing that improved your hiring outcomes?
