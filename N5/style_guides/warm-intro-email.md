# Style Guide: Warm Intro Email

## Purpose
Connect two people with relevant overlap, establishing credibility and value without over-selling

## Structure
1. Warm greeting + context refresh
2. Person being introduced (name + brief background)
3. Credibility signal (why I can vouch)
4. Value proposition (what makes them/their work unique)
5. Connection rationale (why this intro makes sense)
6. Warm close (hand off to them)

## Length
- Target: 120-160 words (3-4 short paragraphs)
- Maximum: 200 words
- Minimum: 100 words

## Tone
- Warm and casual (not formal business)
- Confident but not promotional
- Personal (use "I" statements about my experience)
- Direct (no fluff or excessive context)

## Style
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Active voice
- Concrete details over abstract claims
- Lead with the person, not their company
- One clear value prop, not a feature list

## Required Elements
- [x] Context for why I'm making intro
- [x] Person's relevant background (1-2 key credentials)
- [x] My personal experience/credibility signal
- [x] Clear value proposition (what's unique)
- [x] Connection rationale (why this makes sense)
- [x] Clean handoff close

## Forbidden Elements
- ❌ Generic promotional language
- ❌ Feature lists or bullet points
- ❌ Asking for specific next steps
- ❌ Over-explaining or excessive context
- ❌ Formal business tone
- ❌ Multiple value propositions

## Validation Criteria
- [ ] Length: 100-200 words
- [ ] Structure: All 6 sections present in order
- [ ] Tone: Casual, warm, confident
- [ ] Credibility: Personal experience mentioned
- [ ] Value prop: Single, clear, differentiated
- [ ] Close: Clean handoff without asking

## Notes
- Keep it conversational - this is a personal intro, not a pitch
- Lead with relationship context, not company names
- Value prop should be specific and differentiated, not generic "they're great"
- Don't ask recipient to do anything - just facilitate connection
