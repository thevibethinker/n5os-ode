# D1.4a: Panel Purposes & Success Criteria

## Objective

Define what each panel is trying to accomplish and what success looks like — BEFORE seeing candidate data.

## Context

This is a framework-first exercise. We are defining "what good looks like" without bias from the specific candidate.

**Audience:** CEO/Founder of FlowFuse who knows the role deeply
**Decision:** "Is this candidate worth 30 minutes of my time?"
**Success for Careerspan:** Even if the candidate isn't a fit, the employer trusts the analysis

---

# Panel Definitions

## Panel 1: The Thesis

### Purpose

The 80/20 panel. If the CEO reads nothing else, they should get 80% of the decision-making value. The job is to synthesize the candidate into a clear, compact narrative that answers:

- Who is this person? (type/mold)
- What do you get if you hire them?
- What's the trade-off? (no one is perfect)

### Success Criteria

| Criterion | What It Looks Like |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Single paragraph** | 4-5 sentences max. Respect attention span. |
| **Specificity** | Uses actual evidence from the profile, not generic praise. |
| **Clear trade-off** | Names something the candidate lacks or struggles with. No perfect candidates = credibility. |
| **Buried lead?** | First sentence conveys the core verdict. No "setup" required. |
| **Standalone value** | Reader can make a preliminary go/no-go decision after reading just this panel. |

### Anti-Patterns

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Generic praise** | "Highly motivated," "team player," "fast learner" without evidence. |
| **Laundry list** | Listing accomplishments instead of synthesizing what they add up to. |
| **No trade-off** | Implies the candidate is perfect. Destroys credibility. |
| **Buried lead** | Takes 10+ seconds to understand who this person is. |
| **Resume regurgitation** | Just summarizing their CV. No synthesis or judgment. |

---

## Panel 2: Hard Skills - Bar Cleared

### Purpose

De-risk the meeting. The CEO wants to know: "Am I going to waste 30 minutes on someone who can't do the job?" This panel answers the binary question: **Can they do the technical work?**

### Success Criteria

| Criterion | What It Looks Like |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **JD-driven** | Every requirement from the job description is addressed. |
| **Binary clarity** | Cleared / Not Cleared / Unclear. No ambiguity. |
| **Evidence-backed** | Each "Cleared" has a 1-sentence proof point (where/how demonstrated). |
| **Scannable** | Table format. CEO can scan in 15 seconds. |
| **Honest about gaps** | "Not Cleared" items are named clearly, not hidden. |

### Anti-Patterns

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Vague hedging** | "Some experience with," "familiar with concepts." |
| **Missing JD items** | Not addressing requirements that the CEO cares about. |
| **Evidence mismatch** | Saying "Cleared: React" but proof point mentions jQuery. |
| **Too wordy** | Long paragraphs instead of table rows. |
| **No verdict** | Just listing skills without saying whether the bar is met. |

---

## Panel 3: Differentiators

### Purpose

Move beyond "can they do the job?" to **"why would we hire THEM over others?"** This is about the soft skills, rare combinations, and signal that a resume can't capture but that makes someone remarkable.

### Success Criteria

| Criterion | What It Looks Like |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **2-4 items max** | Quality over quantity. If nothing stands out, say so. |
| **Actually differentiating** | Not "good communicator" (cliché) — but specific, unusual strengths. |
| **Evidence-anchored** | Each differentiator has a "proof point" from their story. |
| **Role-relevant** | Differentiators that matter for THIS job, not just cool facts. |
| **Signal-to-noise** | If nothing differentiating exists, the panel says so explicitly. |

### Anti-Patterns

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Generic soft skills** | "Strong work ethic," "detail-oriented" without specificity. |
| **Table stakes** | Things every decent candidate should have. |
| **Claims without proof** | "Natural leader" but no evidence of leadership. |
| **Unrelated trivia** | "Fluent in Esperanto" — cool, but not relevant unless the role needs it. |
| **Everything is a differentiator** | Dilutes meaning. If 10 things are special, nothing is. |

---

## Panel 4: Why This Meeting Matters

### Purpose

Give the CEO a roadmap for the 30-minute conversation. They're going to invest time — what should they explore? What's at stake? This connects culture fit, company-candidate alignment, and creates a specific agenda.

### Success Criteria

| Criterion | What It Looks Like |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **2-3 exploration areas** | Focused, not a laundry list of interview topics. |
| **Specific questions** | Each area has a question to ask or thing to probe. |
| **Company-connected** | Leverages FlowFuse culture/values research. Not generic. |
| **Actionable** | CEO knows what to listen for during the meeting. |
| **Purpose-driven** | Makes the meeting feel intentional, not just exploratory. |

### Anti-Patterns

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Generic questions** | "Tell me about yourself," "What's your weakness?" |
| **No company context** | Could apply to any job at any company. |
| **Unfocused** | 5+ things to explore in a 30-minute meeting. |
| **Non-actionable** | "Get to know them" — no clear agenda. |
| **No stake named** | Doesn't explain WHY these areas matter for this specific hire. |

# Cross-Panel Principles

## 80/20 Principle

Panel 1 (The Thesis) must stand alone. A CEO reading only Panel 1 should be able to make a preliminary decision with 80% confidence. Panels 2-4 provide depth and de-risking, but the core value judgment lives in Panel 1.

## Trade-Offs as Credibility

Every candidate has trade-offs. We build trust by naming them honestly, not hiding them. A panel that says "this person is perfect at everything" is not helpful — it's not credible.

## Specificity Over Generality

Every claim should have evidence. "Strong engineering background" is noise. "Built and scaled a distributed system serving 10M users" is signal.

## When The Panel Has Nothing Good To Say

If a panel's purpose can't be fulfilled (e.g., no differentiators, or the hard skills aren't cleared), the panel must SAY SO explicitly. Silence or vague language is worse than a clear "not a fit."

Key rubric inputs:

- Panel 2: JD coverage, binary clarity, evidence match
- Panel 3: Differentiation, proof anchoring, role relevance
- Panel 4: Focus, specificity, company connection
