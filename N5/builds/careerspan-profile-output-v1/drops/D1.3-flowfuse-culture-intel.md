## Core Values (From Handbook)

### 📈 Results

1. **Results over Hours**: "We value results over the hours you spend working. We appreciate the effort, but value the results. Make users happy, care about the team member you helped. You schedule your day to make the most out of it for you. Don't brag about hours worked, take pride in your achievements."

2. **Do it now**: "Growth is exponential; Ship today, observe first results tomorrow, observe compounding effects in just 2 days."

3. **Ownership**: "Take initiative, keep stakeholders in the loop, find and resolve bottlenecks. Collaborate with others. There's one individual responsible for your achievements; You! Something is never 'not my job'. If you see an opportunity to drive something forward, do it."

4. **Disagree and commit**: "Discussion is healthy, but decisions do not need full consensus. Disagree with a point-of-view, make your case, but accept other solutions are valid too. Once a choice is made, commit as a team to follow it. Allow others to prove themselves. Reaching consensus for every decision does not scale."

5. **Keep it short and simple**: "Don't over-engineer your solution or let the scope creep beyond what makes sense for an iteration. Fast progress requires fast understanding. Boring solutions are good. Allow everyone to understand your solution. There will always be another iteration."

### 🔁 Iterative Improvement

1. **Small Steps**: "Every mountain can be climbed with small steps. Make a change, reassess your direction, repeat."

2. **Bias for Action**: "Don't overthink, do what is the most natural in this moment."

3. **Accepting Change**: "Be eager to accept change: Is it better than yesterday, and can we improve on it tomorrow?"

4. **Everything is a Draft**: "Today won't be perfect, tomorrow will be better. Perfection is the death of progress."

5. **Good enough vs. Excellence**: "Decide where good is good enough; understand where excellence is our company's edge. Apply the Pareto principle."

### 👥 Collaborative Community

1. **Be inclusive**: "in a community everyone is different; allow them to be. We value empathy, courtesy, and respect for each other."

2. **Information wants to be free**: "Store information in public places by default: our Handbook and GitHub issues. Allow others to understand you and your decisions."

3. **Knowledge is Power**: "Share your knowledge with others. This can be through writing documentation, handbook entries, creating tutorials, keeping a meeting agenda, or running workshops with others."

4. **Think big picture**: "It's not 'your' issue/bug, the whole community can have opinions and provide suggestions."

### ⛑️ Constructive Candor

1. **Feedback aids growth**: "it reinforces what is good and helps drive improvement where needed."

2. **Be considerate**: "feedback is about helping others to improve, not scoring points."

3. **Provide timely feedback**: "it is better to address things when they are fresh in our minds and quicker to remedy."

4. **Assume positive intent**: "When receiving feedback. When you feel defensive it's OK to respond later."

5. **Be optimistic**: "treat situations as if a positive outcome is always possible."

6. **Own mistakes openly**: "Be open and honest about your own mistakes, take ownership of them and their resolve."

7. **Appreciate each other**: "Say thanks often, preferably in public."

### 🤝 Customer Empathy

1. **Focus on the customer**: "We want them to be successful."

2. **Be curious, not judgmental**: "Understanding starts with asking questions."

3. **Listen and respond quickly**: "Listen to feedback and respond quickly and respectfully."

4. **Show empathy in all interactions**: "Our solutions aim to solve customer problems effectively and efficiently."

## Communication Norms

### Async-First Approach

- **Asynchronous standups via Geekbot**: Standup meetings run asynchronously with prompts sent at 9:00 local time each day
- **Standups in departmental channels**: Updates live in Sales, Marketing, BizOps, and Engineering channels to maintain signal-to-noise as they grow
- **Recommended standup format**:
  - Bad: "More work on special feature"
  - Better: "Align on plan for building special feature with Manny and work on it"
  - Best: Connect work to broader goals, focus on clear specific actions

### Slack Channel Structure

| Type | Prefix | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Department | `#dept-` | Department conversations |
| Project | `#proj-` | Work with clear endpoint |
| GitHub | `#gh-` | Automated repo notifications |
| Ops | `#ops-` | Operations alerts |
| Customer | `#cust-` | Customer discussions |

### Meetings

- **No Agenda, No Attenda**: If no agenda items 5 minutes before meeting, don't have it
- **Google Calendar Delegation**: Enable "Modify event" permissions for all team events by default (especially helpful for async remote)
- **Coffee calls**: Optional social calls matched every two weeks via `#virtual-coffee` channel
- **Monday Team Catchup**: First 10 minutes mandatory (Department Spotlight), remainder optional for social updates

### GitHub Expectations

- Respond to mentions and review requests within **two business days**
- All code tracked in GitHub with issues for feature ideas and requests
- Handbook and GitHub as default public storage for information

## Decision-Making Style

### DRI (Directly Responsible Individual)

- **Single owner**: One individual ultimately responsible for success/failure of each initiative
- **Final say authority**: DRI makes final call after gathering stakeholder input
- **No decision paralysis**: "Reaching consensus for every decision does not scale"
- **CEO/CTO override**: Reserved power, "should very seldomly happen"
- **Disagree and Commit**: Once decision made, team commits to following it

### RACI Framework

Used for cross-functional projects with multiple stakeholders:
- **Responsible**: One person executing (never more than one)
- **Accountable**: Ensures project delivers on KPI (may be multiple, may align with Responsible)
- **Consulted**: Experts whose input needed
- **Informed**: One-way communication to those who need to know outcome

## Work-Life Balance Signals

### Remote Work Practices

- **Fully remote**: Team spans multiple countries and timezones
- **Coworking allowance**: "Recommended to work at least one day a week outside of home" — coworking space expense allowed
- **Separation encouraged**: "It's advised to separate work and private life as much as possible"
- **Notification boundaries**: "Turn off notification outside of your intended working hours for email, Slack, and GitHub where possible"
- **Device separation option**: While work devices can be used for private purposes, separate personal devices recommended for boundary maintenance

### 1:1 Cadence

- **Individual contributors**: At least once monthly
- **Biweekly/weekly**: May be appropriate depending on role, tenure, and needs
- **Direct report owns agenda**: Use shared Google Doc, add items as they come to mind
- **Not a status update**: 1:1s are for alignment, difficulties, brainstorming — status goes to GitHub/standups
- **Skip level meetings**: Every 6-8 weeks for speaking with manager's manager
