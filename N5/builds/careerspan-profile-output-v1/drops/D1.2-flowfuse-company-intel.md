# FlowFuse Company Intelligence

## Company Overview

FlowFuse is an Industrial Application Platform founded in 2021 by Nick O'Leary (co-creator of Node-RED) and Zeger-Jan (ZJ) van de Weg (early GitLab employee). The company provides an enterprise-grade platform built on top of Node-RED that enables organizations to deploy and manage industrial applications at scale, now enhanced with AI-powered copilot capabilities. Headquartered in San Francisco, FlowFuse operates as a fully remote company with team members distributed across Europe, North America, and Asia.[^1][^2]

The company has experienced significant growth momentum—according to their CEO ZJ van de Weg, FlowFuse achieved nearly 5x growth in customers and revenue in the past year alone, with this trend accelerating. In June 2025, FlowFuse raised $7.2M in a Series A round led by Senovo, with participation from Bright Pixel, Uncorrelated, Westwave, and Open Core Ventures. Total funding to date is approximately $14.5M across two rounds.[^3][^4]

The founding team brings deep technical pedigree: Nick O'Leary created Node-RED in 2013 while at IBM and continues to lead the open-source project today, while ZJ van de Weg spent significant time at GitLab scaling engineering operations. This combination gives FlowFuse unique credibility in both the Node-RED ecosystem and enterprise software delivery. PitchBook indicates the company currently has approximately 19 employees, suggesting an early-stage team with significant growth runway following their recent funding.[^2][^5]

## Product Focus

FlowFuse extends the open-source Node-RED visual programming environment with enterprise-grade capabilities required for production industrial deployments. The platform enables teams to:

- **Connect disparate systems**: Bridge IT and OT (Operational Technology) by connecting machines, industrial protocols (Modbus, OPC-UA, MQTT), and enterprise systems (ERPs, databases)
- **Build at scale**: Collaboratively develop Node-RED flows with version control, multi-user editing, and DevOps pipelines
- **Deploy remotely**: Manage hundreds to thousands of Node-RED instances across edge devices and facilities with centralized control
- **AI-assisted development**: FlowFuse Expert, powered by Model Context Protocol (MCP), generates Node-RED flows, JavaScript functions, SQL queries, and dashboards from natural language prompts

Key solutions include IT/OT middleware, Unified Namespace (UNS) implementation, SCADA modernization, Manufacturing Execution Systems (MES), edge connectivity, and data integration.[^1][^6]

A major differentiator is FlowFuse's deep AI integration. Rather than bolting a generic LLM onto the platform, FlowFuse uses MCP to connect AI directly to live industrial data, schemas, and documentation. This enables the copilot to generate deployable artifacts that understand a company's specific processes, protocols, and governance requirements in real-time.[^7]

The platform is available as FlowFuse Cloud (managed SaaS) or as a self-hosted deployment for customers requiring on-premises control. Customers include industrial leaders such as Bosch, Epcor, Henkel, and Energinet, spanning manufacturing, utilities, and industrial sectors.[^1]

## Technical Priorities

Based on company communications and product roadmap, FlowFuse's technical priorities center on:

1. **AI Integration via Model Context Protocol (MCP)**: FlowFuse is actively building MCP nodes that expose industrial data to LLMs in a controlled way, enabling natural language querying of live machine state, alarms, and logs. Recent releases (v2.25, v2.26) have introduced role-based access control for MCP nodes, making AI-powered tools safe for enterprise deployment.[^8]

2. **Data Orchestration and Unified Namespace**: The company is investing in building a robust data orchestration layer that enables a Unified Namespace approach. This includes MQTT broker capabilities and improved data transport between producers and consumers. A persistent data storage layer is planned to allow querying of historical events.[^4]

3. **Enterprise Governance at Scale**: A core focus is making Node-RED suitable for regulated industries and enterprise environments. This includes compliance frameworks, audit trails, role-based access controls, approval workflows, and the ability to run hundreds or thousands of Node-RED instances centrally. The goal is to extend DevOps practices to operational technology teams—ensuring development happens outside production systems.[^4]

4. **Self-Service for Non-Developers**: FlowFuse aims to empower "citizen developers"—mechanical, electrical, and operational engineers who understand their operations but lack traditional software development expertise. The AI assistant is positioned as an intelligent guide that helps users build their first flow, suggests optimal configurations, explains complex functions, and removes boilerplate work for experienced users.[^4]

The core technology stack includes Node.js/JavaScript (71.8% of their codebase), Vue.js for frontend (27.1%), with SCSS, Docker, and HTML for additional components. The main FlowFuse repository has 373 stars and 82 forks on GitHub, with 117 releases indicating active development velocity.[^9]

## Hiring Context Implications

### Company Challenges

Based on FlowFuse's positioning and growth trajectory, the company likely faces several challenges relevant to hiring:

- **IT/OT Integration Complexity**: Industrial customers struggle with fragmented data silos between physical shop floor equipment and digital enterprise systems. FlowFuse positions itself as the solution to this $50B+ problem of unplanned downtime across manufacturing globally, but the complexity of real-world industrial environments creates technical and relationship challenges.[^1]

- **Enterprise Adoption Friction**: While Node-RED has millions of deployments, moving from proof-of-concept to production-grade enterprise deployment requires security, compliance, and governance capabilities that traditional Node-RED doesn't provide. FlowFuse must convince enterprises to adopt a newer platform over legacy SCADA systems.[^1]

- **AI Validation in Industrial Context**: While AI is a major strategic bet, industrial customers are conservative about deploying AI in production environments, particularly when it affects control systems. Demonstrating reliable, safe AI assistance in this context is likely an ongoing challenge.[^7]

- **Scaling Platform**: With 5x growth in customers and revenue, the company is scaling its platform to support thousands of concurrent Node-RED instances across multiple facilities for enterprise customers. This requires significant infrastructure and DevOps investments.[^4]

### Who Would Thrive Here

Candidates who succeed at FlowFuse are likely to demonstrate:

- **Full-stack JavaScript proficiency**: The core platform is built on Node.js and Vue.js. Full-stack development capability with these technologies is essential, especially for engineering roles.

- **Node-RED or visual programming experience**: Deep familiarity with Node-RED, flow-based programming, or similar low-code environments is highly valuable, as it's the core technology the platform extends.

- **AI/LLM integration experience**: With FlowFuse Expert being a major differentiator, candidates with experience integrating LLMs into production applications, particularly using emerging protocols like Model Context Protocol (MCP), would be particularly valuable.

- **Cross-functional collaboration skills**: As a remote company bridging IT and OT domains, the ability to collaborate across functions (engineering, product, customer success) and communicate with non-technical stakeholders is important.

- **Open-source DNA**: FlowFuse is deeply committed to the Node-RED open-source project and has an open-source culture. Candidates who contribute to open-source, engage with technical communities, and embrace transparency would align well.

- **Enterprise software experience**: Understanding enterprise requirements around security, compliance, governance, and DevOps practices is relevant, as FlowFuse positions itself as an enterprise-grade platform.

- **Comfort with ambiguity and iteration**: As a Series A company experiencing rapid growth, the ability to work in a fast-paced environment, iterate quickly, and handle ambiguity is important. The company values "Iterative Improvement" as a core principle.
