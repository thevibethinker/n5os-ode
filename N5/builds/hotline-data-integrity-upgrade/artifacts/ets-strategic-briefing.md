## Part 2: The Strategic Moment — Why ETS Is Under Pressure

### 2.1 SAT Contract Loss (~$300M/year revenue impact)

In **June 2024**, the College Board ended its decades-long contract with ETS for SAT development and processing. The College Board moved SAT production in-house and to a digital format.

**Estimated revenue impact:** ~$300 million annually — roughly 27% of ETS's total revenue.

**Source:** Inside Higher Ed reporting, cited in BSchools.org analysis ¹

**Context:** This was ETS's single largest revenue line item. The loss forced ETS to accelerate its diversification strategy.

### 2.2 GRE Volume Collapse

The test-optional movement in graduate admissions has devastated GRE demand:

| Academic Year | GRE Tests Administered | Change |
|--------------|----------------------|--------|
| 2018/19 | 532,826 | Baseline |
| 2023/24 | 256,215 | **-52% decline** |

**Source:** ToeflResources.com data, cited in BSchools.org ¹

**Additional context:** Over 90% of U.S. colleges and universities now have test-optional policies for the 2026 admission cycle.¹

**Inference (labeled):** The GRE volume decline likely accelerated after COVID-era test-optional policies became permanent at many institutions. ETS's response — making GRE shorter, cheaper, and available at home — has not reversed the trend.

### 2.3 Workforce Reductions

ETS has conducted **six rounds of layoffs since April 2020.**

| Date | Details | Source |
|------|---------|--------|
| April 2020 | First round (COVID-related) | Inside Higher Ed, cited in BSchools.org ¹ |
| Multiple rounds 2020–2024 | Ongoing restructuring | BSchools.org compilation ¹ |
| Late 2025 | **757 layoffs** (California operations — lost state testing contract) | BSchools.org citing CA WARN filings ¹ |

### 2.4 Potential Sale of GRE and TOEFL (January 2026)

**This is the most significant recent development.**

On **January 27, 2026**, the Wall Street Journal reported that ETS is **exploring selling or finding strategic investors for its GRE and TOEFL businesses.**

**Source:** Inside Higher Ed, "ETS Seeks to Sell GRE, TOEFL" (Jan 27, 2026) ⁴, citing Wall Street Journal reporting

**What this means:**
- ETS's two most recognizable products may be divested
- This signals a potential full pivot from legacy testing to workforce/talent solutions
- Any acquirer of GRE/TOEFL would likely want volume growth — which has been elusive
- If the sale happens, ETS becomes a fundamentally different company, centered on Futurenav, PSI, and workforce products

**Inference (labeled):** The potential GRE/TOEFL sale, combined with the SAT contract loss, suggests ETS leadership is actively reshaping the organization away from its testing heritage toward workforce talent solutions. This is the strategic context in which they're evaluating Careerspan.

### 2.5 Financial Summary

| Metric | Value | Trajectory |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Total Revenue | ~$1.1B | Flat (SAT loss offset by PSI acquisition revenue) |
| Profitability | Razor-thin (~2% margin in 2024) | Recovering from 2023 loss |
| GRE Revenue | Declining | -52% volume since 2018/19 |
| SAT Revenue | **Gone** | Lost June 2024 (~$300M/year) |
| Headcount | Declining | Six rounds of layoffs since 2020 |

**Inference (labeled):** ETS is a ~$1.1B revenue organization running near breakeven while simultaneously losing its two largest legacy revenue streams (SAT contract gone, GRE declining, TOEFL potentially for sale). The aggressive M&A strategy and Futurenav investment represent an existential pivot, not just a growth initiative.
