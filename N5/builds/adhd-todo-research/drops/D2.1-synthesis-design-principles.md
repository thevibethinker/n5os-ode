# D2.1: Synthesis — Design Principles for V's Task System

## Objective
Synthesize all research from Stream 1 (D1.1-D1.5) into a coherent set of design principles for building V's ADHD-optimized task/accountability system within Zo.

## Context
V wants to build the "best goddamn to-do list app" deeply integrated with Zo. Key requirements:
- **Organization**: Domain > Project (ephemeral/permanent/recurring) > Task
- **Single Sentinel**: One scheduled agent checking frequently, texting reminders
- **Nag Mode**: Tasks that BLOCK Zo access until completed — "ultimate productivity enforcement"
- **Accountability Partner**: Not just a to-do list, but a chief of staff who runs V down
- **ADHD-aware**: Built for someone with ADHD + anxiety who struggles with email follow-ups

## Deliverable
Create a **Design Principles Document** that V can absorb to understand how to build the system. Structure:

1. **Executive Summary** (1 page max)
   - The 5-7 core principles that should guide the system
   - What makes this different from every other to-do app

2. **Core Design Principles** (detail each)
   - Each principle with rationale from research
   - How it applies to V's specific system

3. **Feature Recommendations**
   - Must-haves (research strongly supports)
   - Should-haves (research suggests)
   - Avoid (research warns against)

4. **Nag Mode Design**
   - How to implement "blocking" effectively
   - Safeguards against shame spirals
   - Escalation patterns

5. **The Accountability Partner Model**
   - What makes a good AI accountability partner
   - Tone, timing, persistence patterns

6. **Gamification Guidance**
   - Whether/how to integrate Arsenal theme
   - Sustainable vs. gimmicky approaches

7. **Implementation Priorities**
   - What to build first
   - What can wait
