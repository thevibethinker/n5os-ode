# D1.4: Anxiety & Task Avoidance — Breaking Paralysis

## Objective
Research the specific problem of task avoidance driven by anxiety, especially around emails and follow-ups. Understand the psychology and discover interventions that break the paralysis.

## Context
The user (V) specifically mentioned:
- ADHD combined with severe anxiety
- Particular difficulty with email follow-ups
- Avoidance patterns that compound (the longer you avoid, the worse it gets)

## Research Questions
1. What is the relationship between ADHD and anxiety in task avoidance?
2. Why do emails specifically trigger avoidance? (social evaluation, perceived stakes)
3. What is the "doom pile" phenomenon and how do you address it?
4. What interventions break task initiation paralysis?
5. How do you handle tasks that have been avoided so long they feel "impossible"?
6. What role does perfectionism play in ADHD task avoidance?

## Concepts to Explore
- Task initiation vs. task completion (different problems)
- The "wall of awful" concept (ADHD community term)
- Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD)
- Anxiety-driven procrastination vs. ADHD procrastination
- The 2-minute rule and its variants
- "Eating the frog" vs. "starting with wins"

## Sources to Prioritize
- ADHD + anxiety comorbidity research
- How to ADHD (YouTube/community)
- ADHD coaching best practices
- CBT/ACT approaches to task avoidance
- Email-specific productivity research

## Deliverable
A research summary with:
1. **Executive Summary** — Key insights for breaking avoidance
2. **Anatomy of Avoidance** — Why it happens, especially with emails
3. **Intervention Strategies** — What works to break paralysis
4. **System Design** — How a task system can help (vs. hurt) avoidance
5. **Email-Specific Tactics** — Strategies for the email problem specifically
