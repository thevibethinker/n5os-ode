# D1.3: External Accountability — Behavioral Psychology

## Objective
Research the psychology of external accountability, commitment devices, and why external consequences work when internal motivation fails. This is especially relevant because we want to build a "nag mode" that blocks Zo access until tasks are done.

## Research Questions
1. What is the psychology of commitment devices? (Ulysses contracts, pre-commitment)
2. Why does external accountability work better than internal motivation for some people?
3. What does research say about body doubling and co-working?
4. What are the risks/downsides of punishment-based systems? (shame spirals, avoidance)
5. How do successful accountability partnerships work?
6. What's the optimal "punishment" intensity — too harsh vs. too soft?

## Concepts to Explore
- Commitment devices (economics literature)
- Implementation intentions
- Body doubling
- Accountability partnerships
- Loss aversion in behavior change
- Ulysses contracts
- The role of social pressure

## Sources to Prioritize
- Behavioral economics literature (Thaler, Ariely, etc.)
- Research on StickK, Beeminder, and similar commitment platforms
- Psychology of habit formation (BJ Fogg, James Clear perspectives)
- ADHD-specific accountability research

## Deliverable
A research summary with:
1. **Executive Summary** — Why external accountability works (and when it doesn't)
2. **Mechanism Analysis** — The psychology of why it works
3. **Best Practices** — How to design effective accountability systems
4. **Pitfalls** — When external pressure backfires (shame, avoidance, rebellion)
5. **Design Implications** — How to build "nag mode" that works without causing harm
