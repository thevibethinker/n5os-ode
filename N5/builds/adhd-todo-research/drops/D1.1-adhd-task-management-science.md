# D1.1: ADHD & Task Management — Clinical/Scientific Literature

## Objective
Research what clinical psychology, neuroscience, and ADHD specialists say about effective task management systems for ADHD brains. Understand WHY traditional to-do lists fail and what actually works.

## Research Questions
1. What are the core executive function deficits in ADHD that affect task management? (working memory, time blindness, task initiation, etc.)
2. What does the research say about effective interventions for task completion in ADHD?
3. What role does external structure vs. internal motivation play?
4. What are the recommended strategies from ADHD coaches and clinicians?
5. Are there specific frameworks (like "body doubling") with research backing?

## Sources to Prioritize
- Peer-reviewed research (PubMed, Google Scholar)
- CHADD (Children and Adults with ADHD) resources
- ADDitude Magazine (practitioner-facing)
- Books by Russell Barkley, Edward Hallowell, or similar ADHD experts
- Recent meta-analyses on ADHD and productivity

## Deliverable
A structured research summary with:
1. **Executive Summary** — 3-5 key takeaways for system design
2. **Core Deficits** — What ADHD does to task management capability
3. **What Works** — Evidence-based strategies (with citations)
4. **What Fails** — Why typical systems don't work for ADHD
5. **Design Implications** — Concrete recommendations for building a task system
