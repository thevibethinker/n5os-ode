# D1.5: Gamification & ADHD — When It Works

## Objective
Research whether gamification helps or hurts ADHD productivity. The user mentioned wanting to revive an "Arsenal FC themed productivity system" — understand when theming/gamification is effective vs. gimmicky.

## Research Questions
1. Does gamification actually help ADHD users, or is it just novelty that wears off?
2. What types of gamification work? (points, streaks, themes, competition, narrative)
3. When does gamification backfire? (shame from broken streaks, over-complication)
4. Is there research on "interest-based nervous systems" and how that relates?
5. What makes a gamified system sustainable vs. abandoned after 2 weeks?
6. Does personalizing the theme (e.g., Arsenal FC) increase engagement?

## Apps/Systems to Examine
- Habitica (full RPG system)
- Forest (simple gamification)
- Duolingo (streak-based)
- Finch (pet/care gamification)
- Pokémon GO (movement gamification)
- Themed productivity systems (sports, games, etc.)

## Concepts to Explore
- Dopamine and reward systems in ADHD
- The "interest-based nervous system" (ADHD concept)
- Novelty-seeking and why new systems work then fail
- Intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation
- Variable reward schedules

## Sources to Prioritize
- Research on gamification effectiveness (especially for ADHD)
- ADHD community discussions on gamified apps
- Game design principles applied to productivity
- Behavioral psychology of game mechanics

## Deliverable
A research summary with:
1. **Executive Summary** — Does gamification work for ADHD? When?
2. **What Works** — Gamification mechanics that help
3. **What Fails** — Mechanics that backfire or lose effectiveness
4. **Sustainability** — How to make it last beyond novelty
5. **Arsenal Integration** — Should we theme it? How to do it right?
