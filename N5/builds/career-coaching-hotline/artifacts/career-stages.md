# Career Stage Framework

Grounded in V's coaching philosophy across 10+ years and 24 source documents. These stages drive the diagnostic, knowledge base routing, and Careerspan value prop targeting.

## Stage 1: Groundwork (Pre-Search)

**Profile:** Students, recent graduates, early-career professionals who haven't started a serious job search yet. They may be exploring options, finishing school, or just beginning to think about career direction.

**Defining signals:**
- Haven't done structured self-reflection on skills/experiences
- No master resume or career inventory
- Unclear on what they want from their next role
- May be considering internships or first full-time roles
- Haven't identified target industries or roles

**Core pain points:**
- "I don't know what I want to do"
- "I don't know what I'm good at"
- "I don't know where to start"
- Limited understanding of how hiring actually works

**V's coaching angle:** Introspection comes first. You are "an amorphous cloud of skills and experiences and competencies" — before you can present yourself to employers, you need to understand what you have to offer. The Art of the Brag starts with knowing what to brag about.

**Primary knowledge base topics:** Self-reflection, groundwork, anecdote development, introspection 101, internship creation

---

## Stage 2: Materials (Active Preparation)

**Profile:** People who know what they want but are building or refining their job search materials. They may have started applying but aren't getting traction. Employed or unemployed.

**Defining signals:**
- Have some idea of target roles/industries
- Working on resume, cover letter, LinkedIn
- May be submitting applications but not hearing back
- Haven't adopted the master resume approach
- Materials aren't tailored to specific roles

**Core pain points:**
- "I'm applying but not getting interviews"
- "I don't know if my resume is good"
- "I don't know how to tailor my materials"
- "ATS systems are screening me out"

**V's coaching angle:** 63% of recruiters want tailored resumes. Only 2% of resumes result in interviews. The master resume + subtraction method is the foundation. Every bullet needs an impact metric or scale indicator. The 5-second rule: recruiters decide in 5 seconds whether to spend 30 more.

**Primary knowledge base topics:** Resume customization, AISS bullet construction, ATS optimization, cover letters, master resume concept, materials preparation

---

## Stage 3: Outreach (Active Search)

**Profile:** People whose materials are in reasonable shape but who need to expand their job search strategy beyond applications. They need networking, outreach, and systematic effort.

**Defining signals:**
- Have a resume but are relying solely on job boards
- Not networking or doing cold outreach
- Applying to many jobs without strategic targeting
- May have been searching for 2+ months without results
- Not leveraging referrals (referrals make candidates 4x more likely to get hired)

**Core pain points:**
- "I'm applying to everything but nothing is working"
- "I don't know how to network without feeling awkward"
- "I've been at this for months"
- "I'm sending 50+ applications with no response"

**V's coaching angle:** Companies have more options than you — you won't be taken seriously unless you show you're serious. Cold outreach done right earns respect. No one wants to waste their time, but most people want to help someone who comes off as professional, reliable, and enthusiastic. Networking isn't optional — it's one of 4 essential levers.

**Primary knowledge base topics:** Networking in the modern era, cold outreach, LinkedIn strategy, systematic job hunting, the 4 levers framework

---

## Stage 4: Performance (Interview & Conversion)

**Profile:** People who are getting interviews or opportunities but not converting them into offers. They may also be navigating offer negotiation or deciding between opportunities.

**Defining signals:**
- Getting some interviews but not advancing
- Struggle to articulate their value clearly
- Don't prepare flexibly enough for varied interview formats
- May not follow up effectively after conversations
- Haven't mastered the "tell me about yourself" answer

**Core pain points:**
- "I get interviews but never get the offer"
- "I freeze up in interviews"
- "I don't know how to talk about myself"
- "I'm not sure which opportunity to choose"

**V's coaching angle:** The best interview prep is knowing yourself inside and out. Prepare anecdotes in START format (Situation, Task, Action, Result, Takeaway). You're presenting a composite of your best attributes — no one thing is unique, it's the combination. Delivery matters as much as content.

**Primary knowledge base topics:** Interview preparation, the Art of the Brag (delivery), START format, self-reflection integration, career decision-making

---

## Stage 5: Transition (Career Change or Crisis)

**Profile:** Experienced professionals navigating a significant career shift — industry change, return after a gap, layoff recovery, or senior-level strategic repositioning. Different from Stage 1-4 because they have substantial experience but face unique translation challenges.

**Defining signals:**
- Have 5+ years of experience but changing direction
- Recently laid off or experiencing career disruption
- Trying to break into a new industry or role
- Have transferable skills but struggle to communicate them
- May feel overqualified for some roles, underqualified for others

**Core pain points:**
- "I have experience but it's not in the right field"
- "I was laid off and don't know what's next"
- "I feel like I'm starting over"
- "How do I compete with people who have direct experience?"

**V's coaching angle:** Breaking into new industries requires reframing, not reinventing. Career services fail these people the most — the "muzzling effect" prevents honest guidance about the effort required. 46% of new hires fail within 18 months, and 89% fail for attitudinal reasons, not technical skills. This means transferable human skills matter enormously — you just need to frame them correctly.

**Primary knowledge base topics:** Breaking into new industries, career preparation strategy, self-reflection for experienced professionals, the systemic problems with career services, reframing experience

---

## Stage Classification Notes

- **Stages are not strictly sequential.** A senior executive in a layoff might be Stage 5 (Transition) but also need Stage 2 (Materials) work. The diagnostic identifies the *primary* stage to prioritize coaching.
- **Multiple stages can co-exist.** The tool returns a primary stage plus secondary pain points.
- **Stage 1 (Groundwork) is the foundation.** V's philosophy: introspection drives everything else. Even experienced professionals benefit from returning to groundwork.
- **The 4 Levers framework** (Groundwork → Applying → Networking → Interviewing) maps roughly to Stages 1-4, with Stage 5 as a cross-cutting concern for career changers.
