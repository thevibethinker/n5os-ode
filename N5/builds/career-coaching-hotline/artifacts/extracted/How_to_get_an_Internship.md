How to get an “institutionalˮ internship

Company with an established process and desire for bringing in interns

Key features:
 -   Application “seasonˮ (large companies) or just-in-time recruiting
 -   Track record of recruiting interns over last 35 years
 -   Formal application process
 -   Joining as part of a cohort



                        Approach this like an applicant!

How NOT to approach things

1.   Jump straight into applying for internships without doing enough groundwork

2.   Prioritize applying to “moreˮ internships over applying more effectively

3.   Spend time redesigning materials that donʼt need to be designed (donʼt submit
     Canva resumes or PDFʼs to application systems)

4.   Submit the same or similar materials everywhere - regardless of role or
     company

5.   Donʼt prepare for interviews until he or she is invited

6.   Donʼt approach internship hunting systematically and with attention to detail

How to approach this process


1.   Apply to the right internships and present yourselves in the right way

2.   Create ATS and reader friendly materials tailored to the role and
     company

3.   Set yourself apart through their knowledge of the company or the role

4.   Prepare for a variety of interview questions and situations

5.   Put in consistent and systematic effort

How to create your own internship
Company (usually a small one) that does not run regular internship programs


 -   No set timings or duration
 -   No specific application cycle
 -   No established processes for vetting interns
 -   Not factoring interns into their hiring decision-making
 -   You will likely be the only intern


                      Approach this like an entrepreneur!

How to create your own internship
1.   Pick an industry or role in which you would like more experience
     If optimizing for industry
      ❏ Research the problem spaces and solutions
      ❏ Understand the players and their role in the ecosystem
     If optimizing for role
      ❏ Analyze entry level roles in that position
      ❏ Compare your skills to the responsibilities - which ones can you do?
2.   Find companies in the “goldilocksˮ zone 550 employees)
3.   Hustle!
      ❏ Check out their socials (donʼt forget Glassdoor!
      ❏ Look at their job board - what roles are they hiring for?
      ❏ Find founders/team members who post frequently on LinkedIn - engage
           with their content
      ❏ Put forward a “case for myselfˮ - email, LI messages, “coldˮ applying, etc.

Pro tips for creating your own internships

1.   Donʼt be shy! The only way this works is if you put yourself out there. If you
     do it right, people will respect it!
2.   Be thoughtful - no one wants to receive a cookie-cutter message
3.   Be open-minded - there are a lot of great companies out there. Look
     broadly!
4.   Be specific about the value you can give to them
5.   Learn from your mistakes and iterate on your approach
6.   Donʼt lose hope - itʼs all a numberʼs game!
