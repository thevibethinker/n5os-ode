Recap
• Cover letter is about THEM
• Basic structure
  – Paragraph 1: Intro + why you are applying
  – Paragraph 2-3: How you are qualiﬁed
  – Conclusion: 1-2 sentences rounding oﬀ your
    points and reiterating interest
  – Sign-oﬀ with name and signature

AI 101
• When most people say AI = Large
  Language Model
• Usually people are referring to OpenAI’s
  ChatGPT or Anthropic’s Claude
• Relies on a “chat” interface
• You tell the AI something, it responds with
  relevant information

AI 101
• What kind of information can you send the AI
  – (1) Role context (framing)
  – (2) Instructions (prompts)
  – (3) Reference material
• Once the AI has returned something, you can
  reﬁne it further using additional prompting
• Treat each chat as though it’s self-contained

Rules of the Road
• Treat using an AI like a collaboration
  – You need to set the AI up for success with the
    information you provide and what you ask from it
• The less you share with the AI, the more it will
  either (a) fabricate or (b) output something
  generic
• AI is not great at “word counts” – try going by #
  of paragraphs and reﬁning with prompts (e.g.
  “Make the last paragraph more succient)

My advised approach to
    AI-generated cover letterse
• Step 1: Present framing
• Step 2: Digest JD, resume, and my thoughts on
  the company and role
• Step 3: Generate a cover letter as speciﬁed

Step 1: Framing
• You want to open the conversation by telling the
  AI what "role” it’s playing
  – This improves performance signiﬁcantly
• You want to specify
  – Who the AI is emulating
  – Who you are and what your “overall” needs from the
    AI are
  – What would constitute success by working with the
    AI

Step 2: Digest reference material
• Prompts = the instructions you want the
  AI to carry out
• Reference material = information the AI
  should consider when processing your
  request
  – Sometimes, the entire instruction can be to
    ask the AI to digest some reference material
    for future use

Step 2: Digest reference material
• For cover letters, you should be giving the
  AI 3 types of reference material:
  – (1) The job description
  – (2) Your resume/summary of most relevant
    experiences
  – (3) Thoughts on the company + role

Why go through these steps?
• The AI isn’t “magic” and it certainly
  doesn’t know your life history – you have
  to set it up for success
• The more clear and compartmentalized
  you make your instructions and
  information to the AI, the better the results

Improving the output
• Improving input
   – Go beyond the resume and include relevant reﬂections
   – Dig deep when developing talking points for the company and
     role
• Further specifying “voice” related information - diction,
  formality, and tone
• Reﬁnement prompting – all the instructions you give
  after the “output” to hone it further
   – E.g. “Make paragraph 2 more succient”, “make the tone less
     formal”, “use a diﬀerent example in your conclusion”, etc
• Providing the AI with examples of your writing by asking
  it to “digest” past cover letters

Bonus: Developing Company
          Talking Points
• If you need stronger company related talking points, try
  the following
  – (1) Open a new chat and prompt the AI to help you develop
    talking points by asking questions one by one
      • Perform additional research on the company/role to answer
        eﬀectively
  – (2) Ask the AI to summarize your responses into some key bullet
    points that convey the nature of your interest
  – (3) Use the output as the “thoughts on company and role”
    reference material for the AI in the main cover letter workﬂow

Litmus tests for a good cover
              letter
• “Name blind” test – if I were to remove the
  company and role name and read it, would I
  know exactly what job and organization it’s for
  – Same test works with your name – could anyone
    have written this cover letter or only you
• Resume repetition test – does the content
  repeat resume content or extend on that point
  – Read your resume before reading your cover letter to
    see this clearly

Words of Caution
• The AI starts to forget details over the course of
  a conversation (limited context window)
  – If you notice its memory slipping, start over with a
    new conversation
  – Try and use a separate “chat” for each cover letter
• There is ALWAYS a risk of hallucinating, so read
  EVERYTHING before submission
