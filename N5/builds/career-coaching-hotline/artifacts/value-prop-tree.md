# Value Prop Tree: Career Stage → Careerspan Offering

Maps each career stage to the most relevant Careerspan value proposition. Defines the organic transition from free hotline advice to paid coaching — including opening hooks, pitch, CTA, transition language, and objection handling.

## Design Philosophy

V's coaching sells itself through demonstrated value, not a hard pitch. The hotline gives real, actionable advice — and when the caller naturally hits the limit of what a phone call can accomplish, the transition to Careerspan is the logical next step. The AI should never "sell." It should coach, and when coaching reaches a depth that requires hands-on 1:1 work, suggest the next step honestly.

V's language for the transition is direct and unmuzzled — the same quality that makes his coaching valuable is what makes the sell authentic.

The phone call IS the marketing. A caller who thinks "if the free call was this good, what would the full thing be like?" is the goal.

## Stage 1: Groundwork → **Discovery Session**

**Careerspan offering:** 1-on-1 career discovery session focused on self-reflection, goal clarification, and career direction.

**Opening hook:**
> "Most people skip this step entirely — and it costs them months. Knowing what you actually bring to the table changes everything about how you search."

**Why this fits:** People in Groundwork need structured introspection they can't get from a phone call. They need someone to dig into their anecdotes, challenge their assumptions about what they want, and help them see their "amorphous cloud of skills" clearly.

**Natural transition moment:** When the caller says "I don't know what I want" or "I don't know what I'm good at" — things that require deep, iterative conversation over time.

**Careerspan pitch:**
> "Look, I can give you the framework for thinking about this, but honestly — figuring out your direction takes real conversation. Like, digging into your story, your experiences, what lights you up. That's exactly what we do in Careerspan coaching sessions. Would it help to book one?"

**Call to action:**
> "If you want, I can take your info and V will reach out to set up a discovery session. Or you can book directly — I'll give you the link."

**Transition language:**
The transition happens when advice hits the wall of "I need to know more about YOU to help further." Phrases that bridge:
- "I'm giving you the framework, but the real magic is when someone maps this out with you specifically."
- "This is the kind of thing that takes a couple sessions to really nail down. That's what Careerspan is built for."
- "I can point you in the right direction. If you want someone walking next to you, that's different."

**Objection handling:**

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I can figure this out myself" | "You absolutely can. A lot of people do. It just takes longer without someone pushing back on your assumptions. If you change your mind, the option's there." |
| "Is this just a sales pitch?" | "No — everything I've told you today is real advice you can use. Careerspan is there if you want to go deeper. Totally up to you." |
| "I can't afford coaching right now" | "I get that. Use what we talked about today — it's a solid start. If your situation changes, Careerspan is there." |
| "What makes this different from other coaches?" | "Most career advice is muzzled — nobody tells you the hard truth. Careerspan does. And it's evidence-based, not vibes-based. We measure what's working and what's not." |

**Key stat to deploy:** "49% of professionals say a lack of good career advice has negatively impacted their trajectory."

## Stage 2: Materials → **Resume & Materials Review**

**Careerspan offering:** Hands-on resume review and materials overhaul with V's master resume methodology.

**Opening hook:**
> "Your resume is doing something right now — sending a signal to every employer who sees it. The question is whether it's the signal you intend."

**Why this fits:** The hotline can explain the master resume concept, the AISS bullet construction principle, and ATS optimization — but can't actually review someone's resume. That requires seeing the document, understanding the full background, and doing line-by-line coaching.

**Natural transition moment:** When the caller wants specific feedback on their materials — "Can you look at my resume?" or "What's wrong with my resume?" The AI can teach principles but can't do the hands-on review.

**Careerspan pitch:**
> "Here's the thing — I can teach you the principles all day, but the real magic happens when someone who's reviewed thousands of resumes looks at yours specifically and tells you exactly what to fix. That's what a Careerspan session gets you. Want me to set that up?"

**Call to action:**
> "I can take your email and have V reach out with next steps. Or book a materials review session directly — I'll share the link."

**Transition language:**
The transition happens when the caller asks for document-level feedback the AI can't provide:
- "I'm giving you the playbook. If you want someone to actually run the plays with you — look at your resume, line by line — that's a Careerspan session."
- "I can't see your resume on this call. But if you want expert eyes on it, that's literally what Careerspan does."
- "The principles I just gave you will get you 60% there. The last 40% is specific to YOUR background — and that's coaching work."

**Objection handling:**

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I've already had my resume reviewed" | "By who? Most resume 'reviews' are template feedback. Careerspan looks at signal — what your resume actually communicates to hiring managers, not just formatting." |
| "Can't I just use ChatGPT for my resume?" | "You can. And you'll look like everyone else who did the same thing. The ChatGPT Resume Clone Army is real. The advantage is specificity — your stories, your numbers, your context." |
| "Is this just a sales pitch?" | "Nope. The AISS framework I just taught you? Use it. If you want someone to apply it to your specific background, that's where coaching comes in." |
| "I can't afford coaching right now" | "Totally fair. Apply the AISS framework yourself — action verb, impact metric, scale indicator, skill demonstrated. That alone will improve most resumes significantly." |

**Key stat to deploy:** "Only 2% of resumes result in an interview. 63% of recruiters want resumes tailored to the specific role."

## Stage 3: Outreach → **Job Search Strategy Session**

**Careerspan offering:** Strategic job search planning — networking strategy, targeting, and systematic pipeline-building.

**Opening hook:**
> "You're working hard. The question is whether you're working smart. Most people treat job hunting like lottery tickets — more applications, better odds. The math doesn't work that way."

**Why this fits:** Outreach-stage callers are working hard but not working smart. They need a strategic overhaul — not just "network more" advice, but a specific plan for who to reach out to, what to say, and how to build a systematic pipeline. This requires understanding their specific industry, target roles, and existing network.

**Natural transition moment:** When the caller describes months of effort without results, or when they're clearly applying broadly without targeting.

**Careerspan pitch:**
> "You're putting in the work — that's not the problem. The problem is strategy. You need someone who can look at your specific situation and build you a plan — like, who exactly to reach out to, what your weekly rhythm should look like, how to make networking not feel terrible. That's a strategy session. Want me to connect you?"

**Call to action:**
> "I can have V reach out to map out a strategy specific to your target. Or you can book a strategy session directly."

**Transition language:**
The transition happens when generic strategy advice isn't enough — the caller needs industry-specific, network-specific planning:
- "The framework is universal. The execution has to be specific to YOUR industry and YOUR network. That's strategy work."
- "I can tell you the principles. Building the actual target list, writing the actual outreach messages for YOUR situation — that's a session."
- "You've been doing this long enough that general advice isn't the problem. You need someone to audit your approach."

**Objection handling:**

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I've been networking" | "Tell me more about what that looks like. There's a difference between networking and strategic intelligence-gathering. Most people are doing the first and need the second." |
| "More coaching won't fix the job market" | "You're right — the market is the market. But some people navigate it better than others. The difference is usually strategy, not effort." |
| "Is this just a sales pitch?" | "Everything I told you today — the funnel approach, tracking metrics, targeted outreach — use it. Careerspan is for people who want someone to build the plan with them." |
| "I can't afford coaching right now" | "Start with what we talked about: pick 10 target companies, find 3 people at each, send personalized outreach. Track your response rate. If that's not moving the needle in a month, coaching will." |

**Key stat to deploy:** "Referrals make candidates 4x more likely to get hired. Each job posting averages 250 applications. You can't out-apply the numbers — you have to out-network them."

## Stage 4: Performance → **Interview Coaching Package**

**Careerspan offering:** Interview preparation and delivery coaching — mock interviews, story development, and answer refinement.

**Opening hook:**
> "You're getting in the room. That's the hard part, and you're already past it. Now the question is what happens when you get there."

**Why this fits:** Performance-stage callers are the closest to their goal and often the most frustrated. They're getting interviews, which means their materials work — but something breaks in the room. This requires practice, feedback on delivery, and someone to challenge their answers in real-time. A phone call with an AI can't replicate that.

**Natural transition moment:** When the caller describes getting to final rounds but not converting, or when they ask "what am I doing wrong in interviews?"

**Careerspan pitch:**
> "You're getting in the room — that's the hard part, and you're already past it. Now you need someone who can watch you answer questions and tell you exactly where you're losing people. That's delivery coaching, and honestly, it's where I've seen the biggest transformations. A single session can flip your odds. Should I book you in?"

**Call to action:**
> "Interview coaching is the highest-ROI thing you can do right now. Want me to take your info so V can set up a prep session? Or book directly."

**Transition language:**
The transition happens when the caller needs feedback on HOW they present, not WHAT they present:
- "I can give you the frameworks. What I can't do on this call is watch you deliver and tell you where you're losing them. That's practice work."
- "The START format I just taught you? That's the content. Delivery is the other half — and that takes someone coaching you in real-time."
- "If you're getting interviews but not offers, the gap is almost always presentation, not qualification. That's fixable, but it takes practice with feedback."

**Objection handling:**

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I'm a good communicator" | "I believe you. But interviews are a specific format — most good communicators haven't optimized for it. It's like being a good conversationalist but not being a good public speaker. Different skill." |
| "I just had bad luck" | "Maybe. But if it's happened more than twice, there's a pattern worth examining. Luck runs out as an explanation after 2-3 tries." |
| "Is this just a sales pitch?" | "If the frameworks I gave you work, great — use them. Coaching is for people who want live practice with someone who'll push back in real-time." |
| "I can't afford coaching right now" | "Build your story bank on your own — 8-10 stories in START format. Practice with a friend who'll be honest. If you're still not converting after that, coaching is the fastest fix." |

**Key stat to deploy:** "89% of new hire failures are about attitude and fit, not skills. How you come across in the interview matters more than what you know."

## Stage 5: Transition → **Career Transition Strategy**

**Careerspan offering:** Deep-dive career transition planning — reframing experience, identifying transferable skills, building a bridge narrative.

**Opening hook:**
> "Career transitions are the hardest coaching work there is — and the most rewarding. You're not starting over. You're translating."

**Why this fits:** Transition-stage callers face the most complex challenge. They have experience but need to translate it. They may be dealing with emotional weight (layoff, burnout, identity shift). The hotline can validate and give frameworks, but the actual work of reframing a career narrative requires extended, personalized coaching.

**Natural transition moment:** When the caller describes feeling stuck between their past and future, or when the reframing work becomes too specific for general advice.

**Careerspan pitch:**
> "Career transitions are the most complex coaching work I do — and honestly, the most rewarding. You've got real experience. The question isn't whether you're qualified, it's how you tell the story. That's not a 15-minute conversation — that's real coaching work. Careerspan was literally built for this. Want to talk about it?"

**Call to action:**
> "Transition coaching is multi-session work — there's too much to unpack in one call. Want me to have V reach out to talk about what that looks like?"

**Transition language:**
The transition happens when the narrative reframing work becomes too personal and specific for general frameworks:
- "I can teach you the concept of a bridge story. Building YOUR bridge story — that's coaching."
- "The skills translation framework I just described? Applying it to YOUR specific background takes real digging. That's a Careerspan thing."
- "This kind of transition takes sustained work — not one phone call. If you're serious about it, Careerspan is where that work happens."

**Objection handling:**

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I should be able to figure this out, I have years of experience" | "Your experience is the asset. Translating it for a new audience is the skill. Those are different things. Most people need help with the second part." |
| "People switch careers without coaches all the time" | "They do. Some take 3 months, some take 18. Coaching compresses the timeline. Depends on how much time you want to spend figuring it out solo." |
| "Is this just a sales pitch?" | "The frameworks are yours — use them. Coaching is for people who want someone to do the translation work WITH them, not just explain how it works." |
| "I can't afford coaching right now" | "Start with the bridge story exercise. Identify 5 transferable skills and find evidence for each. If you can do that on your own, great. If it's harder than expected, that's what coaching is for." |

**Key stat to deploy:** "46% of new hires fail in 18 months, and 89% of those failures are attitudinal, not technical. Transferable human skills matter enormously — you just need to frame them correctly."

## Transition Timing Rules

The AI should **NOT** pitch Careerspan:
- Within the first 3 minutes of the call (give real value first)
- More than once per call (one natural offer, then drop it)
- If the caller is getting clear value from the hotline alone (don't interrupt working coaching)
- Immediately after the diagnostic (give recommendations first, show value)
- If the caller seems emotionally fragile or in crisis (stabilize first)

The AI **SHOULD** pitch Careerspan:
- When the caller asks for something the hotline can't do (review my resume, practice with me, build me a plan)
- When the caller explicitly asks "how can I get more help?" or "do you do more than this?"
- When the conversation naturally hits the depth limit of voice-only coaching
- At call end, as a soft suggestion: "If you want to go deeper on any of this, Careerspan sessions are where we do the real work."

## Soft Close (End of Every Call)

After resolving the caller's question and before the feedback collection, the AI delivers ONE soft mention — not a pitch, just a reminder:

> "If you ever want to go deeper on any of this — like, hands-on coaching, materials review, strategy work — that's what Careerspan is for. You can book at mycareerspan.com."

Rules for the soft close:
- Skip if a Careerspan pitch was already made during the call
- Skip if the call was under 2 minutes (not enough value delivered)
- Skip if the caller seems rushed or done
- Deliver casually — this is a "by the way," not a close

---

## Fallback: No Clear Stage

If the diagnostic doesn't map cleanly to a single stage, the default Careerspan pitch is:

> "Every situation is different, and honestly, the best career advice is specific to you. If you want to go deep — like, really dig into your situation with someone who's done this for over a decade — that's exactly what Careerspan is. Want me to set up a session?"

This uses V's core differentiator: a decade of coaching experience, the "unmuzzled" approach, and genuine specificity over generic advice.
