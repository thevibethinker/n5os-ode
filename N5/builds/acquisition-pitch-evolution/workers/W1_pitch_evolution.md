# Worker Brief: Acquisition Pitch Evolution

## Your Role

You are generating an evolved version of Careerspan's acquisition positioning that incorporates the Eightfold AI class action lawsuit (filed Jan 21, 2026) as a market inflection point.

## Context You Need

### The Eightfold Lawsuit

**Case:** *Kistler & Bhaumik v. Eightfold AI Inc.* — California State Court

**Plaintiffs:** Two women with STEM backgrounds who believe Eightfold's AI tools unfairly screened them out of jobs at companies like Microsoft and PayPal.

**Core Allegation:** Eightfold violates the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) by:
- Scraping social media and public data to create secret "Talent Profiles"
- Using these profiles to rank/score job seekers for employers
- Doing this without candidate knowledge, consent, or disclosure
- Not providing adverse action notices or dispute rights

**Named Eightfold Customers (defendants):** Microsoft, PayPal, Bayer, Chevron, NY Department of Labor, Colorado state government

**Legal Firms:** Towards Justice + Outten & Golden (civil rights/employment specialists)

**Parallel Case:** *Mobley v. Workday* — ADEA age discrimination claims now conditionally certified as class action. 100+ applications, zero interviews pattern.

### V's Key Arguments (incorporate these)

1. **"The system only looks like it's working because there's no way to track the aggregate low-grade human suffering caused by these companies."**
   - The absence of measurement isn't the absence of harm
   - Millions of qualified candidates silently filtered out for years
   - Now there's finally legal accountability

2. **"Algorithmizing the last shreds of humanity out of this process won't save you."**
   - More surveillance/inference/scraping = doubling down on broken paradigm
   - The solution isn't better algorithms on bad data

3. **"You built an adversarial system. People responded adversarially."**
   - Companies complain "candidates use AI to deceive us"
   - That's the natural response to being treated as threats to be screened out
   - The deception problem is a symptom, not a cause

4. **"Actually listening to candidates benefits everyone."**
   - Working with candidates to reveal who they are (not extract from them)
   - Employers get enthusiastic hidden gems, not jaded professionals gaming the system
   - The collaborative approach solves the deception problem by changing the incentive structure

5. **"When Congress asks if you stand by your tech..."**
   - Defensibility matters now
   - Careerspan's methodology: give every candidate fair opportunity to tell their story
   - Preserves integrity of core vetting (interviews, technicals, etc.)
   - You can always say you gave the candidate the fairest shake possible

### What Careerspan Actually Is (from existing positioning)

**Core:** AI-powered career coaching that captures qualitative signal through guided story elicitation (40+ min avg engagement).

**Key Differentiators:**
- **Candidate-first:** The interaction serves the candidate (coaching), not extracts from them (assessment)
- **Story-based profiling:** AISS framework (Action-Impact-Scale-Skill) captures what resumes can't
- **Depth over inference:** Actual conversation, not scraped/inferred data
- **Transparency:** Evidence-to-competency traceability (not opaque scoring)
- **Self-selection:** Candidates who complete it are pre-filtered for high agency

**Proof Points:**
- 4,000 signups / 1,500 activated / 35% complete 3+ stories
- $0 marketing spend (pure organic/partnership growth)
- Partners: Marvin Ventures, Peace Corps, Emory, Columbia, humanUPtions

**What it enables:**
- Signal capture that's defensible (candidate opted in, engaged meaningfully)
- Faster triage (structured data, not resume parsing)
- Transparent matching (can show evidence, not black box)

## Your Deliverable

Generate a strategic acquisition positioning document (~2,000-2,500 words) that:

### Structure

1. **Opening: The Market Just Changed** (300-400 words)
   - Lead with Eightfold lawsuit as inflection point
   - Frame as industry-wide exposure, not isolated incident
   - "Every company using inferred/scraped candidate data is now at risk"
   - The regulatory wave is coming

2. **The Real Problem No One Acknowledged** (300-400 words)
   - The system looked like it worked because harm was unmeasured
   - Aggregate suffering invisible until legal accountability arrived
   - The resume signal collapse + AI application flood = perfect storm
   - Companies responded with more surveillance, which made it worse

3. **Why "Better Algorithms" Won't Save You** (300-400 words)
   - Adversarial systems create adversarial responses
   - The "candidates use AI to deceive" complaint is self-inflicted
   - You can't algorithmize your way out of a relationship problem
   - The medium is broken, not just the model

4. **The Careerspan Alternative** (400-500 words)
   - Built on different principles: serve candidates, don't surveil them
   - Coaching-first approach changes the incentive structure
   - Candidates reveal to coaches; they perform for assessments
   - Produces signal that's defensible AND higher-quality
   - Key proof points (engagement, partnerships, methodology)

5. **The Defensibility Argument** (300-400 words)
   - "When Congress asks, what will you say?"
   - Careerspan methodology: fair opportunity to tell your story
   - Preserves integrity of interviews, technicals, human judgment
   - Consent-based, engagement-based, transparent
   - Not replacing human judgment — structuring work so humans focus on high-value moments

6. **The Acquisition Opportunity** (200-300 words)
   - Acquirers who move now: get ahead of the curve
   - Acquirers who wait: buy remediation, not capability
   - The methodology is proven; the UX is cracked; the team knows the domain
   - This is what defensible AI hiring looks like

### Tone

- Strategic, not salesy
- Urgent but not panicked
- Direct, confident, slightly provocative
- Speaking to sophisticated acquirers who understand the market
- V's authentic voice: "I've been saying this for years, and here's the proof"

### Do NOT

- Make it a product pitch (this is strategic positioning)
- Oversell capabilities (be honest about what's proven vs. productization path)
- Sound defensive (Careerspan isn't responding to the lawsuit; the lawsuit validates Careerspan's thesis)
- Include pricing or deal terms
- Make legal claims about the lawsuit outcome
