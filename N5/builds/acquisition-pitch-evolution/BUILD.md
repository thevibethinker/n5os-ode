# Build: Acquisition Pitch Evolution — Post-Eightfold Lawsuit

## Objective

Evolve Careerspan's acquisition positioning to incorporate the Eightfold class action lawsuit as a market inflection point. The lawsuit validates everything V has been saying — and creates urgency for acquirers to get ahead of the regulatory/legal wave by acquiring technology that has an actual relationship with candidates.

## Context: The Eightfold Lawsuit (Jan 21, 2026)

**Case:** *Kistler & Bhaumik v. Eightfold AI Inc.* — California State Court class action

**Core Allegation:** Eightfold generates secret "Talent Profiles" by scraping social media and public data, then uses these to rank/score job seekers without their knowledge — violating the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA).

**Why This Matters for Acquirers:**
1. **Legal exposure is now real.** Companies using AI hiring tools face FCRA liability for "consumer reports" generated without candidate consent/disclosure.
2. **The Workday case is parallel.** *Mobley v. Workday* (ADEA claims conditionally certified) shows this isn't isolated.
3. **Congress is watching.** Regulatory scrutiny will intensify. Companies need defensible hiring practices.
4. **The whole industry is exposed.** Every company using inferred/scraped data for candidate scoring is at risk.

**The Key Insight (from V's reflection):**
> "The system only looks like it's working because there's no way to track the aggregate low-grade human suffering caused by these companies."

The absence of measurement isn't the absence of harm. Millions of qualified candidates have been silently filtered out by opaque algorithms for years. Now there's legal accountability.

## V's Core Arguments (from this conversation)

1. **The facade is cracking.** ATS companies charge enterprise prices for systems built on surveillance, not relationship. The Eightfold lawsuit exposes what V has been saying for years.

2. **Adversarial systems create adversarial responses.** When companies complain "candidates use AI to deceive," they're describing the natural response to their own adversarial approach. You built a system that treats candidates as threats; they responded accordingly.

3. **Algorithmizing the last shreds of humanity won't fix this.** More surveillance, more scraping, more inference — this is doubling down on a broken paradigm.

4. **The alternative exists.** Actually listening to candidates. Working with them to reveal who they are. Judging them on their best. This benefits everyone — employers get enthusiastic hidden gems, not jaded professionals seeking any port in the storm.

5. **Defensibility matters now.** When Congress asks "do you stand by your tech?" — companies using Careerspan's methodology can answer: "We give every candidate a fair opportunity to tell their story while preserving the integrity of interviews and technical assessments."

## Strategic Frame for Acquirers

**The pitch is NOT:** "Careerspan is better technology."

**The pitch IS:** "The regulatory/legal landscape just shifted. Companies that deployed surveillance-based hiring tech are exposed. Careerspan is the defensible alternative — a methodology built on candidate relationship, not candidate surveillance. Acquirers who move now get ahead of the curve. Acquirers who wait will be buying remediation, not capability."
