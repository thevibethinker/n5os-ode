# Drop D1.3: N5OS Pipeline Tactics Study

## Context

V is preparing a 10-minute opening segment for a Next Play community seminar called "Fundamentals of AI Productivity" happening today (Jan 29, 2026). The core thesis is **context engineering** at three levels: Conversation → Product/Environment → Pipeline.

This Drop focuses on **Level 3: Pipeline Engineering** — connecting AI to external data sources and systems to create a "Meta-OS" that is proactive and reactive.

## Objective

Study V's N5OS system and extract **pipeline-level tactics** — how V has wired together integrations, webhooks, scheduled tasks, and data flows to create a living system.

## Research Instructions

1. **Study V's integrations:**
   - Look in N5/Integrations/ for configured integrations
   - `ls -la /home/workspace/N5/Integrations/`
   - Note which external services are connected and how

2. **Study scheduled agents/tasks:**
   - Look for any documentation about scheduled tasks
   - Search for patterns around automation, proactive AI behavior

3. **Study webhook patterns:**
   - Look in N5/Integrations/ for webhook receivers
   - The Fillout integration is a good example: `file 'N5/Integrations/fillout/'`

4. **Study the concept of "Meta-OS":**
   - Search for: "meta-os", "meta operating system", "N5OS", "personal operating system"
   - Look for any articulation of how the pieces fit together

5. **Study data flow patterns:**
   - How does data from meetings, emails, calendar flow into V's system?
   - Look at the meeting ingestion pipeline as an example
