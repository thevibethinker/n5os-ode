# D1.2: Anti-Patterns Reference Document

## Context

We're building a frontend design skill. A key differentiator from generic AI design is explicit guardrails against "AI slop" — the clichéd, generic aesthetic that makes AI-generated designs immediately recognizable (in a bad way).

This reference document will be used by the main SKILL.md to ensure generated designs avoid common pitfalls.

## MUST DO

1. **Create `Skills/frontend/references/anti-patterns.md`** — A comprehensive catalog of what to avoid

2. **Structure the document** with these sections:

   **Color Anti-Patterns:**
   - Purple-to-blue gradients (the #1 AI slop signal)
   - Overly saturated neon accents
   - Too many colors (more than 3-4)
   - Generic "tech" palettes (blue + gray)
   - Gradients used as a crutch instead of intentional design

   **Layout Anti-Patterns:**
   - Perfect symmetry everywhere (feels robotic)
   - Generic 3-column feature grids
   - No visual hierarchy (everything same weight)
   - Excessive whitespace OR cramped spacing
   - Predictable hero → features → testimonials → CTA flow without variation

   **Typography Anti-Patterns:**
   - Too many font families (stick to 1-2)
   - Poor contrast (gray text on white)
   - No clear heading hierarchy
   - Overly stylized display fonts for body text
   - Inconsistent sizing scale

   **Imagery Anti-Patterns:**
   - Generic isometric illustrations (the blob people)
   - Stock photos of diverse people pointing at screens
   - Overly polished 3D renders
   - Abstract gradients as hero backgrounds
   - AI-generated images (often have telltale artifacts)

   **Animation Anti-Patterns:**
   - Everything animates on scroll
   - Parallax effects everywhere
   - Bouncing elements
   - Slow fade-ins that delay content visibility
   - Animation for animation's sake

   **Copy Anti-Patterns:**
   - "Revolutionize your workflow"
   - "Seamless integration"
   - Buzzword soup
   - Overly long hero headlines
   - Generic CTAs ("Get Started", "Learn More")

3. **For each anti-pattern, provide:**
   - What it looks like (specific description)
   - Why it's bad (the underlying problem)
   - What to do instead (the fix)

4. **Add a "Taste Test" section** — Quick checklist:
   - [ ] Would this design look at home on a 2019 Dribbble shot? (If yes, reconsider)
   - [ ] Does it have a purple gradient? (Avoid unless specifically requested)
   - [ ] Are there more than 2 fonts? (Usually too many)
   - [ ] Does every section animate? (Probably too much)
   - [ ] Could you swap the content for any other SaaS and it still work? (Too generic)

## MUST NOT DO

- Do NOT include positive examples (that's what templates are for)
- Do NOT be preachy or condescending — this is a reference, not a lecture
- Do NOT make it so restrictive that all creativity is blocked
- Do NOT touch any other files

## Scope

**Files to create:**
- `Skills/frontend/references/anti-patterns.md`

**Files NOT to touch:**
- `Skills/frontend/SKILL.md` (D1.1's job)
- `Skills/frontend/templates/*` (Wave 2)

## Expected Output

A comprehensive anti-patterns reference that:
1. Catalogs specific, recognizable AI design clichés
2. Explains why each is problematic
3. Provides alternatives
4. Is scannable and usable as a quick reference
5. Has a taste-test checklist for self-review
