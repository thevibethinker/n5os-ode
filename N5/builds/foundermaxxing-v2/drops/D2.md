# D2: Competitor Landscape + Messaging + Conversion

## Objective

Three-part research drop:
1. Map the competitive landscape for AI productivity programs aimed at founders/executives
2. Study landing pages that convert well for premium ($100-500/mo) membership products
3. Refine FounderMaxxing's messaging relative to what exists

## Part 1: Competitive Landscape

Research and catalog:
- AI productivity courses/communities for founders (e.g., AI for Business Leaders, Reforge AI track, etc.)
- Premium founder communities with AI components (YPO, EO, Hampton, etc.)
- Solo operator / vibe coder communities
- Founder productivity programs (non-AI)

For each, document:
- Price point
- Format (async, live, community, course)
- Target audience (technical vs non-technical)
- Key value prop
- What they're missing

Identify the specific WHITE SPACE that FounderMaxxing occupies:
- Non-technical founders (not engineers)
- Live builds (not lectures)
- AI-native infrastructure (not just using AI)
- Practitioner-led (V builds daily, not an academic)
- Meta OS pedagogy (heuristics, not tool tutorials)

## Part 2: Conversion Analysis

Study 5-10 landing pages for premium membership products ($100-500/mo) that are known to convert well. Focus on:
- Information hierarchy (what comes first, second, third)
- How they handle pricing psychology
- Social proof placement and format
- Form friction (short vs long applications)
- Urgency/scarcity techniques that don't feel sleazy
- Mobile vs desktop differences
- What makes you actually want to sign up vs bounce

Sources to check: IndieHackers case studies, landing page teardowns, ConvertKit creator economy reports.
