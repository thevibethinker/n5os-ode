# B31: Research & Market Intelligence

## Objective

Capture research findings, market intelligence, competitive analysis, and data-driven insights discussed in the meeting.

## What to Capture

**Research Findings:**
- Studies or analyses mentioned
- Key data points
- Insights or conclusions
- Methodology if relevant

**Market Intelligence:**
- Market size and trends
- Customer segments
- Industry dynamics
- Growth projections

**Competitive Intelligence:**
- Competitor strategies
- Market positioning
- Strengths and weaknesses
- Emerging threats

**Customer Insights:**
- User research findings
- Customer feedback patterns
- Behavioral insights
- Needs and pain points

**Data & Metrics:**
- Key numbers and statistics
- Performance data
- Benchmarks
- Trends over time

## Output Format

### Research Findings

**Study: [Name/Topic]**
- **Source:** [Where this comes from]
- **Key Findings:**
  - [Finding 1]
  - [Finding 2]
- **Implications:** [What this means for us]
- **Reliability:** [Quality/credibility assessment]

### Market Intelligence

**Market Segment: [Name]**
- **Size:** [TAM/SAM/SOM if discussed]
- **Growth:** [Trends and projections]
- **Dynamics:** [Key factors shaping market]
- **Opportunity:** [Where we can play]

### Competitive Landscape

**Competitor: [Name]**
- **Position:** [How they're positioned]
- **Strategy:** [Their approach]
- **Strengths:** [What they do well]
- **Weaknesses:** [Where they're vulnerable]
- **Implications:** [How this affects us]

### Customer Insights

**Insight: [Finding Name]**
- **Evidence:** [Data or feedback supporting this]
- **Implications:** [What we should do about it]
- **Confidence:** [How reliable is this]

### Key Data Points

- **[Metric]:** [Value] ([Context or trend])
- **[Metric]:** [Value] ([Context or trend])

## Generation Guidelines

1. Capture data accurately:
   - Exact numbers when stated
   - Sources when mentioned
   - Context for interpretation

2. Assess reliability:
   - How credible is the source?
   - How recent is the data?
   - Any caveats or limitations?

3. Extract insights:
   - What does the data mean?
   - What should we do about it?
   - How does it change our thinking?

4. Connect to strategy:
   - How does this inform decisions?
   - What opportunities does it reveal?
   - What risks does it highlight?

5. Note gaps:
   - What research is needed?
   - What data is missing?
   - What assumptions need validation?

## When to Generate

Generate B31 for meetings involving:
- Research review or presentation
- Market analysis discussions
- Competitive intelligence sharing
- Customer research findings
- Data-driven decision making

Skip for meetings without significant research or intelligence content.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check:
- [ ] Data captured accurately
- [ ] Sources documented
- [ ] Insights extracted, not just data
- [ ] Implications for strategy noted
- [ ] Reliability/credibility assessed
- [ ] Competitive context included
- [ ] Gaps or research needs identified
- [ ] Actionable for decision-making
