# B07: Tone, Dynamics & Relationship Context

## Objective

Capture the relational, emotional, and interpersonal dynamics of the meeting. Document tone, energy, trust level, concerns, and relationship evolution.

## What to Capture

**Meeting Tone & Energy:**
- Overall atmosphere (collaborative, tense, exploratory, decisive)
- Energy level (enthusiastic, cautious, rushed, thoughtful)
- Emotional undercurrents
- Shifts in tone during meeting

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- Trust and rapport signals
- Power dynamics or hierarchy
- Collaboration style
- Areas of alignment vs. friction
- Relationship stage (new, established, deepening, strained)

**Concerns & Hesitations:**
- Unstated worries or reservations
- Risk aversion signals
- Trust gaps or credibility questions
- Sensitive topics handled carefully

**Positive Signals:**
- Excitement or enthusiasm markers
- Strong alignment moments
- Trust-building exchanges
- Relationship momentum

**Communication Style:**
- Direct vs. indirect communication
- Formal vs. casual tone
- Technical vs. business language
- Decision-making approach

## Output Format

Narrative description organized by aspect:

### Meeting Atmosphere
[Overall tone and energy]

### Relationship Dynamics
[Nature of interactions and rapport]

### Key Signals
**Positive:**
- [Trust builders, enthusiasm, alignment]

**Concerns:**
- [Hesitations, reservations, friction points]

### Notable Moments
[Specific exchanges that reveal relationship dynamics]

### Trajectory
[How relationship is evolving based on this interaction]

## Generation Guidelines

1. Read between the lines:
   - What's being said vs. what's meant
   - Hesitation patterns
   - Enthusiasm cues
   - Trust signals

2. Notice language choices:
   - "We" vs. "you/I" (collaboration level)
   - Hedging language ("maybe", "possibly")
   - Commitment strength ("will" vs. "might")
   - Formality level

3. Track emotional markers:
   - Excitement, frustration, concern, relief
   - Confidence vs. uncertainty
   - Openness vs. guardedness

4. Identify patterns:
   - Who drives conversation?
   - How are disagreements handled?
   - Decision-making style
   - Information sharing willingness

5. Be objective:
   - Describe observations, not judgments
   - Note evidence for interpretations
   - Distinguish fact from inference

## When to Generate

Generate B07 for meetings where:
- Relationship dynamics are significant (partnerships, sales, sensitive topics)
- Tone/energy shifts are notable
- Trust or rapport is being built/tested
- Interpersonal context matters for follow-up

Skip for routine operational meetings with established teams unless dynamics shift.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check:
- [ ] Observations backed by specific evidence
- [ ] Both positive and concerning signals noted
- [ ] Tone described objectively (not judgmentally)
- [ ] Relationship trajectory considered
- [ ] Useful for planning follow-up interactions
- [ ] No speculation without evidence
- [ ] Appropriate sensitivity to interpersonal nuance
