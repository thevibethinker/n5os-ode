# B10: Context Connections & Cross-References

## Objective

Link this meeting to relevant existing knowledge, past meetings, ongoing projects, and broader context. Build the web of connected information.

## What to Capture

**Related Meetings:**
- Past meetings referenced or relevant
- Future meetings mentioned
- Series or sequence this belongs to

**Connected Projects:**
- Active projects this meeting touches
- Initiatives this advances or affects
- Workstreams involved

**Knowledge Base Connections:**
- Relevant documents in workspace
- Notes or strategies that apply
- Frameworks or models relevant
- Past decisions that inform this

**People & Relationships:**
- Who else should know about this
- Relationships that connect to this
- Network effects or implications

**Topics & Themes:**
- Broader themes this meeting exemplifies
- Patterns across multiple contexts
- Strategic threads this advances

**Timeline Context:**
- Where this fits in larger timeline
- Dependencies on past events
- Implications for future

## Output Format

### Related Meetings
- **[Meeting Date/Title]**
  - Connection: [How it relates]
  - Key Links: [Specific themes or decisions that connect]
  - File: [Link to meeting folder if available]

### Active Projects
- **[Project Name]**
  - Relevance: [How this meeting impacts project]
  - Status Update: [What changed based on this meeting]

### Knowledge Base Links
- **[Document/Note Title]**
  - Location: [Path to file]
  - Relevance: [Why it connects]
  - Application: [How to apply this knowledge]

### Broader Context
[Narrative describing where this meeting fits in the bigger picture]

### Strategic Threads
- **[Theme/Pattern]**
  - Evidence: [What in this meeting supports this]
  - Trajectory: [How this advances the thread]

## Generation Guidelines

1. Think connections:
   - What past meetings relate?
   - What projects does this touch?
   - What knowledge applies?

2. Cross-reference explicitly:
   - Reference specific meetings
   - Link to documents
   - Name projects and initiatives

3. Build the web:
   - Show relationships
   - Reveal patterns
   - Connect dots

4. Add strategic value:
   - Not just "this relates to that"
   - Explain WHY connections matter
   - Show implications

5. Make actionable:
   - Who should see this connection?
   - What should be updated?
   - Where should links be added?

## When to Generate

Generate B10 when meeting:
- References past meetings or decisions
- Advances ongoing projects
- Connects to existing knowledge
- Part of larger sequence or pattern
- Has implications for other work

Skip for isolated, one-off operational meetings.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check:
- [ ] Related meetings identified and linked
- [ ] Project connections explicit
- [ ] Knowledge base cross-references included
- [ ] Strategic patterns noted
- [ ] Timeline context provided
- [ ] Implications for other work surfaced
- [ ] Links are accurate and helpful
