# B12: Product Strategy & Roadmap

## Objective

Capture product vision, strategy, roadmap discussions, and product-specific decisions from the meeting.

## What to Capture

**Product Vision:**
- Long-term product direction
- Target users and use cases
- Value proposition
- Differentiation strategy

**Feature Priorities:**
- Features discussed or prioritized
- Rationale for priority decisions
- Feature trade-offs evaluated

**Roadmap:**
- Timeline or sequencing
- Milestones
- Release planning
- Dependencies between features

**User Insights:**
- Customer feedback mentioned
- User needs discussed
- Pain points identified
- Usage patterns or data

**Competitive Analysis:**
- Competitor features mentioned
- Market positioning
- Gaps and opportunities

**Product Decisions:**
- Build vs. buy decisions
- Feature scope decisions
- Design direction choices
- Prioritization decisions

## Output Format

### Product Vision
[High-level direction and goals]

### Feature Priorities

**High Priority:**
- **[Feature Name]**
  - User Value: [Why users need this]
  - Business Value: [Why business prioritizes this]
  - Effort: [Complexity estimate if discussed]

**Medium Priority:**
- [Features in consideration]

**Deferred:**
- [Features postponed with rationale]

### Roadmap & Timeline

**Q[X] [YEAR]:**
- [Feature/milestone]
- [Feature/milestone]

**Dependencies:**
[What must happen before what]

### User Insights
- [Key feedback or insight]
- [Pain point identified]
- [Usage pattern]

### Competitive Landscape
- [Competitor strength/weakness]
- [Market opportunity]

### Product Decisions
- **Decision:** [What was decided]
  - Rationale: [Why]
  - Alternatives: [What was considered]
  - Impact: [Implications]

## Generation Guidelines

1. Focus on product level:
   - User value and experience
   - Feature decisions
   - Market positioning
   - Product strategy

2. Capture rationale:
   - Why these priorities?
   - Why this sequence?
   - What's the strategic thinking?

3. Connect to users:
   - How does this help users?
   - What problems does it solve?
   - What feedback drove this?

4. Note trade-offs:
   - What was chosen?
   - What was not chosen?
   - Why?

5. Timeline context:
   - When will things happen?
   - What's the sequence?
   - What gates or dependencies?

## When to Generate

Generate B12 for meetings involving:
- Product planning or strategy
- Roadmap discussions
- Feature prioritization
- Product reviews
- User feedback synthesis
- Competitive analysis

Skip for meetings not focused on product decisions.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check:
- [ ] Product vision and strategy clear
- [ ] Priorities documented with rationale
- [ ] Timeline or roadmap captured
- [ ] User value emphasized
- [ ] Competitive context included
- [ ] Decisions documented with reasoning
- [ ] Useful for product team planning
