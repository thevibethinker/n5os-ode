# B10: Risk Register & Mitigation

## Objective

Document risks, concerns, potential problems, and mitigation strategies discussed during the meeting.

## What to Capture

**Identified Risks:**
- Technical risks
- Business risks
- Execution risks
- Relationship/political risks
- Market/timing risks
- Resource/capacity risks

**Risk Attributes:**
- Likelihood (high/medium/low)
- Impact if occurs (high/medium/low)
- Timeframe (when could this happen)
- Indicators (how would we know)

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Prevention approaches
- Contingency plans
- Risk transfer options
- Monitoring plan

**Assumptions & Dependencies:**
- Critical assumptions being made
- Dependencies that could fail
- Single points of failure

## Output Format

### Risk: [Risk Name/Description]

**Category:** [Technical/Business/Execution/etc.]

**Description:**
[What could go wrong, clear statement of the risk]

**Likelihood:** High/Medium/Low  
**Impact:** High/Medium/Low  
**Timeframe:** [When this could occur]

**Indicators:**
- [How we'd know this risk is materializing]
- [Warning signs to watch]

**Mitigation:**
- **Prevention:** [How to reduce likelihood]
- **Contingency:** [What to do if it happens]
- **Owner:** [Who's responsible for managing this]

**Dependencies:**
[What this risk depends on or affects]

---

### Critical Assumptions
- [Assumption 1]: If this proves false, [impact]
- [Assumption 2]: If this proves false, [impact]

## Generation Guidelines

1. Listen for risk language:
   - "What if..."
   - "The concern is..."
   - "We need to watch out for..."
   - "A risk here is..."
   - "If X doesn't happen..."
   - "We're assuming..."

2. Assess severity:
   - How likely is this?
   - How bad if it happens?
   - When could it occur?

3. Capture mitigation:
   - What can prevent it?
   - What's plan B?
   - Who's monitoring?

4. Note assumptions:
   - What must be true for plans to work?
   - What happens if assumptions fail?

5. Be specific:
   - Concrete risks, not vague concerns
   - Measurable indicators
   - Actionable mitigation

## Risk Categories

**Technical:** Implementation challenges, technical feasibility, integration issues

**Business:** Market acceptance, pricing, competition, revenue model

**Execution:** Resource availability, timing, coordination, skill gaps

**Relationship:** Trust, political dynamics, stakeholder alignment

**Market:** Timing, competitive moves, regulatory changes

**Resource:** Budget, people, time, capacity constraints

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check:
- [ ] All significant risks captured
- [ ] Likelihood and impact assessed
- [ ] Mitigation strategies included
- [ ] Ownership assigned when possible
- [ ] Critical assumptions documented
- [ ] Warning indicators specified
- [ ] Organized by category or priority
- [ ] Actionable and specific

## Note on Duplicate Block Code

B10 has two variants:
- **B10_CONTEXT_CONNECTIONS**: Cross-references and knowledge links
- **B10_RISK_REGISTER**: Risks and mitigation

Generate the one that's most relevant to meeting content, or both if both apply significantly.
