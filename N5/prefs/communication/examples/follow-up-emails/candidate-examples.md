## BRIEF LENGTH EXAMPLES (3-5 sentences)

### Example 1: VC Check-In (Pam)
**Length:** 4 sentences  
**Context:** Brief reconnection attempt  
**Subject:** `Follow-up Email – Pam VC check-in`

```
Hey Pam,

Just wanted to check in and see if you had a chance to look at my last email. No pressure—I know things get busy—but wanted to make sure it didn't fall through the cracks.

Happy to resend or clarify anything if helpful.

Best,
```

**Why this works:**
- Classic low-pressure tone
- Direct purpose
- Offers help
- Short and respectful

---

### Example 2: Re-Engagement (Ed/Superposition)
**Length:** 4 sentences  
**Context:** Following up after no response  
**Subject:** `Follow-up Email – Ed @ Superposition partnership exploration`

```
Hey Ed - hope you're doing well.

Just wanted to touch base on this email and see if there's still time for us to connect next week and move this along.

Happy to work around your schedule - if we can do mornings or early afternoons ET, I can bring our head of AI along so you can probe the technical side of our capabilities.

Best,
```

**Why this works:**
- Wellness check
- Low-pressure language
- Flexibility offer
- Brings value (head of AI)

### Example 3: Alumni Network (Felix @ Rice)
**Length:** 5 sentences  
**Context:** Warm alumni connection


**Why this works:**

- Low-pressure ask
- Alumni warmth

## MEDIUM LENGTH EXAMPLES (6-10 sentences)

### Example 4: Darius Follow-Up
**Length:** 7 sentences  
**Context:** Partnership discussion momentum  
**Subject:** `Follow-up Email – Darius partnership discussion`

```
Hey Darius,

Great chatting the other day. I know you mentioned exploring some ideas on your end—curious if anything has crystallized or if there's a way I can help move things forward.

We're actively building out the partnerships pipeline right now, so would love to find alignment if it makes sense.

I've been thinking about what you mentioned re: [specific detail from conversation]. That resonated with me because [connection to Careerspan value prop].

Happy to reconnect whenever makes sense. Let me know if next week works or if you'd prefer to circle back later.

Best,
```

**Why this works:**
- References specific conversation detail
- Shows momentum on their end
- Offers flexibility
- Connection to value

### Example 5: Multi-Party Intro (Heather x Landidly)
**Length:** 6 sentences  
**Context:** Warm introduction coordination  
**Subject:** `Follow-up Email – Heather x Careerspan [Partnership • Landidly • Weston intro]`

```
Hey all,

It's such a pleasure to be connected to Landidly, @Weston. Heather is an old friend and a trusted opinion in our book, and I'm glad that she felt it made sense to bring us together!

```

**Why this works:**
- Warm acknowledgment of introducer
- Delegates scheduling clearly
- Appreciative tone
- Action-oriented

## DETAILED LENGTH EXAMPLES (10-15+ sentences or structured)

### Example 6: Partnership Terms Recap (Edmund @ Superposition)
**Length:** 15+ sentences with structured bullets  
**Context:** Post-call detailed partnership discussion  
**Subject:** `Follow-up Email – Edmund @ Superposition partnership terms`

```
Hi Edmund—

Great chatting earlier. Thanks for powering through the conversation on your walk through the city.

Here are the approximate partnership terms we discussed:

I have our three key areas of alignment + some additional thoughts on revenue/commission.


**API Integration: Careerspan as A Data Layer**

Your voice agent generates rich JDs we turn into evaluation matrices—we surface candidates from our coaching pool who fit the bill through semantic evaluation, prioritizing candidates that we have better signal on i.e. storytellers.


**Role-Specific Pilots: Start with Non-Standard Engineers**

I think your intuition is spot on; Careerspan is goated where latent signals matter because, in your own words, one can't "brute force understanding if someone has that interest." Based on our discussion, it seems this applies to:

* **Forward-Deployed Engineer**
* **GTM Engineer**
* **Solutions Architect**


**Commission Structure Thoughts**

[Details on commission model discussed]


Let me know if this maps to what you remember and where you want to iterate.

Best,
```

**Why this works:**
- Structured for clarity
- Direct quote callback ("brute force understanding")
- Organized with headers
- Invites iteration
- Acknowledges human context (walk through city)

### Example 7: Multi-Stakeholder Coordination
**Length:** 8 sentences  
**Context:** Following up with multiple people involved  
**Subject:** `Follow-up Email – [Name] x [Company] [Topic • Follow-up • Coordination]`

```
Hey all,

Following up on our conversation from last week. I wanted to make sure we're aligned on next steps before moving forward.

Here's what I have on my end:
- [Action item 1]
- [Action item 2]  
- [Action item 3]

@Howie can help coordinate timing for our next sync. Let me know if anything doesn't map to what you remember.

Best,
```

**Why this works:**
- Clear recap
- Bullet structure for readability
- Delegates coordination
- Invites correction
