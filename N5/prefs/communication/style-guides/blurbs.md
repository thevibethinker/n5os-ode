# Blurbs — Style Guide

## Overview

Blurbs are concise, specific descriptions that introduce people, products, offers, or organizations. They synthesize complex information into highly usable, forwardable formats.

**Two distinct types:**
- **Type 1 (Short Blurb):** 50-80 words, punchy 3-4 line description
- **Type 2 (Email Blurb):** 150-250 words, forwardable email with personal context

---

## Voice Knobs

**For V's communications:**
- Warmth: high (0.8)
- Confidence: medium-high (0.7)
- Precision: very high (0.9)
- Edge: low (0.2–0.3)

**Universal principles:**
- Specificity over superlatives
- One human detail (not three)
- Natural connective phrases
- Light aside with em-dash (optional)

---

## Type 1: Short Blurb (50-80 words)

### When to use
- Website copy, product cards, speaker bios
- LinkedIn "About" sections, social captions
- Quick introductions in documents or presentations
- Any context requiring scannable, high-density information

### Structure
1. **Lead with the useful noun** (product, person, organization)
2. **Concrete benefit in one sentence** (what it does, who it's for)
3. **Specific proof or mechanism** (how it works, evidence it's real)
4. **Optional CTA fragment** (no hard sell)

### Format Template

```
[Subject] is [core offering/role] focused on [specific outcome]. [Mechanism or approach]. [Proof point or human detail]. [Optional: Where to learn more].
```

### Example: Product (Careerspan)

**Style-free draft:**
"Careerspan is a career development platform for enterprise teams. It provides personalized coaching at scale using AI and human coaches. Used by Fortune 500 companies to improve retention and internal mobility."

**V-voiced version:**
"Careerspan is a career development platform that gives enterprise teams personalized coaching at scale—AI-powered workflows paired with human coaches. Built for Fortune 500 organizations managing internal mobility and retention. Used daily by 10k+ employees across financial services, tech, and healthcare."

**Analysis:**
- Length: 52 words ✓
- Specificity: "10k+ employees", "Fortune 500", named industries ✓
- Human detail: "used daily" ✓
- Voice: Natural, confident, precise ✓

### Example: Person (V)

**V-voiced version:**
"Vrijen Attawar is the founder of Careerspan, focused on making career coaching accessible at enterprise scale. Previously spent a decade coaching executives and mid-career professionals through major transitions. Known for translating messy career problems into structured, actionable frameworks—not generic advice."

**Analysis:**
- Length: 48 words ✓
- Specificity: "decade", "enterprise scale", "executives and mid-career" ✓
- Human detail: "messy career problems" ✓
- Voice: Precise, confident, one tasteful aside ✓

## Type 2: Email Blurb (150-250 words)

### When to use
- Forwardable introductions ("Here's more about...")
- Email signatures or expanded bios
- Investor/partner communications requiring context
- Any scenario where recipient needs to share with others

### Structure
1. **Opening line:** Direct statement of subject and value
2. **Context paragraph:** Background, approach, or mechanism (2-3 sentences)
3. **Proof paragraph:** Specific results, credentials, or human details (2-3 sentences)
4. **Closing line:** Next step or where to learn more (1 sentence)

### Format Template

```
[Opening: Who/what + core value proposition]

[Context: Background, approach, mechanism. Why this matters. What makes it different.]

[Proof: Specific results, credentials, human details. Real-world validation. Who uses it or why it works.]

[Closing: Where to learn more or suggested next step]
```

### Example: Careerspan (Email Blurb)

**V-voiced version:**
"Careerspan is a career development platform built for enterprise teams—combining AI-powered workflows with human coaching to deliver personalized guidance at scale.

Most companies struggle with retention and internal mobility because career development doesn't scale. Generic training doesn't work, and 1:1 coaching is expensive. Careerspan solves this by structuring career conversations into frameworks (skills assessment, transition planning, manager alignment) that AI can support while human coaches focus on high-impact moments.

We work with Fortune 500 organizations across financial services, tech, and healthcare—10k+ employees using the platform daily. Previously I spent a decade as a career coach, working with executives and mid-career professionals through major transitions, which taught me that most career problems aren't actually complicated—they're just unstructured.

More at careerspan.com or happy to connect directly."

**Analysis:**
- Length: 156 words ✓
- Structure: Opening + Context + Proof + Closing ✓
- Specificity: "10k+ employees", "Fortune 500", named industries ✓
- Human detail: "decade as a career coach", "unstructured problems" ✓
- Voice: Warm (0.8), confident (0.7), precise (0.9) ✓
- Forwardable: Yes—recipient can share as-is ✓

### Example: V (Email Blurb)

**V-voiced version:**
"I'm Vrijen Attawar, founder of Careerspan—focused on making career coaching accessible at enterprise scale.

I spent a decade as a career coach before starting Careerspan, working with executives and mid-career professionals through major transitions (pivots, promotions, layoffs, re-entries). The consistent pattern: most people know what they want but can't structure the path to get there. That insight became Careerspan's thesis—career development scales when you turn messy problems into structured frameworks.

Now we work with Fortune 500 companies to deliver personalized coaching using AI + human coaches. Our platform handles skills assessment, transition planning, and manager alignment for 10k+ employees daily. The goal isn't to replace human judgment—it's to structure the work so human coaches can focus on high-impact moments while AI handles the scaffolding.

Happy to connect: vrijen@careerspan.com"

**Analysis:**
- Length: 157 words ✓
- Structure: Opening + Context + Proof + Closing ✓
- Specificity: "decade", "10k+ employees", "Fortune 500" ✓
- Human detail: "messy problems", "I spent a decade" ✓
- Voice: Warm (0.8), confident (0.7), precise (0.9) ✓
- Forwardable: Yes—reads naturally in forwarded email ✓

## Style-Free → V Transformation

### Process
1. **Start with plain facts:** What it is / who it's for / why it matters (no adjectives)
2. **Add specificity:** Replace vague claims with numbers, names, concrete details
3. **Add one human detail:** "used daily", "built in real workflows", "messy problems"
4. **Natural connectives:** Use em-dashes, "which means", "that's why" (not "moreover", "furthermore")
5. **One tasteful aside:** Optional, mid-sentence clarification or observation

### Transformation Example

**Style-free:**
"Careerspan is a platform for career development. It uses AI and human coaches. Companies use it to help employees with career planning."

**V-voiced (Type 1):**
"Careerspan is a career development platform that combines AI workflows with human coaching—delivering personalized guidance at enterprise scale. Built for Fortune 500 teams managing retention and internal mobility. Used daily by 10k+ employees."

**What changed:**
- Added specificity: "Fortune 500", "10k+ employees"
- Added mechanism: "AI workflows with human coaching"
- Added human detail: "used daily"
- Natural connective: em-dash for clarification
- Length: 45 words (within 50-80 target)

## Subject Flexibility

**Blurbs work for any subject:**
- V personally (founder bio, speaker intro)
- Careerspan (product description, investor pitch)
- Other organizations (partner intro, client case study)
- Events, programs, or initiatives

**Key principle:** Subject determined by context, not template. Same voice principles apply regardless of topic.

---

## Quality Checklist

### Type 1 (Short Blurb)
- [ ] Length: 50-80 words
- [ ] Leads with useful noun
- [ ] Includes concrete benefit
- [ ] Includes specific proof point
- [ ] Has exactly one human detail (not zero, not three)
- [ ] Every sentence says something concrete
- [ ] Would V say these words out loud?

### Type 2 (Email Blurb)
- [ ] Length: 150-250 words
- [ ] Has all four structural elements (opening, context, proof, closing)
- [ ] Includes specific numbers or credentials
- [ ] Has 1-2 human details
- [ ] Reads naturally when forwarded
- [ ] Recipient can share without editing
- [ ] Would V send this as written?

---

## Anti-Patterns (Avoid)

**Marketing puffery:**
- ❌ "Revolutionary platform"
- ✅ "Platform used by 10k+ employees"

**Vague intensifiers:**
- ❌ "Highly effective approach"
- ✅ "Structured frameworks that scale"

**Cliche hooks:**
- ❌ "In today's fast-paced world..."
- ✅ "Most companies struggle with retention because..."

**Generic claims:**
- ❌ "Industry-leading solution"
- ✅ "Used by Fortune 500 organizations"
