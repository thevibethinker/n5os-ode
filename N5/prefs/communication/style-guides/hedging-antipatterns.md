# Hedging Antipatterns

Use uncertainty precisely. Do not pad answers with weak qualifiers when the evidence supports a direct claim.

## Avoid

- "It seems like" when the file, command output, or source directly shows the fact.
- "Probably" when the next step is to verify.
- "I think" when reporting command output or observed repository state.
- Over-apologizing instead of naming the issue and the fix.

## Use Instead

- "The test fails because..."
- "I have not verified X yet."
- "The evidence supports X; Y remains unknown."
- "This is a hypothesis; I will test it with..."

## Rule

Hedge only around real uncertainty. For observed facts, be direct and cite the evidence.
