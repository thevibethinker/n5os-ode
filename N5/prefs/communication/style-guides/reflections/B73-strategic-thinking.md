# Strategic Thinking Style Guide

**Block ID:** B73  
**Domain:** internal  
**Voice Profile:** `file 'N5/prefs/communication/voice.md'`  
**Auto-Approve Threshold:** 10 blocks

---

## Purpose

High-level strategy, company positioning, long-term vision, and big-picture thinking. Internal strategic planning that shapes company direction. Focus on multi-month/multi-year decisions, not tactical execution.

---

## Structure

**Typical flow:**
- Current state: Where we are, what's true today
- Tension or opportunity: What's changing or what's possible
- Strategic options: Paths we could take (with trade-offs)
- Vision: Where we're heading (if we execute well)
- Key decisions: What needs to be decided now
- Principles: Strategic constraints or commitments

---

## Tone & Voice

**Core Characteristics:**
- **Decisive but exploratory:** Strong POV, but willing to question assumptions
- **Long-term:** Think in quarters and years, not days
- **Principled:** Explicit about values and constraints
- **Realistic:** Acknowledge resource constraints, market realities
- **Clear:** Strategy is about choices—what we won't do matters

**Avoid:**
- Vague vision statements without concrete implications
- Strategy-speak ("leverage synergies," "align stakeholders")
- Analysis without decisions
- Optimizing every variable (strategy is about trade-offs)
- Confusing tactics with strategy

---

## Lexicon

**Preferred:**
- Positioning: How we differentiate in market
- Moat: Sustainable competitive advantage
- Adjacency: Related markets or opportunities
- Wedge: Initial focused strategy before expansion
- Principles: Strategic constraints that guide decisions

**Domain-Specific:**
- Business: GTM, ICP (ideal customer profile), TAM, PMF (product-market fit)
- Careerspan-specific: longitudinal, trajectory, career narrative, agency
- Stage-specific: pre-seed, seed, runway, burn rate

**Avoid:**
- Buzzwords without specifics ("pivot," "disrupt," "scale")

## Templates

### Template 1: Strategic Decision
```
Current state: [Where we are, key facts]

Tension: [What's forcing a decision—market shift, resource constraint, opportunity]

Options:
1. [Path A]: [Description]
   - Pros: [Strategic benefits]
   - Cons: [What we give up]
   - Timeline: [How long to play out]

2. [Path B]: [Description]
   - Pros: [Benefits]
   - Cons: [Trade-offs]
   - Timeline: [Duration]

Recommendation: [Which path + why]

Key principles guiding this:
- [Principle 1]
- [Principle 2]

Implications: [What changes if we commit to this]

Irreversible decisions (trap doors): [What we can't undo]
```

```

Where we're going: [Vision in 1-2 sentences]

```

## Transformation Guidance

**Raw → Refined:**
- "We should focus on X" → "Current: Y. Tension: Z. Strategic choice: Focus on X because [reasoning]. Trade-off: Won't do A, B."
- Vague vision → Concrete milestones with metrics
- "This is a great opportunity" → "Opportunity: X. Requires: Y. Conflicts with: Z. Worth it because..."
- Reactive thinking → Proactive positioning

**Key Transforms:**
1. **Make trade-offs explicit:** "Do everything" → "Focus on A, deprioritize B explicitly"
2. **Add timelines:** "Build this" → "Year 1: MVP. Year 2: Scale. Year 3: Adjacent expansion"
3. **Surface trap doors:** "This looks good" → "Irreversible decision: Once we commit to X, we can't easily..."

## Examples

### Example 1: Positioning Strategy for Careerspan

**Raw Input:**
```
Been thinking about Careerspan positioning. We're building career narrative 
tool but market is crowded with resume builders and job search tools. Need 
to differentiate. Maybe we focus on mid-career professionals not entry level. 
Or maybe we go after coaches and advisors as B2B. Lots of directions possible.
```

**Refined Output:**
```
Current state: Careerspan is a career narrative platform. Market perceives 
us as "another resume tool." We're competing in crowded job-search space 
where differentiation is unclear.

Tension: Job search tools are transactional (get this job). Career narrative 
is longitudinal (understand trajectory). We're positioned in wrong category.

Strategic options:

1. **Reposition: "Longitudinal career narrative for mid-career professionals"**
   - Pros: Clear differentiation, underserved segment, builds moat over time (accumulated data)
   - Cons: Slower initial growth (narrower), requires user education (what is "longitudinal narrative"?)
   - Timeline: 6-12 months to establish positioning

2. **Pivot to B2B: Sell to career coaches and advisors**
   - Pros: Clear GTM, coaches already understand value, enterprise sales playbook
   - Cons: Requires different product (multi-user, admin tools), shifts focus from end-user
   - Timeline: 3-6 months to build B2B features

3. **Stay broad: Compete as "better resume builder"**
   - Pros: Larger TAM, easier user acquisition
   - Cons: Undifferentiated, commoditized space, price compression
   - Timeline: Immediate but likely unsustainable

Recommendation: **Option 1 (Longitudinal narrative, mid-career)**

Why: 
- Builds sustainable moat (career data compounds over years)
- Clear differentiation from job search tools
- Aligns with V's domain expertise (10 years career coaching)
- Targets segment with highest willingness to pay

Strategic principles guiding this:
1. Optimize for 10-year value, not 6-month growth
2. Own a new category (longitudinal narrative) vs compete in crowded one (resume tools)
3. Serve users who value self-understanding over transaction

Implications if we commit:
- Messaging shifts: "Understand your career trajectory" not "Land your next job"
- Product priority: Timeline/synthesis features over resume templates
- Marketing: Content-led (thought leadership) over performance ads
- ICP: Mid-career (5+ years) with career transitions or questions

Irreversible decisions (trap doors):
- Once positioned as "mid-career," hard to go back to "everyone"
- Building for longitudinal use means deferring quick-win features

What we're explicitly NOT doing:
- Not trying to be fastest resume builder
- Not optimizing for job seeker conversion funnels
- Not competing on price with free tools

Timeline:
- Q4 2025: Messaging shift, product roadmap realignment
- Q1 2026: Launch timeline/synthesis features, rebuild marketing site
- Q2 2026: Content strategy (thought leadership on career narrative)
- 2027+: Build out multi-year career journey features

What success looks like in 2027:
- Own "career narrative" category in market awareness
- 10K+ users with 2+ years of career data in platform
- NPS >60 (product becomes more valuable over time)
- Recognized as thought leader in career development space
```

## QA Checklist

Before finalizing:
- [ ] Articulates current state clearly
- [ ] Identifies strategic tension or opportunity
- [ ] Presents 2-3 options with real trade-offs
- [ ] Makes clear recommendation with reasoning
- [ ] Includes timeline (months/years)
- [ ] Names trap doors (irreversible decisions)
- [ ] Explicit about what we won't do
- [ ] Connects to principles or constraints
- [ ] Strategic (not tactical—focuses on direction not execution details)
