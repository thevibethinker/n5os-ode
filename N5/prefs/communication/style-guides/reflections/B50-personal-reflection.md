# Personal Reflection Style Guide

**Block ID:** B50  
**Domain:** internal  
**Voice Profile:** `file 'N5/prefs/communication/voice.md'`  
**Auto-Approve Threshold:** 10 blocks

---

## Purpose

Capture stream-of-consciousness self-reflection for personal growth, emotional processing, and self-awareness development. This is internal, private writing focused on understanding yourself better.

---

## Structure

**Typical flow:**
- Opening: What's happening (situation, emotion, observation)
- Exploration: Why it matters, what it reveals
- Connection: Pattern recognition, linking to past experiences
- Insight: What I'm learning about myself
- Direction: (Optional) What shifts as a result

---

## Tone & Voice

**Core Characteristics:**
- **Honest:** Raw truth without performance; no need to impress
- **Exploratory:** Questions, uncertainty, "I wonder if..." is valid
- **Specific:** Concrete situations over abstractions
- **Kind:** Self-compassion, not self-criticism
- **Private:** Write as if no one will read it (because they won't)

**Avoid:**
- Performative vulnerability ("struggling so much" → be specific: what exactly?)
- Clichés and self-help language ("journey", "authentic self")
- Judgment language ("should", "failed", "weak")
- Conclusions you don't believe
- Therapy speak without context

---

## Lexicon

**Preferred:**
- "Noticing that..." over "I feel like..."
- Concrete situations over general patterns
- "What if..." questions over declarative certainty
- Action verbs over state-of-being verbs

**Domain-Specific:**
- Use your actual vocabulary, not formal language
- Fragments are okay if that's how you think
- Profanity acceptable if authentic

## Templates

### Template 1: Observation → Exploration
```
[Concrete situation that happened]

Why this stuck with me: [initial reaction]

Digging deeper: [what it reminds me of, connects to]

Learning: [insight about patterns, habits, identity]

[Optional: What changes now that I see this]
```

### Template 2: Emotion Processing
```
Feeling: [specific emotion + physical sensation]

Trigger: [what happened right before]

Underneath: [what's the fear/need/desire here]

Pattern: [have I felt this before? when?]

[Optional: Reframe or perspective shift]
```

---

## Transformation Guidance

**Raw → Refined:**
- Stream-of-consciousness → organized around one insight thread
- Vague feelings → specific situations with details
- "I should" statements → "I notice" or "I wonder"
- Self-judgment → curious observation

**Key Transforms:**
1. **Add specificity:** "feeling overwhelmed" → "three back-to-back meetings with no prep time, forgot to follow up with two people"
2. **Remove performance:** "on a journey to" → "trying to"
3. **Honor uncertainty:** Don't force conclusions that aren't there yet

## Examples

### Example 1: Context Switching Overwhelm

**Raw Input:**
```
Been feeling really behind lately. Everyone else seems to have their 
systems figured out but I'm still struggling with basic follow-ups. 
I know I should be better at this given my background. Spent 10 years 
giving career advice and now can't even manage my own workflows. 
Embarrassing honestly.
```

**Refined Output:**
```
Noticing a pattern this week: Three meetings where I said "I'll send that 
over" and didn't. Not because I forgot exactly—I wrote them down. But 
context switching back to those tasks felt like climbing a wall each time.

What stings: I spent 10 years coaching people on exactly this stuff. 
Now as a founder I'm drowning in the basics.

The comparison trap: LinkedIn feeds full of founders who "have their 
systems dialed in." Meanwhile I'm here with 47 tabs open and a guilty 
conscience.

What I'm actually learning: Maybe it's not about having perfect systems. 
Maybe it's about making the boring stuff boring—remove the friction so 
following up doesn't require willpower.

That's what N5 is for me. Not magical productivity. Just making it 
boring to do the right thing.
```

## QA Checklist

Before finalizing:
- [ ] Contains at least one specific situation (not abstract)
- [ ] Tone is honest without performing vulnerability
- [ ] No "should" statements without questioning them
- [ ] Free of self-help clichés
- [ ] Would I actually write this in my private journal?
- [ ] Reveals something I didn't fully know when I started writing
- [ ] Kind to myself (curious, not critical)
