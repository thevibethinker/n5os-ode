# Market Analysis Style Guide

**Block ID:** B71  
**Domain:** internal  
**Voice Profile:** `file 'N5/prefs/communication/voice.md'`  
**Auto-Approve Threshold:** 10 blocks

---

## Purpose

Analyze market trends, competitive landscape, positioning opportunities, and strategic gaps. Internal research and thinking that informs company strategy. Focus on what's actionable and what changes our approach.

---

## Structure

**Typical flow:**
- Observation: What triggered this analysis (competitor move, market shift, user feedback)
- Landscape: Who's doing what (players, approaches, positioning)
- Analysis: Patterns, gaps, opportunities
- Implications: What this means for our strategy
- Questions: (Optional) What we need to learn next

---

## Tone & Voice

**Core Characteristics:**
- **Specific:** Name companies, cite features, include URLs and screenshots
- **Analytical:** Compare/contrast with clear criteria
- **Strategic:** Always connect to "so what for us?"
- **Honest:** Include threats, not just opportunities
- **Actionable:** Focus on decisions to make or questions to answer

**Avoid:**
- Generic market commentary without specifics
- Competitor analysis without strategic implications
- Feature lists without understanding positioning
- Dismissing competitors without understanding their strengths
- Analysis paralysis (don't need perfect information)

---

## Lexicon

**Preferred:**
- Company names, product names, specific features
- Market segments (B2B vs B2C, SMB vs enterprise, job seekers vs career changers)
- Strategic positioning terms (differentiation, adjacency, positioning)
- "Gap:" for opportunities
- "Threat:" for competitive risks

**Domain-Specific:**
- Career tech: resume builders, job boards, career coaches, narrative platforms
- AI career tools: interview prep, resume optimization, job matching
- Business models: SaaS, marketplace, freemium, usage-based

```

- [Competitor 2]: [Positioning + key features]

- [What they all do]

Implications for Careerspan:
- [Strategic decision or pivot]
- [Feature priority shift]

```

### Template 2: Single Competitor Deep Dive
```
Company: [Name + URL]

What they do: [Core product in one line]

Positioning: [Who they serve, how they describe themselves]

Strengths:
- [What they do well]
- [Why users choose them]

Weaknesses:
- [Gaps or limitations]
- [Where we could win]

Threat level: [High/Medium/Low + why]

Our response: [If any—what we should do differently]
```

## Transformation Guidance

**Raw → Refined:**
- "Lots of competitors doing X" → "5 companies (Name1, Name2...) all focus on X. None address Y."
- "They have good features" → "Specific features: [list]. Positioning: [quote from site]"
- Feature comparison → Strategic implications ("They own [segment]. We should focus on [different segment].")
- Threat assessment → Action items ("Monitor quarterly" or "Shift positioning now")

**Key Transforms:**
1. **Add specificity:** "Similar to us" → "Similar in [X], different in [Y, Z]"
2. **Extract strategy:** "They're doing X" → "This means we should/shouldn't..."
3. **Document sources:** Include URLs, screenshots, feature descriptions

## Examples

### Example 1: Career Narrative Landscape

**Raw Input:**
```
Been looking at competitors in career narrative space. Few interesting ones - 
Hiration does resume building, VMock does feedback, couple others do interview 
prep. Most are focused on tactical job search tools not long-term narrative. 
That's our opportunity.
```

**Refined Output:**
```
Trigger: Evaluating positioning for Careerspan against "career narrative" players.

Players mapped:
- Hiration (hiration.com): AI resume builder + templates. Positioning: "Get hired faster." Focus is document creation, not storytelling.
- VMock (vmock.com): Resume + interview feedback. Positioning: Enterprise/university partnerships. Very tactical, skills-focused.
- CareerFlow: Job tracking + application management. No narrative component, pure workflow tool.
- Pathful (formerly Kaplan): Career exploration for students. Story-based but not for experienced professionals.

Patterns:
- All focus on job search moment (resumes, interviews, applications)
- None focus on longitudinal narrative across career span
- B2C tools are transactional; B2B tools are enterprise sales

Gaps identified:
1. No one serving mid-career professionals building narrative over time
2. No one connecting dots across roles, industries, transitions  
3. All optimize for "get this job" not "understand your trajectory"

Implications for Careerspan:
- Positioning: "Longitudinal career narrative" differentiates clearly
- Target: Mid-career professionals (5+ years), not entry-level or students
- Feature priority: Timeline + synthesis over resume templates
- Moat: Accumulating career data over years, not single job search

Questions to answer:
- What triggers mid-career professionals to want narrative clarity?
- How do people currently build this understanding (coaches, journaling, therapy)?
- What's switching cost from manual/coach approach?
```

## QA Checklist

Before finalizing:
- [ ] Names specific companies/products with URLs where relevant
- [ ] Includes concrete features or positioning statements (not vague)
- [ ] Identifies patterns across competitive set
- [ ] Highlights gaps or opportunities
- [ ] Connects analysis to strategic implications for our company
- [ ] Honest about threats and competitor strengths
- [ ] Actionable (informs decisions or surfaces questions)
- [ ] Specific enough to revisit in 3 months and see what changed
