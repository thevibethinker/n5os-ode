# Insight Compound Style Guide

**Block ID:** B90  
**Domain:** internal  
**Voice Profile:** `file 'N5/prefs/communication/voice.md'`  
**Auto-Approve Threshold:** 10 blocks

---

## Purpose

Connect insights across multiple reflections to identify emerging patterns, recurring themes, and cumulative learning. This is where individual observations become systemic understanding. Meta-synthesis that reveals what you're learning over weeks/months.

---

## Structure

**Typical flow:**
- Pattern observation: What keeps showing up
- Source reflections: Which blocks/insights connect (link to B50-B82 reflections)
- Synthesis: What the pattern reveals
- Implications: What this means for decisions/behavior
- Open questions: What's still unclear

**Frequency:** Weekly or monthly (not every day—needs time for patterns to emerge)

---

## Tone & Voice

**Core Characteristics:**
- **Pattern-seeking:** Looking across reflections for themes
- **Connective:** Drawing lines between disparate observations
- **Temporal:** Aware of how thinking has evolved over time
- **Humble:** "I'm noticing X, but still figuring out Y"
- **Actionable:** Patterns should inform decisions or shift behavior
- **Curious:** Leads to better questions, not just answers

**Avoid:**
- Forcing patterns that aren't there
- Over-generalizing from 2-3 data points
- Treating patterns as final conclusions (remain open)
- Navel-gazing without implications
- Disconnected from source material (always reference specific reflections)

---

## Lexicon

**Pattern Language:**
- Recurring theme: Shows up multiple times
- Through-line: Common thread across contexts
- Emerging pattern: Starting to notice
- Tension: Two patterns in conflict
- Shift: Change in thinking over time

**Connective Phrases:**
- "Across [B50-003, B71-012, B73-005]..."
- "This connects to earlier thinking in [block]..."
- "Pattern emerged between [date range]..."
- "Noticed a shift from [old pattern] to [new pattern]..."

---

## Transformation Guidance

**Raw → Refined:**
- "I keep thinking about X" → "Pattern observed across [B-codes]: X shows up in these contexts..."
- Multiple observations → Grouped by theme with source blocks linked
- Vague connection → Specific through-line with evidence
- "This is interesting" → "This suggests [implication]"

**Key Transforms:**
1. **Link to sources:** Always reference specific B-block IDs
2. **Add temporal context:** When did pattern emerge? Over what timeframe?
3. **Surface implications:** Pattern → So what? → Action or insight
4. **Acknowledge uncertainty:** "Still figuring out..." or "Open question..."

## Examples

### Example 1: Pattern Across Internal Reflections

**Raw Input:**
```
Been noticing that when I reflect on product decisions, I always start with 
user needs but when I reflect on strategic decisions, I start with business 
constraints. Feels like two different frameworks. Not sure if that's a problem 
or just how different decision types work.
```

**Refined Output:**
```
**Pattern Observed:** Decision frameworks vary by domain (product vs. strategy)

**Source Reflections:**
- B72-003 (Oct 18) — Product decision started with user job-to-be-done
- B72-007 (Oct 22) — Feature prioritization led with user pain points
- B73-002 (Oct 15) — Strategic decision started with runway constraints
- B73-008 (Oct 24) — Positioning decision led with competitive differentiation
- B71-005 (Oct 20) — Market analysis focused on business viability first

**Synthesis:**

Product decisions consistently start with **user needs → solution → business case**.

Strategic decisions start with **business constraints → options → user implications**.

The frameworks are inverted. Not wrong—they reflect different optimization functions:
- Product: Optimize for user value, constrained by feasibility
- Strategy: Optimize for business sustainability, constrained by user value

**Why This Matters:**

1. **Cross-domain decisions are harder:** When product decision has strategic implications (e.g., pricing), unclear which framework to lead with. Recent example: Timeline feature (product lens) vs. AI synthesis (strategic lens) — took 3 weeks to decide because frameworks conflicted.

2. **Communication clarity:** When pitching product to advisors (strategic thinkers), I need to translate user-led reasoning into business-led framing.

3. **Team alignment:** Engineers think product-first, advisors think strategy-first. I'm the translator.

**Implications:**

- For cross-domain decisions: Name the tension explicitly ("This is product-led thinking, but here's the strategic constraint")
- Create decision templates that surface both lenses
- When stuck on decision: Ask "Am I solving a product problem or strategic problem?"

**Open Questions:**

- Are there decisions where frameworks truly conflict (not just different starting points)?
- How do successful founders integrate these lenses faster?
- Should I develop a unified framework, or keep them separate?

**Next Actions:**

- Create decision template that includes both lenses (B73 strategic thinking + B72 product analysis)
- Test on next big decision (Q1 roadmap prioritization)
```

### Example 2: Temporal Shift

**Raw Input:**
```
Went back and read my reflections from September. I was way more focused on 
competitors then. Now I barely mention them. Feels like a shift but not sure 
what it means.
```

**Refined Output:**
```
**Pattern Observed:** Shift from competitor-focused to user-focused thinking (Sept → Oct)

**Source Reflections:**

**September (Weeks 1-4):**
- B71-001, B71-003, B71-006, B71-009 — Market analysis, competitor positioning
- B73-001 — Strategic decision framed around competitive differentiation
- Keywords: "competitors," "differentiation," "market positioning"

**October (Weeks 1-4):**
- B72-004, B72-008, B72-011 — Product decisions led with user needs
- B60-009 — Learning synthesis on career coaching patterns (not competitors)
- B73-005 — Strategic decision focused on long-term user value
- Keywords: "users," "narrative," "career journey"

**Synthesis:**

In September, I was in "startup founder mode"—obsessed with market positioning and competitive differentiation. Every decision filtered through: "How do we stand out?"

In October, shifted to "domain expert mode"—focused on user problems I understand deeply from 10 years coaching. Competitors mentioned less; user psychology and career patterns more.

**Why This Matters:**

The shift isn't just framing—it changes **what I prioritize**.

September lens: "Build features competitors don't have"
October lens: "Build features users actually need"

These can align (unique features that solve real problems) but don't always. Competitor focus can lead to feature bloat; user focus can lead to undifferentiated solutions.

**Possible Explanations:**

1. **Confidence:** As positioning clarifies (longitudinal narrative), less need to check competitors
2. **Stage shift:** Pre-seed = market validation; early product = user validation
3. **Return to roots:** October reconnection with coaching experience (my actual expertise)

**What I'm Learning:**

Best decisions integrate both:
- Lead with user insight (October strength)
- Validate competitive positioning (September strength)

Recent example that worked: Timeline feature decision
- Started with user need (October): "People can't see career progression"
- Validated competitive angle (September): "Timelines exist but not with our synthesis approach"

**Open Questions:**

- Is October shift sustainable or do I need to reintroduce competitive awareness?
- At what stage should founders shift from market-focused to user-focused?
- How do I balance domain expertise (users) with market reality (competitors)?

**Next Actions:**

- In next strategic reflection (B73), explicitly check both lenses: user + competitive
- Monthly review: Am I swinging too far in one direction?
```
