# Follow-up Emails — Style Guide

Use when: nudging after a meeting, circling back on intros, confirming next steps, gentle reminders

Length: 80–220 words (concise, skimmable)

Voice knobs:
- Warmth: high (0.85)
- Confidence: medium-high (0.7–0.8)
- Humility: medium (0.5–0.6)
- Precision: high (0.9)

Structure:
1) Context anchor in first line (what this is about)
2) One-line value or update
3) Clear, single ask with a fallback
4) Simple close

V patterns:
- “Hope you’re doing well!” level warmth without fluff
- Specific details (“AI Collective”, “host and moderator”, numbers, dates)
- One-sentence paragraphs to create emphasis sparingly
- Direct but kind asks; offer an escape hatch

Style-free → V transformation:
- Write a neutral “status + ask” version
- Add V cadence: specific nouns, a natural aside, conversational close

Templates:
Subject: Quick follow-up on [topic]

Hi [Name],

Good to meet [context] / Appreciate [previous action]. Quick update: [one-line value].

If helpful, I can [concrete next step]. Otherwise happy to [fallback].

Warmly,
Vrijen

QA checklist:
- Is the first line enough to remind them what this is?
- Is there exactly one ask?
- Does it sound like a human who respects their time?
