# Directness Calibration Guide

**Purpose:** Context-appropriate directness levels. V wants more directness overall, but calibrated to situation. This isn't "be blunt everywhere" — it's "stop hedging when hedging serves no one."

**Core insight:** Directness ≠ coldness. V's warmth comes from genuine interest, specificity, and follow-through — not from soft language.

---

## The Directness Spectrum

```
[0.0] ←— Hedged —— Measured —— Direct —— Sharp —→ [1.0]
         ↑                        ↑
     (current V              (target V
      default)                default)
```

**Current calibration:** V defaults to 0.4-0.5 (measured-to-hedged)
**Target calibration:** V wants default at 0.7-0.8 (direct-to-sharp)

The goal: shift the baseline right, while retaining ability to modulate left when context demands.

## Directness vs. Warmth (Not a Tradeoff)

Common misconception: "Direct = cold, hedging = warm."

Reality: Directness and warmth are orthogonal.

| | Low Warmth | High Warmth |
|---|---|---|
| **Hedging** | Passive-aggressive | People-pleasing |
| **Direct** | Blunt/cold | **V's target voice** |

**High warmth + high directness looks like:**

> "Loved your talk on [X]. One challenge: I don't see how [Y] works for [Z] use case. What am I missing?"

Not:

> "Great talk! I had some thoughts but I'm not sure if they're valid. I guess I was wondering about [Y]? Not sure if I'm understanding it right."

## Warmth Signals That Don't Require Hedging

Replace hedging with these direct warmth markers:

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "I hope you're doing well" | [Specific personal callback] "How did the marathon go?" |
| "I don't want to impose" | "I know your time is tight — this'll take 5 minutes." |
| "If you have a chance" | "Would Tuesday work?" |
| "I think maybe..." | "I'd suggest..." |
| "No worries if not" | "Let me know either way by Friday." |
| "Just checking in" | "Wanted to make sure you have what you need for [X]." |
| "Totally understand if you're busy" | [Delete — if they're too busy, they'll say so] |

## The "Tougher" Energy V Wants

V explicitly stated: *"I almost want to psych myself into being a tougher person than I am."*

This isn't about being harsh. It's about:

1. **Not pre-apologizing** for existing or taking up space
2. **Not giving escape hatches** that signal you expect rejection
3. **Not over-explaining** before making a request
4. **Not cushioning** opinions you actually believe
5. **Not equivocating** when you know the answer

### Toughness Transformation Examples

**Pre-apologizing → Confident presence:**
- ❌ "Sorry to bother you, I know you're busy, but..."
- ✅ "Quick question:"

**Escape hatches → Clear expectations:**
- ❌ "No worries if not, totally understand if you can't"
- ✅ "Let me know by Tuesday."

**Over-explaining → Clean ask:**
- ❌ "So the reason I'm reaching out is because I've been thinking about this for a while and I wanted to see if maybe..."
- ✅ "Can we [specific ask]?"

**Cushioned opinion → Direct stance:**
- ❌ "I could be wrong but I sort of feel like maybe this approach might have some issues?"
- ✅ "I have concerns about [X]. Here's why: [reason]."

**Equivocating → Decisive answer:**
- ❌ "I mean, it could work either way, I guess? It depends on a lot of factors."
- ✅ "I'd go with [A]. Here's why."

## Context Modulation: When to Dial Back

Directness isn't always 1.0. Context-aware calibration:

### Dial back to 0.5-0.6 when:

1. **First interaction with high-value contact** — Measured professionalism, then loosen
2. **Cross-cultural communication** — Some cultures read directness as rude
3. **Emotionally charged topics** — Lead with empathy, then direct
4. **Genuine uncertainty** — If you actually don't know, hedging is honest
5. **Power asymmetry (upward)** — Pick battles; be direct when it matters

### Stay at 0.8-0.9 when:

1. **With people who know you** — They expect V's real voice
2. **Time-sensitive asks** — Urgency justifies directness
3. **Follow-ups** — Context is established; get to point
4. **Operational communication** — Clarity prevents errors
5. **Your expertise is clear** — Hedging undercuts credibility

## Quality Check: The Directness Audit

Before sending, ask:

1. **First sentence** — Is it the point, or throat-clearing?
2. **Qualifiers** — Can I delete "just," "maybe," "probably" without loss?
3. **Escape hatches** — Am I signaling I expect rejection?
4. **Apologies** — Am I apologizing for something that doesn't warrant it?
5. **Ask clarity** — Would a stranger know exactly what I want?
6. **Deadline** — Have I given a specific timeframe?

If the answer to 1 is "throat-clearing" or any of 2-4 is "yes" — revise.
