# Follow-Up Email Style Guide

**Purpose:** Codify V's authentic voice patterns for post-meeting follow-up emails  
**Source:** High-engagement follow-ups (quick responses, long productive threads)  
**Application:** Use with voice transformation system for meeting intelligence communications

---

## 1. SUBJECT LINE FORMAT

### Pattern: Standardized Structure
```
Follow-up Email – [Name] x [Company] [Topic • Topic • Topic]
```

**Rules:**
- "Follow-up Email" with hyphen (not "Followup" or "Follow up")
- Em dash separator (–) not regular dash (-)
- Format: [Name] x [Company] [Topic • Topic • Topic]
- Use bullet separator (•) between context items
- Capitalize key nouns in context

**Examples from corpus:**
- `Follow-up Email – Fei x Careerspan [Humble Bundle • FOHE Pilot]`
- `Follow-up Email – Mark x Vrijen [Intros • Simulations]`
- `Follow-up Email – Peter x Careerspan [intro • TC and Lensa connections]`

---

## 2. OPENING: RESONANT MOMENT CALLBACK

### Pattern: Reference Specific Conversation Detail

**HIGH PRIORITY:** Open with a specific, memorable moment from the conversation

**Signature patterns:**
- "Great chatting [timeframe]"
- "Thanks for [specific human context from meeting]"
- "I'm excited about / energized by [specific concept discussed]"
- "Quick apology for delay" (when applicable)

**Examples from high-engagement emails:**
- "Great chatting earlier. Thanks for powering through the conversation on your walk through the city." ✓
- "I'm excited about the humble bundle concept we mapped out. Your elimination of upfront onboarding fees removes the biggest friction point..." ✓
- "I can't thank you enough for that stellar call last week. Your insights on Lensa and the Talent Collective connections were absolutely spot-on..." ✓
- "It was such a pleasure connecting last week, and hearing about your professional and entrepreneurial journey." ✓

**Why this works:**
- Proves you were actually listening
- Creates immediate rapport
- Makes email feel personal, not templated

---

## 3. BODY STRUCTURE

### Part 1: Show Progress Already Made (1-2 sentences)

**Pattern:** Demonstrate momentum since meeting

**Examples:**
- "I've reached out to Jeff Taylor this week about offering CareerSpan free to Monster alums"
- "Speaking with Ray tomorrow about testing with FOHE's 5,000 members"
- "We're actively building out the partnerships pipeline right now"

**Why this works:** Shows you took action immediately, not just sending email

---

### Part 2: Organized Deliverables (Core of Email)

**Format: Bulleted lists with bold headers**

**Structure:**
```
*Here's what I promised:*

* *[Deliverable 1]* – [Description with specifics]
* *[Deliverable 2]* – [Context and value prop]
* *[Deliverable 3]* – [Link or next step]
```

**From Mark email (delivered on 6 promises):**
```
*Here's what I promised:*

* *Kamina Singh intro* – She's been instrumental in getting us into a dozen+ universities and knows career centers inside-out. She'll be a tremendous ally for your pilots.

* *Career/HR tech founder roundtable* – You'll love this crew. We have 20+ founders tackling adjacent problems...

[continues with 4 more specific deliverables]
```

**From Fei email (partnership next steps):**
```
*Next Steps:*

* *Bundle formalization:* I'll document our offering this week – Nira for networking, Careerspan for career development, plus Warmer Jobs and Practice Interviews

* *FOHE pilot:* Speaking with Ray tomorrow about testing with FOHE's 5,000 members...

* *Partner alignment:* Scheduling with Asher and Jeff to coordinate implementation
```

**Rules:**
- Use bold asterisks for headers: `*Header*`
- Em dash (–) after header before description
- Mix immediate value (links, access) with future value (intros pending)
- Include WHY each item matters (context for value)
- Be specific about timelines when committing

---

### Part 3: Make Recipient's Life Easy

**Pattern:** Provide ready-to-use content (blurbs, links, instructions)

**Examples from corpus:**
- "Here's a trial link for you and your team to explore: [link]"
- "For the intros, here are the relevant blurbs:" [provides 2 detailed blurbs]
- "Register here for our next session: [link]"
- "@Howie will propose some times and send along my booking link"

**Why this works:** Reduces friction, shows you're doing the work

---

### Part 4: Forward-Looking Next Steps

**Pattern:** Clear, actionable coordination

**Signature phrases:**
- "Let me know if [timeframe] works"
- "Let's compare notes after..."
- "Looking forward to [specific next action]"
- "Once we [milestone], let's [next step] together"

**Examples:**
- "I'll have the formalized concept ready by Friday, plus notes from Ray. Once we gauge the soft interest in our idea, let's co-pitch their leadership together."
- "Let's compare notes after you try the product and have touched base with all these folks"

---

## 4. CLOSING

### Pattern: Warm + Professional

**Default:** "Best,"

**Signature block:** Already handled by email system

**Optional add before close:**
- Appreciation: "Really appreciate you being in our corner as we build..."
- Genuine excitement: "Looking forward to collaborating on this!"
- Momentum language: "Excited to see where this goes"

**Examples from corpus:**
- "Logan and I are particularly energized by how this addresses the real pain point..." → "Looking forward to collaborating on this!"
- "As always, you've given us precisely the right strategic directions to pursue!"

---

## 5. GREETING

**Default:** Hi [Name]

**Use "Hey":** Only in very warm/established relationships, OR when mirroring recipient's tone from previous exchange

**From corpus analysis:**
- "Hi Fei," ✓ (professional partnership)
- "Hey Mark—" ✓ (after warm conversation, note em dash)
- "Hey Peter," ✓ (established relationship)

## 6. VOICE CHARACTERISTICS

### 6.1 Organized Thinking

**Characteristic:** Use structure to make complex info digestible

**Signature patterns:**
- Bold headers for sections
- Bullets for lists
- Mix of narrative + structure
- Headers like: "*Here's what I promised:*" "*Next steps:*" "*Key follow-ups:*"

---

### 6.2 Specific Over Generic

**Characteristic:** Reference precise details from conversation

**Signature patterns:**
- Direct quotes: "in your own words, one can't 'brute force understanding if someone has that interest'"
- Specific numbers: "5,000 members", "20+ founders", "dozen+ universities"
- Named concepts: "humble bundle concept", "arranged marriage framing", "job arbitrage approach"

---

### 6.3 Momentum Language

**Characteristic:** Forward-looking, energetic, collaborative

**Signature words/phrases:**
- "energized", "excited", "genuinely", "spot-on"
- "let's [action] together"
- "would love to", "looking forward to"
- "actively building", "making solid progress"

---

### 6.4 Low-Pressure Collaboration

**Characteristic:** Position as collaborative, not transactional

**Signature phrases:**
- "happy to clarify"
- "let me know how I can help"
- "when convenient"
- "no rush at all"
- "whenever makes sense"

**Examples:**
- "Would love that connection to Talent Collective principals when convenient"
- "Let me know if next week works or if you'd prefer to circle back later"

---

### 6.5 Acknowledgment of Reality

**Characteristic:** Honest about delays, constraints, context

**Signature patterns:**
- "Quick apology for the delay" / "First, my apologies for delay"
- "I know you're a busy guy"
- "Thanks for bearing with us while..."
- Acknowledge specific context: "Thanks for powering through the conversation on your walk"

---

## 7. LENGTH GUIDELINES

### Medium-Long (Typical for post-meeting follow-ups)
- **10-15 sentences** when delivering on multiple promises
- Heavy use of structured bullets
- Mix narrative with lists
- Longer is FINE when organized well

**From corpus:**
- Mark email: ~15 sentences with 6 bulleted deliverables = HIGH ENGAGEMENT ✓
- Fei email: ~12 sentences with 3 bulleted next steps = SAME DAY RESPONSE ✓

**Rule:** If organized with bullets/headers, length is not a problem

---

## 8. AVOID / NEVER USE

- ❌ "Hope this email finds you well" (too generic)
- ❌ "Just checking in" without substance
- ❌ "Circling back" (overused corporate speak)
- ❌ "Per our conversation" (too formal)
- ❌ Apologizing excessively (one acknowledgment max)
- ❌ Generic promises without specifics
- ❌ Asking for things without offering value first

## 9. QUALITY CHECKLIST

**Before sending, verify:**

**Resonance:**
- [ ] Referenced specific moment/quote from meeting
- [ ] Used their language/concepts

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Every promised item included
- [ ] Links/blurbs provided (not "coming soon")
- [ ] Bold headers used for organization
- [ ] Specific timelines given where applicable

**Forward momentum:**
- [ ] Clear next step identified
- [ ] Coordination made easy (links, times, etc.)
- [ ] Shows progress already made

**Voice:**
- [ ] Sounds genuinely excited/engaged
- [ ] Low-pressure, collaborative tone
- [ ] Specific details (not generic)

**Format:**
- [ ] Subject line follows pattern
- [ ] Greeting appropriate (Hi default)
- [ ] "Best," closing
- [ ] Howie refs included when applicable

**Technical:**
- [ ] No spelling errors
- [ ] Em dashes used correctly (–)
- [ ] Links formatted properly
- [ ] V-OS tags present in signature

**Length:**
- [ ] 10-15 sentences typical for deliverables
- [ ] Well-organized with bullets/headers
- [ ] Each sentence earns its place
