# B31_STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

---

**Perspective:** Advisor / GTM coach speaking from founder-facing hiring experience

### Insight 1 — Recruiter-as-service is a viable short-term GTM
- Evidence (quote): "In the short term you can just do the services...you can be a recruiter on steroids." 
- Why it matters: Immediate revenue and higher per-customer value accelerate runway and buy time to build the platform/data layer.
- Signal strength: ● ● ● ● ○

### Insight 2 — Founders / team leads are a high-value ICP because of time scarcity
- Evidence: "Founders are desperate for a little bit of time back"; targeting team leads who lack HR is pragmatic.
- Why it matters: Time-aversion maps to willingness to pay; outreach should focus on recent fundraises and team leads with hiring signals.
- Signal strength: ● ● ● ● ○

### Insight 3 — Role selection impacts monetization and supply/demand dynamics
- Evidence: Discussion on engineers (high demand) vs. product roles (qualitative fit); recruiters/companies pay top-dollar for engineers.
- Why it matters: Prioritize high-demand roles (engineering) for initial monetization; use product/growth roles to diversify once supply scales.
- Signal strength: ● ● ● ○ ○

### Insight 4 — Community partnerships (cornerstone communities) provide defensible supply
- Evidence: "If we can lock down a couple of cornerstone communities...we would have a pretty stable supply side"
- Why it matters: Strategic partnerships with community owners reduce acquisition costs and improve candidate signal.
- Signal strength: ● ● ● ○ ○
