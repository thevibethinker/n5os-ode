# Vrijen's POV on the Future of Careerspan

**Strategic Vision for Disrupting the Career Development Market**

---

## 1. Overall Positioning & Vision for Careerspan

### Present Identity vs. Future Goal

**Present State**
- Described as a "career development platform"—a label that resonates with users

**Long-Term Ambition**
- To become the "world's first career companion"
- Shift from resume/interview prep to **lifelong strategic guidance**

### The Core Hypothesis

**Problem**
- Existing hiring systems rely on a superficial, "two-dimensional" resume approach
- This approach overlooks a candidate's personal values, soft skills, and mindset
- Soft skills and values are critical in determining successful hires, yet typical job platforms focus primarily on keywords and technical skills

**Solution**
- Build a platform that captures and amplifies these deeper dimensions of professional identity

### The Facebook Analogy

**How Facebook Thrived**
- Facebook gave people a compelling reason to share valuable personal data
- Then leveraged that data effectively for network effects

**Careerspan's Equivalent**
- Encourage job seekers to provide rich information about strengths, experiences, and future goals
- Use this data to match them to better opportunities, upskilling paths, and networks
- Create a network effect around career development

---

## 2. Problem with Existing Career Tools & the Resume

### "End Product Orientation"

**Current Problem**
- Traditional career tools (resume builders, cover-letter generators) focus on one-time outputs
- Rather than ongoing professional development or deeper self-knowledge

**Implication**
- Users get a resume but not the deeper work of professional identity development

### Resumes as a Weak Proxy

**Why Resumes Fail**
- Resumes are "two-dimensional"—they barely capture an individual's broader attributes
- They miss values, soft skills, growth potential, and professional trajectory
- **Result:** A hiring process dominated by keywords and technical screening, overshadowing candidate's full potential

### Market Entrenchment

**Current Startup Approach**
- Many startups optimize the existing resume-based funnel for B2B clients
- Focus on advanced parsing, ATS integrations, technical features
- Rather than reimagining how personal data and candidacy are represented

**Why This is Problematic**
- Perpetuates the same limitations of the resume-based system
- Misses the opportunity to fundamentally reshape hiring

---

## 3. Candidate-First Approach & AI-Mediated Coaching

### AI Coaching as a Core Feature

**Differentiator**
- Conversational AI that engages users, prompting them to detail:
  - Strengths
  - Experiences
  - Goals
  - Personal values

**Impact**
- Helps people discover aspects of themselves they might not otherwise articulate
- User feedback: "I am learning things about myself that I didn't know before"

### Incentivizing Deep Engagement

**The Accessibility Challenge**
- Many candidates—particularly those from non-elite backgrounds—seldom have consistent 1:1 mentorship
- Many are unsure how to self-advocate

**The Solution**
- Having an always-available, nonjudgmental AI makes it easier for them to:
  - Share achievements
  - Reflect on their capabilities
  - Uncover hidden strengths

### Bridging the "Hidden Curriculum"

**The Equity Problem**
- Those lacking built-in career guidance at home or school face unspoken disadvantages
- Privileged backgrounds come with informal mentorship; others don't

**How Careerspan Helps**
- Empowers individuals to articulate their professional identity more effectively
- Democratizes access to career coaching practices

---

## 4. Capturing & Quantifying Soft Skills and Values

### Prevalence Analysis

**Current Approach**
- Careerspan reviews user conversations for recurring words/phrases tied to recognized skills or values
- Uses simple pattern matching in the near term

### Not Measuring Mastery

**Important Distinction**
- The immediate focus is to **uncover important traits, not to evaluate skill mastery**
- Mastery or proficiency assessment can come later (from employers)

### Future Integration

**Long-Term Plan**
- Connect with standardized skill taxonomies (e.g., Lightcast)
- Map identified user traits onto recognized skill frameworks
- Create interoperability with industry standards

---

## 5. Building a "3D Profile" to Replace the Resume

### The "Invisible Resume"

**Vision**
- Careerspan's end-goal is a persistent, evolving career profile reflecting:
  - Individual's motivations
  - Values
  - Soft skills
  - Standard accomplishments

### Dynamic Data Over Time

**How It Works**
- As users continue conversations with the AI, the platform accumulates a deep record
- Personal growth is tracked and refined
- Each user's professional story becomes richer and more authentic over time

### Potential for Industry Shift

**Market Impact**
- By providing a far richer candidate profile than a static resume
- Careerspan hopes to reshape how hiring managers evaluate individuals
- Lead to more holistic job matches

---

## 6. Partnership vs. Building a Standalone ATS / Job Board

### API Integration Strategy

**Why Not Build a New ATS?**
- Instead of creating a new ATS or job board, Careerspan offers an **API**
- Allows existing platforms (Workday, Greenhouse, etc.) to tap into richer user data
- Avoids reinventing the wheel while pioneering how candidate profiles are populated and assessed

### Avoiding the B2B Status Quo

**Common Mistake**
- Many startups chase direct enterprise contracts
- Double down on the resume-based funnel
- Perpetuate existing limitations

**Careerspan's Approach**
- Focus on candidates first
- Gain user trust and data accumulation
- Eventually companies will seek out the platform's data pipeline

### Efficiency Over Competition

**Business Logic**
- Building a complete job board requires extensive feature parity with incumbents
- Partnerships let Careerspan concentrate on:
  - Delivering deeper user insights
  - Rather than duplicating established ATS functionalities

---

## 7. Economic Model & Go-to-Market Thoughts

### User Attraction & Retention

**Key Principle**
- The product must demonstrate immediate value to individual job seekers
- Examples: better reflection, better representation of their strengths

### Scaling Through Partnerships

**1–3 Year Horizon**
- Careerspan will evolve from "career development platform" to true "career companion"
- This evolution disrupts the existing resume paradigm
- Partnerships with HR tech systems are central to scaling this vision

### Building Network Effects

**The Flywheel**
1. Users build rich profiles and get value
2. Users share their data and stories
3. Employers want access to this richer data
4. Platform becomes the "data layer of the job market"

## High-Level Summary

### Short-Term Role (0-12 months)
Operate as a user-friendly career development platform with AI-driven coaching to help candidates deepen their self-understanding.

### Core Attraction
- Continuous, reflective AI conversations
- Gather nuanced data—values, goals, soft skills, achievements
- Currently neglected by resume-centric tools

### Medium-Term Objective (1-3 years)
Evolve into a full-fledged "career companion," offering:
- Ongoing guidance
- 3D candidate profile built over time
- Direct feed into ATS solutions via API partnerships

### Ultimate Goal
Reshape the hiring process by providing a far richer, more candidate-centric data layer than the flat resume, ensuring that the right opportunities and individuals connect on a more holistic basis.

---

## Strategic Principles

1. **Candidate-First:** Focus on user value before enterprise revenue
2. **Data Over Documents:** Build profiles, not just better resumes
3. **Partnership Play:** Integrate with incumbents rather than compete
4. **Soft Skills Focus:** Capture what traditional systems miss
5. **Network Effects:** Create value through community and data sharing
6. **Long-Term Vision:** 3-year horizon to transform the industry

---

## The Vision in Practice

**Today:** Job seekers use Careerspan to understand themselves better, articulate their strengths, and tell their stories to themselves and others.

**Tomorrow:** Employers use Careerspan data as a core input into hiring decisions, making matches based on values, potential, and holistic fit rather than keyword matching.

**Future:** Careerspan becomes the "data layer of the job market"—the canonical source of truth about professional identity and potential.
