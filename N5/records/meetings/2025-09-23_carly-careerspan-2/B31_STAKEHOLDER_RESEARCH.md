### 3. Hiring managers write "garbage" JDs that don't reflect actual needs or trade-offs

**Evidence:** "Sitting down with the executive recruiter and sitting down with the hiring manager, we go through this conversation where the original job description is basically garbage. And like, basically she outlined a purple unicorn."

**Why it matters:** JDs are unreliable input for matching systems. Careerspan needs to either (1) help rewrite JDs as part of intake or (2) extract implicit requirements through conversation with hiring manager, not just parse the JD. ### 6. "Ghost jobs" signal latent need for passive candidate pipeline, but mature companies solve this with CRM, not fake postings

**Evidence:** "I read the stat that was like 60 of HR is fine with ghost jobs... My understanding is that HR and talent folks get that given the pressure that they're under to just magically generate candidates, it makes sense to have something open for longer than you are officially looking... [Carly]: We don't do that. We have, we have like a very clear window... I do think that a large part of what keeps our talent acquisition team busy, even when we're not having a huge hiring season, is active CRM management, active candidate outreach."

**Why it matters:** Ghost jobs are symptom of lacking candidate pipeline infrastructure. Mature companies solve this with CRM + active outreach. Immature/transitioning companies might be market for Careerspan's "always-on pool" model. But top-tier TA teams already have a solution (CRM), so product needs to be better than existing CRM workflows, not just "better than ghost jobs."
