## Strategic Decisions

### 1. Pursue Collaborative Relationship vs. Competitive
- **Who Decided:** Mutual decision
- **Decision:** Both parties agreed to explore collaboration and partnership opportunities rather than viewing each other as competitors
- **Rationale:** 
  - Products are highly aligned in philosophy
  - $2 trillion industry has space for multiple players
  - Both see value in working together against incumbent platforms (LinkedIn, ATS systems)
- **Impact:** Opens door for data partnerships, product integrations, and joint go-to-market strategies
- **Category:** Strategic Partnership
- **Quote:** "I think the future is in, like, working together. Like, there's, it's a 2 trillion F in dollar industry, like, space for all of us" (Vrijen)

### 2. Include Emily in Career Tech Founder Roundtable
- **Who Decided:** Vrijen Attawar
- **Decision:** Invited Emily to join monthly HR tech founder roundtable
- **Rationale:** 
  - Emily's ideological alignment with Careerspan philosophy
  - Her unique perspective as former recruiter turned founder
  - Value of "rebellious wavelength" founders in the group
- **Impact:** Expands Emily's network and creates ongoing touchpoint for collaboration
- **Category:** Network Building
- **Quote:** "Love for you to join. It's also like just really great founders folks. You can bounce ideas with everyone's Sort of pretty pro collaboration there"

### 3. Keep Conversation Open Around Integration
- **Who Decided:** Vrijen Attawar (with Emily's interest)
- **Decision:** Maintain ongoing discussion about potential product integration, specifically around using Careerspan data for tailored resume generation
- **Rationale:**
  - Careerspan has corpus of candidate reflections/experiences
  - Emily building productized reverse recruiting tool
  - Both see value in tailored content generation vs. bullet-point manipulation
- **Impact:** Potential technical integration that could differentiate both products
- **Category:** Product Integration
- **Quote:** "One of the things that, on my list of like, damn, this would be sweet, but I don't want to build this out is the ability to take career span data and selectively use that to tailor things on the fly"

## Tactical Decisions

### 4. Emily to Bootstrap (No VC Funding)
- **Who Decided:** Emily Nelson de Velasco
- **Decision:** Fully bootstrap product development without seeking venture capital
- **Rationale:**
  - Wants to maintain control and avoid "everybody else" approach
  - Testing with own reverse recruiting clients provides revenue
  - ML engineer partnership provides technical capability via barter
- **Impact:** Slower growth but higher control; aligns with her independent approach
- **Category:** Funding Strategy
- **Quote:** "I need to fund this. I'm bootstrapping 100. I'm going zero funding whatsoever because everybody else"

### 5. Emily's Initial Product: Internal Use First
- **Who Decided:** Emily Nelson de Velasco
- **Decision:** Build initial product for use with her own reverse recruiting clients as testing ground
- **Rationale:**
  - Dog-fooding approach validates product before broader launch
  - Existing clients provide immediate use cases
  - Can fund development through existing service revenue
- **Impact:** De-risks product development, ensures product-market fit
- **Category:** Product Development / GTM
- **Quote:** "The initial product is going to be for me to use with my clients. And this is my testing. Right."

## Philosophical Alignment (Implicit Decisions)

### 6. Shared Market Diagnosis: AI Arms Race is Harmful
- **Who Agreed:** Both Vrijen and Emily
- **Agreement:** Current state of hiring market is a zero-sum AI arms race creating hyper-competition without value
- **Rationale:**
  - Job seekers gaining efficiency, recruiters losing efficiency
  - Everyone using AI to "not lose out" rather than genuine improvement
  - "Cancerous" evolution toward fabrication and gaming
- **Impact:** Validates both companies' approaches of authenticity over gaming
- **Category:** Market Analysis
- **Quote:** "It is an arms race. Like we are AI ing for the sake of AI... it is totally a zero sum game" (Emily); "It's competitive. It's like, it's cancerous almost" (Vrijen)

### 7. Recruiter Perspective is Undervalued in Career Tech
- **Who Agreed:** Both Vrijen and Emily
- **Agreement:** Former recruiters are underrepresented as founders, leading to poor products
- **Rationale:**
  - "Tech bros" who've had great careers building tools, not people who've helped others have great careers
  - Recruiter knowledge critical since they're first filter
  - Career coaches and recruiters have domain expertise tech founders lack
- **Impact:** Validates both founders' unique positioning and approach
- **Category:** Competitive Positioning
- **Quote:** "It's people that have had great careers is how I put it. Not people that have helped other people have great careers. And that's the whole difference" (Vrijen)
