**Perspective:** Speaking as Director-level leader at workforce development organization (Education at Work)

### Insight 1: Concrete job outcomes drive engagement with career development tools, insights alone don't

**Evidence:** "I think that might be the missing piece for us... It's not just for your own insights and career reflection, but it's also making you part of a pool that you've got visibility with companies. That's a very different offering... the insights are incredible... [but] nobody deems it as essential or urgent while in the moment."

**Why it matters:** Career tool builders consistently overweight "insights" value and underweight "job placement" value - users will engage with reflection/assessment IF there's clear path to employment, not for self-knowledge alone.

### Insight 2: Early career candidates need institutional endorsement to stand out in AI-saturated application environment

**Evidence:** "Are our students really standing out? If it's not companies that there's a relationship with or a knowledge of Education at Work and that these students are vetted... these are quality candidates with a much higher baseline because we've had them, we've prepared them."

**Why it matters:** Generic job board access is insufficient for early career candidates - they need verification/endorsement layer that proves quality and work-readiness; institutional backing (EAW, universities) provides this credibility layer that individual applications cannot.

### Insight 3: Organizational transitions (acquisitions, new parent orgs) create windows for new tool adoption

**Evidence:** "We made the transition to ASA September 1st, so we're three weeks in now, still getting some stuff settled... it should give us more access to like get more ins with companies... it's an organization with significant resources and significant presence networks."

**Why it matters:** Change management moments (new parent org, new resources, new priorities) create openness to trying new tools and partnerships that wouldn't be considered during stable operational periods.
