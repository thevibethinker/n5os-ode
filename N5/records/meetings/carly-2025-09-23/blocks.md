### PRODUCT_IDEA_EXTRACTION
---
**Feedback**: [Useful/Not Useful]
---
*   **Idea**: Weighted Job Description Builder
    *   **Description**: Hiring managers can "dial up and down" different requirements (agency, direct experience, CPA, order-to-cash expertise, etc.) to see candidate trade-offs and compose realistic shortlists instead of chasing "purple unicorns."
    *   **Rationale**: Solves the gap between garbage JDs and what hiring managers actually need. Shows vectors of compromise (pricey vs. affordable, direct vs. transferable experience, high risk vs. low risk).
    *   **Source**: "Being able to kind of dial up and down different parts of the job description would be amazing" (Carly, 09:57) + Logan's "dials" concept (Vrijen, 10:04-10:54)
    *   **Confidence**: High – Carly validated this solves her lived pain

*   **Idea**: Hyperlinked Candidate Narratives
    *   **Description**: One paragraph per candidate with inline hyperlinks to specific resume sections/stories that prove each claim (e.g., click "change management" → jumps to teaching/rubrics story).
    *   **Rationale**: Hiring managers will spend "two minutes" on better data vs. "single digit seconds" on resumes. Validates AI claims with evidence.
    *   **Source**: "I would love nothing more than a short paragraph with the hyperlinks to the places in the materials that prove the points" (Carly, 14:02-14:24)
    *   **Confidence**: High – directly stated as "nirvana"

*   **Idea**: Job Description BS Detector (for candidates)
    *   **Description**: Scan JDs for red flags (unrealistic expectations, no compensation info, no success metrics) and generate interview questions to dig into those gaps.
    *   **Rationale**: Inverse of candidate BS detector – builds trust with job seekers, generates viral content, positions Careerspan as candidate advocate.
    *   **Source**: Demo of Coke Order-to-Cash role analysis (20:30-22:22); Carly loved it ("I love it" @ 22:47)
    *   **Confidence**: Medium-High – Carly suggested it's useful even for JD writers, not just candidates

*   **Idea**: Dynamic Hiring Rhythm (Improvement Signal)
    *   **Description**: Candidates can see week-over-week how they're improving their fit score (60 → 80) in response to a specific job posting. Signals motivation/agency to hiring managers.
    *   **Rationale**: "Wouldn't you want to know that this person put in an hour and a half of self-reflecting to match you?" Unique signal no other platform offers.
    *   **Source**: Vrijen @ 15:16-16:21; Carly responded "Yes."
    *   **Confidence**: Medium – interesting differentiation, implementation TBD
