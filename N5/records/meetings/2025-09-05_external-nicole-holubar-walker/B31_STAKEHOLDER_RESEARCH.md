# B31_STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

**Perspective:** Speaking as an alumni-engagement leader at a major university (Emory)

### Insight 1 — Targeted cohort pilots are operationally preferable
- Evidence: Nicole recommended piloting with Wise Heart donors / young alumni and mentioned budget cycles and FY timing.
- Why it matters: Smaller, high-engagement cohorts produce clearer signals and higher response rates; easier to justify budget.
- Signal strength: ●●●●○

### Insight 2 — Geography (Atlanta) provides practical sourcing and employer relationship advantages
- Evidence: Discussion highlighted Atlanta as largest external market after Atlanta and the practicality of ground relationships.
- Why it matters: Local employer networks improve odds of private, off-market opportunities and make employer outreach easier.
- Signal strength: ●●●○○

### Insight 3 — Alumni channels favor curated, relationship-first activations (reverse career fairs, founder mixers)
- Evidence: Multiple mentions of reverse career fair and founder-curated mixers as potential high-value events.
- Why it matters: Events that surface curated talent and invite employer participation can accelerate placements and strengthen alumni ties.
- Signal strength: ●●●○○

### Insight 4 — Internal restructuring affects outreach channels and decision velocity
- Evidence: Nicole referenced division restructuring and role movement in donor relations.
- Why it matters: Changes in org structure can slow approvals and require re-engagement to re-establish contacts.
- Signal strength: ●●○○○
