**Perspective:** Internal product leadership discussing product/monetization trade-offs

### Key Insights (3)

1. Unit economics for job distribution are fragile at scale
   - Evidence: Ilse\'s cost examples and spreadsheet discussion (multiple cost scenarios)
   - Why it matters: Without automation or improved filters, scaling distribution will increase costs linearly and risk runway
   - Signal strength: ● ● ● ● ○

2. Narrative prep is a defensible differentiation
   - Evidence: multiple references to stories unlocking more user value and improving application outcomes
   - Why it matters: Differentiation enables Careerspan to compete vs. aggregation or ATS by delivering unique user lift
   - Signal strength: ● ● ● ○ ○

3. Short-term execution trade-offs (speed > long-term reliability) are deliberate
   - Evidence: Decision to use Framer, Upwork, and lighter engineering gates for quick wins
   - Why it matters: Accelerates product velocity but requires a roadmap to migrate to more robust infrastructure (natural DB) to lower cost later
   - Signal strength: ● ● ● ○ ○

---

**Implication:** Prioritize two parallel tracks — (1) immediate execution to capture user momentum (site, content, testimonials) and (2) engineering work to reduce per-delivery cost (filters, DB migration, automation).
