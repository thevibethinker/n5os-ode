### Idea 1: Comprehensive System Approach to Workflow Automation

**Description:** Build a "well-bounded system" for workflow management rather than discrete independent workflows. System should handle context and state management across interconnected, non-sequential workflows.

**Rationale:** Solves the fundamental problem that discrete workflow automation (Zapier-style) fails for knowledge workers because their work is inherently interconnected - "our workflows are interrelated" and "workflows are not sequential."

**Source:** Vrijen (06:33) - "What I essentially set out to do with Zoe was to design a comprehensive, like a well-bounded sort of system as opposed to individual workflows."

**Confidence:** High (this is the core Zo value prop that resonated with Gabi)

### Idea 2: Context and State Management as First-Class Features

**Description:** Design workflow tools explicitly around context preservation and state tracking rather than task execution. Focus on answering "how much context am I holding in my head?" and "what is the state of all the different bits of information I need?"

**Rationale:** Addresses root cause of founder/VC productivity challenges - mental overhead of tracking status across many parallel initiatives, not just automating individual tasks.

**Source:** Vrijen (05:52-06:33) - "For founders and VCs the problem is context and state."

**Confidence:** High (Vrijen positioned this as differentiated insight; Gabi didn't push back)

### Idea 3: Non-Linear Workflow Visual Representation

**Description:** Create workflow visualization that shows branching, looping, and context-switching patterns rather than linear step-by-step diagrams. Make the "all in my brain" mental model externally visible.

**Rationale:** Would help users who struggle to map their workflows for automation because traditional linear diagrams don't match their actual work patterns.

**Source:** Implied by Gabi's description (05:35) - "I'm doing this and then it goes here and maybe here and then it comes back here and then there"

**Confidence:** Medium (product idea inferred from pain point; not explicitly discussed as solution)

### Idea 4: AI-Assisted Workflow Discovery

**Description:** Tool that helps users identify which workflows to automate by observing/analyzing their work patterns, rather than requiring users to arrive with pre-defined process maps.

**Rationale:** Solves Gabi's blocker: "I have not been able to really figure out yet where would I automate the best workflows." Users shouldn't need to solve the structuring problem before they can get automation help.

**Source:** Gabi (04:13) explaining why she hasn't used Zapier despite seeing value

**Confidence:** Medium (addresses stated pain point but solution approach wasn't discussed; speculative)
