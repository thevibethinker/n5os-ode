## Executive Summary

The team has decided to fundamentally shift our Q4 go-to-market strategy from passive inbound recruiting to proactive community sourcing. We will position Careerspan as a "virtual in-house recruiter" for founders, delivering 10-20 highly vetted candidate bundles sourced from trusted professional communities. This change addresses persistent inbound quality issues and aims to combine the speed of traditional recruiting with the quality of referrals.

**Key Decision:** Focus on company clients (not universities) for immediate revenue velocity.

**Timeline:** Pilot framework to be outlined by end of week, with execution beginning early next week.

---

## Strategic Context

### Problem Statement
Current inbound recruiting model produces inconsistent candidate quality despite reasonable volume. This creates operational overhead and undermines client trust.

### Proposed Solution
Shift to curated candidate bundles (10-20 per role) sourced from high-trust professional communities:
- YC alumni groups
- Industry-specific Slack channels
- Technical professional networks

### Positioning
Frame Careerspan as "virtual in-house recruiter" rather than transactional recruitment service - embedded strategic talent partner for founders.

## Key Decisions Made

1. **GTM Focus:** Target companies first (defer universities)
   - Rationale: Faster monetization, clearer ROI
   - Impact: Concentrates Q4 sales efforts on B2B revenue

2. **Sourcing Model:** Community-based candidate curation
   - Rationale: Addresses quality issues, leverages network trust
   - Impact: Requires new community partnership capabilities

3. **Division of Labor:** Specialized swimlanes
   - Logan: Technical communities
   - Vrijen: Business/founder networks
   - Rationale: Prevents operational overlap while maintaining focus

4. **Pilot Framework:** Three-step validation
   - Step 1: Identify 3 high-yield communities
   - Step 2: Create standardized role pipeline briefs
   - Step 3: Deliver 10-20 candidate bundles with SLA
