# B31_STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

---

**Perspective:** Insights derived from a founder/advisor active in talent, community, and product spaces (Shujaat Ahmad).

### Insight 1 — Soft skills remain a primary pain point
- **Evidence (quote):** "Soft skill is where the biggest struggle has been for across all generations." (06:45)
- **Implication:** Market demand likely centers on scaffolded, practice-based soft-skill training rather than purely technical upskilling.
- **Signal strength:** ● ● ● ● ○
- **Strategic value:** Prioritize product features and content that build communication, adaptability, and learning-how-to-learn.

### Insight 2 — Community channels amplify product adoption
- **Evidence:** Shujaat emphasizes community distribution and the role of community leaders as validators (35:38–35:52).
- **Implication:** Early traction will be faster via engaged product communities (PM networks) than broad advertising.
- **Signal strength:** ● ● ● ● ○
- **Strategic value:** Invest in community outreach and champion programs for PM cohorts.

### Insight 3 — Practical, research-backed content increases credibility
- **Evidence:** Repeated references to academic frameworks (self-determination theory) and translating them into practice (18:38–22:19).
- **Implication:** Evidence-backed frameworks (short research summaries, micro-courses) help differentiate and persuade partners/advisors.
- **Signal strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○

### Insight 4 — Expectation clarity for advisors is critical
- **Evidence:** Conversation about skin-in-the-game and clear deliverables (31:04–33:11).
- **Implication:** When engaging advisors or community leaders, define explicit expectations (leads/month, advisory scope) to avoid mismatch.
- **Signal strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○

**Actionable:** Use these insights to refine pilot design (focus on PMs via community champions, include short evidence-backed content, and predefine advisor engagement tiers).
