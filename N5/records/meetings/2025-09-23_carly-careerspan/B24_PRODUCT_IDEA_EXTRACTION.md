### Idea 1: Adaptive Requirement Dials / Trade-Off Toggles

**Description:** Allow hiring managers to adjust relative importance of job requirements with visual sliders/dials. After shortlisting candidates, system shows vectors of compromise (e.g., "these candidates are affordable but less experienced; these are experienced but pricey") and lets manager dial up/down dimensions.

**Rationale:** Job descriptions list requirements as binary (have/don't have), but reality is continuous (more/less). Hiring managers know they can't get everything—they want explicit control over trade-offs rather than hidden algorithmic filtering.

**Source:** Carly - "Being able to kind of dial up and down different [components] of the job description would be amazing" (~10:04) + Logan's concept of "dials where you could say, hey, how much agency do I want in someone?"

**Confidence:** High - Explicitly requested by Carly in response to her lived pain point (order-to-cash VP purple unicorn). Logan already has design concept. Clear problem-solution fit.

### Idea 2: Narrative Candidate Summary with Hyperlinked Evidence

**Description:** Present each candidate as 1 short paragraph (ChatGPT-style) with inline hyperlinks to resume sections/stories that prove each claim. E.g., "Drawing a connection between teaching and change management" → click to see the specific story.

**Rationale:** Hiring managers will spend MORE time per candidate if data is more useful—problem isn't speed, it's signal quality. Narrative format with evidence links gives depth without requiring resume scanning.

**Source:** Carly - "I would love nothing more...than a short paragraph...with the hyperlinks to the places in the materials that they've shared that prove the points like ChatGPT...that would be nirvana" (~14:13-14:21)

**Confidence:** High - Explicitly validated as "nirvana" format. Carly described exact implementation details (hyperlinks like ChatGPT), not just vague concept. Already aligns with Careerspan's core positioning.

### Idea 3: Engagement Signal as Differentiator

**Description:** Surface candidate engagement/motivation as explicit signal. E.g., "This candidate improved their score from 60 to 80 in the 3 days after you posted this role—they spent 1.5 hours filling out stories in response to your job."

**Rationale:** Traditional ATS can't show motivation/agency signals. Careerspan's dynamic system lets candidates respond to jobs over time, creating unique signal about who's genuinely interested vs. mass-applying.

**Source:** Vrijen pitched concept, Carly responded "Yes" immediately when asked if she'd want to know about score improvement (~16:21)

**Confidence:** Medium - Carly validated the concept, but didn't elaborate or push back. Could be "sounds nice" vs. "I'd actually use this." Needs more validation with other hiring managers.

### Idea 4: BS Detector / Consistency Checker (Visible in UI)

**Description:** Surface "fluffy vs. detailed" story analysis in candidate profiles. Show inconsistencies across resume, stories, and self-reported skills. E.g., "Claims project management experience but provides no specific examples with metrics or outcomes."

**Rationale:** Hiring managers default to skepticism about candidate claims. If Careerspan can't demonstrate BS detection, they won't trust any of the analysis. Credibility mechanisms must be visible and legible, not hidden in backend.

**Source:** Carly - "How are we checking for bullshit?" (~16:24) + Vrijen's mention of consistency checker in development

**Confidence:** High - Carly's question was blunt and direct, signaling this is a foundational requirement not a nice-to-have. Lack of visible BS detection is a trust barrier that blocks adoption.

---

### Idea 5: Transparent Methodology for Subjective Metrics

**Description:** For any subjective/opaque metric (e.g., "uniqueness"), provide 1-click access to methodology explanation. Tooltip or help doc that explains: "Uniqueness measures transferability of experience based on [specific formula]. Higher score = more off-the-beaten-path background."

**Rationale:** Corporate hiring managers are skeptics—any metric that feels like "black box AI" will be dismissed. Perception of credibility matters more than technical accuracy. Transparent methodology builds trust even if simplified.

**Source:** Carly - "That one's, that one is likely to get caught in the sniff test" (~04:29, re: uniqueness metric)

**Confidence:** High - Direct warning about credibility risk. Carly is signaling this will be a blocker for adoption if not addressed. Similar feedback likely from other corporate TA professionals.

### Idea 6: "Meal Palette" Pipeline Composition

**Description:** Allow hiring managers to compose ideal candidate pipeline by mixing communities/sources. E.g., "Give me 30% ex-Googlers, 20% Peace Corps, 50% from bootcamps" or "Attribute 10 credits to veterans group, 5 to career changers network."

**Rationale:** New signifiers beyond "what university did you go to"—professional associations, communities as talent sources. Hiring managers want off-the-beaten-path candidates but don't know how to source them.

**Source:** Vrijen pitched concept (~07:31-08:36), Carly responded with order-to-cash analogy about "multiple levers I could pull"

**Confidence:** Low-Medium - Carly engaged with the concept but didn't strongly validate it. Analogy was about requirement trade-offs (Idea #1), not community sourcing. Needs more validation about whether hiring managers actually think this way.
