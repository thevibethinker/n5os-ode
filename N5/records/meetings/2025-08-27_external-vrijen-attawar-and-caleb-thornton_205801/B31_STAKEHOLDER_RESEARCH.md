**Perspective:** Speaking as an event organizer / investor convenor (Seed to Table)

### Insights

1. High-concentration investor events can materially accelerate funding outcomes for early-stage startups (claimed 60% conversion within 6–8 months).  
   - Implication: Events are a high-leverage GTM channel for fundraising and lead generation.  
   - Strategic value: Prioritize quick, high-quality investor exposure over broader but lower-intent channels.

2. Sponsorship-driven economics reduce founder cost to participate (sponsors cover majority of fees; sponsors also provide promotion and travel vouchers).  
   - Implication: Sponsor relationships are a primary enabler for founder attendance and can be negotiated to reduce net cash needs.  
   - Strategic value: Invest time in sponsor-aligned pitch materials (one-minute video, photos, sponsor-targeted summaries).

3. Sector specialization matters (events are sector-specific: AI, cleantech, govtech, gaming, life science).  
   - Implication: Select events aligned with Careerspan’s signal (AI event is a good fit).  
   - Strategic value: Prioritize sector-fit events where investor audience interest aligns with product narrative.

4. Event assets (1-minute video, photos, lead lists) are part of the productized offering and become marketing/PR assets post-event.  
   - Implication: Ensure Careerspan provides concise, media-friendly content and a clear one-minute pitch.  
   - Strategic value: Use post-event assets for fundraising and marketing amplification.
