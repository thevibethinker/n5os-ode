**Perspective:** Speaking as a product leader with experience in energy/efficiency and product-market GTM

### Insight 1 — Startups/scale-ups are the highest-opportunity buyer segment
- Evidence: Rajesh recommends steering clear of Fortune 500; cites urgency and willingness to pay (25:32)
- Why it matters: Prioritize GTM efforts toward scale-ups where hiring urgency yields faster adoption and clearer ROI
- Signal strength: ● ● ● ● ○
- Category: GTM & Distribution

### Insight 2 — Agent model (ongoing check-ins) differentiates consumer offering
- Evidence: Rajesh imagines weekly calls with an agent and values the ongoing relationship (16:33)
- Why it matters: A subscription/agent model can create sustained candidate loyalty and recurring revenue
- Signal strength: ● ● ● ○ ○
- Category: Product Strategy

### Insight 3 — Voice input and quick voice-to-job capture is a high-value UX
- Evidence: Rajesh: "...do a voice memo and just text it to me and I'll get it into our platform" (15:22–15:32)
- Why it matters: Lower-friction employer input (voice memo) can unlock faster job ingestion and higher employer participation
- Signal strength: ● ● ● ○ ○
- Category: Product Strategy / GTM

### Insight 4 — Monetization needs dual strategy: employers (placement fees) + consumer options
- Evidence: Conversation explores employer-side monetization and suggested consumer subscription (23:45–24:09)
- Why it matters: Relying solely on placement fees leaves many users unmonetized; consumer subscriptions or agent subscriptions provide diversification
- Signal strength: ● ● ○ ○ ○
- Category: Business Model
