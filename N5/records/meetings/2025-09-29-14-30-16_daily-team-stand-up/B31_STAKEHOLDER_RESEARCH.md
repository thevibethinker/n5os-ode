**Perspective:** Internal team (product development philosophy)

### Product Development Insights

1. **Hold off on speculative UX until validated by customer traction**
   - Evidence: "I don't want to build a whole new thing until we're, like, ready for it... I don't want to, like, go back to putting a whole dashboard that we scrapped a few minutes later."
   - Why it matters: Prevents engineering waste from building features that get immediately rolled back; discipline to wait for data beats intuition-driven development
   - Signal strength: ● ● ● ○ ○
   - Category: Product Strategy
   - Source: Internal team learning from past experience

2. **Product simplification through removal is as valuable as building new features**
   - Evidence: "It gets rid of a bunch of stuff that's just constantly having to be considered, even though it's, like, not part of the current vision... focusing on, like, one core thing"
   - Why it matters: Old features create ongoing maintenance burden and distraction; removing technical debt from outdated vision frees engineering capacity for current priorities
   - Signal strength: ● ● ● ○ ○
   - Category: Product Strategy
   - Source: Engineering perspective (Danny)

3. **Magic link user flow changes dashboard requirements**
   - Evidence: "If our expectation is most people are coming in from Magic Links, then that makes even less sense [to have a dashboard]"
   - Why it matters: User entry point fundamentally changes what initial experience should be; magic links imply direct-to-feature flow rather than dashboard-first navigation
   - Signal strength: ● ● ○ ○ ○
   - Category: Product Strategy
   - Source: User flow analysis (Ilse)

4. **Founder burnout requires proactive sabbatical, not pushing through**
   - Evidence: "I myself am sort of realizing my output is, like, way lower than it needs to be, and it's just because of burnout... I think it's something deeper"
   - Why it matters: Models healthy leadership behavior; demonstrates that output quality matters more than constant presence; prevents burnout from becoming crisis
   - Signal strength: ● ● ● ● ○
   - Category: Founder Health / Team Culture
   - Source: V's self-awareness and team's supportive response

**Note:** These are internal strategic learnings rather than external market intelligence. For true B31 landscape insights, external stakeholder meetings are more appropriate sources.
