# Decisions Made

Meeting between Vrijen Attawar (Careerspan) and Usha Srinivasan (BeReady)
Date: 2025-10-10

## Strategic Decisions

### 1. Careerspan: Paused Bridge Round to Focus on Go-to-Market
- **Decision:** Temporarily postponed bridge round fundraising to concentrate on GTM execution and revenue generation
- **Decided by:** Vrijen Attawar / Careerspan leadership
- **Timing:** Recent (current state)
- **Rationale:** Need to demonstrate business model viability and traction before approaching investors again
- **Impact:** 
  - Positive: Allows focus on core business execution and validation
  - Risk: Runway considerations, but have existing family office support
  - Timeline: Plan to resume fundraising in ~3 months
- **Category:** Strategic / Resource Allocation

### 2. Careerspan: Business Model Evolution (Scan + Intro → Placement Fee)
- **Decision:** Bootstrap toward per-placement model by first charging for database scans or warm intros, rather than going directly to placement fees
- **Decided by:** Vrijen Attawar / Careerspan team
- **Rationale:** Need to build trust in matching system before employers will pay per-placement; fixed-fee scanning or intro fees provide easier entry point
- **Impact:**
  - Lower initial barrier to entry for employers
  - Allows validation of matching quality before higher-stakes placement fees
  - Builds case studies and proof points
- **Category:** Business Model / GTM Strategy

### 3. Careerspan: Target Company Size (≤3 Person Talent Teams)
- **Decision:** Focus on companies with talent teams of three people or under
- **Decided by:** Vrijen Attawar / Careerspan team
- **Rationale:** These companies need talent but lack resources for extensive recruiting; Careerspan can serve as "virtual in-house recruiter"
- **Impact:**
  - Clear ICP definition
  - Positions Careerspan as recruiter replacement/augmentation
  - May limit market size but increases value proposition clarity
- **Category:** Market Segmentation / GTM Strategy

### 4. Careerspan: Geographic Focus (Boston + New York)
- **Decision:** Focus initially on Boston and New York markets
- **Decided by:** Vrijen Attawar / Careerspan team
- **Rationale:** Founder presence in these locations (Vrijen in Brooklyn, co-founder in Boston)
- **Impact:**
  - Enables more direct engagement and relationship building
  - Concentrates resources in high-density tech markets
  - Limits initial geographic expansion
- **Category:** Market Segmentation / GTM Strategy

### 5. Careerspan: Role Focus (Engineering, Product, Growth)
- **Decision:** Niche down to engineer, product, and growth roles on candidate side
- **Decided by:** Vrijen Attawar / Careerspan team
- **Rationale:**
  - High-value, high-paying roles (good revenue potential per placement)
  - Product and growth roles are qualitative—lack good evaluation systems (differentiation opportunity)
  - Engineering roles offer volume/scale potential
- **Impact:**
  - Clear positioning and messaging
  - Can build specialized evaluation frameworks
  - May exclude other valuable role types
- **Category:** Product / Market Segmentation

### 6. BeReady: Maintain Not-for-Profit Model
- **Decision:** Continue operating as not-for-profit social enterprise rather than pursuing VC track
- **Decided by:** Usha Srinivasan
- **Rationale:** Mission-driven goal to help immigrants get jobs and settle in Canada; "giving back to the community"; personal immigrant experience informs mission
- **Impact:**
  - Mission alignment over profit maximization
  - Access to grants, IP funding from Canadian government
  - Different growth trajectory than VC-backed competitors
  - May limit scaling velocity but maintains mission purity
- **Category:** Strategic / Organizational Structure

### 7. BeReady: Focus on Soft Skills Over Technical Training
- **Decision:** Concentrate on soft skills, interview preparation, business etiquette, and language support rather than technical skills training
- **Decided by:** Usha Srinivasan
- **Rationale:** "They already have the technical skills" — immigrants have qualifications but struggle with cultural adaptation, interview skills, language challenges
- **Impact:**
  - Clear differentiation from technical bootcamps and training programs
  - Addresses real pain point for immigrant talent
  - Complements Careerspan's matching focus
- **Category:** Product Strategy

### 8. BeReady: Spun Out from University Setting
- **Decision:** Left university setting to operate independently
- **Decided by:** Usha Srinivasan
- **Timing:** Recent (just completed)
- **Rationale:** "Only so much we could do" within university constraints; need more flexibility to scale and serve broader population
- **Impact:**
  - Greater operational flexibility
  - Trade-off: Lost institutional support and credibility
  - Now "employee of one" with contractors
  - Opens door to new partnerships (like potential Careerspan collaboration)
- **Category:** Organizational / Strategic

## Tactical Decisions

### 9. Vrijen: Openness to White-Label Collaboration
- **Decision:** Expressed willingness to white-label Careerspan's platform for BeReady
- **Decided by:** Vrijen Attawar
- **Rationale:** "We can very much so [white label]. That's not an issue at all."
- **Impact:**
  - Opens partnership opportunity
  - Could provide revenue channel and validation
  - Raises questions about data ownership and user relationship management
- **Category:** Partnership / Business Development

### 10. Usha: Need Time to Evaluate Collaboration
- **Decision:** Will take time to consider whether to partner with Careerspan or continue with current technical development
- **Decided by:** Usha Srinivasan
- **Rationale:** Just started independent operations; already working with developers who built initial platform; need to evaluate strategic fit
- **Impact:**
  - No immediate commitment
  - Opens evaluation period for both parties
  - Preserves optionality
- **Category:** Partnership Evaluation

## Pending Decisions (Discussed but Not Decided)

- **Quote:** "All of the data rights are centered in the individual, but that also constrains us into working with them directly in some capacity"
- **Impact on Partnership:** Needs to be worked through if white-label arrangement moves forward

### Specific Collaboration Structure
- **Status:** Multiple models discussed but none selected
- **Options Raised:**

  - Shared candidate pool
  - Geographic specialization (Careerspan US, BeReady Canada)

- **Next Step:** Requires follow-up conversation after Usha evaluates trial

## Decision Themes

1. **Convergent Thinking:** Both organizations reached similar conclusions about problem (fair hiring, whole-person evaluation) and solution approach (guided self-reflection, algorithmic matching, anonymized profiles)

2. **Values Tension:** Both express concern about elitism in hiring systems; trying to balance revenue generation with equity goals

3. **Geographic Complementarity:** Careerspan (US) and BeReady (Canada) serve different markets, reducing direct competition

4. **Mission vs. Model Difference:** Shared mission but different organizational models (VC-backed for-profit vs. not-for-profit social enterprise)
