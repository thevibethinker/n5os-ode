## Key Decisions and Agreements

• **Pivot employer outreach to "candidate cards" approach** — Alex recommended creating digestible "playing card" style candidate summaries (picture, 2-3 experience points, 2-3 story snippets) delivered via trackable URL bundles. This breaks the mold of resume-heavy recruiter outreach and provides instant value to hiring managers.

• **Target hiring managers directly, not HR/talent teams** — Rationale: HR likes traditional resume review processes (job security), but busy team leads/engineering managers will respond better to human-centric, pre-filtered candidate presentations. Hiring managers have authority to skip ATS anyway.

• **Implement "goods up front" sales approach** — Send candidate bundles immediately without building relationship first. Alex noted recruiters waste time trying to establish rapport before showing candidates, which is annoying. Lead with value: "Here are candidates, do you want to pay for them?"

• **Use URL hosting for engagement metrics** — Track click-through rates, profiles browsed, time spent to optimize outreach strategy. Each URL is unique per employer to gather performance data.

• **Categorize candidates thematically, not percentage fit scores** — Sections like "Clear Fits" (on-paper matches) and "Hidden Gems" (career transitioners, atypical profiles) feel more curated and human than algorithmic percentage scores.

## Strategic Context

**Product Positioning**: Careerspan has shifted from selling behavioral insights (too high burden of proof, buyers jaded) to a staffing play: "If we get good candidates in and introduce them to employers, we make money." This is more objective and testable.

**Current GTM**: Targeting product management roles in NYC/SF/Boston. Using "magic link" system (candidates apply through Careerspan), embedding in communities, reaching out to high-quality candidates directly. Employers agreed to $300-400 per warm intro, contingent on seeing quality.

**Product Strength**: Alex praised the coaching interface simplicity and story accumulation mechanic (candidates tell stories to fill gaps, stories persist across applications). Noted this creates defensible candidate data that traditional recruiters can't replicate. The nuanced chatbot implementation (speeds up/slows down based on response quality) impressed technical users.

**Hiring Manager Pain Point**: Resume review is "mind-numbingly boring" and time-consuming. Product managers especially have varied backgrounds (career transitioners, IC-to-PM pivots), making atypical profiles harder to evaluate. Careerspan's story-based insights surface "what the resume misses."

Vrijen acknowledged need to be "front-footed" after previous insecurity about product quality—now has data showing strong engagement metrics.
