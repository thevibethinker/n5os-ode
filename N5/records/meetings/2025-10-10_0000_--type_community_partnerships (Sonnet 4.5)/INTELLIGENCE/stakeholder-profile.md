## Product & Approach

### Platform Mechanics
- **Candidate intake:** Resume upload + 1-minute video (critical thinking question) + 500-word pitch
- **No job applications:** Candidates complete profile but don't apply for specific jobs
- **Matching:** Algorithm searches employer job descriptions against candidate profiles
- **Matching output:** Green/yellow/red percent match system
- **Privacy:** Names scrubbed from profiles before employer view
- **Employer action:** "Click to interview" directly from platform
- **Accept/decline tracking:** Platform manages employer response workflow

### Strategic Focus

**Candidate Side:**
- **Target population:** Immigrants (express entry visa, international students, startup visa holders)
- **Value proposition:** Soft skills development, not technical training
  - Interview preparation
  - Business etiquette coaching
  - Language support
  - Cultural adaptation
- **Explicit rejection of technical training:** "They already have the technical skills. What we focus on more on is their soft skills."

**Employer Side:**
- **Target:** SMEs under 200 employees (not just startups)
- **Breadth:** Manufacturing, food processing, various industries
- **Role types:** IT, marketing, various functional roles (broader than niche tech roles)
- **Excluded:** Union roles, fractional roles
- **Value proposition:** Access to qualified immigrant talent often overlooked by traditional hiring

### Key Insight on Immigrant Behavior
> "When they're new to the country, they apply to large companies that's the brand that they see and they feel like, oh it's a prestige thing, you know, like I've arrived, I have landed a job in Google big bank or something like that. And so it's a perception change for them to actually think about small businesses as an opportunity."

**Usha's role:** Helping immigrants see SMEs as valuable career opportunities while helping SMEs access this talent pool.
