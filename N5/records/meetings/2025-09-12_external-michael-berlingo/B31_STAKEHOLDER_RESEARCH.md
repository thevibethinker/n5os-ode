**Perspective:** Speaking as a founder building distribution into universities/high schools

### Key Insights

1. University procurement and budget constraints are a primary adoption barrier
   - Evidence: Michael contrasts colleges vs. high schools (11:54–12:36) and notes career services "have no money"
   - Why it matters: Free/low-friction offerings and event-driven co-promotion are stronger early levers than direct sales
   - Signal strength: ● ● ● ● ○

2. Handshake dominates distribution but often fails students (low usefulness despite high cost)
   - Evidence: Michael recounts asking students about Handshake usefulness; none raised hands (10:41–10:55)
   - Why it matters: Opportunity to position Starter U + Careerspan as alternatives that surface real opportunities, not just listings
   - Signal strength: ● ● ● ● ○

3. Community and club-level distribution (international student groups, alumni networks) is a high-leverage, underutilized channel
   - Evidence: Vrijen recommends targeting international student clubs and WhatsApp/Telegram groups (17:34–18:51)
   - Why it matters: Bypasses institutional procurement inertia; can deliver highly engaged, motivated cohorts for early traction
   - Signal strength: ● ● ● ○ ○

**Actionable implication:** Prioritize creating a one-pager and a simple tracking code to enable Starter U to promise measurable outcomes when speaking to schools and community groups.
