# B31_STAKEHOLDER_RESEARCH

---
**Perspective:** Speaking as a community operator / event lead (Alfred Sogja)

### Insight 1 — Private communities are the primary distribution layer
- **Evidence (quote):** "There are a lot of communities out there... start by us individually as members going into communities and dogfooding."  
- **Why it matters:** Private communities concentrate high-signal candidates and enable trusted distribution channels for roles and perks.  
- **Signal strength:** ● ● ● ● ○
- **Strategic value:** Prioritize community-first pilots to validate ambassador incentives and acquisition funnels.

### Insight 2 — Ambassador-stipend model scales outreach effectively
- **Evidence (quote):** Discussion of paid ambassadors (fixed + variable) and activation metrics as a repeatable tactic.  
- **Why it matters:** Incentivized power users can seed engagement and provide social proof; variable pay aligns incentives with activation.  
- **Signal strength:** ● ● ● ○ ○
- **Strategic value:** Build a small pilot (5–10 ambassadors) with clear activation KPIs before scaling.

### Insight 3 — Operational friction from partner coordination is a key blocker
- **Evidence (quote):** Repeated operational issues with partners (missed invites, unclear responsibilities, late deliverables).  
- **Why it matters:** Partner execution risk can undermine community pilots; mitigations needed (clear playbooks, minimal dependencies).  
- **Signal strength:** ● ● ● ● ○
- **Strategic value:** Design minimal-dependency pilots and clear partner SLAs to reduce execution risk.
