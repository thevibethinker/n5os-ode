# PLAN_OF_ACTION

## Title: Employer-Side Playing Card System Rollout (TENTATIVE)

**Confidence Level**: TENTATIVE (general timeline agreed, but execution details and technical approach need scoping)

---

## Immediate (This Week)

**Technical Scoping with Logan**
- Determine build complexity for URL-hosted candidate card system with engagement tracking
- Decide: custom build vs. Gamma auto-generation vs. existing presentation tools
- Expected outcome: Timeline estimate and resource requirements for MVP

**Card Format Workshop**
- Define exact information architecture: picture, name, 2-3 experience bullets, 1-2 story highlights, "Clear Fit" vs. "Hidden Gem" categorization
- Create visual mockups (simple, clean, not corporate/boring)
- Test that stories pull programmatically from existing candidate database
- Expected outcome: Locked card template ready for population

**Generate 3 Candidate Bundles**
- Create URL bundles for NY, Boston, SF product manager roles
- Populate with top candidates from existing database
- Add programmatic "why relevant to you" customization for each job posting
- Expected outcome: 3 testable bundles ready for outreach

---

## Near-Term (2-3 Weeks)

**Direct-to-Hiring-Manager Outreach Test**
- Identify 20-30 product manager job postings with hiring manager contact info (not HR)
- A/B test: fresh postings (<1 week) vs. stale postings (1+ month)
- Send URL bundles with minimal email copy: "Found your posting, here are candidates, click if interested"
- Track: click-through rate, profiles viewed, time spent, response rate
- Expected outcome: Data on conversion rates and optimal timing strategy

**Free vs. Paid First Intro Test**
- Split outreach: half offers first candidate free, half charges from day one
- Measure: which approach gets more responses, which converts to paid relationships
- Expected outcome: Validated pricing approach for cold outreach

**Refine Card Format Based on Feedback**
- Gather qualitative responses from hiring managers who engage
- Iterate on information density, visual style, categorization labels
- Expected outcome: Refined card template with proven engagement

---

## Checkpoint (3-4 Weeks Out)

**Evaluate Results and Decide**
- Review metrics: Did URL bundles outperform traditional recruiter approach?
- Did hiring managers engage more than HR?
- What's the conversion rate from click to conversation?
- Did timing (fresh vs. stale postings) affect outcomes?

**Decision Point**:
- **If strong positive signals**: Scale outreach, build more robust card generation system, potentially hire BD person to run high-volume outreach
- **If mixed signals**: Iterate on card format, test different job roles (engineering vs. PM), refine messaging
- **If weak signals**: Reassess direct-to-hiring-manager assumption, potentially test HR route as backup, or pivot back to community-first GTM

---

## End State

**Repeatable Employer Acquisition System**
- Automated or semi-automated candidate card generation from database
- URL bundle creation pipeline that can be deployed at scale
- Proven outreach messaging that converts hiring managers to paying customers
- Clear pricing model (per-intro or subscription) based on what market responds to
- Steady flow of employer demand that justifies scaling up candidate acquisition

**Success Looks Like**:
- 10+ employers actively receiving candidate bundles per week
- 20-30% click-through rate on URL bundles (benchmark to establish)
- 5-10% conversion from click to paid warm intro
- Employers requesting more candidates (demand signal for scaling candidate side)
- Card-based presentation becomes recognizable Careerspan differentiator vs. traditional recruiters
