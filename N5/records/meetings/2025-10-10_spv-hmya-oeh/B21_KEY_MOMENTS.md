## Memorable Quotes

**1. "We don't want to put more money into a leaky bucket"**
- Who: Logan Currie
- When: Mid-conversation during fundraising discussion
- Context: This captured the core strategic pivot from raising more capital to pursuing exit. Reflects mature assessment that without proven revenue stream, additional funding would be irresponsible. The metaphor stuck as shorthand for the entire strategic shift.

**2. "We are not an AI company. We are a roll up company and we are rolling up recruiting firms."**
- Who: Vrijen Attawar
- When: Presenting alternative positioning strategy
- Context: Bold reframe of entire company narrative - from struggling tech startup to PE-backed industry consolidator. This wasn't just a marketing pivot; it represented fundamentally different capital strategy, investor pool, and acquisition thesis. Logan's response ("That's expensive") showed initial skepticism but willingness to engage.

**3. "Automatable payroll... Is that what we're calling it now?"**
- Who: Logan Currie (reacting to Harvard Business School lecture content)
- When: Early in conversation, discussing AI's impact
- Context: Sardonic commentary on how enterprise software companies are now targeting not just software budgets but "automatable payroll" (i.e., jobs that can be replaced). The phrase disturbed both founders, revealing shared values about technology's social impact even while building in the space.

**4. "It is re-concentrating power in the hands of people that can wield it and have the money"**
- Who: Vrijen Attawar
- When: Discussing AI democratization paradox
- Context: Insight that current LLMs are "pseudo-democratizing" rather than truly democratizing technology. Takes significant effort to wield effectively (as Vrijen experiences with Zo Computer setup), creating temporary advantage for technical sophisticates. This awareness informs their urgency about exit timing.

**5. "That's your ticket. That's my ticket. That's everybody's ticket to something at the next."**
- Who: Logan Currie
- When: Discussing $5M exit scenario payouts
- Context: Making the abstract exit strategy concrete and emotionally resonant. The specific numbers ($1.3M for Vrijen, $600K for Logan, $400K for Ilsa) transformed vague "good outcome" into tangible life-changing payouts. Logan's framing as "tickets" to next chapter created clear motivation.
