**Perspective:** Speaking as a senior product professional and job seeker

### Insight 1 — Recruiter translation gap (Signal: ● ● ● ● ○)
- Evidence: "If the person I'm talking to can't understand what I'm saying... then the translation isn't happening" (06:17).
- Why it matters: Recruiters often lack domain fluency for senior PM roles; candidates suffer in downstream evaluation.
- Strategic value: Careerspan can create recruiter-facing artifacts (scripts, bulletized case narratives) that lower the translation burden.

### Insight 2 — Networking is less organic post-relocation (Signal: ● ● ● ○ ○)
- Evidence: "My network was in Austin and I'm in New York... it's hard to revive connections" (01:05).
- Why it matters: Warm introductions are harder to scale for relocated candidates; community embedding (Careerspan in Slack groups) is valuable.
- Strategic value: Embed Careerspan into targeted communities to surface warm intros and reduce friction.

### Insight 3 — Coaching purchase without explanatory feedback reduces learning (Signal: ● ● ● ○ ○)
- Evidence: "I dropped 5k on a career coach... feedback did not give me context" (15:28–17:26).
- Why it matters: Candidates pay for coaching but often don't get teach-back; there's demand for coaching that builds candidate capability, not just outputs.
- Strategic value: Offer 'teach-the-method' deliverables (voice explanations, incremental exercises) to increase long-term user value and retention.
