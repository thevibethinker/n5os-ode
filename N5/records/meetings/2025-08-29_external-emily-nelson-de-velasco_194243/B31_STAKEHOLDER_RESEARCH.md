**Perspective:** Speaking as a former recruiter turned product founder (operational recruiting experience informing product decisions)

### Insight 1 — AI efficiency creates a zero-sum hiring arms race
- Evidence (quote): "It is an arms race." (03:42)
- Why it matters: Tools that increase candidate efficiency can simultaneously reduce recruiter effectiveness; matching must account for incentives
- Signal strength: ●●●●

### Insight 2 — Integration complexity is a competitive moat for employers
- Evidence (paraphrase): multiple references to integration and productizing reverse recruiting (20:14, 27:37)
- Why it matters: Partners that remove integration tax (data-layer approach) will be preferred by employers and community owners
- Signal strength: ●●●

### Insight 3 — Candidate-first positioning is a durable differentiator
- Evidence (multiple): emphasis on helping candidates be better and storing experiences on Careerspan (06:28, 31:17)
- Why it matters: Products that scaffold reflection and CI (candidate intelligence) win over purely directive resume tools
- Signal strength: ●●●

### Insight 4 — Founder is dogfooding with real clients and an ML engineer
- Evidence (23:56–24:01; 27:37–27:53): Emily is testing with clients and has an ML engineer building product
- Why it matters: Lower technical friction for early integrations and potential for co-developed prototypes/pilots
- Signal strength: ●●●●

**Action:** Use these insights to prioritize technical exploration (APIs, export/import of candidate reflection data) and to craft partnership messaging emphasizing shared candidate-first values.
