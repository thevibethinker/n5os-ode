## Executive Summary

Careerspan will pursue a strategic sale process targeting close by January 2026, with a floor of $5M and upside potential depending on buyer fit. This is a proactive, well-timed move—not a fire sale—that capitalizes on current momentum, defensible tech, and emerging distribution assets while the market is actively consolidating.

**Key Decision:** Shift from fundraising/revenue growth mode to buyer-readiness mode. Optimize for proof points that drive valuation (tech depth + distribution breadth), while managing team transition ethically and maintaining operational stability.

---

## Strategic Rationale

### Why Now?

1. **Market timing:** Enterprise budgets shifting from "software" to "software + labor" (automatable payroll); M&A activity strong in HR tech, background verification, and future-of-work platforms
2. **Competitive positioning:** Current tech stack and community relationships provide unique data moat—harder to replicate if delayed
3. **Founder clarity:** Team has learned significantly; an exit provides capital and optionality for next ventures
4. **Risk management:** Revenue uncertainty creates tension; sale locks value and provides clean reset

### What Makes Us Valuable?

**Tech:**
- Conversational intelligence and verification/consistency systems
- Unique candidate data from coaching interactions
- Proven engagement and retention metrics

**Distribution:**
- Active community partnerships (Aspire, others in pipeline)
- Brand credibility in future-of-work and coaching spaces
- Scalable onboarding framework

**Dual Optionality:** Can sell to deep-pocketed HR/ATS buyers for tech, OR to consumer/work-tech platforms for distribution + data.
