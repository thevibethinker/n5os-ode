## Vrijen's Key Statements

### On Careerspan's Evolution

> "With ChatGPT, when the tech launched, essentially started as a career tech, sort of pure play, built a really great sort of system. We have over 500 monthly active users, over 1000 hours of coaching recorded with Rai, some really solid numbers that indicate a lot of user love. But with how terrible the market is and the pain points we're seeing around AI generated resumes, it dawned on us that really there's only one way for things to go, and we need a new route for people to be considered."

**Context**: Candid narrative about pivot from coaching tool to talent marketplace. Shows strategic adaptability.

---

### On Data Layer Vision

> "Our goal is to eventually just be the data layer for the rest of the jobs in the hiring market. So to the extent that we think our role as an intermediary could actually help activate the latent potential of many different products and platforms, we want to build an Internet of careers around the qualitative data that can be captured through Careerspan."

**Context**: Long-term positioning. This is the "why partner with us" vision that resonates with platform players like FutureFit.

---

### On Qualitative Profiling

> "It gives you what the resume should be in the 21st century, right? It is a sort of aggregated, traceable sort of understanding of who you are. That can then be relaged to help you be more efficient in different parts of the process and more importantly, convey sides of yourself to the employer that don't come through on the resume, but largely drive the failed hires."

**Context**: Core value prop. Addresses the "resume doesn't capture what matters" pain that everyone in this space knows.

---

### On Matching Methodology

> "We've spent essentially two years really learning how to build with non reasoning models, but in a highly deterministic way. And so we have a lot of confidence in our ability to extract the right stuff, very high levels of consistency. Very sort of like clear cut, well prepared data that can be ingested by any platform that just agrees on the data structure with us."

**Context**: Technical credibility signal. Addresses concerns about LLM reliability/consistency in production systems.

---

### On Partnership Openness

> "Totally works for us just to sort of throw that out there. That is something we're totally fine with. I think it's only a question of the right partner and the right deal, but it's something we'd be totally comfortable with."

**Context**: Response to embedded/white-labeled integration model. Shows flexibility without desperation.

---

> "I would love to take this back to the team and sort of sound their opinion out as well, but I think for us, it would be massively beneficial and not at all an issue to, again, for the right partner and the right deal, figure out a way to achieve that level of white labelness, because it's something we consciously acknowledge is both a barrier to uptake for all parties involved, but also adds a needless friction that can be addressed with some concerted effort."

**Context**: Acknowledging team buy-in needed (not solo decision), but signaling strong interest. Balances enthusiasm with realistic process.

## Quotes NOT to Use (Red Flags)

### Vrijen's Potential Overstatement

> "With our sort of basically the only tool in the world that can actually tailor resumes, because we actually make the effort of getting to know the whole you."

**Why it's risky**: "Only tool in the world" is hyperbolic. Hamoon knows the market—competitors exist (Teal, Rezi, etc.). Could undermine credibility. Better framing: "One of the few tools that tailors based on deep qualitative profiling vs. keyword swapping."

### Vrijen's Vision Before Proof

> "Once we're embedded enough, that would just get auto generated."

**Context**: Talking about team personality sync. This is future state, not current capability. Hamoon's body language likely shifted here—he wants present, not roadmap.
