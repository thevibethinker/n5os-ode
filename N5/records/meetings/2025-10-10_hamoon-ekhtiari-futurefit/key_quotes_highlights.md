## Vrijen's Quotes (Internal Reference)

### On Careerspan's Vision
> "Our goal is to eventually just be the data layer for the rest of the jobs and hiring market. So to the extent that we think our role as an intermediary could actually help activate the latent potential of many different products and platforms, we want to build an Internet of careers around the qualitative data that can be captured through Careerspan."

**Context**: Articulating long-term strategy. This resonated with Hamoon—worth repeating in follow-up materials.

---

> "It gives you what the resume should be in the 21st century, right? It is a sort of aggregated, traceable understanding of who you are. That can then be relaged to help you be more efficient in different parts of the process and more importantly, convey sides of yourself to the employer that don't come through on the resume, but largely drive the failed hires."

**Context**: Explaining Careerspan's core value prop. Frames product as infrastructure for modern hiring, not incremental improvement.

---

### On Technical Capability
> "We've spent essentially two years really learning how to build with non-reasoning models, but in a highly deterministic way. And so we have a lot of confidence in our ability to extract the right stuff, very high levels of consistency."

**Context**: Addressing technical rigor. Important credibility signal—not relying on latest models, but on engineered reliability.

---

> "The tool leverages the great power of LLMs to essentially perform data science with concepts and words. And using that to its fullest extent."

**Context**: Describing Careerspan's extraction methodology. Positions LLMs as analytical layer, not generative fluff.

---

### On Partnership Openness
> "Totally works for us just to sort of throw that out there. That is something we're totally fine with. I think it's only a question of the right partner and the right deal, but it's something we'd be totally comfortable with."

**Context**: Responding to embedded integration model. Strong signal of flexibility and pragmatism.

---

> "We had about a dozen or so startups that, in addition to the one we already integrated with, that were keen or are keen on sort of doing some form of an integration. So I think it's really just a matter of incentives."

**Context**: Demonstrating partnership traction without overpromising. Shows demand validation but doesn't name-drop excessively.

---

### On Go-to-Market Evolution
> "With how terrible the market is and the pain points we're seeing around AI generated resumes, it dawned on us that really there's only one way for things to go, and we need a new route for people to be considered."

**Context**: Explaining pivot to talent pool model. Acknowledges market forces driving strategy shift—transparent about external pressures.

---

> "We take employers directly to those communities and act as a snapshot in time of like this is the potential talent pool that Careerspan has for you."

**Context**: Describing community-first GTM. Different from job board model—proactive curation vs. reactive posting.

## Quote Usage Guide

**For external comms (emails, proposals)**:
- Use Hamoon's endorsement quotes (granularity/nuance, interesting/complex)
- Paraphrase partnership philosophy quotes to establish shared values

**For internal morale/updates**:
- Use Hamoon's mission alignment quote ("this world is hard")
- Use his validation of Careerspan's approach

**For investor/board materials**:
- Use FutureFit's scale context (200K users/year)
- Use Vrijen's "21st century resume" framing
- Use "Internet of careers" vision statement

**For product/tech discussions**:
- Use Vrijen's deterministic extraction quote
- Use Hamoon's UX fragmentation learning
- Use embedded integration model description

**Avoid overusing**:
- Acquisition/acqui-hire mentions (premature for public comms)
- "Dozen startups interested" (could sound desperate or unfocused)
- Vision-heavy quotes without current capability anchors
