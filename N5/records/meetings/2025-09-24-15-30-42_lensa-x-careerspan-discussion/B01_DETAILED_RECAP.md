### Key Decisions and Agreements

• **Test Partnership Agreed** - Lensa will run a traffic acquisition test with Careerspan starting October 2025, distributing jobs via XML feeds/API to Careerspan's network in exchange for user registrations back to Lensa
  - **Rationale:** Lensa values fresh job seekers from high-quality networks; Careerspan needs clean job data to distribute to their talent pool
  - **Why it matters:** This gives Careerspan access to 24 million jobs without scraping effort, while Lensa gets access to highly engaged, conscientious job seekers from premium communities

• **Test Parameters Established** - $2,500 per feed starting budget, 15 cent CPC pricing, AppCast master publisher account setup required
  - **Rationale:** Standard test structure for new traffic partners; Lensa will monitor ROI and scale up/down based on performance
  - **Why it matters:** Low-risk test allows both sides to validate value before scaling; clear metrics for success

• **Content Filtering Agreement** - Lensa will provide filtered job categories (product roles initially) focused on Boston/SF/NY markets, expandable to engineering and growth roles within 3 months
  - **Rationale:** Careerspan's V1 matching system is cost-inefficient, currently processing 70-80 jobs/week for 700-1000 activated users
  - **Why it matters:** Prevents Careerspan from processing millions of irrelevant jobs; aligns with their product-first GTM strategy

• **User Flow Design** - Jobs distributed via Careerspan email → users click through → routed to Lensa for registration/application
  - **Rationale:** Keeps user flow friction-free (Lensa already provides jobs for free); allows Careerspan to maintain current email distribution model
  - **Why it matters:** Preserves Careerspan's matching strength (75-80% open rate, 15-16% CTR) while giving Lensa monetizable registrations

They buy traffic through CPC/CPA partnerships, co-registration, and XML feeds. Most partners are incestuous within job board industry. Mai's role is user acquisition - she needs new and returning job seekers to monetize through job alerts.

**Careerspan's Position:** Building talent network through high-signal communities (McKinsey alumni, Zooglers, Ivy League). Offering free consumer product in exchange for rich data beyond resumes. Currently distributing jobs to 700-1000 activated users (20+ minutes coaching engagement minimum), planning to onboard 200-400 users/week starting next week. Currently scraping jobs but finding it labor-intensive to clean JDs.

**Value Exchange:** Careerspan gets clean, categorized job data at scale. Lensa gets access to conscientious, engaged job seekers who return at 2-3% monthly reactivation rate and exhibit high email engagement. Lensa values this because job seekers typically "bounce from one platform to the next" - Careerspan's retention signals quality.
