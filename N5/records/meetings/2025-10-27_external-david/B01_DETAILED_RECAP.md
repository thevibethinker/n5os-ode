## Strategic Context

Careerspan is in classic "extend runway to exit" mode - team size reduced, product is functional but needs proof points. The strategic challenge is creating optionality: acquihire/asset purchase are baseline scenarios, but revenue generation could unlock different valuation models (multiple of revenue vs. just covering investors). David emphasized finding the right strategic buyer who has a specific gap that Careerspan fills - his example was companies like Superposition who have recruiting side but not the marketplace side. The business broker marketplace angle is novel: it surfaces Careerspan to career tech investors who buy/renovate/flip Internet businesses, broadening the buyer pool beyond just strategic acquirers.

The family office investment structure provides flexibility but still creates a floor (need to return capital ideally). David correctly identified this as both a constraint and a negotiating angle - buyers understand covering investor returns as a floor price.
