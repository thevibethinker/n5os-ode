**Why it matters**: Early warning systems require explicit baseline benchmarking, not just subjective "I feel fine" assessments. Founders need proactive detection mechanisms before productivity collapse occurs.

#### 3. Hiring decisions hinge on "seeing someone in the role" moment - not credential verification but visualization/intuition

**Evidence**: "There is a moment where you can, like, see them in the role. Like, it is about, like, being a. There's the moment where you're like, I can see it. Right? And then once you can see it's generally a question of, so how do I, like, justify it? Like, the justification follows the, like, intuitive I. This is, like, passing my smell test."

**Why it matters**: Careerspan's information architecture should optimize for enabling this visualization moment, not just presenting credentials. The "justification follows intuition" sequence means traditional resume format is solving the wrong problem - it justifies but doesn't trigger the initial "I can see it" spark.
