## 💡 Most Valuable Insights

### The AI Arms Race Problem
Emily's thesis: "It is an arms race. We are AI-ing for the sake of AI. Everybody's gaining efficiency, but while job seekers are gaining efficiency, recruiters are losing efficiency... it's totally a zero sum game. Like it's giving us nothing except this hyper competitive market."

**Vrijen's response**: "It's cancerous almost. The market is evolving in one direction and it's completely unproductive and it's doing so more efficiently than ever."

### ATS is Dead
Zapier leadership openly admitted ATS is dead. Fabrication at all-time high. Recruiting teams frustrated because "all the interviews suck." Quality signal completely destroyed.

### The Career Tech Problem
Emily on Teal: "It'll take a job description and be like, oh, your resume would look a lot better if it said you went to the moon... And then I'm like, I've sat in that recruiter chair, right. And I'll be like, so how was it on the moon? And the candidate's gonna be like, the what?"

Both founders: Career tech companies go for "lowest hanging fruit" (resume output) instead of substantive inward work. Tools encourage lying vs genuine improvement.

### "Be Good, Not Look Good"
Emily: "We need real talk. We don't need to like, oh, this is how you look good. No, what we need is this is how you be good. Like, okay, here's a gap... I can either write that you've been to the moon, or I can say, hey, this is a gap. You haven't been to the moon. How can we get you to the moon so that you can achieve your goals?"

This perfectly mirrors Careerspan's gap-filling approach Vrijen demoed.
