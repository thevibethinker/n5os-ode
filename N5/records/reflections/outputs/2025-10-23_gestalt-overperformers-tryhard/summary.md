# Summary

**Date:** 2025-10-23  
**Type:** Product Strategy  
**Source:** 2025-10-23_gestalt-overperformers-tryhard.m4a

## Core Idea

Careerspan should identify and specifically target "overperformers"—people whose actual output/capability exceeds their current job level—as both a value proposition for candidates ("we'll help you advocate for yourself or find better") and a positioning for employers ("find the tryhards, the 10x people you want on your team").

## Key Insights

1. **Overperformer Detection as Product Feature:** If Careerspan can tell someone they're overperforming relative to their role, that's immediate actionable value ("time to ask for a raise" or "get a better job")

2. **Employer Value Prop:** Reframe Careerspan as "how to find the high-agency tryhards"—the people who consistently deliver beyond expectations

3. **Gestalt Hiring Concept:** Evaluate people as "bundles of skills" rather than linear credentials—which bundle best fits the hiring manager's specific context?

4. **Data Point:** Over 50% of Careerspan users appear to be overperforming according to internal metrics → "highest concentration of overperformers on the Internet"

## Strategic Implications

- **Candidate positioning:** "Show all sides of yourself" → attract overperformers who don't fit traditional molds
- **Employer positioning:** "Find the best people" → specifically target high-agency, high-output individuals
- **Potential feature:** Zo as talent agent for identified overperformers
- **Natural fit:** Overperformers often have non-linear careers that require gestalt evaluation (vs. credential scanning)
