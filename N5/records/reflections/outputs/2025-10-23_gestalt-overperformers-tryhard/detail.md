## The Overperformer Value Proposition

### For Candidates

**Core mechanism:** Careerspan can detect when someone is performing above their current job level and proactively tell them:
- "You deserve to advocate for a raise"
- "You're clearly doing too much for this role"
- "We can help you get a better job"

**Why powerful:** Most people don't realize they're undervalued relative to their output. Careerspan surfacing this creates immediate, actionable value.

**Usage extension:** Becomes an ongoing value-add for the platform—"we'll tell you when it's time to level up"

### For Employers

**Core positioning:** "If you want to find the best people, use Careerspan"

**Specific targeting:** The 10x people, the tryhards, the high-agency folks who "show up and deliver"—the ones you want "on your team, on your cap table"

**Insight:** Seniority titles don't necessarily represent what people are actually doing. Careerspan can surface those doing senior-level work at mid-level titles.

## Gestalt Hiring Framework

### Concept

Move from **linear credential evaluation** to **bundle-based assessment**:
- People are a "bundle of skills and experiences"
- The question becomes: "Is this combination of things the best fit for me as the employer/hiring manager?"
- Context matters: what's the right bundle for THIS specific role/team?

### Implementation Challenge

Careerspan needs to:
1. Help users articulate what "meaningful bundles" look like for their context
2. Evaluate candidates as bundles rather than credential checklists
3. Match bundles to specific hiring manager needs

**Open question:** "Which of those bundles is a career?" → how to define what constitutes a coherent career bundle vs. random skills

## The "Highest Concentration of Overperformers" Thesis

### Data Point

"Over 50% of our users are overperforming according to our system"

### Interpretation

This makes sense because:
1. Careerspan attracts people who don't fit traditional molds
2. High-agency people are more likely to seek coaching/career tools
3. Self-selection bias: overperformers are actively working on their careers

### Strategic Implication

**Market positioning:** Careerspan as the place where overperformers congregate → creates network effects for both sides of the marketplace

## Positioning Clarity

### Brand Message Crystallization

"We are for the high-agency tryhards"

**Three angles:**
1. **For job seekers:** "Show the most sides of yourself" (gestalt profile)
2. **For overperformers:** "We'll tell you when you deserve better"
3. **For employers:** "Find the tryhards" (highest-signal candidates)

### Why This Works

- Differentiates from resume-scanning tools
- Appeals to non-linear career paths (PM staffing focus alignment)
- Natural fit with "goods upfront" approach (bundles of candidates)
- Addresses real pain: traditional hiring misses context-dependent excellence

## Open Questions

1. **Bundle definition:** What makes a "meaningful bundle" vs. incoherent skill mix?
2. **Overperformer metrics:** What signals indicate overperformance vs. appropriate leveling?
3. **Gestalt evaluation scale:** Can this work at volume or only for curated warm intros?
4. **Employer education:** Do hiring managers understand "gestalt hiring" or need reframing?
