# B21: Key Quotes & Strategic Questions

## Key Quotes

**"Finding multiple bids is key. Run it as a sales process. Don't get too excited. Be skeptical until something is real."**  
— Ray  
*Context:* Ray was describing his own exit funnel—seeding 30–40 targets and watching the list shrink to one real offer. The reminder to treat sale conversations like a pipeline keeps logouts from settling for the first shiny signal.

**"You got to keep your skeptical hat and keep grinding until it becomes real."**  
— Ray  
*Context:* In the opening riff on inbound interest, Ray stressed that early curiosity feels good but is still a “no” until it becomes a yes. That skepticism is the guardrail between excitement and closing value.

**"If you're backs against the rope and you only have three months left, and you only are down to one person that wants to do a real thing, they're not going to pay you anything, basically because you don't have any better options."**  
— Ray  
*Context:* This came just after V admitted the runway was roughly three months. It frames the urgency: multiple legit bids are the only firewall against lowball leverage in desperate timing.

**"If you get to the point where you do have, you know, the ability to make it something decent out of this from a financial perspective, then just know that you can get very creative with how compensation sort of, and transactions can be structured."**  
— Ray  
*Context:* Ray was explaining how earnouts, revenue shares, and milestone-based deals can stretch the payout even when the upfront cash is modest. It nudges V to model what a conservative and optimistic deal look like.

**"The main thing is can we get obviously, the optics and the boost of being exited down there, that's most important. Actually, most important is, I think, paying back family and friends, then that, then the investor."**  
— Vrijen Attawar  
*Context:* V was clarifying the personal order of stakes—priority one is family/friends, then investors, with the exit optics and opportunity as the final payoff. It grounds the deal sweetener beyond valuation alone.

## Strategic Questions Raised

**"Would Calendly have any particular need for the tech, or would it be a pure talent thing?"**  
— Ray  
*Significance:* This question forces V (and the team) to map each buyer to a thesis—talent-only, mission alignment, or license opportunity—so Ray can coach them on tailoring the pitch and diligence accordingly.

**"What are you kind of optimizing for? Do you know that yet or not yet?"**  
— Ray  
*Significance:* Ray used this to draw out whether V was chasing optics, cash, or personal freedom. The follow-up clarified that V is willing to accept a PR exit if it opens meaningful individual off-ramps.

**"Are you familiar with kind of the difference between sort of asset purchases versus..."**  
— Ray  
*Significance:* His prompt about exit structures signals there are different tax, liability, and integration implications depending on whether the deal is an asset sale or share/stock change. It triggered the suggestion to call a lawyer before term sheets.
