Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025-11-29

1. Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how the personal "Personal Workout Tracker (Zo)" application ("the App") handles your information. The App is a private, non-commercial tool that runs on your own Zo Computer server. It is used solely by you to track workouts and related health metrics for personal purposes.

2. Data Collected

The App may store the following types of information:

  • Workout details (date, time, duration, distance, modality, notes).
  • Qualitative check-ins (such as whether you wanted to work out, whether you did, and whether you regretted it).
  • Fitness and activity data retrieved from Fitbit (for example: activity logs, distance, duration, calories, and heart-rate related metrics).
  • Technical metadata needed to communicate with the Fitbit Web API (for example: OAuth tokens and scopes).

All data is stored in a SQLite database and configuration files on your Zo Computer server. No commercial third-party operates this database on your behalf.

3. Data Sources

The App may receive data from:

  • Manual entries you provide (for example, via text input or notes).
  • Images or screenshots you upload (for example, treadmill console photos) which may be parsed into structured data.
  • The official Fitbit Web API, when you authorize the App to access your Fitbit account.

4. How Data Is Used

Your data is used only to:

  • Record and display workouts and related health metrics to you.
  • Derive basic statistics and trends about your activity (for example, volume over time or changes in performance).
  • Reconcile data from multiple sources (for example, Fitbit vs treadmill readings) to build a single workout history for you.

The App does not use your information to serve advertising or to run any commercial service.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

By design, the App does not share your workout or health data with any third party other than Fitbit itself, as required to communicate with the Fitbit Web API that you explicitly authorize. Data is not sold or shared for marketing purposes.

However, because the App is running on infrastructure that you control, you are responsible for any additional services you connect to your Zo Computer or any other parties you choose to grant access to your server.

6. Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored on your Zo Computer server in files and a local SQLite database. Zo provides mechanisms such as server snapshots and access controls, but you are ultimately responsible for protecting access to your Zo Computer account and any credentials stored on it.

Fitbit API credentials (such as client ID, client secret, access token, and refresh token) are stored in configuration files on your Zo Computer. These are used only to access your Fitbit data for the App's functionality.

7. Retention

Workout and health data is retained for as long as you keep it in the App. You can delete or modify entries by updating or removing the underlying database files, or by using any management tools you build on top of this system.

8. Your Rights and Choices

Because the App runs entirely on your own Zo Computer, you effectively have full control over your data. You may:

  • Revoke Fitbit access at any time using Fitbit's account settings.
  • Delete or modify your workout history from the SQLite database or associated files.
  • Stop using the App or remove it from your Zo Computer.

9. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time as you evolve the App. Any changes apply on a go-forward basis from the date they are posted at this URL.

10. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or how your data is used within this App, contact the developer/owner of this Zo Computer instance.
