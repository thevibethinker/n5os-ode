# The Truffle Pig's Guide to Hiring the 0.1%

Most hiring processes are designed to filter out the incompetent, not to identify the exceptional.

If you look at how the modern ATS works, it is explicitly built to reject 95% of applicants based on keyword matching before a human ever sees them.

And if you consider yourself a rigorous hiring manager?

You are the most likely to hire like a bureaucrat.

There are plenty of "perfect on paper" candidates who are one minor ambiguity away from a total meltdown, and there are plenty of "non-traditional" candidates who are abnormally high-agency, strategic, and impactful.

But your process can't tell the difference.

Rather than talking about "culture fit," "behavioral interviewing," or anything else that ChatGPT can regurgitate for you—I want to give you something different.

We're going to start with "Box Score" hiring and work our way towards "Game Tape" scouting.

If you read carefully, you'll start to develop the instinct of a truffle pig.

---

## Why your "rigorous" process keeps mis-hiring

> "The map is not the territory." – Alfred Korzybski

If you want to understand what elite talent looks like, start by understanding what it does not look like.

Bureaucratic hiring:

- **Is keyword-obsessed.** You jam a complex human into a set of boolean search strings.
- **Is reductionistic.** You reduce a career to "years of experience" or "logos on a resume."
- **Is risk-averse.** You only trust candidates who have already done the exact same job at a similar company (usually means bored and expensive).
- **Does not question.** Your justification is "that's just how the req was written."

Bureaucratic hiring is about closing your mind once you've reached the limits of the resume.

It is stopping your thinking too early. Reacting with your pre-programmed bias toward "Prestige" instead of reaching any novel insight about the actual human capacity in front of you.

Which brings us to the truffle pig's definition of Talent Scouting:

**The ability to evaluate the "Tape" over the "Box Score."**

---

## The "Tape" vs. The "Box Score"

The **Box Score** is the lagging indicator. The title, the tenure, the university, the certifications. It tells you what happened—but not *how* it happened or *who* made it happen.

The **Tape** is the leading indicator. The artifacts, the decision memos, the code commits, the specific way they deconstruct a problem in real-time. The immutable evidence of their mental models in action.

A Resume is horizontal. It lists facts about specific domains where someone was present. People with great resumes are "qualified"—but we don't need more qualified people. We need effective people.

A Portfolio (The Tape) is vertical. It shows the sophistication of their operating system. Someone can have a sparse resume but a dense portfolio of thinking, and use that capacity to generate outcomes that a "senior" person could never touch.

The "Perfect Candidate" failure mode: horizontally qualified but vertically empty. A VP with all the right logos who can't navigate ambiguity. A PM who knows every framework but can't ship.

## The Altitudes of Talent

Here's how I think about it:

**Lines of Talent** are skills you can verify.
Python, SQL, GAAP Accounting, Enterprise Sales. When you filter for these, you're filtering for baseline. Necessary but insufficient.

**Levels of Talent** are how they apply those skills.

- **Level 1 (Commodity)** – Executes tasks as defined. Follows the ticket. *"I did what you asked."*
- **Level 2 (Competent)** – Owns the outcome. Solves the immediate problem. *"I fixed the bug."*
- **Level 3 (Strategic)** – Owns the system that generates tasks. Prevents the problem from recurring. *"I refactored the module so this class of bugs is impossible."*
- **Level 4 (Generative)** – Creates value that wasn't asked for. Sees the market opportunity hidden in the technical debt. *"I realized this bug was actually a feature request, so I built a prototype."*
- **Level 5 (Superunicorn)** – Transcends the role entirely. Doesn't fill a seat—changes the trajectory of the company.

Levels 1 and 2 are Box Score hires. You can find them with keywords.

Levels 3-5 are Tape hires. You can only find them by auditing their thinking.

The Superunicorns are not looking for jobs on LinkedIn. They're hiding in plain sight—working on their own projects, or buried in a role that doesn't fit them.

## The Truffle Pig Playbook

Most companies operate in the "Job Board Economy." Post a req, get 1,000 applicants, use AI to filter down to "Safe Bets."

This is a race to the bottom.

In a world where AI makes average work free, the value of elite work goes to infinity. The difference between a Level 3 engineer and a Level 5 engineer isn't 2x—it's 100x.

To find these people, you have to stop acting like a gatekeeper and start acting like a scout.

**1. Stop reading resumes first.**
Look at the portfolio, the blog, the GitHub, the side project. Look at the Tape before you look at the Box Score.

**2. Stop interviewing for knowledge.**
Interview for *understanding*. Give them a real problem you are facing right now and watch them work. Not a LeetCode problem—an actual ambiguous situation from your business.

**3. Stop hiring for the role you have.**
Hire for the trajectory they are on. A Level 4 moving toward Level 5 is worth more than a Level 3 who has been static for years.

**4. Learn to smell truffles.**
The signal you're looking for is *how they think*, not *what they know*. When you ask them about a past project, do they describe what happened? Or do they describe the decisions they made, the tradeoffs they considered, and what they would do differently?

The truffle is in the reasoning, not the result.

The next time you see a resume that doesn't quite fit the box score, but has a flash of brilliance in the tape...

Do not collapse in on yourself.

Sit with it. Question it. Realize that your bureaucratic brain wants to feel safe.

But safety is death in a high-performance environment.

The truffle pigs win.
