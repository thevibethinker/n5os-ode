# Fundamentals of AI Productivity — Seminar Plan v4

## The Refined Thesis

**The Bait-and-Switch:** "There are no fundamentals — there is no spoon."

The fundamental truth is that there ARE no universal fundamentals. Everyone needs to build their own system. What we're teaching is **how to think about designing your own system** — not a system to copy.

**The Core Insight:** Most of us cannot conceive the extent to which we've internalized the idea that computers are "dumb" — only able to solve linear, deterministic tasks. That's no longer true. AI has reasoning that doesn't require programming out every edge case. Opening your mind to new ways of interfacing with technology is the real unlock.

### The Actual Fundamentals (Meta-Level)

1. **Systems thinking + ownership** — You must take ownership of your own productivity
2. **Build for YOUR brain, goals, truth** — Productivity is personal
3. **Exploration is required** — No static state; adapt, pick new tools, stay competitive OR love the game
4. **Motivation is fuel** — Find what drives YOU
5. **Cognitive bootstrapping** — Start with the smallest problem. Your capacity grows over time. Just START.
6. **The biggest skill is knowing how to use AI to solve YOUR problem** — Like tech support's biggest skill being Googling

## Session Flow (Revised)

**Total Runtime: ~60-75 min**

### 1. V Opening (5 min)
**Purpose:** Set frame, defuse overwhelm, present survey data, meta-demo

- "There is no spoon" — there are no universal fundamentals
- Present Fillout survey data LIVE: "Here's what YOU said you're struggling with"
- **Meta-demo moment:** "By the way — the dashboard you're looking at? I built it. I'm not a developer. Let me show you how."
  - Quick reveal: Fillout form → webhook → Zo dashboard
  - "This took 30 minutes to set up. Now it works forever."
- **The 3x Rule:** "If you expect to do something 3+ times, consider building for the future"
- Set up the arc: "We're going to start small and build up"

### 2. V Tactics — Cognitive Bootstrapping (10 min)
**Purpose:** Ground-level, ChatGPT-accessible techniques anyone can use

**The $20/month Constraint:** Everything in this section works with just ChatGPT Pro.

#### Core Tactics to Demo:

**Tactic 1: Socratic Dialogue**
- Use AI to improve your thinking through questions
- Prompt pattern: "Help me think through X by asking me questions, one at a time. Don't give me answers — draw them out of me."
- Demo: Walk through a real decision you're wrestling with

**Tactic 2: Poking Holes / Devil's Advocate**
- Use AI to stress-test your thinking
- Prompt pattern: "I believe X. Your job is to find every flaw, weakness, and blind spot in this reasoning. Be ruthless."
- Variation: "Argue against me as if you were [specific critic/perspective]"

**Tactic 3: Vibe Teacher**
- Use AI to help you learn by teaching
- Prompt pattern: "I'm going to explain X to you. Your job is to be a curious student — ask clarifying questions, point out where I'm unclear, and help me refine my understanding."
- The act of articulating forces crystallization

#### Additional Tactics (choose 1-2):

**Tactic 4: Rubber Duck Debugging**
- Explain your problem out loud to AI
- Often the act of articulating reveals the solution
- "I'm stuck on X. I'm going to explain it to you, and you just listen and ask clarifying questions."

**Tactic 5: Assumption Surfacing**
- "What assumptions am I making that I haven't explicitly stated?"
- Surfaces hidden constraints and beliefs

**Tactic 6: Pre-Mortem**
- "Assume this plan fails. Why did it fail? What went wrong?"
- Proactive failure analysis

**Tactic 7: Steel-Manning**
- "What's the strongest possible version of the argument against my position?"
- Better than devil's advocate — you're building the best counter-case

**Tactic 8: Perspective Shifting**
- "Argue this from the perspective of [specific person/role]"
- CEO, skeptical investor, end user, competitor, regulator, etc.

### 3. Logan Demo — Claude Code (15 min)
**Purpose:** Show what's possible with a coding-capable AI, even for non-coders

**Logan's Frame:**

**Handout:** Link to Logan's GitHub page for the visualization tool

### 4. Ben Demo — Zo (15 min)
**Purpose:** Introduce the agentic work environment

**Ben's Frame:**
- This is what it looks like when the AI has persistent memory, tools, and agency
- "Velocity coding" — make it work, then make it good
- The difference between chat (transactional) and environment (persistent)

**Demo possibilities (align with Ben):**
- Building something live in Zo
- Showing integrations / data providers
- Demonstrating scheduled tasks / agents
- The compound effect of small automations

### 5. V Close — Sizzle Reel + Primitives (10 min)
**Purpose:** Show the ceiling, teach the primitives, provide resources

#### Part A: Sizzle Reel (5 min)
Quick hits of what's possible with Zo at a sophisticated level:

- **Direct clone:** "Here's a repo — clone it into your Zo and use it"
- **Indirect study:** "Paste a GitHub link and say 'study this, import as prompts'"
- **N5OS sophistication:** Brief glimpse of what a fully-built personal OS looks like
- Frame: "This is what you can aspire to. We'll give you the building blocks."

#### Part B: Primitives Overview (5 min)
Concepts everyone should understand to level up:

| Primitive | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| **Prompt** | Your interface to AI | The quality of your input determines output |
| **Context Window** | AI's working memory | Understand limitations and chunking |
| **System Prompt** | Persistent instructions | Shape behavior across conversations |
| **API** | Programmatic access to services | Connect AI to real-world data |
| **Webhook** | Event-triggered actions | Automate responses to external events |
| **MCP** | Model Context Protocol | Standardized tool/context interface |
| **Scheduled Task** | Time-triggered AI actions | Automation that runs without you |
| **Database** | Persistent structured data | Memory that outlasts conversations |

### 6. Q&A + Resources (10-15 min)
- Open Q&A
- Share resources (repos, N5OS ODE, handouts)
- CTA: "Try ONE thing this week — start with the smallest problem"
