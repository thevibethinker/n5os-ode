## Question 1: Deal Flow Context Switching

**Question Type:** Multiple choice (single select) + Open text follow-up

**Question:**
When you're evaluating multiple companies in a single day, what's the biggest friction point?

- [ ] Finding the last conversation or email thread with each founder
- [ ] Remembering which questions I already asked vs. still need to ask
- [ ] Reconstructing my initial thesis after seeing 10 other pitches
- [ ] Synthesizing scattered notes from partner discussions, emails, and meetings
- [ ] Other: [open text field]

**Follow-up (conditional on selection):**
Walk me through the last time this happened. What were you trying to do, and how much time did it cost you?

[Open text field - 2-3 sentences]

**JTBD Insight:** Maps to "When I'm context-switching between deals, I want to instantly recall my thesis and open questions, so I can have substantive conversations without re-reading everything."

## Question 2: Portfolio Intelligence

**Question Type:** Ranking + Open text

**Question:**
Rank these scenarios by how often you wish you had instant access to the information (1 = most frequent, 5 = least frequent):

- ___ A founder mentions they're facing a specific challenge, and I want to know which of my other portfolio companies solved this
- ___ I'm in a partner meeting and need to quickly recall a key metric or update from a specific company
- ___ A potential hire reaches out, and I want to know if they have connections to my portfolio
- ___ I'm preparing for a board meeting and need to synthesize updates from the last 3 months
- ___ I'm writing an investment memo and need to reference similar past deals

**Follow-up:**
For your #1 ranked scenario: How do you handle this today, and what's the time/quality trade-off?

[Open text field - 2-3 sentences]

**JTBD Insight:** Maps to "When I need portfolio intelligence, I want to query my network and history like a database, so I can provide immediate, high-value guidance."

## Question 3: Investment Memo Creation

**Question Type:** Scale + Open text

**Question:**

**Research & synthesis:** 1 (minimal) → 5 (most time)
**Writing & articulation:** 1 (minimal) → 5 (most time)
**Formatting & organization:** 1 (minimal) → 5 (most time)
**Gathering input from partners:** 1 (minimal) → 5 (most time)
**Finding supporting data/examples:** 1 (minimal) → 5 (most time)

**Follow-up:**
If you could eliminate or dramatically speed up ONE of these, which would have the biggest impact on your decision quality (not just speed)?

[Open text field - 1-2 sentences]
