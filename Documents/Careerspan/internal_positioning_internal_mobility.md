# Internal Mobility Positioning: Careerspan as Enterprise Career Development Layer

## Thesis

Careerspan is the missing **signal capture layer** that internal talent marketplaces need but can't build—a coaching-grade qualitative engine that reveals what employees actually want and can do, not just what's in their HR profile.

**The most untapped talent source is the one you already have.**

---

## Why Now

### The Internal Mobility Crisis Is Acute

**1. External hiring is a coin flip—and an expensive one.**

- **46% of new hires fail within 18 months**
- **90% of those failures are due to values, mindset, and soft skills mismatch**—not technical competence
- You're putting round pegs in square holes, and it costs 50-200% of salary every time

The data is clear: betting on known quantities beats rolling the dice on external candidates. Your existing employees are already culture-fit tested, context-aware, and relationship-embedded.

**2. Your managers aren't talent spotters. They're managers.**

Managers are busy managing. They don't have time—or the training—to deeply understand each employee's career aspirations, hidden capabilities, and growth trajectory. Performance reviews capture what someone *did*, not what they *want* or *could do*.

You need:
- An objective lens that sees beyond the manager's limited view
- The employee's own reflection on their career and how they're doing
- A structured way to capture aspirations that aren't visible in day-to-day work

**3. Skills-based hiring is the new mandate—but nobody has good skills data.**

LinkedIn, Deloitte, McKinsey—everyone is pushing skills-over-credentials. The dirty secret: nobody has good skills data on existing employees. Performance reviews are garbage. Self-reported skills are gamed. Managers don't know their people.

**4. The major players are building internal mobility, but they're stuck.**

- Gloat ($90M Series D) is the category leader but runs on structured data
- Eightfold ($410M Series E) has the AI but lacks the qualitative signal
- Workday acquired HiredScore and is building career hub—massive distribution, weak differentiation
- Everyone is building the *matching infrastructure*. Nobody is solving *signal capture*.

**5. The quiet quitting problem.**

Gallup says 60%+ of workers are disengaged. The first sign of re-engagement is when someone articulates what they want next. Careerspan already gets users to do this—40 minutes of deep reflection, on average.

**The gap in the market:** Internal talent marketplaces are being built on the same lousy HR data that has always existed. Someone needs to generate the *new* data—the qualitative layer about aspirations, hidden skills, and career trajectory. That's Careerspan.

## The Careerspan Advantage

### What We Have That Others Don't

**1. Proven Signal Capture UX**

- Average activated user spends 40+ minutes in deep self-reflection
- 2 stories per activated user = structured career narratives, not checkbox profiles
- 35% complete 3+ stories (exceptional retention for a reflection tool)
- **4,000 total signups from zero marketing spend**—entirely organic growth through community partnerships
- This is the hard problem. Getting employees to actually articulate their aspirations and experiences is UX-hard. We've cracked it.

**2. Story-Based Profiling > Skills Taxonomies**

- AISS Framework (Action-Impact-Scale-Skill) extracts structured data from natural narratives
- Captures what assessments and performance reviews miss: context, motivation, growth trajectory
- Skills emerge from stories, not self-reports (harder to game, richer signal)
- A story about leading a cross-functional project tells you more than a checkbox saying "Project Management: Expert"

**3. Coaching Conversations as Data Source**

- Every coaching interaction surfaces aspirations, frustrations, hidden capabilities
- This is intelligence that HR never gets—the "what I really want" conversation
- Employees trust a coaching context. They don't trust a career portal.
- Captures signals that only coaching reveals:
  - How someone responds to challenge and feedback
  - Self-awareness trajectory over time
  - Gap between stated ambitions and follow-through
  - Pattern recognition across career decisions

**4. Human-First, Yet Highly Detailed**

Careerspan offers a **human, intuitive approach** to career reflection that nevertheless produces **highly detailed and informative** data. It doesn't feel like an HR assessment—it feels like working with a coach. But the output is structured, analyzable, and actionable for talent decisions.

**5. Semantic Matching Beyond Keywords**

- Embedding-based matching on career narratives
- "I want to lead cross-functional initiatives" matches to roles requiring collaboration leadership
- Context-aware, not just keyword matching

## Objections & Rebuttals

### "Why wouldn't Gloat/Eightfold just build this?"

**Objection:** These are well-funded companies with strong eng teams. If story-based profiling is so valuable, why wouldn't they build it in-house?

**Rebuttal:**

1. **Different DNA.** Gloat/Eightfold are infrastructure companies. They build matching algorithms and integrations. Coaching-based UX requires different expertise—human psychology, narrative elicitation, trust-building in digital contexts. V has a decade of coaching expertise that informed every design decision.
2. **Time-to-market.** Building this from scratch is 18-24 months minimum. Acquiring Careerspan is instant capability.
3. **Validated UX is hard to replicate.** The 40-minute engagement metric isn't luck—it's the result of hundreds of iterations on the coaching flow. Hard to reverse-engineer.
4. **Build vs. buy math.** For a company like Gloat ($90M raised), a sub-$10M acquisition that adds a differentiated capability is obvious ROI.

### "Will enterprises actually deploy a coaching tool internally?"

**Objection:** HR already has too many tools. Why would they add another one?

**Rebuttal:**

1. **Not "another tool"—a data layer.** Careerspan integrates into existing workflows. It's the signal source, not a standalone destination.
2. **Already proven in enterprise-adjacent contexts.** Peace Corps, Columbia, Emory—institutional deployments that demonstrate the model works at scale.
3. **Coaching as incentive.** Pair profile completion with coaching access. Value exchange for the employee.

### "Careerspan is a coaching company, not a product company"

**Objection:** The real asset is V and Ilse, not the tech. You're buying an agency, not a platform.

**Rebuttal:**

1. **Both are assets.** V's coaching expertise informed the AISS framework and UX design. Ilse's technical velocity built the multi-agent architecture. Team + tech is the package.
2. **Tech is transferable.** The platform works without V personally coaching every user. The automation is real.
3. **Team as differentiator.** In acqui-hire scenarios, the team is often the point. V's decade of coaching + Logan's workforce expertise + Ilse's technical execution = rare combination.

### "Internal mobility is crowded—everyone is building this"

**Objection:** Gloat, Eightfold, Workday, Phenom, Beamery—the space is saturated.

**Rebuttal:**

1. **Crowded market validates the opportunity.** If everyone is building internal mobility, it's clearly the priority. The question is: who has the best data layer?
2. **Careerspan isn't competing—it's complementing.** We're not building another marketplace. We're building the signal layer that makes marketplaces work.
3. **No one has solved signal capture.** The crowded players are all building on the same limited data. Careerspan adds new data.
