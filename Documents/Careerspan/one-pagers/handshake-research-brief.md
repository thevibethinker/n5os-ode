## Strategic Situation

### The "Refounding" (2024)
- Lord declared a "refounding" in early 2024
- Created Handshake AI as separate business unit
- AI unit focuses on providing expert feedback for training frontier AI models (OpenAI, Anthropic)
- Laid off 15% of workforce (~100 people) to fund pivot
- Handshake AI: $0 → $100M ARR in 8 months
- AI business projected to surpass core recruiting by end of 2025

### Core Business Stagnation
| Year | Revenue | YoY Growth |
|------|---------|------------|
| 2020 | $36M | — |
| 2021 | $75M | 108% |
| 2022 | $120M | 60% |
| 2023 | $180M | 50% |
| 2024 | $190M | **6%** |

- Growth deceleration driven by mass layoffs and hiring freezes in late 2023
- Core business is "stable, mature SaaS operation" (Lord's framing)
- Leadership attention has shifted to AI

### The Moat
- 10 years of university relationships
- "Ford Focus strategy" — drove campus to campus to win career centers
- Universities integrate Handshake into SSO, often mandatory for career services
- Student acquisition cost: essentially zero
- Very low churn — universities rarely switch

### Product
- **Core:** Three-sided marketplace (students, universities, employers)
- **Student experience:** TikTok-style career feed, one-click applications, event RSVPs
- **Employer product:** Talent Engagement Suite (TES) — targeting by 50+ data points, messaging campaigns, analytics
- **University product:** Career services software replacing legacy systems (Symplicity, etc.)
- **Handshake AI:** Data labeling marketplace connecting PhDs to AI labs

### Competitive Landscape
**Core recruiting:**
- LinkedIn (dominant for experienced workers, weak for early talent)
- Symplicity (legacy incumbent, losing ground)
- RippleMatch, Yello/WayUp, Parker Dewey (niche early-career players)

**AI data labeling:**
- Scale AI, Mercor, Surge AI, Labelbox, Invisible

## Strategic Gaps (Careerspan Fit)

| Gap | Handshake State | Careerspan Solution |
|-----|-----------------|---------------------|
| Signal quality | Structured data only (GPA, major, coursework) | Story-based behavioral signal |
| Engagement depth | Job applications, event RSVPs | 40+ min coaching conversations |
| Moving upstream | Students arrive when job-hunting | Captures signal before job search |
| Differentiated data | Same profile data as competitors | AISS framework—proprietary narratives |
| Community access | University career centers | Communities that block recruiters (Peace Corps, etc.) |

## Pitch Angle for Handshake

**Frame:** "You built distribution. We built signal. Together: unstoppable."

**Narrative:**
1. You democratized access. Every student can reach every employer.
2. But access without signal is noise. Employers can find students; they can't tell who'll succeed.
3. Careerspan captures what profiles can't: how people think, not just what they've done.
4. You have 1,600 universities. We have the coaching infrastructure to capture signal at scale.
5. Together, you're not just a job board—you're the only platform that can tell employers WHO to hire.

**Risk mitigation:** "We're not here to replace your product. We're the missing layer that makes your product defensible."

## Steel Man: Why Handshake Might Not Care

1. They're pivoting away from recruiting—AI is the future
2. They already have distribution—why complicate the model?
3. Could acqui-hire the team rather than acquire the company
4. Story-based assessment doesn't fit their TikTok-feed UX easily
