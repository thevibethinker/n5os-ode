## PART 1: OPENING SECTION (10 min, ~12 slides)

---

### Slide 1: Title
**Headline:** Fundamentals of AI Productivity

**Subhead:** There is no spoon.

**Visual:** Abstract gradient or minimal geometric design. Clean, modern.

---

### Slide 2: The Room
**Headline:** You told us what you're struggling with.

**Visual:** Embed or screenshot of live survey dashboard (Gamma link)

**Speaker note:** "Before we dive in — let's look at what YOU said."

---

### Slide 3: The Data
**Headline:** 75% of you are scratching the surface.

**Bullets (minimal):**
- Using tools ✓
- Getting results... sometimes
- Can't level up

**Visual:** Simple pie chart or stat callout boxes

---

### Slide 4: The Real Problem
**Headline:** You have tactics. You don't have a mental model.

**Subhead:** The problem isn't knowledge. It's coherence.

**Visual:** Scattered puzzle pieces (abstract) — lots of pieces, no picture

---

### Slide 5: The Thesis
**Headline:** Magic is when intent becomes outcome.

**Subhead:** A thousand years ago, you grew the crops. Today, you tap a screen and dinner arrives.

**Visual:** Side-by-side: farm field → phone with food delivery app. Or: abstract "gap closing" visual.

---

### Slide 6: Your Job
**Headline:** Learn to collapse the gap.

**Subhead:** That's context engineering.

**Visual:** Two points connected by shrinking arrow. "Intent" → "Outcome"

---

### Slide 7: The Framework
**Headline:** Three levels of context engineering.

**Visual:** Vertical stack or pyramid diagram:
```
Level 3: PIPELINE — Living
Level 2: ENVIRONMENT — Persistent  
Level 1: CONVERSATION — Ephemeral
```

**Subhead:** Master all three → the Meta-OS emerges.

---

### Slide 8: Level 1 — Conversation
**Headline:** Engineer the moment.

**Tactics (short bullets):**
- Delay the draft — build context first
- Clarification gates — make AI ask YOU questions
- Adversarial probing — stress-test before you trust
- Threshold rubrics — control WHEN it answers

**Visual:** Chat bubble illustration or conversation flow diagram

---

### Slide 9: Level 2 — Environment
**Headline:** Engineer what persists.

**Tactics (short bullets):**
- Personalization instructions — custom instructions, rules, CLAUDE.md
- Memory — let it compound over time
- Tool access — what can AI reach?
- Cognitive guardrails — encode YOUR standards

**Visual:** Settings/config icon, or "layers" building up over time

---

### Slide 10: The Caveat
**Headline:** Signal-to-noise matters.

**Subhead:** You can drown the AI in context long before you hit the token limit.

**Visual:** Simple noise/signal wave diagram — too much noise obscures signal

---

### Slide 11: Level 3 — Pipeline
**Headline:** Engineer the living system.

**Tactics (short bullets):**
- Import from the wild — GitHub repos, prompt libraries, templates
- Bring data in — webhooks, APIs, scheduled syncs
- Let it act — scheduled tasks, automated workflows

**Visual:** Flow diagram with arrows: External World → Your System → Actions

---

### Slide 12: The Outcome
**Headline:** The Meta-OS is a gestalt.

**Subhead:** Greater than the sum of its parts. An accretion of adapted building blocks.

**Visual:** Abstract: scattered blocks assembling into coherent structure. Or: Venn diagram where overlap = something new.

**Speaker note:** "Logan will show Level 2 in action. Ben will show the full environment. I'll come back to show what these levels look like fully built."

## PART 2: DEMO SECTION (5 min, ~8 slides)

---

### Slide 13: The Proof
**Headline:** What happens when you stack the levels?

**Subhead:** Three systems. All built the same way. By someone with no technical training.

**Visual:** Three icons representing: Flights, Meetings, Dashboard

---

### Slide 14: The Only Pattern
**Headline:** "What are the blocks?"

**Steps (numbered, large):**
1. I have a need
2. I ask AI: "What are the blocks?"
3. Build each block, one conversation at a time
4. Connect the blocks

**Visual:** Simple linear flow diagram or building blocks assembling

---

### Slide 15: System 1 — Flight Search
**Headline:** "Flights to LA next weekend."

**Subhead:** Filtered by MY preferences. Automatically.

**The blocks:**
- A place to store my rules (config)
- A pipe to flight data (API)
- A recipe that applies my rules (script)

**Visual:** Screenshot of flight search output OR hypothetical terminal/results view

**Quote callout:** "I didn't learn Python. I learned that scripts are recipes."

---

### Slide 16: System 2 — Meeting Intelligence
**Headline:** Every meeting becomes searchable memory.

**Subhead:** Who was there. What was decided. What I committed to.

**The blocks:**
- Capture (transcripts land somewhere)
- Organize (raw → structured)
- Understand (extract intelligence)
- Archive (file for later)

**Visual:** Folder tree screenshot or flow diagram: Recording → Transcript → Intelligence → Archive

**Quote callout:** "Six conversations. I didn't understand any of it upfront."

---

### Slide 17: System 3 — This Dashboard
**Headline:** You're inside one of my systems right now.

**Subhead:** The survey dashboard? Same pattern.

**The blocks:**
- Webhook receives responses
- Script computes stats
- Gamma generates visuals
- Scheduled task refreshes every 12 hours

**Visual:** Screenshot of the actual survey dashboard they saw at the start

---

### Slide 18: The Translation
**Headline:** Complexity is an illusion.

**Translations (two columns):**
| Scary Term | Actually Just... |
|------------|------------------|
| Config file | A place to store preferences |
| Script | A recipe |
| API | A pipe between systems |
| Webhook | A trigger |
| State machine | "Where am I in the process?" |

**Visual:** Split screen or simple icon translations

---

### Slide 19: The Unlock
**Headline:** Once you see the blocks, you just see assembly.

**Visual:** Same scattered puzzle from Slide 4, now assembled into clear picture

---

### Slide 20: The Ask
**Headline:** Try ONE thing this week.

**Bullets:**
- Start with Level 1
- One tactic, one conversation
- Your capacity grows from there

**Subhead:** The Meta-OS isn't a destination. It's an emergent property of sustained attention.

**Visual:** Single step forward, or seedling growing — small start, big potential
