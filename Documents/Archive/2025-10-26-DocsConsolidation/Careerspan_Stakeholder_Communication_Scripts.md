# Careerspan Stakeholder Communication Scripts
## Team, Investor & Family Conversations - Detailed Scripts

**Purpose**: Reduce cognitive load during high-stakes conversations by providing battle-tested scripts for each key stakeholder group

## I. Team Communication Scripts

### Script 1: Danny & Rochel (Wind-Down with Placement Support)

**Context**: Most difficult conversation - communicating wind-down while offering placement as first clients

**Setting**: In-person, private, 1:1 first, then potentially together  
**Timing**: Week of Oct 20–24  
**Goal**: Preserve dignity, offer clear path forward, maintain goodwill

**Opening (First 60 seconds)**:

"Thank you both for making time. I want to be direct and honest with you about where we are and where we're heading.

Logan and I have spent the last month doing a comprehensive strategic review of Careerspan. We looked at every path forward - raising more money, continuing as-is, selling, and pivoting the business model.

What we've decided is that we're going to fundamentally shift how Careerspan operates. This shift means that your current roles - as they exist today - won't continue past December 31st.

I want to be extremely clear: this is not a reflection of your work or your value. You've both been exceptional. This is about the business model no longer being sustainable in its current form."

**Pause. Let them process. Answer immediate questions.**

**The Path Forward (Next 2-3 minutes)**:

"Here's what we're committed to:

**Immediate (This Month)**:
- Full salary and benefits through December 31st
- We will not ask you to work beyond what you're comfortable with
- We'll provide reference letters highlighting your specific contributions

**The Placement Opportunity (November-December)**:
- Logan and I have a hypothesis: the best proof that Careerspan's method works is placing incredible people like you
- We'd like to offer you Careerspan Select services - essentially, we become your personal placement team
- This means: We build your profile, tap our network, do warm intros, and treat you as our #1 priority client
- Our goal: Get you placed into great roles that are better than what you'd find on your own
- No cost to you. This is us investing in proving our model works.

**What We Need from You**:
- Honesty about what you want next in your career
- Willingness to let us try this experiment
- Patience as we figure this out together
- Discretion - we're not announcing this broadly yet

**What This is NOT**:
- This is not us washing our hands of responsibility
- This is not charity or a consolation prize
- This is us putting our money where our mouth is about Careerspan's placement model

Do you have questions about any of this?"

**Anticipated Questions & Responses**:

**Q**: "Why is this happening?"  
**A**: "The business model we built - B2B SaaS with a large team - requires raising significant VC money to scale. We've determined that path isn't right for us anymore. We're shifting to a smaller, services-led model that can be profitable at much smaller scale. That model doesn't support the current team size."

**Q**: "Can't you just keep paying us?"  
**A**: "Our current burn rate is unsustainable past December without raising more money. We've chosen not to raise more money. We're shifting to a model that can be profitable at 2-3 people, not 6-7."

**Q**: "What if I don't want you to help me find a job?"  
**A**: "Totally your call. We'll provide the reference letter and severance as committed. The placement offer is optional - it's there if you want it."

**Q**: "How long will the placement process take?"  
**A**: "Honest answer: we don't know. We're treating this as a real test of our placement model. Our goal is to get you placed within 60–90 days. If it takes longer, we'll keep supporting you through the process."

**Q**: "Are you shutting down Careerspan?"  
**A**: "No. Careerspan the platform continues. We're actually committed to growing the user base. What's changing is the business model and team size."

**Closing**:

"I know this is a lot to process. Take the time you need. We can talk as many times as you want over the next week.

One more thing I want to say: I'm genuinely grateful for what you've built here. [Specific example for each person of their contribution]. That doesn't go away just because the business model is changing.

Let's schedule time to talk again in 2-3 days. In the meantime, please reach out anytime if questions come up."

**Follow-Up (48–72 hours later)**:

"How are you feeling about everything we discussed? What questions have come up?"

[Listen. Address questions. Then:]

"If you're open to it, I'd like to start working on your Careerspan Select profile this week. Even if you're not sure about the placement piece, having the profile built gives you options. Can we schedule 60 minutes to start that?"

### Script 2: Ilse (Retention Offer - Part-Time Jan–Feb)

**Context**: Key person retention for transition period

**Setting**: In-person, private  
**Timing**: Week of Oct 20–24  
**Goal**: Secure part-time commitment for Jan–Feb 2026 transition

**Opening**:

"Thank you for making time. I want to talk with you about the future of Careerspan and specifically about your role.

Logan and I are making a significant shift in how Careerspan operates starting January 1st. We're moving from a B2B SaaS model with a full team to a much leaner, services-focused model.

This means the team is winding down at the end of the year. **But - we'd like you to stay on part-time through February if you're open to it.**"

**The Offer**:

"Here's what we're proposing:

**Current Role (Oct-Dec)**:
- Continue as-is through December 31st
- Full salary and benefits
- We'll work with you on transition planning

**Part-Time Role (Jan–Feb 2026)**:
- 15–20 hours per week, remote-first
- Compensation: [$X per month - calculate based on hourly rate]
- Scope: [Specific tasks - examples: user support, maintaining platform, documentation]
- Flexible schedule - you tell us what hours work for you

**Why We're Asking**:
- You know the platform better than anyone
- We need someone technical during the transition while we figure out next steps
- This gives you income while exploring what's next for you

**What Happens After February**:
- We reassess based on where the business is
- Potential paths: continue part-time, transition to contractor, or full exit with referral support

**No Pressure**:
- If you want to be done Dec 31st, that's completely okay
- If you need time to think about this, take it
- If you want to negotiate different terms, let's talk

What questions do you have?"

**Anticipated Questions & Responses**:

**Q**: "Why me specifically?"  
**A**: "You've been the most consistent, reliable person on product/tech. We trust you. And candidly, we can't afford to bring in someone new to learn everything you already know."

**Q**: "What if I get another job offer in January?"  
**A**: "Take it. We'd give you 2 weeks notice to wrap things up. Your career comes first."

**Q**: "Can this become full-time again?"  
**A**: "I can't promise that. The business model we're pursuing is specifically designed to run with 2-3 people max. But if we discover we need full-time help, you'd be first person we'd talk to."

**Q**: "What specifically would I be doing Jan-Feb?"  
**A**: [Provide concrete list - example: User support tickets, bug fixes, documentation updates, training V/Logan on platform maintenance]

**Closing**:

"Take a few days to think about this. Let's talk again [specific date]. And thank you - for everything you've done to get us here. That doesn't change regardless of what you decide next."

## II. Investor Communication Scripts

### Script 1: Investor Update Email (Initial Announcement)

**To**: All investors  
**Subject**: Careerspan Strategic Update - Pivot to Dual-Brand Model  
**Timing**: After team communications (early November)

**Email**:

Subject: Careerspan Strategic Update - Pivot to Dual-Brand Model

Hi [Investor Name],

I'm writing to share an important strategic update about Careerspan.

**TL;DR**: We're pivoting from VC-backed B2B SaaS to a dual-brand bootstrapped model combining platform growth (Careerspan) with services revenue (V&L LLC). We will not be raising additional VC funding.

**Context**:

Over the past month, Logan and I conducted a comprehensive strategic review. We looked at every path: raising Series A, continuing our current course, selling, or pivoting to a new model.

What became clear: the VC-growth path requires capital deployment and team scaling that doesn't align with our current traction, the market environment, or frankly, what we want to build.

**The New Model**:

We're executing a dual-brand strategy:

1. **Careerspan (Platform)**: Free B2C platform for passive job seekers. Focus on user growth and engagement. Positioning as an asset to sell in 12–18 months.

2. **V&L LLC (Services)**: Immediate revenue through two streams:
   - Careerspan Select: High-touch placement services ($2k per candidate, targeting 10 placements in 90 days)
   - AI Consulting (V) / B2C Content Products (Logan): Leveraging our individual expertise

**Financial Plan**:
- Target: $25k/month combined revenue by Q1 2026
- Timeline: 90-day sprint to prove model, reassess in Jan 2026
- Team: Wind down current team by Dec 31st, operate as 2-person team

**What This Means for You**:

- **Equity**: Your equity stake remains unchanged
- **Return Scenario**: Exit likely comes through Careerspan platform sale (12-18 months) vs. traditional VC path (3-5+ years)
- **Updates**: We'll send monthly progress updates on revenue and user growth metrics

**Why We Believe This Works**:

- Market validation: 100 conversations confirmed demand for both placement and consulting services
- Proven model: Multiple case studies of bootstrap-first-then-scale (see: dbt Labs, Waymaker, InsideSales.com)
- Founder-market fit: We're playing to our strengths vs. forcing a model that doesn't fit

**Our Commitment**:

We're not giving up on creating value for shareholders. We're choosing a different path to get there - one that we believe has a higher probability of success given market conditions and our strengths.

**Next Steps**:

- Open to questions: Reply to this email or schedule time to discuss
- Monthly updates: We'll send metrics and progress updates first week of each month
- Open door: We value your counsel and would welcome your input as we execute this pivot

Thank you for believing in us and backing Careerspan. We're committed to making this pivot successful and generating a return for everyone who supported us.

Best,
V

### Script 2: 1:1 Investor Call (For Key/Lead Investors)

**Context**: Deeper conversation for investors who led rounds or have board seats

**Timing**: Week of Nov 3–7 (after email sent)

**Opening**:

"Thanks for making time. I wanted to walk through our strategic pivot in more detail and get your input.

You saw the email, but let me give you the fuller context..."

[Walk through the decision-making process, metrics that informed the pivot, and the case studies/validation you've gathered]

**Key Discussion Points**:

1. **Revenue Traction**: "In the first 30 days of services push, we've generated $X. This gives us confidence in the $25k/month target."

2. **User Growth Plan**: "We're targeting 50k users by Dec 31st, 100k by June 2026. We've identified three growth levers: [community partnerships, alumni groups, Lensa partnership]."

3. **Exit Timeline**: "Our thesis is that if we hit 100k engaged users + demonstrate placement success, we become an attractive acquisition target for companies like Jack & Jill by mid-2026."

4. **Risk Mitigation**: "Biggest risks are [revenue taking longer than 90 days to hit target]. Our kill-switch criteria: if we're not at $15k/month by Jan 1st, we reassess the model."

**Questions to Expect**:

**Q**: "Why not just sell now?"  
**A**: "We explored that. The challenge is we don't have enough traction yet to command a price that would generate meaningful returns for shareholders. We believe 6-12 months of execution on this model will create a more valuable asset to sell."

**Q**: "What if this doesn't work?"  
**A**: "We have a 90-day checkpoint. If we're not hitting revenue targets by Jan 1st, we'll either (a) double down on what's working and cut what isn't, or (b) explore sale/wind-down options with a more clear-eyed view of what the business is actually worth."

**Q**: "Why not raise more money?"  
**A**: "We could. But the amount we'd need to raise ($2M+) would require significant dilution and commit us to a 3-5 year growth path that we're not confident we can execute given our current traction. This pivot gives us optionality to either sell sooner or scale profitably."

**Q**: "What do you need from me?"  
**A**: "Honestly? Patience and trust. We're asking for 90 days to prove this model. After that, we'll have real data to show you. If you have intros to potential Careerspan Select clients or consulting opportunities, that'd be helpful - but not expected."

**Closing**:

"I know this isn't the path you expected when you invested. We're committed to making this work and generating a return. Thank you for supporting us, and for taking the time to hear us out."

## III. Family/Personal Communication Scripts

### Script 1: Parents (V's family)

**Context**: Communicating major business pivot to parents who are financially and emotionally invested

**Setting**: In-person dinner or video call  
**Timing**: After investor and team communications (early November)

**Opening**:

"I wanted to share what's happening with Careerspan because I know you care and have been following along.

Short version: We're making a big change to the business model. It's going to feel like a step backward in some ways, but we believe it's actually the smartest path forward."

**The Explanation**:

"Here's what's happening:

**What's Changing**:
- We're winding down the team (everyone exits Dec 31st except me and Logan)
- We're shifting from 'raise VC money and scale fast' to 'bootstrap with services revenue and grow steadily'
- I'm going back to doing consulting work to pay the bills while we build

**Why We're Doing This**:
- The VC path requires raising $2M+ and committing to 5+ years of high-stress, low-control building
- We realized we don't want that life
- We've found a path that lets us build something profitable, sustainable, and aligned with what we actually enjoy doing

**What Success Looks Like**:
- In 90 days: We're making $25k/month from services (consulting + placement work)
- In 12 months: Careerspan has 100k users and we sell it for $X (enough to make investors whole and give us a nice outcome)
- In 18 months: We're running a profitable services business we love

**What Could Go Wrong**:
- Revenue takes longer to ramp than we expect
- We can't sell Careerspan for a meaningful price
- We end up just running a services business (which honestly, isn't the worst outcome)

**How I'm Feeling About It**:
- Relieved, honestly
- Excited to get back to work I'm good at
- Nervous about the uncertainty, but confident in our ability to execute

**What I Need from You**:
- Trust that Logan and I know what we're doing
- Patience as we figure this out
- Maybe some celebrating when we hit milestones? (first client, first placement, etc.)

Do you have questions or concerns I can address?"

**Anticipated Questions**:

**Q**: "Does this mean you failed?"  
**A**: "No. It means we're choosing a different definition of success. Most startups fail by running out of money or not finding product-market fit. We're pivoting while we have runway and options. That's a position of strength, not failure."

**Q**: "Will you be okay financially?"  
**A**: "Yes. Our goal is to replace our salaries within 90 days through services revenue. Worst case, I can take on more consulting clients. We're not in a desperate financial situation."

**Q**: "What happens to everyone who worked for you?"  
**A**: "We're helping them transition. Full pay through Dec 31st, plus we're offering to help place them in new roles using Careerspan's placement services. We're taking care of people."

**Closing**:

"I know this is a lot. I wanted you to hear it directly from me. I'll keep you updated as things progress. Love you guys."

## V. Red Flags to Avoid

**DON'T SAY**:
- "We failed" or "We couldn't make it work"
- "We're giving up" or "We're shutting down"
- "We ran out of options"
- "We didn't raise because investors didn't believe in us"

**DO SAY**:
- "We're pivoting based on strategic clarity"
- "We're optimizing for sustainable growth and founder happiness"
- "We're choosing a path that aligns with market conditions and our strengths"
- "We're executing a Bootstrap First, Raise Later strategy"
