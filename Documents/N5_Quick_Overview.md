# N5: A 3-Minute Overview

**What is N5?** A personal operating system for organizing information and automating work.

---

## The Problem

Most people's information is scattered everywhere:

- Meeting notes in one app
- Important insights buried in email
- Action items across multiple tools
- Research scattered in browser tabs
- Key knowledge trapped in your head

**Result:** You waste time searching, forget important details, and constantly reinvent the wheel.

---

## The N5 Solution

N5 is a simple folder structure + workflow system that keeps everything organized and findable:

```
Knowledge/     ← Things you need to remember forever
Lists/         ← Things you need to do
Records/       ← Things you're working on right now
N5/            ← The system that makes it all work
Documents/     ← Instructions and archives
```

That's it. Five folders that change how you work.

---

## How It Works

### **Knowledge/** — Your Second Brain

Store insights, frameworks, and important information you'll need again.

**Example:** Instead of re-reading the same article 10 times, you save the key insights once in `Knowledge/market_intelligence/`. Next time you need it, you know exactly where it is.

---

### **Lists/** — What Needs Doing

Simple text files that track your action items and priorities.

**Example:** All your tasks live in `Lists/action_items.md`. No complex project management software—just clear, organized lists.

---

### **Records/** — Your Working Space

Temporary files you're actively working on. Like your desk—things come in, get processed, then move to permanent storage or get thrown away.

**Example:** Meeting notes go here first. Then you extract the important parts to Knowledge/ and Lists/, and archive the rest.

---

### **N5/** — The Automation Engine

Scripts and commands that handle repetitive work for you.

**Example:** Instead of manually processing every meeting, you run a command that automatically extracts action items and insights, then files everything in the right place.

---

### **Documents/** — The Manual

How-to guides, system documentation, and historical records.

**Example:** Instructions for how your workflows work, so you (or someone else) can follow them later.

---

## Why It Works

### 1. **Everything Has a Place**
No more "where did I save that?" You know exactly where things go.

### 2. **Designed for AI**
AI agents can read your system, understand it, and help automate it. They know where to find information and where to put new things.

### 3. **Simple but Powerful**
Start with just folders and text files. Add automation as you need it. No complex software to learn.

### 4. **You Own It**
Plain text files in folders. Works with any tool. No vendor lock-in. Copy it anywhere.

---

## Real Example: Processing a Meeting

**Old way:**
1. Meeting happens
2. Notes scattered in your notebook
3. Action items floating in your head
4. Important insights forgotten
5. Next week: "Wait, what did they say?"

**N5 way:**
1. Meeting happens → transcript saved to `Records/Company/meetings/`
2. Run command: "Process this meeting"
3. AI extracts action items → `Lists/action_items.md`
4. AI extracts key insights → `Knowledge/market_intelligence/`
5. Original transcript → archived to `Documents/Archive/`

**Result:** 2 minutes of work, everything organized and findable forever.

## Core Philosophy

N5 follows a few simple rules:

1. **Single Source of Truth** — One place for each piece of information (not scattered)
2. **Human-Readable** — Everything is plain text you can read and understand
3. **Portable** — You own your files, works with any tool
4. **Improvable** — Start simple, add automation as you learn

## Questions?

**"Isn't this just folders?"**  
Yes! But with principles about what goes where, how to process information, and how to automate workflows. The structure is simple; the power comes from consistency.

**"Do I need to be technical?"**  
No. Start with just organizing files. Add scripts and automation later as you learn.

**"What if I already have systems?"**  
N5 can wrap around existing tools. You don't have to migrate everything—start with new information and gradually bring old stuff in.

**"How long until it's useful?"**  
Day 1: You'll know where things are.  
Week 1: You'll stop losing information.  
Month 1: You'll wonder how you worked without it.
