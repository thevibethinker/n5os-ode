# Critical Review & Improvements

## What's Working

✅ **Mirrors pre-survey** — Q1 directly compares to pre-survey sentiment question  
✅ **Two testimonial sources** — Q4 (aha moment) and Q6 (what you'll try) generate quotes  
✅ **NPS included** — Industry standard Ben will recognize  
✅ **Action-oriented** — Q5 and Q6 measure behavioral intent, not just satisfaction  
✅ **Friendly tone** — Subtext/captions feel human, not corporate  
✅ **Pacing question** — Useful feedback for calibrating future sessions  
✅ **Optional depth** — Core survey is 8 required questions; extras are opt-in

## Potential Issues & Alternatives

### Issue 1: Q3 Options May Create Awkwardness
The "which part was most valuable" question lists all three presenters. If most people pick one presenter, the others might feel slighted.

**Alternative A:** Remove presenter attribution, focus on concepts:
- The three-level framework
- The "what are the blocks?" decomposition method
- Seeing real systems built by a non-technical person
- The Zo environment demo
- The meta-demo (survey dashboard)

**Alternative B:** Keep it — Ben probably wants to know what landed. The data is useful even if politically sensitive.

**Recommendation:** Keep it. Ben is sophisticated; he'll want real signal. The question is framed around "value to you" not "who was best."

---

### Issue 2: Missing "Pre-Survey Callback" Opportunity

The pre-survey asked about specific challenges:
- "I don't know where to start"
- "I start but can't stick with it"  
- "I get results but they're inconsistent"
- "I know the basics but can't level up"

We could add a question that directly addresses whether those were resolved.

**Potential Addition (Q2.5):**

> **Before the session, many of you mentioned challenges like "not knowing where to start" or "can't level up past the basics." Do you feel like today's session addressed your specific challenge?**

**Options:**
- Yes, directly — I have a clearer path now
- Partially — Some helpful framing, but still have questions
- Not really — My challenge wasn't addressed
- I didn't have that challenge going in

**Trade-off:** Adds length. Only worth it if V wants to explicitly claim "we solved what you came in with."

---

### Issue 3: No "Quote Permission" Question

If V wants to use testimonials publicly (website, future pitch decks), should ask permission.

**Potential Addition (after Q4 or Q9):**

> **Can we share your feedback (anonymously) in future materials?**

**Options:**
- Yes, feel free
- No, keep it private

**Trade-off:** Adds friction. Many people skip these. Alternative: Include blanket statement in survey intro ("Responses may be quoted anonymously") and let people opt out via the open-ended fields.

---

### Issue 4: "Meta-OS" Reference in Q10

Q10 mentions "The Meta-OS concept V mentioned" — but looking at the transcript, this was a brief closing reference. Might confuse people who didn't catch it.

**Fix:** Either remove this option, or change to: "Building a personal AI operating system over time"

---

### Issue 5: Missing "Share with Ben" Nudge

V's goal is to impress Ben. The completion screen could be more strategic:

**Enhanced Completion Message:**

> We really appreciate you taking the time!
>
> **If today's session was valuable, we'd love it if you dropped a quick note in the NextPlay Slack** — Ben and the team read every comment, and it helps us bring more sessions like this to the community.
>
> Thanks for learning with us! 🙏

This gently encourages public testimonials in the community space where Ben will see them.

---
