# Founder Memo: The Overperformer Positioning
**Author:** Vrijen Attawar  
**Date:** 2025-10-24  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Distribution:** Internal strategy reference

---

## Executive Summary

Careerspan's core value proposition centers on **identifying and connecting over-performers**—people so good that companies make space for them, even when they didn't know they had the need.

This positioning solves a dual-sided problem:
- **For candidates:** Dynamic evaluation that reveals where you're over-performing (and under-performing), giving you leverage to negotiate better pay or find roles that match your ambitions
- **For hiring managers:** Access to pre-vetted over-performers instead of manually screening hundreds of generic applications

**Tagline:** "Bespoke hiring at scale"

**Pricing exploration:** ~$500 to post a job with the Careerspan Network

---

## The Core Insight

There are some people that are so good you make space for them. They fit your needs so well that you didn't even know what need you had until they showed up.

This is the category of talent every company wants—over-performers who deliver above-normal results. But the current hiring infrastructure makes it nearly impossible to identify these people efficiently.

**The problem:**
- Traditional ATS systems optimize for volume, not quality
- Hiring managers spend hours manually screening to find a handful of qualified candidates
- High-performing candidates have no way to signal their value beyond a resume

**Our solution:**
Careerspan dynamically evaluates candidates to identify over-performers and connects them with companies looking for exactly what they bring.

---

## The Candidate Value Proposition

### Why candidates use Careerspan:

1. **Career clarity:** We help you articulate what you're doing and see how you measure up against the standard for your role
2. **Performance visibility:** We identify areas where you're over-performing or under-performing
3. **Strategic positioning:** If you're crushing it, you either need to be paid more or you need a role that matches your ambitions
4. **Access to opportunity:** Because Careerspan identifies you as an over-performer, that becomes your credential—companies want to talk to you

### The incentive loop:

Candidates fill out their Careerspan profiles not just for career planning and self-edification, but because **being identified as an over-performer opens doors**.

This creates a natural moat: the best candidates have an incentive to use Careerspan because it gives them an edge—they're not just another resume in the pile.

---

## The Company Value Proposition

### Why hiring managers use Careerspan:

**Alternative 1 (Current State):**  
Use every other ATS system → get flooded with applications → manually screen hundreds of resumes → hope to find qualified candidates

**Alternative 2 (Careerspan):**  
Post a job with the Careerspan Network for ~$500 → only see candidates who are highly suited to your needs and have been evaluated as over-performers → hire with confidence

### The trust equation:

We need to position ourselves around one of two trust vectors:
1. **Trust in candidate quality:** The people we surface are demonstrably good
2. **Trust in network size/scale:** We have enough over-performers in the pool to match any need

Initial hypothesis: **Lead with candidate quality.** The $500 price point is justified if the people we surface are so good that hiring managers would rather pay a small fee than wade through the noise.

### Bespoke hiring at scale

This is our killer positioning line.

Traditional hiring forces a choice:
- **Bespoke/boutique recruiting:** High-touch, expensive, slow
- **Scale/ATS platforms:** Fast, cheap, low quality

Careerspan breaks this trade-off. We deliver high-quality, highly-suited candidates at scale through dynamic evaluation—not manual curation.

## Strategic Implications

### 1. Product positioning

**Core message:** Careerspan is a system for identifying the people you want to work with—the ones who try hard, show up, and deliver above-normal results.

**Candidate-side messaging:**
- "See how you measure up"
- "Are you over-performing? You either need to be paid more or you need a new role"
- "Your performance on Careerspan is your credential"

**Company-side messaging:**
- "Stop screening the world manually"
- "Only see candidates who are highly suited to your needs"
- "Bespoke hiring at scale"

### 2. Go-to-market options

**Option A:** Lead with candidate quality trust  
- Position: "We surface over-performers. Period."
- Price: ~$500 to post a job
- Value prop: The people we surface are so good, you want to use it

**Option B:** Lead with network scale + quality perk  
- Position: "Evaluate candidates against our standard + get access to the Careerspan Network as a perk"
- Price: TBD (potentially tiered)
- Value prop: Use us to screen your own pipeline + tap into our over-performer network

**Recommendation:** Start with Option A. Simpler message, clearer differentiation, stronger positioning against ATS incumbents.

### 3. Revenue model implications

**For candidates:**
- Consideration: Could we charge candidates just for the career evaluation/planning tool?
- Current stance: Don't charge candidates—maximize network effects and candidate quality/volume
- Trade-off: If we don't charge candidates, we need companies to approach them (requires putting profiles out there)

**For companies:**
- Primary revenue: ~$500 per job posting (flat fee or success-based TBD)
- Value justification: Time saved screening + quality of candidates surfaced >> cost
- Scalability: If we can prove ROI, this scales with company adoption

### 4. Competitive differentiation

**vs. Traditional ATS (Greenhouse, Lever, etc.):**
- They optimize for volume and process management
- We optimize for candidate quality and dynamic evaluation
- **Our edge:** Pre-vetted over-performers, not just applicant tracking

**vs. Recruiting firms:**
- They offer bespoke but expensive and slow
- We offer bespoke at scale through technology
- **Our edge:** Speed + cost + quality, powered by dynamic evaluation

**vs. Talent networks (Toptal, etc.):**
- They curate and vet manually
- We evaluate dynamically and systematically
- **Our edge:** Scalability + transparency + candidate ownership of their performance data

## Open Questions

1. **Trust positioning:** Do we lead with "trust in candidate quality" or "trust in network size"?  
   - Initial hypothesis: Quality first, scale later

2. **Pricing:** Is ~$500 per job posting the right price point?  
   - Need to test willingness-to-pay and ROI perception
   - Consider tiered pricing (basic network access vs. premium features)

3. **Candidate incentive:** Do we charge candidates for the evaluation tool, or keep it free to maximize network effects?  
   - Current stance: Free for candidates, monetize company-side
   - Risk: If we don't charge, we need strong company-side traction to justify candidate investment

4. **Go-to-market sequencing:** Do we start with companies or candidates?  
   - Chicken-and-egg problem: Companies want access to over-performers, but we need over-performers in the network first
   - Hypothesis: Start with candidates (career evaluation tool + performance visibility), then layer in company access once we have critical mass
