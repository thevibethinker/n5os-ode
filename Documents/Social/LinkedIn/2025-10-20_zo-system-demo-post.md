# LinkedIn Post: Zo Demo Hook

Most founders I talk to are drowning in tools.

Notion for docs. Slack for comms. Gmail for everything else. And somehow, the most important insights—the ones from quick voice memos between meetings—get lost.

Here's what I built instead:

I email myself a 30-second voice memo tagged [Reflect].

While I'm in my next meeting, my personal server (Zo):
- Transcribes it
- Extracts key points and action items
- Cross-references past conversations
- Stages a summary for my approval
- Files it in my searchable knowledge base

**Zero manual work. Complete audit trail. I own all the files.**

The difference between this and ChatGPT? Context.

ChatGPT gives you a generic answer.
Zo knows your past decisions, your stakeholders, your voice—because it lives in your infrastructure and learns from your files.

Last week, I asked Zo to draft a follow-up email to a strategic partner. It pulled:
- Notes from our last three meetings
- Their company's current priorities
- My communication patterns
- Open action items between us

The result? An email that sounded like me, referenced specific conversations, and moved things forward.

That's not possible with a chat window that forgets everything.

This is what automation should be: **Working on your actual work. In your formats. On your terms.**
